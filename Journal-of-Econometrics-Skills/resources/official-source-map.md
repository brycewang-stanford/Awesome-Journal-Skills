## Official journal pages

- **https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/journal-of-econometrics/publish/guide-for-authors** —
  ScienceDirect Guide for Authors. Verifies: current **Submit your article** link points to
  **Editorial Manager**; JoE accepts regular full-length papers, short papers under 15 pages,
  themed issues, invited "how to" papers, and Annals Issues; review is **single anonymized** with
  editor screening and usually at least two reviewers; the submission fee is **USD $75** for new
  submissions and resubmissions over one year, with no student discounts/refunds and VAT for
  European authors; abstracts must be concise/factual and **not exceed 250 words**; initial source
  files should be editable (`.doc/.docx` or `.tex`) and PDF is not an acceptable source file for the
  Guide's source-file requirement; dataset references use the **`[dataset]`** tag with repository,
  version/year, and persistent identifier; optional **Data in Brief / MethodsX** co-submission is
  handled through Editorial Manager.
- **https://sites.google.com/view/journalofeconometrics/guidelines** — The journal's own
  self-administered author guidelines. Verifies: the page still says all submissions are made
  through **Editorial Express**; proof of payment is required; the linked guideline buckets are
  **Regular Issues, Annals Issues, and Themed Issues**.
- **https://sites.google.com/view/journalofeconometrics/themes** — Themed (Theme) Issues page.
  Verifies: Themed Issues go through the regular editorial process; proposal organizers may serve as
  **Guest Associate Editors** who make recommendations to the Co-Editors.
- **https://editorialexpress.com/cgi-bin/e-editor/e-submit.cgi?dbase=je** — JoE submission portal on
  **Editorial Express** (database `je`). Verifies: the page currently says **"This page is for
  resubmissons only!"** and links new submissions to Editorial Manager; within the Editorial Express
  flow, main text should be around 40 pages at least 1.5 spacing and 11pt font, manuscripts must be
  uploaded in PDF format, and the form has a six-step submission process.

## History, scope, editors, metrics

- **https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Journal_of_Econometrics** — Verifies: publisher **Elsevier**;
  **founded 1973**; Co-Editors-in-Chief **Michael Jansson (UC Berkeley)** and **Aureo de Paula
  (University College London)**; peer-reviewed; **"Fellow of the Journal of Econometrics"**
  designation for authors who publish four or more articles in the journal. Scope covers
  econometric theory and applied methodology.

## Scope summary (used as load-bearing framing)

- **Field focus:** econometric methodology spanning theory and applied methods — identification,
  estimation, testing, decision, and prediction problems arising in economic research, plus
  applications of econometric techniques to economics. Source: scope statements on the Elsevier /
  ScienceDirect journal homepage and Wikipedia (above), accessed 2026-06-01.

## Open issues to confirm live

- **Portal routing is an official-page conflict, not an inference.** ScienceDirect now links new
  JoE submissions to **Editorial Manager**. The Editorial Express `dbase=je` page says it is for
  resubmissions only and links new submissions to Editorial Manager. The JoE Google Sites
  Guidelines page still says all submissions are made through **Editorial Express**. This pack now
  tells users to start from ScienceDirect/current official links for new submissions and to use
  Editorial Express for resubmission/track materials when the live official page directs them there.
- **~40-page / PDF-only guidance is Editorial Express-form guidance.** Treat it as a practical
  resubmission-flow norm, not as a ScienceDirect Guide hard limit for new submissions.
- **Full current editor roster is outside this refreshed map.** The pack keeps the two
  Co-Editors-in-Chief as contextual, non-blocking information and does not rely on a complete board
  roster for workflow decisions.
- **Replication policy is encouragement-first.** The official guide verifies dataset citation,
  data-linking support, and Data in Brief / MethodsX co-submission. The pack should recommend a
  strong archive and data-availability statement, while avoiding any claim that JoE has a universal
  Data-Editor-vetted archive comparable to JAE or Econometric Society journals.
