# CA Pre-Submission Checklist (Current Anthropology)

Current Anthropology is the transnational, **all-subfields** journal (sociocultural, archaeology,
biological/physical, linguistic, plus ethnohistory, prehistory, and applied work), published by the
**University of Chicago Press for the Wenner-Gren Foundation for Anthropological Research**, submitted
through **Editorial Manager**. Its defining feature is the **CA✩ Treatment**: an accepted **Major Article**
is sent to invited international commentators whose **signed Comments are published alongside it**,
followed by the **author's Reply**. Verify all volatile specifics (caps, portal prompts, anonymity model,
editors, data policy wording, copyright form) on the official CA / Wenner-Gren / UChicago Press pages
before submitting.

## 1. Scope, fit & article type

- [ ] The contribution matters to anthropology **writ large**, not only the home subfield
- [ ] The right **article type** is chosen:
      Major Article (field-shaping; gets the CA✩ Treatment) / Report / Forum / Discussion-Comment /
      Current Applications
- [ ] For a **Major Article**: the intervention is bold and synthetic enough that **international
      commentators would have substantive things to debate**
- [ ] Distinguished from siblings (not *American Anthropologist*, *American Ethnologist*, or
      *Cultural Anthropology*) — CA is **Wenner-Gren-sponsored** with the **CA✩ comment-and-reply** format
- [ ] Confirmed against the current CA aims & scope

## 2. Length & abstract

- [ ] **Major Article 6,000–10,000 words** (incl. references and endnotes); **Report 3,000–5,000 words**
      (incl. refs/notes); **Discussion/Comment ≤ 800 words** including references
- [ ] **Abstract ≤ 200 words** (Major Article and Report), stating question, approach, and intervention
- [ ] Figure/table count within cap: **12** (Major Article) / **4** (Report)

## 3. Files & formats (Editorial Manager)

- [ ] Text + tables in a **single `.doc` or `.rtf`** file (**not** a PDF)
- [ ] Figures (graphs and images) supplied as **separate TIFF (`.tif`) or EPS (`.eps`)** files
- [ ] Title-page info ready: title, short title, authors and affiliations, corresponding-author contact
- [ ] Within portal file-size and file-type limits

## 4. House style & references

- [ ] **Free-format** acceptable at submission; **final accepted files** use **Chicago Manual of Style
      author-date**
- [ ] References **alphabetized** and **not embedded in endnotes**
- [ ] **DOIs** included in references where available
- [ ] Every in-text citation is in the list and vice versa; all references real and accurate
- [ ] Transnational **citational politics** considered — regional/Indigenous scholarship represented
- [ ] Jargon minimized; main ideas comprehensible to nonspecialists; inclusive, care-ful language

## 5. Evidence & argument quality (built for the CA✩ Treatment)

- [ ] Intervention stated by the end of the introduction; argument is portable across subfields
- [ ] Bold and defensible enough to **reward and survive published Comments**
- [ ] Likely **commentators / camps anticipated** and pre-engaged in the positioning
- [ ] Positionality / reflexivity made explicit where appropriate
- [ ] Interpretive claims grounded in shown evidence; the strongest rival reading adjudicated
- [ ] (Biological/archaeological) effect sizes + uncertainty reported; measures validated;
      ancestry not conflated with social race

## 6. Figures, media & permissions

- [ ] Each exhibit is self-contained (caption, labels, source, date/place) and **argues**, not decorates
- [ ] Alt text / image descriptions provided; color encoding legible in grayscale where meaning depends on it
- [ ] **Permissions/credits** secured for archival, museum, and others' images/media
- [ ] People depicted **consented** to that use; identifiers handled per consent
- [ ] Sacred/heritage materials and ancestral remains handled per community authority (some withheld)

## 7. Ethics & accountability

- [ ] **Informed consent** obtained and ongoing; covers publication and media use
- [ ] **IRB/ethics approval** documented where applicable
- [ ] **Anonymization** strategy protects vulnerable/criminalized interlocutors; limits stated
- [ ] **Heritage / NAGPRA / repatriation** obligations met for remains, objects, and biological samples
- [ ] Competing obligations (to people, communities, scholarship, profession) weighed and explained
- [ ] **Ethics statement** included in the submission

## 8. Copyright & originality

- [ ] **Wenner-Gren copyright assignment** understood; you hold the rights you will assign (incl.
      co-held community materials, archival images, others' media)
- [ ] Original, unpublished, not under review elsewhere; preprint status checked vs. CA/UChicago policy
- [ ] Files staged: anonymized/main `.doc`/`.rtf` manuscript + separate TIFF/EPS figures + title-page
      info + ethics statement + any forms
