## How to use this library

CA is anthropology's transnational, all-subfields journal, and its Major Articles are **debated in print**
under the CA✩ Treatment. Each paper below earns its place by making a result in one subfield legible and
*arguable* for the whole discipline (see
[`../../skills/curranthro-topic-selection/SKILL.md`](../../skills/curranthro-topic-selection/SKILL.md) and
[`../../skills/curranthro-theory-building/SKILL.md`](../../skills/curranthro-theory-building/SKILL.md)).
Pick the row whose **subfield × method** is closest to yours, then study how it (1) makes an all-fields,
commentary-worthy claim, and (2) defends its evidence on its own methodological terms (see
[`../../skills/curranthro-research-design/SKILL.md`](../../skills/curranthro-research-design/SKILL.md)).

## By subfield × method

### Sociocultural (transnational / political subjectivity) — CA✩ format

- **Ong, Aihwa. "Cultural Citizenship as Subject-Making: Immigrants Negotiate Racial and Cultural
  Boundaries in the United States [and Comments and Reply]," CA 1996, 37(5):737–762.**
  Verified: UChicago Press DOI `10.1086/204560`; published with signed Comments and a Reply.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a bold theoretical reframing (cultural citizenship as "subject-making")
    pitched at a level the whole discipline can argue with — exactly the kind of agenda-setting claim the
    CA✩ Treatment rewards. Study the **lead article + Comments + Reply** as one debate.
  - **Self-check:** is your concept bold and portable enough that international commentators would engage
    it (see `curranthro-theory-building`: commentary stress test)?

### Reflexive / disciplinary critique — CA✩ format

- **Strathern, Marilyn. "Out of Context: The Persuasive Fictions of Anthropology [and Comments and
  Reply]," CA 1987, 28(3):251–281.**
  Verified: UChicago Press DOI `10.1086/203527`; Comments by Crick, Fardon, Hatch, Jarvie, Pinxten,
  Rabinow, Tonkin, Tyler, and a Reply.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** turns the discipline's own writing into the object of analysis — a
    reflexive, field-shaping argument that drew a wide international commentary. A model of how a
    provocation is built to *invite* debate rather than foreclose it.
  - **Self-check:** does your framing make the reader notice the anthropologist's position and stakes
    (see `curranthro-theory-building`: positionality)?

### Archaeology (archaeological theory / inference) — CA✩ format

- **Earle, Timothy K., and Robert W. Preucel. "Processual Archaeology and the Radical Critique [and
  Comments and Reply]," CA 1987, 28(4):501–538.**
  Verified: UChicago Press DOI `10.1086/203551`; Comments from a large international panel (Brumfiel,
  Hodder, Gilman, Trigger, and others) and a Reply.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a methodological debate about how far archaeological *inference* can reach —
    staged as a Major Article whose published Comments map the field's camps. Shows how to write the lead
    piece so the commentary deepens, not derails, the argument.
  - **Self-check:** are you explicit about how far your material evidence licenses interpretive claims
    (see `curranthro-research-design`: the adjudication test)?

### Linguistic anthropology (programmatic synthesis)

- **Duranti, Alessandro. "Language as Culture in U.S. Anthropology: Three Paradigms," CA 2003,
  44(3):323–347.**
  Verified: UChicago Press DOI `10.1086/368118` (also on JSTOR at the same DOI).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a synthetic, agenda-setting account of how language has been theorized
    across U.S. anthropology — legible to all subfields, not a linguistics-internal point. A model of the
    "set the terms of future research" Major Article.
  - **Self-check:** does your language analysis show what the discourse *indexes* about culture, beyond its
    form (see `curranthro-data-analysis`: linguistic row)?

### Comparative method / theory — CA✩ format (author 待核实)

- **"The Comparative Method in Anthropology [and Comments and Reply]," CA 1994, 35(5).**
  Verified as CA with the CA✩ comment format: UChicago Press DOI `10.1086/204317`. **Lead author 待核实** —
  confirm the byline and page range on the UChicago Press record before citing.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a methodological Major Article on comparison itself, published with
    international Comments and a Reply — useful for seeing how a *methods* argument earns the CA✩ Treatment.

### Biological / physical anthropology (life-history synthesis) — Wenner-Gren symposium supplement (待核实)

- **"Plasticity in Human Life History Strategy: Implications for Contemporary Human Variation and the
  Evolution of Genus Homo," CA 2012, 53(S6).**
  Verified as CA: UChicago Press DOI `10.1086/667410`. This is a **Wenner-Gren symposium supplement (S6)**
  piece (a special-issue format, *not* the standard CA✩ commented Major Article). **Authors 待核实** —
  confirm the byline and pages on the UChicago Press record before citing.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** ties a *biological* synthesis (life-history plasticity) to claims about
    contemporary human variation and evolution that sociocultural readers can use — biology made legible
    across the discipline (see `curranthro-research-design`: biological mode). Note it sits in a supplement,
    so it does not model the CA✩ comment apparatus.
