# External Tools - CoRL

Access date: 2026-07-08

Open these surfaces before giving any cycle-specific advice. Note the access
caveat: in this pack's verification environment, direct fetches of corl.org
returned 403 and pages had to be read via search-engine renderings — if the same
happens to you, cross-check any single rendering against OpenReview and PMLR
before trusting a date or cap.

## Official surfaces

- CoRL 2026 year-site: https://www.corl.org/
- Call for Papers: https://www.corl.org/contributions/call-for-papers
- Instruction for Authors (page limits, template, video, GenAI policy):
  https://www.corl.org/contributions/instruction-for-authors
- Instruction for Rebuttal (one-page PDF, deadlines):
  https://www.corl.org/contributions/instruction-for-rebuttal
- Reviewer-side instructions (scoring rubric, first-round gate, discussion):
  https://www.corl.org/contributions/instruction-for-reviewers and
  https://www.corl.org/contributions/instruction-for-reviews
- OpenReview group:
  https://openreview.net/group?id=robot-learning.org/CoRL/2026/Conference
- PMLR volume index (venue attribution and volume anchors):
  https://proceedings.mlr.press/
- Robot Learning Foundation (series steward): https://www.robot-learning.org/
- dblp CoRL stream (edition history cross-check):
  https://dblp.org/db/conf/corl/index.html

Past year-sites follow a `NNNN.corl.org` pattern (e.g., https://2025.corl.org/,
https://2024.corl.org/) — useful for diffing how rules changed between cycles.

## Author-side verification passes

Run these five passes against the live pages before deadline-sensitive action:

1. **Calendar pass** — abstract vs paper deadline spacing, rebuttal date,
   discussion window, decision date, camera-ready date, and the clock convention
   for each (do not assume AoE everywhere; 2026 renderings disagreed on the May
   deadline clock).
2. **Format pass** — current page accounting (2026: 8-page body *including* the
   mandatory Limitations section; 9 pages at camera-ready), the year's template
   name, and appendix placement rules.
3. **Anonymity pass** — the double-anonymous wording, what counts as an
   identifying link (project sites, repos, video hosting), and any new
   disclosure fields (the GenAI policy appeared mid-cycle in 2026).
4. **Supplementary pass** — video size/length caps, allowed file types, and the
   PMLR no-video rule that shapes camera-ready hosting plans.
5. **Process pass** — the first-round rejection wording, rebuttal format, who
   sees what (reviewer/AC/SAC), and whether reviews of accepted papers will be
   public that year.

## Do not infer

- Do not project 2026 dates, page rules, or the rebuttal format onto CoRL 2027;
  every one of these is chair-set per edition.
- Do not assume the PMLR volume number or publication-year alignment for a new
  cycle; volumes are assigned late and years can offset from the conference.
- Do not treat search-rendering phrasings as verbatim policy text; where wording
  matters (dual submission scope, GenAI disclosure), read the actual page.
- Where sources conflict, precedence runs: current-year corl.org instruction
  pages > OpenReview venue announcements > PMLR volume pages > everything else —
  and record the conflict in your notes.
