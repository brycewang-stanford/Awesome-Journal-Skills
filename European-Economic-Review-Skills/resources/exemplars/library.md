# EER Exemplars Library (method × topic)

> **Verified via web search, access date 2026-06.** Every paper below was checked to confirm it actually
> appeared in the ***European Economic Review*** (Elsevier, ScienceDirect) and carries the
> **`10.1016/j.euroecorev…`** DOI stem (older articles use the `10.1016/0014-2921…` stem) — the marker
> that confirms EER specifically. Year, volume, and pages were read off the RePEc / Elsevier article pages.
> Papers that could not be fully verified as EER were **omitted** — this is deliberately a short, clean
> list rather than a long, uncertain one.
>
> **Sibling-confusion guard (do not misattribute).** EER is **not** the *Journal of the European Economic
> Association* (**JEEA**, the EEA's own flagship, Oxford UP / `10.1111/jeea…`), **not** *The Economic
> Journal* (RES flagship, Oxford UP), and **not** an Elsevier field journal such as the *Journal of
> Monetary Economics* (`10.1016/j.jmoneco…`) or *Labour Economics* (`10.1016/j.labeco…`). Only the
> **`10.1016/j.euroecorev…`** (or `…/0014-2921…`) stem confirms EER. The omissions section records the
> sibling-confusion guardrail.
>
> **Use principle (zero hallucination):** this file gives **design positioning only**. It does not reproduce
> or invent estimates or findings — read the original before citing any number. For EER-specific policy,
> scope, and house rules, see [`../official-source-map.md`](../official-source-map.md).

## How to use this library

Pick the row whose **method × question** is closest to yours, then study how that paper makes a
*credible* result answer a *general-interest* economic question — the EER bar
(see [`../../skills/eer-topic-selection/SKILL.md`](../../skills/eer-topic-selection/SKILL.md) and
[`../../skills/eer-writing-style/SKILL.md`](../../skills/eer-writing-style/SKILL.md)). For each, ask the
self-check before claiming your paper is "EER-shaped." Remember EER's house norm: report **standard errors /
confidence intervals**, and let the magnitude carry the result
([`../../skills/eer-tables-figures/SKILL.md`](../../skills/eer-tables-figures/SKILL.md)).

## By method

### Macro growth theory (endogenous growth, dynamics & stability)

- **Peretto, "Robust endogenous growth," EER 2018, 108:49–77.**
  Verified: RePEc `eee/eecrev/v108y2018icp49-77` (DOI `10.1016/j.euroecorev.2018.06.007`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a theory paper that delivers a **general** result — existence *and* global
    stability of the endogenous-growth steady state, with careful transitional dynamics — rather than a
    knife-edge construction. The lesson (the growth rate need not hinge on scale effects) travels to a
    broad macro readership, the EER theory bar. Routes to
    [`../../skills/eer-theory-model/SKILL.md`](../../skills/eer-theory-model/SKILL.md) (Branch A, generality + intuition).
  - **Self-check:** is your main proposition stated first, shown to be general (not a knife-edge), and given
    economic intuition a non-specialist can state?

### Political economy / economic history (long-run panel, financial crises)

- **Funke, Schularick & Trebesch, "Going to extremes: Politics after financial crises, 1870–2014," EER 2016, 88:227–260.**
  Verified: RePEc `eee/eecrev/v88y2016icp227-260` (DOI `10.1016/j.euroecorev.2016.03.006`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a first-order, general-interest question (do financial crises radicalize
    politics?) answered with a large long-run cross-country panel and transparent reduced-form evidence — a
    result that crosses macro, political economy, and history. Exactly the breadth EER rewards.
  - **Self-check:** does your reduced-form result speak to a question economists outside your subfield care
    about, with the empirical pattern shown cleanly rather than buried?

### Labor / search theory with heterogeneity (technical change, job finding)

- **Basov, King & Uren, "Worker heterogeneity, the job-finding rate, and technical change," EER 2014, 70:159–177.**
  Verified: RePEc `eee/eecrev/v70y2014icp159-177` (DOI `10.1016/j.euroecorev.2014.04.008`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a labor-search model with worker heterogeneity used to interpret a substantive
    pattern (the job-finding rate under technical change) — theory disciplined toward an empirical reading, the
    [`../../skills/eer-theory-model/SKILL.md`](../../skills/eer-theory-model/SKILL.md) Branch C bar.
  - **Self-check:** does your model deliver a testable comparative static about something a general labor/macro
    reader cares about, not a decorative formalism?

### Experimental economics (strategic play, learning from advice)

- **Cooper & Kagel, "A failure to communicate: an experimental investigation of the effects of advice on strategic play," EER 2016, 82:24–45.**
  Verified: RePEc `eee/eecrev/v82y2016icp24-45` (DOI `10.1016/j.euroecorev.2015.10.008`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a clean lab experiment on a general behavioral question (when does advice
    improve strategic play?), with the controlled design and reporting EER experimental work expects —
    instructions, balance, and transparent inference. Pairs with
    [`../../skills/eer-identification/SKILL.md`](../../skills/eer-identification/SKILL.md) Branch D.
  - **Self-check:** is your estimand explicit, your design pre-registered/instructions-documented where
    applicable, and your inference honest about multiple comparisons?

### International / trade with labor heterogeneity (quantitative trade & migration)

- **Heiland & Kohler, "Heterogeneous workers, trade, and migration," EER 2022, 144:104077.**
  Verified: RePEc `eee/eecrev/v144y2022ics0014292122000265` (DOI `10.1016/j.euroecorev.2022.104077`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a quantitative trade-and-migration model with heterogeneous workers — a
    general-interest international result built on a disciplined model, the
    [`../../skills/eer-theory-model/SKILL.md`](../../skills/eer-theory-model/SKILL.md) Branch B bar
    (model + mapping to data + counterfactual).
  - **Self-check:** is your quantitative model disciplined by data, validated beyond targeted moments, and
    used for a counterfactual a reduced-form design could not deliver?

## Omitted for lack of full verification, or to avoid sibling-confusion (do not attribute to EER without re-checking)

To keep the list zero-hallucination, the following were **handled with care**:

- **Several EER-area candidates surfaced in search (e.g., recent 2026 macro/labor articles such as work on
  unemployment insurance and the wealth distribution)** were **left off** because their exact EER
  volume/page/DOI could not be confirmed to the same standard in one pass — the article number was visible
  but not a full `10.1016/j.euroecorev…` DOI with volume/pages. Add them only after reading the ScienceDirect /
  RePEc article page and confirming the DOI stem.
- **JEEA / The Economic Journal papers** in the same subject areas are **not** EER. JEEA carries an Oxford
  UP / `10.1111/jeea…` identifier and The Economic Journal is the RES flagship; neither shares the
  `10.1016/j.euroecorev…` stem. Listed here only as the sibling-confusion guardrail.

Before adding any new paper to this library, confirm it on `sciencedirect.com` or `ideas.repec.org`
(article page with volume/pages) **and** that the DOI begins `10.1016/j.euroecorev` (or `10.1016/0014-2921`
for older volumes), then update the access-date header. When in doubt, omit.
