EER is general-interest across all of economics; the structure below works for

---

## Title page
- **Title** — concise, result- or mechanism-forward, generalist-legible
- **Authors & affiliations**; corresponding author + email
- **Declaration of interest** statement (Elsevier requirement; "none" if none)
- **Funding** statement
- **Acknowledgments** (optional)

## Research highlights
- 3–5 short bullets, each a concrete finding (not a topic)
- Example shape: "A 10% input-cost shock raises retail prices 6.2% (s.e. 0.7)."

## Abstract
- Concise and **result-forward**: state the headline finding, not just the method

## Keywords and JEL codes
- Keywords: 4–6, cross-field where the result travels
- JEL codes: accurate primary + secondary

## 1. Introduction (the EER arc)
- Question → why hard/open → approach → **headline result with SE/CI** → mechanism → contribution & lesson → scope → brief roadmap
- Numbers carry standard errors / confidence intervals, never significance asterisks

## 2. Theory / Model (if applicable)
- Main proposition or model object stated first; minimal, defended assumptions; intuition for every formal result

## 3. Data and institutional setting
- Sources, sample construction, key variables; data-availability statement consistent with the deposit

## 4. Empirical strategy / Identification
- Design, the identifying assumption, and the main threat pre-empted (DiD / IV / RDD / experiment)

## 5. Results
- One headline table (SEs under estimates); key figure with uncertainty bands; magnitudes spelled out in interpretable units

## 6. Robustness
- Coefficient-stability / sensitivity battery tied to specific objections; fragilities stated honestly

## 7. Mechanisms / Counterfactual (if applicable)
- What drives the result; for quantitative work, a counterfactual the structure delivers, with uncertainty

## 8. Conclusion
- Restate the contribution and the portable lesson; state scope and what is not claimed

## Appendix / Supplementary material
- Proofs, additional robustness, extra exhibits; the main paper remains self-contained

## Declarations
- Declaration of interest; funding; data and code availability statement (mandatory replication policy); CRediT author roles; AI-use disclosure (AI not an author)
