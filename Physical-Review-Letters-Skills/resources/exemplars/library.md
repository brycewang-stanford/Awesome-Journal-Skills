# PRL Exemplars Library (subfield × method)

> **Verified via web search, access date 2026-06-08.** Every Letter below was checked to confirm it
> actually appeared in ***Physical Review Letters*** (not a sibling journal), with the canonical APS DOI
> whose stem is **`10.1103/PhysRevLett...`**. Candidates that turned out to be in a sibling or other
> journal were **omitted** and are documented at the bottom. It is deliberately a short, clean,
> fully-verified list (6 verified) rather than a long, uncertain one.
>
> **Sibling-confusion guard — PRL is NOT:** *Physical Review X* (`PhysRevX`), *Physical Review
> D / C / B / A / E* (`PhysRevD`, `PhysRevB`, ...), *Reviews of Modern Physics* (`RevModPhys`),
> *Physics Letters B* (`physletb`), or *Nature Physics*. A DOI is only accepted here if its stem is
> exactly `10.1103/PhysRevLett`.
>
> **Use principle (zero hallucination):** this file gives **design positioning only** — how each Letter
> clears the PRL importance-and-broad-interest gate and structures one central claim. It does **not**
> reproduce or invent measured values; read the original on `link.aps.org` before citing any number.

## How to use this library

Pick the row whose **subfield × method** is closest to yours, then study how that Letter makes a single
result clear the PRL **two-part gate** (importance AND broad interest — see
[`../../skills/prl-scope-fit/SKILL.md`](../../skills/prl-scope-fit/SKILL.md)) and front-loads its central
claim (see [`../../skills/prl-results-framing/SKILL.md`](../../skills/prl-results-framing/SKILL.md)). For
each, ask the self-check question before claiming your Letter is "PRL-shaped."

## By subfield × method

### Gravitation / astrophysics — large-collaboration decisive measurement

- **B. P. Abbott *et al.* (LIGO Scientific Collaboration & Virgo Collaboration), "Observation of
  Gravitational Waves from a Binary Black Hole Merger," Phys. Rev. Lett. 116, 061102 (2016).**
  Verified: DOI `10.1103/PhysRevLett.116.061102` — `link.aps.org/doi/10.1103/PhysRevLett.116.061102`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a single, unambiguous first detection settling a decades-open question;
    the title *is* the central claim and the abstract leads with the finding and its significance — the
    archetypal PRL "decisive measurement" that the whole of physics wants to read.
  - **Self-check:** does your headline result settle one widely-discussed open question, stated in the
    title, so an out-of-subfield physicist grasps it from the abstract alone?

### Condensed matter — transport experiment revealing a new phenomenon

- **D. C. Tsui, H. L. Störmer & A. C. Gossard, "Two-Dimensional Magnetotransport in the Extreme Quantum
  Limit," Phys. Rev. Lett. 48, 1559 (1982).**
  Verified: DOI `10.1103/PhysRevLett.48.1559` — `link.aps.org/doi/10.1103/PhysRevLett.48.1559`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a clean transport signature announces a qualitatively new state of matter
    (the fractional quantum Hall effect) — a *new phenomenon*, not an incremental refinement, which is
    exactly the importance bar in `prl-scope-fit`.
  - **Self-check:** does your measurement reveal a qualitatively new effect, or only measure a known one
    more precisely (the latter usually fails the importance gate)?

### Condensed matter / metrology — precision measurement with cross-field consequence

- **K. v. Klitzing, G. Dorda & M. Pepper, "New Method for High-Accuracy Determination of the
  Fine-Structure Constant Based on Quantized Hall Resistance," Phys. Rev. Lett. 45, 494 (1980).**
  Verified: DOI `10.1103/PhysRevLett.45.494` — `link.aps.org/doi/10.1103/PhysRevLett.45.494`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a condensed-matter measurement (quantized Hall resistance) reaches *out*
    of its subfield to metrology and fundamental constants — the broad-interest leg of the gate made
    concrete; the title names the cross-field payoff, not just the device.
  - **Self-check:** can you name a *different* community (here, metrology) for whom your precision result
    is load-bearing — the `prl-scope-fit` "neighbor test"?

### Quantum information — theory introducing a protocol

- **C. H. Bennett, G. Brassard, C. Crépeau, R. Jozsa, A. Peres & W. K. Wootters, "Teleporting an Unknown
  Quantum State via Dual Classical and Einstein-Podolsky-Rosen Channels," Phys. Rev. Lett. 70, 1895
  (1993).**
  Verified: DOI `10.1103/PhysRevLett.70.1895` — `link.aps.org/doi/10.1103/PhysRevLett.70.1895`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a compact theoretical idea (quantum teleportation) stated plainly enough
    that its consequences are immediately legible across quantum information, foundations, and
    experiment — PRL publishes theory when one idea reframes a field.
  - **Self-check:** can your central idea, and why it matters, be stated in one paragraph a
    non-specialist physicist grasps immediately?

### AMO / cold atoms — experiment realizing a sought-after state

- **C. C. Bradley, C. A. Sackett, J. J. Tollett & R. G. Hulet, "Evidence of Bose-Einstein Condensation in
  an Atomic Gas with Attractive Interactions," Phys. Rev. Lett. 75, 1687 (1995).**
  Verified: DOI `10.1103/PhysRevLett.75.1687` — `link.aps.org/doi/10.1103/PhysRevLett.75.1687`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a decisive experimental realization in a long-sought regime (BEC in a
    dilute atomic gas, here with attractive interactions) — importance from *unlocking a regime* others
    could not reach, framed for broad AMO and statistical-physics interest.
  - **Self-check:** does your experiment reach a regime the field has been trying and failing to access,
    rather than extend an already-accessible one?

### Particle physics — theory proposing an organizing principle

- **N. Cabibbo, "Unitary Symmetry and Leptonic Decays," Phys. Rev. Lett. 10, 531 (1963).**
  Verified: DOI `10.1103/PhysRevLett.10.531` — `link.aps.org/doi/10.1103/PhysRevLett.10.531`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a short theory Letter introduces a single organizing parameter (the
    mixing angle) that reorganizes how a whole class of weak decays is understood — maximal importance
    from minimal length, the PRL ideal.
  - **Self-check:** does your Letter introduce one principle/parameter that simplifies many previously
    separate facts, expressible within the strict length limit?

## Omitted for sibling-journal / venue mismatch (do NOT attribute to PRL)

To keep the list zero-hallucination, these landmark candidates were **excluded** after web verification
because they did **not** appear in *Physical Review Letters*:

- **J. M. Kosterlitz & D. J. Thouless, "Ordering, metastability and phase transitions in two-dimensional
  systems" (1973)** — verified to be in ***J. Phys. C: Solid State Phys.*** 6, 1181 (DOI
  `10.1088/0022-3719/6/7/010`), **not PRL**. Listed here only as a guardrail.
- **ATLAS Collaboration, "Observation of a new particle in the search for the Standard Model Higgs
  boson..." (2012)** — verified to be in ***Physics Letters B*** 716, 1 (DOI
  `10.1016/j.physletb.2012.08.020`), **not PRL**. A different journal with a confusingly similar name.
- **M. H. Anderson *et al.*, "Observation of Bose-Einstein Condensation in a Dilute Atomic Vapor"
  (Rb, 1995)** — appeared in ***Science***, **not PRL**. The PRL BEC landmark verified above is the
  attractive-interaction ⁷Li Letter (Bradley *et al.*, `PhysRevLett.75.1687`); do not conflate the two.

Before adding any new Letter to this library, confirm on `link.aps.org` (or `journals.aps.org/prl`) that
the article page is **Physical Review Letters** and that its DOI stem is exactly **`10.1103/PhysRevLett`**
— not `PhysRevX`, `PhysRevD/C/B/A/E`, `RevModPhys`, `physletb`, or a non-APS DOI — then update the
access-date header. When in doubt, omit.
