> actually appeared in **New Media & Society** (SAGE), with author, year, and volume/issue, against the

## By method

### Qualitative interviews + concept-building (social media, identity)

- **Marwick & boyd, "I tweet honestly, I tweet passionately: Twitter users, context collapse, and the imagined audience," NM&S 2011, 13(1):114–133.**
  Verified: SAGE `journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/1461444810365313`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** qualitative study of Twitter use raised to **portable concepts** —
    "context collapse" and the "imagined audience" — that scholars across fields now use far beyond
    Twitter. The platform is the site; the concept is the contribution (`newms-theory-building`).
  - **Self-check:** does your interview study name a concept other researchers can carry to a different
    platform, with scope conditions?

### Critical / theoretical concept article (platforms, datafication)

- **Gillespie, "The politics of 'platforms'," NM&S 2010, 12(3):347–364.**
  Verified: SAGE `journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/1461444809342738`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a critical-theoretical paper with no dataset that interrogates the word
    "platform" itself, showing how the term performs political work — a contribution made entirely
    through argument (`newms-theory-building`, critical/theoretical track).
  - **Self-check:** if you have no data, does the argument make visible something prior framings hid, and
    is it tied to concrete digital-media practice?

### Critical algorithm study (algorithms & power)

- **Bucher, "Want to be on the top? Algorithmic power and the threat of invisibility on Facebook," NM&S 2012, 14(7):1164–1180.**
  Verified: SAGE `journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/1461444812440159`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** analyzes one platform's ranking algorithm (EdgeRank) but extracts a
    general claim about algorithmic power and visibility — inverting a classic surveillance argument
    rather than describing a feature (`newms-topic-selection`: concept over platform).
  - **Self-check:** does your algorithm study yield a mechanism about power/visibility, not a
    feature walkthrough?

### Networked-privacy theorizing (privacy & surveillance)

- **Marwick & boyd, "Networked privacy: How teenagers negotiate context in social media," NM&S 2014, 16(7):1051–1067.**
  Verified: SAGE `journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/1461444814543995`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** re-specifies **privacy as relational and networked** rather than
    individual control — exactly the "challenge a dominant framing" positioning move
    (`newms-literature-positioning`).
  - **Self-check:** does your paper sharpen a travelling concept (here, privacy) against a named
    orthodoxy?

### Quantitative digital-divide measurement (digital inequality)

- **van Deursen & van Dijk, "The first-level digital divide shifts from inequalities in physical access to inequalities in material access," NM&S 2019, 21(2):354–375.**
  Verified: SAGE `journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/1461444818797082`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** quantitative survey work that reframes a long-running debate (what the
    "first-level" divide now is), turning measurement into a conceptual update — the quantitative strand
    of NM&S done with a portable claim, not just a coefficient (`newms-data-analysis`).
  - **Self-check:** does your quantitative result advance a conceptual debate the field actively has?

### Methods contribution / app studies (computational-adjacent, critical)

- **Light, Burgess & Duguay, "The walkthrough method: An approach to the study of apps," NM&S 2018, 20(3):881–900.**
  Verified: SAGE `journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/1461444816675438`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** offers a **named method** for studying apps as semi-closed sociotechnical
    systems — a methodological contribution that travels across any app, validated by application
    (`newms-research-design`, computational/critical track).
  - **Self-check:** if your contribution is methodological, is it named, generalizable, and demonstrated?

## Omitted for sibling-journal / verification reasons (do not attribute to NM&S without re-checking)

To keep the list zero-hallucination, these widely-cited works were **excluded** — each was verified to
be in a **sibling venue** or an edited book, not NM&S:

- **boyd, "Social Network Sites as Networked Publics: Affordances, Dynamics, and Implications" (2010)** —
  a **book chapter** in *A Networked Self* (ed. Papacharissi), **not an NM&S article**.
- **Gandini, "Digital labour: an empty signifier?" (2021)** — verified in **Media, Culture & Society**
  43(2):369–380, **not NM&S**.
- **Wood, Graham, Lehdonvirta & Hjorth, "Good Gig, Bad Gig" / "(Dis)embeddedness of digital labour"** —
  in *Work, Employment and Society* / *Sociology*, **not NM&S**.
- **Vitak, "The Impact of Context Collapse and Privacy on Social Network Site Disclosures" (2012)** — in
  the **Journal of Broadcasting & Electronic Media**, **not NM&S**.
- **Treem & Leonardi, "Social Media Use in Organizations" (affordances of visibility/persistence)** — in
  *Communication Yearbook* / *Annals of the ICA*, **not NM&S**.

Before adding any new paper to this library, confirm it on `journals.sagepub.com/home/nms` /
`journals.sagepub.com/doi/...` (NM&S article page with volume/issue), update the access-date header, and
watch the *Journal of Communication* / *Communication Research* / *Information, Communication & Society*
/ *Social Media + Society* / *Media, Culture & Society* / *JCMC* and edited-book traps. **When in doubt,
omit.**
