# Worked Example: Reframing an Introduction for Journal of Economic Behavior and Organization

This is a fictional example. It does not describe a real paper.

## Before

> This paper studies an important topic using a large dataset and several robustness checks. We contribute to the literature by adding new evidence and showing heterogeneous effects.

## Diagnosis

- The first sentence does not name the JEBO audience or the journal's behavioral economics, organization, institutions, experiments, and decision-making outside frictionless textbook settings scope.
- The contribution could fit several siblings: Experimental Economics, Games and Economic Behavior, Management Science, and Journal of Public Economics.
- The mechanism words that matter here are absent: behavioral mechanism, institutional setting, laboratory evidence, field-experiment design.

## After

> This paper asks how **behavioral mechanism** changes the decision margin that JEBO readers care about. The design ties the estimate to **institutional setting**, separates the main mechanism from **laboratory evidence**, and reports uncertainty in a way that makes the policy or theory implication portable beyond the study setting.
