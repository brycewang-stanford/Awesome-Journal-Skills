## How to use this library

Pick the row whose **method × question** is closest to yours, then study how that paper makes a
*specific* environmental measurement or synthesis carry a *direction-setting, multidisciplinary* lesson
— the ES&T bar (see
[`../../skills/est-topic-selection/SKILL.md`](../../skills/est-topic-selection/SKILL.md) and
[`../../skills/est-writing-style/SKILL.md`](../../skills/est-writing-style/SKILL.md)). For each, ask the
self-check question before claiming your paper is "ES&T-shaped."

## By method

### Field campaign with a mass balance across a treatment system (process/fate)

- **Murphy, Ewins, Carbonnier & Quinn, "Wastewater Treatment Works (WwTW) as a Source of Microplastics
  in the Aquatic Environment," _Environ. Sci. Technol._ 2016, 50(11):5800–5808.**
  Verified: `pubs.acs.org/doi/10.1021/acs.est.5b05416` (DOI 10.1021/acs.est.5b05416).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** samples a real treatment works stage by stage and tracks where
    microplastics are removed vs. released — a process study whose significance ("this works still
    releases a large daily load downstream") is concrete and quantitative, not generic. Pairs with
    [`../../skills/est-study-design/SKILL.md`](../../skills/est-study-design/SKILL.md) (mass balance) and
    [`../../skills/est-data-analysis/SKILL.md`](../../skills/est-data-analysis/SKILL.md).
  - **Self-check:** does your design follow the contaminant through the real system and account for
    where it goes (removed vs. discharged), rather than reporting a single end-of-pipe number?

### National-scale environmental monitoring / reconnaissance (occurrence & exposure)

- **Kolpin, Furlong, Meyer, Thurman, Zaugg, Barber & Buxton, "Pharmaceuticals, Hormones, and Other
  Organic Wastewater Contaminants in U.S. Streams, 1999–2000: A National Reconnaissance,"
  _Environ. Sci. Technol._ 2002, 36(6):1202–1211.**
  Verified: `pubs.acs.org/doi/10.1021/es011055j` (DOI 10.1021/es011055j).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** the first nationwide survey of contaminants of emerging concern in
    streams — a broad-reach occurrence study whose significance is the *pattern across a continent*, not
    one site, and which set the agenda for a whole subfield (direction-setting).
  - **Self-check:** does your monitoring answer a question at a scale or breadth that reframes how the
    field views the contaminant, rather than adding one more local dataset?

### Critical Review that synthesizes fate/transformation across a material class

- **Lowry, Gregory, Apte & Lead, "Transformations of Nanomaterials in the Environment,"
  _Environ. Sci. Technol._ 2012, 46(13):6893–6899.**
  Verified: `pubs.acs.org/doi/10.1021/es300839e` (DOI 10.1021/es300839e).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** reframes how to think about engineered nanomaterials — arguing the
    *as-synthesized* particle is the wrong unit of analysis because environmental transformations
    govern fate and toxicity. A synthesis that *identifies the gap* rather than summarizing, the
    ES&T Critical Review bar (see
    [`../../skills/est-topic-selection/SKILL.md`](../../skills/est-topic-selection/SKILL.md)).
  - **Self-check:** does your review change the *unit of analysis* or the questions people ask, instead
    of cataloguing what is known?

### Review of occurrence, human exposure, and toxicity for an emerging contaminant class (tox/exposure)

- **Chen, Kannan, Tan, Zheng, Feng, Wu & Widelka, "Bisphenol Analogues Other Than BPA: Environmental
  Occurrence, Human Exposure, and Toxicity—A Review," _Environ. Sci. Technol._ 2016, 50(11):5438–5453.**
  Verified: `pubs.acs.org/doi/10.1021/acs.est.5b05387` (DOI 10.1021/acs.est.5b05387).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** consolidates a fast-moving "regrettable substitution" story (BPA
    alternatives) across environment, exposure, and toxicity — multidisciplinary reach (chemists,
    toxicologists, regulators) is built into its structure.
  - **Self-check:** does your synthesis connect occurrence → exposure → effect so that audiences beyond
    your sub-area can act on it?

### Critical Review of a treatment/oxidation process with a control problem (environmental engineering)

- **Morrison, Hogard, Pearce, Mohan, Pisarenko, Dickenson, von Gunten & Wert, "Critical Review on
  Bromate Formation during Ozonation and Control Options for Its Minimization,"
  _Environ. Sci. Technol._ 2023, 57(47):18393–18409.**
  Verified: `pubs.acs.org/doi/10.1021/acs.est.3c00538` (DOI 10.1021/acs.est.3c00538).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** an engineering-treatment review organized around a real design tension
    (ozonation works, but forms a regulated byproduct) and the options to manage it — the
    science→practice framing ES&T rewards. Useful alongside
    [`../../skills/est-study-design/SKILL.md`](../../skills/est-study-design/SKILL.md).
  - **Self-check:** is your treatment work framed around a tradeoff a practitioner actually faces (here,
    disinfection vs. byproduct formation), with actionable control options?

### Synthesis arguing a planetary-scale / policy-relevant claim (science–policy interface)

- **Cousins, Johansson, Salter, Sha & Scheringer, "Outside the Safe Operating Space of a New Planetary
  Boundary for Per- and Polyfluoroalkyl Substances (PFAS)," _Environ. Sci. Technol._ 2022,
  56:11172–11179.**
  Verified: `pubs.acs.org/doi/10.1021/acs.est.2c02765` (DOI 10.1021/acs.est.2c02765).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** marshals existing environmental measurements against proposed guideline
    levels to make a single, broad, direction-setting argument that crosses into the policy interface —
    a model of letting evidence carry a generalizable claim without overclaiming new data.
  - **Self-check:** does your synthesis turn known measurements into one memorable, defensible claim of
    consequence, rather than restating the literature?

## Omitted for lack of full ES&T verification (do not attribute to ES&T without re-checking)

To keep the list zero-hallucination, candidates were **excluded** when web search could not confirm an
ES&T (`10.1021/es...` / `10.1021/acs.est...`) placement, or confirmed a *sibling* venue instead:

- **Hellweg & Milà i Canals, "Emerging approaches, challenges and opportunities in life cycle
  assessment" (2014)** — a prominent LCA review, but web search places it in **_Science_** (AAAS),
  **not ES&T**. Listed here only as a guardrail.
- **Recent microplastics-in-wastewater and PFAS-in-wastewater titles surfaced during searching** (e.g.,
  pieces with `10.1021/acsestwater...` DOIs) belong to **_ACS ES&T Water_**, and the
  population-wide PFAS-in-drinking-water reconnaissance with a `10.1021/acs.estlett...` DOI belongs to
  **_ES&T Letters_** — both are **separate journals**, so they are excluded by the sibling-confusion
  guard above.

Before adding any new paper to this library, confirm it on `pubs.acs.org` (article page showing
_Environmental Science & Technology_ with a `10.1021/es...` or `10.1021/acs.est...` DOI, volume/issue,
and pages) and update the access-date header. When in doubt, omit.
