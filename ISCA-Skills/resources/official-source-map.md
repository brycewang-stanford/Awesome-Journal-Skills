# Official Source Map - ISCA

Access date: 2026-07-08

This map records the official sources behind every ISCA-specific fact in this pack.
ISCA is jointly run by ACM SIGARCH and IEEE-CS TCCA, and each edition's organizing
committee re-decides deadlines, page rules, review mechanics, and artifact policy.
Reopen the current edition's site under `iscaconf.org` before giving submission-ready
advice.

direct fetches of `iscaconf.org`, `ieeetcca.org`, `dl.acm.org`, `sigarch.org`, and
`wikicfp.com`, so the pages below were read through search-engine renderings of the
exact URLs and cross-checked against one another (SIGARCH call postings vs the TCCA
cross-post vs the conference site vs ACM DL/dblp records). Open every primary URL
directly before relying on any date, cap, or policy stated here.

## Verified facts used in the skills (2026-cycle snapshot)

- ISCA 2026 was the **53rd** edition, held at the Raleigh Convention Center in
  Raleigh, North Carolina, USA, **June 27 - July 1, 2026** — i.e., it had just
  concluded when this pack was checked (2026-07-08).
- The main track collected **abstracts on November 10, 2025** and **full papers on
  November 17, 2025**; a separate **industry track** collected abstracts December 5
  and papers December 12, 2025.
- Reviewing ran in **two rounds** (Round 1 reviews due December 19, 2025; Round 2
  reviews due February 13, 2026), followed by a combined **rebuttal/revision period
  February 16 - March 6, 2026** in which authors respond and may revise.
- Submissions were capped at **11 pages of single-spaced two-column text, not
  including references**, with **no limit on reference pages**; LaTeX users were
  pointed at the **ISCA 2026 IEEE template**; upload was a printable PDF on
  **HotCRP** (`isca2026.hotcrp.com`).
- Review was **double-blind**: author names appear only in the submission form, PDF
  metadata must be scrubbed, artifact links (e.g., GitHub) must be fully anonymized,
  and — explicitly — references must **not** be omitted or anonymized for blinding.
- **Artifact evaluation** was voluntary and post-acceptance, run under the **ACM
  Artifact Review and Badging** policy, with badges printed on papers and attached
  as ACM DL metadata; participation did not affect acceptance decisions.
- ISCA is co-sponsored by **ACM SIGARCH and IEEE-CS TCCA** — both bodies posted the
  2026 call — and the joint branding appears in the edition title ("IEEE/ACM 53rd").
- The **Influential ISCA Paper Award** (SIGARCH + TCCA) is presented annually at the
  conference to a past ISCA paper of demonstrated long-term impact; the 2020-2025
  winners listed in source 9 anchor the exemplars library.
- ISCA 2025 (the previous edition) was the 52nd, in Tokyo, Japan, June 21-25, 2025,
  with proceedings published by ACM (source 8).
