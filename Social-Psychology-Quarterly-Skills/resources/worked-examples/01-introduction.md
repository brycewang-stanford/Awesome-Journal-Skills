# Worked Example: An SPQ-Style Introduction (before → after)

This demonstrates the **SPQ introduction arc** from `spq-writing-style` and `spq-theory-building`:
**question → the structure–individual link → tradition + named social-psychological mechanism →
design that captures the link → finding → contribution and scope conditions → brief roadmap** — with the
SPQ rule that **by the end of the introduction the reader knows the question, the tradition, the mechanism
connecting social structure to the individual, and why it matters**, written so a reader from *either*
parent discipline (sociology or psychology) can follow, with tradition-specific terms defined on first use.

This is a **sociological social-psychology** paper, not a psychology paper: the contribution is the *link
between social structure/process and the individual* (the self, identity, emotion, cognition, interaction),
not a clever individual-cognition finding (that would fit JPSP/Psychological Science) and not a macro
stratification pattern with no individual mechanism (that would fit ASR/AJS).

**Illustrative paper (fictional):** *"Vouched For: How Being Verified by Higher-Status Coworkers Sustains
the Work Identities of the Newly Promoted."* Fictional setting: a survey-plus-vignette study of newly
promoted frontline supervisors in a chain of mid-sized firms. The setting and all numbers are invented.

---

## Before (buries the link — typical first-draft intro)

> Identity has been studied extensively in sociology and psychology. Many scholars have examined the self,
> roles, and how people feel about their work. In this paper, we use a survey with an embedded vignette
> experiment and estimate multilevel models to study supervisors after promotion. We measure identity
> salience, self-esteem, and several workplace variables, and we run a series of regressions with
> individuals nested in firms. Workplace identity is an important topic. Section 2 reviews the literature,
> Section 3 describes the data, Section 4 presents the models, Section 5 reports results, and Section 6
> concludes.

**What's wrong (against `spq-writing-style` / `spq-topic-selection` / `spq-theory-building`):**

- **Leads with method** ("we use a survey with an embedded vignette … multilevel models") instead of the
  question and the structure–individual link — a named anti-pattern.
- **No structure–individual link on page one.** A reader cannot tell what *social* process is doing the
  work, or why the self is the outcome rather than a control.
- **No named social-psychological mechanism** — "a finding with no named mechanism" is an SPQ anti-pattern;
  identity verification, status, and the social structure that supplies them are never stated.
- **Tradition unlocated** (symbolic interaction / SSP / group processes / identity / affect), so a reader
  from the other parent discipline cannot place the claim.
- **Throat-clearing** ("has been studied extensively," "is an important topic") with vague stakes and no
  scope conditions.
- **Over-signposted roadmap** doing the work the argument should do.

---

## After (SPQ arc — question + structure–individual link + named mechanism, tradition located)

> **When a worker is promoted into a new role, what makes the new work identity *stick* rather than feel
> like an impostor's costume — and where does that come from in the social structure of the workplace?**
> We argue that the new identity is sustained not by private resolve but by **identity verification**:
> being treated by others *as* the kind of supervisor one is claiming to be. Crucially, *whose* verification
> counts is patterned by status — verification from **higher-status coworkers** does the stabilizing work,
> while verification from peers does much less. *(question + the structure–individual link + the named
> mechanism, in the first breath; "identity verification" — the reflected appraisal that one's role identity
> matches how others treat one — defined on first use for a cross-disciplinary reader.)*
>
> This sits in **identity theory**, at its junction with **status and expectation-states** ideas from the
> group-processes tradition: the self is anchored in the interaction order, but the interaction order is
> itself stratified, so the social structure of status decides which verifying responses a newcomer can
> actually obtain. *(tradition located; the two programs named and bridged.)*
>
> Studying this cleanly is hard because verification, status, and identity strength are entangled in
> observational workplace data: confident supervisors may simply elicit more affirming treatment. We
> address this with a survey of newly promoted supervisors paired with an **embedded vignette experiment**
> that randomizes the *status of the coworker* who verifies (or fails to verify) a supervisory claim,
> holding the verifying act constant — so the status of the source, not the supervisor's prior confidence,
> drives the contrast. *(design that captures the link; the rival "confident people draw praise" account is
> named and the manipulation is built to rule it out.)*
>
> Verification from a higher-status coworker raises supervisors' identity confidence by **0.42 standard
> deviations** relative to identical verification from a peer, and the gap is widest for supervisors **new
> to the authority structure** — those without prior managerial standing to fall back on. *(finding, with
> the scope condition built in.)*
>
> Our contribution is to show that **identity verification is a status-structured resource, not an
> interpersonal nicety**: the same affirming act stabilizes the self only when it descends the status
> hierarchy, which relocates a classic identity-theory process inside the social structure that rations it.
> *(contribution stated against a specific prior view — verification as a generic dyadic process.)* The
> argument is **scope-conditioned** to settings with a legible status order and publicly claimed role
> identities; we do not claim it for diffuse identities or flat peer groups. *(scope conditions, per
> `spq-theory-building`.)* Beyond promotion, the result implies that **wherever the self depends on others'
> confirmation, who is structurally positioned to confirm it becomes a mechanism of durable
> inequality** — those near the top of a status order can stabilize newcomers' selves, or withhold that
> stabilization, at low cost to themselves. *(broad lesson that travels, while staying social-psychological.)*
>
> The paper proceeds as follows. The next section develops the verification-by-status argument and its
> observable implications; we then describe the survey and vignette design, report the results, and close
> with scope conditions and implications for the stratification of the self. *(brief roadmap — no
> over-signposting.)*

## Why the "after" meets the SPQ bar

Mapped to the skill checklists:

- **Structure–individual link stated by the end of the intro** — the self (work identity) is the outcome,
  and *social structure* (the status order that rations verification) is the cause, not a control
  (`spq-writing-style`: front-load the structure–individual link; `spq-topic-selection` fit test #1).
- **Named social-psychological mechanism** — *identity verification*, status-patterned — not a bare
  correlation (`spq-theory-building`: "a finding with no named mechanism" is an anti-pattern).
- **Tradition located and bridged** — identity theory at its junction with status/expectation states; a
  reader from psychology can follow because *identity verification* and *status* are defined on first use
  (`spq-writing-style`: speak across the two parent disciplines).
- **Reads across sociology and psychology** — argument-first prose; the claim leads, evidence supports
  (`spq-writing-style`: argument-first).
- **Design adjudicates the leading rival** — the vignette randomizes the *status of the verifier*, ruling
  out "confident people draw praise," which is the adjudication sentence
  ([`spq-research-design`](../../skills/spq-research-design/SKILL.md): the adjudication test).
- **Scope conditions stated** — legible status order, publicly claimed role identities; not claimed for
  flat peer groups (`spq-theory-building`: universal claims with no scope conditions are an anti-pattern).
- **Not too psychological, not too macro** — it is neither a pure individual-cognition finding (JPSP /
  Psychological Science) nor a macro stratification pattern with no individual mechanism (ASR / AJS); the
  link *is* the contribution (`spq-topic-selection` fit verdict).
- **Format discipline applies downstream** — the companion abstract must be **~150 words, non-identifying,
  with keywords**, and the manuscript blinded with a separate title page (`spq-writing-style`; venue facts
  in [`../official-source-map.md`](../official-source-map.md)).
