edition's Call for Papers (long and short), the EasyChair instance named in it, and
the conference site before giving any deadline-sensitive advice.

## Verified facts used in the skills

- WSDM is an annual, traditionally single-track, highly selective ACM conference on
  web search and data mining, pronounced "wisdom," jointly sponsored by SIGIR,
  SIGKDD, SIGMOD, and SIGWEB (sponsor line of WSDM '25), publishing archival
  proceedings in the ACM Digital Library.
- The conference meets in late winter (February/March) with submission deadlines the
  previous August: WSDM 2026 abstracts August 7, full papers August 14, 2025, both
  23:59 AoE, via EasyChair; notification was listed as October 23, 2025.
- WSDM 2026 (19th edition) took place February 22-26, 2026 in Boise, Idaho, USA.
  WSDM 2025 (18th) was March 10-14, 2025 in Hannover, Germany. WSDM 2027 (20th) is
  scheduled for February 15-19, 2027 in Hong Kong.
- The 2026 long-paper budget was 9 pages **including appendices**, with unrestricted
  space only for references and the required ethical-considerations section; the
  2025 budget was 8 pages plus up to 2 extra pages shared by references and the
  ethics discussion. WSDM's page budget is historically tight and appendices are
  inside it - the exact numbers move every edition.
- Submissions use the ACM `acmart` class (`sigconf`, `anonymous`, `review` options
  per the 2025 CFP) and must be anonymized. Review is double-blind at the PC/SPC
  level while Associate Chairs can see authorship metadata to check conflicts -
  the CFP's own phrase is "a combination of single-blind reviewing and double-blind
  reviewing."
- Each manuscript receives at least three PC reviews plus a senior PC member's
  assessment; WSDM historically runs **no author rebuttal phase** (documented in the
  2017 reviewing experiment), so the submitted PDF is the authors' entire case.
- A section on ethical implications of the research (societal impact, privacy,
  fairness, misuse, mitigations) was required in 2025 and 2026 and does not count
  against the page limit.
- WSDM 2026 introduced a short-paper track (first in the series) with September 2025
  deadlines, a 10-submission-per-author cap, and desk rejection of placeholder
  abstracts; demonstration submissions were capped at 4 pages inclusive of
  everything.
- WSDM 2025 accepted more than 100 of over 600 submissions (~16-17%); PC chairs were
  Meeyoung Cha, Marie-Francine Moens, and Marc Najork. Like every conference in this
  collection, WSDM has no standing editor-in-chief - general and program chairs
  rotate per edition.
- The WSDM 2017 single-blind/double-blind experiment (Tomkins, Zhang & Heavlin,
  PNAS 2017) is the venue's documented rationale for strict anonymization.
- Verified exemplar papers (see `exemplars/library.md`): Craswell et al. WSDM 2008;
  Yang & Leskovec WSDM 2013; Joachims et al. WSDM 2017; Tang & Wang WSDM 2018;
  Chen et al. WSDM 2019 - each pinned to an ACM DL DOI and dblp record.
