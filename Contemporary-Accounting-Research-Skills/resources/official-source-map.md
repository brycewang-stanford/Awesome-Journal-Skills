## VERIFIED facts injected into the skills (with source #)

- **Sponsor / publisher:** owned and sponsored by the Canadian Academic Accounting Association (CAAA / l'Association canadienne des professeurs de comptabilité); published by Wiley Periodicals Inc. under a publishing partnership since 2010; premier CAAA research journal; published quarterly (Mar/Jun/Sep/Dec). (#1, #7)
- **Scope (method-agnostic):** CAR welcomes "interesting and intellectually rigorous work in all topic areas of accounting, using any appropriate method, and based in any discipline or research tradition" — deliberately broad-tent relative to peers. (#3)
- **Dominant methodology:** no single mandated method; the published mix is dominated by quantitative archival/capital-markets empirical work, while CAR is one of the most receptive top-tier accounting journals to experimental, analytical/modeling, field, and qualitative research; the Data Integrity policy is framed around empirical research (archival, experimental, field studies, surveys, and simulations). (#3, #5)
- **Editor-in-Chief:** Anup Srivastava (University of Calgary, Haskayne School of Business) — **re-verified current on 2026-06-22** via the UCalgary faculty profile, the CAAA CAR editorial-team page, and the 2026 CAR Conference call (Srivastava signs as EIC); Deputy Editors-in-Chief Elizabeth Demers (Waterloo), Alexander Edwards (Toronto), Hervé Stolowy (HEC Paris); Consulting Editors Partha Mohanram, Linda Myers, Khim Kelly; ~24 subject Editors; Managing Editor Kelley Kissner. EIC reviews/approves all acceptance decisions. Re-verify the masthead before submission. (#4, #3)
- **Submission system:** Editorial Manager (EM) at editorialmanager.com/car. New submissions require separate files: title page, blind original manuscript, author declaration form, ethics-approval verification for research with human participants, and proof of payment; optional cover letter, research instrument, related article(s). Data Integrity items 1–3 collected in EM at submission. (#6, #2, #5)
- **Submission fee:** CA/US $250 for CAAA members, CA/US $600 for non-members (Canadian funds in Canada, US funds elsewhere); Visa or Mastercard; receipt uploaded with submission. Fee waivers (e.g., earned by prompt reviewing) uploaded as proof of payment. Invited revisions pay no further fee. Desk-rejected manuscripts on/after Apr 1, 2021 receive a 50% refund; otherwise non-refundable; no refund where a waiver was used. (#2)
- **Length:** overall page limit of 50 pages, with a maximum of 30 pages of main text (12-pt, double-spaced, 8.5"×11", 1-inch margins); the limit excludes title page, abstract, and references; papers (especially qualitative) may modestly exceed the 30-page main-text guideline if the 50-page overall limit holds, with overflow in an online-only supplementary appendix. Prepared in Microsoft Word or LaTeX; submitted in Word or PDF. (#2)
- **Abstract:** not more than 300 words on an abstract page that begins the manuscript and includes the paper's title (research question and importance, predictions if applicable, method(s), principal findings and key implications), followed by up to six keywords. (#2)
- **Bilingual abstracts:** the CAAA translates every CAR abstract into French; abstracts appear in the journal in both English and French (Canadian bilingual mandate). (#1, #2)
- **Review model:** anonymous (double-anonymous / double-blind) peer review; the blind manuscript file carries no author-identifying information, with a separate title page; the Editor normally assigns two reviewers who provide reports and recommendations; the assigned subject Editor makes the disposition and the EIC reviews all acceptance decisions; reviewers report conflicts of interest and are discouraged from identifying authors. (#3, #2)
- **Data Integrity and Code Sharing Policy (effective May 1, 2020):** applies to all new empirical submissions (archival, experimental, field studies, surveys, simulations). Initial submission (in EM): (1) brief description of how data were acquired/produced and their sources, plus the full research instrument for surveys/experiments; (2) for proprietary organizational data, agreement to provide a credible means of verifying the source/site on editor request and disclosure of any non-disclosure restrictions; (3) disclosure of which authors had data access and ran the analyses. Prior to acceptance: (4) a data availability statement including a repository link, with data cited in the reference list; (5) assurance (consistent with NSF guidelines) that data and code will be maintained by at least one author for at least six years. Final version: archive the program(s)/code that created the final dataset in a public repository, with usage explanation including variable definitions, observation-omission criteria (e.g., outliers), and modifications (winsorizing, truncating); proprietary code may be withheld if authors instead explain how others can reproduce the final dataset. Exception requests accompany the initial submission and are approved by the EIC only in exceptional circumstances. A title-page data availability statement is required if the paper uses data. (#5, #2)
- **Formatting / style:** 12-pt, double-spaced; in-text notes as footnotes, not endnotes; references begin on a new page after the text; each exhibit (appendix, table, figure) begins on a new page; pages numbered sequentially after the abstract page; the separate title page carries author names/affiliations, corresponding-author contact, acknowledgements, and the data availability statement; accepted papers conform to the CAR Style Guide; LaTeX authors may use Wiley's New Journal Design (NJD) template. (#2)
- **Ethics-approval verification:** for any research involving human participants (experiments, interviews, surveys, including secondary human-participant data), authors must upload institutional REB/IRB clearance, an REB-issued exemption, or a senior-administrator letter where no review board exists; a bare assertion is not accepted; failure is grounds for withdrawal by the EIC. (#2)
- **Author Declaration:** signed by all authors; includes an overlap-disclosure rule — if a submission shares substantial data, sites, materials, participants, or modeling analysis with any other paper (published or not), authors must disclose and discuss the overlap and provide a copy if the other work is not public. (#2)
- **Generative-AI rules (from Jan 1, 2024):** authors must declare any generative-AI use and describe it in Methods (grammar/reference tools exempt); reviewers are prohibited from using generative-AI tools in the review process. (#3)
- **CAR Conference:** a dedicated annual Contemporary Accounting Research Conference (40+ editions, supported by CPA Canada) feeds a stream of conference papers; accepted conference papers carry a special acknowledgement footnote. (#1, #2)
- **Author X/Twitter handles** collected at the final-version stage so CAR can tag authors when promoting forthcoming articles. (#2)
- **Appeals:** formal appeals process with a one-month waiting period, a six-month deadline, and a new submission fee required to appeal. (#3)
