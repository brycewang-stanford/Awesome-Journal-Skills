# *Journal of Economic Growth* Exemplars Library (method × topic)

> **Verified via web search, access date 2026-06-08.** Every paper below was checked to confirm it
> actually appeared in the **_Journal of Economic Growth_** (JEG) — the specialist Springer Nature
> (Kluwer/Springer) quarterly for growth and dynamic macroeconomics, **EiC Oded Galor**, founded 1996 —
> with year and volume/issue, against the journal's own Springer article pages
> (`link.springer.com/article/...`), the RePEc/IDEAS series record (`ideas.repec.org/a/kap/jecgro/...`),
> and corroborating Google Scholar lookups carrying the journal name. Papers that could not be fully
> verified **as _JEG_** were **omitted** — this is deliberately a short, clean list, not a long, uncertain
> one.
>
> **Sibling-journal guard.** *JEG* is **not** the *Quarterly Journal of Economics* (QJE), the *American
> Economic Review* (AER), the *Review of Economic Studies* (RES), or the *Journal of Development
> Economics*. Many of the most famous "growth" papers — colonial origins, genetic diversity, the
> diffusion of development — live in those general-interest venues, **not** in JEG. The
> [Omitted](#omitted-for-lack-of-full-verification-do-not-attribute-to-jeg) section records the traps
> checked and rejected.
>
> **Use principle (zero hallucination):** this file gives **design positioning only**. It does not
> reproduce or invent coefficients, elasticities, or specific findings — read the original on Springer /
> RePEc before citing any number. For JEG-specific scope, fees, and house style (APA author-year, 150–250
> word abstract, JEL codes, Springer declarations), see [`../official-source-map.md`](../official-source-map.md).

## By method

### Cross-country level regression with an instrument that adjudicates rival channels (institutions vs. geography)

- **Rodrik, Subramanian & Trebbi, "Institutions Rule: The Primacy of Institutions Over Geography and Integration in Economic Development," _Journal of Economic Growth_ 2004, 9(2):131–165.**
  Verified: Springer `link.springer.com/article/10.1023/B:JOEG.0000031425.72248.85`; RePEc `kap/jecgro/v9y2004i2p131-165`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** horse-races three candidate deep determinants of income — institutions,
    geography, integration — in one estimating equation, instrumenting institutions and trade so the
    coefficients can be read as *which channel dominates*. The
    [`jeg-identification-strategy`](../../skills/jeg-identification-strategy/SKILL.md) move of building the
    research design *around* the rival explanation rather than controlling it away.
  - **Self-check:** does your cross-country specification let the reader see which deep determinant wins,
    with an instrument whose exclusion restriction you defend — not just a significant coefficient on your
    favourite regressor?

### Dynamic panel of growth rates with a nonlinear / heterogeneous relationship (inequality & growth)

- **Barro, "Inequality and Growth in a Panel of Countries," _Journal of Economic Growth_ 2000, 5(1):5–32.**
  Verified: Springer `link.springer.com/article/10.1023/A:1009850119329`; RePEc `kap/jecgro/v5y2000i1p5-32`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** estimates the inequality–growth relation in a multi-period country panel and
    finds it *changes sign with development level* rather than being a single number — a heterogeneity
    result framed as the contribution, not a nuisance. The
    [`jeg-data-analysis`](../../skills/jeg-data-analysis/SKILL.md) discipline of panel growth regressions
    plus explicit interaction structure.
  - **Self-check:** is your headline a *conditional* growth relationship (who, where, when it holds) rather
    than an average that averages away the phenomenon?

### Construction and validation of a new cross-country measure (fractionalization)

- **Alesina, Devleeschauwer, Easterly, Kurlat & Wacziarg, "Fractionalization," _Journal of Economic Growth_ 2003, 8(2):155–194.**
  Verified: Springer `link.springer.com/article/10.1023/A:1024471506938`; RePEc `kap/jecgro/v8y2003i2p155-94`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** delivers new ethnic, linguistic, and religious fractionalization indices for
    ~190 countries, *separating dimensions earlier work conflated*, then re-tests their effects on
    institutions and growth. A measurement-contribution paper —
    [`jeg-contribution-framing`](../../skills/jeg-contribution-framing/SKILL.md): "the first comprehensive
    measure that distinguishes X from Y," with the data as the public good.
  - **Self-check:** does your new measure *distinguish* components prior single indices ran together, and do
    you show the distinction changes a substantive growth conclusion?

### Quantitative unified-growth theory disciplined against the historical transition (fertility decline)

- **Doepke, "Accounting for Fertility Decline During the Transition to Growth," _Journal of Economic Growth_ 2004, 9(3):347–383.**
  Verified: RePEc `kap/jecgro/v9y2004i3p347-383`; Springer `link.springer.com/article/...` (DOI on the JEG article page).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** builds a calibrated growth model with endogenous fertility and education and
    asks *which mechanism* (mortality decline, education subsidies, child-labour restrictions) can quantitatively
    reproduce the observed demographic transition — theory whose "identification" is its quantitative match
    to history, the JEG theory-paper standard in
    [`jeg-identification-strategy`](../../skills/jeg-identification-strategy/SKILL.md).
  - **Self-check:** if you write a growth model, do you discipline it against a *quantitative target* and rule
    competing mechanisms in or out, rather than only proving an existence result?

### Long-run panel documenting a worldwide transition and its determinants (democracy & growth)

- **Murtin & Wacziarg, "The Democratic Transition," _Journal of Economic Growth_ 2014, 19(2):141–181.**
  Verified: Springer `link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10887-013-9100-6`; RePEc `kap/jecgro/v19y2014i2p141-181`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** assembles two centuries of cross-country data to characterize the long-run
    movement toward democracy and test income vs. education as drivers — a description-plus-determinants
    contribution pitched at the deep-determinants-of-development audience JEG serves
    ([`jeg-topic-selection`](../../skills/jeg-topic-selection/SKILL.md): a first-order, long-horizon growth
    question, not a short-panel policy evaluation).
  - **Self-check:** is your question a *first-order, long-horizon* growth/development question that a JEG
    reader sees as central, rather than a narrow applied-micro evaluation better suited to a field journal?

## Omitted for lack of full verification (do not attribute to _JEG_)

To keep the list zero-hallucination, these famous "growth" papers were **excluded** after web-checking —
each is real and important, but **not in _JEG_** (the sibling-journal traps the brief warned of):

- **Acemoglu, Johnson & Robinson, "The Colonial Origins of Comparative Development" (2001)** — verified in
  the **_American Economic Review_** 91(5):1369–1401, **not _JEG_**. The settler-mortality-IV classic.
- **Spolaore & Wacziarg, "The Diffusion of Development" (2009)** — verified in the **_Quarterly Journal of
  Economics_** 124(2):469–529, **not _JEG_**.
- **Nunn & Wantchekon, "The Slave Trade and the Origins of Mistrust in Africa" (2011)** — verified in the
  **_American Economic Review_** 101(7):3221–3252, **not _JEG_**.
- **Galor & Moav, "Natural Selection and the Origin of Economic Growth" (2002)** — verified in the
  **_Quarterly Journal of Economics_** 117(4):1133–1191, **not _JEG_**. (Unified-growth ideas central to the
  JEG community, but the paper itself is QJE.)
- **Ashraf & Galor, "Dynamics and Stagnation in the Malthusian Epoch" (2011)** — verified in the **_American
  Economic Review_** 101(5):2003–2041, **not _JEG_**. (Their genetic-diversity paper is also AER, 2013.)

Before adding any new paper to this library, confirm it on `link.springer.com/journal/10887` (or the
RePEc `kap/jecgro` series record) with volume/issue, and update the access-date header. When in doubt,
**omit**.
