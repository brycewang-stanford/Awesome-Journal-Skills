# Official Source Map - ISSTA

Access date: 2026-07-09

This map records the official and archival sources behind every ISSTA-specific fact in this
pack. ISSTA policies are cycle-specific and the submission model in particular has changed
between editions; reopen the current Research Papers call, important-dates page, artifact
call, and ACM proceedings instructions before giving submission-ready advice.

## Access-method note

Direct `conf.researchr.org` fetches were frequently blocked by the site gateway in the
verification environment. Where that happened, the exact official URL was read through a
search-engine rendering of that page and then cross-checked against a second surface — the
ISSTA HotCRP site, the ACM Digital Library proceedings volume, or dblp. Facts confirmed on
only one surface, or not confirmable at all on 2026-07-09, are marked **待核实** rather than
asserted.

## Verified 2026 facts used in skills

- ISSTA 2026 is the 35th ACM SIGSOFT International Symposium on Software Testing and Analysis,
  co-located with SPLASH as SPLASH/ISSTA 2026, in Oakland, California, USA, October 3-9, 2026.
- Submission is through HotCRP (`issta2026.hotcrp.com`); proceedings appear in the ACM Digital
  Library, not in PMLR.
- The 2026 research-paper deadline was January 29, 2026 (AoE), with author response March 24-26,
  initial notification April 16, a major-revision deadline of May 21, and final notification
  June 25, 2026.
- First-round outcomes are Accept, Major Revision, or Reject; Major-Revision papers resubmit
  against the later deadline for a terminal decision. Earlier editions (e.g. ISSTA 2023, 2024)
  ran two rolling submission deadlines where first-deadline papers could receive a major revision
  to the second deadline — the multi-round model is real ISSTA history but its exact shape is
  cycle-specific.
- ISSTA 2026 invited three submission kinds: Research Papers, Experience Papers, and Replicability
  Studies.
- Reviewing is double-anonymous; each paper gets at least three PC reviews, with more solicited
  on expertise or reviewer disagreement.
- Submissions are limited to 18 pages excluding references — every section, figure, and appendix
  counts against the 18. Exceeding it, or tampering with the ACM `sigconf` format, is a
  desk-reject trigger. (Older editions used 10 pages plus 2 for references.)
- Evaluation criteria named by the call: originality, importance of contribution, soundness,
  evaluation, quality of presentation, appropriate comparison to related work, and
  verifiability/transparency.
- Artifact evaluation awards ACM badges — Artifacts Available (Zenodo deposit required),
  Artifacts Evaluated Functional, Artifacts Evaluated Reusable, and Results Reproduced.

## Organizers and fee model

- ISSTA is a conference series, run by ACM SIGSOFT. Like every conference in this collection it has
  NO standing editor-in-chief; the rotating analogue is the per-edition General Chair and Program
  Chairs, appointed by SIGSOFT/the steering committee and turning over each year. Re-check the
  current organization page before relying on any name.
- ISSTA 2027 is announced for Singapore (organizers reported as Jun Sun, Dan Hao, and Jacques
  Klein); its dates and deadlines are **待核实** on 2026-07-09.
- There is NO article-processing charge (APC). ISSTA funds itself through registration; at least
  one author registers and presents. Accepted papers are published by ACM in the ACM Digital
  Library under the standard ACM proceedings model (open-access status depends on ACM OPEN and
  any author election — confirm per paper).
