# External Tools - ISSTA

Access date: 2026-07-09

Open these surfaces after reopening the current ISSTA cycle pages. ISSTA rules — above all the
submission model — change by edition, so treat every entry below as a pointer to re-verify, not
as a fact to reuse.

## Author-side verification passes

Run these before committing to a plan; start with the submission-model check because it is the
part most likely to have changed since the previous edition.

- **Submission model.** Confirm how many deadlines exist this cycle, whether outcomes include
  Major Revision, and the exact revision deadline. Do not assume the single-deadline 2026 shape.
- **Format.** Confirm the ACM `sigconf` template, the `review` and `anonymous` options, and the
  current page limit (18 excluding references in 2026), then export and inspect the PDF.
- **Double-anonymous integrity.** Author names and affiliations removed; self-citations phrased in
  the third person; repository links anonymized or withheld; no identifying PDF metadata.
- **Artifact intent.** Decide which ACM badges you will pursue and whether a Zenodo deposit is
  required this cycle; the artifact call is separate from the paper call and has its own deadline.
- **Evidence.** Subject programs and benchmark versions pinned; tool baselines fairly configured;
  statistical comparison chosen (non-parametric test plus effect size) before the numbers are run.

## Cross-check surfaces (when the primary page is gateway-blocked)

- ACM Digital Library: verify proceedings edition number, page ranges, and publication dates.
- dblp (`dblp.org/db/conf/issta`): verify that a paper is an ISSTA research-track paper, not a
  co-located workshop or a sibling-venue paper.
- IEEE Xplore: cross-check related work that lives at ICST, ICSE, or ASE rather than ISSTA.

## Do not infer

- Do not infer any ISSTA 2027 date, deadline count, or venue detail from the 2026 cycle.
- Do not assume the page limit, badge set, Zenodo requirement, or criteria wording are stable.
- If two official surfaces disagree, treat the newest Research Papers call or a direct chair
  communication as controlling, and record the conflict rather than silently choosing one.
