# External Tools - USENIX FAST

Access date: 2026-07-09

Use these surfaces after reopening the current FAST cycle pages. FAST mechanics — number and dates
of deadlines, page limits, one-shot-revision and shepherding rules, artifact badges — are decided
per edition, and the `usenix.org`/`dl.acm.org` gateway may block direct fetches (read via search
renderings and cross-check dblp and the USENIX proceedings mirror). Do not confuse this venue with
same-named events in other communities; this is the **USENIX** File and Storage Technologies
conference only.

- FAST conference series hub: https://www.usenix.org/conferences/byname/146
- FAST '26 home (Santa Clara, Feb 24-26, 2026): https://www.usenix.org/conference/fast26
- FAST '26 Call for Papers: https://www.usenix.org/conference/fast26/call-for-papers
- FAST '26 double-blind guidance: https://www.usenix.org/conference/fast26/double-blind-guidance
- FAST '27 Call for Papers (next live target): https://www.usenix.org/conference/fast27/call-for-papers

## Cross-check surfaces (when the gateway blocks the primary page)

- dblp FAST stream: https://dblp.org/db/conf/fast/index.html (per-year tables of contents for
  exemplar verification and title/author/year confirmation).
- ACM Digital Library mirror of the USENIX FAST proceedings (for DOIs and page numbers).
- The FAST HotCRP `deadlines` page for the machine-readable cutoff the CFP paraphrases (each
  deadline has its own HotCRP instance).
- SNIA IOTTA trace repository for the standard block/object traces a storage evaluation cites.

## Author-side checks

- HotCRP profile, conflicts, coauthor emails, topic tags, and — critically — **which of the two
  deadlines** (Spring or Fall) you are targeting; each has a separate HotCRP site and cutoff.
- USENIX two-column template compliance, the current page limit (FAST '27: 12 pages long / 6 short,
  **excluding references**), and PDF export at 10-point on 12-point leading.
- Double-blind sweep: anonymize the PDF and references, write about your own prior work in the
  third person, and scrub identity from any linked artifact, tool name, or trace-hosting URL.
- Artifact-availability story: where the code, traces, and scripts will live, and which USENIX
  badges (Available / Functional / Reproduced) you will pursue on the separate post-acceptance
  timeline.
- Real-device provenance: exact drive/SSD/NVM models, firmware versions, host and kernel, and
  trace identifiers, recorded at measurement time.

## Do not infer

- Do not infer the number or dates of deadlines: FAST runs two per year (Spring and Fall) with
  distinct cutoffs and HotCRP sites. Confirm both on the current CFP.
- Do not assume the page limit, template revision, one-shot-revision mechanics, artifact badges, or
  open-science / AI-disclosure wording carry over between editions.
- Do not assume a one-shot revision is a soft accept: its instructions can require specific new
  experiments, and the resubmission can be rejected. Budget it as a second full deadline.
- If pages disagree, treat the newest Call for Papers, the HotCRP deadline page, or a direct chair
  announcement as controlling, and record the conflict.
