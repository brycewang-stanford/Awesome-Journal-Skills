# Navigating JIM Review (jim-review-process)

## When to trigger

- The paper was just submitted and the team wants to know what happens next
- Months have passed with no word and someone wants to nudge the editor
- The group is estimating revision timelines for planning (tenure clocks, dissertations)
- Pre-submission, to engineer around JIM's known desk-reject triggers

## The pipeline

1. **Administrative screen.** ScholarOne staff/editorial check: file split (title page vs. anonymized main document), 50-page cap, 200-word abstract, formatting. Failures bounce back without review — annoying but recoverable.
2. **Editor screen.** The Editor-in-Chief (Ayşegül Özsomer since 2024 — verify the masthead) reads for scope and bar. A meaningful share of submissions end here; see the desk-reject list below.
3. **AE and reviewers.** Surviving papers go to an associate/area editor and typically two to three double-anonymized reviewers drawn from the international marketing community — expect at least one measurement-focused reader on any multi-country survey, and reviewers located in or expert on your sampled regions.
4. **Decision.** Reject; reject-and-resubmit (a real door at AMA journals — a new submission with a fresh review team is being invited); major revision; minor revision; conditional accept. First rounds at specialist AMA journals commonly take roughly three to five months — treat any figure as folklore until confirmed on current data; the office responds to polite status queries after ~4 months of silence.

## Desk-reject triggers specific to JIM

- **Single-country data, no cross-national theory.** The setting is foreign; the theory is not international. The most common trigger by reputation.
- **Culture as epilogue.** Country differences found, then explained post hoc with a cultural dimension never hypothesized.
- **No equivalence evidence.** Cross-country scale comparisons with no invariance testing or translation protocol.
- **Domestic replication abroad.** "X, established in the U.S., holds in country Y" with no boundary-condition theorizing.
- **Wrong journal.** An IB/strategy paper (FDI, governance) without a marketing mechanism, or a general marketing paper where the international element is a robustness footnote.
- **Managerial silence.** No implication a border-crossing firm could act on.

If any of these describes the paper, do not submit hoping for reviewer mercy — route back to `jim-topic-selection` or `jim-methods`.

## What JIM reviewers reward

Reviewer-proofing is cheaper than rebutting. Papers that fare well arrive with: a pre-committed invariance table; a country-selection rationale on the focal dimension; formal tests of every claimed country difference; implications framed as market profiles; and honest scope statements ("our two-country contrast cannot separate income from individualism; we therefore…"). Pre-empting the alternative explanation a regional expert will raise — response styles, sampling differences, a local market shock during fieldwork — signals craft.

## Waiting productively

- Log the manuscript ID, submission date, and status changes; ScholarOne statuses ("under review," "awaiting AE recommendation") are informative.
- Draft the anticipated-objections memo now: the five hardest questions and your best answers, with analyses pre-run. It becomes the rebuttal skeleton.
- Consider a **JIM paper development workshop** (the journal runs them periodically at AMS/AMA conferences) for the *next* project — they are fit-calibration gold.
- Do not submit the paper elsewhere in parallel; AMA journals enforce exclusivity.
- Watch for special-issue calls (recent examples: global sustainability; AI in international marketing) — a well-matched special issue changes the reviewer pool and the fit conversation for follow-on work.

## Decision-letter triage preview

Reject with signal (reviewers engaged the theory) → fix and target IMR/JWB or rebuild for a new JIM submission if invited. Reject-and-resubmit → treat as a major revision with the freedom (and risk) of a fresh team. Major revision → the modal good outcome; expect measurement and identification demands, possibly a new country or study. Minor revision → execute precisely, no scope creep. Full playbook: `jim-rebuttal`.

## Checklist

- [ ] Package engineered against all six desk-reject triggers before submission
- [ ] Manuscript ID and dates logged; status meanings understood
- [ ] Anticipated-objections memo drafted while waiting
- [ ] No concurrent submission anywhere
- [ ] Timeline expectations set from current information, not folklore
- [ ] Masthead and process facts re-checked on ama.org (they rotate)

## Anti-patterns

- Nudging the editor at week six of a normal cycle
- Reading "under review" tea leaves daily instead of writing the objections memo
- Treating reject-and-resubmit as a rejection (it is an invitation) or as an acceptance (it is not)
- Arguing scope with a desk rejection instead of fixing the international-theory gap it named
- Planning a tenure case around a single JIM round going perfectly
