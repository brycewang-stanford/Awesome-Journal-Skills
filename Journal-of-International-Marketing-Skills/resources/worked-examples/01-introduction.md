# Worked Example: A JIM-Shaped Introduction

Fictional paper, invented numbers — the point is the *architecture*. The example follows the structure taught in `jim-writing-style` and the framing bar of `jim-topic-selection` / `jim-contribution-framing`.

## The draft that gets desk-rejected

> Globalization has dramatically increased the importance of international markets. Influencer marketing is a growing phenomenon that has received much attention. However, no study has examined influencer marketing in Southeast Asia. Using survey data from Thailand, we show that influencer credibility increases purchase intention. We contribute by extending influencer research to a new context.

Why it dies at the editor screen: the opener is boilerplate; the gap is geographic ("not yet studied in Y"), not theoretical; one country means no cross-national comparison and nothing for the border-crossing manager; the "contribution" concedes it is a context extension — JIM's canonical desk-reject.

## The JIM-shaped rewrite (annotated)

> **[¶1 — the cross-border tension, concretely]** When a global beauty brand ran identical influencer campaigns in Germany and Indonesia, the same creative that lifted sales 14% in Jakarta moved nothing in Munich. Practitioner evidence of such reversals is mounting, and the academic record mirrors it: influencer-credibility effects reported in U.S. samples are two to three times the size of those in Northern European ones.
>
> *Annotation:* opens with a decision-relevant cross-border puzzle plus a documented cross-national inconsistency — the two legitimate JIM entry points, in one paragraph.
>
> **[¶2 — why existing answers fail across borders]** Influencer research treats credibility as a universal persuasion mechanism, yet its microfoundation — trust in a stranger's judgment — is exactly what institutional and cultural environments condition. Where consumer-protection institutions are weak, we argue, parasocial trust *substitutes* for institutional trust, amplifying influencer effects; where institutions are strong, it is redundant. Existing single-country studies cannot see this because the substitute has no within-country variation.
>
> *Annotation:* the mechanism (trust substitution) is theorized a priori and tied to a named country-level dimension (institutional strength) — culture/institutions as engine, not epilogue. The last sentence states why the question is *inherently* cross-national: the relabel test passes.
>
> **[¶3 — question, design, answer]** We test this substitution account with matched consumer panels in eight countries spanning the World Bank rule-of-law range, with full metric and partial scalar invariance established for all focal scales. Influencer-credibility effects on purchase intention are 2.4 times larger in the weakest-institution tercile, and the gradient tracks institutional quality — not income, individualism, or social-media penetration.
>
> *Annotation:* countries as theory-chosen levels; invariance evidence announced in the introduction (a JIM fluency signal); the answer disclosed, with rival country-level explanations already named and separated.
>
> **[¶4 — contributions, ledger-style]** Theoretically, we redraw the boundary of the credibility mechanism: it is not universal but institutionally contingent, which reconciles the divergent published effect sizes. Managerially, we give global brand managers an allocation rule — shift influencer budget share toward markets in the weak-institution profile, where our estimates imply roughly a third higher return per exposure — stated in market-profile terms that extend beyond the eight sampled countries.
>
> *Annotation:* one theory row (boundary redrawn + inconsistency reconciled) and one manager row (actor, action, magnitude, market-profile condition) — the double gate of `jim-contribution-framing`.

## Transfer checklist

- Open with a border-crossing decision or a cross-national inconsistency — never "globalization has increased…"
- Name the country-level dimension and the mechanism *before* the data appear
- State why single-country designs cannot answer the question (the relabel test, made explicit)
- Disclose the answer and the invariance status in the introduction
- Write contributions as one theory delta + one manager rule with profile-based scope
