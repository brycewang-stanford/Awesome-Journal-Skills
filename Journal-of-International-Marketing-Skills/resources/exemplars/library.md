# JIM Exemplar Library — International Marketing Canon

Well-known, real papers that JIM authors and reviewers treat as shared vocabulary. Venues are labeled honestly — the canon spans JIM and its neighbors. **Verify each citation against the publisher's DOI page before citing in a manuscript** (details below are kept deliberately coarse where memory could err). Compiled 2026-07-16.

## Methodological spine

1. **Steenkamp, Jan-Benedict E. M., and Hans Baumgartner (1998), "Assessing Measurement Invariance in Cross-National Consumer Research," *Journal of Consumer Research*, 25 (1), 78–90.** The invariance protocol (configural → metric → scalar, partial invariance) that JIM reviewers expect any multi-country scale comparison to follow.
2. **Hult, G. Tomas M., et al. (2008), "Data Equivalence in Cross-Cultural International Business Research: Assessment and Guidelines," *Journal of International Business Studies*, 39 (6).** The broader equivalence checklist — construct, translation, sampling — cited alongside Steenkamp–Baumgartner.
3. **Steenkamp, Jan-Benedict E. M. (2001), "The Role of National Culture in International Marketing Research," *International Marketing Review*, 18 (1).** How to use culture as a theoretical variable rather than a label.

## Export marketing and international strategy

4. **Cavusgil, S. Tamer, and Shaoming Zou (1994), "Marketing Strategy–Performance Relationship: An Investigation of the Empirical Link in Export Market Ventures," *Journal of Marketing*, 58 (1).** The founding empirical template of the export strategy–performance stream (Cavusgil also founded JIM itself in 1993).
5. **Zou, Shaoming, and S. Tamer Cavusgil (2002), "The GMS: A Broad Conceptualization of Global Marketing Strategy and Its Effect on Firm Performance," *Journal of Marketing*, 66 (4).** The standardization–adaptation debate consolidated into a testable framework.
6. **Katsikeas, Constantine S., Leonidas C. Leonidou, and Neil A. Morgan (2000), "Firm-Level Export Performance Assessment: Review, Evaluation, and Development," *Journal of the Academy of Marketing Science*, 28 (4).** The discipline for measuring export performance — the dependent variable of half the stream.
7. **Morgan, Neil A., Constantine S. Katsikeas, and Douglas W. Vorhies (2012), "Export Marketing Strategy Implementation, Export Marketing Capabilities, and Export Venture Performance," *Journal of the Academy of Marketing Science*, 40 (2).** Capabilities-era extension of the export-performance tradition.
8. **Knight, Gary A., and S. Tamer Cavusgil (2004), "Innovation, Organizational Capabilities, and the Born-Global Firm," *Journal of International Business Studies*, 35 (2).** Anchor of international entrepreneurship / born-global research.

## Global branding and cross-cultural consumer behavior

9. **Batra, Rajeev, et al. (2000), "Effects of Brand Local and Nonlocal Origin on Consumer Attitudes in Developing Countries," *Journal of Consumer Psychology*, 9 (2).** Early anchor of the local–nonlocal brand-origin literature.
10. **Steenkamp, Jan-Benedict E. M., Rajeev Batra, and Dana L. Alden (2003), "How Perceived Brand Globalness Creates Brand Value," *Journal of International Business Studies*, 34 (1).** Perceived brand globalness — the construct at the center of the global-branding stream.
11. **Özsomer, Ayşegül (2012), "The Interplay Between Global and Local Brands: A Closer Look at Perceived Brand Globalness and Local Iconness," *Journal of International Marketing*, 20 (2).** A JIM-published treatment of the global–local tension — by the journal's current editor; useful for reading the house bar first-hand.
12. **Sheth, Jagdish N. (2011), "Impact of Emerging Markets on Marketing: Rethinking Existing Perspectives and Practices," *Journal of Marketing*, 75 (4).** The agenda paper behind the emerging-markets stream.

## How to use this library

- **Positioning:** anchor your stream with 2–3 of these plus recent JIM papers from the last five volumes (search the SAGE archive).
- **Methods defense:** citing #1–#3 signals you know the equivalence bar; *executing* them (see `jim-methods`, `jim-data-analysis`) clears it.
- **Guardrail:** venue accuracy matters — several canon papers live in JM/JIBS/JAMS, not JIM; misattributing them is a credibility tell reviewers notice.
