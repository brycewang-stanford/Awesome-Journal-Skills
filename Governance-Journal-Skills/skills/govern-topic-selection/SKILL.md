# Topic Selection & Fit (govern-topic-selection)

*Governance* is the leading journal of **executive politics, public policy, public administration, and
the organization of the state**, read comparatively and internationally (Wiley-Blackwell, in
association with IPSA RC27 — Structure & Organization of Government). The bar is not "new to one
country's administration" — it is **"changes how we understand governing across cases."** Use this
skill to pressure-test fit before you invest.

## When to trigger

- Choosing among possible projects or framings for a *Governance* submission
- A reviewer/colleague said the paper feels "too descriptive" or "single-country case study only"
- Unsure whether the topic is in scope vs. corporate governance or a literature review
- Deciding how to frame an empirical finding so it speaks to comparative governance

## The "is this a Governance question?" test

A strong *Governance* paper usually clears all four:

1. **It is about governing.** The object is executive politics, the policy process, public
   administration, bureaucratic/institutional reform, governance networks, accountability, regulation,
   or the organization of the state — not corporate boards or firm governance.
2. **It travels.** The mechanism or claim is portable across countries, levels of government, or policy
   domains. A single rich case is welcome **if** it is a case *of* something general (see
   `govern-theory-building` on concept formation).
3. **It is a contribution, not a description.** It revises a theory of the state/institutions/policy
   process, supplies a decisive comparative test, reconceptualizes a governance phenomenon, or corrects
   the record — not "this reform happened in country X."
4. **Credible on its own methodological terms.** Quantitative, qualitative, comparative-historical, or
   mixed — each is welcome and judged rigorously (see `govern-research-design`).

## Framing the contribution (reach past one case)

| If your material is… | Reach the comparative-institutional readership by… |
|----------------------|----------------------------------------------------|
| One country's reform | naming the general mechanism (delegation, blame avoidance, layering) and saying what else it should predict |
| A cross-national dataset | foregrounding the institutional logic, not just the coefficient; what does it teach about governing? |
| An agency / bureaucracy study | tying it to accountability, capacity, or executive control as general phenomena |
| A policy-process episode | connecting to agenda-setting, instrument choice, or policy feedback theory that others can use |
| A governance-network case | showing what it reveals about coordination/authority beyond the single network |

## Hard scope screen (route OUT if any is true)

- **Corporate governance** (firm boards, shareholders, corporate compliance) — hard out of scope.
- A **literature review / state-of-the-field essay** or a **pure bibliometric / citation-mapping** study
  — *Governance* typically does not publish these.
- A practitioner how-to with no theoretical contribution → likely **PAR**, not *Governance*.
- Pure regulatory-instrument design with no comparative/institutional reach → likely **Regulation & Governance**.

## Length and shape (检索于 2026-06；以官网为准)

Treat **≤ 9,000 words excluding citations/bibliography** as the working cap (a third-party listing
cites 3,000–5,000; treat the official 9,000 as binding, 待核实). Abstract **~150 words**. Scope the
question so it can be answered convincingly within that cap — a sprawling multi-country, multi-outcome
design rarely fits.

## Anti-patterns

- "This reform has not been studied in country X" as the whole contribution (descriptive, off-fit)
- Submitting a literature review or bibliometric map (out of scope)
- A study of corporate governance mistaking the journal's name
- A single case with no claim about what it is a case *of*
- Padding a sharp comparative point into a sprawling design that breaks the word cap

## Output format

```
【Question】one sentence
【Governing object】executive politics / policy process / PA / institutions / networks / the state
【Comparative reach】who across countries/domains cares, and why
【Contribution type】theory / decisive test / reconceptualization / corrected record
【Fit verdict】strong / needs reframing / off-fit (corporate gov / lit-review / sibling journal)
【Next】govern-literature-positioning
```
