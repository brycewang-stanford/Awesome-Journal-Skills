# External Tools - IPSN (and its SenSys successor)

Access date: 2026-07-09

Use these surfaces after reopening the current cycle. IPSN's mechanics — track structure, page
budget, artifact awards — were decided per edition, and IPSN itself **merged into SenSys (Embedded
AI and Sensing Systems)** from 2025-2026, so the *live* target is the successor SenSys call, not a
standalone IPSN page. The `ipsn.acm.org` / `dl.acm.org` / `dblp.org` / CPS-IoT Week gateway may
block direct fetches (read via search renderings and cross-check ACM DL, IEEE Xplore, and dblp). Do
not confuse this venue with the WHO *International Pathogen Surveillance Network* or an IEEE
IP-over-sensor-network workshop.

## Official workflow (historical IPSN + live successor)

- IPSN series archive (historical): https://ipsn.acm.org/
- IPSN 2024 home (last standalone; Hong Kong): https://ipsn.acm.org/2024/
- IPSN 2024 call for papers (IP + SPOTS, double-blind, ≤12 pp ACM): https://ipsn.acm.org/2024/cfp.html
- IPSN 2024 research HotCRP: https://ipsn2024.hotcrp.com/
- IPSN 2024 awards (Best Paper, Best Research Artifact): https://ipsn.acm.org/2024/awards.html
- **Successor — SenSys (Embedded AI and Sensing Systems):** https://sensys.acm.org/
- SenSys 2026 call for papers (merged venue): https://sensys.acm.org/2026/cfp.html
- SenSys 2027 call (next live target as it appears): https://sensys.acm.org/2027/cfp.html
- CPS-IoT Week 2026 (Saint Malo): https://cps-iot-week2026.inria.fr/
- CPS-IoT Week series hub: https://cps-iot-week.org/

## Cross-check surfaces (when the gateway blocks the primary page)

- ACM DL IPSN proceedings (per-year tables of contents) and IEEE Xplore IPSN records — the
  two-publisher footprint means a paper may be found under either.
- dblp IPSN stream: https://dblp.org/db/conf/ipsn/index.html (edition years, author lists, titles).
- The CPS-IoT Week program pages for co-location facts (RTAS/HSCC/ICCPS neighbors and dates).
- The SenSys HotCRP `deadlines` page for the machine-readable cutoff the CFP paraphrases.

## Author-side checks

- **Track choice first:** IP track (algorithms/estimation/signal-processing/inference/localization)
  vs SPOTS track (hardware/embedded-software/tools/deployment). In the successor venue, confirm
  whether the split survives or has become a single track with categories.
- HotCRP profile, conflicts, coauthor emails, topic tags, and any registration/abstract step that
  precedes the full-paper deadline.
- ACM Primary Article Template compliance, the current page budget (IPSN 2024: ≤12 pp inclusive;
  SenSys 2026: 12/6), and two-column 9 pt export.
- **Double-blind sweep for hardware papers:** anonymize the PDF, board photos, oscilloscope
  screenshots with lab watermarks, firmware repo owners, dataset/DOI links, and testbed/building
  names that reveal the institution.
- Dataset and firmware release plan; DOI-issuing archive (Zenodo / IEEE DataPort / figshare) for
  the artifact and the Best Research Artifact Award.
- Demo/poster track intent (historically a separate HotCRP; in the merged venue, demos/posters
  publish in the IEEE proceedings — verify).

## Do not infer

- **Do not assume IPSN still runs standalone.** It does not after 2024; the live venue is SenSys at
  CPS-IoT Week. Route accordingly.
- Do not assume the IP/SPOTS split, the ≤12-page budget, the double-blind rebuttal mechanics, the
  award set, or any AI-disclosure wording carry over between editions or into the successor.
- If pages disagree, treat the newest successor (SenSys) call, the HotCRP deadline page, or a direct
  chair announcement as controlling, and record the conflict.
