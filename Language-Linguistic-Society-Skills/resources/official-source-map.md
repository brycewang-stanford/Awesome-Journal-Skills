# Language (LSA) Official Source Map

Primary sources behind the *Language* facts used in this skill pack. **Checked: 2026-07-16.** Some CUP
and LSA pages may require browser verification; authors should live-check the official pages before
submitting or quoting operational details, because *Language* changed publisher in January 2026 and
several rules are still settling.

## Official Journal, Submission, and Author Pages

- **https://www.linguisticsociety.org/publications/language** — LSA's own page for *Language*. First
  route to the journal's identity, sections, and publisher status.
- **https://www.lsadc.org/language** — LSA (lsadc.org) journal page: continuous publication since 1925,
  four issues per year, the four online-only sections (since 2013), and current editorial contacts
  (`language@lsadc.org`).
- **https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/language** — *Language* on Cambridge Core. Verifies the
  **Cambridge University Press** publisher move (effective January 2026) and **open access from Volume
  102 (2026)**; use for current issues and the masthead.
- **https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/language/information/author-instructions/preparing-your-materials**
  — CUP author instructions ("Preparing your materials"). Verifies submission via **ScholarOne**, the
  **Overleaf template**, article types and word limits (General Research Article ≤18,000 words inclusive
  of notes/charts/tables/appendices but excluding references, with submissions over 12,000 words held to
  a more stringent standard; Review Article ≤5,000 words excluding references; Research Reports as a
  smaller targeted contribution), and **double-anonymous** review. *(This page returned an access wall
  to automated fetch on the check date — re-verify the exact current limits in a browser.)*
- **https://languagelsa.org/index.php/language/about/submissions** — the journal's editorial
  (OJS-hosted) submissions page: section definitions and the submission process.
- **https://languagelsa.org/index.php/language/Notes** — *Notes to Contributors* (editorial site).
- **https://www.linguisticsociety.org/lsa-publications/language/notes-to-contributors** — LSA-hosted
  Notes to Contributors; use for glossing, example, and manuscript-preparation expectations.

## House Style, Glossing, and Examples

- **https://www.lsadc.org/rc_files/5/Language_Journal_Style_Sheet.pdf** — the **Language Style Sheet**
  (LSA), the authoritative house citation and formatting standard for the journal.
- **https://www.linguisticsociety.org/resource/unified-style-sheet-linguistics** — the **Unified Style
  Sheet for Linguistics Journals** (approved by the LSA, January 7, 2007), on which the Language Style
  Sheet's author-date system is based. *(If this exact path 404s, search "Unified Style Sheet for
  Linguistics" on linguisticsociety.org — re-check.)*
- **https://www.eva.mpg.de/lingua/resources/glossing-rules.php** — the **Leipzig Glossing Rules**
  (Max Planck Institute), the standard for interlinear morpheme-by-morpheme glosses.

## Identity, History, and Publisher Context

- **https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/language** and **https://www.lsadc.org/language** — use
  together as the live source for the current editors (**Shelome Gooden** and **Michael Putnam** on the
  check date), publisher, and open-access terms; the editorship rotates, so re-confirm the masthead
  before naming an editor in a submission-facing document.

## Data, Code, and Supplementary Materials

- **CUP author instructions + LSA Notes to Contributors** — use the live pages to check the current
  data-availability and supplementary-materials policy. This pack treats documented, reproducible data
  and correctly sourced glosses as strong craft norms, while **not** asserting a specific editor-verified
  replication mandate unless the live pages state one. Respect consultant/community consent (see
  `lang-data-and-transparency`).

## Operational Live-Check Items (re-check on the official site)

- **Abstract length** — a short abstract is required; the exact word target (commonly cited around 150
  words) was **not firmly confirmed** on the check date — verify on the CUP author page.
- **Research Report word cap** — confirmed as "smaller/targeted" but the exact number needs a
  browser check.
- **Open-access APC / waivers / read-and-publish** for Volume 102+ — verify current terms.
- **Accepted file formats** (LaTeX/Overleaf vs. Word) on ScholarOne — verify.
- **Current masthead** (editors, associate editors, section editors) — verify.

Check the live CUP/LSA pages immediately before upload for word/abstract limits, accepted formats,
open-access terms, the current masthead, section mechanics (including Perspectives), and the current
data or supplementary-materials policy.
