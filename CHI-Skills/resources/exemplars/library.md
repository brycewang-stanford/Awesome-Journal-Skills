> Library record (title, authors, proceedings, DOI) via search renderings of the
> `dl.acm.org` pages, cross-checked with the authors' own copies where posted. CHI
> proceedings live in the ACM DL — open access for the full corpus since January

> Computing Systems."
>
> **Sibling-venue trap:** the ACM DL hosts CHI beside CSCW, UIST, DIS, IUI, and
> ASSETS, and "presented at CHI" sometimes colloquially covers Late-Breaking Work,
> workshops, or Extended Abstracts. Before citing a paper *as a CHI Papers-track
> exemplar*, confirm the DL record says CHI Conference *Proceedings* (not Extended
> Abstracts) for the right year.
>
> **Zero-hallucination rule:** this library is for positioning and craft study only.
> It states no result numbers beyond what the verified metadata and abstracts carry.
> Read the paper in the DL before citing anything from it. If you add a row you

## Verified exemplars

### Methodology — eliciting design knowledge from users

- **Wobbrock, Morris & Wilson, "User-Defined Gestures for Surface Computing,"
  CHI 2009, DOI 10.1145/1518701.1518866** (dl.acm.org record verified).
  - **Why an exemplar:** the gesture-elicitation method itself became the reusable
    contribution — participants produce the design vocabulary, the paper contributes
    the method and the resulting gesture set. A model for making a *methodology*
    claim carry an artifact-flavored study.
  - **Self-check:** if your contribution is a method, has anyone besides you run it
    end to end — and does the paper give them everything needed to?

### Understanding users — accessibility through appropriation

- **Pradhan, Mehta & Findlater, "'Accessibility Came by Accident': Use of
  Voice-Controlled Intelligent Personal Assistants by People with Disabilities,"
  CHI 2018, DOI 10.1145/3173574.3174033** (dl.acm.org record verified).
  - **Why an exemplar:** two modest studies (review content analysis + interviews
    with visually impaired users) yield a finding nobody designed for — mainstream
    voice assistants working as accessibility devices. Shows how *understanding
    users* claims scale with honest, situated evidence rather than sample size.
  - **Self-check:** does your empirical claim stay inside what your participants
    and setting can support, and still say something the field did not know?

### Design guidelines — synthesizing and validating knowledge

- **Amershi et al., "Guidelines for Human-AI Interaction," CHI 2019,
  DOI 10.1145/3290605.3300233** (dl.acm.org record verified).
  - **Why an exemplar:** guidelines distilled from literature and practice, then
    *validated* through systematic evaluation with design practitioners against
    real AI products — the validation is what turns opinion into a contribution.
    The canonical recent model for theory/guideline-type CHI papers.
  - **Self-check:** if you offer guidelines or implications, what evidence shows
    they are usable and discriminating, beyond your own assertion?

### Controlled experiment — human-AI decision making

- **Bansal et al., "Does the Whole Exceed its Parts? The Effect of AI Explanations
  on Complementary Team Performance," CHI 2021, DOI 10.1145/3411764.3445717**
  (dl.acm.org record verified).
  - **Why an exemplar:** a controlled, multi-dataset study built around a precise
    comparison (does explanation help the *team* beat both human and AI alone?),
    reporting a result that pushed against the field's optimism. A model for
    powered, preregistration-era experimental CHI work with a claim that survives
    its own scoping.
  - **Self-check:** is your experimental comparison the one your headline claim
    actually needs — and would a null still be publishable as evidence?

### Understanding practice — probing emerging technology use

- **Zamfirescu-Pereira, Wong, Hartmann & Yang, "Why Johnny Can't Prompt: How
  Non-AI Experts Try (and Fail) to Design LLM Prompts," CHI 2023,
  DOI 10.1145/3544548.3581388** (dl.acm.org record verified).
  - **Why an exemplar:** a design probe study of non-experts prompting LLMs,
    published at the exact moment the field needed the finding — evidence of how a
    fast-moving technology meets real users, gathered with a purpose-built probe.
    The model for studying AI-infused interaction while the ground is still moving.
  - **Self-check:** if your object of study is a moving technology, is the system
    version pinned and described well enough that the finding stays interpretable
    after the technology moves (`chi-reproducibility`)?

## Adding rows: the verification recipe

1. Locate the paper's `dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/...` record; quote title and authors
   from it, not from a citation manager or memory.
2. Confirm the containing proceedings line reads "Proceedings of the <year> CHI
   Conference on Human Factors in Computing Systems" — not Extended Abstracts, not
   a sibling conference, not adjunct proceedings.
3. Record the DOI and your access date beside the row.
4. State only what metadata and the abstract support; no award claims, no numbers,
   no venue folklore. If any step fails, the row enters as 待核实 with no authors
   named.
