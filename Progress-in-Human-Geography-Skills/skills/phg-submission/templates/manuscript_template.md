# PiHG Review-Essay Manuscript Template (skeleton)

A structural skeleton for a *Progress in Human Geography* **review article**. This is a **review essay**,
not an empirical paper — there is **no methods or results section**. The spine is a synthetic argument
about a literature. Delete the guidance notes before submitting. Keep the whole essay **within ~8,000 words
including endnotes** (excluding the bibliography); prepare it **double-anonymous** with a separate title
page. (检索于 2026-07-16；以官网为准.)

---

## [Title]

> Argument-forward and specific. Signal the intervention, not just the topic
> (e.g., "Toward a relational account of…"), not ("A review of…"). No author identifiers.

## Abstract

> **Unstructured, ~100 words.** State the debate, the intervention (what the field gets wrong), the
> organizing concept, and the forward agenda — not a topic description. See `phg-writing-style`.

**Keywords:** keyword one; keyword two; keyword three; keyword four; keyword five
> Minimum 5, specific to the topic.

---

## 1. Introduction

> Do the real work here (see `phg-argument-synthesis`, `phg-critical-intervention`,
> `phg-contribution-framing`, and the worked example). By the end the reader should know:
> - the **state of the debate** (a live, contested literature) — and "why now?"
> - **what the field keeps getting wrong** (the critical intervention)
> - the **organizing concept** that re-orders it
> - the **portable, forward-looking agenda** the essay delivers
> - a brief roadmap (no over-signposting)

## 2. [The organizing concept]

> Develop the load-bearing concept (`phg-theory-building`): import / extend / coin / genealogize. Name the
> relation or mechanism it captures and its scope conditions. This is theory doing synthetic work, not a
> literature-review section.

## 3. [The synthetic argument — sub-claim sections]

> Organize by your **sub-claims**, NOT by the literature's own buckets or paper-by-paper
> (`phg-argument-synthesis`). Under each sub-claim, marshal evidence from across the corpus, bring
> non-citing works into contact, and engage disconfirming work honestly. Consider a **conceptual exhibit**
> (typology / framework diagram) here — see `phg-tables-figures`.

### 3.1 [Sub-claim one]
### 3.2 [Sub-claim two]
### 3.3 [Sub-claim three]

> Positioning throughout should be fair and current: steelman each camp, attribute precisely, and reach
> beyond Anglophone/Global-North work where it exists (`phg-literature-positioning`).

## 4. [The critical re-ordering]

> Deliver the two-step (`phg-critical-intervention`): diagnose the flaw precisely, then supply the better
> account and show how it rearranges what counted as evidence, contradiction, or progress. Own the cost of
> your re-ordering.

## 5. [Agenda / conclusion]

> Deliver the forward agenda (`phg-contribution-framing`): specific, concept-driven directions, with
> handles for multiple sub-fields. **Not** "more research is needed." Restate the contribution and its
> stakes for the discipline.

---

## Notes (endnotes)

> Endnotes **count toward the word cap** — use sparingly.

## References

> **SAGE Harvard** (author–date), consistent throughout. Excluded from the word cap. Every in-text
> citation must resolve; attributions precise.
