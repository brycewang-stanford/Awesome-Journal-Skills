# PiHG Review / Progress Report — Lightweight Scaffold

A minimal structural scaffold for a *Progress in Human Geography* critical review, conceptual
intervention, or commissioned progress report. **This is a skeleton, not content.** PiHG publishes
**no empirical results or detailed cases** — every section synthesizes and argues about others' work.
Confirm strand word limits, the abstract limit, and house style on the SAGE PiHG author pages
(检索于 2026-06；以官网为准). Adjust depth to the route: a progress report is short and tightly scoped;
a review essay is longer and free-standing.

---

## Title page (separate file for double-anonymized review)

- Title (states the debate and signals the argument, not just a topic label)
- Author(s), affiliations, ORCID iDs, corresponding author
- Funding statement · conflict-of-interest declaration · AI-tool-use disclosure
- Content strand (Perspectives / Reviews / Opinions / Biography / Key Publication) or "commissioned progress report"

## Abstract (~100–150 words — limit 待核实; unstructured)

- The subfield/debate and why a critical synthesis is needed now
- The organizing argument (the conceptual spine) and the headline state-of-the-art takeaway
- The forward agenda — not a single empirical finding

## 1. Introduction — the review opening arc

- Frame the subfield/debate for geographers broadly → why now (a turn / new work / political fault line)
- The organizing argument and the spine the reader will be given
- What the review argues and where the field should go (stated up front) → brief roadmap

## 2. The conceptual framework (the spine)

- State, in one sentence, the argument about the subfield's trajectory
- Lay out the cells/turns/framings (MECE-ish) that organize the literature

## 3–N. The synthesis (one section per framework cell)

- Engage the field-defining interventions critically (what they reframed + their limits)
- Make works *argue with each other* (synthesis prose, not work-by-work summary)
- Steelman each tradition; weigh interpretive, critical, and quantitative work on its own terms
- Cite confirmatory work in clusters; foreground only cell-defining interventions

## N+1. Reflexivity & coverage (apparatus)

- Positionality where the standpoint shapes the appraisal
- A brief coverage account (databases, terms/traditions, selection logic, saturation)

## N+2. Conclusion — the forward agenda

- Turn the thin/empty framework cells into a concrete research agenda
- Name specific frontiers, concepts, or methods that are ripe — never "more research is needed"

## References

- Complete and accurate; SAGE/PiHG house style applied at production
- Concepts and turns attributed to their originators
