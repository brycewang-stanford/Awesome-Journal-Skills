# Social Forces Official Source Map

Primary and authoritative sources behind the Social-Forces-specific facts injected into this skill
pack. Access date for all entries: **2026-06-02**. Re-verify volatile items (editor and editorial
term, exact word/abstract caps, processing fee/APC, data-policy wording, table/figure limits) on the
official page before relying on them — journal pages change.

## Official journal, submission & author pages

- **https://academic.oup.com/sf/pages/General_Instructions**
  — Social Forces General Instructions (Oxford Academic). Verifies: **"Articles submitted to Social
  Forces may not exceed 10,000 words (including text, endnotes, and references)"**; **"may not
  contain more than 10 tables and figure panels"**; the journal **"conducts double-anonymized peer
  review"**; identifying information (title, author names/affiliations, correspondence,
  acknowledgements, abstract) goes on a **separate Title Page**; submission via **ScholarOne
  Manuscript Central** (`mc.manuscriptcentral.com/sf`), in Word or .odt; references may be in any
  readable style at first submission but **Chicago Manual of Style, 17th edition author-date** is
  required at final submission; **"The inclusion of a data availability statement is a requirement
  for papers published in the Journal"**; **non-refundable processing fee of $50 ($20 for
  manuscripts written solely by students)**; **"The Journal does not have page charges"**;
  supplementary materials limited to **10 pages**; ORCID encouraged.
- **https://academic.oup.com/sf/pages/author-guidelines**
  — Social Forces Author Guidelines (Oxford Academic). Corroborates the 10,000-word cap, the
  10-table/figure-panel limit, the separate title page for double-anonymized review, the Chicago
  17th-edition author-date style, the data availability statement requirement, the $50/$20 processing
  fee, ORCID, and the 10-page supplementary-materials cap.
- **https://academic.oup.com/sf/pages/Instructions_To_Authors**
  — Instructions to Authors landing page (Oxford Academic).
- **https://mc.manuscriptcentral.com/sf** — ScholarOne Manuscript Central submission portal.
- **https://academic.oup.com/sf** — Social Forces on Oxford Academic (publisher home, current issues,
  advance articles).

## Owner, publisher, history & scope

- **https://academic.oup.com/sf/pages/About**
  — About Social Forces. Verifies: **established 1922**; published by **Oxford University Press** in
  partnership with the **Department of Sociology at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill**;
  describes itself as **"a global leader among social research journals"** publishing **"articles of
  interest to a general social science audience"** and emphasizing **"cutting-edge sociological
  inquiry"** at the intersections with **social psychology, anthropology, political science, history,
  and economics**; **quarterly** (four issues per year). Reports (2024 metrics on the page) an Impact
  Factor of 3.1, a CiteScore of 5.6, and a **median review-to-decision timeline of ~71 days**.
- **https://sociology.unc.edu/social-forces/** — UNC Department of Sociology page for Social Forces
  (editorial home).
- **https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_Forces** — Verifies: founded **1922** by **Howard W. Odum**
  (as *The Journal of Social Forces*; renamed *Social Forces* from volume 4, 1925); quarterly;
  published by OUP for UNC Chapel Hill; concentrates on sociology with a multidisciplinary reach
  (social psychology, anthropology, political science, history, economics); historically tied to the
  Southern Sociological Society; among the oldest continuously published US sociology journals.
- **https://academic.oup.com/sf/article/101/1/4/6646932** — "100 Years of Social Forces" centennial
  essay (context on the journal's history, regional origins, and internationalization).

## Open access & licensing

- **https://academic.oup.com/sf/pages/General_Instructions** (Open Access section) — Verifies: OUP
  open-access options with **Creative Commons** licenses (**CC BY, CC BY-NC, CC BY-NC-ND**); article
  processing charges (APCs) apply **only** if the author chooses open access; standard subscription
  publication carries the processing fee but **no page charges**.
