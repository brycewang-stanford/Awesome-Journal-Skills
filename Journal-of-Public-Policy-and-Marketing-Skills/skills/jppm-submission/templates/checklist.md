# JPP&M Submission Checklist

Checked 2026-07-16 against the AMA journals submission guidelines; re-check volatile items on ama.org and the SageTrack portal before uploading.

## Fit and framing

1. The Policy Contribution Statement (≤300 words, first page of the main document) answers all three required questions: the policy conversation joined, the advance beyond existing marketing-and-policy literature, and the specific stakeholders affected and how.
2. The abstract (≤200 words, unstructured) states the policy question, method, headline finding in decision units, and the stakeholder implication.
3. At least 3 keywords, specific to the topic (e.g., "front-of-package labeling," "data privacy," "consumer vulnerability"), follow the abstract.
4. Every implication names an actor (FTC, FDA, CFPB, state regulators, NGO, regulated marketers) acting within its actual authority.

## Files and format

5. Two-file split for double-anonymized review: (1) title page with authors, affiliations, acknowledgments, funding; (2) anonymized main document.
6. Main document ≤50 pages, double-spaced, 1-inch margins, inclusive of title, abstract, keywords, text, footnotes, references, tables, figures, and print appendices (statement excluded). Tables and references may be single-spaced.
7. Web appendix prepared separately for the overflow robustness battery and supplementary exhibits.
8. Citations, references, headings, and exhibits follow the AMA journals house style.

## Anonymization

9. No author-identifying text: self-citations in third person; data partners described generically ("a large public university," "a national retailer").
10. File metadata scrubbed (Author properties, comments, tracked changes) in every uploaded file, including figures and appendices.
11. Pre-registration and repository links are anonymized view-only versions.

## Evidence and ethics

12. Evaluations show their counterfactual logic in the statement/abstract; experiments show mandatable stimuli and policy-target samples.
13. IRB/ethics approval stated; heightened-care documentation included for vulnerable-population studies.
14. Conflicts of interest, funding sources, and any AI-use disclosure prepared per current AMA policy.
15. Data-availability statement drafted: what is deposited, what is restricted, and why.

## Final pass

16. Portal address confirmed live: https://mc.manuscriptcentral.com/ama_jppm.
17. Current editorial policies re-checked (editor transition, formats, fees if any) on the AMA page.
18. The next action is submission — not another uncontrolled rewrite.
