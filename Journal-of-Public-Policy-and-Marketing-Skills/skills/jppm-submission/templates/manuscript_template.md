# JPP&M Manuscript Skeleton

Structure for a Journal of Public Policy & Marketing main document (anonymized file). The title page is a separate file. Re-check current formatting details on the AMA journals submission-guidelines page.

## Policy Contribution Statement (first page; ≤300 words; excluded from the 50-page cap)

Three obligatory moves, in order:
1. The policy conversation this research initiates or joins (name the rule, debate, or enforcement question).
2. How the manuscript moves understanding beyond the existing marketing and public policy literature.
3. Which specific policy stakeholders are impacted by the results, and how they could act.

## Title

Name the instrument and the outcome (e.g., "Do Front-of-Package Warning Labels Reduce Sugary-Drink Purchases? Evidence from …"). Avoid cleverness that hides the policy question.

## Abstract (≤200 words, unstructured) + Keywords (≥3)

Policy question → design in one clause → headline finding with direction and magnitude in decision units → stakeholder implication.

## 1. Introduction

- The marketplace harm or pending regulatory decision, with a sourced fact.
- What the marketing-and-policy literature and the regulatory record establish; the decision-relevant unknown.
- What this paper does (design + setting, plain language) and finds (magnitude, heterogeneity).
- Who should do what: a two-sentence preview of the implications.

## 2. Policy Background and Conceptual Framework

- The instrument precisely: who mandates what, on whom, enforced how; current legal status, date-stamped.
- The theorized pathway: instrument → consumer/firm response → welfare outcome, with hypotheses (including vulnerable-subgroup and unintended-consequence predictions).

## 3. Method

- Design (experiment / DiD / RDD / synthetic control / survey / meta-analysis) and why it answers the policy question.
- For evaluations: counterfactual, assignment logic, data sources. For experiments: stimuli (show them), conditions mapped to the regulator's choice set, sample and its policy relevance, power, pre-registration.

## 4. Results

- Headline treatment effects in decision units with CIs.
- Identification diagnostics (event study / pre-trends / density / placebos).
- Pre-specified heterogeneity across policy-relevant subgroups, family-corrected.
- Unintended-consequence outcomes (substitution, boomerang) estimated or bounded.

## 5. Robustness

- Organized by threat (selection, anticipation, firm response, measurement), not by test name; battery details to the web appendix.

## 6. Policy Implications

- One primary recommendation per stakeholder, within authority, tied to the estimate that licenses it.
- Marketer-facing guidance for firms subject to the rule.
- Trade-offs and limits: costs, burden, scope conditions, the extrapolation required to reach policy scale.

## 7. Conclusion

- What the policy conversation now knows that it did not; what remains genuinely open.

## References / Tables / Figures / Print Appendices

- AMA journals style; all count toward the 50-page cap — budget accordingly.

## Web Appendix (separate file, outside the cap)

- Full robustness battery, additional exhibits, instrument texts, survey items, deviations log.
