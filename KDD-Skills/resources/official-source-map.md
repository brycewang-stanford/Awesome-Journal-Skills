# Official Source Map - KDD (ACM SIGKDD)

Access date: 2026-07-08

This map records the official sources behind every KDD-specific fact used in this pack.
KDD runs multiple submission cycles per year and revises its track CFPs, page budgets,
review mechanics, and ACM publication terms between cycles, so reopen the current-cycle
CFP for the specific track (Research or Applied Data Science) plus the matching
OpenReview group before giving deadline- or policy-sensitive advice.

were blocked by the network gateway of the build environment, so the page contents
behind the URLs below were verified through web-search renderings of those exact
official pages, cross-checked against the KDD OpenReview venue groups. Open the
primary URLs directly before relying on any deadline, page budget, or fee.

## Verified 2026-cycle facts used in skills

- KDD 2026 is the 32nd ACM SIGKDD Conference on Knowledge Discovery and Data Mining,
  held August 9-13, 2026 at ICC Jeju, Jeju, Korea (sources 1, 9).
- KDD 2026 ran **two submission cycles**. Cycle 1: abstract July 24, 2025, full paper
  July 31, 2025. Cycle 2: abstract February 1, 2026, full paper February 8, 2026. All
  deadlines end-of-day Anywhere on Earth (sources 1, 2).
- Submissions go to **either** the Research Track **or** the Applied Data Science Track,
  never both; KDD 2026 also fielded Datasets and Benchmarks, AI for Sciences, and Blue
  Sky Ideas tracks (sources 2, 3, 7, 8).
- OpenReview manages submission and review; every listed author needs a complete
  OpenReview profile with institutional history going back at least five years,
  homepage, DBLP (if prior publications exist), ORCID, advisors, and recent
  publications (source 2).
- Research Track format: English, double-column ACM template, recommended LaTeX setting
  `\documentclass[sigconf,anonymous,review]{acmart}`; submissions are **8 content
  pages** plus references and an optional appendix; review is double-blind (source 2).
- Accepted papers get a uniform proceedings budget: **12 pages total, of which 9 are
  content pages** — one extra content page over the submission, with references plus
  appendix capped at 3 pages (source 2).
- ADS Track submissions must describe deployed applications and **quantify post-launch
  performance; submissions without that quantification are desk-rejected**. Exceptions
  are possible for systems demonstrably blocked from deployment (source 3).
- Authors respond to each review in a rebuttal period; area chairs weigh rebuttals and
  reviewer discussion, and the PC chairs make final decisions on factors including
  technical merit, originality, impact, execution, presentation, related work,
  reproducibility, and ethics (source 2).
- **Rebuttals and discussion do not allow hyperlinks**, so code or data a team wants
  reviewers to see must be referenced in the submission itself (source 2).
- Decisions include **Resubmit**: a Cycle 1 Resubmit paper may return in Cycle 2, must
  declare the previous OpenReview forum id, and must prepend a one-page summary of
  changes to the PDF (source 2).
- Any use of generative-AI tools must be disclosed and elaborated in the submission
  form, and ACM's authorship policy governs who may be listed as an author (sources
  2, 3, 8).
- ACM publishes KDD proceedings in the Digital Library; under the ACM Open model,
  authors from non-participating institutions face an article-processing charge unless
  they receive a waiver, and ACM announced a temporary 2026 subsidy of 65% to ease the
  transition (sources 2, 9).
