# External Tools - KDD

Access date: 2026-07-08

Open these after confirming which submission cycle and which track (Research or Applied
Data Science) the paper targets. KDD's dual-cycle calendar means "the current CFP" is
ambiguous unless the cycle is named first.

## Official workflow

- KDD 2026 conference site: https://kdd2026.kdd.org/
- Research Track CFP: https://kdd2026.kdd.org/research-track-call-for-papers/
- Applied Data Science (ADS) Track CFP: https://kdd2026.kdd.org/applied-data-science-ads-track-call-for-papers/
- Datasets and Benchmarks Track CFP: https://kdd2026.kdd.org/datasets-and-benchmarks-track-call-for-papers/
- AI for Sciences Track CFP: https://kdd2026.kdd.org/ai4sciences-track-call-for-papers/
- OpenReview groups (per track x cycle), e.g.
  https://openreview.net/group?id=KDD.org/2026/Research_Track_Cycle_2 and
  https://openreview.net/group?id=KDD.org/2026/ADS_Track_Cycle_2
- ACM Digital Library KDD proceedings series: https://dl.acm.org/ (e.g.
  https://dl.acm.org/doi/proceedings/10.1145/3770854 for KDD '26)
- SIGKDD organization site: https://kdd.org/

## Author-side checks

- Track choice locked before writing: Research vs ADS vs Datasets and Benchmarks vs AI
  for Sciences — the same PDF cannot go to Research and ADS simultaneously.
- Cycle choice: which abstract/paper deadline pair applies, in AoE time.
- OpenReview profile completeness for **every** author: five-year affiliation history,
  homepage, DBLP, ORCID, advisors, recent publications.
- ACM sigconf template with the anonymous/review options; 8-content-page budget plus
  references and optional appendix (Research Track, 2026 cycles).
- Code/data repository referenced inside the submission, because rebuttals cannot carry
  links.
- ADS only: post-launch performance quantification present, or a documented
  blocked-from-deployment justification.
- Resubmissions: previous OpenReview forum id declared and a one-page change summary
  prepended to the PDF.
- Generative-AI disclosure fields in the submission form.
- After acceptance: ACM e-rights form, TAPS source upload, ACM Open APC or subsidy
  status, registration, and presentation obligations.

## Do not infer

- Do not project KDD 2026 cycle dates onto later editions; even the number of cycles
  can change.
- Do not assume Research Track page budgets apply to ADS, Datasets and Benchmarks, or
  AI for Sciences submissions — each track publishes its own CFP.
- Do not assume rebuttal mechanics (per-review response, link ban, Resubmit option)
  survive unchanged between cycles of the same edition.
- If a track CFP and an OpenReview announcement disagree, treat the newer OpenReview
  instruction or direct chair communication as controlling, and record the conflict in
  the project log.
