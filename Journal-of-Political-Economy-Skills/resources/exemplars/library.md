## How to use this library

Pick the row whose **method × question** is closest to yours, then study how that paper makes a
mechanism-driven economic argument carry a portable, general-interest lesson — the JPE bar (see
[`../../skills/jpe-topic-selection/SKILL.md`](../../skills/jpe-topic-selection/SKILL.md) and
[`../../skills/jpe-writing-style/SKILL.md`](../../skills/jpe-writing-style/SKILL.md)). JPE's premium is on
**rigorous economic reasoning with a price-theoretic / mechanism-driven angle**, not an atheoretical
correlation. For each exemplar, ask the self-check before claiming your paper is "JPE-shaped."

## By method

### A single mechanism that reorganizes a field (price theory)

- **Stigler, "The Economics of Information," JPE 1961, 69(3):213–225.**
  Verified: RePEc `ideas.repec.org/a/ucp/jpolec/v69y1961p213.html`; UChicago Press DOI `10.1086/258464`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** turns "search is costly" into a price-theoretic model of price dispersion
    and optimal search — one transparent mechanism that founded the economics of information and search.
    The canonical "small idea, enormous reach" JPE paper.
  - **Self-check:** can your central mechanism be stated in one sentence, and does the paper test or model
    *that mechanism* rather than a net correlation (`jpe-topic-selection` mechanism test)?

### Sorting / local public finance theory (equilibrium with mobility)

- **Tiebout, "A Pure Theory of Local Expenditures," JPE 1956, 64(5):416–424.**
  Verified: RePEc `ideas.repec.org/a/ucp/jpolec/v64y1956p416.html`; UChicago Press DOI `10.1086/257839`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** "voting with your feet" gives a non-political, equilibrium solution to the
    local public-goods problem — exactly the equilibrium/incentive reasoning a Chicago referee rewards
    (`jpe-topic-selection` incentives/equilibrium filter).
  - **Self-check:** have you taken seriously how agents optimize and how the market clears, rather than
    estimating a partial effect that a referee will say nets out in equilibrium?

### Applied price theory of non-market behavior (mechanism-driven applied theory)

- **Becker, "Crime and Punishment: An Economic Approach," JPE 1968, 76(2):169–217.**
  Verified: RePEc `ideas.repec.org/a/ucp/jpolec/v76y1968p169.html`; UChicago Press DOI `10.1086/259394`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** applies rational-choice price theory to crime — offenders respond to the
    expected cost of punishment — and derives optimal-enforcement implications. The Chicago lineage the
    `jpe-topic-selection` and `jpe-literature-positioning` skills point to as the JPE antecedent.
  - **Self-check:** does economic theory make a sign/shape prediction here that your evidence can confirm
    or refute (`jpe-topic-selection` theory-linkage), not just a measured association?

### Human-capital model meets the earnings distribution (theory + empirics)

- **Mincer, "Investment in Human Capital and Personal Income Distribution," JPE 1958, 66(4):281–302.**
  Verified: RePEc `ideas.repec.org/a/ucp/jpolec/v66y1958p281.html`; UChicago Press DOI `10.1086/258055`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a human-capital investment model *explains* a first-order empirical fact —
    the shape of the personal income distribution — integrating theory and evidence as
    [`../../skills/jpe-theory-model/SKILL.md`](../../skills/jpe-theory-model/SKILL.md) recommends.
  - **Self-check:** does your model map cleanly to data and explain a fact the field already cares about,
    rather than calibrate a parameter no one disputes?

### Endogenous-growth theory with increasing returns (macro / growth theory)

- **Romer, "Increasing Returns and Long-Run Growth," JPE 1986, 94(5):1002–1037.**
  Verified: UChicago Press article page `journals.uchicago.edu/doi/abs/10.1086/261420` (JPE Vol. 94,
  No. 5); RePEc `ideas.repec.org/s/ucp/jpolec.html`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** makes knowledge an input with increasing marginal productivity inside a
    competitive equilibrium, generating endogenous long-run growth — a model whose lesson is first-order
    and portable far beyond its setting (`jpe-topic-selection` generality filter). A natural fit for
    **JPE Macroeconomics** routing today (see `jpe-topic-selection` on the 2023 companions).
  - **Self-check:** is the lesson portable beyond the specific setting, and would a non-specialist
    economist care about the answer?

### A closed-form model that creates a market (finance / asset-pricing theory)

- **Black & Scholes, "The Pricing of Options and Corporate Liabilities," JPE 1973, 81(3):637–654.**
  Verified: RePEc `ideas.repec.org/a/ucp/jpolec/v81y1973i3p637-54.html`; UChicago Press DOI
  `10.1086/260062`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a no-arbitrage mechanism yields a closed-form option price — theory so sharp
    and testable it reshaped both the literature and real markets. The model *is* the contribution.
  - **Self-check:** does your theoretical result deliver one memorable, testable object (a formula, a
    counterfactual magnitude), stated plainly before the algebra (`jpe-writing-style`)?

## Omitted for venue or lack of full verification (do not attribute to JPE without re-checking)

To keep the list zero-hallucination, the following well-known candidates were **excluded** after checking
the venue — most are *not* JPE. They are listed only as guardrails against the JPE-vs-AER-vs-QJE-vs-RESTud
misattribution that the `jpe-literature-positioning` skill warns about:

- **Becker, "A Theory of the Allocation of Time" (1965)** — verified to be in *The Economic Journal*
  75(299):493–517 (DOI `10.2307/2228949`), **not JPE**. Frequently cited as "JPE 1965" (including in some
  of this pack's own skill prose); the venue is *The Economic Journal*. Becker's *JPE* antecedents that
  *are* verified above are "Crime and Punishment" (1968) and the work in this list — cite those when you
  want the JPE lineage.
- **Acemoglu, Johnson & Robinson, "The Colonial Origins of Comparative Development" (2001)** — *American
  Economic Review* 91(5):1369–1401, **not JPE**.
- **Mortensen & Pissarides, "Job Creation and Job Destruction in the Theory of Unemployment" (1994)** —
  *Review of Economic Studies* 61(3):397–415, **not JPE**.
- **Card & Krueger, "School Quality and Black-White Relative Earnings" (1992)** — *Quarterly Journal of
  Economics* 107(1):151–200, **not JPE**.
- **Card, "The Impact of the Mariel Boatlift on the Miami Labor Market" (1990)** — *Industrial and Labor
  Relations Review* 43(2):245–257, **not JPE**.
- **Levitt, "Understanding Why Crime Fell in the 1990s" (2004)** — *Journal of Economic Perspectives*
  18(1):163–190, **not JPE** (note: a different Becker-lineage crime paper, Becker 1968, *is* JPE and is
  listed above).

Before adding any new paper to this library, confirm it on a UChicago Press JPE article page
(`journals.uchicago.edu/doi/...`) or RePEc `ideas.repec.org/a/ucp/jpolec/...` with volume/issue, and
update the access-date header. When in doubt, omit.
