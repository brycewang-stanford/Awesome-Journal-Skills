# Official Source Map - MobiCom

Access date: 2026-07-09

This map records the official sources behind every MobiCom-specific fact in this pack.
MobiCom reissues its Call for Papers, deadline pair, review timeline, and artifact rules
for every edition, and the same year runs **two submission deadlines that feed different
editions**, so a fact that was true for one round can be stale by the next. Reopen the
current `sigmobile.org/mobicom/<year>` pages and the `mobicom<yy>.hotcrp.com/deadlines`
page before giving submission-ready advice.

## Access-method note

Direct fetches of `sigmobile.org` (the MobiCom conference and CFP pages),
`mobicom26.hotcrp.com` (the submission and `/deadlines` pages), and `dblp.org` returned
**HTTP 403** in the verification environment on 2026-07-09. All facts below were therefore
read through search-engine renderings of the exact official URLs, cross-checked across
multiple independent renderings (the SIGMOBILE conference pages, the HotCRP pages, dblp
records, ACM Digital Library proceedings landings, and author/institution pages for
exemplars). Anything that could not be confirmed this way is listed under 待核实 below and
is **not** stated as fact anywhere in the pack.

## Verified facts used in the skills (2026-07-09 snapshot)

- MobiCom 2026 is the **32nd** ACM International Conference on Mobile Computing and
  Networking: **October 26-30, 2026, Austin, TX, USA** (source 1).
- MobiCom is the **ACM SIGMOBILE** flagship for **mobile computing and wireless
  networking** and runs a **multi-deadline model** — two submission deadlines per year,
  each with its own PC meeting, in place since the 25th edition (sources 1, 4).
- Each deadline feeds the corresponding edition, so a **round can feed a later edition**
  than the calendar year of the deadline; the summer/fall round opening after MobiCom 2026
  competes for MobiCom 2027 (source 4; and the reported 2027 dates in source 9).
- The MobiCom 2026 **winter/spring round** registered abstracts by **March 7, 2026** and
  took full papers by **March 14, 2026**; the printed 7:59:59 am EDT paper time
  corresponds to an **11:59 pm AoE** cutoff (source 3).
- Review runs in **two rounds per deadline**: papers not advancing past round 1 get an
  **early notification with reviews**; papers reaching round 2 have their reviews released
  and are given a **rebuttal window**; a **one-shot revision** process is retained beyond
  accept/reject (source 2).
- Reviewing is **double-blind** — author identities withheld from reviewers (source 2).
- The submission **body is capped at 12 single-spaced numbered pages including figures and
  tables**; **references** may extend beyond the cap and **appendices** placed after the
  references are not counted (source 2).
- The required format is **double column** (columns 9.25in x 3.33in, 0.33in gutter, no
  more than 55 lines per column, font no smaller than 10pt, US letter) (source 2).
- Accepted papers may earn the three **ACM artifact badges** — Artifacts Available,
  Artifacts Evaluated — Functional, and Results Reproduced — from a separate committee;
  the badges are independent (source 5).
- The **SIGMOBILE Test-of-Time Paper Award** recognizes decade-plus impact and is
  presented at MobiCom (source 6).
- Submissions are uploaded to the per-edition **HotCRP** site `mobicom<yy>.hotcrp.com`
  (source 3).

## Reported facts (secondary renderings; labeled as reported wherever used)

- MobiCom '26 second-round **final notification ~June 22, 2026** and MobiCom '27
  first-round **submission ~September 3, 2026 / notification ~November 24, 2026** are
  reported by a community deadline aggregator, which marks the 2027 dates estimated
  because the CFP is not yet posted (source 9).
- Award selections used in the exemplars library — the MobiCom '13 Best Paper for WiSee
  and the SIGMOBILE Test-of-Time selections — are reported by author institutions and the
  SIGMOBILE award pages (sources 6, 8).

## 待核实 (could not be verified live on 2026-07-09 — never state as fact)

- The **MobiCom 2027 Call for Papers**: its exact two deadlines (the ~September 3, 2026
  first-round date is an aggregator estimate), the two-round timeline, and whether format
  and revision rules carry over unchanged.
- MobiCom 2026 **general-chair and program-chair names** (the committee page did not
  render in retrievable text).
- The **exact length or format of the rebuttal** (word/character limit, whether it is
  per-review or a single response) and the exact **rebuttal-window dates** for either round.
- The MobiCom 2027 **artifact call**: badge set, artifact deadlines, and whether the AEC
  coordinates with the sysartifacts community site.
- **Registration and in-person presentation requirements**, presenter obligations, and any
  remote-presentation option for accepted papers.
- The exact **ethics / human-subjects / spectrum-and-measurement policy** wording and the
  current **AI-use policy** for the 2027 cycle.

## Still cycle-volatile

Deadline dates and the AoE-vs-printed-clock convention, which edition each round feeds,
the two-round timeline and rebuttal mechanics, the one-shot-revision rules, the page and
column geometry, artifact badges and their deadlines, award definitions, HotCRP site names,
and registration duties. Every skill in this pack treats the numbers above as a dated
snapshot, not a standing rule.
