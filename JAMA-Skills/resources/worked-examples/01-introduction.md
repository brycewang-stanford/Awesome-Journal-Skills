# Worked Example: A JAMA-Style Structured Abstract + Introduction (before → after)

This demonstrates the **JAMA Original Investigation opening**: the **Key Points box**, the
**structured abstract** with JAMA's exact heading set (Importance / Objective / Design,
Setting, and Participants / Interventions / Main Outcomes and Measures / Results /
Conclusions and Relevance / Trial Registration), and an IMRaD Introduction that ends in a
single pre-specified objective — written for a general clinician audience, free of spin.
Every requirement below is drawn from this pack's own skill files; the bracketed source
notes point to the skill that demands it.

**Illustrative study (fictional):** *"Pharmacist-Led Medication Review vs Usual Care for
Preventing Hospital Readmission in Older Adults: A Randomized Clinical Trial."* Fictional
setting: a multicenter trial of adults aged 65 years or older discharged after an acute
medical admission.

---

## Before (block-paragraph abstract + buried intro — typical first draft)

> **Abstract.** Polypharmacy is common in older adults and medication errors are an
> important problem. We did a randomized study to see whether a pharmacist-led medication
> review would help. Patients were randomized to the intervention or control and followed
> up. The intervention markedly reduced readmissions and was safe and well tolerated, and
> we believe it should be widely adopted to improve outcomes in this vulnerable population.
>
> **Introduction.** Hospital readmission has been studied extensively. Many interventions
> have been tried, with mixed results. In this paper we use an intention-to-treat analysis
> with a Cox proportional-hazards model to study a pharmacist medication-review program.
> This is an important topic for health systems.

**What's wrong (against this pack's skills):**

- **Block-paragraph abstract** ignoring JAMA's structured headings — a named anti-pattern
  ([`jama-structured-abstract`](../../skills/jama-structured-abstract/SKILL.md)).
- **No Key Points box** (Question / Findings / Meaning), which many JAMA article types
  require ([`jama-structured-abstract`](../../skills/jama-structured-abstract/SKILL.md)).
- **Spin:** "markedly reduced" and "safe and well tolerated" with no numbers, no 95% CIs,
  and no harms data ([`jama-writing-style`](../../skills/jama-writing-style/SKILL.md)).
- **Conclusion overreaches** ("should be widely adopted") beyond the evidence
  ([`jama-writing-style`](../../skills/jama-writing-style/SKILL.md)).
- **No Trial Registration line** on a randomized trial
  ([`jama-structured-abstract`](../../skills/jama-structured-abstract/SKILL.md);
  [`jama-reporting-standards`](../../skills/jama-reporting-standards/SKILL.md)).
- **Intro leads with method** ("we use a Cox model") instead of the clinical question, and
  states **no single pre-specified primary outcome**
  ([`jama-study-design`](../../skills/jama-study-design/SKILL.md)).
- **No CONSORT framing** for an RCT (design, randomization, ITT)
  ([`jama-reporting-standards`](../../skills/jama-reporting-standards/SKILL.md)).

---

## After — Key Points box

> **Key Points**
>
> **Question** &nbsp;Does a pharmacist-led medication review at discharge reduce 90-day
> hospital readmission in adults aged 65 years or older?
>
> **Findings** &nbsp;In this randomized clinical trial of 1842 older adults, 90-day
> all-cause readmission was 18.3% with pharmacist-led review vs 23.1% with usual care, a
> statistically significant difference (hazard ratio, 0.76 [95% CI, 0.63-0.92]).
>
> **Meaning** &nbsp;Among older adults discharged after an acute medical admission, a
> pharmacist-led medication review reduced 90-day readmission; replication and
> cost-effectiveness assessment are warranted before broad implementation.

*(Question / Findings / Meaning, each one restrained sentence; Findings names the design
and the key number with its 95% CI; Meaning does not overstate — per
[`jama-structured-abstract`](../../skills/jama-structured-abstract/SKILL.md).)*

---

## After — Structured abstract (JAMA Original Investigation headings, in order)

> **Importance** &nbsp;Older adults are frequently readmitted after hospital discharge, and
> medication-related problems are a common, potentially preventable contributor. Whether a
> structured pharmacist-led medication review reduces readmission is uncertain.
>
> **Objective** &nbsp;To determine whether a pharmacist-led medication review at discharge,
> compared with usual care, reduces 90-day all-cause hospital readmission among older adults.
>
> **Design, Setting, and Participants** &nbsp;Multicenter, parallel-group, randomized
> clinical trial at 6 hospitals. Adults aged 65 years or older discharged after an acute
> medical admission were enrolled from January 2023 to June 2024 and followed for 90 days
> (final follow-up September 2024). Of 2104 screened, 1842 were randomized.
>
> **Interventions** &nbsp;Participants were randomized 1:1 to a pharmacist-led medication
> review with reconciliation and a follow-up telephone call (n = 921) or to usual discharge
> care (n = 921).
>
> **Main Outcomes and Measures** &nbsp;The pre-specified primary outcome was all-cause
> hospital readmission within 90 days. Secondary outcomes included emergency department
> visits and a composite of adverse drug events; all were pre-specified.
>
> **Results** &nbsp;Among 1842 randomized participants (mean [SD] age, 76.4 [7.1] years;
> 978 [53.1%] women), 1798 (97.6%) completed follow-up. By intention-to-treat, 90-day
> readmission occurred in 169 of 921 participants (18.3%) in the intervention group vs 213
> of 921 (23.1%) in the usual-care group (hazard ratio, 0.76 [95% CI, 0.63-0.92];
> P = .005). Emergency department visits did not differ significantly (14.1% vs 15.8%;
> hazard ratio, 0.88 [95% CI, 0.71-1.09]). Adverse drug events were reported in 41 (4.5%)
> and 58 (6.3%) participants, respectively.
>
> **Conclusions and Relevance** &nbsp;Among older adults discharged after an acute medical
> admission, a pharmacist-led medication review reduced 90-day all-cause readmission
> compared with usual care. These findings support further evaluation, including
> cost-effectiveness, before broad implementation.
>
> **Trial Registration** &nbsp;ClinicalTrials.gov Identifier: NCT00000000 *(fictional
> placeholder)*

---

## After — Introduction (IMRaD, ending in one pre-specified objective)

> Hospital readmission within 90 days is common among older adults discharged after an
> acute medical illness and is associated with substantial morbidity, cost, and loss of
> independence. Medication-related problems — including reconciliation errors, drug
> interactions, and nonadherence — are among the more potentially preventable contributors,
> particularly in patients receiving multiple medications. *(why it matters, for a general
> clinician reader — [`jama-writing-style`](../../skills/jama-writing-style/SKILL.md).)*
>
> Prior studies of discharge medication interventions have reported mixed results, often
> limited by single-center designs, heterogeneous components, or outcomes that did not
> capture all-cause readmission. Whether a standardized, pharmacist-led review delivered at
> the point of discharge reduces readmission in a general older population remains uncertain.
> *(what is unresolved — frames the gap without throat-clearing.)*
>
> We conducted a multicenter randomized clinical trial to test this question. The
> **pre-specified primary objective was to determine whether a pharmacist-led medication
> review at discharge, compared with usual care, reduces 90-day all-cause hospital
> readmission** among adults aged 65 years or older. *(a single, pre-specified, patient-
> relevant primary outcome stated as the objective —
> [`jama-study-design`](../../skills/jama-study-design/SKILL.md); the method is left to the
> Methods section, not led with.)*

---

## Why the "after" meets the JAMA bar

Mapped to this pack's skill checklists:

- **Structured abstract uses JAMA's exact headings in order**, and the **Results lead with
  numbers** (18.3% vs 23.1%; HR, 0.76 [95% CI, 0.63-0.92]) rather than adjectives
  ([`jama-structured-abstract`](../../skills/jama-structured-abstract/SKILL.md)).
- **Key Points box present** (Question / Findings / Meaning), consistent with the abstract
  and naming the design
  ([`jama-structured-abstract`](../../skills/jama-structured-abstract/SKILL.md)).
- **Trial Registration line present** for the RCT
  ([`jama-structured-abstract`](../../skills/jama-structured-abstract/SKILL.md);
  [`jama-reporting-standards`](../../skills/jama-reporting-standards/SKILL.md)).
- **No spin:** "markedly" and "safe and well tolerated" are gone; the primary result is
  stated plainly with its CI and P value, harms are reported quantitatively, and the
  conclusion is bounded to the studied population
  ([`jama-writing-style`](../../skills/jama-writing-style/SKILL.md)).
- **CONSORT-consistent framing:** numbers screened, randomized, and analyzed; 1:1
  allocation; **intention-to-treat** as the primary analysis — the pieces the CONSORT flow
  diagram and checklist require
  ([`jama-reporting-standards`](../../skills/jama-reporting-standards/SKILL.md);
  [`jama-study-design`](../../skills/jama-study-design/SKILL.md)).
- **Single pre-specified primary outcome**, named in both the abstract and the Introduction
  objective; secondaries clearly labeled and not promoted
  ([`jama-study-design`](../../skills/jama-study-design/SKILL.md)).

### Note on observational studies (STROBE)

Had this been a **cohort** rather than a trial, the same skills require swapping
**Interventions** for **Exposures** in the abstract, dropping the Trial Registration line,
using **associational language** ("was associated with," not "reduced"), reporting
**both unadjusted and adjusted** estimates, and following **STROBE** with a participant-flow
diagram instead of CONSORT
([`jama-structured-abstract`](../../skills/jama-structured-abstract/SKILL.md);
[`jama-reporting-standards`](../../skills/jama-reporting-standards/SKILL.md);
[`jama-writing-style`](../../skills/jama-writing-style/SKILL.md)).
