# Worked Example: Reframing an Introduction for Journal of Money, Credit and Banking

This is a fictional example. It does not describe a real paper.

## Before

> This paper studies an important topic using a large dataset and several robustness checks. We contribute to the literature by adding new evidence and showing heterogeneous effects.

## Diagnosis

- The first sentence does not name the JMCB audience or the journal's monetary economics, banking, credit markets, financial intermediation, and macro-finance scope.
- The contribution could fit several siblings: Journal of Monetary Economics, Review of Economic Dynamics, Journal of Finance, and Journal of Financial Intermediation.
- The mechanism words that matter here are absent: monetary transmission, bank balance sheets, credit frictions, central-bank relevance.

## After

> This paper asks how **monetary transmission** changes the decision margin that JMCB readers care about. The design ties the estimate to **bank balance sheets**, separates the main mechanism from **credit frictions**, and reports uncertainty in a way that makes the policy or theory implication portable beyond the study setting.
