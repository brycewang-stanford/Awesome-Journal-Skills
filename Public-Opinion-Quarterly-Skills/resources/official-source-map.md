# POQ Official Source Map

Primary and authoritative sources behind the POQ-specific facts injected into this skill pack.
Access date for all entries: **2026-06-01**. Re-verify volatile items (editors and editorial term,
exact word/abstract caps, fee/APC, data-policy wording, accepted submission types, embargo length) on
the official page before relying on them — journal pages change.

## Official journal, submission & author pages

- **https://academic.oup.com/poq/pages/general_instructions**
  — POQ General Instructions (author guidelines). Verifies: five submission types and their caps —
  **Original Articles ≤ 6,500 words of text and notes** (excluding figures, tables, references, and
  appendices); **Research Notes under 3,000 words** of text and notes (single findings, important
  extensions/replications); **Polls in Context ≤ 2,500 words**; **Research Syntheses ≤ 6,500 words**
  (main text, including footnotes/endnotes); **Book Reviews typically ~1,200 words**. Submission via
  **ScholarOne Manuscripts** (`mc.manuscriptcentral.com/poq`); **double-blind** peer review, typically
  at least two (most often three) referees. **AAPOR-standard disclosure** required: POQ requires
  manuscripts to **disclose these elements for all data reported (if applicable) or link to publicly
  available documentation** — disclosure of funding; **exact question wording** of all survey
  questions; a clear description of the **population under study**; a description of the **sample
  design**; **method and dates of data collection**; **the response rate and details of its
  calculation** (per AAPOR Standard Definitions); **sample sizes and a discussion of the precision of
  the findings**; and a discussion of any **design effect due to clustering and weighting**. Authors
  must include an **"Appendix A: Disclosure Elements"** detailing each required methodological element.
  Replication data and code must be placed in **POQ's permanent Dataverse archive**, allowing an
  independent researcher to **reproduce exactly all published tables and figures**; a **Data
  Availability Statement** is required in the article's endmatter; an embargo of up to twelve months
  from the article's appearance in an issue may be requested.
- **https://academic.oup.com/poq/pages/why-submit**
  — Why Submit to POQ. Verifies: simple online submission via ScholarOne Manuscripts; detailed
  double-blind peer review with clear policies and expectations for authors.
- **https://academic.oup.com/poq**
  — POQ on Oxford Academic (publisher home, current issues, advance articles).

## Data replication & transparency

- **https://dataverse.harvard.edu/dataverse/poq** — **Public Opinion Quarterly** collection on
  **Harvard Dataverse** (POQ's permanent Dataverse archive). Verifies: replication materials are
  hosted on Harvard Dataverse. Materials must allow an independent researcher to reproduce exactly all
  published tables and figures; archived **before accepted manuscripts advance to typesetting**.
- **https://aapor.org/standards-and-ethics/transparency-initiative/** — **AAPOR Transparency
  Initiative (TI)**. Verifies: launched **October 2014** to encourage research organizations to
  disclose their methodological procedures fully and rigorously when reporting survey-based findings;
  proactive, educational approach to routine disclosure of survey methods.
- **https://aapor.org/standards-and-ethics/best-practices/** — AAPOR Best Practices for Survey
  Research (the underlying disclosure norms POQ adopts).

## Owner, publisher, affiliation, history

- **https://academic.oup.com/poq/pages/About** — POQ About page. Verifies: published by **Oxford
  University Press**; an **official journal of the American Association for Public Opinion Research
  (AAPOR)**; **published since 1937**; among the most frequently cited journals of its kind;
  selectively publishes **theoretical contributions to opinion and communication research, analyses of
  current public opinion, and investigations of methodological issues involved in survey validity**
  (questionnaire construction, interviewing and interviewers, sampling strategy, mode of
  administration).
- **https://aapor.org/** — American Association for Public Opinion Research (owner/affiliate). AAPOR's
  three journals are POQ, the Journal of Survey Statistics and Methodology, and Survey Practice; the
  editors of all three now require reporting of the disclosure standards.
- **https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Public_Opinion_Quarterly** — Verifies: POQ founded **1937**;
  leading journal of public opinion / survey research; published by OUP for AAPOR.

  section **"Polls in Context" (≤ 2,500 words)** and a longer **"Poll Trends"** form; the historical
  "The Polls — Trends" and "The Polls — Review" labels appear in older issues. Treat **Polls in
  Context** as the current short-form label and confirm the exact current section names and caps on
  the live guidelines page. 需复核.
