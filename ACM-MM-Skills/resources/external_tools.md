# External Tools - ACM MM

Access date: 2026-07-09

Use these tools after reopening the current ACM MM cycle pages. ACM MM rules change by
edition, and access to `*.acmmm.org` may be gated (direct fetches returned HTTP 403 at
check time — read via search renderings and cross-check ACM DL / OpenReview / dblp).

## Official workflow

- ACM MM 2026 site (Calls & Dates, Important Dates): https://2026.acmmm.org/
- ACM MM 2026 Call for Technical Papers: https://2026.acmmm.org/site/cfp-guidelines.html
- ACM MM 2026 Topics of Interest (thematic areas): https://2026.acmmm.org/site/topics.html
- ACM MM 2026 Review Process Guidelines: https://2026.acmmm.org/site/review-process-guidelines.html
- Track calls (Reproducibility / Open Source / Dataset / BNI / Grand Challenge): the
  `call-*.html` pages under https://2026.acmmm.org/site/
- ACM MM 2026 OpenReview group: https://openreview.net/group?id=acmmm.org/ACMMM/2026
- ACM Digital Library ACM MM proceedings: https://dl.acm.org/conference/mm
- dblp ACM MM index: https://dblp.org/db/conf/mm/index.html
- ACM SIGMM: https://www.sigmm.org/
- ACM sigconf template / Master Article Templates: https://www.acm.org/publications/proceedings-template

## Author-side checks

- OpenReview profiles, conflicts, coauthor list, title, abstract, and the chosen **thematic
  area** for the main track.
- The current ACM `sigconf` template, the 6–8-page body budget, references-only overflow
  pages, and PDF export.
- Double-blind anonymity across the paper, appendix, supplement, code/data, media assets,
  file metadata, and repository links — unless submitting to a **named single-blind track**
  (Reproducibility, Open Source Software, Dataset), where the artifact identity is expected.
- Supplementary-material deadline (a separate date after the paper), archive contents, and
  whether video/audio demos render without proprietary players.
- Track fit: main track vs. Brave New Ideas, Grand Challenge, Open Source Software, Dataset,
  or Reproducibility — each has a distinct call, format, and blinding rule.
- Rebuttal window, anonymity of the rebuttal, and the no-new-links rule.
- Camera-ready package: de-anonymization, ACM rights form, CCS concepts, sigconf
  compliance, registration, and artifact/badge release.

## Do not infer

- Do not infer future-cycle dates or the MM 2027 venue details from ACM MM 2026.
- Do not assume the same thematic-area list, active track set, page budget, double-blind
  exceptions, rebuttal mechanics, badge tiers, or camera-ready forms without reopening the
  current official pages and each track's own call.
- Do not treat a paper indexed under a SIGMM-adjacent venue (ICMR, MMSys, ACM MM Asia, or
  the TOMM journal) as an ACM MM main-conference paper; verify the dblp edition record.
- If official pages disagree (as the 2026 notification date did across the CFP and
  Important-Dates pages), use the newest CFP, OpenReview announcement, track call, or direct
  chair communication as the controlling source and record the conflict.
