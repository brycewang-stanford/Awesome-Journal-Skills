# Official Source Map - ACM MM

Access date: 2026-07-09

This map records the official sources used for ACM MM-specific facts in this pack. ACM MM
policies are cycle-specific and rewritten each edition by rotating chairs; reopen the
current-year conference site, its OpenReview groups, each track's own call, and ACM's
artifact-badging definitions before giving submission-ready advice.

## Access method (read this first)

Direct fetches of `2026.acmmm.org` returned **HTTP 403 Forbidden** in the verification
environment. Every official page below was therefore read through **search-engine
renderings of the exact URL** and cross-checked against the ACM MM 2026 OpenReview groups,
the ACM Digital Library, dblp's `conf/mm` index, and `sigmm.org`. Where a fact could not be
pinned to the official page with confidence, it is marked **待核实** rather than asserted.
Two class of number carry an explicit note: the **notification date** (the CFP page and the
Important-Dates/Dataset pages disagree) and the **camera-ready date** (seen only on
deadline mirrors at check time).

## Verified 2026 facts used in skills

- ACM MM 2026 is the 34th ACM International Conference on Multimedia, scheduled for
  November 10–14, 2026 in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, organized under ACM SIGMM.
- Submissions were made through OpenReview.
- The 2026 timetable listed: abstract March 25, 2026; regular paper April 1, 2026;
  supplementary material April 8, 2026; rebuttal June 4, 2026; author notification in early
  July 2026; camera-ready reported August 6, 2026 (all AoE).
- Regular papers were 6–8 pages of body in the ACM `sigconf` template, with references
  permitted on up to two additional pages that must contain only references; overlength
  papers were desk-rejected without review.
- The main track and most tracks used double-blind review; the Reproducibility, Open Source
  Software, and Dataset tracks were single-blind because the artifact cannot be anonymized.
- The main track was organized into sixteen thematic areas (see source 4); a submission
  selects a thematic area.
- ACM MM 2026 ran multiple tracks: Main, Dataset, Brave New Ideas, Multimedia Grand
  Challenge, Open Source Software Competition, Reproducibility, Interactive Art, Doctoral
  Symposium, workshops, tutorials, panels, and demos.
- An optional anonymous rebuttal was offered after reviews.
- Accepted papers are published in the ACM Digital Library.

## Organizers and fee model

- ACM MM is the flagship conference of ACM SIGMM (the Special Interest Group on Multimedia).
  Like every CS/AI conference in this collection, it has NO standing editor-in-chief; the
  rotating analogue is the per-edition General Chairs and Program Chairs, appointed by the
  SIGMM community and the ACM MM Steering Committee, rotating yearly. Re-check the current
  organization page before relying on a name.
- There is NO article-processing charge (APC) for the main track. ACM MM is funded through
  registration; at least one author must register and present. Publication is in the ACM
  Digital Library under ACM's rights framework; ACM's open-access options (including ACM
  Open) and the sigconf rights block are settled at camera-ready. 待核实: the exact
  author-side rights/OA form and any per-track cost differences.
