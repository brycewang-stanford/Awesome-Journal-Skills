# Exemplar Library — *Production and Operations Management* (POM)

> **Verified via web search, access date 2026-06-08.** Each paper was confirmed on Wiley Online
> Library under *Production and Operations Management* (the journal of the Production and Operations
> Management Society, POMS; DOI stem `1937-5956`). The DOI is the verification anchor; where a full
> co-author list could not be confirmed in one pass it is shown as "et al." Positioning aid — read the
> originals; do not reproduce numbers. *(Note: POM moved publishers after 2023; older articles retain
> the Wiley `1937-5956` DOIs cited here.)*
>
> **Sibling-confusion guard.** POM is NOT: *Operations Research* (`opre`), *Management Science*
> (`mnsc`), *Manufacturing & Service Operations Management* (M&SOM, `msom`), *Journal of Operations
> Management* (JOM). Verify the `1937-5956` DOI before citing.

## How to use

- **State the operational decision, then the method.** POM rewards work whose analytical or empirical
  apparatus improves a real operations/SCM decision; lead with the decision (see `pom-writing-style` /
  `pom-contribution-framing`).
- **Pair structure with evidence.** Analytical POM papers carry a numerical study; empirical/behavioral
  POM papers (e.g., the information-sharing experiment) take identification and incentives seriously —
  the vendored `code/` supports the empirical lane.
- **Position across the INFORMS/POMS family.** Be explicit about why the paper is POM rather than
  *Management Science* / *M&SOM* / JOM (`pom-literature-positioning`).
