## How to use this library

BJPS is a **broad, internationally-oriented generalist** journal: each paper below earns its place by
making a result in one subfield or country legible and useful to a *wide* political-science readership
(see [`../../skills/bjps-topic-selection/SKILL.md`](../../skills/bjps-topic-selection/SKILL.md) and
[`../../skills/bjps-theory-building/SKILL.md`](../../skills/bjps-theory-building/SKILL.md)). Pick the row
whose **subfield × method** is closest to yours, then study how it (1) states wide interest, and (2)
defends its design on its own methodological terms (see
[`../../skills/bjps-research-design/SKILL.md`](../../skills/bjps-research-design/SKILL.md)). Run the
self-check before claiming your paper is "BJPS-shaped."

## By method

### Experiment (political psychology / behaviour)

- **McGraw, "Avoiding Blame: An Experimental Investigation of Political Excuses and Justifications," BJPS 1990, 20(1):119–131.**
  Verified: `ideas.repec.org/a/cup/bjposi/v20y1990i01p119-131_00.html`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** an early experimental study that isolates a portable mechanism — how
    politicians' *accounts* (excuses vs. justifications) shape citizens' blame — that travels far beyond
    its specific stimuli to accountability, retrospection, and elite communication broadly (see
    `bjps-research-design`: experiments judged on their own terms; `bjps-theory-building`: name a portable
    mechanism).
  - **Self-check:** does your experiment isolate a *named, portable mechanism* a scholar in another
    subfield could import, not just a treatment effect specific to your stimuli?

### Observational panel / international political economy (comparative–IR)

- **Li & Reuveny, "Economic Globalization and Democracy: An Empirical Analysis," BJPS 2003, 33(1):29–54.**
  Verified: `ideas.repec.org/a/cup/bjposi/v33y2003i01p29-54_00.html`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a cross-national panel test of a *general* claim (does globalization help
    or harm democracy?) that decomposes "globalization" into distinct channels rather than treating it as
    one variable — a model of making a single empirical analysis speak to a debate comparativists, IPE
    scholars, and democratization researchers all share.
  - **Self-check:** if your independent variable is a broad concept, do you disaggregate it into channels
    with distinct, theorized effects rather than testing a single undifferentiated measure?

### Replication / reappraisal (comparative political behaviour — economic voting)

- **Royed, Leyden & Borrelli, "Is 'Clarity of Responsibility' Important for Economic Voting? Revisiting Powell and Whitten's Hypothesis," BJPS 2000, 30(4):669–698.**
  Verified: `ideas.repec.org/a/cup/bjposi/v30y2000i04p669-698_21.html`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a disciplined *reappraisal* of an influential cross-national hypothesis —
    the prototype of a contribution whose value is correcting/qualifying the record rather than reporting
    a brand-new finding (pairs with the **Comment**/reappraisal logic in
    [`../../skills/bjps-literature-positioning/SKILL.md`](../../skills/bjps-literature-positioning/SKILL.md)).
  - **Self-check:** if your contribution is a reappraisal, do you engage the original claim precisely and
    show what specifically changes (holds / is conditional / fails) under your test?

### Cross-national institutional analysis (comparative political economy)

- **Scruggs, "Institutions and Environmental Performance in Seventeen Western Democracies," BJPS 1999, 29(1):1–31.**
  Verified: `ideas.repec.org/a/cup/bjposi/v29y1999i01p1-31_00.html`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** links a *general institutional* argument (neo-corporatism vs. pluralism) to
    comparative outcomes across seventeen democracies — the institutionalist comparative design BJPS is
    known for, where the cross-country variation *is* the test (see `bjps-research-design`: "say what the
    case is a case *of*, and what it generalizes to").
  - **Self-check:** does your cross-national design let the institutional variation you theorize carry the
    identification, with a clear claim about what it generalizes to?

### Review / synthesis of a causal debate (political behaviour)

- **Persson, "Education and Political Participation," BJPS 2015, 45(3):689–703.**
  Verified: `ideas.repec.org/a/cup/bjposi/v45y2015i03p689-703_00.html`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a critical synthesis of a contested *causal* question (does education cause
    participation, or proxy for prior advantage?) — a model of consolidating a debate and clarifying what
    would settle it, of wide interest across behaviour, methods, and political economy.
  - **Self-check:** if your piece organizes a debate, do you say precisely what evidence would adjudicate
    it, rather than only cataloguing positions?

## Omitted for lack of BJPS verification (do NOT attribute to BJPS without re-checking)

To keep the list zero-hallucination, the following plausible candidates were **excluded** after web
search placed them in a *sibling* journal, not BJPS. They are listed only as guardrails:

- **Powell & Whitten, "A Cross-National Analysis of Economic Voting" (1993)** — verified to be in the
  *American Journal of Political Science* 37(2):391–414, **not BJPS**. (BJPS *does* host a 2000 reappraisal
  of this hypothesis — Royed, Leyden & Borrelli, listed above — so do not conflate the two.)
- **Hibbs, "Political Parties and Macroeconomic Policy" (1977)** — verified to be in the *American
  Political Science Review* 71(4):1467–1487, **not BJPS**. A common partisan-theory misattribution.
- **Fearon, "Rationalist Explanations for War" (1995)** — verified to be in *International Organization*
  49(3):379–414, **not BJPS**.
- **Stoll, "Social Cleavages and the Number of Parties" (2008)** — verified to be in *Comparative
  Political Studies* 41(11):1439–1465, **not BJPS**.
- **Golder, "Ideological Congruence and Electoral Institutions" (2010)** — verified to be in the
  *American Journal of Political Science* 54(1):90–106, **not BJPS**.
- **Achen & Bartels, *Democracy for Realists* (2016)** — a Princeton University Press *book*, not a BJPS
  article. Listed because it surfaces in "political behaviour + BJPS" searches.

Before adding any new paper to this library, confirm it on Cambridge Core
(`cambridge.org/core/journals/british-journal-of-political-science`) with volume/issue/pages, cross-check
the RePEc `cup:bjposi` record, and update the access-date header. When in doubt, omit.
