# Topic Selection & Fit (bjps-topic-selection)

BJPS is a **broadly based, general-interest** political-science journal that "aims to cover developments
across a wide range of countries and specialisms." The bar is not "new to my subfield" or "first study
of country X" — it is **"of wide interest to political scientists."** Use this skill to pressure-test
fit before you invest.

## When to trigger

- Choosing among possible projects or framings for a BJPS submission
- A reviewer/colleague said the paper feels "too narrow," "single-country," or "subfield-only"
- Deciding between a **Research Article** and a **Letter**
- Considering a **Comment** on a published BJPS piece

## The BJPS fit test

A strong BJPS paper usually clears all four:

1. **Wide interest.** A scholar in a *different* subfield or studying a *different* region should see
   why it matters. Frame the stakes for politics broadly — institutions, behaviour, representation,
   conflict, political economy, normative order.
2. **A real contribution, not just a finding.** It changes how the field thinks, measures, or argues —
   a new mechanism, a decisive test, a reconceptualization, a corrected record.
3. **Credible on its own methodological terms.** Quantitative, qualitative, formal, experimental, or
   interpretive — each is welcome, but each must be done rigorously (see `bjps-research-design`).
4. **A clean, answerable scope.** Sharp enough to answer convincingly within ~10,000 words (Article) or
   ~4,000 (Letter) (caps 待核实 — confirm on the live page).

## Travel past your case and your subfield

| Home base | Reach the general reader by… |
|-----------|------------------------------|
| Single-country case | drawing the general mechanism the case illustrates; speak to comparative theory others can use |
| One subfield (e.g., IR) | connecting to a broad phenomenon (cooperation, conflict, institutions) a comparativist or behaviouralist recognizes |
| Political behaviour / opinion | tie the micro result to representation, accountability, or institutional design |
| Political theory | show why the normative/conceptual stakes matter for empirical work or political life |
| Methodology | show what substantive question the method newly answers — not method for its own sake |

> BJPS is international by design: a US-only or single-country result that is *framed* as US-specific is
> off-fit; the same result framed as a test of a general mechanism can be a strong BJPS paper.

## Format choice

- **Research Article** — full study, broad claim, ~10,000 words (待核实).
- **Letter** — one decisive contribution (a clean test, a measurement advance, a focused reappraisal),
  ~4,000 words (待核实). A complete short paper, not a truncated Article.
- **Comment** — a critique, correction, or reappraisal of a specific published BJPS article (engage the
  original directly; see `bjps-literature-positioning`).

## Anti-patterns

- "It's never been studied in country X" as the whole contribution (descriptive, single-case)
- A clever method demo with no substantive political-science payoff
- A sprawling question that cannot be answered within the length cap
- Choosing Article length out of habit when a Letter would land harder
- Pitching the paper to one subfield's frontier with no general stake

## Output format

```
【Question】one sentence
【Wide interest】who outside the subfield/region cares, and why
【Contribution type】theory / test / measurement / reconceptualization / corrected record
【Format】Research Article / Letter / Comment
【Fit verdict】strong / needs reframing / off-fit (why)
【Next】bjps-literature-positioning
```

## The two-axis fit screen (BJPS-specific)

BJPS asks the contribution to travel on **two** axes at once, and a paper can pass one while failing the
other:

| | Travels across subfields | Stuck in one subfield |
|---|---|---|
| **Travels across countries/cases** | Strong BJPS fit | Reframe to a cross-subfield stake |
| **Stuck in one case** | Reframe the case as a test of a general mechanism | Off-fit — likely a national or specialist outlet |

A clean US-only result framed as US-specific sits in the bottom-right; the *same* result framed as a
test of a general institutional mechanism moves to the top-left. The reframing is in the *claim*, not
the data — which is why topic selection precedes positioning.

## Calibration anchors (hedged)

- BJPS is international by design: a single-country study is welcome, but only when framed as speaking to
  a general comparative question others can use.
- Pick the format for impact, not habit — a decisive single result often lands harder as a **Letter**
  than as a padded Article; confirm the current caps on the live page (待核实).
