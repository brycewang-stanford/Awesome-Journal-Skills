# Literature Positioning (bjps-literature-positioning)

BJPS is read across comparative politics, IR, theory, behaviour, political economy, and methods, and
draws contributions from related disciplines (sociology, social psychology, economics, philosophy).
Positioning is therefore not throat-clearing — it is how a non-specialist editor and reviewer see that
the paper speaks to a **live, general** debate rather than a parochial one.

## When to trigger

- Drafting or revising the introduction and the "contribution" paragraph
- A reviewer said you "missed obvious work" or "don't engage the broader debate"
- Your country/subfield literature is solid but the paper doesn't connect to general theory
- You need to distinguish your contribution from the closest prior papers
- Writing a **Comment** that must engage the target article precisely

## How BJPS wants the literature engaged

1. **Engage the debate, not a pile of citations.** Identify the live disagreement or open question the
   paper speaks to, and cite the works that *define* it — including across subfields and countries.
2. **Two audiences at once.** Satisfy specialists (you know the frontier) *and* generalists (why it
   matters). A single-country study should connect to the comparative theory it tests.
3. **Name the gap precisely.** Not "little is known" — say what is contested, mismeasured,
   under-theorized, or untested, and why resolving it advances the field generally.
4. **Position the contribution as a move in the debate.** "Prior work argues X via mechanism M; we show
   M fails / is conditional on C / is better explained by M′."
5. **Pre-empt the obvious objection.** BJPS reviewers are expert; acknowledge the strongest rival and
   say how your design adjudicates it (hand off to `bjps-research-design`).

## Cross-subfield / cross-national engagement (a distinctive BJPS demand)

| If your paper is… | also engage… |
|-------------------|--------------|
| a single-country empirical test | the general comparative theory the case speaks to |
| an IR / formal argument | the substantive empirical work the model bears on |
| a measurement contribution | the substantive literatures that will use the measure |
| a behaviour/opinion finding | the institutional or representational stake it informs |
| normative / theoretical | the empirical work the argument bears on, where applicable |

## Anti-patterns

- A "literature dump" with no organizing debate
- Engaging only your own subfield, or only the literature on one country (a top BJPS rejection reason)
- Strawmanning prior work, or hiding the closest competitor paper
- Self-citation that breaks anonymity (BJPS is double-blind — see `bjps-submission`)
- Claiming "first to study" when the contribution is incremental
- For a Comment: misreading or caricaturing the target article rather than engaging its actual claim

## Output format

```
【Debate】the live disagreement / open question
【Key works】the 3-6 that define it (incl. cross-subfield / cross-national)
【Gap】what is contested / mismeasured / untested
【Move】how this paper changes the debate
【Strongest rival】and how the design will adjudicate it
【Next】bjps-theory-building
```

## Engagement depth BJPS editors expect, by paper type

Because BJPS is a broad, international generalist journal, "the relevant literature" is rarely just your
home subfield's or your home country's. The desk screen asks whether a non-specialist editor sees a
general debate; this table sets the depth that usually clears it.

| Paper type | Literature it must engage | The parochialism trap to avoid |
|------------|---------------------------|--------------------------------|
| Single-country empirical | the cross-national theory the case tests | a country literature with no general claim |
| Comparative / political economy | the general institutional theory it speaks to | a region study read only by area specialists |
| IR / formal | the substantive empirical work the model bears on | a model debate detached from political outcomes |
| Behaviour / opinion | the representation/accountability stake it informs | a single-survey finding with no broader payoff |
| Measurement / methods | the substantive literatures that will use the measure | method-for-its-own-sake with no consumer |

## Calibration anchors (hedged)

- BJPS's premium is **wide interest**, so positioning that satisfies only one subfield's or one
  country's frontier is structurally weak even when the within-niche contribution is real.
- Methodological pluralism extends to literatures: a normative argument may legitimately engage
  empirical work, and a single case may legitimately speak to general comparative theory.
- The exact desk-screen wording can change — confirm against the journal's current author and
  peer-review pages before relying on it.
