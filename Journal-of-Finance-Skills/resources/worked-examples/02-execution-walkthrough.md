# Worked Example: Executing a Staggered-DiD Identification (StatsPAI walkthrough)

The fictional paper claims a **state-level disclosure reform** (adopted in staggered
years 2011 / 2013 / 2015) caused treated firms to **cut book leverage**. The panel is
400 firms × 15 years (2005–2019), balanced, with a never-treated control group and
**cohort-heterogeneous, dynamic effects** built in — exactly the setting where naive
two-way fixed effects (TWFE) is biased. Below, the agent runs the JF identification
bar instead of merely advising it.

> **How to read this:** the point is not the specific coefficients (the data are
> synthetic) — it is that the *workflow produces a defensible, audited number with one
> tool call per step*, and that the cross-checks behave exactly as the methods
> literature predicts.

---

## Step 0–1 — Let the data declare its shape, then pick the estimator

`detect_design(data_path=…)` → **panel**, confidence 1.0, unit `firm_id`, time `year`,
400 units × 15 periods, balanced.

`recommend(data_path=…, y=leverage, treatment=treat, time=year, id=firm_id,
design="did")` → ranked: **(1) Callaway–Sant'Anna (2021) staggered DiD**, **(2)
Sun–Abraham (2021) interaction-weighted**. The advisor refuses to hand back a bare
TWFE for staggered timing — the same call `jf-identification` makes in prose.

## Step 2 — Fit the heterogeneity-robust estimator (and keep the handle)

`callaway_santanna(…, g=cohort, i=firm_id, t=year, y=leverage, as_handle=true)`:

| Estimand | ATT | SE | 95% CI | p |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Callaway–Sant'Anna (DR, never-treated control) | **−0.0272** | 0.0030 | [−0.0332, −0.0213] | ≈0 |

Returned `result_id` = `r_ffee0f65` — the handle every downstream check chains from
(no re-supplying data). The pre-period group-time coefficients come back small and
mostly insignificant (e.g. relative time −3: +0.0003, p = 0.96), the executed version
of "show flat pre-trends."

## Step 3 — Show the TWFE bias you are correcting (the pre-emption, executed)

A skeptic's first objection is "why not plain TWFE?" Run it and show why:

`feols(fml="leverage ~ treat | firm_id + year", vcov={"CRV1":"firm_id"})`:

| Estimator | Coef on `treat` | SE | t |
|---|---|---|---|
| Naive TWFE (two-way FE) | **−0.0227** | 0.0018 | −12.8 |
| Callaway–Sant'Anna (clean) | **−0.0272** | 0.0030 | — |

TWFE is **attenuated ~17% toward zero** relative to the heterogeneity-robust estimate —
the textbook negative-weight / already-treated-control contamination (Goodman-Bacon
2021). This *is* the "TWFE is biased under staggered adoption" pre-emption from
`jf-identification`, now a number rather than a claim.

## Step 4 — Cross-check with a second modern estimator

`sun_abraham(…, g=cohort, i=firm_id, t=year, y=leverage)`:

| Estimand | ATT | SE | 95% CI |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sun–Abraham (2021) | **−0.0277** | 0.0033 | [−0.0341, −0.0213] |

CS (−0.0272) and SA (−0.0277) agree to the third decimal — the reassuring signal JF
referees look for. Report both (the routing table in `jf-identification` says exactly
this).

## Step 5 — Audit the result; let the tool enumerate what the design still owes

`audit_result(result_id="r_ffee0f65")`:

- **parallel_trends — PASSED.** Joint pre-trend test p = **0.155** (> 0.10 threshold);
  fail to reject parallel trends.
- **honest_did — missing** → run `sensitivity_rr` / `honest_did_from_result` (Step 6).
- **bacon_decomposition — missing** → already shown via the TWFE-vs-CS gap in Step 3.
- (Two MCMC-convergence checks flagged low-importance; not applicable to this estimator.)

The audit turns "what robustness do I owe?" into a checklist with the exact next
function to call — no guessing the battery.

## Step 6 — Honest sensitivity: how much pre-trend violation would overturn it?

`honest_did_from_result(result_id="r_ffee0f65", method="SD", e=0)` (Rambachan–Roth 2023):

| Smoothness bound M | 95% robust CI | Rejects 0? |
|---|---|---|
| 0 (= standard) | [−0.0191, −0.0029] | yes |
| 0.0021 | [−0.0212, −0.0009] | yes |
| 0.0041 | [−0.0233, +0.0012] | no |
| 0.0124 | [−0.0315, +0.0095] | no |

`max_rejecting_M` = **0.0021**: the effect survives small parallel-trends violations
but becomes inconclusive once allowed deviations reach ~0.004. Reporting this honest
breakdown point — instead of leaning on the pre-trend test alone (which is low-power,
per Roth 2022) — is precisely the modern-inference discipline `jf-robustness` asks for.
