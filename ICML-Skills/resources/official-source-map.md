## Verified 2026 facts used in skills

- Main-track submission uses OpenReview and ICML LaTeX style; no non-LaTeX typesetting support.
- Submission main body limit is 8 pages; references and unlimited appendices are in the same PDF.
- Submission PDF limit is 50MB; camera-ready PDF limit is 20MB.
- Supplementary material has two classes: supplementary manuscripts and code/data. It is due with
  the paper and must be anonymized.
- Code/data are encouraged for reproducibility; anonymized repositories are allowed, but their
  branch must not be modified after the submission deadline.
- All submissions are double-blind; author identity leaks in paper or supplement can lead to
  rejection.
- Initial reviews are followed by author response; there is no revised-paper upload during feedback.
- Reviewers and authors may have one additional communication round after author responses.
- ICML 2026 aims for three reviews per paper, though the exact number may vary.
- Main-track papers require an impact statement; accepted papers require a lay summary in
  OpenReview.
- Camera-ready main body allows 9 pages and must include appendices in the PDF; there is no
  camera-ready supplementary material.
- Accepted papers can have either in-person presentation or proceedings-only status; proceedings-only
  accepted papers still require a registration path.
- Accepted papers publish original submission, supplementary material, reviews, meta-reviews,
  rebuttal, and reviewer-author discussion on OpenReview in addition to camera-ready.
- Concurrent ICML submissions with overlapping authors are treated as prior work and must be cited
  and discussed when a reasonable reviewer would expect it; anonymized PDFs of cited concurrent
  submissions must be included in supplementary material.
- Authors may use generative AI tools but remain responsible for all content; LLMs cannot be authors;
  prompt injection is prohibited and can cause desk rejection.
- ICML 2026 has a reviewer LLM-use framework with Policy A and Policy B.

## Organizers and fee model (verified 2026-06-22)

- ICML is run by the International Machine Learning Society (IMLS). Like every CS/AI conference in
  this collection, it has NO standing editor-in-chief; the rotating analogue is the per-edition
  General Chair and Program Chairs. ICML 2026 General Chair was Tong Zhang (UIUC); Program Chairs were
  Miroslav Dudik, Martin Jaggi, Alekh Agarwal, and Sharon (Yixuan) Li. These appointments rotate
  every cycle — re-check the current committees page before relying on a name.
- There is NO article-processing charge (APC). ICML funds the conference through registration fees;
  at least one author must register to present. Accepted papers are published open-access in PMLR
  (Proceedings of Machine Learning Research), which charges no author fee. Cross-checked against the
  ICML 2026 committees page and PMLR's open-access model (source 8).
