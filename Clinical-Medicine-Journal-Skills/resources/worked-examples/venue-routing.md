# Worked Routing Examples

These examples are fictional diagnostic scenarios for venue routing. They are
venue-fit aids, not clinical advice, and do not describe real submissions or
publication facts.

## Case 1: General Big-Four Or Specialty Title?

Manuscript: a clinical study with a meaningful endpoint in one disease area.

Route:
- Test the general big-four (NEJM/Lancet/JAMA/BMJ, in the natural-science bundle)
  only when the question, population, and practice implications reach all of
  medicine, not one specialty.
- Choose a JAMA Network or Lancet specialty title (`jama-cardiology`,
  `jama-oncology`, `the-lancet-respiratory-medicine`) when a specialty audience is
  the real reviewer community.
- Choose a society specialty flagship when the disease-area society's readership
  is where the result must land.

## Case 2: Match Study Design To Reporting Checklist

Manuscript: any clinical study; the design dictates the checklist.

Route:
- Randomized trial: register the trial and report to CONSORT before routing.
- Observational study: report to STROBE.
- Systematic review or meta-analysis: report to PRISMA.
- Diagnostic-accuracy study: report to STARD.
- A design-checklist mismatch is a desk-reject risk; fix it before venue choice.

## Case 3: Review Or Primary Study?

Manuscript: a synthesis of a clinical field.

Route:
- `nature-reviews-clinical-oncology` is invited-review-only; do not route an
  uninvited primary study or unsolicited review there.
- Route primary studies to a matching primary-research venue with the correct
  reporting checklist.

## Case 4: Society-Pair Routing Within A Specialty

Manuscript: a nephrology or hepatology study.

Route:
- Nephrology: weigh `journal-of-the-american-society-of-nephrology` against
  `kidney-international` by audience and study type.
- Hepatology: weigh `journal-of-hepatology` against `hepatology` the same way.
- Trial registration and the matching reporting checklist remain prerequisites
  re-checked on each venue's official page.
