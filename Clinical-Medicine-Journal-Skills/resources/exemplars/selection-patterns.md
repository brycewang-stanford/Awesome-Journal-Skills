# Venue-Selection Patterns

These patterns guide routing across clinical-medicine venue families. They are
venue-fit aids, not clinical advice, and are not paper exemplars.

## General Versus Specialty Versus Society

- The general big-four (NEJM/Lancet/JAMA/BMJ, in the natural-science bundle) need
  reach across all of medicine; a strong single-specialty result is not that.
- JAMA Network and Lancet specialty titles (`jama-cardiology`, `jama-oncology`,
  `the-lancet-respiratory-medicine`) fit when a specialty audience judges the work.
- Society specialty flagships fit when the disease-area society's readership is the
  intended community.

## Study Design Drives The Checklist

- Randomized trial → CONSORT, with prospective trial registration.
- Observational study → STROBE.
- Systematic review or meta-analysis → PRISMA.
- Diagnostic-accuracy study → STARD.
- Registration and the matching checklist are prerequisites, re-checked on live
  official sources before any venue decision.

## Review Venues Are Invited

- `nature-reviews-clinical-oncology` is invited-review-only; route uninvited
  primary studies elsewhere.

## Society-Pair Routing

- Nephrology pairs `journal-of-the-american-society-of-nephrology` with
  `kidney-international`; choose by audience and study type.
- Hepatology pairs `journal-of-hepatology` with `hepatology` the same way.
- These choices are venue fit only and never substitute for clinical judgment.
