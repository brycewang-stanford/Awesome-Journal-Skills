# Source Basis and Official-Check Notes

This pack encodes journal-fit and house-style heuristics for 30 flagship
English-language **specialty clinical-medicine** journals — deepening the
clinical-medicine category beyond the general big-four (NEJM, Lancet, JAMA, BMJ)
and the broad-specialty venues already covered by the sibling
`English-NaturalScience-Journal-Skills` bundle. It is a positioning and re-framing
aid, **not** a static substitute for any journal's live author instructions, and it
does not give clinical or regulatory advice.

For per-journal starting queries, use `official-source-map.md` in this directory.

## What this bundle covers (and why these venues)

- **JAMA Network specialty journals.** JAMA Internal Medicine, JAMA Oncology, JAMA
  Cardiology, JAMA Neurology, JAMA Pediatrics, JAMA Psychiatry, and JAMA Surgery.
- **The Lancet specialty titles.** The Lancet Respiratory Medicine, The Lancet
  Diabetes & Endocrinology, The Lancet Public Health, and The Lancet Psychiatry.
- **Society / specialty flagships.** Annals of Oncology and Nature Reviews Clinical
  Oncology; the American Journal of Respiratory and Critical Care Medicine; the
  Journal of the American Society of Nephrology and Kidney International; the Journal
  of Clinical Endocrinology and Metabolism and Diabetologia; the Journal of
  Hepatology, Hepatology, and the American Journal of Gastroenterology; the Journal
  of Allergy and Clinical Immunology; Arthritis & Rheumatology; Brain; Stroke;
  Radiology; Anesthesiology; Critical Care Medicine; the American Journal of
  Obstetrics and Gynecology; and European Urology.

## Stable, public facts used for fit (no volatile numbers)

Only durable structural conventions inform fit — e.g. the structured-abstract and
clinical-evidence-hierarchy expectations of major clinical journals; the JAMA
Network and Lancet family structures; the society affiliations (ATS, ASN, ESMO,
Endocrine Society, EASL/AASLD, ACG, AAAAI, ACR, AHA/ASA for Stroke, RSNA, ASA, SCCM,
EAU); the largely-invited character of Nature Reviews Clinical Oncology; and the
field-standard expectations for **trial registration** (ICMJE), **reporting
guidelines** (CONSORT for RCTs, PRISMA for systematic reviews, STROBE for
observational studies, STARD for diagnostic-accuracy studies, ARRIVE for animal
work), ethics/IRB approval, informed consent, and data-sharing statements.

## What each profile deliberately does NOT assert

To stay correct over time, every profile **omits volatile facts** and defers them to
the official-submission checklist instead of stating a number:

- No impact factors, acceptance rates, ISSNs, exact word/figure/reference limits,
  APC amounts, or editor names.
- No fabricated citations (literatures are referred to generically).
- No clinical, diagnostic, or treatment advice — these are venue-fit tools only.

## Operational rule for every journal skill

1. Before submitting, search the **live official site or submission system** for
   "<journal name> instructions for authors" and follow the current version.
2. Trust the publisher's own domain or submission system (JAMA Network, The Lancet /
   Elsevier Editorial Manager, Wiley, OUP, the society systems) over any third-party
   or aggregator copy.
3. Re-check article types and length limits, structured-abstract format, the
   applicable reporting guideline and checklist (CONSORT/PRISMA/STROBE/STARD/ARRIVE),
   **trial registration** and the registration-number requirement, ethics/IRB and
   informed-consent statements, data-sharing statements, ICMJE authorship and
   conflict-of-interest disclosure, funding, AI-use disclosure, preprint policy, and
   open-access / licensing terms.
4. For umbrella families (JAMA Network, Lancet, Nature Portfolio, Wiley, OUP), check
   both the journal-specific page and the family policy page; family-wide ICMJE and
   reporting rules often coexist with journal-specific article limits.
5. If the live official instructions conflict with a skill, the official instructions
   win.
