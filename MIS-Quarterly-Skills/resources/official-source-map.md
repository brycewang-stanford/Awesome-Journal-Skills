## VERIFIED facts injected into the skills (with source #)

- **Field/scope:** information systems research — behavioral science, design science, and the economics/organizational study of information technology; covers IT-based services, management of IT resources, and the use, impact, and economics of IT. (#9, #11)
- **Publisher/sponsor:** published by the Management Information Systems Research Center (MISRC), Carlson School of Management, University of Minnesota; an official journal of the Association for Information Systems (AIS); quarterly (March, June, September, December). (#9)
- **Editor-in-Chief:** Susan A. (Sue) Brown, University of Arizona — 14th EIC; term January 1, 2024 – December 31, 2026. (#7, #8)
- **Submission system:** ScholarOne Manuscripts is the only accepted method; manuscript submitted as a Word (.doc/.docx) file; **no fee to submit**. (#1, #5)
- **Manuscript categories and page limits (count ALL content — text, tables, figures, references, appendices):** Research Article 50 pp (primary); Research Notes ~half a Research Article; Theory Development 55 pp; Theory-Generative Research Synthesis 65 pp; also Issues and Opinions and a Design Science category; over-length submissions/revisions returned to authors; supplementary materials generally discouraged. (#1, #3)
- **First page / abstract / keywords:** first manuscript page includes title, abstract, and keywords (5-10 specific terms). (#6)
- **Formatting/style:** follow the MISQ Style Guide; APA 7th edition citations/references; in-text citation leads with the cited information, not the author name (e.g., "... best team in the nation (Smith & Jones, 1997)"); major heads bold/all caps/centered, subheads title case. (#5, #6)
- **Review model:** double-anonymous (double-blind); author/reviewer identities known to the EIC, Senior Editor, Associate Editor, and editorial staff, never to reviewers; manuscript must contain no identifying information; SE + AE initial review then external reviewers; the Senior Editor owns the decision; many manuscripts go through one or more revision rounds. (#2)
- **Research transparency:** pluralistic, genre-appropriate transparency policy; a transparency commitment is declared/uploaded at submission (Step 2: Upload Files, under Miscellaneous); transparency materials document design/data/analysis and include procedures and/or code sufficient to permit replication; collaboration with AIS Transactions on Replication Research and replication badges. (#1, #4)
- **Reciprocal service:** by submitting, authors agree to review up to three papers per year for the journal if invited. (#1)
- **Design-science genre:** MISQ is the canonical home of IS design science research, anchored by Hevner, March, Park & Ram (2004), with its own submission track and build-and-evaluate evaluation criteria for IT artifacts. (#10)
- **Four IS research traditions:** behavioral, design science, economics, and organizational — explicitly pluralistic and welcoming cross-tradition blending. (#11)
