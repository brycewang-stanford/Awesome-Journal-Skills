# Official Source Map - The Web Conference (WWW)

Access date: 2026-07-08

This map records the official sources behind every Web Conference-specific fact in this
pack. The venue reorganizes its research tracks, deadlines, page arithmetic, submission
platform, and special calls **every edition**, and the 2026 edition even moved its
conference dates mid-cycle, so reopen the current edition's site before giving any
deadline- or policy-sensitive advice.

**Access-method note (2026-07-08):** the network gateway of the build environment
refused direct fetches of `www2026.thewebconf.org`, `thewebconf.org`, `sigweb.org`,
`openreview.net` / `api2.openreview.net`, and `web.archive.org` (CONNECT 403). All
page contents behind the URLs below were therefore verified through search-engine
renderings of those exact official pages, cross-checked against ACM Digital Library
proceedings records, dblp entries, and the conference's official X/Twitter account.
Open the primary URLs directly before relying on any date, page count, or fee.

## Verified 2026-edition facts used in the skills

- WWW 2026 is the **35th edition** of the series, held in **Dubai, UAE**, postponed
  from April 13-17 to **June 29 - July 3, 2026** by an announcement of March 10, 2026
  (sources 1, 12).
- The 2026 **research tracks** were: Economics, online markets and human computation;
  Graph algorithms and modeling for the Web; Responsible Web; Search and
  retrieval-augmented AI; Security and privacy; Semantics and knowledge; Social
  networks and social media; Systems and infrastructure for Web, mobile, and WoT;
  User modeling, personalization and recommendation; Web mining and content analysis
  (source 2).
- 2026 research/industry timetable: **abstract September 30, 2025; full paper
  October 7, 2025; rebuttal November 24 - December 1, 2025; notification January 13,
  2026; camera-ready January 25, 2026**, all end-of-day AoE, deadlines strict
  (sources 3, 7, 12).
- Format: one PDF containing **8 pages of main paper**, then references, then an
  optional appendix (reproducibility details, proofs, pseudo-code), **at most 12
  pages in total**; the first 8 pages must be self-contained because reviewers are
  not required to read past them; ACM double-column template with
  `\documentclass[sigconf, anonymous, review]{acmart}`, Word Interim Template
  permitted (source 2).
- Accepted papers all receive the same proceedings budget — **12 pages, of which 8
  are content pages** — and are published by ACM in the Digital Library (sources 2, 8).
- Review is **double-blind**; submissions omit author names, affiliations,
  acknowledgements, and identifying self-citations; anonymized preprints on arXiv or
  SSRN are tolerated but must not be cited by the submission (source 2).
- The **author list is final at the abstract deadline** — no additions, removals, or
  reordering afterward, only spelling or affiliation corrections (source 2).
- Each author may appear on **at most 7 submissions** cumulatively across the
  research tracks (source 2).
- The 2026 research tracks ran on **EasyChair** with complete, up-to-date profiles
  required for every listed author — a platform change from the OpenReview groups
  used by the 2024 and 2025 editions — while the 2026 Industry track and several
  workshops ran on OpenReview (sources 2, 7, 9).
- **LLMs cannot be listed as authors**; authors may use them to rephrase text but
  remain solely responsible for the content (source 2).
- **Artifact badging**: accepted papers from all main/companion tracks except
  workshops may earn the "Artifacts Available" ACM DL badge via a light availability
  check of an archival-repository deposit, submitted with the camera-ready (source 6).
- Short papers (4 pages including references, deadlines Nov 10/17, 2025) and the
  Web4Good track (deadlines Nov 23/30, 2025) offered later routes into the **main
  proceedings** in the same cycle (sources 4, 5).
- Series governance: IW3C2 ran WWW from 1994 until early 2022; the series was renamed
  The Web Conference in 2018 and has been managed by **ACM/SIGWEB** with a steering
  committee since 2022; there is **no standing editor-in-chief** — general and
  program chairs rotate per edition (sources 10, 11).
- WWW 2027 will be held in **Dublin, Ireland** (source 10).
