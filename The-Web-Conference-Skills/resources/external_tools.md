## Submission platforms (verify per edition and per track)

- 2026 research tracks ran on EasyChair (per the CFP); 2024-2025 editions used
  OpenReview (`ACM.org/TheWebConf/2025/Conference` etc.); the 2026 Industry track
  used OpenReview (`ACM.org/TheWebConf/2026/Industry`). The platform is a per-track,
  per-edition decision — never send co-authors profile instructions before checking
  the current call.

## Author-side checks tied to these tools

- Current track list and per-track scope (re-cut most editions).
- Abstract vs. paper deadline pair, rebuttal window, notification, camera-ready.
- Page arithmetic for the chosen lane (research 8+refs+appendix ≤ 12 in 2026;
  short papers 4 including references in 2026).
- Author-list freeze timing, per-author submission cap, preprint and LLM policy
  wording.
- All co-authors' platform profiles complete before the abstract deadline.
- Registration deadlines and presentation obligations — in 2026 these moved after
  the postponement; official channels only.

## Conflict rule

When any two sources disagree — cached CFP vs. live page, tweet vs. site, this
pack vs. anything current — the newest official edition page or direct chair
communication wins. Record the discrepancy in your working notes and, if this pack
is affected, update `official-source-map.md` with a fresh access date.
