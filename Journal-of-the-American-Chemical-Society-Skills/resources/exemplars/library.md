## By subfield × method

### Asymmetric organocatalysis — "first" enantioselective transformation (organic)

- **Ahrendt, Borths & MacMillan, "New Strategies for Organic Catalysis: The First
  Highly Enantioselective Organocatalytic Diels–Alder Reaction," JACS 2000,
  122(17):4243–4244.**
  Verified: `pubs.acs.org/doi/10.1021/ja000092s` (DOI 10.1021/ja000092s).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** introduces a *concept* (iminium/imidazolidinone
    organocatalysis) rather than one reaction — the breadth driver is a new mode of
    catalysis others adopt, exactly the `jacs-scope-fit` "concept" route to breadth.
    A clean, single high-impact result: the Communication archetype.
  - **Self-check:** is your advance a *generalizable principle* a chemist outside
    your subfield will reuse, not a one-substrate optimization?

### Organometallic catalysis — mechanism that explains a workhorse catalyst (organometallic)

- **Sanford, Love & Grubbs, "Mechanism and Activity of Ruthenium Olefin Metathesis
  Catalysts," JACS 2001, 123(27):6543–6554.**
  Verified: `pubs.acs.org/doi/10.1021/ja010624k` (DOI 10.1021/ja010624k).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** turns a widely-used catalyst into *understood*
    chemistry — ligand effects on initiation/activity backed by kinetics. The
    breadth driver is conceptual insight that travels across every metathesis user.
  - **Self-check:** does your mechanistic study change how others will *choose or
    design* a catalyst, or only rationalize one result?

### Cross-coupling / catalyst development — enabling C–N bond formation (organic methodology)

- **Wolfe, Wagaw & Buchwald, "An Improved Catalyst System for Aromatic
  Carbon–Nitrogen Bond Formation: The Possible Involvement of Bis(Phosphine)
  Palladium Complexes as Key Intermediates," JACS 1996, 118(30):7215–7216.**
  Verified: `pubs.acs.org/doi/10.1021/ja9608306` (DOI 10.1021/ja9608306).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a *method others will adopt* (a practical Pd/BINAP
    system for aryl C–N coupling) — `jacs-scope-fit`'s "enabling utility" breadth
    driver, paired with a mechanistic hypothesis. Concise Communication scope.
  - **Self-check:** will synthetic chemists outside your lab *run your method* on
    their own problems, or is it a niche scope extension better suited to a
    specialized journal?

### Supramolecular chemistry — a designed molecular machine (physical-organic / supramolecular)

- **Anelli, Spencer & Stoddart, "A Molecular Shuttle," JACS 1991,
  113(13):5131–5133.**
  Verified: `pubs.acs.org/doi/10.1021/ja00013a096` (DOI 10.1021/ja00013a096).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a single designed [2]rotaxane demonstrates a *new
    concept* (controllable mechanical motion in a molecule) — broad interest from a
    minimal, decisive system; the conceptual breadth driver at its purest.
  - **Self-check:** does one well-characterized system *establish a principle*, so
    the advance is the idea, not the compound count?

### Mechanistic / physical-organic — dissecting a bistable switch (physical chemistry)

- **Choi, Tseng, Dichtel, Heath, Stoddart et al., "Mechanism of the
  Stoddart–Heath Bistable Rotaxane Molecular Switch," JACS 2004,
  126(19):6029–6030.** *(Author list verified on the ACS article page; cite the
  page for the exact byline.)*
  Verified: `pubs.acs.org/doi/10.1021/ja036498x` (DOI 10.1021/ja036498x).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** quantitative mechanism (switching kinetics/
    thermodynamics) underpinning a device-relevant molecule — physical-chemistry
    rigor serving a broad-interest claim about how molecular switches work.
  - **Self-check:** do your physical measurements *adjudicate a mechanism* the field
    debates, rather than report a number no one disputes?

### Chemical biology — a bioorthogonal reaction for living systems (biological chemistry)

- **Agard, Prescher & Bertozzi, "A Strain-Promoted [3 + 2] Azide–Alkyne
  Cycloaddition for Covalent Modification of Biomolecules in Living Systems," JACS
  2004, 126(46):15046–15047.**
  Verified: `pubs.acs.org/doi/10.1021/ja044996f` (DOI 10.1021/ja044996f).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a copper-free (SPAAC) reaction that works in living
    systems — *enabling utility* with breadth across chemistry and biology; concise
    Communication landing a tool the whole field uses.
  - **Self-check:** does your method *unlock experiments others could not run*, so
    the breadth is the enabled science, not the reaction alone?
