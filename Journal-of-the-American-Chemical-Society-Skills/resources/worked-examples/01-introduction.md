# Worked Example: A JACS-Style Abstract and Opening (before → after)

This demonstrates the **JACS advance arc** drawn from this pack's own skills.
From `jacs-results-framing`, an abstract is **problem → advance → key quantitative
evidence → significance**, with at least one hard number (yield, ee, TON, rate, λ,
R-factor). From `jacs-writing-style`, prose is concise and declarative, boosters
are replaced by facts, and every adjective is matched to evidence. From
`jacs-scope-fit`, the opening must pass both the **advance test** and the
**breadth test** (will chemists outside the subfield care?). From `jacs-figures`,
the **TOC/abstract graphic must encode the central advance, not decorate** — and
the format choice (Communication vs Article) is set explicitly per
`jacs-length-management`.

**Illustrative paper (fictional):** *"Catalytic Enantioselective C(sp³)–H
Fluorination of Unactivated Alkanes with a Confined Phosphate Catalyst."*
Fictional system: a chiral catalyst **1** that fluorinates a methylene C–H bond in
simple alkanes with high enantioselectivity.

---

## Before (buries the advance — typical first-draft abstract)

> A variety of novel chiral catalysts were synthesized and their reactivity was
> investigated. C–H functionalization is a very important area of modern
> chemistry that has been studied extensively. Herein we report a new catalyst
> that shows excellent enantioselectivity and good yields under mild conditions
> for the fluorination of alkanes. The reaction is general and proceeds cleanly.
> This work represents an unprecedented advance and should be of broad interest.

**What's wrong (against this pack's skills):**

- **No quantitative result.** `jacs-results-framing` requires at least one hard
  number (yield, ee, ...); none appears.
- **Booster pile-up.** "novel", "very important", "excellent", "mild conditions",
  "clean", "unprecedented" — all on the `jacs-writing-style` booster blacklist,
  each to be replaced by a number or a stated condition.
- **Advance asserted, not shown.** Significance claimed by adjective ("broad
  interest") rather than demonstrated — the `jacs-results-framing` anti-pattern.
- **No problem framed and no delta vs prior art** (`jacs-scope-fit` advance test
  and `jacs-writing-style` claim discipline: state the closest prior art and the
  specific delta).
- **No breadth driver named** (generality / concept / enabling utility /
  selectivity), so the breadth test is unaddressed.

---

## After — Communication abstract (concise, urgent, single high-impact result)

Per `jacs-length-management`, this advance is a single, clean, high-impact result
whose full proof can live in the SI — a **Communication**.

> **Site- and enantioselective fluorination of unactivated C(sp³)–H bonds remains
> an unsolved problem because no catalyst differentiates the enantiotopic hydrogens
> of an isolated methylene.** *(problem, framed for broad interest.)* We report a
> confined chiral phosphate catalyst **1** that fluorinates secondary C–H bonds in
> unactivated alkanes in up to **92% yield and 94% ee** across **23 substrates**,
> with a turnover number of **480**. *(advance + key quantitative evidence — hard
> numbers, scope, TON.)* Mechanistic and kinetic data are consistent with
> enantiodetermining hydrogen-atom transfer within the catalyst pocket rather than
> free-radical diffusion. *(evidence for the mechanistic basis; hedged to match the
> data.)* Because selectivity derives from a substrate-independent confined pocket,
> the method installs a fluorine stereocenter at a position previously inaccessible
> to asymmetric catalysis, **enabling late-stage ¹⁸F labeling of saturated
> scaffolds.** *(significance — the enabling-utility breadth driver, stated as what
> is now possible.)*

**TOC / abstract graphic (described, not drawn).** Per `jacs-figures`, the TOC
encodes the advance in one glance: alkane C–H bond → product with a **bold wedge
C–F stereocenter**, catalyst **1** over the arrow, and **94% ee** beside the
product. It shows the central claim (enantioselective C–H → C–F), not a decorative
reaction collage.

---

## After — Article opening (when scope + mechanism are the story)

Per `jacs-length-management`, if the paper instead carries a full scope table,
several mechanistic probes, and characterized intermediates, it is an **Article**;
the same advance is framed as a complete account. The opening paragraph still
leads with the advance, tightly (`jacs-writing-style` section discipline:
Introduction frames the gap and ends with what the paper shows).

> Selective C–H functionalization has reshaped how complex molecules are built, yet
> **asymmetric** functionalization of *unactivated* secondary C–H bonds is still
> rare: existing catalysts rely on a neighboring directing group or an activated
> position, so an isolated methylene in a simple alkane has remained off-limits.
> *(gap, with the closest prior art's limitation stated as the delta.)* Here we
> show that a confined chiral phosphate, **1**, renders the two hydrogens of such a
> methylene distinguishable, delivering enantioenriched alkyl fluorides directly
> from hydrocarbons. We establish the scope (23 substrates, up to 94% ee), probe
> the mechanism (kinetics, radical clocks, and a characterized catalyst–substrate
> adduct), and demonstrate late-stage labeling of a saturated drug scaffold.
> *(ends with exactly what the paper shows — the `jacs-writing-style` rule that the
> introduction ends with the thesis.)*

---

## Why the "after" meets the JACS bar

Mapped to this pack's checklists:

- **Abstract is problem → advance → number → significance** with hard numbers
  (92% yield, 94% ee, 23 substrates, TON 480) — `jacs-results-framing` abstract
  discipline and its "at least one hard quantitative result" checklist item.
- **Every booster replaced by a fact** — no "novel/excellent/mild/clean/
  unprecedented" survives; conditions and numbers carry the claims
  (`jacs-writing-style` booster blacklist).
- **Breadth driver named** — *enabling utility* (a fluorine stereocenter and ¹⁸F
  labeling now possible), satisfying the `jacs-scope-fit` breadth test, not just
  the advance test.
- **Delta vs prior art stated** — "no neighboring directing group / activated
  position required," matching the `jacs-writing-style` rule to state the closest
  prior art and the specific delta, and `jacs-results-framing` "cite the closest
  prior art and state the delta in one line."
- **Claim strength matched to evidence** — "consistent with ... rather than free
  radical" is hedged where the data are mechanistic-suggestive, per
  `jacs-writing-style` claim discipline.
- **Format chosen explicitly** — Communication vs Article decided on
  urgency/single-result vs scope+mechanism, per `jacs-length-management`'s decision
  table, with full proof routed to the SI for the Communication.
- **TOC graphic encodes the advance** — the C–H → C–F stereocenter with ee, per
  `jacs-figures` ("TOC graphic encodes the advance") and `jacs-results-framing`
  ("the TOC/abstract graphic must visually encode the central advance").
