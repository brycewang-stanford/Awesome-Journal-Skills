# IO Official Source Map

Primary and authoritative sources behind the *International Organization* (IO)-specific facts injected
into this skill pack. Access date for all entries: **2026-06-02**. Re-verify volatile items (editors

## Official journal, submission & author pages

- **https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/international-organization/information/author-instructions/preparing-your-materials**
  — Cambridge Core author instructions (preparing your materials). Verifies: **Research Articles
  maximum 14,000 words**; **Research Notes maximum 8,000 words**; **Essays maximum 10,000 words**;
  word limits **include tables, figures and notes** but **do not include the bibliography/reference
  list**; authors submit an **abstract, word count, and acknowledgments separately** from the
  manuscript; **double-blind** review — **omit self-references after the title page** and refer to
  your own work in the **third person** (e.g., "Matthews 2022," not "Author"); **supplementary
  material should rarely exceed twenty pages**, especially on initial submission; **ORCID** registration
  for **corresponding authors**; accepted manuscripts use **short author-date footnotes** rather than
  parenthetical references; submission via **Editorial Manager**.
- **https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/international-organization/information/author-instructions**
  — Author instructions landing page. Verifies: IO "covers the entire field of international affairs"
  and features "articles that propose generalizable theories"; an international (or cross-border)
  phenomenon must be a **major cause or effect**; directs authors to the Research transparency policy
  before submission.
- **https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/international-organization**
  — IO on Cambridge Core (publisher home, current issues, ISSN 0020-8183 print / 1531-5088 online).

## Research transparency & data policy (the IO signature)

- **https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/international-organization/information/journal-policies/research-transparency**
  — IO Research Transparency policy. Verifies: **authors do not provide data or command files on
  initial submission**; the editorial staff **request data and command files at conditional
  acceptance**; **prior to final acceptance, results of papers using quantitative data and proofs of
  formal models are verified by IO staff** — editors **do not issue final acceptance until all results
  of all reported analyses are confirmed**; authors **upload quantitative datasets and supporting files
  to the IO Dataverse** on final acceptance, which mints a **DOI** to be included in the published
  article; qualitative work is **strongly encouraged** to deposit at the **Qualitative Data Repository
  (QDR)** at Syracuse; a **Data Availability Statement** is **required for quantitative articles** (and
  encouraged for qualitative), appearing before the reference list. (Re-verify the exact wording and
  any DA-RT/JETS references on the live page — 需复核 on verbatim policy text.)
- **https://dataverse.harvard.edu/dataverse/IOJ** — **International Organization (IO) Journal
  Dataverse** on **Harvard Dataverse**. Verifies: replication materials for IO articles are hosted on
  Harvard Dataverse (the IO Dataverse).

## Scope, owner, publisher, editors, history

- **https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/international-organization/information/about-this-journal**
  — About this journal. Verifies: published by **Cambridge University Press on behalf of the
  International Organization Foundation**; "a leading peer-reviewed journal that covers the entire field
  of international affairs"; features "articles that propose generalizable theories, improve social
  scientific knowledge, and/or offer new empirical insights on the broad range of subjects that
  constitute international relations and global politics"; **quarterly (4 issues/year)**;
  ISSN 0020-8183 / 1531-5088.
- **https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_Organization_(journal)** — Verifies: founded **1947**
  (originally by the World Peace Foundation); **considered the leading journal in the field of
  international relations**; **2022 impact factor 7.8**, ranked **1st of 96 in International Relations**
  and **3rd of 187 in Political Science** per Journal Citation Reports; began with a **United Nations**
  focus and **expanded to a general IR journal**; **credited with helping establish international
  political economy (IPE)** as a subfield and with theoretical pluralism (rationalist and constructivist
  alongside realist and liberal); past editors include **Robert Keohane, Peter Katzenstein, Stephen
  Krasner, Lisa Martin, Erik Voeten**.
