## How to use this library

Pick the row whose **field × method** is closest to yours, then study how that paper makes a **specific,
rigorous** result carry a **broad, general-scientific** lesson — the *Science* bar (see
[`../../skills/sci-fit/SKILL.md`](../../skills/sci-fit/SKILL.md) and
[`../../skills/sci-framing/SKILL.md`](../../skills/sci-framing/SKILL.md)). For each, ask the self-check
question before claiming your manuscript is "*Science*-shaped."

## By field × method

### Molecular biology / biochemistry — engineered enzyme as a tool (Research Article)

- **Jinek, Chylinski, Fonfara, Hauer, Doudna & Charpentier, "A Programmable Dual-RNA–Guided DNA
  Endonuclease in Adaptive Bacterial Immunity," *Science* 2012, 337(6096):816–821.**
  Verified: DOI `10.1126/science.1225829` (`science.org/doi/10.1126/science.1225829`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a precise biochemical result (a two-RNA-guided endonuclease, reprogrammed
    as a single guide) is framed as a **general, programmable capability** — the advance, not the assay,
    leads. The classic `sci-framing` move: "we discovered that S does X," with the broad tool-for-the-field
    implication on the surface.
  - **Self-check:** does your mechanism generalize into a capability a non-specialist can name, rather
    than a characterization of one system?

### Paleogenomics / human evolution — population-scale sequencing (Research Article)

- **Green, Krause, Briggs, … Pääbo et al., "A Draft Sequence of the Neandertal Genome," *Science* 2010,
  328(5979):710–722.**
  Verified: DOI `10.1126/science.1188021` (`science.org/doi/10.1126/science.1188021`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a new dataset at a new scale (a draft archaic genome) answers an old, broad
    question — did archaic and modern humans interbreed? — that a geneticist, an anthropologist, and the
    general reader all care about. The `sci-fit` general-interest test, passed by data scale + broad
    question.
  - **Self-check:** do genuinely new data let you settle a question outside your subfield, not just add
    resolution within it?

### Astrophysics / particle astronomy — large-detector observation (Research Article)

- **IceCube Collaboration (Aartsen et al.), "Evidence for High-Energy Extraterrestrial Neutrinos at the
  IceCube Detector," *Science* 2013, 342(6161):1242856.**
  Verified: DOI `10.1126/science.1242856` (`science.org/doi/10.1126/science.1242856`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a first-of-its-kind observation (astrophysical neutrinos above the
    atmospheric background) opens a new observational window — a "reveals a previously unknown capability"
    result (`sci-fit` rung 4) framed around what the universe can now show us, not the instrument's
    engineering.
  - **Self-check:** is the headline a phenomenon the broad community has wanted to observe, with the
    instrument demoted to the means (`sci-framing`: avoid method-led framing)?

### Condensed-matter physics / materials — new material, transport measurement (Report)

- **Novoselov, Geim, Morozov, Jiang, Zhang, Dubonos, Grigorieva & Firsov, "Electric Field Effect in
  Atomically Thin Carbon Films," *Science* 2004, 306(5696):666–669.**
  Verified: DOI `10.1126/science.1102896` (`science.org/doi/10.1126/science.1102896`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a short, decisive Report — an atomically thin material with a measurable
    field effect — told quickly, with the broad implication (a new 2D electronic platform) on the surface.
    A model of `sci-writing` length discipline: one central advance, told in Report form.
  - **Self-check:** can your single decisive advance be told in a Report (~2,500 words, ≤4 items) without
    losing the broad point?

### Earth & environmental science — global remote sensing / data product (Research Article)

- **Hansen, Potapov, Moore, Hancher, … et al., "High-Resolution Global Maps of 21st-Century Forest Cover
  Change," *Science* 2013, 342(6160):850–853.**
  Verified: DOI `10.1126/science.1244693` (`science.org/doi/10.1126/science.1244693`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a planetary-scale measurement (global forest loss/gain at 30 m) turns a
    method (satellite analysis at scale) into a **broadly usable fact and data product** — the advance is
    the global picture and the shared resource, not the pipeline. Pairs with the data-deposition norms in
    [`../external_tools.md`](../external_tools.md).
  - **Self-check:** does your measurement deliver a single, broadly legible quantity (here, where and how
    much forest changed) that many communities can use?

### Computational social science — large-scale observational analysis (Report)

- **Vosoughi, Roy & Aral, "The Spread of True and False News Online," *Science* 2018, 359(6380):1146–1151.**
  Verified: DOI `10.1126/science.aap9559` (`science.org/doi/10.1126/science.aap9559`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a large observational study of information diffusion frames a quantitative
    contrast (false vs. true news spread) as a **society-wide** finding — the `sci-fit` general-interest
    test met by a question any reader feels, with the advance (falsehood spreads faster/farther) stated
    up front.
  - **Self-check:** does your large-scale analysis answer a question the general public would recognize,
    with the headline contrast quantified and led-with?

## Omitted for sibling-venue or non-*Science* placement (do not attribute to *Science*)

To keep the list zero-hallucination, the following were **excluded** after web verification — each
resolved to a venue that is **not *Science***. They are listed only as guardrails against
sibling-confusion:

- **Watson & Crick, "Molecular Structure of Nucleic Acids: A Structure for Deoxyribose Nucleic Acid"
  (1953)** — published in ***Nature*** 171(4356):737–738, DOI `10.1038/171737a0`. **Not *Science*.**
- **Riess et al., "Observational Evidence from Supernovae for an Accelerating Universe and a Cosmological
  Constant" (1998)** — published in ***The Astronomical Journal*** 116:1009–1038, DOI `10.1086/300499`.
  **Not *Science*.** (The companion Perlmutter et al. 1999 measurement appeared in *The Astrophysical
  Journal*, also not *Science*.)
- **Jumper et al., "Highly Accurate Protein Structure Prediction with AlphaFold" (2021)** — published in
  ***Nature*** 596(7873):583–589, DOI `10.1038/s41586-021-03819-2`. **Not *Science*.**

Before adding any new paper to this library, confirm it on `science.org` (or its `10.1126/science.*` DOI)
that the venue is ***Science*** itself — not *Nature*, *PNAS*, *Cell*, *Science Advances*, or a *Science*
family sub-journal — and update the access-date header. When in doubt, omit.
