# Worked Example: A *Science* Abstract + Opening (before → after)

This demonstrates the **Science arc** drawn from this pack's own skills: lead with the broad **advance**,
not the background (`sci-framing`); pass the **general-interest / significance** bar (`sci-fit`); hold the
short **Report** format with a **claim-first** opening (`sci-writing`); and deliver the two artifacts
*Science* requires — the **one-sentence summary** (≤ ~125 characters) and the **≤ ~125-word abstract**
(`sci-abstract`).

**Illustrative paper (fictional):** *"A single ion channel sets the cold-tolerance limit of pond
insects."* Fictional setting: a freshwater midge whose larvae survive sub-zero winters; a fictional
channel gene we call *ColdGate*. Treat it as a **Report** (`sci-writing`: ~2,500 words, ≤4 display items,
Methods in Supplementary Materials).

---

## Before (lab-report framing — buries the advance)

> **One-sentence summary:** Characterization of *ColdGate* in *Chironomus* larvae. *(restates the title;
> no finding.)*
>
> **Abstract.** Cold tolerance in insects has been studied extensively. Herein we report the cloning and
> functional characterization of *ColdGate*, a transient-receptor-potential (TRP) channel. We performed
> RNA-seq, patch-clamp electrophysiology, CRISPR knockout, and behavioral assays. Knockout larvae
> showed altered cold responses. Wild-type channels were gated by temperature in heterologous
> expression. These results may potentially suggest that *ColdGate* could be involved in cold sensing.
> Importantly, our work elucidates the molecular basis of thermal adaptation. Further work is needed to
> determine the generality of this mechanism across species.
>
> **Opening.** Little is known about the molecular basis of cold tolerance in insects. Many genes have
> been implicated in thermal physiology, and TRP channels are a large family with diverse roles. In this
> study, we applied electrophysiology and genome editing to *Chironomus* to investigate *ColdGate*. We
> describe its properties below.

**What's wrong (against this pack's skills):**

- **One-sentence summary paraphrases the title** and states no finding (`sci-abstract` anti-pattern).
- **Abstract opens with the field and the method recap**, not the stake or the advance (`sci-framing`:
  do not bury the advance below the fold; `sci-abstract`: open with the stake, not the organism/method).
- **No quantified headline result** — "altered cold responses" has no number, CI, or *P*
  (`sci-abstract` / `sci-statistics`: ≥1 quantified result; effect + uncertainty + *n*).
- **Jargon-blacklist hits**: "studied extensively," "Herein we report," "Importantly," "elucidates,"
  "may potentially suggest that it could," "Further work is needed" (`sci-abstract` blacklist; ends on a
  to-do instead of the implication).
- **Opening uses the "Little is known about…" and "we applied method M to system S" anti-frames**
  (`sci-framing` narrative anti-frames).
- **No "why now"** and **no broad lesson** a non-specialist could repeat (`sci-fit` general-interest
  test; `sci-framing` why-now checklist).

---

## After (Science arc — advance first, quantified, general significance)

> **One-sentence summary:** A single ion channel sets the temperature at which freshwater insects freeze,
> revealing one molecular switch behind cold limits. *(declarative; states the finding and its general
> point; not the title; ~118 characters — within the ≤ ~125 cap.)*
>
> **Abstract.** Why some animals survive freezing winters while close relatives cannot is a basic open
> question in physiology. We show that a single temperature-gated channel, *ColdGate*, sets the lethal
> cold limit of midge larvae. Deleting *ColdGate* lowered the temperature larvae survived by 6.2°C
> (95% CI 5.1–7.3; *n* = 40 larvae per genotype; *P* = 0.0007), and re-expressing it restored the limit.
> The channel opens sharply near each population's local winter minimum, so the same switch is tuned to
> different climates. Because one tunable channel can move a survival threshold by several degrees, it
> identifies a candidate target for predicting how cold-adapted species will track a warming world.
> *(five moves — stake / question / approach + quantified result / mechanism / implication; one
> quantified headline effect with CI, n, and exact P; ~112 words, within the ≤ ~125-word cap; no
> subheadings, no citations.)*
>
> **Opening (Report; claim-first, ≈3 short paragraphs).**
>
> Whether an animal lives or dies through a hard freeze is one of the oldest questions in physiology, and
> it is becoming urgent: as winters shift, which species hold their cold limits and which fail is now a
> question about who survives climate change. *(the gap that matters broadly — `sci-framing` paragraph 1;
> the stake is legible to a non-specialist.)*
>
> The barrier has been that cold tolerance looked polygenic and diffuse, with no single handle to test.
> Genome editing in a freeze-surviving midge now lets us knock out and restore candidate genes in an
> animal whose larvae routinely endure sub-zero ponds — making the question tractable for the first time.
> *(why it has been hard / why now — `sci-framing` paragraph 2 and the why-now checklist: a new tool in a
> new system.)*
>
> We find that one temperature-gated channel, *ColdGate*, sets the lethal cold limit: removing it drops
> the survivable temperature by 6.2°C, restoring it rescues survival, and the channel's opening point
> tracks each population's local winter minimum. A single molecular switch, not a diffuse network, gates
> the threshold — and because the switch is tunable, it offers a concrete lever for forecasting how
> cold-limited species respond as the climate warms. *(what we did and found, ending on the broad
> implication — `sci-framing` paragraph 3; reader knows the result before the Results section.)*

## Why the "after" meets the *Science* bar

Mapped to this pack's skill checklists:

- **Advance leads, background follows** — the broad claim is in the first 150 words of both the abstract
  and the opening (`sci-framing`: the broad claim belongs in the first 150 words; do not bury below the
  fold).
- **One-sentence advance is fillable** — "We show that **one channel sets an insect's cold limit**, which
  means **a single tunable switch may forecast climate-driven range shifts**" (`sci-framing` one-sentence
  advance, both brackets filled without jargon); it seeds the one-sentence summary and the last line of
  the intro.
- **Significance is demonstrated, not asserted** — the deletion/rescue plus population-tuning result gives
  the broad climate implication an evidentiary basis, clearing `sci-fit` (rung 4: reveals a previously
  unknown mechanism) and the general-interest test (a physiologist *and* a climate scientist would care).
- **One quantified headline result** carries magnitude + unit + uncertainty + *n* + exact *P*
  (6.2°C; 95% CI 5.1–7.3; *n* = 40; *P* = 0.0007) — `sci-abstract` quantification check and
  `sci-statistics` reporting backbone.
- **Format discipline** — a Report opening with claim-first paragraphs and Methods deferred to
  Supplementary Materials (`sci-writing`: Methods are not in a Report's main text; structure as argument,
  not chronology).
- **Jargon and anti-frames removed** — no "Herein," "Importantly," "elucidate," "Little is known about,"
  "we applied M to S," and the abstract ends on the **implication**, not "further work is needed"
  (`sci-abstract` blacklist; `sci-framing` anti-frame table).
