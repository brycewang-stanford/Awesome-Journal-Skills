# JAAR Official Source Map

Primary and authoritative sources behind the JAAR-specific facts injected into this skill pack.
Access date for all entries: **2026-06-01**. Re-verify volatile items (editors and editorial term,
exact word/abstract caps, citation-style edition, fee/APC, book-review contacts, accepted formats)
on the official page before relying on them — journal pages change.

## Official journal, submission & author pages

- **https://academic.oup.com/jaar/pages/Information_To_Authors**
  — JAAR Information to Authors (Oxford Academic). Verifies: papers should be **roughly 8,000–12,000
  words in length (including references and footnotes)**; an **abstract of not more than 150 words**
  must accompany each manuscript; **double-blind review** (authors do not know reviewers and reviewers
  do not know authors); the article must speak to a question of **"broad and fundamental interest to
  the study of religion"** and reach **beyond the sub-specialty** out of which it is written — *"If, in
  the editor's opinion, this reframing has not taken place, your submission will be returned to you for
  reframing prior to being sent out for peer review."*; the editorial office reads every submission and
  decides whether to send it for review or that it is better served by another journal; **it may take up
  to six months** to a final decision.
- **https://academic.oup.com/jaar/pages/General_Instructions**
  — JAAR General Instructions / Author Guidelines. Verifies: links to the downloadable **Style Guide**
  and to instructions for submitting online; submission via **mc.manuscriptcentral.com/jaarel**
  (ScholarOne / Manuscript Central).
- **https://academic.oup.com/jaar/pages/Submission_Online**
  — Instructions for submitting online. Verifies: the online submission system at
  **http://mc.manuscriptcentral.com/jaarel**; on upload, the document designations include **Main
  Document (anonymous)**, Figure, Table, Supplementary File, and **Title Page** (i.e., an anonymized
  main document plus a separate title page for double-blind review).
- **https://academic.oup.com/DocumentLibrary/jaar/jaar%20author%20style.pdf**
  — "Guidelines for Manuscripts Submitted to the Journal of the American Academy of Religion" + JAAR
  Style Sheet. Verifies: **rejection rate of about 90%** (≈ eight articles per issue, ≈ thirty-two per
  year); the **"broad and fundamental"** framing demand and the requirement that an article *"have a
  point"* (analysis over description); **double-blind review**; **CITATIONS AND REFERENCES: use in-text
  citations, not footnotes** — footnotes are fine for points outside the body of the article but not for
  citations; alphabetized, year-keyed reference list (e.g., `(Wolf 1996: 33)`, `Clarkson notes … (1995:
  45–46)`); serial comma; gender-neutral language; italicize foreign words; *"For issues not addressed
  on this sheet, please consult the Chicago Manual of Style, 15th edition."*

## Scope, owner, publisher, and "why publish"

- **https://academic.oup.com/jaar/pages/why-publish**
  — Why submit to JAAR. Verifies: published **on behalf of the American Academy of Religion (AAR)**, the
  **largest scholarly society dedicated to the academic study of religion**; publishes **top scholarly
  articles covering the full range of world religious traditions** plus **studies of the methodologies
  by which these traditions are explored**; a **book review section** in each issue; *"widely considered
  to be the top academic journal in the field of religious studies, with a global readership"*;
  **double-anonymized peer review** with an average time to first decision of **106 days (as of 2022)**;
  optional **open access** license with an OA charge alongside the standard license, and OUP Read &
  Publish agreements.
- **https://academic.oup.com/jaar/pages/About_the_American_Academy_of_Religion**
  — About the AAR. Verifies: owner = **American Academy of Religion**; publisher = **Oxford University
  Press**.
- **https://academic.oup.com/jaar/pages/book_reviewer_guidelines**
  — Book Reviewer Guidelines. Verifies: JAAR **does not accept unsolicited book reviews** and **does not
  publish book reviews from graduate students**; reviews are **commissioned** through the **Book Review
  Editor**; the editorial office will not field queries about coverage of submitted books.
