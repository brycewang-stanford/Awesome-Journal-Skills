# JAAR Resources

Action-oriented resources for the *Journal of the American Academy of Religion* (JAAR) skill pack. The
`skills/` give advice; this directory lets an agent **model** a JAAR-style section and **benchmark**
against verified exemplars. Pair these with the relevant `skills/jaar-*/SKILL.md`.

> **No code kit — this is a humanities venue.** JAAR is the leading general journal in **religious
> studies**: its articles are interpretive humanities scholarship (primary texts, traditions, history,
> material/visual culture, fieldwork), with **no datasets, statistics, replication code, or hypothesis
> tests**. There is deliberately no `code/` directory here; the analogue of a "method kit" is the
> source-and-method craft in the skills and the exemplars below.

## What's here

| Resource | Use it to... |
| --- | --- |
| [`worked-examples/01-introduction.md`](worked-examples/01-introduction.md) | See a before→after rewrite of a religious-studies article introduction in JAAR house style — a contestable point of broad and fundamental interest, method-conscious and non-confessional, primary sources read closely, **in-text author-date citations** (no data, no number). Illustrative fictional article. |
| [`exemplars/library.md`](exemplars/library.md) | Benchmark against **real, web-verified JAAR articles** organized by subfield × method. Positioning and craft signal only — no paraphrased findings, no invented quotations. |
| [`official-source-map.md`](official-source-map.md) | **JAAR-specific policy & facts:** Information to Authors (word count, 150-word abstract, double-blind review, the "broad and fundamental interest" / reframing requirement), ScholarOne submission, the in-text author-date style sheet, and editors/scope. The authoritative pack source. |
| [`external_tools.md`](external_tools.md) | External tools and primary-source databases for the study of religion referenced by the pack. |

## Suggested workflow

1. Scope and frame with [`../skills/jaar-topic-selection`](../skills/jaar-topic-selection/SKILL.md) and
   [`../skills/jaar-scholarly-positioning`](../skills/jaar-scholarly-positioning/SKILL.md); sharpen the
   thesis with [`../skills/jaar-argument-development`](../skills/jaar-argument-development/SKILL.md).
2. Make the framework load-bearing with
   [`../skills/jaar-theory-and-method`](../skills/jaar-theory-and-method/SKILL.md) and ground it in
   primary materials via [`../skills/jaar-sources-and-evidence`](../skills/jaar-sources-and-evidence/SKILL.md).
3. Model the introduction on [`worked-examples/01-introduction.md`](worked-examples/01-introduction.md),
   then polish prose and references with
   [`../skills/jaar-writing-style`](../skills/jaar-writing-style/SKILL.md) and
   [`../skills/jaar-citation-and-style`](../skills/jaar-citation-and-style/SKILL.md) (in-text author-date,
   not footnotes).
4. Benchmark against verified JAAR articles in [`exemplars/library.md`](exemplars/library.md); confirm
   submission policy, scope, and style in [`official-source-map.md`](official-source-map.md).
