## How to use this library

Pick the row whose **subfield × method** is closest to yours, then study how that article carries a
*specific* case or reading into a claim of **broad and fundamental interest to the study of religion** —
the JAAR bar (see [`../../skills/jaar-topic-selection/SKILL.md`](../../skills/jaar-topic-selection/SKILL.md)
and [`../../skills/jaar-argument-development/SKILL.md`](../../skills/jaar-argument-development/SKILL.md)).
For each, ask the self-check question before claiming your piece is "JAAR-shaped." Model your own
introduction on [`../worked-examples/01-introduction.md`](../worked-examples/01-introduction.md).

## By method / subfield

### Theory & method of the discipline (reflexive methodological essay)

- **Furey, Constance M., "Body, Society, and Subjectivity in Religious Studies," JAAR 2012, 80(1):7–33.**
  Verified: `academic.oup.com/jaar/article/80/1/7/1008659`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** it does not study one tradition; it argues about how the field's "turn to
    the body" reshaped the study of religion and warns that it can re-inscribe an individualist subject —
    a claim every subfield must reckon with. The model of a piece whose contribution is a **category /
    method correction** (`jaar-theory-and-method`, `jaar-argument-development`).
  - **Self-check:** does your argument change how *other* scholars of religion work, not only how this
    tradition is read?

### Philosophy of method / historiography of religion (critical essay)

- **Roberts, Tyler, "Between the Lines: Exceeding Historicism in the Study of Religion," JAAR 2006, 74(3):697–719.**
  Verified: `academic.oup.com/jaar/article-abstract/74/3/697/895707` (DOI `10.1093/jaarel/lfj092`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** it intervenes in a field-wide debate about historicism's limits rather than
    a single case — a contestable thesis about *how we should study religion at all*, which is the
    "broad and fundamental interest" JAAR demands (`jaar-scholarly-positioning`).
  - **Self-check:** are you moving a live methodological debate the whole field recognizes, or only adding
    to one area's literature?

### Comparison & classification (theoretical / disciplinary)

- **Segal, Robert A., "Classification and Comparison in the Study of Religion: The Work of Jonathan Z. Smith," JAAR 2005, 73(4):1175–1188.**
  Verified: `academic.oup.com/jaar/article-abstract/73/4/1175/761800`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** it treats comparison and classification — the discipline's load-bearing
    operations — as the object of argument, exactly the "studies of the methods by which religion is
    explored" JAAR publishes (`jaar-theory-and-method`).
  - **Self-check:** if you compare, have you justified the *basis* of comparison rather than relying on
    decontextualized analogy?

### Religion, colonialism & the category "religion" (critical / postcolonial)

- **Johnson, Sylvester A., "Religions in All Ages and Places: Discerning Colonialism with Jonathan Z. Smith," JAAR 2019, 87(1):30–36.**
  Verified: `academic.oup.com/jaar/article-abstract/87/1/30/5288004` (DOI `10.1093/jaarel/lfy047`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** it puts the loaded category "religion" under scrutiny through the lens of
    colonialism — using contested terms critically, not as natural kinds, the move
    `jaar-theory-and-method` foregrounds.
  - **Self-check:** are your central categories (religion, myth, the secular) examined critically rather
    than assumed?

### Secularity / the secular (conceptual essay)

- **Furani, Khaled, "Is There a Postsecular?," JAAR 2015, 83(1):1–26.**
  Verified: `academic.oup.com/jaar/article-abstract/83/1/1/690265` (DOI `10.1093/jaarel/lfu082`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a tightly argued conceptual intervention on "the postsecular" that bears on
    every subfield touching secularity and modernity — broad interest carried by a sharp, contestable
    thesis (`jaar-argument-development`, `jaar-topic-selection`).
  - **Self-check:** can a scholar outside your area state your thesis and see why it matters in one
    paragraph?

### Gender, feminist theory & religion (theory-driven)

- **Gottschall, Marilyn, "The Ethical Implications of the Deconstruction of Gender," JAAR 2002, 70(2):279–299.**
  Verified: `academic.oup.com/jaar/article-abstract/70/2/279/756865`
  (full text PDF: `academic.oup.com/jaar/article-pdf/70/2/279/2275852/700279.pdf`). The Oxford Academic
  byline lists **M. Gottschall** (Whittier College), and the JAAR 70(2), June 2002 table of contents
  lists **Marilyn Gottschall** among the issue's authors.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** it brings feminist/gender theory (Butler) to bear on the ethical subject in
    religious studies — a piece whose contribution is a **theoretical lens portable across traditions**,
    the cross-field move in `jaar-scholarly-positioning`.
  - **Self-check:** does your theoretical apparatus do load-bearing work, or is it name-dropped in the
    intro and absent from the analysis?

### Sibling-journal guard — checked and NOT in JAAR

Several frequently-cited religious-studies essays are routinely *assumed* to be "in JAAR" but were
verified to belong to a **different** journal. They are listed here only so they are never misattributed:

- **Jonathan Z. Smith, "The Bare Facts of Ritual" (1980)** — verified in ***History of Religions***
  20(1/2):112–127 (University of Chicago Press), **not JAAR**.
- **Mircea Eliade & Joseph M. Kitagawa's programmatic essays on method** appear largely in
  ***History of Religions*** (Chicago) and edited volumes, **not JAAR**.
- **Wilfred Cantwell Smith / Talal Asad's foundational pieces on "religion" as a category** circulate via
  monographs and journals such as ***History of Religions*** and ***Religion***, **not JAAR** — confirm the
  venue before citing any as a JAAR article.
- General rule: a paper in the *Journal of Religion*, *History of Religions* (both Chicago), *Harvard
  Theological Review*, *Religion* (Routledge), *Numen* (Brill), or the *Journal of the American Oriental
  Society* is **not** a JAAR exemplar.

Before adding any new article to this library, confirm it on `academic.oup.com/jaar` (article page with
author byline + volume/issue and a `10.1093/jaarel/...` DOI) and update the access-date header. When in
doubt, omit.
