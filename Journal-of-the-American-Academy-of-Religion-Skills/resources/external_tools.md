# External Tools & Resources for JAAR-Style Religious-Studies Scholarship

Research tools, databases, and references useful when preparing a *Journal of the American Academy of
Religion* (JAAR) article. JAAR is a **humanities** venue in the academic study of religion: scholarship
is textual, historical, philosophical/ethical, comparative, or ethnographic — **not** statistical or
replication-based. The tools below support primary-source work, citation, and method, not data analysis.
Verify any JAAR-specific policy in [`official-source-map.md`](official-source-map.md).

## 1. Primary sources, texts & databases

| Resource | Use |
|----------|-----|
| ATLA Religion Database (EBSCO) | Core index for religion scholarship and book reviews |
| Index Theologicus / Index Islamicus | Specialist bibliographies (Christian theology; Islamic studies) |
| TLG, Perseus, CCEL | Classical, patristic, and Christian primary texts |
| SARIT, GRETIL, CBETA | Indic / Sanskrit and Chinese Buddhist canonical corpora |
| Sefaria, Tanzil/Quran.com, Pāli Tipiṭaka | Jewish, Islamic, and Buddhist scriptural corpora |
| Archive.org, HathiTrust, national/manuscript archives | Historical documents and rare materials |

> Cite from primary sources directly (see `jaar-sources-and-evidence`); note editions and translators.

## 2. Languages, translation & transliteration
- Lexicons and dictionaries for the relevant tradition-languages (e.g., Greek, Latin, Hebrew, Arabic,
  Sanskrit, Pāli, Classical Chinese, Tibetan)
- A **consistent transliteration system** per subfield (ALA-LC romanization tables; IAST for Sanskrit;
  pinyin for Chinese) — keep it consistent throughout (see `jaar-citation-and-style`)
- Note the edition/translation you rely on; supply originals where the argument turns on wording

## 3. Theory & method references
- Method-in-the-study-of-religion literature (phenomenological, historical-critical, social-theoretical,
  feminist/postcolonial, cognitive, ritual studies, comparative method)
- Reflexivity and the critique of categories (religion, myth, ritual, mysticism, the secular) — JAAR
  publishes studies *of* methodology, so engage it explicitly (see `jaar-theory-and-method`)
- Comparative method: justify the basis of comparison; avoid decontextualized analogy

## 4. Reference management & citation
- **Zotero** (with ATLA / library connectors), EndNote, BibTeX — but note JAAR uses **in-text
  author-date** citations, **not** footnote citations; footnotes are for substance only
- Build an **alphabetized, year-keyed reference list** per the JAAR style sheet
- Fallback for unaddressed issues: **Chicago Manual of Style** (confirm the current edition the live
  style sheet cites — the downloadable sheet references the 15th edition; 待核实)

## 5. Writing & manuscript prep
- Word processor producing **.docx**; prepare an **anonymized main document** + a separate **title page**
  for double-blind review
- House conventions: **gender-neutral language**, **serial (Oxford) comma**, **italicize foreign words**
- Abstract **≤ 150 words**; article roughly **8,000–12,000 words including references and footnotes**

## 6. Fieldwork & ethics (for ethnographic work)
- IRB/ethics review, informed consent, and confidentiality for human participants
- Positionality and reflexivity documentation; fair representation of interlocutors

## 7. Process & portal
| Item | Note |
|------|------|
| Submission portal | **ScholarOne / Manuscript Central**, `mc.manuscriptcentral.com/jaarel` |
| Owner / publisher | **American Academy of Religion (AAR)** / **Oxford University Press** |
| Review model | **Double-blind (double-anonymous)** |
| Length | **~8,000–12,000 words** incl. references + footnotes (待核实 exact range) |
| Abstract | **≤ 150 words** |
| Citations | **In-text author-date**; footnotes substantive only; CMOS fallback |
| Framing gate | Must address **broad and fundamental interest to the study of religion** or be returned to reframe |
| Book reviews | **Commissioned only**; no unsolicited reviews; none from graduate students |

## 8. Cautions
1. **Verify volatile specifics** (editors/term, exact length, CMOS edition, OA APC) on the official
   OUP/AAR pages — they change; unverified items are marked 待核实.
2. **Reframe for broad significance** before submitting — subfield-only framing is returned pre-review.
3. **Use in-text author-date citations**, not footnote citations — the most common JAAR style error.
4. **Anonymize** the main document and strip metadata; keep the title page separate.
5. **Stay non-confessional** — analyze the study of religion; do not advocate for a tradition.
