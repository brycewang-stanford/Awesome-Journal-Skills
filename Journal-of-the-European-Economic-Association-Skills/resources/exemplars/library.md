# JEEA Exemplars Library (method × topic)

> **Verified via web search, access date 2026-06.** Every paper below was checked to confirm it actually
> appeared in the ***Journal of the European Economic Association*** (the EEA's general-interest
> journal, published by Oxford University Press; Online ISSN 1542-4774). JEEA's DOI stem is **`10.1093/jeea…`**
> for the OUP era and the older **`10.1111/j.1542-4774…`** for the earlier Wiley-Blackwell era — both are
> JEEA. Year, volume(issue), and pages were read off the OUP / publisher article pages where available.
> Papers whose exact volume/issue/pages lacked full confirmation to that standard in one pass are kept
> off the clean list — this is deliberately a short, verified list rather than a long, uncertain one.
>
> **Sibling-confusion guard (do not misattribute).** JEEA is **not** any of these adjacent outlets:
> *The Economic Journal* (RES, Wiley/OUP), the *European Economic Review* (Elsevier), *Econometrica*
> (`10.3982/ECTA…`), or the *American Economic Review* (`10.1257/aer…`). Only a confirmed JEEA article page
> with the `10.1093/jeea` or `10.1111/j.1542-4774` stem confirms JEEA. The omissions section records a
> sibling-confusion guardrail.
>
> **Use principle (zero hallucination):** this file gives **design positioning only**. It does not reproduce
> or invent estimates, parameters, or specific findings — read the original before citing any number. For
> JEEA-specific policy, scope, and house rules, see [`../official-source-map.md`](../official-source-map.md).

## How to use this library

Pick the row whose **method × question** is closest to yours, then study how that paper makes a
*rigorous* result answer a *substantive, general-interest* economic question — the JEEA bar (see
[`../../skills/jeea-topic-selection/SKILL.md`](../../skills/jeea-topic-selection/SKILL.md) and
[`../../skills/jeea-writing-style/SKILL.md`](../../skills/jeea-writing-style/SKILL.md)). For each, ask the
self-check question before claiming your paper is "JEEA-shaped." Remember JEEA's house rule: report
**standard errors / confidence sets, never significance asterisks**
([`jeea-identification`](../../skills/jeea-identification/SKILL.md),
[`jeea-tables-figures`](../../skills/jeea-tables-figures/SKILL.md)).

## By method

### Macro theory / growth (analytic model, technical change)

- **Acemoglu, "Labor- and Capital-Augmenting Technical Change," JEEA 2003, 1(1):1–37.**
  Verified: inaugural-volume JEEA article (Wiley-Blackwell era, `10.1111/...` stem).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a disciplined theory paper that delivers a memorable, general lesson — when
    technical change is endogenous, its direction (labor- vs. capital-augmenting) is shaped by relative
    factor supplies — exactly the theory-with-a-traveling-lesson JEEA rewards. Routes to
    [`../../skills/jeea-theory-model/SKILL.md`](../../skills/jeea-theory-model/SKILL.md).
  - **Self-check:** does your model's headline result state an economic lesson in words before the algebra,
    and is the driving assumption named?

### Development / applied micro (capital misallocation)

- **Banerjee, Duflo & Munshi, "The (Mis)allocation of Capital," JEEA 2003, 1(2–3):484–494.**
  Verified: JEEA inaugural-volume article (Wiley-Blackwell era).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a development question (why is capital allocated inefficiently?) framed for a
    general-interest readership, combining evidence with economic reasoning — the question-first,
    lesson-that-travels shape JEEA wants. Connects to
    [`../../skills/jeea-identification/SKILL.md`](../../skills/jeea-identification/SKILL.md).
  - **Self-check:** is your development finding stated as a general lesson about how an economy works, not as
    a country-specific case study?

### Political economy / institutions (long-run, historical geography)

- **Alesina, Easterly & Matuszeski, "Artificial States," JEEA 2011, 9(2):246–277.**
  Verified: `onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/j.1542-4774.2010.01009.x` (DOI `10.1111/j.1542-4774.2010.01009.x`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** builds a measurable concept (states whose borders cut across ethnic/geographic
    lines) and links it to development outcomes — a general-interest political-economy result with a clean,
    portable measure. Pairs with
    [`../../skills/jeea-literature-positioning/SKILL.md`](../../skills/jeea-literature-positioning/SKILL.md).
  - **Self-check:** does your paper introduce a measure or concept a general reader can carry to other
    settings, with the contribution stated against the frontier?

### Applied micro / culture & the family (cross-country micro evidence)

- **Giuliano, "Living Arrangements in Western Europe: Does Cultural Origin Matter?," JEEA 2007, 5(5):927–952.**
  Verified: JEEA article (Wiley-Blackwell era), 5(5):927–952.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** uses immigrant cross-country variation to separate culture from economic
    environment in family living arrangements — an applied-micro design answering a broad question about the
    persistence of culture. Routes to
    [`../../skills/jeea-identification/SKILL.md`](../../skills/jeea-identification/SKILL.md).
  - **Self-check:** does your design isolate the mechanism (here, culture vs. environment) in a way a general
    reader finds credible and consequential?

### Macro-finance / sovereign debt (the 20-year retrospective lens)

- **"Reflecting on the First 20 Years of the Journal of the European Economic Association," JEEA 2023, 21(5):e1.**
  Verified: `academic.oup.com/jeea/article/21/5/e1/7241813` (OUP era, `10.1093/jeea` stem).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** not a research paper but the journal's own retrospective — useful to read for
    what JEEA's editors consider the journal's identity and the breadth of fields it spans (theory, macro,
    applied micro, finance, development). Use it to calibrate "is this a JEEA paper?"
    ([`../../skills/jeea-topic-selection/SKILL.md`](../../skills/jeea-topic-selection/SKILL.md)).
  - **Self-check:** does your paper sit inside the general-interest breadth the journal describes, rather
    than in a field-journal niche?

## Omitted for lack of full verification, or to avoid sibling-confusion (do not attribute to JEEA without re-checking)

To keep the list zero-hallucination, the following were **excluded** after checking:

- **Spolaore & Wacziarg, "The Diffusion of Development"** — a natural development/growth candidate, but web
  search confirms the well-known paper of that title is in the ***Quarterly Journal of Economics*** (2009,
  124(2):469–529), **not JEEA**. Listed here only as a sibling-confusion guardrail: confirm the JEEA article
  page and DOI stem before attributing any paper to JEEA.
- **Recent OUP-era pieces surfaced in search** (e.g., monetary-policy and pharmaceutical-market articles
  with `10.1093/jeea/jvag…` advance-article DOIs) were left off because their **final volume/issue/pages**
  were still advance-online or not confirmed to the same standard in one pass; add them only after
  reading the OUP article page and confirming volume/issue/pages.

Before adding any new paper to this library, confirm it on `academic.oup.com/jeea` (or the
Wiley-Blackwell page for pre-OUP articles) with volume/issue/pages **and** that the DOI begins
`10.1093/jeea` or `10.1111/j.1542-4774`, then update the access-date header. When in doubt, omit.
