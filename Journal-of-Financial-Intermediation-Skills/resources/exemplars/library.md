# *Journal of Financial Intermediation* Exemplars Library (method × topic)

> **Verified via web search, access date 2026-06-08.** Every paper below was checked to confirm it
> actually appeared in the **_Journal of Financial Intermediation_** (JFI) — the Elsevier banking-and-
> intermediation field journal (ISSN 1042-9573) — with year and volume/issue, against the publisher record
> on ScienceDirect (`sciencedirect.com/.../journal-of-financial-intermediation`), the IDEAS/RePEc series
> record (`ideas.repec.org/a/eee/jfinin/...`), EconPapers, and corroborating Google Scholar lookups
> carrying the journal name. Papers that could not be fully verified **as JFI** were **omitted** — this is
> deliberately a short, clean list, not a long, uncertain one.
>
> **Publisher / identity.** JFI is published by **Elsevier** and handled through Editorial Manager; it uses
> **author-date (Elsevier)** referencing with numbered sections, and publishes **both theory and empirics**
> of banking and intermediation. For JFI-specific scope, fees, blinding, and house style, see
> [`../official-source-map.md`](../official-source-map.md).
>
> **Sibling-journal guard (read this before citing anything).** JFI is a strong field journal, but it sits
> **below the top three** finance outlets — the **_Journal of Finance_ (JF)**, **_Review of Financial
> Studies_ (RFS)**, and **_Journal of Financial Economics_ (JFE)**. Many of the most famous intermediation
> papers — relationship-banking theory, deposit-insurance-and-stability, liquidity-creation theory,
> small-business relationship lending — are in those three, or in the **_American Economic Review_ (AER)**,
> the **_Journal of Monetary Economics_ (JME)**, the **_Journal of Business_**, **_JFQA_**, or the
> **_Journal of Banking & Finance_ (JBF)** — a sibling in this very repo. The RePEc code for JFI is
> `eee/jfinin`; an intermediation paper indexed under `bla/jfinan` (JF), `oup/rfinst` (RFS), `eee/jfinec`
> (JFE), or `eee/jbfina` (JBF) is **not** a JFI paper. The
> [Omitted](#omitted-for-lack-of-full-verification-do-not-attribute-to-jfi) section records the specific
> traps checked and rejected. When an intermediation paper "feels canonical," that is a signal to **suspect
> JF/RFS/JFE**, not JFI.
>
> **Use principle (zero hallucination):** this file gives **design positioning only**. It does not
> reproduce or invent coefficients, valuations, or specific findings — read the original on ScienceDirect
> before citing any number.

## How to use this library

Pick the row whose **method × intermediation question** is closest to yours, then study how that paper
turns a bank/credit quantity into a contribution that clears the JFI desk screen — *it must teach
something about intermediation: the institution, the friction, the mechanism, and the economic
consequence* (see
[`../../skills/jfi-topic-selection/SKILL.md`](../../skills/jfi-topic-selection/SKILL.md),
[`../../skills/jfi-contribution-framing/SKILL.md`](../../skills/jfi-contribution-framing/SKILL.md), and
[`../../skills/jfi-identification-strategy/SKILL.md`](../../skills/jfi-identification-strategy/SKILL.md)).
For each, ask the self-check question before claiming your paper is "JFI-shaped."

## By method

### Cross-country valuation regression linking ownership to value (governance / bank valuation)

- **Caprio, Laeven & Levine, "Governance and bank valuation," _Journal of Financial Intermediation_ 2007, 16(4):584–617.**
  Verified: RePEc `ideas.repec.org/a/eee/jfinin/v16y2007i4p584-617.html`; EconPapers `article/eeejfinin/v_3a16_3ay_3a2007`; SSRN `abstract_id=478667`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** relates **bank value** to ownership structure and cash-flow rights across
    countries, holding regulation fixed, so the contribution is about *the intermediary's* governance
    friction rather than generic firm governance — exactly the
    [`jfi-contribution-framing`](../../skills/jfi-contribution-framing/SKILL.md) demand that the paper teach
    something about the institution, not "banks as just another firm."
  - **Self-check:** does your result speak to a friction *specific to intermediaries* (here, owner cash-flow
    rights and bank value), legible to a banking referee?

### Institutional-memory test of a credit-cycle mechanism (lending standards / procyclicality)

- **Berger & Udell, "The institutional memory hypothesis and the procyclicality of bank lending behavior," _Journal of Financial Intermediation_ 2004, 13(4):458–495.**
  Verified: RePEc `ideas.repec.org/a/eee/jfinin/v13y2004i4p458-495.html`; ScienceDirect `pii/S1042957304000373`; SSRN `abstract_id=386622`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** proposes and tests a *named* mechanism — loan officers' fading memory of past
    defaults loosens standards as the cycle ages — and shows it in U.S. bank data, the
    [`jfi-identification-strategy`](../../skills/jfi-identification-strategy/SKILL.md) pattern of turning a
    behavioral story about the intermediary into a falsifiable, time-in-cycle prediction rather than a
    correlation.
  - **Self-check:** is your credit-cycle finding tied to a specific intermediary mechanism with an
    observable time signature, not just "lending is procyclical"?

### Bank board panel relating governance to performance (bank governance)

- **Adams & Mehran, "Bank board structure and performance: Evidence for large bank holding companies," _Journal of Financial Intermediation_ 2012, 21(2):243–267.**
  Verified: RePEc `ideas.repec.org/a/eee/jfinin/...`; SSRN `abstract_id=1945548`; Google Scholar (journal *Journal of Financial Intermediation*, vol. 21, pp. 243–267).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a long bank-holding-company **panel** arguing that board structure that suits
    *banks* (larger boards) differs from the non-financial-firm prescription, because banks' complexity and
    regulation change the monitoring problem — the
    [`jfi-topic-selection`](../../skills/jfi-topic-selection/SKILL.md) insistence that the paper be
    *centrally about banks*, not a generic governance regression that happens to use bank data.
  - **Self-check:** does your design exploit what makes the *intermediary* different (complexity,
    regulation, opacity), so the result would not simply transfer from a non-financial sample?

### Failure-prediction hazard model isolating an activity channel (bank failure / nontraditional activities)

- **DeYoung & Torna, "Nontraditional banking activities and bank failures during the financial crisis," _Journal of Financial Intermediation_ 2013, 22(3):397–421.**
  Verified: RePEc `ideas.repec.org/a/eee/jfinin/v22y2013i3p397-421.html`; ScienceDirect `pii/S1042957313000028`; SSRN `abstract_id=2032246`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a **bank-failure hazard model** that splits nontraditional income into
    *asset-based* (which raises failure risk) versus *pure fee-based* (which lowers it), so the contribution
    is a sharp activity-channel decomposition, not a blanket "nontraditional banking is risky" claim — the
    [`jfi-data-analysis`](../../skills/jfi-data-analysis/SKILL.md) discipline of decomposing the regressor
    until the mechanism is visible.
  - **Self-check:** when you split your risk driver into the sub-activities theory distinguishes, do the
    signs separate as the mechanism predicts — or does the aggregate hide offsetting channels?

## Omitted for lack of full verification (do not attribute to _JFI_ without re-checking)

To keep the list zero-hallucination, these strong candidates were **excluded** after web-checking — each
is a real, important paper, but **not in JFI** (the sibling-journal traps the brief warned of). Most famous
intermediation papers live in the top three (JF / RFS / JFE) or in AER / JME:

- **Boot & Thakor, "Can Relationship Banking Survive Competition?" (2000)** — verified in **_The Journal of
  Finance_** 55(2):679–713, **not JFI**. The canonical relationship-banking-vs-competition theory paper.
- **Petersen & Rajan, "The Benefits of Lending Relationships: Evidence from Small Business Data" (1994)** —
  verified in **_The Journal of Finance_** 49(1):3–37, **not JFI**. The foundational relationship-lending
  empirics.
- **Berger & Udell, "Relationship Lending and Lines of Credit in Small Firm Finance" (1995)** — verified in
  **_The Journal of Business_** 68(3):351–381, **not JFI**. (Berger & Udell *do* have a genuinely-JFI 2004
  paper above — different paper, different venue; do not merge them.)
- **Demirgüç-Kunt & Detragiache, "Does deposit insurance increase banking system stability? An empirical
  investigation" (2002)** — verified in the **_Journal of Monetary Economics_** 49(7):1373–1406, **not
  JFI**.
- **Gorton & Pennacchi, "Financial Intermediaries and Liquidity Creation" (1990)** — verified in **_The
  Journal of Finance_** 45(1):49–71, **not JFI**. The canonical liquidity-creation theory paper.
- **Berlin & Mester, "Deposits and Relationship Lending" (1999)** — verified in the **_Review of Financial
  Studies_** 12(3):579–607, **not JFI**.

Before adding any new paper to this library, confirm it on ScienceDirect (or the
`ideas.repec.org/a/eee/jfinin/...` RePEc record) with volume/issue, and update the access-date header.
When in doubt — especially when an intermediation paper "feels canonical" — **suspect JF/RFS/JFE and
omit**.
