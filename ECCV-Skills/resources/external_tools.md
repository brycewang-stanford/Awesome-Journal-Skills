# External Tools - ECCV

Access date: 2026-07-08

Open these surfaces before giving cycle-specific ECCV advice. ECCV rules are
rewritten every two years; direct fetches of `eccv.ecva.net` were blocked in the
verification environment (see the access-method note in
[`official-source-map.md`](official-source-map.md)), so confirm each page loads
for you directly.

## Five author-side verification passes

1. **Clock pass** — confirm each deadline's timezone on the Dates page. ECCV 2026
   used 11pm CET for the paper deadline and AoE for camera-ready; assuming one
   convention for both loses hours.
2. **Kit pass** — download the current author kit and rebuild the PDF from it;
   the LNCS template changed after 2024 and will change again. Check the page
   count with figures included, references excluded.
3. **Policy pass** — reread the dual-submission four-page definition, the
   codebase-citation ban, and the social-media embargo against your project's
   actual arXiv/repo/announcement state.
4. **Duty pass** — list every co-author's ECCV reviewing assignment and its
   deadline; 2026-style enforcement can desk-reject the paper for a co-author's
   late or LLM-written review.
5. **Record pass** — after acceptance, verify the paper on three surfaces:
   SpringerLink (DOI + metadata), the ECVA mirror (PDF + supplement), and your
   arXiv version (updated within Springer's self-archiving terms).

## Cross-check surfaces (when official pages are unreachable)

- dblp ECCV stream for venue attribution of any cited paper.
- SpringerLink volume front matter for edition numbers and acceptance counts.
- The OpenReview group page for live workflow state and forms.
- Deadline aggregators only for discovery, never as the final word — they
  disagreed by a day on the 2026 paper deadline due to timezone conversion.

## Do not infer

- Do not project 2026 dates, page rules, rebuttal format, or enforcement wording
  onto 2028; every one of them is chair-decided per cycle.
- Do not assume CVF open access serves ECCV papers — the free mirror is ECVA's.
- Do not treat an aggregator's AoE rendering of a CET deadline as official.
- When official pages conflict, the newest official page wins; record the
  conflict in the source map with its access date.
