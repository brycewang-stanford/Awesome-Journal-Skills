# ASQ Exemplars Library (theme × method)

> **Verified via web search, access date 2026-06-07.** Every paper below was checked to confirm it
> actually appeared in *Administrative Science Quarterly* (ASQ, founded 1956 at Cornell, published by
> SAGE), with author, year, and volume/issue/pages. Confirmations were drawn from the SAGE ASQ article
> pages (`journals.sagepub.com`), JSTOR stable records, and the publishers' own citation records.
> Papers that could **not** be confirmed as ASQ were **omitted** and documented at the bottom — this is
> deliberately a short, clean list (6 verified) rather than a long, uncertain one.
>
> **Sibling-confusion guardrail.** ASQ is routinely confused with its neighbors. The papers below are
> in ASQ specifically — **not** *Academy of Management Journal/Review*, *Organization Science*,
> *Strategic Management Journal*, *American Sociological Review*, *American Journal of Sociology*, or
> *Journal of Management*. Several landmark organizational papers people *assume* are ASQ are actually in
> *AJS* (see omissions). Always re-confirm the venue before citing.
>
> **Use principle (zero hallucination):** this file gives **design and framing positioning only**. It
> does not reproduce or invent findings, coefficients, or quotes — read the original before citing
> anything specific. For ASQ-specific policy and scope, see
> [`../official-source-map.md`](../official-source-map.md).

## How to use this library

Pick the row whose **theme × method** is closest to yours, then study how that paper turns a *rich
organizational context* into a *theoretical contribution that travels* — the ASQ bar (see
[`../../skills/asq-topic-selection/SKILL.md`](../../skills/asq-topic-selection/SKILL.md),
[`../../skills/asq-theory-development/SKILL.md`](../../skills/asq-theory-development/SKILL.md), and
[`../../skills/asq-writing-style/SKILL.md`](../../skills/asq-writing-style/SKILL.md)). For each, ask the
self-check question before claiming your paper is "ASQ-shaped." Note the spread of methods: ASQ treats a
single immersive ethnography and a large archival study as equally first-class.

## By method

### Formal model that reframes how organizations decide (organizational theory)

- **Cohen, March & Olsen, "A Garbage Can Model of Organizational Choice," ASQ 1972, 17(1):1–25.**
  Verified: JSTOR / publisher citation records confirm ASQ vol. 17, pp. 1–25.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a single counterintuitive image — problems, solutions, and participants
    sloshing in a "garbage can" rather than a means-ends sequence — gave the field a lens for
    "organized anarchies" it could not un-see. The archetype `asq-contribution-framing` names: a
    *reframing*, not a finding.
  - **Self-check:** does your core idea overturn the reader's default model of how organizations behave,
    statable in one vivid paragraph?

### Inductive ethnography of technology and structure (qualitative / observational)

- **Barley, "Technology as an Occasion for Structuring: Evidence from Observations of CT Scanners and the
  Social Order of Radiology Departments," ASQ 1986, 31(1):78–108.**
  Verified: ERIC EJ338774 and publisher citation records confirm ASQ vol. 31(1), pp. 78–108.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** theorizes *structuring as a process* — the same technology occasions
    different social orders in different departments — rather than estimating an effect of technology on
    structure. The model `asq-theory-development` points to for matching **process theory** to a process
    phenomenon. The fictional worked example
    [`../worked-examples/01-introduction.md`](../worked-examples/01-introduction.md) is built to echo
    this lineage.
  - **Self-check:** is your phenomenon an unfolding sequence you can show over time, or have you flattened
    a process into "factor A raised outcome B"?

### Interpretive analysis of a single critical case (qualitative / historical)

- **Weick, "The Collapse of Sensemaking in Organizations: The Mann Gulch Disaster," ASQ 1993,
  38(4):628–652.**
  Verified: JSTOR stable/2393339 confirms ASQ vol. 38(4), pp. 628–652.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a single historical case (a 1949 wildfire) carries a profound theoretical
    contribution about how sensemaking and structure collapse together — the canonical demonstration that
    *craft and narrative* can be the vehicle of theory at ASQ (`asq-writing-style`).
  - **Self-check:** can a single, deeply rendered case deliver a general theoretical insight, with the
    narrative *earning* the surprise rather than decorating it?

### Embeddedness and networks, ethnography + archival (mixed-method / sociological)

- **Uzzi, "Social Structure and Competition in Interfirm Networks: The Paradox of Embeddedness," ASQ
  1997, 42(1):35–67.**
  Verified: publisher citation records confirm ASQ vol. 42(1), pp. 35–67.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** names a *paradox* — embeddedness both enables and, past a threshold,
    cripples firms — and supports it by pairing fieldwork with network data. A model of a
    **boundary/contingency** contribution (`asq-contribution-framing`) in ASQ's economic-sociology
    wheelhouse.
  - **Self-check:** does your mechanism reverse or break at a boundary you can specify, rather than
    holding monotonically?

### Inductive ethnography of innovation and knowledge (qualitative / grounded)

- **Hargadon & Sutton, "Technology Brokering and Innovation in a Product Development Firm," ASQ 1997,
  42(4):716–749.**
  Verified: publisher citation records confirm ASQ vol. 42(4), pp. 716–749.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** an immersive study of one design firm yields a *named process model* —
    technology brokering across industries — that travels well beyond the case. The
    grounded-theory-to-process-model arc `asq-data-analysis` describes (data structure → mechanism →
    model).
  - **Self-check:** does your fieldwork produce a transferable process model with a name, or just a
    vivid description of one site?

### Status and quality in a market, quantitative archival (variance / sociological)

- **Benjamin & Podolny, "Status, Quality, and Social Order in the California Wine Industry," ASQ 1999,
  44(3):563–589.**
  Verified: SAGE `journals.sagepub.com/doi/abs/10.2307/2666962` confirms ASQ vol. 44(3), pp. 563–589.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a quantitative, archival study that nonetheless leads with a *sociological
    mechanism* — status shaping the relationship between quality and reward — showing ASQ publishes
    variance work when the theory, not the estimator, is the point (`asq-methods`).
  - **Self-check:** in your quantitative paper, is the mechanism the lead and the identification a means
    to it — or are you treating the technique as the contribution?

## Omitted for lack of ASQ confirmation (sibling-confusion traps — do not attribute to ASQ)

To keep the list zero-hallucination, these frequently-miscited organizational landmarks were **excluded**
after web verification because they are **not** in ASQ:

- **Hannan & Freeman, "The Population Ecology of Organizations" (1977)** — verified to be in the
  *American Journal of Sociology* 82(5):929–964, **not ASQ**. (Population-ecology work later appeared in
  ASQ, but this founding paper did not.) Listed only as a guardrail.
- **Zuckerman, "The Categorical Imperative: Securities Analysts and the Illegitimacy Discount" (1999)** —
  verified to be in the *American Journal of Sociology* 104(5):1398–1438, **not ASQ**. A canonical
  categories paper, but the venue is AJS.
- **Podolny, "A Status-Based Model of Market Competition" (1993)** — verified to be in the *American
  Journal of Sociology* 98(4):829–872, **not ASQ**. (Distinct from the verified ASQ paper Benjamin &
  Podolny 1999 above; do not conflate the two.)

Before adding any new paper to this library, confirm it on a SAGE ASQ article page (with volume/issue) or
an equivalent authoritative record, and update the access-date header. When in doubt — especially for
sociology-of-organizations classics that may be in *AJS* or *ASR* — **omit**.
