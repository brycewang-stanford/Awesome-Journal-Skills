# Worked Example: An ASQ-Style Introduction (before → after)

This demonstrates the **ASQ narrative arc** from `asq-writing-style`:
**open with the puzzle → build tension (why existing theory fails) → your insight → evidence →
reframing → close on generativity** — foregrounding theory throughout, matching *process voice* to a
*process phenomenon* (`asq-theory-development`), and stating a **theoretical contribution, not a
finding** (`asq-contribution-framing`). ASQ prizes strong theory, a rich and vivid organizational
context, and treats qualitative and quantitative work as equally first-class
(`asq-topic-selection`, `asq-methods`).

**Illustrative paper (fictional):** *"Becoming Trusted: How a Hospital Pharmacy Reorganized Authority
Around a New Robot."* Fictional setting: a single teaching hospital where an automated dispensing
robot was introduced into the central pharmacy, studied through an 18-month ethnography (fictional).

---

## Before (buries the idea — typical first-draft intro)

> Technology adoption in organizations has received considerable attention in the literature. Many
> scholars have examined how new technologies affect organizational structure, and findings have been
> mixed. In this paper, we study the adoption of an automated dispensing robot in a hospital pharmacy.
> We conducted an 18-month qualitative study involving interviews and observation, and we coded the
> data using a grounded-theory approach. We find that the robot increased the autonomy of pharmacy
> technicians and changed the division of labor. These findings have implications for the literature on
> technology and organizations. Section 2 reviews the relevant literature, Section 3 describes our
> methods, Section 4 presents our findings, and Section 5 discusses implications.

**What's wrong (against `asq-writing-style`, `asq-topic-selection`, `asq-theory-development`):**

- **Opens with a literature catalog, not a puzzle** — the named anti-pattern "front-loading the
  literature so the puzzle never lands." A smart reader is given no anomaly to be curious about.
- **Leads with method** ("we conducted an 18-month qualitative study... coded using grounded theory")
  before the reader knows what tension the study resolves — method-led framing.
- **Variance-speak imposed on a process phenomenon:** "increased the autonomy... changed the division
  of labor" flattens an unfolding reorganization into "factor A raised outcome B"
  (`asq-theory-development` punishes exactly this mismatch).
- **A finding, not a contribution:** "we find that the robot increased autonomy" answers a question; it
  does not *reframe* how organization theorists understand anything (`asq-contribution-framing`).
- **No surprise, no overturned prior** — nothing the reader is set up to feel wrong about.
- **Theory is invisible** except as a closing gesture ("implications for the literature").
- **Over-signposted roadmap** doing the work the argument should do.
- **Ends on a findings recap**, not on generativity.

---

## After (ASQ arc — puzzle → tension → insight → evidence → reframing → generativity)

> When a hospital pharmacy installed a dispensing robot meant to remove human judgment from a routine
> task, everyone expected the pharmacists — the licensed experts — to gain control and the technicians
> to fade into machine-minders. The opposite happened: within a year it was the *technicians*, not the
> pharmacists, whom physicians phoned when a dose looked wrong, and the pharmacists found themselves
> deferring to a group they had long supervised. *(the puzzle, on page one — an anomaly that makes the
> reader curious and slightly uncomfortable, not a literature dump.)*
>
> This is hard to explain with how we usually think about workplace technology. The dominant view treats
> a new machine as a *thing* that redistributes skill and authority according to who it deskills and who
> it augments — so authority should have flowed to whoever the robot made more capable. But that view
> reads the robot's effect off its design, and our case shows authority moving in a direction the design
> did not predict. *(tension: why the existing, design-determinist account fails here — theory
> foregrounded.)*
>
> We argue instead that the robot did not *redistribute* authority so much as it **opened an occasion in
> which authority had to be renegotiated** — and that who emerged as trusted depended not on the machine's
> capabilities but on **who came to be seen as able to vouch for the machine's output to outsiders.**
> Technicians, who tended the robot hourly, accumulated the situated knowledge to say *when the machine
> was wrong*; that warranting work, performed repeatedly in front of physicians and nurses, is what
> reconstituted them as trustworthy. Authority followed the work of vouching, not the tool.
> *(the insight, stated as a named generative mechanism — actors + structure + processual logic —
> in process voice: occasioning, renegotiating, becoming trusted.)*
>
> We trace this over 18 months of ethnography in one pharmacy, showing the sequence by which a routine
> machine became an occasion for restructuring: an initial disruption, a contest over who could speak
> for the robot, and a settling into a new, durable order in which technicians held warranting
> authority. Disconfirming moments — episodes where pharmacists briefly reclaimed the role — sharpen
> rather than weaken the account, marking the conditions under which the mechanism holds.
> *(evidence: a process display and negative cases, matched to the phenomenon — `asq-data-analysis`.)*
>
> Our contribution is to **reconceive organizational technology as an occasion for the renegotiation of
> authority rather than a determinant of it** — relocating the engine of structural change from the
> tool's capabilities to the social work of warranting its outputs. Whereas prior work asked *what a
> technology does to a structure*, we show *how a structure is reconstituted around the question of who can be
> trusted to speak for the technology.* *(contribution stated early, with its type named — a reframing —
> and the overturned prior made explicit; `asq-contribution-framing`.)* If authority accrues to those
> who warrant a machine rather than those it augments, the same lens should travel to algorithmic
> management, clinical AI, and any setting where opaque tools must be vouched for to outsiders — opening
> the question of how warranting authority is won, lost, and contested elsewhere. *(closes on
> generativity — new questions, not a recap.)*

---

## Why the "after" meets the ASQ bar

Mapped to the skill checklists:

- **Opens with the puzzle, not a literature dump** — the reversal (technicians, not pharmacists, gain
  authority) lands on page one and sets up the surprise the reader will feel
  (`asq-writing-style`: "open with the puzzle"; `asq-topic-selection`: "a puzzle, not a topic").
- **A single theoretical thread runs throughout** — the authority-as-renegotiation idea is visible in
  the puzzle, the tension, the mechanism, the evidence, and the contribution, not just intro and
  discussion (`asq-writing-style`).
- **Process voice matches a process phenomenon** — "occasion," "renegotiated," "became," "settling into
  a new order" narrate an unfolding sequence rather than flattening it into X→Y
  (`asq-theory-development`: process vs. variance is the central choice).
- **A named generative mechanism, not a construct** — actors (technicians, pharmacists, physicians),
  social structure (the pharmacy's status/authority order), and processual logic (repeated warranting
  in front of outsiders reconstitutes who is trusted). It yields a non-obvious implication — authority
  follows warranting work, not augmentation.
- **A contribution, not a finding** — the deliverable is a *reframing* of what organizational technology
  *is* (an occasion for renegotiation), with the overturned prior (design determinism) named; this is
  the ASQ register, not "the robot raised technician autonomy" (`asq-contribution-framing`).
- **Qualitative work treated as first-class** — a single immersive case carries the paper because the
  insight is deep and the craft is high (`asq-methods`, `asq-topic-selection`).
- **Closes on generativity** — extends to algorithmic management and clinical AI and opens new questions,
  rather than restating findings (`asq-writing-style`, `asq-contribution-framing`).
- **One-paragraph contribution claim is fillable:** "We study [why authority moved to technicians, not
  pharmacists, after a robot's arrival] in [a hospital pharmacy]. Existing work expects [authority to
  follow the tool's augmentation]. We show instead that [authority follows the social work of warranting
  the tool], because [repeated vouching to outsiders reconstitutes who is trusted]. This reframes
  [technology as an occasion for renegotiating authority]." — every slot filled, as
  `asq-topic-selection` requires.
