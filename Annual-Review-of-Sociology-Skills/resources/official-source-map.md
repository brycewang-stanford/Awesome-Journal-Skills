## Sibling boundary

- **ASR (American Sociological Review) / AJS (American Journal of Sociology)** — sociology's primary
  research flagships; they publish original empirical findings, while ARSoc publishes invited reviews of
  a literature or subfield.
- **Sociological Theory** — theory development, not a survey-of-record review series.
- **Annual Review of Psychology / Annual Review of Economics** — sister Annual Reviews series in
  adjacent disciplines; similar invitation-led model, different field.
- Handbook chapters — deeper/longer/exhaustive; ARSoc reviews are bounded, annual-volume pieces aimed at
  accessibility across sociology.

## Live-check guardrails

- Treat editor names and committee composition as live roster facts; re-check the Editorial Committee
  page before naming them.
- Treat length, abstract, keyword, reference-style, figure, and disclosure details as article-specific
  delivery facts controlled by the current Annual Reviews author resources and editor correspondence.
- Treat S2O/open-access status as year-dependent; confirm that the relevant volume remains covered.
- Treat impact metrics as annual and external to the manuscript workflow; do not use stale JIF wording in
  author-facing advice.
- Exemplar articles should be checked in the official Annual Reviews archive or on the DOI page before
  being cited as real models.
