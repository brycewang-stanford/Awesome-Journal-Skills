# JEP Exemplars Library (theme × type)

> **Verified via web search, access date 2026-06.** Every paper below was checked to confirm it
> actually appeared in the ***Journal of Economic Perspectives*** (the American Economic Association's
> open-access, non-technical synthesis journal). Most carry a **`10.1257/jep…` DOI stem** — the marker
> that confirms JEP specifically; one older article (2003) predates that stem and carries a legacy
> `10.1257/0895330…` DOI, noted honestly in its row. Year, volume(issue), and pages were read off the
> AEA article pages (`aeaweb.org/articles?id=…`). Papers that could not be fully verified as JEP were
> **omitted** — this is deliberately a short, clean list rather than a long, uncertain one.
>
> **Sibling-confusion guard (do not misattribute).** JEP is **not** any of these, which share the AEA
> `10.1257` prefix or the subject area:
> the *American Economic Review* (`10.1257/aer…`), the *Journal of Economic Literature*
> (JEL, `10.1257/jel…`), the *AEJ* journals (`10.1257/app|mac|mic|pol…`), or QJE/Econometrica. Only the
> **`10.1257/jep…`** stem (or, for pre-2007 articles, a JEP masthead on the AEA article page) confirms
> JEP. The omissions section records papers excluded on exactly this ground.
>
> **Use principle (zero hallucination):** this file gives **positioning only**. It does not reproduce
> or invent findings or numbers — read the original before citing anything. For JEP-specific policy,
> scope, and house rules, see [`../official-source-map.md`](../official-source-map.md).

## How to use this library

Pick the row whose **theme × type** is closest to yours, then study how that article makes a *rigorous*
synthesis *accessible* to a broad audience — the JEP bar (see
[`../../skills/jep-topic-selection/SKILL.md`](../../skills/jep-topic-selection/SKILL.md) and
[`../../skills/jep-writing-style/SKILL.md`](../../skills/jep-writing-style/SKILL.md)). For each, ask the
self-check before claiming your draft is "JEP-shaped." Remember JEP's house norm: communicate evidence
through **intuition and plain magnitudes, never equations or significance asterisks** in the body
([`jep-evidence-without-equations`](../../skills/jep-evidence-without-equations/SKILL.md)).

## By type

### Methods made accessible (a maturing toolkit explained to all economists)

- **Mullainathan & Spiess, "Machine Learning: An Applied Econometric Approach," JEP 2017, 31(2):87–106.**
  Verified: `aeaweb.org/articles?id=10.1257/jep.31.2.87` (DOI `10.1257/jep.31.2.87`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** explains what machine learning *is* for an economist — prediction vs.
    estimation — without drowning the reader in algorithms, and shows where it helps and where it
    misleads. A technical literature translated into a usable mental model. Routes to
    [`../../skills/jep-accessibility-and-translation/SKILL.md`](../../skills/jep-accessibility-and-translation/SKILL.md).
  - **Self-check:** can a non-specialist economist finish your piece knowing *when* to reach for the
    method and *what it does not do*, without needing the math?

- **Athey & Imbens, "The State of Applied Econometrics: Causality and Policy Evaluation," JEP 2017, 31(2):3–32.**
  Verified: `aeaweb.org/articles?id=10.1257/jep.31.2.3` (DOI `10.1257/jep.31.2.3`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a tour of modern causal tools (synthetic control, RDD, robustness, ML for
    causal effects) pitched at the *applied researcher*, not the econometric theorist — synthesis that
    organizes a fast-moving field around what a practitioner needs to know.
  - **Self-check:** is your synthesis organized around the *reader's* decisions, not around a chronological
    list of papers?

### Macro synthesis (organizing a hard empirical question)

- **Nakamura & Steinsson, "Identification in Macroeconomics," JEP 2018, 32(3):59–86.**
  Verified: `aeaweb.org/articles?id=10.1257/jep.32.3.59` (DOI `10.1257/jep.32.3.59`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** takes a genuinely hard problem — how do we learn anything causal in
    macro? — and gives a broad audience an intuitive map of the strategies and their limits, with
    examples rather than estimators. Connects to
    [`../../skills/jep-narrative-arc/SKILL.md`](../../skills/jep-narrative-arc/SKILL.md).
  - **Self-check:** does your piece convey *why the question is hard* before parading answers?

### Inequality & mobility (a policy-relevant synthesis with balance)

- **Corak, "Income Inequality, Equality of Opportunity, and Intergenerational Mobility," JEP 2013, 27(3):79–102.**
  Verified: `aeaweb.org/articles?id=10.1257/jep.27.3.79` (DOI `10.1257/jep.27.3.79`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** distills cross-country evidence linking inequality to mobility (the "Great
    Gatsby Curve" discussion) for a broad audience, weighing mechanisms and being honest about what the
    correlations can and cannot establish — model balance for a policy-charged topic
    ([`jep-balance-and-objectivity`](../../skills/jep-balance-and-objectivity/SKILL.md)).
  - **Self-check:** on a politically loaded question, do you separate what the evidence shows from the
    policy view, and flag the uncertainty?

### Labor & social change (a vivid, accessible essay)

- **Goldin, Katz & Kuziemko, "The Homecoming of American College Women: The Reversal of the College Gender Gap," JEP 2006, 20(4):133–156.**
  Verified: `aeaweb.org/articles?id=10.1257/jep.20.4.133` (DOI `10.1257/jep.20.4.133`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a clear narrative about a striking, broadly interesting fact (women
    overtaking men in college), with the evidence woven into a story a general reader follows easily —
    the JEP hook-and-synthesis arc done well.
  - **Self-check:** does your opening give a generalist a vivid fact or puzzle to latch onto in the first
    paragraph?

### Finance synthesis (a debate framed fairly)

- **Shiller, "From Efficient Markets Theory to Behavioral Finance," JEP 2003, 17(1):83–104.**
  Verified: `aeaweb.org/articles?id=10.1257/089533003321164967` — JEP masthead confirmed on the AEA
  article page. **Note:** this 2003 article predates the `10.1257/jep…` stem and carries the legacy AEA
  DOI `10.1257/089533003321164967`; the *journal*, not the DOI stem, confirms JEP here.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** narrates a paradigm shift in finance for a broad audience, presenting the
    efficient-markets and behavioral camps in their strongest forms — a synthesis of a live debate, not a
    brief for one side ([`jep-balance-and-objectivity`](../../skills/jep-balance-and-objectivity/SKILL.md)).
  - **Self-check:** when you summarize a debate, would *both* camps recognize your account of their view
    as fair?

## Omitted for sibling-confusion or lack of full verification (do not attribute to JEP without re-checking)

To keep the list zero-hallucination, the following were **excluded** after checking:

- **Goldin, "A Grand Gender Convergence: Its Last Chapter" (2014)** — a natural JEP-area candidate, but
  web search confirms it is in the ***American Economic Review*** (104(4):1091–1119), **not JEP**. Listed
  here only as a sibling-confusion guardrail: the `aer` stem, not `jep`, is the tell. (Goldin's accessible
  *JEP* piece is the 2006 "Homecoming" article above.)
- **Chetty, Hendren, Kline & Saez, intergenerational-mobility papers (2014)** — "Is the United States
  Still a Land of Opportunity?" is in the ***AER*** (104(5):141–47) and "Where is the Land of
  Opportunity?" is in the ***QJE*** (129(4):1553–1623). Neither is JEP; Corak's 2013 article above is the
  verified JEP synthesis of this theme.
- **DellaVigna, "Psychology and Economics: Evidence from the Field" (2009)** — frequently cited in the
  behavioral area, but it is in the ***Journal of Economic Literature*** (JEL, 47(2):315–72), **not JEP**.
  Excluded as a JEP↔JEL guardrail.

Before adding any new paper to this library, confirm it on `aeaweb.org/articles` (article page with
volume/issue/pages and the JEP masthead) **and** that the DOI begins `10.1257/jep` (or, for pre-2007
articles, that the AEA page shows the JEP masthead), then update the access-date header. When in doubt,
omit. **6 verified beats 15 guessed.**
