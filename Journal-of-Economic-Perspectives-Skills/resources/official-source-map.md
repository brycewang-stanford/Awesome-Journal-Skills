## Official AEA / JEP pages (publisher / editorial authority)

- **https://www.aeaweb.org/journals/jep** — JEP journal home. Verifies: JEP is the
  AEA's accessible journal that "aims to bridge the gap between the general
  interest business and financial press and standard academic journals"; issues
  are **publicly accessible at no charge** (open access, courtesy of the AEA);
  **four issues per year**; founded **1987**; recurring features include
  **symposia** and **"Recommendations for Further Reading."** Accessed 2026-06.
- **https://www.aeaweb.org/journals/jep/submissions** — JEP Guidelines for
  Proposals. Verifies: articles are **primarily solicited by the editorial team**;
  authors should send a **2–5 page article proposal** (preferred over a finished
  manuscript); historically **~10–15%** of published articles originate as
  unsolicited proposals; articles should be **readable by 90 percent or more of
  the AEA membership** and aimed at economists not specialized in the author's
  field; the journal is "scholarly without relying too heavily on mathematical
  notation," explaining ideas through **economic intuition**; **proposals and
  papers are sent as Word or PDF email attachments to jep@aeapubs.org**. Accessed
  2026-06.
- **https://www.aeaweb.org/journals/jep/editors** — JEP Editors. Verifies (检索于
  2026-06；editor roster re-verified 2026-06-22；以官网为准): **Editor — Heidi
  Williams (Dartmouth College)**; **Coeditors — Jeffrey Kling (Washington, DC) and
  Jonathan Parker (MIT)**; **Managing Editor — Timothy Taylor (Macalester College)**;
  **Assistant Managing Editor — Bradley Waldraff (AEA)**. Editor + coeditor names
  re-confirmed via web search of the AEA JEP editors page 2026-06-22. Accessed
  2026-06.
- **https://www.aeaweb.org/journals/jep/issues** — JEP issue archive. Verifies the
  recurring **symposium / article** format and the published-topic pattern.
  Accessed 2026-06.
- **https://www.aeaweb.org/journals/jep/recommendations-for-further-reading** —
  the standing "Recommendations for Further Reading" feature page. Verifies the
  feature exists as a recurring JEP element. Accessed 2026-06.

## AEA-wide policies (shared across AEA journals, applied to JEP)

- **https://www.aeaweb.org/journals/policies** — AEA journal policies hub
  (disclosure, ethics, data/code, AI). Accessed 2026-06.
- **https://www.aeaweb.org/journals/policies/disclosure-policy** — AEA Disclosure
  Policy. Verifies: authors must identify each interested party from whom they
  received financial support **summing to at least US$10,000** over the past three
  years (consulting, retainers, grants, etc.); relevant officer/director/board
  positions disclosed; every article states its **sources of financial support
  (or states there were none)**. Accessed 2026-06.
- **AEA Data and Code Availability Policy / AEA Data and Code Repository** (linked
  from the AEA journal policies hub) — Verifies: authors of conditionally accepted
  papers containing empirical, simulation, or experimental work must provide data,
  code, and documentation sufficient for replication, deposited in a trusted
  repository (e.g., the AEA Data and Code Repository). For JEP — a largely
  non-empirical synthesis venue — this applies to whatever original empirical
  content a piece contains; **需复核** for the exact JEP-specific application.
  Accessed 2026-06.
- **AEA AI policy** (AEA author/editorial policies) — Verifies: **AI cannot be
  listed as an author.** Accessed 2026-06; **需复核** for the precise JEP-specific
  wording.
