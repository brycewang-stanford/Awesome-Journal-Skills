# External Tools & Resources for the Journal of Economic Perspectives (JEP)

Tools, sources, and references useful when preparing a *Journal of Economic
Perspectives* (JEP) **proposal or article**. JEP is the American Economic
Association's **open-access, non-technical synthesis** journal: a typical piece
**distills a body of economics into an accessible argument** for a broad audience
of economists, **not** a new identified result or a replication package. So the
toolkit here is about **finding, organizing, and communicating** a literature —
not about running regressions. Verify volatile process specifics on the official
AEA/JEP pages (检索于 2026-06；以官网为准).

## 1. Literature discovery & synthesis (the JEP core)

| Source | Provider | Use for a JEP synthesis |
|--------|----------|--------------------------|
| EconLit | AEA | The field's index; map the literature you will distill |
| JSTOR / AEA journals | JSTOR / AEA | Read the foundational and recent papers behind the argument |
| NBER / CEPR working papers | NBER / CEPR | The current frontier and "why now" |
| Google Scholar / Semantic Scholar | Google / AI2 | Citation trails, find the survey-level and review pieces |
| RePEc / IDEAS | RePEc | Coverage, author profiles, version tracking |
| Journal of Economic Literature (JEL) | AEA | The **technical** surveys of record — read them to know what JEP should *not* re-do |

> JEP is synthesis for a broad audience; JEL is the exhaustive technical survey.
> Use JEL pieces as inputs, then write the accessible argument JEP wants.

## 2. Reference management & writing

- **Reference managers:** Zotero, BibTeX/BibLaTeX, EndNote — keep a clean,
  complete bibliography; JEP applies **AEA house style at copyediting**, so the
  submitted document need not pre-conform.
- **Word vs. LaTeX:** JEP accepts **Word or PDF**; because the body carries **no
  equations**, Word is often the natural choice for an accessible essay (LaTeX is
  fine but its math edge is unneeded here).
- **Plain-language / readability tools:** readability checkers (e.g.,
  Hemingway-style editors), a generalist test reader from another subfield, and a
  jargon-audit pass — the practical proxies for "readable by 90% of AEA members."

## 3. Exhibits for a general audience

- **Charting:** clear line/bar charts in any tool (R `ggplot2`, Python
  `matplotlib`/`plotnine`, Stata, or a spreadsheet) — but design for clarity, not
  density (see `jep-exhibits-for-general-readers`).
- **Design rules to enforce in the tool:** finding-titles, direct labels, rounded
  numbers, shaded uncertainty bands (not asterisks), grayscale-safe color, no 3-D
  or chartjunk.
- **Tables:** a few labeled, rounded quantities with a one-line source/units note —
  never a transplanted coefficient table.

## 4. Verifying the evidence a synthesis cites

Because a JEP article *weighs* other people's evidence rather than producing its
own, the value of the empirical-methods background hub here is as a **lens for
judging the studies you cite** — is an identification credible, is the inference
valid, is a claim over-stated? See `resources/README.md` for the linked
shared-resources hub (reporting standards + reviewer-objection checklist) used as
**background**, not as a code kit (this pack ships no `code/`).

## 5. Process & submission (verify on the official AEA pages)

| Item | Note |
|------|------|
| Venue | AEA, open access (free to read), four issues/year, founded 1987 |
| What to send | A **2–5 page proposal** (preferred); ~10–15% of articles start unsolicited |
| How to send | **Word or PDF email attachment to jep@aeapubs.org** (re-confirm); editor-driven review, no anonymous-referee portal |
| Accessibility bar | Readable by **≥90% of AEA members**; intuition over notation |
| Disclosure | AEA policy: interested-party ≥ US$10,000 / 3 yrs; funding sources stated |
| Data & code | AEA repository at conditional acceptance, for any empirical content |
| AI | Cannot be listed as an author |

## 6. Cautions

1. **Verify volatile specifics** (editor roster, submission address, front-matter
   rules, any fee) on the official AEA/JEP pages — they change.
2. **JEP wants a proposal, not a manuscript** for cold approaches — don't burn
   effort on a full unsolicited draft.
3. **Don't import research-paper apparatus** — no body equations, no coefficient
   tables, no significance asterisks; communicate via intuition and clear exhibits.
4. **Stay a synthesizer, not an advocate** — the tooling above helps you *map and
   weigh* a literature fairly, which is the JEP product.
