# World Politics Official Source Map

Primary and authoritative sources behind the World Politics–specific facts injected into this skill
pack. Access date for all entries: **2026-06-01**. The authoritative *Guidelines for Contributors* PDF
read for this pack is dated **March 2026**. Re-verify volatile items (editors and term, exact
word/abstract limits, embargo terms, data-policy wording, publisher) on the official page before
relying on them — journal pages change.

## Official journal, submission & author pages

- **https://wpj.princeton.edu/** — **World Politics** official site, hosted by the **Princeton
  Institute for International and Regional Studies (PIIRS)**, Princeton University. Verifies: the
  journal "aims to publish outstanding scholarship in the fields of **international relations and
  comparative politics**"; quarterly; sponsoring institution = **PIIRS / Princeton University**;
  Reviewer Guidelines are posted here and authors are encouraged to read them before submitting.
- **World Politics, *Guidelines for Contributors* (public rev. 7-29-22; PDF dated March 2026)** —
  hosted at `wpj.princeton.edu` and (HTTP 403 to bots) at `press.jhu.edu`. Verifies: two article types
  — **research articles** (pose important substantive questions, advance theoretical debates, present
  original empirical research) and **review articles** (analyze and compare a set of thematically
  related books *and* advance how a field should pursue future work — explicitly "differ from
  conventional book reviews"); submission to **ScholarOne** (`mc.manuscriptcentral.com/wp`); manuscripts
  **double spaced** with word count indicated; **up to 12,500 words including notes and references**;
  **tables, figures, and appendixes need not be included in the word count**; **online supplementary
  material cannot exceed fifteen pages**; **abstract ≤ 150 words**; revised articles may exceed the
  limit if it results from responding to reviewers; **author response memos should not exceed five
  pages, single spaced**; **triple-blind** review with author anonymity preserved through the editorial
  decision; articles "have usually been reviewed by at least **two non-Princeton reviewers** and often
  … one of the editors"; authors "can expect to receive decisions … within **four months**"; the
  journal **does not publish opinion pieces, policy pieces, articles expounding political theory,
  historical articles, or narratives of a current affairs or journalistic nature**; authors with
  research involving direct engagement with human participants must affirm adherence to **APSA's
  Principles and Guidance for Human Subjects Research (2020)**.
- **https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/world-politics/information/instructions-contributors**
  — Cambridge Core instructions-for-authors page (Cambridge hosted the journal through 2022). Verifies:
  "Published on behalf of" PIIRS; submission via `mc.manuscriptcentral.com/wp`; directs authors to the
  full guidelines at `press.jhu.edu/journals/world-politics`.
- **https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/world-politics** — World Politics archive on Cambridge Core.

## Review & data policy

- **World Politics Reviewer Guidelines** (linked from `wpj.princeton.edu`) — Verifies: general
  expectations for all articles (clear research goals; engagement with theoretical and substantive
  literatures; analytic clarity; explicit research design and appropriate methods; substantively
  important conclusions) plus separate expectations for quantitative and qualitative research. Read
  the live page for the current full text. 待核实 for the exact current criteria text.
- **World Politics Dataverse** (linked from the *Guidelines for Contributors*) — Verifies: authors
  who **rely on quantitative data** must deposit replication materials **after acceptance but prior to
  publication** (original data, specialized programs, recode lists, data extracts, and an explanatory
  README describing contents, creation, sources, and how to **replicate the exact numerical results**);
  published pieces must state data availability; **embargoes up to two years** (or other special
  circumstances) may be approved by the **editorial committee** before publication, with special
  consideration for junior scholars.
