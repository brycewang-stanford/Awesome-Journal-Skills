# World Politics Official Source Map

Primary and authoritative sources behind the World Politics–specific facts injected into this skill
pack. Access date for all entries: **2026-06-20**. The authoritative *Guidelines for Contributors* PDF
read for this pack is dated **March 2026** (read in full this cycle from the official

## Official journal, submission & author pages

- **https://wpj.princeton.edu/** — **World Politics** official site, hosted by the **Princeton
  Institute for International and Regional Studies (PIIRS)**, Princeton University. Verifies (per the
  *Guidelines for Contributors* hosted here and cross-checked on Project MUSE/Cambridge this cycle): the
  journal "aims to publish outstanding scholarship in the fields of **international relations and
  comparative politics**"; quarterly; sponsoring institution = **PIIRS / Princeton University**;
  Reviewer Guidelines are posted here and authors are encouraged to read them before submitting. Note:
  the HTML site is not bot-fetchable — these facts were verified via the directly downloadable
  Guidelines PDF (below) and the publisher mirrors (Project MUSE, Cambridge Core). Accessed 2026-06-20
- **https://wpj.princeton.edu/sites/g/files/toruqf2136/files/documents/world_politics_guidelines_for_contributors_public_rev._7-29-22_final.pdf**
  — *Guidelines for Contributors* (public rev. 7-29-22; PDF footer dated **March 2026**), downloaded and
  **read in full this cycle** from the official `wpj.princeton.edu` URL. Verifies: two article types —
  **research articles** (pose important substantive questions, advance theoretical debates, present
  original empirical research) and **review articles** (analyze and compare a set of thematically
  related books *and* advance how a field should pursue future work — explicitly "differ from
  conventional book reviews"); submission to **ScholarOne** (`mc.manuscriptcentral.com/wp`); manuscripts
  **double spaced** with word count indicated; **research and review articles may be up to 12,500 words
  including notes and references**; **tables, figures, and appendixes need not be included in the word
  count**; **online supplementary material cannot exceed fifteen pages**; **abstracts limited to 150
  words**; revised articles may exceed the limit if it results from responding to reviewers; **author
  response memos should not exceed five pages, single spaced**; review based on author anonymity and
  confidentiality of readers'/editors' reports in a **triple-blind** process, with author anonymity
  preserved through the editorial decision; articles "have usually been reviewed by at least **two
  non-Princeton reviewers** and often, but not in all instances, one of the editors"; authors "can
  expect to receive decisions on their submissions within **four months**"; the journal **does not
  publish opinion pieces, policy pieces, articles expounding political theory, historical articles, or
  narratives of a current affairs or journalistic nature**; authors with research involving direct
  engagement with human participants must affirm adherence to **APSA's Principles and Guidance for Human
  Subjects Research (adopted 2020, endorsed by the World Politics editorial committee)**; **Emily Babson**
  is named as **Executive Editor** (Princeton Institute for International and Regional Studies). Accessed 2026-06-20
- **https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/world-politics/information/instructions-contributors**
  — Cambridge Core instructions-for-authors page (Cambridge hosted the journal through Vol. 74 / 2022).
  Verifies: "Published on behalf of" PIIRS; submission via `mc.manuscriptcentral.com/wp`; directs authors
  to the full guidelines at `press.jhu.edu/journals/world-politics`. Accessed 2026-06-20
- **https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/world-politics** — World Politics archive on Cambridge Core.
  Verifies (read this cycle): "Cambridge University Press ceased publication of World Politics on
  completion of **Volume 74 / 2022**. From **Volume 75 / 2023**, the Journal is published by **Johns
  Hopkins University Press**." Accessed 2026-06-20

## Review & data policy

- **World Politics Reviewer Guidelines** (linked from `wpj.princeton.edu`) — Verifies: general
  expectations for all articles (clear research goals; engagement with theoretical and substantive
  literatures; analytic clarity; explicit research design and appropriate methods; substantively
  important conclusions) plus separate expectations for quantitative and qualitative research. Read
  the live page for the current full text. 需复核 for the exact current criteria text.
- **World Politics Dataverse** (linked from the *Guidelines for Contributors*; data-policy text re-read
  in full from the official PDF this cycle) — Verifies: authors who **rely on quantitative data** must
  deposit replication materials **after acceptance but prior to publication** (original data, specialized
  programs, recode lists, data extracts, and an explanatory README describing contents, creation,
  sources, and how to **replicate the exact numerical results**); published pieces must state data
  availability; **embargoes up to two years** (or other special circumstances) may be approved by the
  **editorial committee** before publication, with special consideration for junior scholars. Accessed 2026-06-20
