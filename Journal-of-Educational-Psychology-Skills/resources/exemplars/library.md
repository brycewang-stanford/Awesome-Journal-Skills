# Journal of Educational Psychology Exemplars Library (method × topic)

> **Verified via web search, access date 2026-06.** Every paper below was checked to confirm it actually
> appeared in **_Journal of Educational Psychology_** (American Psychological Association; ISSN 0022-0663),
> with year, volume(issue):pages. Papers that could not be cleanly verified as _Journal of Educational
> Psychology_ — including ones often **confused** with it — were **omitted** and documented below. It is
> deliberately a short, clean list (6 verified) rather than a long, uncertain one.
>
> **Sibling-confusion guard.** _Journal of Educational Psychology_ (JEP, APA) is **not** any of:
> _American Educational Research Journal_ (AERJ, AERA), _Review of Educational Research_ (RER, review),
> _Contemporary Educational Psychology_ (CEP), _Learning and Instruction_, _Educational Psychology Review_
> (review), _Child Development_, _Journal of Personality and Social Psychology_ (JPSP), or _Science_.
> Several famous "education/learning" findings live in those venues — not here. Each row below was checked
> against this list, and the most common confusions are listed at the bottom.
>
> **Use principle (zero hallucination):** this file gives **design positioning only**. It does not
> reproduce or invent effect sizes, sample sizes, or specific findings — read the original on APA PsycNet /
> the publisher before citing any number. For venue policy, scope, and format facts, see
> [`../official-source-map.md`](../official-source-map.md).

## How to use this library

Pick the row whose **method × question** is closest to yours, then study how that paper makes a finding
that is **theoretically grounded, methodologically rigorous, and educationally consequential** carry in a
**full-length, theory-driven** format — the _Journal of Educational Psychology_ bar (see
[`../../skills/jedpsych-topic-selection/SKILL.md`](../../skills/jedpsych-topic-selection/SKILL.md) and
[`../../skills/jedpsych-writing-style/SKILL.md`](../../skills/jedpsych-writing-style/SKILL.md)). For each,
ask the self-check question before claiming your paper is "_Journal of Educational Psychology_-shaped."
Note that **experiments, classroom field studies, longitudinal designs, and multilevel/SEM models**
dominate this venue; design at the right level and a tested mechanism — not narrative — carry the paper.

## By method

### Self-regulated learning / motivation, classroom correlational with measurement (survey + achievement)

- **Pintrich & De Groot, "Motivational and Self-Regulated Learning Components of Classroom Academic
  Performance," _Journal of Educational Psychology_ 1990, 82(1):33–40.** Verified: ERIC EJ442292;
  APA PsycNet record 1990-21075-001 (ISSN 0022-0663).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** ties a motivational/self-regulatory framework to real classroom achievement
    in seventh graders, and operationalizes the constructs with a measure that became foundational — theory
    + measurement + educational outcome in one compact study, the JEP signature
    ([`jedpsych-theory-and-hypotheses`](../../skills/jedpsych-theory-and-hypotheses/SKILL.md)).
  - **Self-check:** does your motivation/SRL construct connect to a learning outcome with a defensible
    measure, not just a self-report correlation?

### Self-regulation theory paper grounded in classroom learning (theoretical + empirical synthesis)

- **Zimmerman, "A Social Cognitive View of Self-Regulated Academic Learning," _Journal of Educational
  Psychology_ 1989, 81(3):329–339.** Verified: APA PsycNet record 1989-39411-001 (ISSN 0022-0663).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** frames self-regulated learning as a social-cognitive process with testable
    components — a model of stating the learning mechanism crisply before deriving predictions
    ([`jedpsych-theory-and-hypotheses`](../../skills/jedpsych-theory-and-hypotheses/SKILL.md)).
  - **Self-check:** is your mechanism stated as a process that generates predictions, not a label?

### Instructional experiment grounded in cognitive-load theory (transfer-focused training study)

- **Paas, "Training Strategies for Attaining Transfer of Problem-Solving Skill in Statistics: A Cognitive
  Load Approach," _Journal of Educational Psychology_ 1992, 84(4):429–434.** Verified: APA PsycNet
  (ISSN 0022-0663).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** an instructional manipulation grounded in a cognitive mechanism (load) with
    a **transfer** outcome — exactly the "does the learning generalize?" question JEP rewards, tested
    experimentally ([`jedpsych-study-design`](../../skills/jedpsych-study-design/SKILL.md)).
  - **Self-check:** does your instructional manipulation target a cognitive mechanism and test transfer,
    not just practiced-task performance?

### Worked-examples experiment with a load mechanism (instructional design study)

- **Paas & Van Merriënboer, "Variability of Worked Examples and Transfer of Geometrical Problem-Solving
  Skills: A Cognitive-Load Approach," _Journal of Educational Psychology_ 1994, 86(1):122–133.**
  Verified: APA PsycNet (ISSN 0022-0663).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** manipulates instructional design (example variability), measures cognitive
    load as the mechanism, and tests transfer — design + mechanism + educationally meaningful outcome.
  - **Self-check:** does your design vary an instructional feature and measure the process that explains
    its effect on learning?

### Large-scale self-concept / context-effect study (re-analysis of longitudinal survey data)

- **Marsh, "The Big-Fish-Little-Pond Effect on Academic Self-Concept," _Journal of Educational
  Psychology_ 1987, 79(3):280–295.** Verified: DOI 10.1037/0022-0663.79.3.280 (ISSN 0022-0663).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a frame-of-reference mechanism (relative standing shapes self-concept)
    tested at scale with rigorous modeling — a model of using a clear theoretical mechanism to interpret a
    contextual effect on a motivational outcome.
  - **Note (page-range caveat):** some secondary sources list 228–295; the APA record gives **280–295** —
    cite the publisher record.
  - **Self-check:** is your contextual/educational effect explained by a stated psychological mechanism,
    and modeled at the right level?

### Classroom child-by-instruction interaction (aptitude-treatment-interaction design)

- **Connor, Morrison & Petrella, "Effective Reading Comprehension Instruction: Examining Child by
  Instruction Interactions," _Journal of Educational Psychology_ 2004, 96(4):682–698.** Verified:
  APA PsycNet (ISSN 0022-0663).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** tests whether the *right* instruction depends on the *child* (an
    aptitude-treatment interaction) in authentic classroom reading — ecologically valid, mechanism-aware,
    and directly relevant to instruction ([`jedpsych-study-design`](../../skills/jedpsych-study-design/SKILL.md)).
  - **Note (sibling guard):** the same team's *algorithm-guided individualized reading* paper (2007) is in
    **_Science_**, and their first-grade _Child × Instruction_ literacy study (2009) is in **_Child
    Development_** — cite the correct venue.
  - **Self-check:** does your instructional effect depend on learner characteristics, and is it tested in a
    real classroom?

## Omitted for lack of _Journal of Educational Psychology_ placement (do not attribute without re-checking)

To keep the list zero-hallucination, these frequently-cited candidates were **excluded** after web
checking — several are classic sibling-journal confusions:

- **Duckworth, Peterson, Matthews & Kelly, "Grit: Perseverance and Passion for Long-Term Goals" (2007)**
  — published in **_Journal of Personality and Social Psychology_** (JPSP), **not** JEP.
- **Blackwell, Trzesniewski & Dweck, "Implicit Theories of Intelligence Predict Achievement…" (2007)**
  — **_Child Development_**, **not** JEP.
- **Pekrun, Goetz, Frenzel, Barchfeld & Perry, "Measuring Emotions in Students' Learning and Performance:
  The Achievement Emotions Questionnaire (AEQ)" (2011)** — **_Contemporary Educational Psychology_** (the
  APA-adjacent Elsevier sibling), **not** JEP.
- **Connor, Morrison, Fishman, Schatschneider & Underwood, "Algorithm-Guided Individualized Reading
  Instruction" (2007)** — **_Science_**; and Connor et al. (2009) _Child × Instruction_ first-grade
  literacy study — **_Child Development_** — distinct from the 2004 _JEP_ paper listed above.
- **Sweller & Cooper, "The Use of Worked Examples as a Substitute for Problem Solving…" (1985)**
  — **_Cognition and Instruction_**, **not** JEP (the closely related JEP cognitive-load papers are the
  Paas studies listed above).
- **Hattie & Timperley, "The Power of Feedback" (2007)** — **_Review of Educational Research_** (RER),
  **not** JEP.

Before adding any new paper to this library, confirm it on APA PsycNet (ISSN 0022-0663, article page with
volume/issue/pages) and update the access-date header. When in doubt, omit. If web verification is
unavailable, mark new rows **待核实 / TO VERIFY** and attach **no** journal attribution until checked.
