# Worked Example: A Journal of Educational Psychology Introduction (before → after)

This demonstrates the **JEP Introduction arc** that the pack's skills require: **educational question +
stakes → the gap, named precisely → the learning/motivation mechanism → the rigorous design that settles
it (nesting, cluster-level power, validated measures) → the contribution and its practice implication** —
written for a **full-length, theory-driven** educational-psychology venue under **masked review**, where
the result must be **important for learning *and* methodologically rigorous for its nested setting**
(`jedpsych-writing-style`, `jedpsych-literature-positioning`).

A note on scope for this pack: JEP evidence comes from **classroom/school experiments, field trials,
quasi-experiments, longitudinal studies, and multilevel/SEM/growth models** — so the persuasive work here
is **design at the right level and mechanism testing**, not an econometrics pipeline. The shared
empirical-methods notes (reviewer-objection checklist, reporting standards) are **background** for any
quasi-experimental component, not the center of a typical submission.

**Illustrative paper (fictional):** *"Teaching Comprehension Monitoring Transfers to Novel Texts: A
Preregistered Cluster-Randomized Trial."* Fictional setting: a teacher-delivered reading-strategy
intervention in 48 upper-elementary classrooms.

---

## Before (buries the idea — typical first-draft intro)

> Reading comprehension has been studied extensively in educational psychology, and many strategies have
> been proposed to improve it across a large literature. Numerous theories address how readers process
> text, and prior work has examined vocabulary, fluency, and background knowledge. In the present research
> we examine whether a comprehension-strategy program improves reading. We conducted a study in which some
> classrooms received the program and others did not, and then students completed a reading test. We
> predicted that the program would improve scores. This is an important and timely topic given concerns
> about literacy. The remainder of this article describes our methods, presents our results, and discusses
> their implications.

**What's wrong (against `jedpsych-writing-style` / `jedpsych-literature-positioning` /
`jedpsych-topic-selection` / `jedpsych-theory-and-hypotheses`):**

- **No precise question, no mechanism, no contribution on page one.** A reader cannot tell what is new or
  *why* the program should work — JEP wants the psychology of learning, not "the program improved scores."
- **Throat-clearing literature history** ("studied extensively," "numerous theories") that surveys instead
  of engaging the **closest prior work** (`jedpsych-literature-positioning` anti-pattern).
- **Gap stated as vagueness** ("examine whether … improves reading"), not a precise, unresolved question.
- **No learning mechanism** — the metacognitive process that would explain transfer is absent
  (`jedpsych-theory-and-hypotheses`).
- **No signal of rigor for the setting** — nothing about classroom randomization, **cluster-level power**,
  nesting, or validated transfer measures, which JEP treats as load-bearing
  (`jedpsych-study-design`, `jedpsych-data-analysis`).
- **No educational-relevance argument** beyond "literacy is important," and an **over-signposted roadmap**.

---

## After (JEP arc — question + mechanism + rigorous nested design, contribution early)

> **Does explicitly teaching comprehension *monitoring* help upper-elementary readers comprehend novel
> texts they were never taught — and does the benefit run through the monitoring process itself?** Whether
> strategy instruction *transfers* to new texts, rather than improving performance only on practiced
> materials, is unresolved and central to how reading is taught. *(educational question + stakes, in the
> first breath — the transfer question is what matters for curricula.)*
>
> The strongest demonstrations that strategy instruction aids comprehension come largely from short,
> researcher-delivered lab studies; whether **teacher-delivered** instruction produces **classroom-level**
> gains on **transfer** under realistic conditions, and whether those gains are explained by improved
> metacognitive monitoring, is largely untested. *(the gap, named precisely against the contrast class —
> not "little is known," per `jedpsych-literature-positioning`.)*
>
> We draw on a metacognitive account: explicit instruction builds **comprehension monitoring**, which lets
> readers detect and repair understanding breakdowns and therefore allocate effort effectively on
> unfamiliar texts. *(the learning mechanism stated before the design, per
> `jedpsych-theory-and-hypotheses`.)*
>
> We test this in a **preregistered cluster-randomized trial** (48 classrooms, 1,089 students), with the
> **number of classrooms** — not students — fixed in advance by a power analysis targeting a small but
> educationally meaningful effect given the intraclass correlation, a pretest covariate, and locked
> exclusion and analysis rules. The confirmatory analysis is a **two-level model**, and a preregistered
> **mediation** test asks whether monitoring gains carry the effect. *(the rigorous, nested design that
> settles it; cluster-level power and the matching multilevel analysis up front, per
> `jedpsych-study-design`.)*
>
> Our contribution is to move strategy instruction from a lab effect to a **classroom-level, teacher-
> delivered, transfer** effect with a **tested mechanism**: it is not only that scores rise, but that they
> rise on untaught texts *because* readers monitor comprehension better. *(contribution stated early,
> relative to a specific prior view.)* For practice, this speaks directly to whether comprehension-
> monitoring instruction belongs in core upper-elementary reading curricula. *(educational relevance made
> explicit — who in education inherits the claim, per `jedpsych-topic-selection`.)*
>
> Data (de-identified), the intervention materials, and analysis code are available with persistent
> identifiers, and the study was preregistered (details in the Transparency and Openness subsection).
> *(transparency flagged where the venue expects it; the subsection itself reports data/materials/code
> availability — see
> [`jedpsych-open-science-and-transparency`](../../skills/jedpsych-open-science-and-transparency/SKILL.md).
> No specific policy wording is invented here; confirm current requirements in
> [`../official-source-map.md`](../official-source-map.md).)*

## Why the "after" meets the JEP bar

Mapped to the pack's skill checklists:

- **Educational question + mechanism front-loaded** — the transfer question and the metacognitive-monitoring
  mechanism appear immediately, marking this as educational *psychology*, not evaluation
  (`jedpsych-theory-and-hypotheses`, `jedpsych-topic-selection`).
- **Surgical positioning against the contrast class** — the gap is named relative to lab, researcher-
  delivered studies, and the contribution is stated early, without a chronological survey
  (`jedpsych-literature-positioning`).
- **Rigor for the nested setting is visible and early** — cluster randomization, **power at the classroom
  level**, a pretest covariate, a two-level model, and a preregistered mediation are stated in the
  Introduction, because at JEP design rigor *is* part of the claim
  (`jedpsych-study-design`, `jedpsych-data-analysis`).
- **Educational relevance made explicit** — the practice implication (core reading curricula) lets the
  editor see the payoff (`jedpsych-topic-selection` relevance filter).
- **Transparency signposted, not improvised** — data/materials/code with identifiers and preregistration
  are flagged; exact policy is deferred to [`../official-source-map.md`](../official-source-map.md), not
  invented (`jedpsych-open-science-and-transparency`).
- **One-sentence hook is fillable:** "We show that [teaching comprehension monitoring transfers to novel
  texts at the classroom level] through [a preregistered cluster-randomized trial with a tested mechanism]"
  — both brackets filled, as `jedpsych-topic-selection` asks.
