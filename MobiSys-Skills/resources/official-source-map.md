## Verified 2026 facts used in skills

- MobiSys 2026 is the 24th Annual ACM International Conference on Mobile Systems,
  Applications, and Services, held June 21–25, 2026 in Cambridge, UK, hosted with the
  University of Cambridge under ACM SIGMOBILE.
- MobiSys 2026 used HotCRP (`mobisys26.hotcrp.com`) with a paper registration step preceding
  paper upload: registration November 28, 2025; paper submission December 5, 2025 (posted in
  UTC/AoE terms).
- Submission body was capped at 12 single-spaced numbered double-column pages including
  figures and tables; references and appendices were outside the cap.
- Review was double-blind (author identities hidden from reviewers, a policy in place since
  2017) and ran in two rounds: papers not advancing past round 1 received early rejection with
  reviews; round-2 survivors received a rebuttal opportunity limited to correcting factual
  errors and answering specific reviewer questions, with new results admissible only when
  directly responsive or promised for camera-ready.
- Artifact evaluation offered three independent ACM badges — Artifacts Available, Artifacts
  Evaluated — Functional, and Results Reproduced — reviewed single-blind by an Artifact
  Evaluation Committee that runs the authors' workflow scripts on the reviewers' own systems;
  passing badges are printed on the paper and recorded in the ACM DL.
- MobiSys proceedings are published in the ACM Digital Library.
- General Chairs were Fahim Kawsar (University of Glasgow) and Cecilia Mascolo (University of
  Cambridge).

## Organizers and fee model

- MobiSys is organized by the ACM Special Interest Group on Mobility of Systems, Users, Data
  and Computing (ACM SIGMOBILE). Like every CS conference in this collection, it has NO
  standing editor-in-chief; the rotating analogue is the per-edition General Chairs and
  Program Chairs. The 2026 General Chairs were Fahim Kawsar and Cecilia Mascolo; the Program
  Chairs are 待核实.
- There is NO article-processing charge. MobiSys funds the conference through registration
  fees; at least one author is expected to register and present in person. Accepted papers
  appear in the ACM Digital Library; ACM's rights and open-access options (including ACM Open)
  are settled at camera-ready rather than via an author-facing APC.
