# External Tools - ITCS

Access date: 2026-07-09

Use these surfaces after reopening the current ITCS cycle pages. ITCS mechanics — the single
early-September deadline, the lightweight double-blind rule, the LIPIcs format, and the absence
of a rebuttal — are decided per edition, and the `itcs2026.hotcrp.com` / `itcs-conf.org` /
`cs.unibocconi.eu` / `drops.dagstuhl.de` gateway may block direct fetches (read via search
renderings and cross-check dblp and LIPIcs/DROPS). Do not confuse this venue with the IEEE
Intelligent Transportation Systems Conference (*ITSC*) or the assorted "Information Technology
and Computer Science" workshops on WikiCFP.

## Cross-check surfaces (when the gateway blocks the primary page)

- dblp ITCS stream: https://dblp.org/db/conf/innovations/index.html (edition numbering, hosts,
  full author/title records; ICS 2010-2011 and ACM DL 2012-2016 editions, LIPIcs from 2017).
- LIPIcs/DROPS ITCS volume tables of contents for accepted-paper lists and page counts.
- The ITCS `itcs-conf.org/itcsNN/itcsNN-accepted.html` accepted-paper pages per edition.
- Preprint homes authors are encouraged to use (and which reveal the full version): arXiv
  (cs.CC / cs.DS / quant-ph), ECCC (Electronic Colloquium on Computational Complexity), and the
  IACR Cryptology ePrint Archive.
- Wikipedia's *Innovations in Theoretical Computer Science* entry for the founding and
  open-access history (initiated by Andrew Yao, 2010; LIPIcs from 2017).

## Author-side checks

- HotCRP profile, coauthors, and conflicts entered before the abstract/registration cutoff
  (early September); the abstract deadline precedes the full-paper deadline by ~2 days.
- **Lightweight double-blind sweep:** remove author names, affiliations, and emails from the PDF;
  neutralize "our previous work [12]" self-references in the prose — but **do not** anonymize the
  reference list itself, and you *may* keep a public arXiv/ECCC/ePrint full version.
- **Format:** single column, font size **>= 11pt**; no hard page limit, but a clear
  **first-10-pages** presentation of merits/significance/novelty; **complete proofs of all
  central claims** included (appendix permitted).
- **Prior-publication check:** the result is not published, presented, or under submission at
  another *archival* conference; a simultaneous *journal* submission is allowed.
- **No rebuttal:** there is no author-response phase — verify the current call still reflects
  this, and pre-empt reviewer objections inside the submission.
- Camera-ready (post-acceptance): the `lipics-v2021` document class, ACM CCS classification,
  keywords, funding acknowledgements, and the LIPIcs Creative-Commons license.

## Do not infer

- Do not infer ITCS 2027's host, dates, or deadline from ITCS 2026 — as of 2026-07-09 these are
  **待核实**; reopen the current call.
- Do not assume the camera-ready page limit, the exact anonymization wording, the dual-submission
  policy, or any generative-AI-disclosure rule carry over between editions.
- Do not import artifact/reproducibility/badging machinery: ITCS is pure theory with no code
  track. The analogue of an artifact is the **full-proof appendix** and the **arXiv/ECCC full
  version**.
- If pages disagree, treat the newest call for papers, the HotCRP deadlines page, or a direct
  chair announcement as controlling, and record the conflict.
