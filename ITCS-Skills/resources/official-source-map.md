# Official Source Map - ITCS

Access date: 2026-07-09

This map records the official and cross-check sources used for ITCS-specific facts in this
pack. ITCS is the **Innovations in Theoretical Computer Science** conference, a pure-theory
venue whose defining ethos is **conceptual novelty** — new models, new questions, new
directions — rather than incremental technical depth. Its papers are published **open access in
LIPIcs** (Leibniz International Proceedings in Informatics) by Schloss Dagstuhl, on a **single
annual deadline** in early September. Reopen the current-cycle call for papers, the HotCRP
deadlines page, and the LIPIcs author instructions before giving submission-ready advice.

## Verified 2026 facts used in skills

- **ITCS 2026 is the 17th Innovations in Theoretical Computer Science Conference**, held at
  **Bocconi University, Milan, Italy, 27-30 January 2026** (sources 3, 6, 8). As of the
  2026-07-09 access date this edition has concluded, so the live *next* target is ITCS 2027
  (details **待核实**, see below).
- **ITCS 2026 calendar:** abstract/registration **4 September 2025**, full-paper submission
  **6 September 2025** (7:59:59 PM EDT), author notification **~10 November 2025**, via
  `itcs2026.hotcrp.com` (sources 1, 2). This is the characteristic ITCS rhythm: **one annual
  deadline in early September**, notification in November, conference the following January.
- **Publication venue is LIPIcs** (Leibniz International Proceedings in Informatics), Schloss
  Dagstuhl, **open access, no article-processing charge**; ITCS 2026 is **LIPIcs Volume 362**
  (sources 6, 7). ITCS has published in LIPIcs since 2017 (source 9).
- **Review is lightweight double-blind:** submissions must not reveal author identity (names,
  affiliations, emails omitted from the PDF), but authors are **encouraged to post full versions
  to arXiv / ECCC / the Cryptology ePrint Archive**, and important references must **not** be
  anonymized (source 5). It is "lightweight" precisely because deanonymization via a public
  preprint is tolerated, not policed.
- **No rebuttal / author-response phase.** ITCS follows the theory-conference norm: the program
  committee reaches accept/reject decisions without an author-response round; there is no
  revise-and-resubmit and no camera-ready re-review of the argument (source 5; general TCS PC
  practice, cross-checked against the community PC-member guide).
- **Format:** single column, font size **at least 11 point**, **no hard submission page limit**;
  it is strongly recommended that the **first 10 pages** give a clear presentation of the paper's
  merits, significance, and place in the literature, and that **complete proofs of all central
  claims** be included (in an appendix if needed) (sources 1, 5).
- **Prior-publication / simultaneous-submission policy:** results already published, presented,
  or under submission at another **archival** conference are ineligible; **simultaneous
  submission to a journal is allowed** (sources 4, 5).
- **The Graduating Bits session** is a standing ITCS tradition: short talks by participants near
  graduation (on either side) to present results, plans, and themselves to the community
  (source 3; multiple editions).

## The conceptual-novelty ethos (what makes ITCS ITCS)

ITCS was founded (as *Innovations in Computer Science*, ICS, 2010) to be the theory venue that
explicitly **prizes conceptual contribution** — a new model, a new question, a new connection,
an unexpected direction — over the polished, incremental, technically deepest result that
STOC/FOCS reward. The selection test a PC applies is closer to *"is this a new question worth
asking, and does it open a direction?"* than *"is this the hardest theorem this year?"* This is
a genuinely different reviewing ethos from STOC and FOCS, and it is the through-line of every
skill in this pack. It is a **pure-theory** venue: papers are judged on **ideas and proofs**,
not code or experiments, so there is **no artifact-evaluation track and no reproducibility
package** in the empirical-CS sense — the analogue is the **full, checkable proof**.

## No editor-in-chief; no APC

Like every conference in this collection, ITCS has **no standing editor-in-chief**; the rotating
analogue is the per-edition **Program Committee Chair** and PC, with a local organizing team.
For ITCS 2026 the program chair is **reported** to be Shubhangi Saraf, with local organizers
Tommaso d'Orsi and Adam Polak and sponsorship from Apple and the Bocconi Department of Computing
Sciences (source 3; read via a single search rendering — treat as **reported**, re-confirm on
the official page). LIPIcs is **open access with no APC**; authors retain copyright under a
Creative Commons license.
