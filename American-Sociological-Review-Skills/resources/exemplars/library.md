# ASR Exemplars Library (method × topic)

> **Verified via web search, access date 2026-06-07.** Every paper below was checked to confirm it
> actually appeared in the **American Sociological Review** (ASA / SAGE), with author, year, and volume/
> issue, against the SAGE article page (`journals.sagepub.com/doi/...`) and, where available, the ASA
> journal record. Papers that could not be fully verified as ASR were **omitted**, and several
> well-known sociology papers that turned out to be in *sibling* journals are listed in the
> "do-not-misattribute" section below as guardrails. This is deliberately a short, clean, verified list
> rather than a long, uncertain one.
>
> **Sibling-confusion warning.** ASR is **not** the *American Journal of Sociology* (AJS), *Social
> Forces*, *Sociological Science*, the *Annual Review of Sociology*, or *Social Psychology Quarterly*.
> Several papers people assume are "ASR classics" are actually in AJS or the Annual Review (see the
> omissions section).
>
> **Use principle (zero hallucination):** this file gives **design positioning only**. It does **not**
> reproduce or invent coefficients, effect sizes, or specific findings — read the original on SAGE or
> the authors' pages before citing any number. For ASR-specific scope, length caps, masking, and house
> citation style, see [`../official-source-map.md`](../official-source-map.md).

## How to use this library

Pick the row whose **method × question** is closest to yours, then study how that paper makes a
*credible, on-its-own-terms* finding carry a **discipline-level theoretical lesson** — the ASR bar (see
[`../../skills/asr-topic-selection/SKILL.md`](../../skills/asr-topic-selection/SKILL.md),
[`../../skills/asr-theory-building/SKILL.md`](../../skills/asr-theory-building/SKILL.md), and
[`../../skills/asr-writing-style/SKILL.md`](../../skills/asr-writing-style/SKILL.md)). For each, ask
the self-check before claiming your paper is "ASR-shaped." Model the introduction on
[`../worked-examples/01-introduction.md`](../worked-examples/01-introduction.md).

## By method

### Field / audit experiment on discrimination (causal applied sociology)

- **Pager, Western & Bonikowski, "Discrimination in a Low-Wage Labor Market: A Field Experiment," ASR 2009, 74(5):777–799.**
  Verified: SAGE `journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/000312240907400505`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** an experimental audit design turns a contested question (does
    discrimination still operate at hiring?) into a credibly causal claim, then attaches it to a general
    mechanism of labor-market stratification — local design, disciplinary payoff. (Do **not** confuse
    with Pager's *single-authored* "Mark of a Criminal Record," which is **AJS 2003**.)
  - **Self-check:** does your design produce variation a referee will accept as exogenous, and does the
    result speak to a mechanism beyond the specific labor market?

### Ethnography that names a portable mechanism (qualitative / field site)

- **Goffman, "On the Run: Wanted Men in a Philadelphia Ghetto," ASR 2009, 74(3):339–357.**
  Verified: SAGE `journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/000312240907400301`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** rich fieldwork is raised to a **named mechanism** (how an apparatus of
    policing and supervision reshapes everyday social ties) rather than left as case description —
    exactly the move `asr-theory-building` demands of ethnography ("say what the case is a case *of*").
  - **Self-check:** does your site reveal a mechanism the discipline can carry elsewhere, with negative
    cases and saturation handled (`asr-research-design`)?

### Heterogeneous treatment effects in stratification (quantitative / demographic)

- **Brand & Xie, "Who Benefits Most from College? Evidence for Negative Selection in Heterogeneous Economic Returns to Higher Education," ASR 2010, 75(2):273–302.**
  Verified: SAGE `journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/0003122410363567`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a propensity-stratified design adjudicates a real theoretical dispute
    (positive vs. negative selection into the returns to college), so the empirics *settle an argument
    the field already has* rather than estimate an undisputed parameter. Pairs with the heterogeneity
    tooling in [`../code/`](../code/).
  - **Self-check:** does your estimate adjudicate a mechanism the literature actively debates, not just
    add a coefficient?

### Network change measured in repeated cross-section (network / quantitative)

- **McPherson, Smith-Lovin & Brashears, "Social Isolation in America: Changes in Core Discussion Networks over Two Decades," ASR 2006, 71(3):353–375.**
  Verified: SAGE `journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/000312240607100301`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a descriptive demographic question about personal networks is framed so it
    bears on social integration broadly — a network finding that the whole discipline must reckon with.
    (Note: an erratum was later issued over a GSS coding artifact — a useful reminder to read the
    original and its corrections before citing numbers.) Do **not** confuse with McPherson, Smith-Lovin
    & Cook, "Birds of a Feather," which is the **Annual Review of Sociology 2001**.
  - **Self-check:** does your descriptive trend carry a mechanism and a stake legible outside network
    sociology?

### Computational measurement of culture (latent-class / computational)

- **Bonikowski & DiMaggio, "Varieties of American Popular Nationalism," ASR 2016, 81(5):949–980.**
  Verified: SAGE `journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/0003122416663683`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** latent class analysis turns survey attitudes into a **typology** that
    reframes a concept (nationalism as heterogeneous kinds, not a single scale) — a methodological move
    in service of a portable cultural-sociology concept, validated against human-interpretable classes.
  - **Self-check:** does your computational result deliver a *substantive concept* the field can use, not
    just a metric (`asr-theory-building`, network/computational track)?

### Comparative-historical analysis of macro outcomes (comparative-historical)

- **Wimmer & Min, "From Empire to Nation-State: Explaining Wars in the Modern World, 1816–2001," ASR 2006, 71(6):867–897.**
  Verified: SAGE `journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/000312240607100601` (also archived on ASA's site).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** assembles a long-run, global historical dataset and specifies a causal
    logic (nation-state formation and ethno-political exclusion as a mechanism of war) — a
    comparative-historical argument with an explicit portable mechanism rather than a loose narrative.
  - **Self-check:** does your historical comparison specify the causal form and the counterfactual, and
    extract a logic that travels beyond the cases (`asr-research-design`, comparative-historical track)?

## Omitted for sibling-journal / verification reasons (do not attribute to ASR without re-checking)

To keep the list zero-hallucination, these widely-cited sociology papers were **excluded** — each was
verified to be in a **sibling journal**, not ASR:

- **Pager, "The Mark of a Criminal Record" (2003)** — verified in the **American Journal of Sociology**
  108(5):937–975, **not ASR**. (Her *co-authored* 2009 low-wage field experiment *is* ASR — listed
  above.)
- **Desmond, "Eviction and the Reproduction of Urban Poverty" (2012)** — verified in **AJS**
  118(1):88–133, **not ASR**.
- **Western & Pettit, "Black-White Wage Inequality, Employment Rates, and Incarceration" (2005)** —
  verified in **AJS** 111(2):553–578, **not ASR**.
- **Vaisey, "Motivation and Justification: A Dual-Process Model of Culture in Action" (2009)** —
  verified in **AJS** 114(6):1675–1715, **not ASR**.
- **Clemens, "Organizational Repertoires and Institutional Change" (1993)** — verified in **AJS**
  98(4):755–798, **not ASR**.
- **McPherson, Smith-Lovin & Cook, "Birds of a Feather: Homophily in Social Networks" (2001)** and
  **DiPrete & Eirich, "Cumulative Advantage as a Mechanism for Inequality" (2006)** — both in the
  **Annual Review of Sociology**, **not ASR**.

Before adding any new paper to this library, confirm it on `journals.sagepub.com/home/asr` /
`journals.sagepub.com/doi/...` (ASR article page with volume/issue) or the ASA journal record, update
the access-date header, and watch the AJS / Social Forces / Sociological Science / Annual Review /
Social Psychology Quarterly traps. **When in doubt, omit.**
