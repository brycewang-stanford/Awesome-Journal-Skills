# External Tools - IEEE INFOCOM

Access date: 2026-07-09

Use these surfaces after reopening the current INFOCOM cycle pages. INFOCOM mechanics — deadlines,
page budget, the early-reject phase, whether any rebuttal runs — are decided per edition, and the
`ieee-infocom.org` / `ieeexplore.ieee.org` / EDAS gateway may block direct fetches (read via search
renderings and cross-check dblp and the ComSoc listing). INFOCOM is the *networking* conference; do
not confuse it with unrelated products that share the name.

## Official workflow

- INFOCOM 2026 home (Tokyo, May 18-21, 2026): https://infocom2026.ieee-infocom.org/
- INFOCOM 2026 Call for Papers: https://infocom2026.ieee-infocom.org/call-papers
- INFOCOM 2026 Submission Guidelines (format, double-blind, page budget):
  https://infocom2026.ieee-infocom.org/submission-guidelines-main-conference
- INFOCOM 2026 Final Paper Submission Guidelines (eCF, PDF eXpress, registration):
  https://infocom2026.ieee-infocom.org/authors/final-paper-submission-guidelines-main-conference
- INFOCOM 2026 EDAS submission portal: https://edas.info/N33581
- INFOCOM 2027 home + CFP (next live target; Honolulu, May 24-27, 2027):
  https://infocom2027.ieee-infocom.org/
- IEEE PDF eXpress (camera-ready validation): https://ieee-pdf-express.org/
- IEEE electronic Copyright Form (eCF): reached via EDAS after acceptance
- IEEE Xplore (publication venue): https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/
- IEEE DataPort (optional dataset deposit): https://ieee-dataport.org/

## Cross-check surfaces (when the gateway blocks the primary page)

- dblp INFOCOM proceedings: https://dblp.org/db/conf/infocom/index.html (per-year editions, papers).
- ComSoc conference listing:
  https://www.comsoc.org/conferences-events/ieee-international-conference-computer-communications-2026
- INFOCOM history: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_Conference_on_Computer_Communications
  and the IEEE CS TCCC history page (https://cs-tccc.org/history/).
- Community statistics mirrors (acceptance rates, submission counts) — treat as reported, not
  official, and label accordingly.
- The EDAS deadline listing for the machine-readable cutoff the CFP paraphrases.

## Author-side checks

- EDAS profile, co-authors, and topic tags — the tags drive the **automated review assignment**, so
  match them to your evidence and subarea.
- The two-step timing: abstract/metadata (with a ≤200-word abstract) before the earlier deadline,
  then the anonymized full paper.
- IEEEtran.cls v1.8 compliance with the exact preamble `\documentclass[10pt, conference,
  letterpaper]{IEEEtran}`, and the page budget (10 pages max, ≤9 of text **including appendices**).
- Double-blind sweep: anonymize the PDF title page, body, and **metadata**; third-person
  self-citations; no testbed/cluster/tool names that reveal identity.
- The five-papers-per-author cap across all your group's submissions.
- Camera-ready gates: IEEE eCF copyright, IEEE PDF eXpress validation, and author registration
  (one registration covers up to three main-conference papers).

## Do not infer

- Do not infer whether the cycle runs an **early-reject** phase or offers **any author rebuttal** —
  INFOCOM 2027 posts an early-reject notification, and INFOCOM has traditionally offered no
  rebuttal; confirm both on the current call (**待核实** per cycle).
- Do not assume a formal **artifact-evaluation / badge track** exists — as of 2026-07-09 none was
  confirmed for INFOCOM (**待核实**).
- Do not assume the page budget, IEEEtran revision, per-author cap, or AI-disclosure wording carry
  over between editions.
- If pages disagree, treat the newest Call for Papers, Submission Guidelines, or a direct chair
  announcement as controlling, and record the conflict.
