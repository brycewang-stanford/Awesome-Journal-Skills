# Official Source Map - IEEE INFOCOM

Access date: 2026-07-09

This map records the official and cross-check sources used for INFOCOM-specific facts in this
pack. INFOCOM is the **IEEE Communications Society (ComSoc)** networking flagship; its papers are
published in the **IEEE INFOCOM Conference Proceedings and IEEE Xplore**. Its calendar, page
budget, and review mechanics are decided per edition. Reopen the current-cycle Call for Papers,
the Submission Guidelines page, and the EDAS site before giving submission-ready advice.

## Access method note

`csconfstats.*` returned **HTTP 403** from the verification gateway on 2026-07-09 (the same block
seen when verifying other IEEE/EDAS venues). Official pages were therefore read through
**search-engine renderings of the exact URLs** (the INFOCOM 2026 and 2027 sites, the Submission
Guidelines and Call for Papers pages, the Final Paper Submission Guidelines) and cross-checked
against the ComSoc conference listing, the IEEE CS TCCC / Wikipedia INFOCOM history, dblp INFOCOM
proceedings, and community statistics mirrors. Facts that could not be pinned to a primary
rendering are labeled **待核实**. Note: INFOCOM is the *networking* conference; do not confuse it
with unrelated products or services that share the name.

## Verified 2026/2027 facts used in skills

- **INFOCOM 2026 is the 45th IEEE International Conference on Computer Communications**, held in
  **Tokyo, Japan, 18-21 May 2026**. As of the 2026-07-09 access date this edition is already
  decided (notifications went out 8 Dec 2025), so the live *next* research target is INFOCOM 2027.
- **INFOCOM 2026 calendar:** abstract/metadata due **24 July 2025**, full paper due **31 July
  2025** (both 11:59pm AoE), acceptance notification **8 December 2025**, final camera-ready
  **9 January 2026** (11:59pm US ET). Submission via **EDAS** (`edas.info/N33581`) (sources 2, 4).
- **INFOCOM 2027 is the 46th edition**, **Honolulu, HI, USA, 24-27 May 2027**; abstract due
  **24 July 2026**, full paper **31 July 2026** (AoE); **early-reject notification 9 October
  2026**; acceptance notification **8 December 2026** (source 5). This is the live next target.
- **Format:** unmodified **IEEEtran.cls version 1.8**, `\documentclass[10pt, conference,
  letterpaper]{IEEEtran}`, 10-point Times, **two-column**. **Page budget: 10 pages maximum**, of
  which the **main text — including figures, tables, appendices, and everything other than
  references — must be no more than 9 pages**; references may occupy the remaining page. Papers
  over the limit are not reviewed (source 3).
- **Review is double-blind:** author identities are removed from the PDF title page, body, and
  metadata; reviewers do not see authors (source 3).
- **Automated review assignment:** submissions are matched to Technical Program Committee (TPC)
  members by an assignment system that analyzes manuscript content against TPC members'
  representative publications (source 3).
- **Two-tier, early-reject pipeline:** INFOCOM 2027 posts an **early-reject notification (9 Oct
  2026)** ahead of the final acceptance notification (8 Dec 2026), consistent with INFOCOM's
  large-scale multi-phase screening (source 5).
- **Author-count cap:** an individual may be listed on **at most FIVE** papers submitted to a
  given INFOCOM; only the first five received count if exceeded (source 2).
- **Publication venue is the IEEE INFOCOM Conference Proceedings and IEEE Xplore**; camera-ready
  requires the **IEEE electronic Copyright Form (eCF)** and **PDF eXpress** validation, and
  **at least one author must register** (one registration covers up to three main-conference
  papers) (source 4).
- **Scale and selectivity:** INFOCOM is a *large* flagship — on the order of ~1,400-1,500
  submissions with an acceptance rate around **18-20%** (INFOCOM 2025: 272/1,458 ≈ 18.7%; INFOCOM
  2024 ≈ 19.6%) (source 9).
- **History:** first held **1982** in Las Vegas; jointly sponsored by the IEEE Computer Society
  and ComSoc until **2002**, when it became solely ComSoc-sponsored (source 7).
- **Co-located tracks:** workshops, a demos/posters track, and a student poster session run
  alongside the main conference on their own calls and page limits (sources 1, 10).

## The review model (honest reading)

INFOCOM runs a **high-volume, double-blind, TPC-driven** process with automated paper-reviewer
matching and a **multi-phase decision** that includes an **early-reject** stage weeks before the
final notification. Unlike the small ACM/USENIX systems venues (SIGCOMM, NSDI, USENIX ATC),
INFOCOM has **traditionally not offered a standard author rebuttal / response period** — reviewer
scores and TPC discussion decide, and the submitted PDF must defend itself without an author
turn. Whether any given cycle adds a rebuttal window is a per-edition decision and is **待核实**
for the current cycle; verify on the live Submission Guidelines / reviewer timeline before
promising authors a response opportunity.

## No editor-in-chief; conference-registration cost model

Like every venue in this collection, INFOCOM has **no standing editor-in-chief**; the rotating
analogue is the per-edition General Co-Chairs and TPC Co-Chairs, appointed under IEEE ComSoc.
Publication cost is **conference registration**, not an article-processing charge, and at least
one author must register and present for the paper to enter IEEE Xplore.
