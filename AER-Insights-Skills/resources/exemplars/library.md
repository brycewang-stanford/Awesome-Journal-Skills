> for **Vol. 4, No. 1, March 2022** and **Vol. 6, No. 1, March 2024**) — confirming it appeared in

> carry the **`10.1257/aeri...`** DOI stem (the journal launched 2019). Ambiguous candidate papers are

> uncertain one.
>

> neighbors, which share the `10.1257` publisher prefix or the subject area:
> the *American Economic Review* (`10.1257/aer…`), the *AEJ* family — *Applied* (`10.1257/app…`),
> *Macroeconomics* (`10.1257/mac…`), *Microeconomics* (`10.1257/mic…`), *Economic Policy* (`10.1257/pol…`) —
> the *Journal of Economic Literature* / *Perspectives* (`10.1257/jel…` / `10.1257/jep…`), or the *QJE*.
> Only the **`10.1257/aeri`** stem confirms AER: Insights. The omissions section records a paper excluded on
> exactly this ground.
>
> **Use principle (zero hallucination):** this file gives **design positioning only**. It does not
> reproduce or invent estimates, parameters, or specific findings — read the original before citing any

## How to use this library

Pick the row whose **method × question** is closest to yours, then study how that paper makes **one
important, well-identified result** stand alone in a short paper — the AER: Insights bar (see
[`../../skills/aeri-topic-selection/SKILL.md`](../../skills/aeri-topic-selection/SKILL.md) and
[`../../skills/aeri-writing-style/SKILL.md`](../../skills/aeri-writing-style/SKILL.md)). For each, ask the
self-check before claiming your paper is "AER: Insights-shaped." Remember the house rule: report **standard
errors / confidence sets, never significance asterisks**
([`aeri-identification`](../../skills/aeri-identification/SKILL.md)), and that the whole argument must fit
the **length cap** with everything secondary in the Supplemental Appendix.

## By method

### Field experiment with a single primary effect (development / firms)

- **Hardy, Kagy & Song, "Gotta Have Money to Make Money? Bargaining Behavior and Financial Need of Microentrepreneurs," AER: Insights 2022, 4(1):1–17.**
  Verified on the AER: Insights Vol. 4 No. 1 (March 2022) issue page, `aeaweb.org/issues/669`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a focused experimental design isolates **one** behavioral mechanism
    (how financial need shapes bargaining), delivering a single clean, general-interest result rather than a
    battery of treatment arms in-text. Routes to
    [`../../skills/aeri-identification/SKILL.md`](../../skills/aeri-identification/SKILL.md) Branch B
    (pre-specified primary estimand) and pairs with
    [`../code/stata/03_did_modern.do`](../code/stata/03_did_modern.do) only for the analysis scaffold.
  - **Self-check:** does your experiment report **one** pre-registered primary effect in-text, with arms and
    heterogeneity in the appendix?

- **Brown, Hardy, Mbiti, McCasland & Salcher, "Can Financial Incentives to Firms Improve Apprenticeship Training? Experimental Evidence from Ghana," AER: Insights 2024, 6(1):120–36.**
  Verified on the AER: Insights Vol. 6 No. 1 (March 2024) issue page, `aeaweb.org/issues/754`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a firm-side RCT answers one policy-relevant question (do incentives raise
    training?) with a single headline contrast, the AER: Insights sweet spot for experiments.
  - **Self-check:** is the policy question answerable by **one** experimental comparison a non-specialist
    grasps immediately?

- **Premand & Rohner, "Cash and Conflict: Large-Scale Experimental Evidence from Niger," AER: Insights 2024, 6(1):137–53.**
  Verified on the AER: Insights Vol. 6 No. 1 (March 2024) issue page, `aeaweb.org/issues/754`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a large field experiment delivers a single, surprising general-interest fact
    (cash transfers and conflict) — important enough to stand alone, short enough to fit the cap.
  - **Self-check:** is your single experimental result **surprising and important** to a general audience,
    not just to the subfield?

### Quasi-experimental / measurement with administrative data

- **DeFusco, Enriquez & Yellen, "Wage Garnishment in the United States: New Facts from Administrative Payroll Records," AER: Insights 2024, 6(1):38–54.**
  Verified on `aeaweb.org/issues/754`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a **new-fact paper** built on administrative data — exactly the
    striking-measurement archetype AER: Insights publishes when one clean fact is important on its own.
    Connects to [`../../skills/aeri-topic-selection/SKILL.md`](../../skills/aeri-topic-selection/SKILL.md)
    (new fact / measurement) and
    [`../../skills/aeri-robustness/SKILL.md`](../../skills/aeri-robustness/SKILL.md) (one measurement-artifact
    check in-text).
  - **Self-check:** is your fact important enough to be the whole paper, and have you ruled out the single
    most threatening measurement artifact in-text?

- **Crane, Koch & Lin, "Real Effects of Markets on Politics: Evidence from US Presidential Elections," AER: Insights 2024, 6(1):73–88.**
  Verified on `aeaweb.org/issues/754`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** an identification-driven empirical result links two domains (markets and
    politics) in one general-interest finding, the kind of cross-cutting single insight AER: Insights favors.
  - **Self-check:** does your design isolate **one** credibly identified link, stated as a single headline?

### Belief / information measurement (survey and experiment design)

- **Roth, Settele & Wohlfart, "Risk Exposure and Acquisition of Macroeconomic Information," AER: Insights 2022, 4(1):34–53.**
  Verified on `aeaweb.org/issues/669`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a clean information-experiment / survey design pins **one** behavioral object
    (how risk exposure drives information acquisition), reported with explicit estimands rather than a
    sprawl of correlations.
  - **Self-check:** is your belief/information result a **single pre-specified estimand**, not a fishing
    expedition across survey items?

- **Drobner, "Motivated Beliefs and Anticipation of Uncertainty Resolution," AER: Insights 2022, 4(1):89–105.**
  Verified on `aeaweb.org/issues/669`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a tightly designed experiment delivers one sharp result on motivated beliefs —
    a compact, surprising contribution that needs no second study to land.
  - **Self-check:** does one experimental design carry the entire claim?

### Theory / mechanism design (a single sharp result)

- **Fernandez, Rudov & Yariv, "Centralized Matching with Incomplete Information," AER: Insights 2022, 4(1):18–33.**
  Verified on `aeaweb.org/issues/669`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a **theory short paper** — one clean result about matching under incomplete
    information, the minimal-model archetype in
    [`../../skills/aeri-theory-model/SKILL.md`](../../skills/aeri-theory-model/SKILL.md) (Case 1: the paper
    IS the theory).
  - **Self-check:** is your model the **smallest** one that produces a single surprising theorem, with
    proofs in the appendix?

- **Pollrich & Strausz, "The Irrelevance of Fee Structures for Certification," AER: Insights 2024, 6(1):55–72.**
  Verified on `aeaweb.org/issues/754`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a single, counterintuitive theoretical result (an irrelevance theorem) stated
    economically — the "sharp surprise in few pages" AER: Insights theory papers aim for.
  - **Self-check:** does your theory deliver one memorable, prior-overturning result rather than a general
    framework?

### Macro / structural with a focused quantitative point

- **McKay & Wieland, "Forward Guidance and Durable Goods Demand," AER: Insights 2022, 4(1):106–22.**
  Verified on `aeaweb.org/issues/669`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a structural macro mechanism is used to make **one** quantitative point about
    forward guidance, with the model kept minimal — the empirical-support modeling case in
    [`../../skills/aeri-theory-model/SKILL.md`](../../skills/aeri-theory-model/SKILL.md) (Case 2).
  - **Self-check:** does your model exist to deliver **one** object (a mechanism or counterfactual), with
    solution detail in the appendix?

## Omitted for lack of full verification, or to avoid sibling-confusion (do not attribute to AER: Insights without re-checking)

To keep the list zero-hallucination, the following were **excluded** after checking:

- **Angrist, Hull, Pathak & Walters, "Race and the Mismeasure of School Quality" (2024, 6(1):20–37)** —
  appears on the verified AER: Insights Vol. 6 No. 1 issue page and is a strong measurement exemplar; it is
  **listed here rather than in the main table only** because its precise method label is best read from the
  article itself before positioning a new paper against it. Confirm on `aeaweb.org/issues/754` and read the
  paper before citing. (Other genuine Vol. 6 No. 1 articles — Avery, Chen & McAdams on social distancing;
  Amaral, Dohmen, Kohl & Schularick on housing and interest rates; Gaduh, Hanna & Olken on corruption in
  social programs — are likewise verified on the issue page and may be added after reading each.)
- **Many frequently-cited "AER: Insights-like" short papers** surfaced in general search were left off
  because their **exact AER: Insights volume/issue/pages and `10.1257/aeri` DOI** was not confirmed in
  one pass against an official issue page; add them only after reading the AEA article page and confirming
  the `10.1257/aeri` stem. **Sibling-confusion guardrail:** a short, single-result economics paper is *not*
  AER: Insights merely because it is short — many such papers are in the *AER* (`aer`), an *AEJ*
  (`app`/`mac`/`mic`/`pol`), or another journal. The `aeri` stem, not brevity, is the tell.

Before adding any new paper to this library, confirm it on an `aeaweb.org` AER: Insights **issue page**
(with volume/issue/pages) **and** that the DOI begins `10.1257/aeri`, then update the access-date header.
When in doubt, omit.
