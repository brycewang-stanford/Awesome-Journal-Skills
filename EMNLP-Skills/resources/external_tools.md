# External Tools - EMNLP

Access date: 2026-07-08

EMNLP's tooling is split between the conference and ACL Rolling Review. Open both sides
before acting: the conference pages own the calendar, tracks, and commitment deadline;
the ARR pages own the format, checklist, and reviewing mechanics.

## Author-side verification passes

Run each pass against the live pages, not against this pack:

1. **Cycle pass** — confirm which ARR cycle feeds the EMNLP edition you target, the
   submission and commitment deadlines, and whether the cycle is shared with a sibling
   conference that requires a venue-preference declaration.
2. **Format pass** — current ACL template version, long/short content-page caps, the
   mandatory Limitations section, uncounted sections, and supplementary-material fields
   on the OpenReview form.
3. **Checklist pass** — the current Responsible NLP checklist questions, which answers
   are desk-reject enforced, and whether the checklist ships as a camera-ready appendix.
4. **Anonymity pass** — anonymized PDF, third-person self-reference, scrubbed
   acknowledgements and repository links; remember preprints are allowed but the
   submission itself must not link identity.
5. **Obligation pass** — the author registration form deadline, reciprocal-reviewing
   duties for the author list, and any qualification rules for reviewers.

## Do not infer

- Do not project the May 2026 cycle's dates onto any later cycle; ARR redraws the
  calendar and the conference alignment every year.
- Do not assume the camera-ready page bonus, Findings presentation options, or demo
  track dates from a previous EMNLP; each is set per edition.
- Do not treat ARR reviewer scores, checklist wording, or desk-reject enforcement as
  stable — ARR revises its own policy midyear and announces changes on its blog.
- When the conference call and an ARR page appear to conflict, the newer statement by
  the body that owns that policy controls (ARR for reviewing rules, the conference for
  its own calendar and decisions); record the conflict and ask the program chairs if
  it is decision-relevant.
