# Official Source Map - EMNLP

Access date: 2026-07-08

This map records the official sources behind every EMNLP-specific fact in this pack.
EMNLP submits through ACL Rolling Review (ARR), so its policy surface is split across
two owners: ARR sets the reviewing rules (format, checklist, anonymity, scores,
resubmission), while the conference sets the calendar, tracks, commitment deadline, and
acceptance decisions. Reopen both the current `20XX.emnlp.org` calls and the live
`aclrollingreview.org` pages before giving submission-ready advice.

## Verified 2026-cycle facts used in the skills

- EMNLP 2026 is held October 24-29, 2026 in Budapest, Hungary, as a hybrid conference
  (physical plus remote elements), announced through the official site and the
  conference's own channels.
- Main-conference papers travel through ACL Rolling Review, not a conference portal:
  ARR May 2026 submission deadline May 25, 2026; author registration form May 27;
  reviews due from reviewers July 6; author-reviewer discussion July 7-13; meta-review
  release July 30; **commitment to EMNLP by August 2, 2026**; acceptance notification
  August 20; camera-ready September 20.
- The May 2026 ARR cycle feeds two conferences (EMNLP 2026 and AACL-IJCNLP 2026);
  authors declared their intended venue at submission time.
- Format under ARR: ACL style templates only; long papers ≤ 8 content pages, short
  papers ≤ 4; unlimited references; a **mandatory Limitations section** placed after
  the conclusion that does not count toward the page limit; optional Ethical
  Considerations also uncounted; nonconforming or template-modified submissions are
  rejected without review.
- Review is double-blind on OpenReview; there is no anonymity period, so
  non-anonymous preprints may be posted and discussed at any time, but the submitted
  PDF must be anonymized (no revealing self-citations, acknowledgements, or
  repository links).
- Every submission files the Responsible NLP Research checklist; ARR has enforced
  desk rejection for incorrect, incomplete, or misleading checklist answers since
  December 2024, and EMNLP 2025 attached the completed checklist to camera-ready
  papers as an appendix.
- ARR reviewers score soundness, excitement, and an overall recommendation; an area
  chair writes a meta-review; after the round, authors commit to a venue or revise
  and resubmit, and resubmissions return to the same reviewer team where possible.
- Acceptance decisions are made by the conference (Senior Area Chairs and Program
  Chairs) after commitment, with two accept tiers: the main conference and Findings
  of EMNLP, the latter published in a separate Anthology volume and historically
  without a guaranteed presentation slot.
- EMNLP 2026 runs an Industry Track with direct OpenReview submission, deadline
  June 16, 2026 AoE, focused on production constraints; recent EMNLP editions also
  ran a System Demonstrations track (78 demo papers in 2025).
- The 2026 CFP explicitly warns against thinly sliced contributions, hallucinated
  citations, and entirely AI-generated papers, while permitting AI writing
  assistance.
- Proceedings and Findings volumes are published open access in the ACL Anthology;
  there is no article-processing charge. Like every conference in this collection,
  EMNLP has no standing editor-in-chief — the rotating analogue is the per-edition
  General Chair and Program Chairs, and ARR has its own rotating editors-in-chief
  independent of any single conference.
