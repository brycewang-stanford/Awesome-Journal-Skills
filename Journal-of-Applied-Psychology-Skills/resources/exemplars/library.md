> **Verified via web search, access date 2026-06.** Every paper below was checked to confirm it actually
> appeared in **_Journal of Applied Psychology_** (American Psychological Association; APA PsycNet /
> `psycnet.apa.org`; DOI family `10.1037/0021-9010…`), with year and volume(issue):pages. Papers that
> could not be cleanly verified as _Journal of Applied Psychology_ — including ones frequently
> **confused** with it — were **omitted** and documented below. It is deliberately a short, clean list

> Processes_ (OBHDP), _Journal of Organizational Behavior_ (JOB), _Administrative Science Quarterly_
> (ASQ), or _Psychological Bulletin_. Several famous I-O findings live in those venues — **not** here.
> Each row below was checked against this list.
>
> **Use principle (zero hallucination):** this file gives **design positioning only**. It does not

## How to use this library

Pick the row whose **method × question** is closest to yours, then study how that paper clears JAP's
**dual gate** — a genuine **theoretical contribution to I-O science** *and* **measurement/design
rigor** (see [`../../skills/joap-topic-selection/SKILL.md`](../../skills/joap-topic-selection/SKILL.md)
and [`../../skills/joap-theory-and-hypotheses/SKILL.md`](../../skills/joap-theory-and-hypotheses/SKILL.md)).
For each, ask the self-check question before claiming your paper is "_JAP_-shaped." Note that **rigorous
field studies, experiments, and psychometric meta-analyses** dominate this venue; cross-sectional
single-source surveys rarely clear the bar.

## By method

### Methods contribution that reshaped the field (critical review + recommendations)

- **Podsakoff, MacKenzie, Lee & Podsakoff, "Common Method Biases in Behavioral Research: A Critical
  Review of the Literature and Recommended Remedies," _Journal of Applied Psychology_ 2003,
  88(5):879–903.** Verified: APA PsycNet (DOI 10.1037/0021-9010.88.5.879).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a comprehensive treatment of common-method variance — sources, design
    remedies, and statistical checks — that became the field's reference point; the intellectual root of
    this pack's CMV emphasis ([`../../skills/joap-study-design/SKILL.md`](../../skills/joap-study-design/SKILL.md)).
  - **Self-check:** does your design *prevent* method bias procedurally, not just test for it post hoc?

### Theory-development article that launched a model (conceptual model + empirical test)

- **Demerouti, Bakker, Nachreiner & Schaufeli, "The Job Demands-Resources Model of Burnout,"
  _Journal of Applied Psychology_ 2001, 86(3):499–512.** Verified: APA PsycNet
  (DOI 10.1037/0021-9010.86.3.499).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a parsimonious theoretical model (demands vs. resources) with a clear
    mechanism and an empirical test, spawning thousands of follow-ups — a model of contribution-first I-O
    theory ([`../../skills/joap-theory-and-hypotheses/SKILL.md`](../../skills/joap-theory-and-hypotheses/SKILL.md)).
  - **Self-check:** does your framework predict something a simpler account cannot, with measurement to match?

### Psychometric meta-analysis that adjudicated a construct (quantitative synthesis)

- **Colquitt, Conlon, Wesson, Porter & Ng, "Justice at the Millennium: A Meta-Analytic Review of 25
  Years of Organizational Justice Research," _Journal of Applied Psychology_ 2001, 86(3):425–445.**
  Verified: APA PsycNet (DOI 10.1037/0021-9010.86.3.425).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a transparent, theory-driven meta-analysis that established the
    dimensionality of organizational justice and its incremental validity — a template for cumulative
    synthesis ([`../../skills/joap-data-analysis/SKILL.md`](../../skills/joap-data-analysis/SKILL.md)).
  - **Self-check:** does your synthesis resolve a construct/measurement question, with a transparent coding protocol?

### Meta-analysis adjudicating a construct's structure (review + meta-analysis)

- **LePine, Erez & Johnson, "The Nature and Dimensionality of Organizational Citizenship Behavior: A
  Critical Review and Meta-Analysis," _Journal of Applied Psychology_ 2002, 87(1):52–65.** Verified:
  APA PsycNet (DOI 10.1037/0021-9010.87.1.52).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** combines a critical conceptual review with a meta-analysis to ask whether
    OCB dimensions are empirically distinct — theory and measurement working together, the JAP signature.
  - **Self-check:** is your contribution about the *structure or validity* of a construct, not just one more correlate?

### Relative-validity meta-analysis of leadership styles (comparative quantitative synthesis)

- **Judge & Piccolo, "Transformational and Transactional Leadership: A Meta-Analytic Test of Their
  Relative Validity," _Journal of Applied Psychology_ 2004, 89(5):755–768.** Verified: APA PsycNet
  (record 2004-19456-002; DOI 10.1037/0021-9010.89.5.755).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** pits leadership styles against one another for relative validity across
    designs (longitudinal, multisource), modeling how to make a *comparative* cumulative claim
    ([`../../skills/joap-literature-positioning/SKILL.md`](../../skills/joap-literature-positioning/SKILL.md)).
  - **Self-check:** does your synthesis establish *relative* contribution, with design-based moderators reported?

## Omitted for lack of _Journal of Applied Psychology_ placement (do not attribute without re-checking)

To keep the list zero-hallucination, these frequently-cited I-O candidates were **excluded** after web
checking — several are classic sibling-journal confusions:

- **Barrick & Mount, "The Big Five Personality Dimensions and Job Performance: A Meta-Analysis" (1991)**
  — **_Personnel Psychology_** 44(1):1–26, **not** _JAP_ (a very common misattribution).
- **Schmidt & Hunter, "The Validity and Utility of Selection Methods in Personnel Psychology" (1998)**
  — **_Psychological Bulletin_** 124(2):262–274, **not** _JAP_.
- **Edmondson, "Psychological Safety and Learning Behavior in Work Teams" (1999)** —
  **_Administrative Science Quarterly_** (ASQ), **not** _JAP_.
- **Harrison, Newman & Roth, "How Important Are Job Attitudes? Meta-Analytic Comparisons…" (2006)** —
  **_Academy of Management Journal_** 49(2):305–325, **not** _JAP_.
- **Bakker, Demerouti et al. JD-R "review/theory" articles** appearing in
  **_Annual Review of Organizational Psychology and Organizational Behavior_** or **_Journal of
  Occupational Health Psychology_** — distinct from the 2001 _JAP_ JD-R article listed above; cite the
  correct venue.

Before adding any new paper to this library, confirm it on **APA PsycNet** under the
_Journal of Applied Psychology_ (DOI `10.1037/0021-9010…`, with volume/issue/pages) and update the
access-date header. When in doubt, omit. If web verification is unavailable, mark new rows
**待核实 / TO VERIFY** and attach **no** journal attribution until checked.
