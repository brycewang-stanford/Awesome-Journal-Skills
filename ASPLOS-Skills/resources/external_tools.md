# External Tools - ASPLOS

Access date: 2026-07-08

Open these official surfaces before acting on anything cycle-sensitive. ASPLOS resets
its deadline structure, template, and review mechanics per edition — the 2027 cycle
itself replaced the three-round model with two rounds, so even "how many deadlines are
there" is a fact to re-check, not to remember.

- ASPLOS 2027 Call for Papers: https://www.asplos-conference.org/asplos2027/cfp/
- ASPLOS conference hub (current edition): https://www.asplos-conference.org/
- April-cycle HotCRP (2027): https://asplos27-apr.hotcrp.com/ — the September-cycle

  window opens.

- Influential Paper Award list (for exemplars):
  https://www.sigarch.org/benefit/awards/acm-sigarch-sigplan-sigops-asplos-influential-paper-award/

If `asplos-conference.org` or HotCRP is unreachable from your network (the gateway used
to build this pack returned 403s), read the pages through a search engine's rendering
of the exact URL and cross-check against ACM DL and the SIGARCH postings — and say so
when you relay a fact.

## Author-side verification passes

Run each pass against the artifacts you will actually upload, not against drafts:

1. **Geometry pass** — compile with the mandatory template untouched; count the 11
   pages including every figure, table, and footnote; confirm references spill past
   page 11 rather than squeezing inside it.
2. **Citation-format pass** — scan the bibliography for "et al.", initials-only
   author names, missing DOIs, and unlinked citation numbers; ASPLOS's full-name rule
   is checked by real reviewers.
3. **Anonymity pass** — search the PDF text and metadata for author names, lab and
   grant identifiers, repository owners, and first-person self-citation; confirm any
   own-prior-work references read as third-party.
4. **Two-page pass** — print pages 1-2 alone and ask whether a rapid reviewer sees
   the problem, the cross-layer insight, and the headline evidence; nothing after
   page 2 is guaranteed a reading in the first round.
5. **HotCRP status pass** — the form state that counts is *complete*, before
   11:59 PM AoE on the cycle deadline; verify the submission shows as complete, not
   merely saved.

## Do not infer

- Do not project the 2027 two-deadline calendar onto 2028; the round structure has
  already changed once and can change again.
- Do not carry page limits, rebuttal norms, revision mechanics, badge criteria, or
  template versions across editions without reopening the current pages.
- Do not assume the ISCA/MICRO/SOSP conventions you know apply here — the citation
  full-name rule, the two-page rapid review, and the revision-counts-as-a-submission
  rule are ASPLOS-specific traps.
- When official pages disagree, the newer CFP text or a direct chairs' announcement
  controls; record the conflict in your notes rather than silently picking one.
