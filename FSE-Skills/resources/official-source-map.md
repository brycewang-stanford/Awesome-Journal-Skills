# Official Source Map - ESEC/FSE

Access date: 2026-07-09

This map records the official and cross-check sources used for FSE-specific facts in this
pack. FSE is the ACM SIGSOFT flagship whose research papers are published as journal articles
in **PACMSE (Proceedings of the ACM on Software Engineering)**; its calendar, page budget, and
review mechanics are decided per edition. Reopen the current-cycle research-papers call, the
Important Dates page, the PACMSE journal pages, and the ACM artifact policy before giving
submission-ready advice.

## Access method note

Direct fetches of `conf.researchr.org` and `dl.acm.org` returned **HTTP 403** from the
verification gateway on 2026-07-09 (the same block seen when verifying other ACM/researchr
venues). Official pages were therefore read through **search-engine renderings of the exact
URLs** and cross-checked against the FSE HotCRP sites, the ACM Digital Library PACMSE tables of
contents, dblp, and the SIGSOFT awards pages. Facts that could not be pinned to a primary
rendering are labeled **待核实**. Beware a name collision: `fse.iacr.org` is *Fast Software
Encryption*, a cryptography conference, and is **not** this FSE — no fact here derives from it.

## Verified 2026/2027 facts used in skills

- **FSE 2026 is the 34th ACM International Conference on the Foundations of Software
  Engineering**, held in Montreal, Canada (Concordia SGW Campus, Downtown Montreal),
  **July 5-9, 2026**. As of the 2026-07-09 access date this edition is in progress, so the
  live *next* research-paper target is FSE 2027.
- **FSE 2026 research-track calendar:** mandatory paper registration **4 September 2025**, full
  paper submission **11 September 2025** (23:59 AoE), via `fse2026.hotcrp.com`. General Chairs
  Shin Hwei Tan and Foutse Khomh; Lili Wei listed as Proceedings Co-Chair (source 2/3;
  organizing-committee page).
- **FSE 2027 is the 35th edition**, Shenzhen, China, **July 12-16, 2027**; research-paper
  deadline **2 October 2026** (AoE); page budget **≤18 pages** for all text and figures plus
  **4 pages** for references only; each submission reviewed by at least three PC members
  (source 4).
- **Publication venue is PACMSE**, the open-access *Proceedings of the ACM on Software
  Engineering* journal. PACMSE Issue FSE volumes: Vol 1 = FSE 2024, Vol 2 = FSE 2025,
  Vol 3 = FSE 2026 (ACM DL tables of contents; sources 5-6).
- **Review is double-anonymous ("heavy"):** anonymity is maintained through all reviewing and
  discussion periods, and in a Major Revision the author response letter must itself stay
  anonymous and add no author-revealing information (source 1).
- **Decision categories include Major Revision**, a journal-style revise-and-resubmit round in
  which (generally) the original reviewers re-review the revised paper within the same cycle,
  rather than a plain accept/reject (source 10).
- **The ACM Primary Article Template** is the required format; artifacts follow the **ACM
  Artifact Review and Badging** scheme (sources 1, 8).
- **The ESEC/FSE Test of Time Award** was installed by the FSE steering committee in 2018 and
  recognizes a highly influential paper published ten years earlier (source 9).

## The PACMSE journal-style model (honest reading of the cycle question)

FSE's move into PACMSE (from FSE 2024) reframed the research track as a **journal**: accepted
papers are PACMSE articles, and the process carries a genuine **Major Revision** round.
Historically FSE ran a *two-deadline* structure so that a Major Revision from an earlier
deadline could be resubmitted to a later one within the same review year; FSE 2024 operated
that dual-deadline model. The 2025, 2026, and 2027 calls each advertise a **single annual
research-paper deadline** with the Major Revision round inside it. Whether a given year runs one
deadline or two is exactly the kind of thing that changes per edition — **verify the cycle count
on the current Important Dates page before advising**, and never carry a previous year's cadence
forward.

## No editor-in-chief; no APC

Like every conference in this collection, FSE has **no standing editor-in-chief**; the rotating
analogue is the per-edition General Chairs and Program/Review Co-Chairs, appointed by the FSE
steering committee and ACM SIGSOFT. PACMSE is **open access with no article-processing charge**;
the cost model is conference registration, and at least one author presents the accepted paper.

- Exact page budget and which template revision is required (FSE 2027 posts 18+4).
- Major Revision mechanics, response-letter format and length, and re-review assignment.
- Artifact-evaluation track deadlines, offered badges, and whether evaluation is mandatory.
