# External Tools & Resources for HLR-Style Legal Scholarship

Databases, citation tooling, and the submission ecosystem useful when preparing a *Harvard Law Review*
(HLR) Article, Essay, or Book Review. HLR is **generalist and student-edited**, cited per **The Bluebook**
(which HLR co-publishes), and placed through the U.S. law-review market (Scholastica multi-submit +
expedite). Check licenses and current access terms before use, and verify any HLR-specific policy in
[`official-source-map.md`](official-source-map.md).

## 1. Legal research & preemption databases

| Source | Provider | Typical use |
|--------|----------|-------------|
| **Westlaw** | Thomson Reuters | Cases, statutes, regulations, journals; KeyCite for citator validity |
| **Lexis / Lexis+** | LexisNexis | Cases, statutes, journals; Shepard's citator |
| **HeinOnline** | William S. Hein & Co. | Deep historical law-review archive (PDF page images), exact pinpoint history |
| **SSRN** | Elsevier | Working papers and forthcoming articles — the highest preemption risk |
| **Google Scholar** | Google | Cross-disciplinary reach, citation chains, gray literature |
| **bepress / Digital Commons** | Elsevier | Institutional repositories, student scholarship |
| **Bloomberg Law** | Bloomberg | Cases, dockets, secondary sources, practice materials |

> For the preemption check, run **all** of SSRN, Westlaw/Lexis, HeinOnline, and Scholar — in-press
> preemption hides on SSRN (see [`../skills/hlr-preemption-check/SKILL.md`](../skills/hlr-preemption-check/SKILL.md)).

## 2. Primary-authority sources (free / official)

- **Supreme Court of the United States** — slip opinions, orders, oral-argument transcripts (supremecourt.gov)
- **govinfo.gov / Congress.gov** — U.S. Code, the Statutes at Large, the Federal Register, legislative history
- **Court websites / PACER** — federal dockets and filings (PACER is fee-based)
- **State legislature and court sites** — state statutes and decisions
- **Caselaw Access Project (Harvard)** — digitized U.S. case law

## 3. Citation & the apparatus

- **The Bluebook: A Uniform System of Citation** — the governing citation manual; HLR co-publishes it with
  the Columbia Law Review, the University of Pennsylvania Law Review, and the Yale Law Journal. Apply the
  **academic (law-review) footnote** conventions, not the practitioner forms.
- **Citators**: **Shepard's** (Lexis) / **KeyCite** (Westlaw) — confirm cited authority is still good law.
- **Perma.cc** (Harvard Library Innovation Lab) — archive web sources against link rot; capture an access date.
- **Reference managers**: Zotero, EndNote — for managing the source file behind a heavy footnote apparatus.
- **Citation-checking aids**: word-processor footnote tools; verify *Id.*/*supra* integrity after edits.

## 4. The submission ecosystem (placement + expedite)

| Tool | Role |
|------|------|
| **Scholastica** | The dominant law-review submission platform — multi-submit, discussion messages, bulk expedite and withdrawal |
| **ExpressO** (bepress) | Alternative/legacy submission service used by some journals |
| Season trackers | Community spreadsheets and Scholastica's submission-season resources track open dates and offer activity |

> Submit in the **Feb-Mar** main season or the smaller **Aug** season; expedite **only with an offer in
> hand** (see [`../skills/hlr-placement-strategy/SKILL.md`](../skills/hlr-placement-strategy/SKILL.md)).

## 5. Writing & manuscript prep

- Word processors with robust footnote handling (Word, or LaTeX for authors who prefer it)
- A clean **source file**: every cited source kept with the cited page marked, for the cite-check / source-pull
- Abstract + CV ready for the Scholastica package

## 6. Process & portal (verify volatile items)

| Item | Note |
|------|------|
| Owner / publisher | **Harvard Law Review Association**; affiliated with **Harvard Law School** |
| Review model | **Student-edited** (not peer-reviewed); rigorous editorial process incl. cite-check / source-pull |
| Portal | **Scholastica** (检索于 2026-06；以官网为准 for any additional channel) |
| Citation style | **The Bluebook** (HLR co-publisher) |
| What HLR publishes | Articles, Essays, Book Reviews; student Notes (unsigned); the Supreme Court issue + Foreword |
| Expedite / decision window | Honors expedites where possible without skipping review stages; ≥ 7-day decision window committed (检索于 2026-06；以官网为准) |
| Fee | **No submission fee / no APC** — HLR takes manuscripts on its own electronic submission system, and article selection is by student editors (Scholastica/ExpressO fees, where they exist, are the peer-journal market, not HLR's own intake). Verified 2026-06-22; 以官网为准 |

## 7. Cautions

1. **Verify volatile specifics** (length guidance, fee, channel, expedite/seven-day policy) on the
   official HLR submissions page — they change; unverified items are marked 待核实 / (检索于 2026-06；以官网为准).
2. **Run the full preemption set** before drafting — skipping SSRN is the costliest mistake.
3. **Cite primary over secondary** and **pin every proposition** — the source-pull verifies each footnote.
4. **Archive web sources** (Perma.cc) with access dates — link rot surfaces at proofs.
5. **Expedite only with an offer in hand** — it is your only leverage and editor goodwill is finite.
