# Harvard Law Review — Resources

Capability layer for the **Harvard Law Review** skill pack. Legal scholarship is **not** an empirical
data pipeline, so this pack **does not vendor a `code/` kit**: the value is in legal research databases,
the citation apparatus, the placement ecosystem, and exemplar awareness. The cross-cutting empirical
material in the shared hub is referenced below only as **background** for the rare empirical-legal piece.

## Contents

| Resource | What it gives an agent |
|---|---|
| [`worked-examples/01-introduction.md`](worked-examples/01-introduction.md) | A before→after rewrite of a law-review article introduction in HLR house style (live problem → thesis → contribution → payoff → roadmap, with the argument front-loaded and support in footnotes). Illustrative **fictional** piece. |

| [`external_tools.md`](external_tools.md) | Legal research databases (Westlaw, Lexis, HeinOnline, SSRN), citation tooling (The Bluebook, Shepard's/KeyCite, Perma.cc), and the submission ecosystem (Scholastica, ExpressO). |

## Background (shared empirical hub — for the rare empirical-legal piece only)

Most HLR pieces are doctrinal and normative and need none of this. If a piece runs original empirical
analysis (an empirical-legal-studies article), the shared hub offers venue-neutral background:

- [reviewer-objection-checklist](../../shared-resources/empirical-methods/reviewer-objection-checklist.md) — objections an empirical design must preempt, by identification strategy.
- [reporting-standards](../../shared-resources/empirical-methods/reporting-standards.md) — modern inference + reporting table stakes for empirical work.

> This is background only. HLR is generalist and student-edited; its scholarship is overwhelmingly
> doctrinal/normative, and the skills + `official-source-map.md` govern what *this* venue requires.

## How to use

1. **Before drafting** — confirm originality with the preemption databases in `external_tools.md`
   (see [`../skills/hlr-preemption-check/SKILL.md`](../skills/hlr-preemption-check/SKILL.md)).
2. **While drafting** — keep the footnote apparatus pull-ready (see
   [`../skills/hlr-footnotes-and-cite-check/SKILL.md`](../skills/hlr-footnotes-and-cite-check/SKILL.md)).
3. **Before submission** — walk `official-source-map.md` for the current student-edited process,
   Scholastica channel, and expedite/seven-day policy.

> Facts here are venue-specific; always defer to this pack's skills and `official-source-map.md` for what
> *Harvard Law Review* specifically requires, and verify volatile items on the official page.
