# Topic Selection & Fit (amanthro-topic-selection)

AA is the **flagship four-field journal** of the American Anthropological Association. The bar is not
"new to my subfield" — it is **"matters to anthropology broadly."** A great paper makes a finding in
sociocultural, archaeological, biological, or linguistic anthropology legible and useful to readers in
the *other* fields. Use this skill to pressure-test fit and section before you invest.

## When to trigger

- Choosing among possible projects or framings for an AA submission
- A reviewer/colleague said the paper feels "too narrow" or "subfield-only"
- Deciding among Research Article and the journal's shorter/engaged sections
- A field or community partner has reframed what the project is *for*

## The AA fit test

A strong AA paper usually clears all four:

1. **Four-field reach.** A scholar in a *different* subfield should see why it matters — the shared
   stakes are anthropology's enduring questions: culture, power, kinship, exchange, language,
   embodiment, deep history, human variation, colonialism and its afterlives.
2. **A real contribution, not just a description.** It changes how the field thinks, theorizes, or
   accounts for something — a new concept, a reframing, a decisive case, a corrected record.
3. **Credible on its own methodological terms.** Ethnographic, archival, material/archaeological,
   bioanthropological, or linguistic — each is welcome but each must be rigorous and reflexive
   (see `amanthro-research-design`).
4. **Ethically sound.** The relationship to interlocutors, communities, descendants, and heritage is
   accountable; consent and care are part of the design, not an afterthought
   (see `amanthro-transparency-and-data`).

## Reach past your home subfield

| Home subfield | Reach to four-field anthropology by… |
|---------------|---------------------------------------|
| Sociocultural | draw the portable concept/mechanism, not just the ethnographic particular; speak to theory others can use |
| Archaeology | connect material/temporal evidence to general claims about social life, not only site chronology |
| Biological / physical | tie human-variation or evolutionary findings to social and cultural meaning, not biology alone |
| Linguistic | show what the language data reveal about culture, power, or cognition beyond the grammar |
| Reflexive / public | make the methodological/ethical/political stake one the whole discipline shares |

## Section choice

- **Research Article** — full study, broad claim, **≤ 8,000 words** (extendable to ~10,500 at the
  editors' discretion on acceptance).
- **Vital Topics Forum** — a curated multi-author debate on a pressing issue (often invited/proposed).
- **World Anthropologies** — anthropology produced beyond the Anglophone center; decenters the canon.
- **Public Anthropologies / Multimodal Anthropologies** — engaged, activist, or media-based work where
  image/sound/film is part of the argument (see `amanthro-tables-figures`).
- **Review Essay** — a synthetic essay across **2–5** recent books (≤ 5,000 words).

## Anti-patterns

- "No one has studied community X" as the whole contribution (descriptive, subfield-only)
- A method showcase with no anthropological payoff readers in other fields can use
- A sprawling project that cannot be argued within the section's word cap
- Picking Research Article out of habit when a forum, essay, or multimodal piece would land harder
- Treating ethics/consent as a later checkbox rather than a reason the project is even askable

## Output format

```
【Question】one sentence
【Four-field reach】who outside the subfield cares, and why
【Contribution type】concept / reframing / decisive case / corrected record
【Section】Research Article / Vital Topics / World / Public / Multimodal / Review Essay
【Ethics flag】any consent / community-accountability / heritage issue to design for now?
【Fit verdict】strong / needs reframing / off-fit (why)
【Next】amanthro-literature-positioning
```
