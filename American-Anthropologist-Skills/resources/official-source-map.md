# AA Official Source Map

Primary and authoritative sources behind the American Anthropologist (AA)-specific facts injected into
this skill pack. Access date for all entries: **2026-06-20**. Re-check volatile items (portal prompts,
editorial-board roster, section availability, APCs, funder rules, and production-file details) on the
live AA / AAA / Wiley pages before using this pack for a live submission.

## Official journal, scope, masthead & transition

- **https://www.americananthropologist.org/** — AA editorial site home. Verifies: AA is the journal of
  the American Anthropological Association; AA welcomes manuscripts from within a single discipline as
  well as across subdisciplines; editors seek theoretically, methodologically, and empirically important
  new work that improves understanding of culturally significant and practically important issues in the
  present and past; main ideas should be comprehensible to nonspecialists; editors encourage clear
  writing and discourage overuse of jargon.
- **https://www.americananthropologist.org/about** — AA "About the Journal" page. Verifies: AA is the
  **flagship journal of the American Anthropological Association**; it advances research on humankind in
  archaeological, biological, sociocultural, and linguistic research, including practicing
  anthropologists and anthropologists outside the academy; current Editor-in-Chief **Elizabeth J. Chin**
  (ArtCenter College of Design) — **current through 2026, re-confirmed 2026-06-22** (Dána-Ain Davis &
  Sameena Mulla take over 2027-01-01) — Managing Editor **Julian Gantt**, associate editors across archaeology,
  biological anthropology, cultural anthropology, linguistic anthropology, multimodal anthropology,
  public anthropology, world anthropology, book reviews, obituaries, and online content, plus an
  editorial board.
- **https://americananthro.org/news/dana-ain-davis-and-sameena-mulla-appointed-editors-in-chief-of-american-anthropologist/**
  — AAA news announcement. Verifies: **Dána-Ain Davis** and **Sameena Mulla** have been selected to serve
  as AA Editors-in-Chief effective **2027-01-01**; they take over from **Elizabeth Chin**, who has been
  Editor-in-Chief since **2020** and is completing her sixth year; AA is published quarterly and advances
  AAA's mission through research articles, commentaries, essays, and reviews of books and multimedia
  work.
- **https://anthrosource.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/journal/15481433** — AnthroSource / Wiley journal
  home. Use as publisher home, current issue, ISSN route, article access, open-access, and author-rights
  entry point.
- **https://authors.wiley.com/asset/ACT.xlsx** — Wiley Author Compliance Tool workbook, `AMAN` /
  ISSN route `15481433` row checked 2026-06-20. Verifies: **Hybrid Open Access**; CC license choice
  **CC BY, CC BY-NC, or CC BY-NC-ND**; optional open-access APC **USD 3,570**; self-archiving policy
  **Non-Standard Policy** with submitted version allowed on submission and accepted version governed by
  copyright/managing-editor guidance; journal-specific Wiley data-sharing tier **No Policy**; Wiley
  preprint-policy field **Does Not Accept Preprints**; ORCID policy **Required**; review model
  **Double anonymized**.

## Submission route, types, length & style

- **https://www.americananthropologist.org/how-to-submit** — AA "Submitting to the Journal" page.
  Verifies:
  - **Aims and scope:** AA advances research on humankind in archaeological, biological,
    sociocultural, and linguistic research, including applied/practicing and international work, and
    welcomes experimental and multimodal contributions.
  - **Portal:** all new manuscripts are submitted through **Research Exchange** as of **2025-04-15**;
    manuscripts started before that date use the earlier workflow.
  - **Fees:** AA charges authors **no submission fee and no publication fee**; optional OnlineOpen is
    separate and only applies when authors choose open access after acceptance.
  - **Current section availability:** AA states it is currently **not accepting proposals for special
    sections or Vital Topics Forums**.
  - **Research Articles:** up to **8,000 words** including figures, tables, references, and notes, but
    excluding transcriptions and the **200-word abstract**; initial submissions above 8,000 words are
    not considered; accepted research articles may extend to **10,500 words** at associate-editor and
    editor-in-chief discretion.
  - **Shorter formats:** Essays **2,000 to 3,000 words**; Commentaries up to **1,500 words**;
    Interviews around **2,000 words**; Review Essays up to **5,000 words** covering **2 to 5** recent
    publications; single-book reviews **1,000 words**.
  - **Review/anonymity:** AA conducts anonymous review; upload a Word main document with no identifying
    author information in the file name, a separate title page with author details, and figures with no
    identifying author information in file names; self-references should be anonymized and omitted from
    the reference list at this stage.
  - **Preparation:** Word document; 12-point Times New Roman; double spaced; left-justified; endnotes;
    final manuscript without tracked changes, macros, fields, or other encoded text.
  - **Style:** AAA journals accept **free-format** submissions; references must include DOI when
    available; final accepted files use Chicago Manual of Style author-date pattern; references should
    be listed alphabetically and not embedded in endnotes.
  - **Ethics/accessibility:** editors encourage inclusive language, reflective land acknowledgments
    where appropriate, citation practices that promote equity/accountability/curiosity/inclusivity, alt
    text and image descriptions, figure captions/credits, and permission clearance for images, film
    quotations, song lyrics, and most fiction/poetry outside the public domain.
  - **Figures/media:** figures should be TIF files at **300 dpi or greater**; tables should be Word
    documents with title, description, alt text, and image descriptions; videos should include
    description and captioning where appropriate.
  - **Author responsibilities:** authors are responsible for content accuracy, quotation attribution,
    legal rights to publish submitted material, manuscript form, and avoiding simultaneous journal
    consideration or prior publication.
  - **ORCID and licensing:** AAA journals require ORCID; accepted papers route the formal corresponding
    author through Wiley Author Licensing Service; OnlineOpen articles go through the same peer-review
    process as subscription articles and, when selected, require a payment form and CC license choice.

## Online content, preprints & sharing

- **https://www.americananthropologist.org/online-content** — AA online-content page. Verifies the
  active online categories used by this pack: **Public Anthropologies**, **World Anthropologies**,
  **Multimodal Anthropologies**, **Virtual Issues**, **Vital Topics Forums**, and **Highlights**.
- **https://www.americananthropologist.org/how-to-submit** — AA manuscript-sharing section. Verifies:
  AA supports authors who wish to upload the submitted version before peer review to AAA's
  **Open Anthropology Research Repository** and points authors to Wiley article-sharing guidance.
  Use this AA-specific sharing route instead of assuming a generic preprint rule, because the Wiley ACT
  row records a stricter generic preprint-policy field for this journal.
- **https://anthrosource.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/hub/journal/15481433/about/open-access** — AA open
  access page. Cross-checks optional OnlineOpen availability and the APC route; use Wiley/AnthroSource
  live pages for currency-specific payment details at acceptance.

## Ethics, data & open-science guardrails

- **https://americananthro.org/about/anthropological-ethics/** — AAA anthropological ethics page.
  Verifies the AAA Principles of Professional Responsibility used by this pack: **Do No Harm**; **Be
  Open and Honest Regarding Your Work**; **Obtain Informed Consent and Necessary Permissions**; **Weigh
  Competing Ethical Obligations Due Collaborators and Affected Parties**; **Make Your Results
  Accessible**; **Protect and Preserve Your Records**; **Maintain Respectful and Ethical Professional
  Relationships**.
- **https://ethics.americananthro.org/ethics-statement-0-preamble/** and
  **https://ethics.americananthro.org/ethics-statement-1-do-no-harm/** — full 2012 AAA ethics
  statement pages. Provide the fuller ethics basis for do-no-harm, informed consent, openness,
  competing obligations, records, and accessibility. Use these as ethics anchors, with the current AAA
  ethics landing page as the concise live index.
- **https://authors.wiley.com/author-resources/Journal-Authors/open-access/data-sharing-citation/data-sharing-policy.html**
  — Wiley data-sharing policy overview. Verifies Wiley's standardized data-sharing tiers and DAS
  templates. Because the AA row in the Wiley ACT workbook lists the journal-specific data-sharing tier
  as **No Policy**, this pack does not encode an AA-specific mandatory data deposit. The operational
  guidance is ethics-centered: share what consent, community authority, and legal constraints allow;
  document withheld sensitive data; and follow any Research Exchange prompt for a data-availability
  statement.

## Pack-level guardrails

- Do not route new AA manuscripts to an earlier platform; use Research Exchange for new submissions.
- Do not treat OnlineOpen APCs as submission or publication fees. AA states there is no submission or
  publication fee; APCs apply only when authors choose open access.
- Do not treat section proposals as open by default; AA currently says special-section and Vital Topics
  Forum proposals are paused.
- Do not hard-code future editorial rosters after the 2027 transition without checking the live AA About
  page and AAA news.
- Do not impose a data-deposit mandate on sensitive ethnographic or heritage data. AA/AAA ethics can
  require withholding or masking material even when other fields would share.
