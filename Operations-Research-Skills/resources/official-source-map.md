## VERIFIED facts injected into the skills (with source #)

- **Sponsor / publisher:** INFORMS (Institute for Operations Research and the Management Sciences). (#1)
- **Editor-in-Chief:** Amy R. Ward (University of Chicago Booth School of Business); term began **1 January 2024**; immediate former EiC **John Birge**. Two back-up EiCs handle conflict-of-interest papers: **Jim Dai** and **Jan Karel Lenstra**. (#1, #8, #9)
- **Field focus:** OR/MS methodology — optimization, stochastic/probabilistic models, simulation, decision analysis — spanning mathematical theory to real-world OR innovations; methodological novelty and rigor prized over purely empirical work. (#1, #2, #4)
- **Review model:** soft double-anonymous. Author names omitted from the submission; reviewers cannot see author names; the Area Editor and Associate Editor can; authors **can** see the handling Area Editor's name but not the Associate Editor or reviewers. (#2)
- **Submission system:** ScholarOne Manuscripts (at mc.manuscriptcentral.com/opre), entered via the INFORMS Author Portal. (#1, #2, #8)
- **Abstract:** must not exceed **200 words**; text-only (minimize math symbols, avoid accented variables). (#2)
- **Keywords:** up to **three**. (#2)
- **Formatting:** 1.5-spaced, **11-point** font, **1-inch** margins all around; PDF at submission (source LaTeX/Word required on acceptance); LaTeX style files provided; author-year citations, e.g., "(Norman 1977)" or "Norman (1977)". (#2)
- **Length tiers (excl. references):** Focused Technical = max **20 pages**; Context-and-Challenge = max **20 pages**; regular = max **30 pages**; lengthy generally no more than **~40 pages**. E-companion for regular/lengthy must not be longer than the manuscript; Context-and-Challenge papers have **no** e-companion. (#2)
- **Equation-free introduction:** the introduction must state the problem, the results, and their significance to the OR community and must **not** contain equations or mathematical notation. (#2)
- **Editor/reviewer nomination:** authors recommend **3 Associate Editors** from the chosen area and suggest **5 reviewers** at submission. (#2)
- **Contribution statement:** since **1 June 2023**, the cover letter must include a statement in **fewer than 500 words** articulating the manuscript's novel, innovative, and rigorous original contribution to operations research. (#3)
- **Area routing:** submissions route into one of the journal's **14 named editorial areas**, overseen by **21 Area Editors** (per the EiC reports): Decision Analysis; Energy and Environment; Financial Engineering; Machine Learning and Data Science; Markets, Platforms, and Revenue Management; Defense and Security; Operations and Supply Chain; Optimization; Real-World OR Innovations; Societal Impact; Simulation; Stochastic Models; Transportation; and Data, Software, and Computation. Area Editors set scope via Area Editors' Statements; authors select the correct area at submission. (#4, #8, #9)
- **Open access:** the **INFORMS Open Option (IOO)** makes an accepted article open access for a **US$3,000** fee, invoiced after acceptance; it does not affect peer review. (#2)
- **Code and Data Disclosure Policy:** for papers with algorithmic/empirical components, authors are expected to provide all code, scripts, and data with instructions sufficient for others to easily reproduce the results; exemptions (non-public/confidential/licensed data, purely methodological papers) requested with rationale from the Area Editor in the cover letter and decided by the Area Editor with final EiC authority. The **last section of the paper must provide a data and code accessibility statement** referring to supporting data, code, and other materials. Papers that do not go through code-and-data review must still make code and data available to readers; papers that complete the review carry front-matter verification and a hosted reproducibility page. (#5, #8)
- **Reproducibility workflow:** materials deposited in the journal's **ORJournal GitHub** organization by forking the template repository and opening a **pull request** to begin review, with required **README** and **LICENSE** (AUTHORS optional) and a prescribed directory structure (src, data, scripts, docs, results) documenting hardware/software dependencies, installation, run scripts, and output logs. (#5, #6)
- **Conference-to-journal policy:** disclose and link any prior conference version in the cover letter; justify incremental contribution sufficient for OR to hold copyright; extended-abstract-only venues exempt; for open-review venues (e.g., NeurIPS) reviewers must form independent evaluations; cite the prior conference version anonymously. (#7)
- **Cadence / identifiers:** bimonthly; ISSN **0030-364X** (print) / **1526-5463** (online). (#1)
- **Policies:** Code and Data Disclosure Policy and Generative AI Guidelines apply. (#1)
- **Generative AI (reviewer/editor side):** AI techniques conforming to the **RELX Responsible AI Principles** may support the review process (e.g., plagiarism checks), but **submitted manuscripts may not be uploaded to public-access generative AI**; the Area Editor, Associate Editor, and reviewers remain responsible for the accuracy and reasoning of their final reports. (#10)

## Items flagged approximate, re-confirm, or 需复核 (unverified)

- **Submission fee — resolved (no fee):** the rendered submission-guidelines page (#2) lists **no submission or processing fee**; the only fee mentioned is the optional INFORMS Open Option open-access fee (US$3,000, after acceptance). Unlike Management Science, *Operations Research* does **not** charge a per-submission fee as of this access. Still verify on the live page before relying on it, as INFORMS fee policy has changed at sibling journals.
- **Submission system branding — re-confirm:** the landing page labels the entry point "INFORMS Author Portal," while the guidelines and EiC reports show the underlying system is ScholarOne Manuscripts at mc.manuscriptcentral.com/opre. Consistent (the portal routes into ScholarOne) but exact current branding may vary.
- **Contribution statement — snippet-corroborated:** the "since 1 June 2023, fewer than 500 words, in the cover letter" contribution-statement requirement (#3) did not appear in the rendered submission-guidelines view on this refresh and is corroborated only by secondary/search sources this cycle; treat the exact wording and effective date as provisional and re-confirm on the live guidelines page.
- **Area Editors roster — re-confirm:** the 14 named areas and their 21 Area Editors are taken from the EiC's 2024 and 2025 annual reports (#8, #9) and are current as of access but rotate; verify specific names against the live Editorial Board / Area Editors' Statements page (#4) before naming any individual.
- **Context-and-Challenge category — re-confirm:** per the 2025 EiC report (#8) this category is currently **invited-only** and the EiC is deciding whether to keep or close it; treat it as provisional rather than an open submission track.
- **Impact factor — confirmed (this access):** the landing page (#1) shows a **2024 impact factor of 2.6** and a **5-year impact factor of 3.3**; these update annually, so re-confirm before quoting.
- **Generative AI author-side rules — 需复核:** the rendered Generative AI Guidelines view exposed only the reviewer/editor portion (#10); the author-side disclosure and no-AI-authorship rules follow the standard INFORMS policy but the OPRE-specific wording was not directly read this cycle.
