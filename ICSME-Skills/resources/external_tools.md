# External Tools - IEEE ICSME

Access date: 2026-07-09

Use these surfaces after reopening the current ICSME cycle pages. ICSME mechanics — deadline
structure, page budget, ROSE-Festival badges, track list — are decided per edition, and the
`conf.researchr.org`, `easychair.org`, and `ieeexplore.ieee.org` gateways may block direct fetches
(read via search renderings and cross-check IEEE Xplore and dblp). ICSME is co-located with **SCAM**
and **VISSOFT**, which share its artifact/ROSE track.

## Cross-check surfaces (when the gateway blocks the primary page)

- IEEE Xplore ICSME proceedings for accepted-paper metadata and DOIs.
- dblp ICSM/ICSME stream: https://dblp.org/db/conf/icsm/index.html (note the ICSM→ICSME rename in 2014).
- The `se-deadlines` aggregator (https://se-deadlines.github.io/) for the machine-readable cutoffs.
- Prior ICSME editions (2023-2025) for track continuity when a 2026 track page is thin.

## Author-side checks

- EasyChair profile, conflicts, coauthor emails, topic tags, and the mandatory **abstract
  registration** that precedes the full-paper deadline.
- IEEEtran conference-template compliance, the current page budget (2026: 10 + 2 references,
  two-column), and PDF export in the two-column format.
- Double-anonymous sweep: anonymize the PDF, the data-availability links, tool names, repository
  owners, and — easy to miss — your **own subject system** if naming it reveals authorship.
- Data-availability statement and an anonymized artifact link or archived supplement.
- ROSE-Festival intent (IEEE **Open Research Object** / **Research Object Reviewed** badges) and its
  separate post-acceptance deadline shared with SCAM and VISSOFT.
- Track fit: research vs. Journal-First (TSE/EMSE) vs. Registered Reports vs. RENE vs. NIER vs.
  tool-demo vs. industry.

## Do not infer

- Do not assume ACM badge names (Available/Functional/Reusable/Reproduced) apply — ICSME is an
  **IEEE** venue with the IEEE research-object badges.
- Do not assume a Major Revision round exists — ICSME's research track is **single-round** with an
  early-decision cut and an author-response rebuttal, not a journal-style revise-and-resubmit.
- Do not assume the page budget, IEEEtran revision, response format, or open-science/AI-disclosure
  wording carry over between editions.
- If pages disagree, treat the newest research-papers call, the EasyChair CFP, or a direct chair
  announcement as controlling, and record the conflict.
