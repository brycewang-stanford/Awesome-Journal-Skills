# ST Exemplars Library (theory papers, by theme)

> **Verified via web search, access date 2026-06.** Every paper below was checked to confirm it
> actually appeared in *Sociological Theory* (ST), with year and volume/issue/pages, against SAGE
> Journals (`journals.sagepub.com`, platform code `stx`), the ST DOI (e-ISSN 1467-9558 / SAGE
> prefix 10.1177), and/or stable bibliographic records. Papers that could not be fully verified as
> **ST** are **omitted** — this is deliberately a short, clean list (4 verified) rather than a
> long, uncertain one.
>
> **ST is theory-focused.** Every entry below is a *conceptual* article: defined concepts,
> explicit mechanisms, and propositions (or a metatheoretical clarification) — **no hypothesis
> tests, no estimation, no results sections.** This file gives **theoretical positioning only**:
> it does not reproduce or invent any paper's specific propositions. Read the original before citing.
>
> **Sibling-confusion guard (do NOT mislabel these as ST):** ST is *not* the **American
> Sociological Review** (ASR), the **American Journal of Sociology** (AJS), *Theory and Society*,
> the *European Journal of Social Theory*, or *Sociological Methodology*. Several canonical theory
> papers were published in those venues, NOT ST — see the omissions section below.
>
> For ST scope, the contribution bar, and a fuller "do-not-misattribute" note, see
> [`../official-source-map.md`](../official-source-map.md).

## How to use this library

Pick the row whose **theme × theoretical move** is closest to yours, then study how that paper
builds a *conceptual* contribution — concepts defined, a mechanism made vivid, and propositions
or a clarification that follow by argument, not by data. That is the ST bar (see
[`../../skills/soctheory-theory-construction/SKILL.md`](../../skills/soctheory-theory-construction/SKILL.md),
[`../../skills/soctheory-contribution-framing/SKILL.md`](../../skills/soctheory-contribution-framing/SKILL.md),
and [`../../skills/soctheory-writing-style/SKILL.md`](../../skills/soctheory-writing-style/SKILL.md)).
For each, ask the self-check question before claiming your paper is "ST-shaped." Model your
introduction on [`../worked-examples/01-introduction.md`](../worked-examples/01-introduction.md).

## By theme

### What "theory" means (metatheory)

- **Abend, G., "The Meaning of 'Theory,'" Sociological Theory 2008, 26(2):173–199.**
  Verified: ST 26(2):173–199 (web-verified bibliographic record).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** shows that "theory" denotes several distinct things in sociology
    (general proposition, explanation, interpretation, worldview, etc.). The field's reminder to
    state *which* kind of theory work you are doing.
  - **Self-check:** can you name which sense of "theory" your paper delivers, or are you sliding
    among several without noticing?

### Justifying a theoretical claim (metatheory / philosophy of social science)

- **Reed, I. A., "Justifying Sociological Knowledge: From Realism to Interpretation,"
  Sociological Theory 2008, 26(2):101–129.**
  Verified: ST 26(2):101–129, DOI 10.1111/j.1467-9558.2008.00321.x (ST e-ISSN 1467-9558; web-verified).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** argues that the *warrant* for a sociological claim is itself a
    theoretical commitment — realism and interpretation justify knowledge differently. Models how
    to make the logic of one's own explanation explicit.
  - **Self-check:** have you stated the warrant for your central claim, or only asserted the claim?

### Formal theory construction (how new theory is generated)

- **Timmermans, S., & Tavory, I., "Theory Construction in Qualitative Research: From Grounded
  Theory to Abductive Analysis," Sociological Theory 2012, 30(3):167–186.**
  Verified: ST 30(3):167–186, DOI 10.1177/0735275112457914 (web-verified; SAGE article page).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** specifies **abduction** as the inferential mechanism by which
    surprising observations generate new theory — a theory *of* theory construction, with its
    central concepts tied to an explicit mechanism rather than to data.
  - **Self-check:** does your account name the inferential mechanism that produces its claims, or
    does it present conclusions with no engine behind them?

### Conceptual sharpening within a tradition (cultural sociology)

- **Lizardo, O., Mowry, R., Sepulvado, B., Stoltz, D. S., Taylor, M. A., Van Ness, J., & Wood, M.,
  "What Are Dual Process Models? Implications for Cultural Analysis in Sociology," Sociological
  Theory 2016, 34(4):287–310.**
  Verified: ST 34(4):287–310 (web-verified bibliographic record).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** disciplines the loose sociological use of "dual-process" by
    importing the precise cognitive-science scaffolding — a "before → after" conceptual advance
    that is differentiated from the prior loose usage, not a relabel.
  - **Self-check:** does your paper *sharpen* a concept the field uses loosely (with a stated
    distinction), or does it merely re-use the loose concept under a new name?

## Omitted for lack of full ST verification, or verified to a SIBLING journal (do not attribute to ST)

To keep the list zero-hallucination, the following are **excluded** as ST exemplars:

- **Emirbayer, M., "Manifesto for a Relational Sociology" (1997)** — verified to be in the
  **_American Journal of Sociology_ 103(2):281–317, NOT ST.** The foundational relational-sociology
  statement is AJS; do not list it as an ST paper.
- **Gross, N., "A Pragmatist Theory of Social Mechanisms" (2009)** — verified to be in the
  **_American Sociological Review_ 74(3):358–379, NOT ST.** A theory paper, but in ST's empirical
  sibling, not ST.
- **Martin, J. L., "What Is Field Theory?" (2003)** — verified to be in the **_American Journal of
  Sociology_ 109(1):1–49, NOT ST.** The widely cited field-theory synthesis is AJS.
- **Lizardo, O., "Improving Cultural Analysis" (2017)** — verified to be in the **_American
  Sociological Review_ 82(1):88–115, NOT ST.** (Lizardo's *dual-process* paper, Lizardo et al.
  2016, IS in ST and is listed above — do not confuse the two.)

Before adding any new paper to this library, confirm it on `journals.sagepub.com` (ST is the
`stx` platform; check the article page shows *Sociological Theory* with volume/issue) and update
the access-date header. **When in doubt — especially for field-theory, mechanisms, cultural, or
relational papers that have near-identical siblings at ASR, AJS, Theory and Society, or EJST —
omit.**
