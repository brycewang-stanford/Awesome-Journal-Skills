# Official Source Map — Sociological Theory (ST)

Citable sources for the ST-specific facts used across these skills. **Sociological Theory** is
the **American Sociological Association's (ASA) dedicated theory journal**, published by SAGE. It
publishes conceptual work in all areas of theory — new substantive theories, history of theory,
metatheory, formal theory construction, and synthetic contributions — and does **not** publish
empirical hypothesis-testing studies (those belong at its empirical siblings, the *American
Sociological Review* (ASR) and the *American Journal of Sociology* (AJS)). All access dates below
are **2026-06-20**. Items that could not be verified from official sources on the access date are
listed under "Still unverified" and are flagged inline in the skills as
`(检索于 2026-06；以官网为准)` or 待核实.

## Verified facts (with source #)

- ST is the **ASA's dedicated theory journal**, published by SAGE; it publishes conceptual
  theory, NOT empirical hypothesis tests. (Sources 1, 3, 5)
- ST scope (Source 1): "work in all areas of theory, including new substantive theories, history
  of theory, metatheory, formal theory construction, and synthetic contributions"; orientation
  is **pluralistic** and welcomes contributions from other fields.
- **Length cap: max ~14,500 words inclusive of footnotes, references, tables, figures, and
  appendices** (Source 1; the HTML also describes papers around 40 manuscript pages). Treated as
  authoritative-but-volatile — verify the exact current figure.
- **Manuscript format** (Source 1): 12-pt standard font, double-spaced, ~1.5-inch margins.
- **Processing fee: $25, non-refundable, waived for ASA student members**, paid via SAGE Track.
  (Source 1)
- **Submission portal**: Manuscript Central at `mc.manuscriptcentral.com/soct`. (Sources 1, 5)
- **Style**: the **ASA Style Guide** (author-date). The 7th edition was published June 2022;
  verify the edition ST currently requires. (Sources 1, 4)
- **Review**: double-anonymous; ASA requires masking and treats simultaneous submission to
  another journal as unethical. (Sources 1, 4)
- **ORCID** is collected from corresponding authors as part of submission. (Source 1)
- Founded **1983**; published quarterly. (Source 3; founding year cross-checked in general
  reference sources — confirm on an official ASA history page before asserting in print.)
