# Official Source Map - ICDM

Access date: 2026-07-09

This map records the official and archival sources behind every ICDM-specific fact in this
pack. ICDM is an **annual IEEE conference**: its rules are re-set each edition by a new
Organizing and Program Committee, so reopen the current-edition Research Track and Applied
Track calls, the author kit, and the IEEE Xplore proceedings record before giving
submission-ready advice.

## Access method (read this first)

The ICDM 2026 host domain (`icdm2026.neu.edu.cn`) and several prior-edition call pages return
**HTTP 403 to direct automated fetches** through this environment's gateway. Every fact below
was therefore verified through **search-result renderings of those official pages** and
**cross-checked against dblp (`dblp.org/db/conf/icdm`) and the IEEE ICDM awards site**. Where a
figure could not be confirmed from at least the official rendering plus one independent index,
it is marked **待核实 (to verify)** rather than asserted. Confirm anything deadline-sensitive by
opening the live page in a browser.

## Verified 2026-cycle facts used in skills

- ICDM 2026 is the **26th IEEE International Conference on Data Mining**, hosted by Northeastern
  University in **Shenyang, China, November 12-15, 2026** (source 1). The 2025 edition (25th) was
  in Washington DC (dblp, source 7); the count is consistent.
- **Single deadline, not cycles.** Abstract deadline **2026-05-30**; full-paper deadline
  **2026-06-06** (23:59:59 AoE); acceptance notification **2026-08-16** (sources 1-2). ICDM does
  not run KDD-style multiple cycles.
- **Research Track is triple-blind.** ICDM has imposed triple-blind submission and review **since
  2011** (source 9): author identities and referee identities are both concealed; names are known
  only to the PC Co-Chairs; author names are disclosed only after ranking and acceptance are
  finalized. Authors must conceal identity and affiliation **throughout the whole paper**, not
  just the first page (sources 2, 8, 9).
- **Applied Track is new in 2026 and is single-blind** (source 3). This is a first-time
  divergence inside one ICDM edition: the two main tracks use different anonymity regimes.
- **Blue Sky Track** (CCC-sponsored) solicits visionary/forward-looking papers (source 4).
- **Format:** IEEE two-column, **maximum 10 pages including the bibliography and any appendices**;
  submissions longer than 10 pages are **rejected without review** (sources 2-3, 9). Everything
  counts inside the 10 pages — there is no separate reference or appendix budget.
- **Regular-to-short acceptance mechanic:** all papers are submitted in full-paper format; based
  on manuscript quality and reviews, **some are accepted as short papers** (source 8). Acceptance
  as a short paper is a real outcome distinct from Accept/Reject.
- **Proceedings** are published by IEEE and indexed in **IEEE Xplore** and dblp (sources 7, and
  IEEE Xplore ICDM proceedings). At least one author must register and present in person for a
  paper to appear (source 2).

## Still cycle-volatile

Edition number aside, treat dates, track lineup (the Applied and Blue Sky tracks were new in
2026), review model per track, anonymity mechanics, response policy, page cap, short-paper
handling, presentation and registration rules, and any artifact policy as **re-set every year**.
If a call page and an IEEE Xplore or committee announcement disagree, prefer the newest official
instruction and record the conflict.
