## How to use this library

Pick the row whose **method × question** is closest to yours, then study how that paper makes a
*credible accounting* result carry a *contribution-bearing* lesson — the TAR bar (see
[`../../skills/tar-topic-selection/SKILL.md`](../../skills/tar-topic-selection/SKILL.md),
[`../../skills/tar-contribution-framing/SKILL.md`](../../skills/tar-contribution-framing/SKILL.md), and
[`../../skills/tar-writing-style/SKILL.md`](../../skills/tar-writing-style/SKILL.md)). For each, ask
the self-check question before claiming your paper is "TAR-shaped." Note the **method spread**: TAR is
method-agnostic, so the list deliberately spans archival, methodology, experimental, and analytical
lanes (`tar-methods`, `tar-theory-development`).

## By method

### Methodology / measurement that the field adopts (archival)

- **Dechow, Sloan & Sweeney, "Detecting Earnings Management," TAR 1995, 70(2):193–225.**
  Verified: TAR, vol. 70, issue 2 (multiple bibliographic sources concur; Google Scholar lookup
  confirms journal/volume/issue/pages).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** evaluates competing accruals models and gives the field a measurement
    instrument (modified-Jones) that thousands of later papers reuse — the contribution *is* the
    method, exactly the "to measurement/method" register in `tar-contribution-framing`.
  - **Self-check:** does your paper hand the field a measure or test it will actually adopt, not just
    apply an existing one to a new sample?

### Archival association with a policy-relevant construct (large-sample archival)

- **Frankel, Johnson & Nelson, "The Relation between Auditors' Fees for Nonaudit Services and Earnings
  Management," TAR 2002, 77 (Supplement):71–105.**
  Verified: TAR, vol. 77, supplement issue, pp. 71–105 (concurring bibliographic sources; note this is
  a conference-supplement issue of TAR, not the regular issue).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** takes a live policy dispute (does buying non-audit services compromise
    auditor independence?) and brings audit-fee data to it — the audit construct does the theoretical
    work, clearing `tar-topic-selection` gate 1. Its contested reception (a rebuttal followed) models
    `tar-rebuttal`-style engagement.
  - **Self-check:** is your association evidence on a question regulators and the field are *actively
    arguing about*, with the accounting construct (not a generic finance variable) central?

### New construct measured over a long horizon (large-sample archival)

- **Dyreng, Hanlon & Maydew, "Long-Run Corporate Tax Avoidance," TAR 2008, 83(1):61–82.**
  Verified: TAR, vol. 83, issue 1, pp. 61–82 (concurring bibliographic sources).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** defines a new, durable measure (the long-run cash effective tax rate)
    and uses it to answer a question prior one-year measures could not — measurement-as-contribution in
    the tax lane (`tar-contribution-framing`, register 2).
  - **Self-check:** does your measure let the field see something existing proxies obscured, with the
    boundary conditions (horizon, population) stated, per `tar-contribution-framing`?

### Experiment isolating an investor-judgment channel (experimental/behavioral)

- **Koonce, McAnally & Mercer, "How Do Investors Judge the Risk of Financial Items?," TAR 2005,
  80(1):221–241.**
  Verified: TAR, vol. 80, issue 1, pp. 221–241 — confirmed on the AAA's own article page
  (`publications.aaahq.org/accounting-review/article/80/1/221`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** manipulates the construct in the lab to isolate *how* investors form
    risk judgments from disclosures — the channel archival data cannot cleanly separate, exactly the
    experimental lane in `tar-methods` and `tar-theory-development`.
  - **Self-check:** does your experiment establish a *mechanism* (a channel), with manipulation/
    attention checks and a fit participant pool, that archival data could not isolate?

### Analytical model with a regulatory payoff (analytical/modeling)

- **Dye, "Mandatory versus Voluntary Disclosures: The Cases of Financial and Real Externalities," TAR
  1990, 65(1):1–24.**
  Verified: TAR, vol. 65, issue 1, pp. 1–24 (Kellogg faculty page + JSTOR issue table of contents for
  TAR 65(1), Jan. 1990).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a disclosure-theory model whose comparative statics translate into a
    *policy* payoff (when does mandating disclosure add social value?) — the analytical lane's
    requirement that a model deliver an accounting/normative implication, per `tar-theory-development`
    ("model without payoff" is the named anti-pattern).
  - **Self-check:** does your model state primitives and equilibrium, and deliver an accounting or
    regulatory implication — not just an elegant equilibrium?

### Value-relevance evidence around a standard (large-sample archival)

- **Barth, Beaver & Landsman, "Value-Relevance of Banks' Fair Value Disclosures under SFAS No. 107,"
  TAR 1996, 71(4):513–537.**
  Verified: TAR, vol. 71, issue 4, pp. 513–537 (SSRN + Stanford GSB faculty publication page concur on
  journal/volume/issue/pages).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** uses a specific standard (SFAS 107) as the institutional setting and
    asks whether the *disclosed* fair values are priced beyond book values — the accounting-measurement
    construct doing the theoretical work in a capital-markets test (`tar-topic-selection` gate 1).
  - **Self-check:** does a concrete standard/setting anchor your test, with the accounting disclosure
    (not the price itself) as the focal construct?

## Omitted for sibling-venue or lack of full TAR confirmation (do not attribute to TAR without re-checking)

To keep the list zero-hallucination, these candidates were **excluded** after web-checking — several
because they are in a **sibling** accounting/finance journal, not The Accounting Review:

- **Leuz, Nanda & Wysocki, "Earnings Management and Investor Protection" (2003)** — verified in the
  *Journal of Financial Economics* 69(3):505–527, **not TAR**.
- **Bartov, Givoly & Hayn, "The Rewards to Meeting or Beating Earnings Expectations" (2002)** —
  verified in the *Journal of Accounting and Economics* 33(2):173–204, **not TAR (JAE)**.
- **Hail & Leuz, "Cost of Capital Effects … Around U.S. Cross-Listings" (2009)** — verified in the
  *Journal of Financial Economics* 93(3):428–454, **not TAR**.
- **Balakrishnan, Core & Verdi, "Reporting Quality and Financing and Investment" (2014)** — verified in
  the *Journal of Accounting Research* 52(1):1–36, **not TAR (JAR)**.
- **Ohlson, "Earnings, Book Values, and Dividends in Equity Valuation" (1995)** — verified in
  *Contemporary Accounting Research* 11(2):661–687, **not TAR (CAR)**; the closely related Feltham–Ohlson
  clean-surplus paper of the same year is also CAR, so neither is listed here.
- **Libby, Bloomfield & Nelson, "Experimental Research in Financial Accounting" (2002)** — verified in
  *Accounting, Organizations and Society* 27(8):775–810, **not TAR**.

Before adding any new paper to this library, confirm it is **The Accounting Review** (AAA) on the
publisher's article page (with volume/issue) — *not* JAR, JAE, CAR, RAST, or a finance journal — and
update the access-date header. When in doubt, omit.
