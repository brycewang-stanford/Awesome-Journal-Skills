# EPSL Letter — Manuscript Skeleton

> A structural template for an Earth and Planetary Science Letters **Letter**. Section proportions
> reflect the letters format: a main text (introduction through conclusions) near the ~6,500-word cap
> (re-check the exact figure on the live Guide for Authors), with the traceability chain and bulk data
> in the supplementary material and discipline repositories. Replace every bracketed slot; delete
> nothing silently.

---

## Title page

**Title** — process-forward, concise. Pattern: *[Observable/mechanism] [verb: controls / records /
requires / limits] [process] [on Earth / on Mars / in subduction zones / ...]*.
Avoid the pattern *"[Method] of [rock unit], [place], [country]"* — it signals a regional study.

**Authors & affiliations** — with the corresponding author marked; ORCID for the corresponding
author at minimum.

**Highlights** — if required by the current guide (re-check): 3–5 bullets, each a finding not a topic,
within the character limit stated there.

**Keywords** — per the current guide's count; include the process, the method family, and the setting.

---

## Abstract (single paragraph)

Slots, in order:

1. The open process question (1 sentence).
2. Why existing constraints cannot settle it (1 sentence).
3. What was measured/modeled and where (1–2 sentences).
4. The headline result: value ± uncertainty (σ-level stated) with units (1 sentence).
5. The portable consequence — what anyone studying this process must now do differently (1–2 sentences).

---

## 1. Introduction (~600–900 words)

- Paragraph 1 — the process question and why it matters beyond one region or method community.
- Paragraph 2 — the constraint landscape: which observables/hypotheses are in tension (name them).
- Paragraph 3 — the discriminating test: "Here we [approach], which separates [A] from [B] because…"

## 2. Materials and methods (~400–700 words in main text)

- Samples/data: source, context (coordinates, stratigraphic/structural position, or mission archive),
  screening criteria.
- Methods in outline: instrument/technique, reference materials and normalization, software with
  versions — one paragraph per method family.
- Pointer: "Full analytical procedures, standards data, blank records, and data tables are provided
  in the Supplementary Material and at [repository] (DOI)."

## 3. Results (~800–1,200 words)

- One subsection per observable; lead each with the pattern, not the inventory.
- Every number: value ± uncertainty (σ-level), n, and the figure/table where it lives.
- Pooled statistics with MSWD interpreted; exclusions stated with their rule.

## 4. Discussion (~1,500–2,500 words)

- 4.1 The discriminating comparison — which hypothesis survives the data, shown quantitatively.
- 4.2 Alternatives addressed — the competing explanations a cross-field referee will raise, each
  tested or bounded (resolution tests, contamination screens, sensitivity cases; details → supplement).
- 4.3 The portable implication — scaled beyond the study system; state the domain of validity.

## 5. Conclusions (3–5 sentences)

Portable claims only; no new material; no hedging that contradicts the abstract.

---

## Back matter

**CRediT authorship contribution statement** — one line per author, standard role names.

**Declaration of competing interests** — Elsevier standard wording, or the specific disclosure.

**Acknowledgements** — funding with grant numbers; facilities; constructive input. Generative-AI use
disclosed here per Elsevier policy, if applicable.

**Data availability** — pattern:
> All [data type] are available at [EarthChem / PANGAEA / MagIC / IRIS-EarthScope / NASA PDS]
> (DOI: …). [Reduction scripts / model configurations] are archived at Zenodo (DOI: …).

**References** — journal author–date house style (re-check current instructions); DOIs included;
decay-constant and reference-material sources cited like any other constraint.

---

## Supplementary material (separate file(s))

- S1. Full analytical methods (instrument settings, chemistry, correction sequence).
- S2. Reference-material results per session, measured vs accepted, with the values used.
- S3. Blank records and blank-sensitivity of the headline numbers.
- S4. Complete data tables (every analysis, including excluded ones), machine-readable.
- S5. Robustness: resolution/recovery tests, sensitivity cases, alternative reductions.
- Figures S1–Sn / Tables S1–Sn — numbered in citation order; every S-item cited from the main text.
