# EPSL Official Source Map

The authoritative sources behind every *Earth and Planetary Science Letters* (EPSL) fact used in this
pack. **Checked: 2026-07-16.** ScienceDirect pages block some automated crawlers, so several entries
were confirmed through search-indexed copies of the official pages; open the live URL in a browser
before relying on any volatile item (word cap, highlights, reference format, editor roster, fees).

## Journal identity (verified)

- **Publisher:** Elsevier. **Founded:** 1966. **ISSN:** 0012-821X (print), 1385-013X (online).
  Sources: the ISSN Portal records for 0012-821X / 1385-013X and the Elsevier shop page
  **https://shop.elsevier.com/journals/earth-and-planetary-science-letters/0012-821X**.
- **Scope:** research on the **physical, chemical and mechanical processes of the Earth and other
  planets, including extrasolar ones**, from deep interiors to atmospheres — spanning geochemistry,
  geophysics, geochronology, geodynamics, petrology, paleomagnetism, cosmochemistry and planetary
  science. Source: the journal home page (below) as indexed 2026-07-16.

## Official journal pages

- **https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/earth-and-planetary-science-letters**
  — journal home: aims & scope, editor roster, current articles. Re-open before naming any editor;
  the editor team is large, subject-organized, and changes.
- **https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/earth-and-planetary-science-letters/publish/guide-for-authors**
  — the Guide for Authors, the controlling source for: article types (**Letters; invited Frontiers
  Papers; Comments and Replies; Errata/Corrigenda; Special Issues**), the statement that EPSL
  publishes **concise, high-impact articles ("Letters") of broad interest** and **discourages
  companion papers**, manuscript formatting, reference style, declarations, and data policy.
- **Editorial Manager entry** — EPSL submissions go through Elsevier's **Editorial Manager**; use the
  "Submit your article" link on the journal home page as the canonical route (the legacy
  `ees.elsevier.com/epsl` address predates the current system; do not trust cached submission URLs).

## Length & format (verified with a re-check flag)

- **Letters cap:** search-indexed copies of the Guide for Authors state a restricted main-text length
  of **no more than 6,500 words from introduction to conclusions** for regular Letters and invited
  Frontiers articles. **Re-check the live guide** for the current figure and for what counts inside it
  (abstract, captions, references are typically outside; confirm).
- **Highlights / keywords / abstract limits:** Elsevier-standard fields whose exact requirements vary
  by journal and over time — **re-check on the live guide**; this pack does not assert numbers.
- **Reference style:** Elsevier author–date house style for this title per the guide —
  **re-check the formatting details** (and grab the current CSL/EndNote style) before formatting.

## Research-data policy

- The Guide for Authors carries the journal's research-data section (data statements, repository
  linking) under **Elsevier's research-data framework**:
  **https://www.elsevier.com/about/policies-and-standards/research-data** — data-availability
  statements and repository deposition supported/expected; "available on request" is the weak case.
- Community FAIR repositories this pack routes to (all independently operated; verified live
  2026-07-16 via their own sites): **EarthChem** (https://www.earthchem.org — NSF-supported
  geochemistry archive, per-dataset DOIs), **PANGAEA** (https://www.pangaea.de — curated Earth &
  environmental data publisher), **IRIS/EarthScope** (https://www.iris.edu — seismological data
  services), **MagIC** (https://www2.earthref.org/MagIC — paleomagnetic archive), **NASA PDS**
  (https://pds.nasa.gov — planetary mission archive), **Zenodo** (https://zenodo.org — code/dataset
  DOIs). Whether a given deposition is *mandated* vs *strongly expected* for a given data type —
  re-check the live guide; community practice treats deposition as the norm regardless.

## Review model & ethics

- **Editor-led review:** manuscripts are handled by a member of the journal's editor team working in
  the paper's sub-field, with external expert referees; decisions rest with the editors. The precise
  reviewer count and any timeline targets are **not asserted** here — re-check on the official page.
- **Publishing ethics:** Elsevier's policies govern — authorship, competing-interest declarations,
  plagiarism screening, and the generative-AI disclosure policy:
  **https://www.elsevier.com/about/policies-and-standards/publishing-ethics**.

## Bounded live-check rules

- Treat the **Guide for Authors** as controlling for article types, the word cap, highlights,
  reference format, and file requirements; anything numeric in this pack that touches those items is
  flagged "re-check".
- **Never name a current editor** (or route a cover letter to one) without re-opening the journal
  home page — the roster is long and turnover is routine.
- **Do not quote impact metrics, acceptance rates, or handling times** as policy; the journal page
  shows current metrics if the user insists on them.
- **Open access:** EPSL publishes under Elsevier's hybrid arrangements; if the author wants gold OA,
  quote the **live** APC and any institutional-agreement coverage from the Elsevier journal page at
  submission time, never a remembered number.
- For **Frontiers Papers**, the type is by **editor invitation** — verify any invitation claim
  against correspondence before selecting the type in Editorial Manager.
