Primary and authoritative sources behind every SMR-specific fact injected into this skill pack.

## Official journal & publisher pages

- **https://journals.sagepub.com/home/smr** — SAGE Journals SMR home. Verifies: published by **SAGE**;
  the journal's identity as a **quantitative/statistical-methodology** outlet for sociology and the
  social sciences. (Editor name and term shown on the editorial-board tab; see 需复核 below.)
- **https://journals.sagepub.com/author-instructions/smr** — SAGE author instructions for SMR (the
  authoritative submission page). Verifies: submission via **ScholarOne Manuscripts**;
  **double-anonymized peer review** (separate title page + anonymized main document); files in **Word,
  LaTeX, or PDF**; in-text citations and references in **American Sociological Association (ASA)**
  style; **dataset references in DataCite** style; **abstract no longer than 150 words and with no
  parenthetical citations** (non-parenthetical citations allowed); a **data-and-code availability
  statement** is required (code/materials expected; trusted public repository; restricted-data
  reasons/conditions stated); **generative-AI use disclosed in the back matter** (none needed if no AI
  tool used); revised manuscripts respond to each reviewer comment by **copying the comment verbatim**
  and replying beneath it.
- **https://us.sagepub.com/en-us/nam/journal/sociological-methods-research** — SAGE US journal page.
  Verifies: SAGE imprint and the methodological scope statement (methods development/evaluation for
  the social sciences).
- **ScholarOne Manuscripts (SMR site, reached via the author-instructions page)** — Verifies: the
  submission system is **ScholarOne** (Author Center → Start New Submission).

## Explicitly NOT applicable (checked, to avoid misattribution)

- **Sociological Methodology** (the **ASA annual**, also SAGE-published) is a *different* venue with a
  different editorial model and cadence. Do **not** conflate it with **Sociological Methods &
  Research**, and do not attribute SM papers/policies to SMR.
- **Psychological Methods** (APA) and **Political Analysis** (poli-sci methods) are neighboring methods
  journals, **not** SMR. Their length, style, and review policies do not transfer.
- SMR uses **ASA style**, **not** APA, Chicago author-date for non-ASA venues, or a journal-specific
  numeric style; do not import another sociology/psychology journal's reference rules.
