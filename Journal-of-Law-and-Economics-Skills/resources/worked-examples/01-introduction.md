# Worked Example: Reframing an Introduction for The Journal of Law and Economics

This is a fictional example. It does not describe a real paper.

## Before

> This paper studies an important topic using a large dataset and several robustness checks. We contribute to the literature by adding new evidence and showing heterogeneous effects.

## Diagnosis

- The first sentence does not name the JLE audience or the journal's law and economics, regulation, property rights, contracts, liability, antitrust, and legal institutions scope.
- The contribution could fit several siblings: Journal of Legal Studies, JLEO, American Law and Economics Review, and Journal of Public Economics.
- The mechanism words that matter here are absent: legal rule variation, institutional doctrine, contracting friction, regulatory incidence.

## After

> This paper asks how **legal rule variation** changes the decision margin that JLE readers care about. The design ties the estimate to **institutional doctrine**, separates the main mechanism from **contracting friction**, and reports uncertainty in a way that makes the policy or theory implication portable beyond the study setting.
