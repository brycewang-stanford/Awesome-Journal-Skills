# External Tools - COLT

Access date: 2026-07-08

Open these after loading the current cycle's pages; COLT re-issues every rule annually
with the new Call for Papers.

## Official workflow

- COLT 2026 conference site: https://learningtheory.org/colt2026/
- COLT 2026 Call for Papers: https://learningtheory.org/colt2026/cfp.html
- COLT 2026 CMT submission portal: https://cmt3.research.microsoft.com/COLT2026
- COLT 2026 code of conduct: https://learningtheory.org/colt2026/code-of-conduct.html
- Association for Computational Learning (parent body): https://www.learningtheory.org/
- PMLR proceedings index (all COLT volumes): https://proceedings.mlr.press/
  - COLT 2025 = v291; COLT 2024 = v247. The current edition's volume is announced at
    publication time.

## Author-side checks against the live cycle

- The current CFP's deadline (a single AoE paper deadline in recent cycles) and the
  exact submission portal link — COLT used CMT in 2025 and 2026 after other systems in
  earlier years, so never assume the portal.
- The current LaTeX template and its anonymization option; page arithmetic (12
  PMLR-formatted body pages excluding references in 2026, unlimited appendix in the
  same PDF).
- The anonymity policy text, including the informed-area-chair and rebuttal-period
  identity-reveal mechanics.
- The parallel-submission and journal-submission-freeze wording.
- Whether an Open Problems track runs this cycle, with its own deadline and
  non-anonymous short format.
- Notification, rebuttal window, camera-ready, registration, and presentation
  obligations — all typically announced after the CFP, none guessable from prior years.

## Community-side references (unofficial, use with judgment)

- arXiv cs.LG / math.ST / stat.ML listings for concurrent-work sweeps before
  submission and before the rebuttal.
- DBLP's COLT index (https://dblp.org/db/conf/colt/) for edition-by-edition
  bibliography when building the known-vs-new ledger.

## Do not infer

- Do not carry deadlines, page limits, portal choice, template names, chair names, or
  track structure forward from any past cycle, including 2026.
- Do not assume NeurIPS/ICML-style mechanisms (abstract deadlines, reproducibility
  checklists, artifact badges, OpenReview discussion) exist at COLT — the 2026 cycle
  had none of them.
- If official pages conflict, the newest CFP text or direct program-chair
  communication controls; record the conflict in `official-source-map.md` with the
  access date.
