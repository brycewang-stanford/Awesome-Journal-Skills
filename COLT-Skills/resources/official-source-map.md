## Verified facts used across the skills

- COLT 2026 is the 39th Annual Conference on Learning Theory, held June 29 - July 3,
  2026 in San Diego, USA.
- The single submission deadline was February 4, 2026, Anywhere on Earth; submissions
  went through Microsoft CMT (not OpenReview) in both the 2025 and 2026 cycles.
- Format: at most 12 PMLR-formatted pages of main body excluding references; no page
  limit on references and appendices; the whole manuscript is one PDF.
- Review was double-anonymous with an informed area chair, who may reveal author
  identities to a reviewer during the rebuttal period upon request when needed for a
  proper review; the LaTeX template's `anon` option suppresses author names.
- Initial reviews are sent to authors before decisions, with an author response
  (rebuttal) opportunity.
- Parallel-submission policy: submissions substantially similar to work published,
  accepted, or in parallel submission to peer-reviewed proceedings venues or journals
  are not allowed, and a paper under COLT review may not be submitted to a journal
  until decisions are released or the paper is withdrawn.
- Accepted papers are published electronically in the Proceedings of Machine Learning
  Research (PMLR); PMLR is open access with no author-side fee, and COLT's cost model
  is conference registration.
- Volume/editor anchors: v247 = COLT 2024 (Agrawal, Roth); v291 = COLT 2025
  (Haghtalab, Moitra). Volume editors are that edition's program chairs.
- COLT is organized under the Association for Computational Learning, and the
  conference is single-track by long-standing practice.
- COLT has a tradition of published open-problem pieces in its proceedings (verified
  instance: PMLR v65:4-7, COLT 2017); the 2025 cycle ran an Open Problems track with
  the format recorded under source 7.
- COLT has no artifact-evaluation track, badges, reproducibility checklist, or
  code-submission requirement in the 2026 CFP — an absence claim, verified to the
  extent that no such mechanism appears in the checked 2026 material.

## Still cycle-volatile even when verified

Deadline, portal, page limits, anonymity mechanics, rebuttal format, overlap policy
wording, open-problem track existence, and all dates are re-decided by each edition's
program chairs. The 2026 facts above are anchors for interpreting this pack, never
substitutes for the live CFP.
