# External Tools - CSCW

Access date: 2026-07-08

CSCW is in the middle of a platform migration — fixed cycles ran on PCS, the rolling
model runs on Manuscript Central — so the first tool check is always "which regime is
this paper in?" Reopen the live pages before every deadline-adjacent action.

## Five author-side passes before any upload

1. **Regime pass.** Confirm whether the paper enters through a residual fixed-cycle
   pathway (PCS) or the rolling pathway (Manuscript Central). The two systems have
   different account setups, metadata fields, and revision mechanics; a paper
   prepared for one does not transfer automatically to the other.
2. **Length-band pass.** Compute the manuscript's word count excluding references
   and appendices. Inside 5,000-12,000 words, length draws no special attention;
   outside it, write one sentence you could defend to an editor about why the
   contribution warrants the length — or cut.
3. **Anonymity pass.** Search the PDF and metadata for author names, institutions,
   city names, grant numbers, repository owners, and acknowledgements. For studies
   of identifiable online communities, also check that quoted posts cannot be
   reverse-searched to the community and thence to the author team's prior papers.
4. **Journal-citation pass.** Verify that references to prior CSCW work published
   since 2017 cite PACMHCI (volume, issue, article number), not a nonexistent
   "CSCW proceedings"; verify each on dblp or the ACM DL.
5. **Ethics pass.** Confirm the paper states IRB/ethics status, consent posture for
   human participants or trace data, and the handling of sensitive community data
   — reviewers in this venue read these sections closely.

## Do not infer

- Do not project the May 2025 cycle's dates onto any future submission; that cycle
  was explicitly the last of its kind.
- Do not assume rolling decisions arrive faster than the advertised 3-4 months, or
  that an acceptance guarantees a slot at the very next conference.
- Do not carry PCS habits (cycle picking, subcommittee selection) into Manuscript
  Central, or vice versa.
- If the rolling CFP, the conference site, and the blog disagree, treat the most
  recently updated official page as controlling and record the conflict in your
  notes.
