## Verified facts encoded in the pack

- **Publisher / identity:** Elsevier journal and official Game Theory Society journal; ISSN 0899-8256. (#1, #3)
- **Scope:** general-interest game theory and applications across social, behavioral, mathematical, and biological sciences; papers should significantly advance frontiers of game theory and applications. (#2, #3)
- **Editor-in-Chief:** Herve Moulin. (#1, #3)
- **Editorial model:** chief editor assigns each paper to one of seven Editors with final decision authority; editors consult anonymous Advisory Editors and reviewers; the assigned Editor communicates the decision to the corresponding author. (#2, #3)
- **Publication / desk-rejection context:** current Guide states about 15 percent of submitted papers are published and about one third desk-rejected when editors judge the paper has no chance of meeting the publication criterion. (#2)
- **Review model:** single-anonymized review; suitable submissions typically go to at least two reviewers. (#2)
- **Abstract:** maximum 250 words. (#2)
- **Conference-version disclosure:** cover letter should explain how the submission differs from any conference version. (#2)
- **Data policy:** the Guide encourages and enables sharing supporting data where appropriate through repository metadata, Mendeley Data, Data Brief, data statements, and related mechanisms; this is not encoded here as a mandatory repository-deposit gate. (#2)
- **Files / submission:** editable Word or LaTeX source files are required for final publication; Elsevier Editorial Manager route applies. (#2, #4)
- **Reference style:** flexible at initial submission if consistent and complete; published style uses the Elsevier numeric/elsarticle format. (#2)
- **Open access:** hybrid journal; APC displayed on ScienceDirect as USD 2,860 excluding tax for optional gold open access. (#1)
- **AI policy:** authors must declare generative-AI use in manuscript preparation where used; basic grammar/spelling/reference tools do not require a declaration under Elsevier's policy. (#2, #5)
