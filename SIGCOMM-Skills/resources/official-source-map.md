# Official Source Map - ACM SIGCOMM

Access date: 2026-07-09

This map records the official sources behind every SIGCOMM-specific fact in this pack.
SIGCOMM reissues its Call for Papers, single-deadline calendar, track list, formatting rules,
and artifact policy for every edition, so a number that held for SIGCOMM 2026 can be stale for
SIGCOMM 2027. Reopen the current `conferences.sigcomm.org/sigcomm/<year>` pages and the
per-edition HotCRP site before giving submission-ready advice.

## Verified facts used in the skills (2026-07-09 snapshot)

- SIGCOMM 2026 is the **40th** edition of the ACM SIGCOMM conference, held
  **August 17-21, 2026, at the Colorado Convention Center, Denver, Colorado, USA** (sources 1, 11).
- SIGCOMM runs **one main-track deadline per edition** feeding one August conference, unlike the
  two-deadline USENIX and SIGMOBILE networking venues. For 2026: **abstract registration
  January 30, 2026 (AoE)**, **full paper February 6, 2026 (AoE)**, both rendered as 6:59:59 AM EST
  the next morning on the HotCRP deadlines page (sources 2, 4).
- The main track has **two lanes**: a **research** track and an **experience** track for
  operational and deployment results (source 2).
- Paper bodies are capped at **12 single-spaced pages including figures and tables**, everything
  before references and appendices; references and optional appendices may run as long as needed,
  and a paper whose pre-reference content exceeds 12 pages **is not reviewed** (source 2).
- Reviewing is **double-blind**; author names and identifying material are withheld from
  reviewers, and both sides observe blinding during the review window (sources 2, 3).
- Submission is on **HotCRP** at `sigcomm26.hotcrp.com`, with **abstract registration preceding
  the full-paper upload** (sources 3, 4).
- The review pipeline layers three author-facing mechanisms distinct to SIGCOMM's culture:
  (a) an **early reject** notification for submissions with a consensus to reject, so authors can
  redirect the work sooner; (b) a **rebuttal** for papers advancing to the discussion phase; and
  (c) a **one-shot revision** for a small set of papers, issued with a merits summary and a list
  of required changes, resubmitted about a month later, **managed by a shepherd** chosen by the
  PC, re-reviewed by the same reviewers where possible, and resulting in **accept or reject only**
  (source 2).
- Accepted papers may be **shepherded** by a PC member who confirms reviewer feedback is
  addressed and **approves any appendix's necessity**; the shepherd signs off the final version in
  HotCRP (sources 2, 5).
- **Camera-ready** papers are due **July 3, 2026, 23:59 UTC**, uploaded to HotCRP as **PDF plus
  source**, processed by the **ACM Publishing System (TAPS)**, and must use an ACM template —
  LaTeX **`acmart.cls`** or the ACM Word template (source 5).
- SIGCOMM runs **artifact evaluation** by a separate committee using **ACM badges** (Artifacts
  Available, Artifacts Evaluated, Results Reproduced); the venue has a strong reproducibility
  culture and encourages public artifact release (source 6).
- SIGCOMM confers a **Test of Time Paper Award** (10-12-year lookback) and a per-edition **Best
  Paper Award**; these anchor the exemplar canon (sources 7, 8).

## Organizers and fee model

- SIGCOMM is the flagship conference of **ACM SIGCOMM** (the ACM Special Interest Group on Data
  Communication). Like every CS/networking conference in this collection, it has **no standing
  editor-in-chief**; the rotating analogue is the per-edition **General Chairs** and **Program
  Committee Co-Chairs**, appointed for one edition under SIGCOMM's published program-chair
  selection best-current-practice. The SIGCOMM 2026 named roster was not retrievable in rendered
  text on 2026-07-09 (source 9) — see 待核实; re-check the organization page before naming anyone.
- There is **no article-processing charge (APC)**. Costs are covered by **registration**; at least
  one author is expected to register and present. Proceedings are published by ACM through TAPS and
  appear in the **ACM Digital Library** and **ACM SIGCOMM Computer Communication Review**; SIGCOMM
  also curates open PDFs of its proceedings via the community site.
