# Psychological Review Exemplars Library (theory/model papers, by form)

> **Verified via web search, access date 2026-06.** Every paper below was checked to confirm it
> actually appeared in *Psychological Review*, with year and volume/issue/pages, against
> publisher-hosted and author-hosted PDFs, ERIC/Semantic Scholar/PhilPapers records, and the verified
> DOI where available. Papers that could not be fully verified as **Psychological Review** are
> **omitted** — this is deliberately a short, clean list (6 verified) rather than a long, uncertain one.
>
> **Psychological Review is theory-only.** Every entry is a *theoretical* contribution — a new formal/
> computational model or a conceptual theory — with explicit assumptions, a mechanism, and derived
> predictions confronted with existing data. **No entry is an empirical report.** This file gives
> **theoretical positioning only**: it does not reproduce or invent any paper's parameters or numbers.
> Read the original before citing.
>
> **Sibling-confusion guard (do NOT mislabel these as Psychological Review):** Review is *not*
> **Psychological Bulletin** (reviews / meta-analyses), **Behavioral and Brain Sciences** (target
> article + commentary), **Trends in Cognitive Sciences** (invited opinion/review), the **JEP family**
> or **Psychological Science** (empirical reports), or **American Psychologist** (broad APA flagship).
> In particular: **Nosofsky's generalized context model paper (1986) appeared in _JEP: General_
> 115(1):39–57 — NOT Psychological Review**, and **Anderson's "ACT: A simple theory of complex
> cognition" (1996) appeared in _American Psychologist_ 51(4):355–365 — NOT Psychological Review.** See
> the omissions section below.
>
> For scope, article types, and a fuller "do-not-misattribute" note, see
> [`../official-source-map.md`](../official-source-map.md).

## How to use this library

Pick the row whose **theoretical form × domain** is closest to yours, then study how that paper builds a
contribution: assumptions made explicit, a mechanism (the why) specified, and predictions *derived* and
confronted with existing data — not an experiment run. That is the Psychological Review bar (see
[`../../skills/psychrev-theory-construction/SKILL.md`](../../skills/psychrev-theory-construction/SKILL.md),
[`../../skills/psychrev-argument-development/SKILL.md`](../../skills/psychrev-argument-development/SKILL.md),
and [`../../skills/psychrev-contribution-framing/SKILL.md`](../../skills/psychrev-contribution-framing/SKILL.md)).
For each, ask the self-check question before claiming your paper is "Review-shaped." Model your
introduction on [`../worked-examples/01-introduction.md`](../worked-examples/01-introduction.md).

## By theoretical form

### Formal model of memory retrieval (sequential sampling)

- **Ratcliff, R., "A theory of memory retrieval," Psychological Review 1978, 85(2):59–108.**
  Verified: DOI 10.1037/0033-295X.85.2.59; vol/issue/pages confirmed (ERIC EJ186250, Semantic Scholar, OALib).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** the diffusion model — a formal account that *derives* accuracy, mean
    reaction time, error latency, and full RT distributions from a small set of assumptions, then
    confronts them with existing data. The template for "a mechanism that yields quantitative signatures."
  - **Self-check:** do your assumptions *derive* the diagnostic patterns, or do you only assert the model can fit them?

### Computational model of cognitive control (PDP / connectionist)

- **Cohen, J. D., Dunbar, K., & McClelland, J. L., "On the control of automatic processes: A parallel distributed processing account of the Stroop effect," Psychological Review 1990, 97(3):332–361.**
  Verified: vol/pages confirmed (Stanford-hosted PDF; ResearchGate record).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a computational model in which automaticity emerges from pathway
    *strength*, simulated to reproduce the time course of the Stroop effect and learning — behavior
    derived from architecture, not stipulated.
  - **Self-check:** does your model's *behavior* (simulated) carry the argument, or only its architecture diagram?

### Connectionist model of perception (interactive activation)

- **McClelland, J. L., & Rumelhart, D. E., "An interactive activation model of context effects in letter perception: Part 1," Psychological Review 1981, 88(5):375–407.**
  Verified: vol/pages confirmed (PhilPapers; Semantic Scholar).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a foundational interactive-activation model showing how top-down and
    bottom-up processing combine to produce a context effect (the word-superiority effect) — a precise
    mechanism with simulated consequences.
  - **Self-check:** is your top-down/bottom-up interaction a stated mechanism with simulated output, or a verbal metaphor?

### Conceptual theory adjudicating rival accounts (judgment)

- **Tversky, A., & Kahneman, D., "Extensional versus intuitive reasoning: The conjunction fallacy in probability judgment," Psychological Review 1983, 90(4):293–315.**
  Verified: vol/issue/pages confirmed (UCSD-hosted PDF; PhilPapers).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a *conceptual* theory (no formal model required) that adjudicates between
    extensional logic and a representativeness mechanism, organizing many phenomena under one account —
    "systematic evaluation of alternative theories" in the journal's own phrasing.
  - **Self-check:** does your conceptual theory adjudicate named rivals on diagnostic cases, or merely catalog a phenomenon?

### Formal model of heuristic inference (bounded rationality)

- **Gigerenzer, G., & Goldstein, D. G., "Reasoning the fast and frugal way: Models of bounded rationality," Psychological Review 1996, 103(4):650–669.**
  Verified: vol/issue/pages confirmed (author-hosted PDF; PhilPapers).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** specifies simple inference algorithms and shows, by analysis and
    simulation, that they perform competitively while violating classical-rationality tenets — a model
    whose *predicted performance* is the contribution, set against the optimization rivals.
  - **Self-check:** can you state, as they do, a precise algorithm and show what it predicts versus the standard rival?

### Integrated cognitive architecture (computational theory of mind)

- **Anderson, J. R., Bothell, D., Byrne, M. D., Douglass, S., Lebiere, C., & Qin, Y., "An integrated theory of the mind," Psychological Review 2004, 111(4):1036–1060.**
  Verified: DOI 10.1037/0033-295X.111.4.1036; vol/issue/pages confirmed (ACT-R site; SciRP).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** ACT-R as an integrated architecture — multiple modules with a stated
    production-system mechanism, mapped to cortical regions and to a range of phenomena. A large-scope
    computational theory that still makes the modules and their integration explicit.
  - **Self-check:** if your theory is broad, are its components and their integration *specified*, or is breadth standing in for mechanism?

## Omitted for lack of full Psychological Review verification, or verified to a SIBLING journal (do not attribute to Review)

To keep the list zero-hallucination, the following are **excluded** as Psychological Review exemplars:

- **Nosofsky, R. M., "Attention, similarity, and the identification–categorization relationship"
  (1986)** — the generalized context model (GCM) paper appeared in **_Journal of Experimental
  Psychology: General_ 115(1):39–57, NOT Psychological Review.** Listed here only as a guardrail; the
  exemplar categorization model people half-remember as "a Review paper" is JEP: General.
- **Anderson, J. R., "ACT: A simple theory of complex cognition" (1996)** — appeared in **_American
  Psychologist_ 51(4):355–365, NOT Psychological Review.** Use the verified ACT-R *Psychological Review*
  paper (Anderson et al., 2004) above instead.
- **Tversky & Kahneman, "Judgment under uncertainty: Heuristics and biases" (1974)** — appeared in
  **_Science_ 185:1124–1131, NOT Psychological Review.** Their 1983 conjunction-fallacy paper above *is*
  the Review paper; do not conflate the two.
- **Rescorla & Wagner (1972)** — the associative-learning model appeared as a **book chapter** (in
  Black & Prokasy, *Classical Conditioning II*), not as a *Psychological Review* article; excluded from
  a Review-article exemplars list.

Before adding any new paper to this library, confirm it on a publisher-hosted Psychological Review
record (or the verified DOI) with volume/issue/pages, and update the access-date header. **When in doubt
— especially for famous models with near-identical homes at JEP, Science, American Psychologist, or in
edited volumes — omit.**
