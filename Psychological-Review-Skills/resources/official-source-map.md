# Official Source Map — Psychological Review

Citable sources for the Psychological Review-specific facts used across these skills.
Psychological Review is the American Psychological Association's (APA) flagship journal for
**theoretical contributions** in scientific psychology — new theory, formal/mathematical/
computational models, and major theoretical syntheses. It does **not** publish primary
empirical reports: data appear only to motivate or constrain theory. All access dates below
are **2026-06** (检索于 2026-06；以官网为准). Items that could not be verified from official
sources are listed under "Still unverified" and flagged inline in the skills.

## Verified facts (with source #)

- Psychological Review publishes **theoretical contributions only**, including systematic
  evaluation of alternative theories; surveys, method/design papers, and empirical reports are
  out of scope. (Source 1)
- Article types include full **Theoretical articles** and shorter **Theoretical Notes**. (Source 1)
- No fixed upper length; **> 15,000-word** texts must be justified. (Source 1)
- Replications are publishable only when relevant to / embedded in new or enhanced theory, and
  should be mentioned in the abstract. (Source 1, secondary submission guides)
- Submission and masked peer review run through **Editorial Manager** (editorialmanager.com/rev). (Source 2)
- Current editor: **Elena L. Grigorenko, PhD** (editor since 2022; web-verified 2026-06-22 via APA journal page + Wikipedia, re-verify before relying). (Source 1)
- Manuscripts follow **APA Style** (currently 7th edition): double-spaced, author–date citations,
  separate anonymized title page for masked review. (Sources 3–4)
- Published by the **American Psychological Association**. (Source 1)

## Sibling-journal guard (do not confuse with Psychological Review)

- **Psychological Bulletin** — integrative reviews and meta-analyses (synthesis of evidence), NOT
  new formal theory. A summary belongs there, not at Review.
- **Behavioral and Brain Sciences (BBS)** — target article + open peer commentary format.
- **Trends in Cognitive Sciences** — short, invited opinion/review pieces.
- **JEP family (e.g., JEP: General, JEP: LMC) / Psychological Science** — empirical reports.
- **American Psychologist** — broad APA flagship; note **Anderson (1996), "ACT: A simple theory
  of complex cognition," appeared in *American Psychologist* 51(4):355–365, NOT Psychological
  Review** — use the verified ACT-R *Psychological Review* paper (Anderson et al., 2004) instead.
- **Nosofsky, R. M. (1986), the generalized context model paper, appeared in *Journal of
  Experimental Psychology: General* 115(1):39–57 — NOT Psychological Review.** Do not list it as a
  Review exemplar.
