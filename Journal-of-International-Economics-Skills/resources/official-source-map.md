## Official journal pages (Elsevier / ScienceDirect)

- **https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/journal-of-international-economics/publish/guide-for-authors** — Guide for Authors. Verifies: JIE is the primary outlet for theoretical and empirical research in international economics; scope includes trade patterns, commercial policy, international institutions, exchange rates, open-economy macroeconomics, country/regional growth and development, international finance, international pricing, sovereign debt, factor mobility, spatial economics, and international monetary/fiscal theory and policy; submissions should be original in motivation or modelling structure; all results should be replicable; materials needed for replication, including programs and data sets, must be available at the JIE secure repository; non-refundable submission fee **USD 190 / EUR 169.20 / JPY 20,660**, with **USD 95** reduced fee when all authors are PhD students; VAT is added for relevant European authors; submission starts through Elsevier/Editorial Manager; abstract cap **150 words**; **1-7 keywords**; highlights are encouraged; graphical abstract is encouraged; editable source files are required for production; references can be submitted in any consistent style and Elsevier applies journal style at proof.
- **https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/journal-of-international-economics/publish/guide-for-authors#article-types** — Article-type section of the Guide. Verifies: regular submissions plus **Short Papers** and **Previously-Reviewed Papers (PRPs)**. Short Papers are at most **6,000 words** and at most **5 exhibits**, must be self-contained, and require a separate word-count PDF excluding the title, abstract, acknowledgment footnote, references, and exhibits. PRPs must have gone through editorial process at **American Economic Review, Journal of Political Economy, Econometrica, Quarterly Journal of Economics, or Review of Economic Studies**; authors submit the decision letter and all referee reports; PRPs carry the same fee as regular submissions, may be sent to additional reviewers, and have no guarantee of publication.
- **https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/journal-of-international-economics/publish/guide-for-authors#peer-review** — Peer-review section of the Guide. Verifies: JIE follows a **single anonymized** review process; submissions are initially assessed by editors; suitable submissions are typically sent to a minimum of two reviewers; only one appeal per submission is considered.
- **https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/journal-of-international-economics/about/editorial-board** — Editorial Board. Verifies: current **Editors** are **Martin Uribe, PhD** (Columbia University) and **Costas Arkolakis, PhD** (Yale University); current Co-Editors include Laura Alfaro, Mary Amiti, Yan Bai, Javier Bianchi, Ryan Chahrour, Paola Conconi, Zhen Huo, Eduardo Morales, Gianmarco Ottaviano, Pablo Ottonello, Jesse Schreger, and Jonathan Vogel; the board lists 69 editors/editorial board members across 10 countries/regions.
- **https://data.mendeley.com/journal/00221996** — JIE on Mendeley Data. Verifies: ISSN **0022-1996**; datasets are associated with articles published in JIE; the page lists open-access JIE replication/data records, including files for recent JIE articles.
- **https://www.elsevier.com/researcher/author/policies-and-guidelines/submission-fees** — Elsevier submission-fees policy page. Verifies: Elsevier economics/finance/accounting submission fees are treated as journal community funds; examples include JIE summer schools and the JIE Bhagwati Award.
