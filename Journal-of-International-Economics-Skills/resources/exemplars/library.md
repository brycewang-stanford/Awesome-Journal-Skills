# *Journal of International Economics* Exemplars Library (method × topic)

> **Verified via web search, access date 2026-06-08.** Every paper below was checked to confirm it
> actually appeared in the **_Journal of International Economics_** (JIE) — the Elsevier field journal for
> international trade and international macro/finance (Editors Costas Arkolakis and Martin Uribe; submission
> via Editorial Manager) — with year and volume/issue, against the journal's ScienceDirect article pages,
> the RePEc/IDEAS series record (`ideas.repec.org/a/eee/inecon/...`), and corroborating Google Scholar
> lookups carrying the journal name. Papers without full confirmation **as _JIE_** were **omitted**
> — this is deliberately a short, clean list, not a long, uncertain one.
>
> **Sibling-journal guard.** *JIE* is **not** the *Journal of International Money and Finance*, the *Review
> of International Economics*, the *American Economic Review*, the *Quarterly Journal of Economics*, the
> *Review of Economic Studies*, or *AEJ: Macroeconomics*. Several of the most-cited "international"
> papers — gravity-with-gravitas, the China shock, currency choice, the diffusion of development — live in
> those venues, **not** in JIE. The [Omitted](#omitted-for-lack-of-full-verification-do-not-attribute-to-jie)
> section records the traps checked and rejected.
>
> **Use principle (zero hallucination):** this file gives **design positioning only**. It does not
> reproduce or invent elasticities, pass-through rates, or specific findings — read the original on
> ScienceDirect / RePEc before citing any number. For JIE-specific scope, the submission fee, the Prior
> Review Process, replication-deposit policy, and house style (≤150-word abstract, 1–7 keywords), see
> [`../official-source-map.md`](../official-source-map.md).

## How to use this library

JIE serves two halves of one field — international **trade** and international **macro/finance**. Pick the
row whose **method × question** is closest to yours, then study how that paper makes a contribution
legible to *both* halves — the JIE bar (see
[`../../skills/jie-topic-selection/SKILL.md`](../../skills/jie-topic-selection/SKILL.md),
[`../../skills/jie-contribution-framing/SKILL.md`](../../skills/jie-contribution-framing/SKILL.md), and
[`../../skills/jie-identification-strategy/SKILL.md`](../../skills/jie-identification-strategy/SKILL.md)).
For each, ask the self-check before claiming your paper is "JIE-shaped."

## By method

### Methodological comparison of open-economy modelling choices (open-economy macro)

- **Schmitt-Grohé & Uribe, "Closing Small Open Economy Models," _Journal of International Economics_ 2003, 61(1):163–185.**
  Verified: ScienceDirect `S0022199602000569`; RePEc `eee/inecon/v61y2003i1p163-185`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** takes a pervasive technical nuisance — the unit-root/initial-condition
    problem in incomplete-markets SOE models — and turns *how you close the model* into the question,
    quantitatively comparing the stationarity-inducing devices. The
    [`jie-identification-strategy`](../../skills/jie-identification-strategy/SKILL.md) discipline that, for a
    structural paper, the "identification" is the model's mapping to the data and the robustness of its
    quantitative implications.
  - **Self-check:** if your contribution is methodological, do you show the modelling choice *materially
    changes* a quantitative open-economy conclusion, rather than asserting one device is "correct"?

### Quantitative model of sovereign default linking the current account to default risk (international finance)

- **Aguiar & Gopinath, "Defaultable Debt, Interest Rates and the Current Account," _Journal of International Economics_ 2006, 69(1):64–83.**
  Verified: ScienceDirect `S0022199605000644`; RePEc `eee/inecon/v69y2006i1p64-83`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** embeds endogenous default in a small-open-economy model and shows that
    *trend* (rather than transitory) income shocks reconcile the model with emerging-market data on
    interest rates and the current account — a quantitative-theory contribution whose payoff is matching
    open-economy business-cycle facts ([`jie-data-analysis`](../../skills/jie-data-analysis/SKILL.md):
    calibration disciplined against moments).
  - **Self-check:** does your structural open-economy model earn its keep by *quantitatively* matching the
    cross-country moments your question is about, with the mechanism doing identified work?

### Gravity estimation confronting the endogeneity of trade policy (trade & commercial policy)

- **Baier & Bergstrand, "Do Free Trade Agreements Actually Increase Members' International Trade?," _Journal of International Economics_ 2007, 71(1):72–95.**
  Verified: ScienceDirect `S0022199606000596`; RePEc `eee/inecon/v71y2007i1p72-95`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** confronts the central applied-trade problem — FTAs are *chosen* by countries
    already trading more — and shows that only a panel fixed-effects design (not the IV or control-function
    fixes) removes the bias, multiplying the estimated FTA effect. The
    [`jie-identification-strategy`](../../skills/jie-identification-strategy/SKILL.md) gravity-endogeneity
    move done explicitly and the [`jie-data-analysis`](../../skills/jie-data-analysis/SKILL.md) reflex of
    matching the estimator to the source of bias.
  - **Self-check:** in your gravity / trade-policy regression, is the policy variable's *selection* problem
    named and addressed with the right fixed-effect or instrument, not assumed away?

### Structural estimation of trade elasticities from price and quantity data (trade measurement)

- **Soderbery, "Estimating Import Supply and Demand Elasticities: Analysis and Implications," _Journal of International Economics_ 2015, 96(1):1–17.**
  Verified: ScienceDirect article; RePEc `eee/inecon/v96y2015i1p1-17`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** diagnoses small-sample and bias problems in the standard
    Feenstra/Broda–Weinstein elasticity estimator and offers a hybrid estimator, *re-deriving* a workhorse
    input (trade elasticities) that downstream welfare and gravity work depends on — a measurement/method
    contribution framed for both halves of the field
    ([`jie-contribution-framing`](../../skills/jie-contribution-framing/SKILL.md): a better estimate of a
    parameter the whole field uses).
  - **Self-check:** if you re-estimate a workhorse parameter, do you show the *bias in the incumbent method*
    and that your fix changes a downstream quantitative conclusion?

### Firm-level micro-econometrics adjudicating selection vs. learning (trade & firms)

- **Bernard & Jensen, "Exceptional Exporter Performance: Cause, Effect, or Both?," _Journal of International Economics_ 1999, 47(1):1–25.**
  Verified: ScienceDirect `S0022199698000270`; RePEc `eee/inecon/v47y1999i1p1-25`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** documents exporter premia in firm-level data and then *separates* the rival
    explanations — good firms self-select into exporting vs. exporting makes firms good — using the timing
    of premia around export entry. A model
    [`jie-identification-strategy`](../../skills/jie-identification-strategy/SKILL.md) adjudication in a
    micro-data frame, and a founding paper of the heterogeneous-firms trade agenda.
  - **Self-check:** does your firm-level result *distinguish* selection from a treatment effect with timing
    or design, rather than read a cross-sectional premium as causal?

## Omitted for lack of full verification (do not attribute to _JIE_)

To keep the list zero-hallucination, these famous "international economics" papers were **excluded** after
web-checking — each is real and important, but **not in _JIE_** (the sibling-journal traps the brief
warned of):

- **Anderson & van Wincoop, "Gravity with Gravitas: A Solution to the Border Puzzle" (2003)** — verified in
  the **_American Economic Review_** 93(1):170–192, **not _JIE_**. The multilateral-resistance gravity classic.
- **Helpman, Melitz & Rubinstein, "Estimating Trade Flows: Trading Partners and Trading Volumes" (2008)** —
  verified in the **_Quarterly Journal of Economics_** 123(2):441–487, **not _JIE_**.
- **Autor, Dorn & Hanson, "The China Syndrome" (2013)** — verified in the **_American Economic Review_**
  103(6):2121–2168, **not _JIE_**.
- **Gopinath, Itskhoki & Rigobon, "Currency Choice and Exchange Rate Pass-Through" (2010)** — verified in the
  **_American Economic Review_** 100(1):304–336, **not _JIE_**.
- **Campa & Goldberg, "Exchange Rate Pass-Through into Import Prices" (2005)** — verified in the **_Review of
  Economics and Statistics_** 87(4):679–690, **not _JIE_**.
- **Melitz & Ottaviano, "Market Size, Trade, and Productivity" (2008)** — verified in the **_Review of
  Economic Studies_** 75(1):295–316, **not _JIE_**.

Before adding any new paper to this library, confirm it on the journal's ScienceDirect page (or the RePEc
`eee/inecon` series record) with volume/issue, and update the access-date header. When in doubt, **omit**.
