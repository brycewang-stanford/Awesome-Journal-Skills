# Worked Example: Reframing an Introduction for Journal of Economic Geography

This is a fictional example. It does not describe a real paper.

## Before

> This paper studies an important topic using a large dataset and several robustness checks. We contribute to the literature by adding new evidence and showing heterogeneous effects.

## Diagnosis

- The first sentence does not name the JEG audience or the journal's economic geography, spatial economics, regional development, innovation clusters, trade, and place-based policy scope.
- The contribution could fit several siblings: Journal of Urban Economics, Regional Studies, Economic Geography, and Journal of International Economics.
- The mechanism words that matter here are absent: spatial clustering, regional divergence, place-based policy, network geography.

## After

> This paper asks how **spatial clustering** changes the decision margin that JEG readers care about. The design ties the estimate to **regional divergence**, separates the main mechanism from **place-based policy**, and reports uncertainty in a way that makes the policy or theory implication portable beyond the study setting.
