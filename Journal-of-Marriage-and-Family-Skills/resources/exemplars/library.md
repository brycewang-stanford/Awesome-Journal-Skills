# JMF Exemplars Library (method × topic)

> **Verified via web search, access date 2026-06-08.** Every paper below was checked against Wiley
> Online Library (`onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/...jomf...` or the legacy `...j.1741-3737...` /
> `...j.0022-2445...` DOIs) to confirm it actually appeared in the **Journal of Marriage and Family**
> (National Council on Family Relations / Wiley), with year and volume/pages. Candidates without full
> JMF venue confirmation were **omitted** — it is deliberately a short, clean list rather than a long,
> uncertain one.
>
> **Same-journal note:** JMF was titled *Journal of Marriage and **the** Family* through 2000 and was
> renamed *Journal of Marriage and Family* from 2001. **Both names are the same journal**; cite each
> paper under the title it carried in its year.
>
> **Use principle (zero hallucination):** this file gives **design positioning only**. It does not
> reproduce or invent coefficients, effect sizes, or specific findings — read the original on Wiley
> before citing any number. For JMF-specific scope, formats, and the structured-abstract spec, see
> [`../official-source-map.md`](../official-source-map.md).

## How to use this library

Pick the row whose **method × question** is closest to yours, then study how that paper makes the
**family, couple, parent–child, or kin relationship central** (not a control variable) and carries a
lesson for the **interdisciplinary family-science audience** — the JMF bar (see
[`../../skills/jmf-topic-selection/SKILL.md`](../../skills/jmf-topic-selection/SKILL.md),
[`../../skills/jmf-theory-and-conceptual-framework/SKILL.md`](../../skills/jmf-theory-and-conceptual-framework/SKILL.md),
and [`../../skills/jmf-writing-style/SKILL.md`](../../skills/jmf-writing-style/SKILL.md)). For each, ask
the self-check question before claiming your paper is "JMF-shaped." Cross-cutting design objections live
in the shared hub: [`../../../shared-resources/empirical-methods/reviewer-objection-checklist.md`](../../../shared-resources/empirical-methods/reviewer-objection-checklist.md).

## By method

### Conceptual / framework paper that reframes a family institution (family theory)

- **Cherlin, "The Deinstitutionalization of American Marriage," *Journal of Marriage and Family* 2004, 66(4):848–861.**
  Verified: `onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/j.0022-2445.2004.00058.x`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** one organizing idea — the weakening of social norms governing marriage —
    stated plainly enough to reorganize how family scholars read cohabitation, same-sex marriage, and
    union instability. The canonical "framework, not regression" JMF contribution; pairs with
    [`../../skills/jmf-theory-and-conceptual-framework/SKILL.md`](../../skills/jmf-theory-and-conceptual-framework/SKILL.md).
  - **Self-check:** does your framework explain a *family* process (norms, roles, transitions), or only
    relabel a correlation?

### Integrative review that adjudicates selection vs. effect (divorce / child wellbeing)

- **Amato, "The Consequences of Divorce for Adults and Children," *Journal of Marriage and Family* 2000, 62(4):1269–1287.**
  Verified: `onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/j.1741-3737.2000.01269.x`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** organizes a sprawling literature under a divorce-stress-adjustment
    framework and confronts the field's standing rival — *selection vs. divorce effect* — head-on, the
    exact adjudication [`../../skills/jmf-research-design/SKILL.md`](../../skills/jmf-research-design/SKILL.md)
    demands.
  - **Self-check:** can you state, in one sentence, what the data would look like if selection rather
    than your mechanism drove the result?

### Mixed-methods test of family formation among low-income parents (demography + qualitative)

- **Gibson-Davis, Edin & McLanahan, "High Hopes but Even Higher Expectations: The Retreat From Marriage Among Low-Income Couples," *Journal of Marriage and Family* 2005, 67(5):1301–1312.**
  Verified: `onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/j.1741-3737.2005.00218.x`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** pairs Fragile-Families survey data with an embedded qualitative strand to
    explain a gap between stated marriage intentions and behavior — a model of multi-method integration
    where each strand does work the other cannot (see the qualitative/mixed section of
    [`../../skills/jmf-research-design/SKILL.md`](../../skills/jmf-research-design/SKILL.md)).
  - **Self-check:** in your mixed design, does each strand add something unavailable from the other strand, with an
    explicit joint interpretation?

### Quantitative synthesis of a dyadic association (meta-analysis / couples)

- **Proulx, Helms & Buehler, "Marital Quality and Personal Well-Being: A Meta-Analysis," *Journal of Marriage and Family* 2007, 69(3):576–593.**
  Verified: `onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/j.1741-3737.2007.00393.x`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** turns dozens of marital-quality studies into a single pooled
    cross-sectional vs. longitudinal contrast and tests moderators — showing how a *relational* construct
    (marital quality) is synthesized rigorously rather than narratively.
  - **Self-check:** is your relational construct measured and validated as a couple/dyadic property, with
    its non-independence respected, before you pool or compare it?

### Decade-in-review that maps a subfield for the interdisciplinary audience (families & health)

- **Carr & Springer, "Advances in Families and Health Research in the 21st Century," *Journal of Marriage and Family* 2010, 72(3):743–761.**
  Verified: `onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/j.1741-3737.2010.00728.x`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a JMF decade-review that organizes "for whom, which outcomes, under what
    conditions" families shape health — the genre that reaches sociologists, demographers, and
    health researchers at once (the interdisciplinary-reach test in
    [`../../skills/jmf-topic-selection/SKILL.md`](../../skills/jmf-topic-selection/SKILL.md)).
  - **Self-check:** would a family scientist from a *different* discipline see why your question matters,
    without digging for the "so what"?

## Omitted for lack of full verification or wrong venue (do not attribute to JMF without re-checking)

To keep the list zero-hallucination, the following well-known candidates were **excluded** after
checking — several are classic **sibling-journal confusions** the JMF skills warn about:

- **McLanahan, "Diverging Destinies: How Children Are Faring Under the Second Demographic Transition"
  (2004)** — verified to be in ***Demography*** 41(4):607–627 (PAA presidential address), **not JMF**.
  Listed only as a guardrail.
- **Sayer & Bianchi, "Women's Economic Independence and the Probability of Divorce" (2000)** — verified
  to be in the ***Journal of Family Issues*** 21(7), **not JMF**. A direct sibling-confusion trap.
- **Sweeney, "Two Decades of Family Change: The Shifting Economic Foundations of Marriage" (2002)** —
  verified to be in the ***American Sociological Review*** 67(1):132–147, **not JMF**.
- **Edin & Kefalas, *Promises I Can Keep* (2005)** — a University of California Press **book**, not a
  JMF article; do not cite as a JMF paper.

**Sibling journals NOT to conflate with JMF** (all distinct venues): *Journal of Family Issues*,
*Family Relations* (the other NCFR journal), *Demography*, *Journal of Family Psychology*. Note again
that *Journal of Marriage and **the** Family* (through 2000) **is** JMF under its former title.

Before adding any new paper to this library, confirm it on `onlinelibrary.wiley.com` (article page with
volume/issue and a `jomf` / `j.1741-3737` / `j.0022-2445` DOI) and update the access-date header. When
in doubt, omit.
