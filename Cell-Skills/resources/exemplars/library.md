# Cell Exemplars Library (method × topic)

> **Verified via web search, access date 2026-06-08.** Every paper below was checked against PubMed
> and/or the publisher record to confirm it actually appeared in ***Cell*** (Cell Press flagship), with
> year, volume/issue, pages, and DOI. Papers that could not be fully verified **as *Cell* itself** were
> **omitted** — it is deliberately a short, clean list (6 verified) rather than a long, uncertain one.
>
> **Sibling-confusion guard (read this).** *Cell* is **not** *Nature*, **not** *Science*, and **not**
> any other Cell Press title — **not** *Molecular Cell*, *Cell Reports*, *Cell Metabolism*,
> *Developmental Cell*, *Immunity*, or *Cell Reports Medicine*. Several landmark genome-editing and
> innate-immunity papers people associate with "Cell" actually ran in *Science*; those are listed in the
> **omitted** section as guardrails, not as exemplars.
>
> **Use principle (zero hallucination):** this file gives **design positioning only**. It does not
> reproduce or invent experimental numbers, effect sizes, or specific findings — read the original on
> the publisher's site before citing any result. For Cell-specific deposition, STAR Methods, and author
> resources, see [`../external_tools.md`](../external_tools.md).

## How to use this library

Pick the row whose **method × question** is closest to yours, then study how that paper makes a
*complete, mechanistic* story carry a *broad* lesson — the Cell bar (see
[`../../skills/cell-fit/SKILL.md`](../../skills/cell-fit/SKILL.md) and
[`../../skills/cell-framing/SKILL.md`](../../skills/cell-framing/SKILL.md)). For each, ask the self-check
question before claiming your paper is "Cell-shaped."

## By method

### Defining a new mechanism by reprogramming a cell state (defined-factor / functional genetics)

- **Takahashi & Yamanaka, "Induction of Pluripotent Stem Cells from Mouse Embryonic and Adult Fibroblast
  Cultures by Defined Factors," *Cell* 2006, 126(4):663–676. DOI: 10.1016/j.cell.2006.07.024.**
  Verified: PubMed 16904174; publisher record (cell.com).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a minimal, defined set of factors is *shown* to be sufficient to convert
    one cell fate into another — a single, decisive mechanism (sufficiency by perturbation) with an
    enormous conceptual advance. The canonical "small, complete cause → field-reorganizing lesson"
    *Cell* paper.
  - **Self-check:** does your central claim rest on a *sufficiency/necessity* perturbation, stated so a
    general life-scientist grasps the advance immediately?

### Repurposing a molecular system into a programmable tool (mechanism → method)

- **Qi, Larson, Gilbert, Doudna, Weissman, Arkin & Lim, "Repurposing CRISPR as an RNA-Guided Platform
  for Sequence-Specific Control of Gene Expression," *Cell* 2013, 152(5):1173–1183.
  DOI: 10.1016/j.cell.2013.02.022.**
  Verified: PubMed 23452860; publisher record (cell.com).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** takes an understood mechanism (catalytically dead Cas9 binding DNA) and
    turns it into a general, programmable platform (CRISPRi) — a mechanism worked out cleanly enough to
    *become* a tool the whole field adopts.
  - **Self-check:** does your mechanism generalize into a reusable principle or platform, not just a
    one-off observation in one gene?

### Massively parallel single-cell method + biological atlas (technology-enabled discovery)

- **Macosko, Basu, Satija, Nemesh, Shekhar, Goldman, … McCarroll, "Highly Parallel Genome-wide
  Expression Profiling of Individual Cells Using Nanoliter Droplets," *Cell* 2015, 161(5):1202–1214.
  DOI: 10.1016/j.cell.2015.05.002.**
  Verified: PubMed 26000488; publisher record (cell.com).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a new measurement technology (Drop-seq) is paired with a biological
    payoff (a retinal cell-type atlas) in the same paper — *Cell* rewards a method only when it delivers
    a complete, broadly relevant biological result, not a methods note alone.
  - **Self-check:** does your new technique answer a real biological question in the same paper, or is it
    a tool demo looking for a home?

### In vivo perturbation system enabling causal disease modeling (mouse genetics / model)

- **Platt, Chen, Zhou, Yim, Swiech, Kempton, … Zhang, "CRISPR-Cas9 Knockin Mice for Genome Editing and
  Cancer Modeling," *Cell* 2014, 159(2):440–455. DOI: 10.1016/j.cell.2014.09.014.**
  Verified: PubMed 25263330; publisher record (cell.com).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** builds an in vivo platform (a Cas9-knockin mouse) and immediately uses it
    to model disease causally in multiple tissues — converging in vitro/in vivo evidence and a clear
    translational consequence, the converging-lines-of-evidence shape `cell-fit` demands.
  - **Self-check:** does your system deliver *causal, in vivo* evidence and a broad biological/clinical
    payoff, not just a reagent?

### Structural biology resolving a mechanism of recognition (X-ray / structure → function)

- **Zhou, Whiteley, de Oliveira Mann, Morehouse, Nowak, Fischer, Gray, Mekalanos & Kranzusch, "Structure
  of the Human cGAS–DNA Complex Reveals Enhanced Control of Immune Surveillance," *Cell* 2018,
  174(2):300–311.e11. DOI: 10.1016/j.cell.2018.06.026.**
  Verified: PubMed 30007416; publisher record (cell.com).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a structure is used to *explain a mechanism* (how human cGAS tunes DNA
    sensing) and is backed by function — structure in service of a biological principle, not a
    coordinate set. Structure + perturbation is the converging evidence *Cell* expects.
  - **Self-check:** does your structure *decide* a mechanistic question and pair with functional data,
    rather than describe a fold?

### Conceptual synthesis that reframes a whole field (landmark review/perspective)

- **Hanahan & Weinberg, "Hallmarks of Cancer: The Next Generation," *Cell* 2011, 144(5):646–674.
  DOI: 10.1016/j.cell.2011.02.013.**
  Verified: PubMed 21376230; publisher record (cell.com).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** organizes a sprawling field into a small set of unifying principles —
    *Cell* publishes synthesis when it changes how a broad community *thinks*, the strongest rung of the
    `cell-fit` significance ladder ("unifies a model"). Use it to calibrate the *breadth* of lesson a
    *Cell* contribution should carry, even for a primary Article.
  - **Self-check:** does your single-paper advance imply a principle that travels beyond your system, the
    way a field-defining synthesis would?

## Omitted for sibling-confusion or lack of full *Cell* verification (do NOT attribute to *Cell*)

To keep the list zero-hallucination, the following high-profile candidates were **excluded** after
checking — most because they ran in a *sibling* venue, not *Cell* itself:

- **Jinek, Chylinski, Fonfara, Hauer, Doudna & Charpentier, "A Programmable Dual-RNA-Guided DNA
  Endonuclease in Adaptive Bacterial Immunity" (2012)** — verified to be in ***Science*** 337(6096):816–821
  (DOI 10.1126/science.1225829), **not *Cell***. Listed only as a guardrail.
- **Cong, Ran, Cox, … Zhang, "Multiplex Genome Engineering Using CRISPR/Cas Systems" (2013)** — verified
  ***Science*** (DOI 10.1126/science.1231143), **not *Cell***.
- **Mali, Yang, Esvelt, … Church, "RNA-Guided Human Genome Engineering via Cas9" (2013)** — verified
  ***Science*** 339(6121):823–826 (DOI 10.1126/science.1232033), **not *Cell***.
- **Sun, Wu, Du, Chen & Chen, "Cyclic GMP-AMP Synthase Is a Cytosolic DNA Sensor…" (2013)** — verified
  ***Science*** 339(6121):786–791 (DOI 10.1126/science.1232458), **not *Cell***. (The *Cell* cGAS
  exemplar above is the 2018 human cGAS–DNA *structure* paper, a different study.)
- **Jiang et al., "Structures of a CRISPR-Cas9 R-loop complex primed for DNA cleavage" (2016)** —
  verified ***Science*** 351(6275):867–871 (DOI 10.1126/science.aad8282), **not *Cell***.

Before adding any new paper to this library, confirm on PubMed and the publisher record that the
**journal field literally reads *Cell*** (not *Molecular Cell* / *Cell Reports* / *Cell Metabolism* /
*Developmental Cell* / *Immunity* / *Cell Reports Medicine*, and not *Nature* / *Science*), capture the
volume(issue):pages and DOI, and update the access-date header. When in doubt, omit.
