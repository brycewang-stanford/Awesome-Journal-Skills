## VERIFIED facts injected into the skills (with source #)

- **Identity / scope:** positive (economics-based) accounting research — the role of accounting within the firm; the information content of accounting numbers in capital markets; accounting in financial contracting and the monitoring of agency relationships; the determination of accounting standards; government regulation of corporate disclosure and the accounting profession; and the theory of the accounting firm. The journal explicitly encourages applying economic theory to explain accounting phenomena. (#1, #6)
- **Publisher / history:** Elsevier; ISSN 0165-4101; established **1979**; bimonthly. (#6)
- **Founding Editors:** Ross L. Watts and Jerold L. Zimmerman — the originators of Positive Accounting Theory. (#5)
- **Co-Editors (Editors-in-Chief):** J. Core (MIT), E. deHaan (Stanford, formerly University of Washington), and W.R. Guay (University of Pennsylvania, Wharton). (#5, #6)
- **Submission system:** Elsevier **Editorial Manager (EM)**; the fee is paid through Elsevier's Submission Start portal (submissionstart.elsevier.com). (#1, #3)
- **Submission fee:** **USD 650** for unsolicited new manuscripts; **no fee** for resubmissions (revisions); paid during the submission process; EU authors add applicable VAT; proceeds fund journal academic activities and reviewer reimbursements. (#1, #2, #4)
- **Review model:** **double anonymized** review; editor first assesses suitability; suitable papers go to a **minimum of two** independent expert reviewers; the Editor makes the final decision and that decision is final. Requires a separate anonymized manuscript plus a title page with author details. (#1, #2)
- **Formatting / style:** Elsevier author-date (Harvard) reference style; alphabetical then chronological reference list with a/b/c suffixes; numbered sections (1, 1.1, 1.1.1); single-column native word-processor format; LaTeX via elsarticle.cls + BibTeX. (#2)
- **Required packaging:** mandatory **Highlights** (2-5 bullets, max 125 characters each); up to **6 keywords** (American spelling); up to **6 JEL classification codes**. (#2)
- **Data/code policy:** **no mandatory** replication archive; the journal follows Elsevier's generic research-data policy that "encourages and enables" data sharing, data linking, a voluntary Data Statement, and the [dataset] citation tag — contrasting with JAR (mandatory) and JFE (mandatory deposit). (#2, #7)
- **JAE Conference pipeline:** invitation-only annual conference (sponsored by Wharton, Charles River Associates, Cambridge Business Publishers; 2025 at U. Penn); selected papers go into a dedicated special conference issue; same USD 650 fee; USD 5,000 attendee-voted Best Paper award. (#4)
- **Editorial structure:** small handful of hands-on co-Editors make final decisions, supported by a large (~40-person) Associate Editor body — no INFORMS-style departmental/area-editor routing. (#5)
- **Article Transfer Service:** papers deemed unsuitable can be desk-rejected without review or redirected to another journal. (#1)

## Items flagged approximate or 待核实 (confirm via sources above)

- **Submission-fee amount — 待核实 against the live page.** The current/2024 Guide for Authors (#1, #2) and the 2025 JAE Conference (#4) state **USD 650**; the Wharton Accounting payment page (#3) still shows USD 500 (apparently a stale mirror). USD 650 is treated as the authoritative current figure; USD 500 is noted only as a historical/stale value.
- **Abstract word limit — 待核实.** JAE's own Guide for Authors specifies only a "concise and factual" abstract (purpose, principal results, major conclusions; must stand alone; avoid references and uncommon abbreviations) with **no numeric cap**. Third-party formatting sites cite ~250 words, but that figure is **not** stated in JAE's official guide and is treated as unverified.
- **Main-text length / page / word limit — not specified.** No page or word limit is stated anywhere in the official Guide for Authors; recorded as *not specified* rather than a confirmed "no limit."
- **Editorial-board roster — 待核实.** Co-Editors beyond the three EICs and the full Associate Editor roster were verified from a **2022 archived** ScienceDirect snapshot (#5) plus 2025 Wikipedia (#6); the live 2026 ScienceDirect editorial-board page returned HTTP 403 to automated fetch and could not be re-confirmed, so the most recent roster may have changed. The three co-Editors (Core, deHaan, Guay) are corroborated by Wikipedia.
- **Highlights / keywords / JEL-code requirements — 待核实 for 2026.** Sourced from the Feb-2024 archived guide (#2); could not be re-verified against the live ScienceDirect page (403 to automated fetch).
- **Live-page access note.** The live ScienceDirect Guide-for-Authors and editorial-board pages block automated fetching (403 / CAPTCHA); facts were corroborated via Elsevier-hosted official text captured in the Wayback Machine and via official sponsor pages (Wharton, EAA/ARC conference).
