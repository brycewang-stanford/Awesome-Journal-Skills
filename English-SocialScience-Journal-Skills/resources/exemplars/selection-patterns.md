# Venue-Selection Patterns

These routing archetypes help an agent avoid prestige-first venue selection.
They are not paper exemplars.

## Economics

- General-interest top five: use only when the paper changes a broad economics
  conversation, not merely because identification is strong.
- Field flagships: use when the mechanism and readers are field-specific
  (labor, public, development, trade, monetary, econometrics, theory).
- Review/perspective venues: use when synthesis, policy interpretation, or
  field-shaping argument is the product, not when the paper is a standard
  empirical article.

## Finance

- Top-3 finance requires a finance-first question and contribution; a finance
  variable inside a broader economics paper is not enough.
- Separate asset pricing, corporate finance, intermediation, market
  microstructure, and international finance before choosing the journal.

## Management And Business

- AMR is theory-only; AMJ is empirical theory-building; ASQ is organization
  theory/sociology of organizations; SMJ is strategy-first.
- Marketing Science is not interchangeable with Journal of Marketing or Journal
  of Marketing Research: model/optimization, managerial strategy, and consumer
  empirical design lead to different targets.
- MISQ/ISR expect an information-systems contribution, not simply a digital
  setting.

## Output Discipline

Return the top venue skill, two alternatives, a venue to avoid, and the reason
the paper belongs to a field audience rather than a prestige target.
