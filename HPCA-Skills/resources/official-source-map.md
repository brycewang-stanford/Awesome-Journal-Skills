# Official Source Map - HPCA

Access date: 2026-07-09

This map records the official sources behind every HPCA-specific fact in this pack.
HPCA is run by the IEEE Computer Society Technical Committee on Computer Architecture
(TCCA), and each edition's organizing committee re-decides deadlines, page rules,
review mechanics, and artifact policy. Reopen the current edition's site under
`20XX.hpca-conf.org` before giving submission-ready advice.

## Verified facts used in the skills (cycle snapshot)

- HPCA 2026 was the **32nd** edition, held in **Sydney, Australia, January 31 -
  February 4, 2026** — the previous edition had just concluded when this pack was
  checked (2026-07-09).
- HPCA 2027 is the **33rd** edition; its **paper submission deadline is July 24,
  2026 (23:59 AoE)**, with abstract-and-conflict registration required about one
  week earlier. Reported acceptance notification is November 6, 2026.
- The main body is capped at **11 pages, not including references** (2025 cycle),
  with a **minimum 10pt font and 12pt leading**; references are **unlimited** and
  **each reference must list all its authors** or the paper may be rejected.
- Review is **double-blind**: author names appear only in the submission form.
  Recent cycles run a **single combined rebuttal/revision window** (not two review
  rounds), and **AI-generated content must be disclosed in the acknowledgments**.
- Submission is on **HotCRP** (`hpca<year>.hotcrp.com`); **artifact evaluation** is
  voluntary, post-acceptance, on a separate HotCRP (`hpca<year>ae.hotcrp.com`), and
  earns **IEEE reproducibility badges** printed on the paper.
- HPCA is sponsored by **IEEE-CS TCCA** alone; there is no ACM co-sponsorship, and
  the badging scheme is IEEE's, not the ACM Artifact Review and Badging policy.
- The **HPCA Test of Time Award** (TCCA) is presented for past HPCA papers of
  demonstrated long-term impact (~18-22 years out); recent winners anchor the
  exemplars library.
