# Consumer-Research Tools & Resources (JCR)

External data sources, analysis software, and writing tools for preparing a **Journal of
Consumer Research (JCR)** manuscript. JCR is explicitly **multi-disciplinary** — drawing on
psychology, anthropology, sociology, economics, marketing, and statistics — and two flagship
traditions coexist under one masthead: **multi-study behavioral experiments** and
interpretive **Consumer Culture Theory (CCT)** work. Verify licensing and the current JCR
research-transparency policy before relying on any resource.

## 1. Participant sourcing & data collection

- **Behavioral / experimental**: Prolific, CloudResearch/MTurk, Qualtrics, Gorilla, university SONA pools (randomization, manipulation and attention checks).
- **Interpretive / CCT**: depth interviews, ethnographic fieldnotes, netnography of online communities, archival/historical consumption records.
- **Field / secondary**: retail scanner & loyalty panels (NielsenIQ, IRI), platform field-experiment partners, social-media/text corpora.

## 2. Analysis software

- **Experiments (dominant quantitative tradition)**: R (`afex`, `emmeans`, `effectsize`), SPSS + **PROCESS macro (Hayes)** for mediation/moderation with bootstrapped CIs, JASP/jamovi; spotlight/floodlight for continuous moderators.
- **Measurement & multi-study synthesis**: `lavaan`, `semTools`, `psych`, `metafor`.
- **Power & design**: G*Power, R `pwr`/`Superpower`.
- **Interpretive / CCT**: NVivo, ATLAS.ti, Dedoose for coding interviews, fieldnotes, and netnographic data; hermeneutic interpretation with an audit trail.

## 3. Transparency repositories (JCR-approved)
OSF, Harvard Dataverse, Qualitative Data Repository (qualitative data), ResearchBox. Provide
statistical/programming replication code (or a written description for proprietary code) and
retain data **≥ 7 years** after publication. Consult JCR's "Research Method Transparency
Guidelines and Reporting Requirements." Preregistration / Registered Reports: OSF Registries,
AsPredicted.

## 4. Reference & writing

- Zotero / EndNote configured to a **Chicago** author-date style (JCR defaults to the Chicago Manual of Style for matters not in its own style guide).
- **Microsoft Word** is required for the manuscript and the final accepted version.

## 5. Submission
ScholarOne Manuscripts at **mc.manuscriptcentral.com/jconres**; fully anonymize for
double-anonymized review; prepare the Step-6 Data Collection Statement; no submission or
publication fee (only optional open-access and color-print charges).

## 6. Verify before you rely
Editorial team, links, limits, fees, approved repositories, and transparency rules change.
Confirm current requirements on consumerresearcher.com and the OUP JCR instructions.
