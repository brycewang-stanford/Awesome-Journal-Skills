# JCR Exemplars Library (method × topic)

> **Verified via web search, access date 2026-06-07.** Every paper below was checked against Oxford
> Academic's JCR article pages (`academic.oup.com/jcr/...`) to confirm it actually appeared in *The
> Journal of Consumer Research* (Oxford University Press), with year and volume/issue. Papers that could
> not be fully verified as JCR were **omitted** — this is deliberately a short, clean list rather than a
> long, uncertain one.
>
> **Sibling-confusion guard.** JCR is **not** the *Journal of Marketing*, the *Journal of Marketing
> Research* (JMR), the *Journal of Consumer Psychology* (JCP), or *Marketing Science*. Several
> frequently-cited consumer-behavior papers live in those sibling venues; a handful of near-misses are
> documented in the omissions section so they are not misattributed to JCR.
>
> **Use principle (zero hallucination):** this file gives **design and framing positioning only**. It
> does not reproduce or invent study results, effect sizes, or coefficients — read the original on
> Oxford Academic before citing any finding. For JCR-specific policy, scope, and house style, see
> [`../official-source-map.md`](../official-source-map.md).

## How to use this library

Pick the row whose **method × question** is closest to yours, then study how that paper makes a
consumption insight carry a **conceptual contribution to consumer theory** — the JCR bar (see
[`../../skills/jcr-topic-selection/SKILL.md`](../../skills/jcr-topic-selection/SKILL.md),
[`../../skills/jcr-theory-development/SKILL.md`](../../skills/jcr-theory-development/SKILL.md), and
[`../../skills/jcr-contribution-framing/SKILL.md`](../../skills/jcr-contribution-framing/SKILL.md)).
JCR runs **two flagship traditions on equal footing** — theory-driven behavioral experiments and
interpretive Consumer Culture Theory (CCT) — so the list spans both. For each, ask the self-check
question before claiming your paper is "JCR-shaped."

## By method

### Conceptual / theory-building about the consumer self (foundational theory)

- **Belk, "Possessions and the Extended Self," JCR 1988, 15(2):139–168.**
  Verified: `academic.oup.com/jcr/article-abstract/15/2/139/1841428`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** advances a single, far-reaching construct — that our possessions become
    part of who we are — and organizes a sprawling set of consumption phenomena (brands, collections,
    attachment, loss) under one idea. The canonical "build the construct, then the field uses it" JCR
    move. Pairs with [`../../skills/jcr-theory-development/SKILL.md`](../../skills/jcr-theory-development/SKILL.md).
  - **Self-check:** does your paper introduce or sharpen a *construct* an interdisciplinary reader can
    grasp in a paragraph and carry into their own consumption context?

### Ethnography of a consumption subculture (interpretive / CCT)

- **Schouten & McAlexander, "Subcultures of Consumption: An Ethnography of the New Bikers," JCR 1995, 22(1):43–61.**
  Verified: `academic.oup.com/jcr/article-abstract/22/1/43/1808114`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** three years of immersive fieldwork with Harley-Davidson owners theorize a
    new analytic category — the subculture of consumption — moving from thick description to a
    second-order conceptual framework, the CCT standard in
    [`../../skills/jcr-methods/SKILL.md`](../../skills/jcr-methods/SKILL.md) and
    [`../../skills/jcr-theory-development/SKILL.md`](../../skills/jcr-theory-development/SKILL.md).
  - **Self-check:** does your immersion run deep enough to reach conceptual claims, not just rich
    description (`jcr-theory-development` anti-pattern "CCT-as-description")?

### Relationship theory imported into the consumer–brand bond (mixed / interpretive)

- **Fournier, "Consumers and Their Brands: Developing Relationship Theory in Consumer Research," JCR 1998, 24(4):343–373.**
  Verified: `academic.oup.com/jcr/article-abstract/24/4/343/1797962`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** borrows a base discipline's *theoretical engine* (interpersonal
    relationship theory) — not a decorative citation — and reconstructs it for the consumer–brand
    domain, grounding the framework in depth case data. A model of `jcr-topic-selection`'s "borrow the
    field's theory, not one paper."
  - **Self-check:** are you importing another field's *logic* and changing a consumer-theory
    conversation, or just name-checking a construct (`jcr-theory-development` anti-pattern
    "borrowed-label theory")?

### Phenomenological depth interviews on lived consumption (interpretive)

- **Mick & Fournier, "Paradoxes of Technology: Consumer Cognizance, Emotions, and Coping Strategies," JCR 1998, 25(2):123–143.**
  Verified: `academic.oup.com/jcr/article-abstract/25/2/123/1799492`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** repeated depth interviews across households yield a conceptual framework
    of technology *paradoxes* and the emotions and coping they provoke — interpretive evidence
    delivering an explicit conceptual structure, the goal in
    [`../../skills/jcr-methods/SKILL.md`](../../skills/jcr-methods/SKILL.md).
  - **Self-check:** does your qualitative design yield second-order constructs and a framework, with a
    trustworthiness plan (immersion, triangulation), rather than illustrative quotes?

### Multi-study behavioral experiment isolating an automatic process (experiments)

- **Fitzsimons, Chartrand & Fitzsimons, "Automatic Effects of Brand Exposure on Motivated Behavior: How Apple Makes You 'Think Different'," JCR 2008, 35(1):21–35.**
  Verified: `academic.oup.com/jcr/article-abstract/35/1/21/1847975`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** the dominant JCR tradition done well — a theory-driven psychological
    **process** (nonconscious brand priming of goal-directed behavior) tested across controlled
    experiments with the moderation the mechanism predicts. The template behind
    [`../../skills/jcr-theory-development/SKILL.md`](../../skills/jcr-theory-development/SKILL.md)'s
    effect → process → boundary chain.
  - **Self-check:** does your package establish the effect, then *mediation/moderation-of-process*, then
    a predicted boundary — not a single study asked to carry a process claim (`jcr-methods`)?

### Affect-as-information judgment experiment (experiments / decision making)

- **Pham, "Representativeness, Relevance, and the Use of Feelings in Decision Making," JCR 1998, 25(2):144–159.**
  Verified: `academic.oup.com/jcr/article-abstract/25/2/144/1799498`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** specifies *when* consumers rely on feelings as information (the
    "how-do-I-feel-about-it?" heuristic) and the conditions that switch reliance on and off — a clean
    process-with-boundary contribution from the judgment-and-decision-making base discipline.
  - **Self-check:** does your experiment pin down a *moderator the process predicts*, turning a
    boundary condition into theory rather than a robustness afterthought (`jcr-theory-development`)?

## Omitted for lack of full JCR verification (do not attribute to JCR without re-checking)

To keep the list zero-hallucination, the following frequently-cited consumer-behavior candidates were
**excluded** after web search placed them in a **sibling venue**, not JCR:

- **Aaker, "Dimensions of Brand Personality" (1997)** — verified to be in the *Journal of Marketing
  Research* (JMR) 34(3):347–356, **not JCR**. Listed here only as a sibling-confusion guardrail.
- **Thaler, "Mental Accounting and Consumer Choice"** — Thaler's mental-accounting work appeared in
  *Marketing Science* (1985) and *Journal of Behavioral Decision Making* (1999), **not JCR**; the exact
  JCR placement could not be confirmed, so it is omitted (many JCR papers *apply* mental accounting,
  but those are different articles).
- **Baumeister/Muraven/Vohs ego-depletion & self-regulation foundations** — the foundational papers are
  in *Journal of Personality and Social Psychology* (1998) and *Psychological Bulletin* (2000), **not
  JCR**. JCR publishes self-control *consumption* applications, but the cited foundations are not JCR.
- **Dhar & Simonson, "The Effect of Forced Choice on Choice"** — the original appears to be a *JMR*
  (2003) paper; only a later JCR commentary/replication surfaced cleanly in search, so the original is
  omitted as not-confirmed-JCR.

Before adding any new paper to this library, confirm it on `academic.oup.com/jcr` (article page with
volume/issue) — **not** on a JMR, JCP, JM, or Marketing Science page — and update the access-date
header. When in doubt, omit.
