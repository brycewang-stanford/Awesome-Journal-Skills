## Verified 2026-cycle facts used in the skills

- UAI 2026 is the 42nd Conference on Uncertainty in Artificial Intelligence, held
  August 17–21, 2026 in Amsterdam, the Netherlands, as an in-person conference with
  virtual elements; tutorials Monday, main conference Tuesday–Thursday, workshops
  Friday.
- Submissions ran on OpenReview, opening January 25, 2026 and closing February 25,
  2026, 23:59 AoE. No separate abstract-registration deadline was posted for 2026.
- Format: UAI LaTeX template; main part ≤ 8 pages; unlimited references and appendices
  after the main part **in the same PDF**; single PDF ≤ 15 MB; optional supplementary
  ZIP (source code, data) ≤ 50 MB, which reviewers are not required to consider.
- Review was double-blind; authors were told to use the third person for their own
  work, avoid names in acknowledgements and detailed grant information, and avoid
  external links that might reveal identity or affiliation.
- Posted review criteria: technical correctness, novelty, whether claims are backed up
  convincingly, and clarity of writing. Code and data availability was strongly
  encouraged.
- Submitting authors agreed that at least one author would review for UAI if asked by
  the Program Chairs.
- 2026 pipeline: bidding Mar 2–9; reviews written Mar 21 – Apr 11; author
  response/discussion Apr 23 – May 2; reviewer+AC discussion May 2–8; notification
  Jun 1; camera-ready Jul 27.
- All accepted papers are presented in poster sessions and spotlight presentations
  (physically or remotely per the 2026 wording), with longer presentations for
  selected papers; all accepted papers are published in a PMLR volume.
- UAI is organized by the AUAI; PMLR is open access with no author-side fee, and the
  conference is funded through registration. Like the other CS conferences in this
  collection, UAI has no standing editor-in-chief — leadership rotates per edition
  (e.g., v244 editors Kiyavash & Mooij for 2024; v286 editors Chiappa & Magliacane
  for 2025).
