## Verified facts encoded in the pack

- **Publisher / identity:** SAGE journal in comparative politics; subscription access; current SAGE author page displays impact factor metrics as contextual figures. (#1, #2)
- **No standard submit/publish fee:** no fees payable to submit or publish in the journal; optional open access may carry separate charges under SAGE Choice/Open Access agreements. (#1)
- **Preprints:** CPS considers manuscripts posted on preprint servers; authors should enter the DOI and avoid posting updated versions while the manuscript is under review. (#1)
- **Review identity:** double-anonymized. (#1)
- **Article type:** original research articles across the range of comparative-political-science methods; only manuscripts meeting CPS aims, scope, and standards are sent out for review. (#1)
- **Evaluation criteria:** important political phenomenon or causal process, subfield-wide research question, literature positioning, appropriate research design, method fit, validation of evidence, and transparent data-analysis process. (#1)
- **Anonymization:** fully anonymized manuscript file sent to peer reviewers; title page withheld from reviewers and carries author names/affiliations, acknowledgments, conflict/funding/ethics/data declarations, and other identifying details. (#1)
- **Length / format:** maximum 11,000 words; references, tables, and figures excluded; longer submissions not considered; double-spaced, 1-inch margins, 12-point font, Times New Roman preferred. (#1)
- **Submission route:** Sage Track / ScholarOne. (#1)
- **ORCID:** ORCID ID required for the submitting author; co-authors are strongly encouraged to link ORCID before acceptance. (#1)
- **Data availability:** title-page declarations include a data availability statement. (#1)
- **Replication materials:** quantitative papers are not granted final acceptance until data, code, log files, and related replication materials are deposited at the CPS Dataverse. (#1, #4)
- **Reference style:** APA reference style; every in-text citation must match a reference-list entry and vice versa. (#1)
