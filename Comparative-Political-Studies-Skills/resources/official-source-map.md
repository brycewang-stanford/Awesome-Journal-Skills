## Official journal, submission & author pages

- **https://journals.sagepub.com/author-instructions/cps**
  — *Comparative Political Studies* Submission Guidelines (SAGE). Verifies: articles **maximum 11,000
  words**, with **references, tables, and figures excluded** from the word limit (longer submissions not
  considered); an **unstructured abstract of ~150 words** between title and main body stating purpose,
  major findings, and conclusions; manuscripts **double-spaced, 1" margins, 12-pt (Times New Roman
  preferred)**; **anonymity** required — information that compromises anonymity is removed/anonymized and
  placed on the **title page**, and the anonymized version goes to reviewers; submission via **SAGE Track
  (ScholarOne)**; **APA-style author-date references** (every in-text cite has a matching list entry and
  vice versa); **ORCID required for the submitting author** (co-authors encouraged to link before
  acceptance); a **data availability statement** is required; **preregistration optional** — an
  anonymized pre-analysis plan may be submitted as supplementary material and supplied to reviewers on
  request. (检索于 2026-06；以官网为准)
- **https://journals.sagepub.com/home/cps**
  — CPS on SAGE Journals (publisher home, current issues, aims & scope).
- **https://us.sagepub.com/en-us/nam/comparative-political-studies/journal200828**
  — SAGE journal page (description, indexing).

## Data access & replication policy

- **https://journals.sagepub.com/author-instructions/cps**
  — CPS data policy (within the submission guidelines). Verifies: **papers presenting quantitative
  analyses will not be granted final acceptance until replication materials (data, code, log files,
  etc.) are deposited at the CPS Dataverse**; authors must include a **data availability statement**;
  if data cannot be shared and sharing is a publication requirement, authors should **consult the
  journal editorial office**.
- **https://dataverse.harvard.edu/dataverse/cps** — **CPS Dataverse** on **Harvard Dataverse**, the
  journal's replication-materials repository.

## Scope / sibling-journal positioning

- CPS is the **comparative-politics specialist**. It is **not**: the **American Political Science
  Review (APSR)** or **American Journal of Political Science (AJPS)** (general US political-science
  flagships); **World Politics** or **International Organization** (IR / IR-adjacent comparative);
  the **British Journal of Political Science** (broad UK generalist); or **Comparative Politics**
  (the CUNY journal — a different publisher, more qualitative-leaning). Use this list as a
  do-not-misattribute guard when building the exemplar library.
