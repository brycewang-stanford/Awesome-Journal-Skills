# Journal of Economic Literature (JEL) Official Source Map

Primary and authoritative sources behind every JEL-specific fact injected into this
skill pack. **Access date for all entries: 2026-06.** JEL is owned and published by
the **American Economic Association (AEA)**; submission runs through the AEA's
ScholarOne Manuscripts portal. Re-verify volatile items (editor, contact email,
submission fee, length/anonymity rules, policy effective dates) before relying on
them — AEA pages change. Items not captured as stable page text are handled in the
live-check rules below.

## Official AEA / JEL pages (publisher / editorial authority)

- **https://www.aeaweb.org/journals/jel/submissions**
  — JEL Guidelines for Proposals (submission page). Verifies: authors are asked to
  begin with a **proposal — a sketch of roughly ten pages (not counting
  references)** describing the **contents** of the proposed article, **why the topic
  would be of interest to readers**, and **the main references to be covered**;
  authors who are unsure of fit may **email the editor to ask about potential
  interest** before submitting a proposal; the journal **will also consider full
  papers** rather than proposals (proposal route is "more efficient"); each
  (co)author must prepare a separate PDF **"Disclosure Statement"** (financial
  conflicts; IRB where applicable) and submissions without these are **incomplete**;
  the **AEA Data and Code Availability Policy** applies; submission is via
  **ScholarOne Manuscripts**; authors should consult the AEA/JEL **Style Guide**.
  The listed editorial contact email as of access was **d.romer@aeapubs.org**
  (volatile — re-confirm). Accessed 2026-06. **Editor re-verified 2026-06-22:**
  the JEL lead editor is **David H. Romer (UC Berkeley)**, appointed July 2022,
  succeeding Steven N. Durlauf (editor 2013–2022) — confirmed via the AEA "Editors
  of the Journal of Economic Literature" page and corroborating sources; the contact
  email above is consistent with this. Re-confirm the masthead before relying.
- **https://www.aeaweb.org/journals/jel** — JEL journal home (AEA). Verifies: JEL is
  one of the AEA's journals; publishes **survey/review articles, book reviews, and
  the JEL classification system / annotated listings**. Accessed 2026-06. Some
  masthead/editor detail should be re-opened on the live page when needed.
- **https://www.aeaweb.org/econlit/jelCodes.php?view=jel** — AEA EconLit JEL Codes.
  Verifies: the **JEL classification system** is maintained by the AEA; structure is
  a single **letter** (20 top categories A–R plus Y, Z), a **two-digit**
  subcategory, and a **three-character** detailed code; the 20 top-level category
  labels (A General Economics and Teaching; B History of Economic Thought,
  Methodology, and Heterodox Approaches; C Mathematical and Quantitative Methods;
  D Microeconomics; E Macroeconomics and Monetary Economics; F International
  Economics; G Financial Economics; H Public Economics; I Health, Education, and
  Welfare; J Labor and Demographic Economics; K Law and Economics; L Industrial
  Organization; M Business Administration and Business Economics / Marketing /
  Accounting / Personnel Economics; N Economic History; O Economic Development,
  Innovation, Technological Change, and Growth; P Political Economy and Comparative
  Economic Systems; Q Agricultural and Natural Resource Economics / Environmental
  and Ecological Economics; R Urban, Rural, Regional, Real Estate, and Transportation
  Economics; Y Miscellaneous Categories; Z Other Special Topics). Accessed 2026-06.
- **https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Journal_of_Economic_Literature** — secondary
  background. Verifies (corroborating, not primary): JEL **founded 1969**; published
  by the AEA; content is survey/review articles, book reviews, and the JEL
  classification system; high citation impact. Used only to corroborate the founding
  year and content type; venue facts are anchored to the AEA pages above. Accessed
  2026-06.

## AEA-wide policies (shared across AEA journals; apply to JEL)

- **AEA Data and Code Availability Policy** (linked from the JEL submission page) —
  Verifies: empirical work, simulations, or experimental methods that an accepted
  article reports must be documented sufficiently for replication; the policy applies
  to JEL submissions that include original quantitative content. **NOTE:** a pure
  literature survey reports no new data of its own, so the bite is mainly on any
  original quantitative synthesis (e.g. a meta-analysis) the survey contributes.
  Accessed 2026-06.
- **AEA Disclosure Policy** (linked from the JEL submission page) — Verifies: the
  per-author Disclosure Statement requirement (financial interests, funding, and
  related-party relationships) and AEA house standards. Accessed 2026-06.

## Bounded live-check rules

- The JEL lead editor is **David H. Romer (UC Berkeley)** (appointed July 2022;
  re-verified 2026-06-22 via the AEA JEL editors page); correspondence goes to
  **d.romer@aeapubs.org**. Re-confirm the masthead before relying on the name/term.
- Do not assert a submission fee or fee waiver. The captured JEL submission text did
  not state one, and the 2026-06-22 web check surfaced no JEL submission-fee figure —
  leave fees unstated rather than guessing.
- Do not assert single- or double-blind review for JEL surveys unless the live AEA
  page states it.
- Use the 10-page proposal guidance as the pack's load-bearing length rule. Read any
  full-paper, abstract, and formatting limits from the current AEA/JEL Style Guide.
- Do not encode proposal-decision timelines as fixed policy unless the AEA page states
  a current number.
