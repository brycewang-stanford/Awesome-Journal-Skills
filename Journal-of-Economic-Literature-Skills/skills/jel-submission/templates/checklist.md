# JEL Pre-Submission Self-Check (8 sections)

A last-pass checklist for a *Journal of Economic Literature* (JEL) **survey/review** proposal or
full article before submitting via the AEA ScholarOne portal. Verify volatile specifics
(submission fee, editor/contact, length and anonymity rules, policy details) on the official
AEA JEL pages — 检索于 2026-06；以官网为准.

## 1. Venue fit (is this a JEL survey at all?)
- [ ] The artefact is a **survey/review of a literature**, not original empirical research
- [ ] The field passed the four JEL fit tests (maturity / importance / need-for-synthesis / tractable scope)
- [ ] It is not a JEP piece (short, non-technical) or a field-journal review (too narrow)

## 2. Route & intake
- [ ] Route chosen: **proposal (~10 pages excluding references)** or **full survey**
- [ ] If proposal: states the **contents**, **why readers will care**, and the **main references to be covered** (the three required elements)
- [ ] Considered a **pre-proposal email to the editor** to test interest (confirm editor contact on the AEA JEL page)

## 3. The survey's spine
- [ ] An explicit **organizing framework** (taxonomy / unifying model / question-sequence / paradigm / methods) — not an annotated bibliography
- [ ] The one-sentence **argument about the field** is stated up front
- [ ] Section architecture mirrors the framework's cells

## 4. Comprehensiveness & balance
- [ ] Coverage swept to **saturation**; no important author/school an informed referee could name as omitted
- [ ] Corpus tiered (foundational / important / confirmatory / tangential); emphasis matches tier
- [ ] Rival schools steelmanned; conflicting findings weighed by credibility + estimand (not vote-count)
- [ ] **Self-citation audited** — author's own work cited at field-warranted tier; emphasis is identity-blind

## 5. Front matter & codes
- [ ] **JEL classification codes** assigned, verified against the current EconLit list, and ordered by centrality
- [ ] Keywords and complete author affiliations present
- [ ] Abstract written in **survey style** (the survey's contribution to the reader), within any style-guide limit

## 6. Exhibits & accessibility
- [ ] Tables/figures **synthesize across studies** (no original-estimate regression output of the author's own)
- [ ] Who-found-what tables compare only **comparable estimands**; finding columns carry a credibility note
- [ ] A non-specialist economist can follow the manuscript start to finish (intuition-first exposition)

## 7. Declarations (incomplete without these)
- [ ] Separate **"Disclosure Statement"** PDF per (co)author (financial conflicts; IRB where applicable)
- [ ] AEA **Data and Code Availability Policy** obligations met for any original quantitative synthesis the survey reports
- [ ] Confirmed not under review elsewhere; AI is **not** listed as an author

## 8. Volatile re-confirms (检索于 2026-06；以官网为准)
- [ ] Submission **fee** (if any) re-checked on the AEA JEL page — not asserted from memory
- [ ] Editor, contact email, and ScholarOne portal specifics re-confirmed
- [ ] Length, abstract, anonymity, and style-guide formatting rules re-confirmed on the AEA/JEL Style Guide
