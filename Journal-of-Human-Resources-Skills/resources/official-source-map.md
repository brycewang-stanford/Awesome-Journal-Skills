# JHR Official Source Map

Primary and authoritative sources behind the *Journal of Human Resources* (JHR)
facts injected into this skill pack. **Access date for all entries: 2026-06-01.**
Re-verify volatile items (Editor, fees, page limits, policy wording) before relying
on them — journal pages change. Items that were not confirmed in an official
source are flagged **需复核** (to be verified) and are never asserted as fact in the skills.

## Official journal pages (UW Press / IRP)

- **https://jhr.uwpress.org/content/authors** — Authors / submission instructions. Verifies:
  - Submission via **http://jhr.msubmit.net**; authors must **NOT** send submissions directly to the editor, coeditors, managing editor, or editorial office.
  - **USD $175 nonrefundable submission fee** for all initial submissions; **no fee** for invited revisions; economic-hardship exemption may be requested; fee does **not** guarantee outside review and is **not refunded** for out-of-scope (management/personnel) papers.
  - Length is **page-based, not word-based**: **maximum 40 pages** including references, tables, and figures (12-point font, 1.5-line spacing for body text, 1-inch margins); **up to 50 pages** if double-spaced; footnotes may use smaller type and single spacing; an **Online Appendix** holds overflow (online-only) but should not be excessive.
  - **Per-author Disclosure Statement** required at submission (funding/support totaling >= USD $10,000 over 3 years, positions, relatives/partners, right-to-review, IRB status), placed up front before the body; failure to disclose can reverse acceptance or trigger retraction.
  - **Author-order randomization** via a superscript "r" on the title page; formal name-change-on-published-papers policy via **ORCID**.

- **https://journals.uwpress.wisc.edu/PDFs/JHR-Editorial-Policies.pdf** — Editorial Policies (dated September 2024). Verifies:
  - **Field focus**: empirical microeconomics — labor, development, health, education, discrimination, retirement; oriented toward research relevant to **public policy**; **will NOT consider management or personnel ("HR") research** despite the journal's name.
  - **Founded 1965**; published by **University of Wisconsin Press**; housed at the **UW-Madison Institute for Research on Poverty (IRP)**.
  - **Review model**: single-anonymous — "Referees are anonymous, but author names are not kept from reviewers"; managed by editor, coeditors, associate editors; **rapid desk-reject WITHOUT outside review** at the handling editor/coeditor's discretion for papers judged unlikely to survive.
  - **Reconciliation requirement**: manuscripts judged partly on whether they reconcile results with prior published work; authors may be required to run comparative estimation and present sensitivity tests.
  - **Review-recycling**: authors may submit a prior journal's decision letter and referee reports, which JHR may use (and may contact the prior journal's editor/referees) to speed review.
  - **Data & Replication policy** (effective Aug 1, 1989; latest revision **July 2022**): accepted manuscripts must preserve data and post it online in a well-curated **public repository** (examples: **ICPSR, the Dataverse Project, or an institutional repository**) in the **public domain under a CC0 1.0 Universal license**; an **archive plan** is required as a footnote at submission with a persistent (preferably **DOI**) link; complete replication materials required (data, code, models, other materials, plus a **read-me** file); a **Data Availability Statement** on the title page for all published papers; waiver requests must be made at initial submission and require (i) a method by which other researchers can obtain the data and (ii) author commitment to provide guidance; for **RCTs**, authors must state whether a **pre-analysis plan** exists, where it is registered, and any deviations.
  - **Submission fee stated as USD $150 in this Sept-2024 PDF** (see discrepancy note below).
  - **Page charges $60/page** apply to accepted papers **only if the author has grant income**; optional **gold open access (CC BY-NC-ND) costs USD $2,500**; preprints may be posted in institutional/funder repositories but **NOT on ResearchGate or Academia.edu** unless the paper is open access.

- **https://jhr.uwpress.org/content/editorial-board** — Editorial board. Verifies:
  - **Editor: Anna Aizer (Brown University)** as of 2026-06-01.
  - Coeditors include Abi Adams-Prassl, Aline Butikofer, Michael Darden, Jeffrey Denning, Taryn Dinkelman, Josh Goodman, Andrew Goodman-Bacon, Lena Hensvik, Ethan Lewis, Michael Lovenheim, Isaac Mbiti, Matthew Neidell, Maya Rossin-Slater, Judith Scott-Clayton, Emilia Simeonova, Kosali Simon, Sergio Urzua, Tara Watson, Junsen Zhang, Ben Zou.

- **https://uwpress.wisc.edu/Journals/Journal-of-Human-Resources** — UW Press journal page. Verifies:
  - Publisher = University of Wisconsin Press; **ISSN 0022-166X (print) / e-ISSN 1548-8004**; scope "among the leading journals in empirical microeconomics"; Editor Anna Aizer.

- **Manuscript citation/reference style — 需复核.** Official pages specify physical formatting (font size, spacing, margins, page count) and a data-citation format, but do **not** name a citation/reference style (e.g., Chicago/APA) for the manuscript body. ## Notes on scope discipline

- JHR's **name is misleading**: it is an **empirical microeconomics** journal and explicitly **does not** publish management or personnel ("HR management") research. The non-refundable fee is **not** returned for out-of-scope submissions. This is the single most consequential fit check in the pack and is sourced from the Editorial Policies PDF and Authors page above.
