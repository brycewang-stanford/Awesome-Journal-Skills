## Verified 2026-cycle facts used in the skills

- STOC 2026 was the 58th ACM Symposium on Theory of Computing, held June 22-26,
  2026 in Salt Lake City, Utah, inside an expanded TheoryFest week (sources 1, 6).
- Submissions were due November 4, 2025, 4:59pm EST on HotCRP, with decisions by
  February 1, 2026 and camera-ready March 31, 2026 AoE (sources 2, 3).
- The reading rule: no length bound; the abstract, table of contents, and first
  twelve pages are read, the remainder at the committee's discretion (source 2).
- Submission format: 11-point or larger, single-column, single-spaced with ample
  spacing, 1-inch margins, letter-size paper (source 2).
- STOC 2026 used double-blind reviewing (source 2).
- SIGACT prior/simultaneous-publication policy applied, with the Science/Nature
  exception; arXiv/ECCC posting does not count as prior publication (source 2).
- Accepted authors were expected to post full versions with proofs on arXiv,
  ECCC, or similar by the camera-ready deadline (source 2).
- The PC chair was Artur Czumaj, University of Warwick (source 2).
- The opt-in LLM pre-submission feedback experiment ran with a November 1, 2025,
  5pm EST upload cutoff and authors-only visibility (sources 4, 12).
- Prize structure: up to four STOC Best Papers; Danny Lewin Best Student Paper
  for all-student papers; Knuth and Gödel Prizes administered through SIGACT
  (sources 7-10).

## Organizers and fee model

STOC has no standing editor-in-chief; the rotating analogue is the per-edition
program-committee chair and local organizers appointed by ACM SIGACT (2026: PC
chair Artur Czumaj — one-cycle fact). There is no article-processing charge:
proceedings publication in the ACM Digital Library is funded through the
conference, and authors' direct costs are registration and travel. The public
full version on arXiv/ECCC costs nothing.
