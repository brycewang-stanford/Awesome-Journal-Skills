# AJS Official Source Map

Primary sources behind the American Journal of Sociology facts used in this skill pack.
Access date for this refresh: **2026-06-20**. UChicago Press journal pages may require
browser verification, so authors should live-check the official pages in a browser before
submitting or quoting operational details.

## Official Journal, Submission, and Author Pages

- **https://www.journals.uchicago.edu/journals/ajs** — AJS journal home at University of
  Chicago Press. Use as the first route to current issues, journal navigation, and the
  current publisher-hosted instructions.
- **https://www.journals.uchicago.edu/journals/ajs/instruct** — AJS Instructions for
  Authors. Public official snippets verify submission via **Editorial Manager**; every
  submission must include an abstract, reference list, and any notes, tables, or figures;
  abstracts are targeted near **150 words** and papers without abstracts are not sent to
  readers; manuscript text is double-spaced; Editorial Manager accepts Word, PDF, and
  LaTeX; a cover page is submitted separately from the manuscript so personal information
  stays out of the manuscript file; alt text is required for figures/images; concurrent
  submission violates Press ethics; overlapping prior/pending work requires an originality
  statement; and authors should prepare the manuscript without identifying information.
- **https://www.journals.uchicago.edu/journals/ajs/submission-fee** — AJS Submission Fee.
  Public official snippets verify a **$30** submission fee for new submissions and a
  graduate-student sole-author waiver, with proof of status potentially requested.
- **https://www.journals.uchicago.edu/journals/ajs/prep-gen** — Manuscript Preparation
  (General). Public official snippets verify AJS's author-date citation system; "et al."
  for three or more authors; the cover-sheet acknowledgment note marked with an asterisk;
  substantive notes starting at "1"; footnotes or endnotes accepted; tables numbered
  consecutively and discretely, with appendix tables labeled A1/A2 or B1/B2; and notes,
  references, tables, figures, and appendices placed in separate post-text sections.
- **https://www.journals.uchicago.edu/pb-assets/docs/journals/ajs-accepted-publication-formatting-1692887363133.pdf**
  — AJS Formatting Guidelines for Publication. Use for accepted-manuscript house-style
  details and final production cleanup.
- **https://www.editorialmanager.com/ucp-ajs** — AJS submission portal on Editorial
  Manager.
- **https://www.journals.uchicago.edu/journals/ajs/further-guides-for-submission** —
  Further guides for submission and any updated supplementary author guidance.

## Editorial Policy and Review Process

- **https://www.journals.uchicago.edu/journals/ajs/editorial** — AJS Editorial Policy.
  Public official snippets verify **double-blind** review for papers deemed appropriate
  for AJS; reviewer identities and author identities are not disclosed to the other side;
  AJS avoids sending papers to people in the submitting author's network; the article side
  has three levels of editorship; the editorial board is the faculty of the University of
  Chicago Department of Sociology; and first-submission reviewer assignment is handled by
  a Manuscript Assignment Board of Assistant Editors supervised by graduate-student
  Associate Editors.
- **https://www.journals.uchicago.edu/journals/ajs/how-does-the-ajs-work** — AJS process
  explanation. Public official snippets verify that assignment editors study blinded
  manuscripts to determine substantive area and method, use references to locate reviewer
  pools without limiting reviewers to cited authors, and **preject** some papers that are
  not in dialogue with current sociology, are opinion pieces, or are current-event
  interpretations without original sociological research.
- **https://www.journals.uchicago.edu/journals/ajs/how-to-review** — AJS reviewer guidance.
  Use to anticipate how reviewers are asked to assess theoretical contribution, evidence,
  and dialogue with sociology.
