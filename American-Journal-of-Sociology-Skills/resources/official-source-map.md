# AJS Official Source Map

Primary and authoritative sources behind the AJS-specific facts injected into this skill pack.
Access date for all entries: **2026-06-01**. Re-verify volatile items (current editor and term, the
submission-fee amount and waiver, abstract/word expectations, citation-style details, data policy) on

## Official journal, submission & author pages (University of Chicago Press)

- **https://www.journals.uchicago.edu/journals/ajs/instruct** — AJS Instructions for Authors.
  Verifies (via search rendering; page is 403 to automated fetch): submission via the **Editorial
  Manager** portal; **every submission must include an abstract, a reference list, and any notes,
  tables, or figures**; **abstracts limited to ~150 words** (as close to 150 as possible; should
  state the research question/puzzle, identify the data, and indicate findings; **papers without an
  abstract are not sent to readers**); manuscripts in a **serif typeface (e.g., Times Roman) no
  smaller than 11 pt (preferably 12), double-spaced, ≥1-inch margins**; **Editorial Manager accepts
  Word, PDF, and LaTeX**; a **cover page must be submitted separately** from the manuscript so
  personal information is not in the manuscript file; **alt text required for all figures/images**
  at submission; **concurrent submission to more than one journal violates the Press's ethical
  standards** and will be rejected; if a paper overlaps prior published/under-review work, prepare a
  brief **originality statement**; prepare the manuscript **without identifying information** and
  word self-references carefully.
- **https://www.journals.uchicago.edu/journals/ajs/submission-fee** — AJS Submission Fee. Verifies
  (via search rendering): new submissions require a **$30 submission fee**; **graduate students
  submitting as sole authors are not required to pay**, but **proof of status may be requested**.
- **https://www.journals.uchicago.edu/journals/ajs/prep-gen** — Manuscript Preparation (General).
  Verifies (via search rendering): **author-date in-text citation** (name + year in parentheses;
  **"et al." for three or more authors**); acknowledgment note on the cover sheet marked with an
  asterisk, **substantive notes start at "1"**; **footnotes or endnotes both acceptable**; tables
  numbered **consecutively and discretely** (table 1, 2, 3 …), appendix tables **A1, A2 / B1, B2**;
  **notes, references, tables, figures, and appendices appear in separate sections after the text, in
  that order**.
- **https://www.journals.uchicago.edu/pb-assets/docs/journals/ajs-accepted-publication-formatting-1692887363133.pdf**
  — AJS Formatting Guidelines for Publication (PDF). Detailed house-style formatting for accepted
  manuscripts.
- **https://www.editorialmanager.com/ucp-ajs** — AJS submission portal on **Editorial Manager**.
- **https://www.journals.uchicago.edu/journals/ajs/further-guides-for-submission** — Further guides
  for submission (supplementary author guidance).

## Editorial policy & review process

- **https://www.journals.uchicago.edu/journals/ajs/editorial** — AJS Editorial Policy. Verifies (via
  search rendering): papers deemed appropriate are reviewed under a **double-blind system** (identity
  of participants not disclosed to authors or referees); AJS **will not knowingly send papers to
  persons within the submitting author's network** (current colleagues, coauthors, PhD cohorts, etc.);
  the article side has **three levels of editorship**, including an **editorial board consisting of
  the entire faculty of the Department of Sociology at the University of Chicago**; on first
  submission, reviewer assignment is done by a **Manuscript Assignment Board** of **Assistant Editors
  supervised by graduate-student Associate Editors**.
- **https://www.journals.uchicago.edu/journals/ajs/how-does-the-ajs-work** — How does the AJS work.
  Verifies (via search rendering): assignment editors (graduate students in the Department) study
  **blinded** versions of the manuscript to determine substantive area and method, then locate
  reviewers (using references to find a piece but **not restricting reviewers to those cited**); AJS
  **"prejects"** some papers without review — those **not in dialogue with current sociology**,
  opinion pieces, or interpretations of current events that are **not original sociological
  research**; prejects are made by editorial associates or the Manuscript Assignment Board, consulting
  the editor when unsure.
- **https://www.journals.uchicago.edu/journals/ajs/how-to-review** — How to review for the AJS
  (reviewer guidance; useful for anticipating reviewer criteria).

## Owner, publisher, editor, history

- **https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_Journal_of_Sociology** — Verifies: AJS **founded 1895** by
  **Albion Small** (editor 1895–1926); **first journal in its discipline** (oldest US sociology
  journal); published by the **University of Chicago Press** for the **Department of Sociology at the
  University of Chicago**, where it has remained **for its entire history**; **bimonthly** (six issues
  per year); publishes original research and book reviews in sociology and related social sciences;
  early emphasis on **social theory and social psychology**, with statistical work prominent from the
  1920s; ranks among the discipline's top journals alongside the American Sociological Review; the
  **Roger V. Gould Prize** (est. 2002) recognizes papers combining clarity, rigor, scientific
  ambition, and empirical rigor; current editor listed as **John Levi Martin** (2022–present).
- **https://www.journals.uchicago.edu/toc/ajs/current** — AJS current issue on the UChicago Press
  site (masthead, current Comment/Reply and book-review content).
