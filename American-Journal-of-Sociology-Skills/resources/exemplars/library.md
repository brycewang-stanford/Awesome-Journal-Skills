# AJS Exemplars Library (method × topic)

> **Verified via web search, access date 2026-06-08.** Every paper below was checked to confirm it
> actually appeared in the **American Journal of Sociology** — the discipline's oldest journal, founded
> 1895, published by the **University of Chicago Press** for the Department of Sociology at the University
> of Chicago — with author, year, and volume/issue, against the UChicago Press article page
> (`journals.uchicago.edu/doi/...`, DOI prefix `10.1086/...`), JSTOR, and corroborating Google Scholar
> lookups carrying the journal name. Papers that lacked full AJS confirmation were **omitted** —
> this is deliberately a short, clean, verified list rather than a long, uncertain one.
>
> **Sibling-journal guard.** AJS is **not** the *American Sociological Review* (ASR, ASA/SAGE), *Social
> Forces* (OUP/UNC), the *Annual Review of Sociology*, *Social Psychology Quarterly*, or *Social
> Problems*. Several papers people assume are "AJS classics" are actually in those venues; the
> [Omitted](#omitted-for-sibling-journal--verification-reasons-do-not-attribute-to-ajs-without-re-checking)
> section records the traps checked and rejected. (Note the reverse trap too: Granovetter's "Strength of
> Weak Ties" *is* AJS — it was rejected by ASR first — so people misremember it as ASR.)
>
> **Use principle (zero hallucination):** this file gives **design positioning only**. It does **not**
> reproduce or invent coefficients, effect sizes, or specific findings — read the original on UChicago
> Press / JSTOR before citing any number. For AJS-specific scope, the "preject" policy, abstract bound,
> and house citation style, see [`../official-source-map.md`](../official-source-map.md).

## How to use this library

Pick the row whose **method × question** is closest to yours, then study how that paper raises a credible
finding into a **portable, discipline-level theoretical contribution** — the AJS bar, which prizes
theoretical ambition and dialogue with current sociology over a bare result (see
[`../../skills/ajs-topic-selection/SKILL.md`](../../skills/ajs-topic-selection/SKILL.md),
[`../../skills/ajs-theory-building/SKILL.md`](../../skills/ajs-theory-building/SKILL.md), and
[`../../skills/ajs-writing-style/SKILL.md`](../../skills/ajs-writing-style/SKILL.md)). For each, ask the
self-check before claiming your paper is "AJS-shaped." Model the introduction on
[`../worked-examples/01-introduction.md`](../worked-examples/01-introduction.md).

## By method

### Field / audit experiment on discrimination (causal applied sociology)

- **Pager, "The Mark of a Criminal Record," AJS 2003, 108(5):937–975.**
  Verified: UChicago Press `journals.uchicago.edu/doi/abs/10.1086/374403`; JSTOR `10.1086/374403`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a matched-pair audit design turns a contested causal question (does a
    criminal record itself block hiring, and how does it interact with race?) into a credibly exogenous
    test, then attaches it to a **general mechanism of stratification and labor-market closure** — local
    design, disciplinary payoff. The [`ajs-theory-building`](../../skills/ajs-theory-building/SKILL.md)
    move of naming a portable mechanism behind a clean experimental contrast.
  - **Self-check:** does your experiment produce variation a referee accepts as exogenous *and* speak to
    a mechanism beyond the specific labor market?

### Mixed-method ethnography + quantitative description (urban poverty)

- **Desmond, "Eviction and the Reproduction of Urban Poverty," AJS 2012, 118(1):88–133.**
  Verified: UChicago Press `journals.uchicago.edu/doi/abs/10.1086/666082` (DOI `10.1086/666082`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** pairs statistical patterning of eviction with ethnographic fieldwork to
    name a **mechanism of poverty reproduction**, framing eviction as a cause rather than a symptom of
    disadvantage — exactly the [`ajs-theory-building`](../../skills/ajs-theory-building/SKILL.md)
    requirement that ethnography deliver a portable concept ("say what the case is a case *of*"), not
    thick description alone.
  - **Self-check:** does your qualitative work surface a mechanism the discipline can carry elsewhere,
    triangulated against the quantitative pattern rather than illustrating it?

### Heterogeneous selection in a stratification outcome (quantitative / demographic)

- **Western & Pettit, "Black-White Wage Inequality, Employment Rates, and Incarceration," AJS 2005, 111(2):553–578.**
  Verified: UChicago Press `journals.uchicago.edu/doi/abs/10.1086/432780` (DOI `10.1086/432780`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** shows that a familiar quantity (the racial wage gap) is **biased by who is
    selected out of work** — incarceration and joblessness — and corrects for it, so the empirics
    *revise a number the field thought it knew* rather than add a coefficient. A model of the
    [`ajs-research-design`](../../skills/ajs-research-design/SKILL.md) selection-adjustment logic with an
    explicit theoretical stake.
  - **Self-check:** does your estimate confront the selection process that generates the observed sample,
    and does the correction change the *interpretation*, not just the point estimate?

### Dual-process model of culture tested in panel data (culture / quantitative)

- **Vaisey, "Motivation and Justification: A Dual-Process Model of Culture in Action," AJS 2009, 114(6):1675–1715.**
  Verified: UChicago Press `journals.uchicago.edu/doi/abs/10.1086/597179` (DOI `10.1086/597179`); Google
  Scholar (AJS 114:1675–1715).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** proposes an explicit **concept** (discursive vs. practical culture) and
    derives an observable implication — that a quick moral-script choice predicts later behavior better
    than articulated principles — then tests it on panel data. The
    [`ajs-theory-building`](../../skills/ajs-theory-building/SKILL.md) ideal: a portable theoretical move
    with stated observable implications, not a regression presented *as* the contribution.
  - **Self-check:** does your paper offer a concept other sociologists can import, with predictions that
    distinguish it from the rival account, rather than report an association?

### Structural analysis of a complete network (network / structural)

- **Bearman, Moody & Stovel, "Chains of Affection: The Structure of Adolescent Romantic and Sexual Networks," AJS 2004, 110(1):44–91.**
  Verified: UChicago Press `journals.uchicago.edu/doi/abs/10.1086/386272` (DOI `10.1086/386272`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** maps a near-complete romantic/sexual network and shows its **structural
    signature** (long chains, few short cycles) is generated by simple local prohibitions — a network
    description raised to a general account of how micro-rules produce macro-structure, the
    [`ajs-theory-building`](../../skills/ajs-theory-building/SKILL.md) "connect structure to social
    theory, not method for its own sake" move.
  - **Self-check:** does your network result deliver a structural mechanism with substantive meaning,
    rather than a descriptive metric?

### Comparative-historical analysis of institutional change (comparative-historical)

- **Clemens, "Organizational Repertoires and Institutional Change: Women's Groups and the Transformation of American Politics, 1890–1920," AJS 1993, 98(4):755–798.**
  Verified: UChicago Press `journals.uchicago.edu/doi/abs/10.1086/230089` (DOI `10.1086/230089`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** introduces the portable concept of **organizational repertoires** to
    explain how excluded groups produce institutional change, building the argument through archival
    historical evidence with an explicit causal logic rather than a chronology — the
    [`ajs-research-design`](../../skills/ajs-research-design/SKILL.md) comparative-historical track done
    with a stated theoretical class the cases instantiate.
  - **Self-check:** does your historical comparison extract a portable logic (the "repertoire" here) that
    travels beyond the cases, with the causal form specified?

## Omitted for sibling-journal / verification reasons (do not attribute to AJS without re-checking)

To keep the list zero-hallucination, these widely-cited sociology papers were **excluded** — each was
verified to be in a **sibling journal**, not AJS:

- **DiMaggio & Powell, "The Iron Cage Revisited" (1983)** — verified in the **American Sociological
  Review** 48(2):147–160, **not AJS**. The canonical institutional-isomorphism paper.
- **Gould, "Multiple Networks and Mobilization in the Paris Commune, 1871" (1991)** — verified in the
  **American Sociological Review** 56(6):716–729, **not AJS**. (Gould's later book is UChicago Press, but
  this article is ASR.)
- **McPherson, Smith-Lovin & Cook, "Birds of a Feather: Homophily in Social Networks" (2001)** — verified
  in the **Annual Review of Sociology** 27:415–444, **not AJS**.
- **Pager, Western & Bonikowski, "Discrimination in a Low-Wage Labor Market: A Field Experiment" (2009)**
  — verified in the **American Sociological Review** 74(5):777–799, **not AJS**. (Pager's *single-authored*
  "Mark of a Criminal Record" *is* AJS — listed above; the co-authored field experiment is ASR.)
- **Reverse trap — keep, don't omit:** **Granovetter, "The Strength of Weak Ties" (1973)** **is AJS**
  78(6):1360–1380, even though it was first submitted to (and rejected by) ASR, which is why many
  misremember it as an ASR paper.

Before adding any new paper to this library, confirm it on `journals.uchicago.edu/doi/...` (AJS article
page with volume/issue, DOI prefix `10.1086/`) or JSTOR, update the access-date header, and watch the
ASR / Social Forces / Annual Review / Social Psychology Quarterly / Social Problems traps. **When in
doubt, omit.**
