# *Journal of Personality and Social Psychology* Exemplars Library (method × topic)

> **Verified via web search, access date 2026-06-08.** Every paper below was checked to confirm it
> actually appeared in **_Journal of Personality and Social Psychology_** (JPSP) — published by the
> **American Psychological Association (APA)**, established 1965, divided into three independently edited
> sections (**Attitudes and Social Cognition (ASC)**, **Interpersonal Relations and Group Processes
> (IRGP)**, **Personality Processes and Individual Differences (PPID)**) — with year and volume(issue):pages,
> against the APA record (`psycnet.apa.org` / `doi.org/10.1037/...`), the authors' archived PDFs, and
> corroborating indexes (PubMed, Google Scholar lookups carrying the journal name). Papers that could not
> be fully verified **as JPSP** were **omitted** — this is deliberately a short, clean list (5 verified),
> not a long, uncertain one.
>
> **Sibling-journal guard.** JPSP is **not** *Journal of Experimental Social Psychology* (JESP),
> *Personality and Social Psychology Bulletin* (PSPB), *Journal of Research in Personality* (JRP),
> *Journal of Personality*, *Journal of Personality Assessment*, *Psychological Science*, or *Science*.
> Several famous "personality / social psychology" papers — and several iconic measurement scales — live
> in those venues; the [Omitted](#omitted-for-lack-of-full-verification-do-not-attribute-to-jpsp) section
> records the traps checked and rejected.
>
> **Use principle (zero hallucination):** this file gives **design positioning only**. It does not
> reproduce or invent effect sizes, sample sizes, or specific findings — read the original on APA PsycNet
> before citing any number. For JPSP-specific scope, per-section word/study caps, masking, JARS, and TOP
> policy, see [`../official-source-map.md`](../official-source-map.md).

## How to use this library

Pick the row whose **method × question** is closest to yours, then study how that paper turns a finding
into a **theoretically innovative, multi-study** contribution — JPSP's first gate is **theoretical
innovation**, and the house format is a lengthy theory-driven introduction followed by **several related
studies** that test a theory or adjudicate competing hypotheses (see
[`../../skills/jpsp-theory-and-hypotheses/SKILL.md`](../../skills/jpsp-theory-and-hypotheses/SKILL.md),
[`../../skills/jpsp-study-design/SKILL.md`](../../skills/jpsp-study-design/SKILL.md), and
[`../../skills/jpsp-writing-style/SKILL.md`](../../skills/jpsp-writing-style/SKILL.md)). For each, ask the
self-check question before claiming your paper is "JPSP-shaped."

## By method

### New measure of an individual-difference construct (scale development + validation, PPID)

- **Carver & White, "Behavioral Inhibition, Behavioral Activation, and Affective Responses to Impending
  Reward and Punishment: The BIS/BAS Scales," _Journal of Personality and Social Psychology_ 1994,
  67(2):319–333.**
  Verified: APA `doi.org/10.1037/0022-3514.67.2.319`; author PDF (faculty.washington.edu lookup carries
  "Journal of Personality and Social Psychology 1994, Vol. 67, No. 2, 319–333").
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** develops a self-report measure of two dispositional motivational systems
    in one study, then establishes convergent and discriminant validity (correlations with affective
    responses) in a second — the
    [`jpsp-theory-and-hypotheses`](../../skills/jpsp-theory-and-hypotheses/SKILL.md) "introduce and
    validate a **new construct** and show it matters" move, executed across linked studies.
  - **Self-check:** does your scale predict something a competing measure does not, with validity evidence
    that goes beyond internal reliability?

### Measurement of implicit cognition (paradigm-defining lab procedure, ASC)

- **Greenwald, McGhee & Schwartz, "Measuring Individual Differences in Implicit Cognition: The Implicit
  Association Test," _Journal of Personality and Social Psychology_ 1998, 74(6):1464–1480.**
  Verified: APA `doi.org/10.1037/0022-3514.74.6.1464`; author PDF (faculty.washington.edu/agg lookup
  carries "Journal of Personality and Social Psychology 1998, Vol. 74, No. 6, 1464–1480"); PubMed.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** introduces a reaction-time procedure (the IAT) and demonstrates it across
    successive studies on different attitude objects — a **new method that operationalizes a previously
    hard-to-measure construct**, exactly the measurement contribution
    [`jpsp-study-design`](../../skills/jpsp-study-design/SKILL.md) and
    [`jpsp-data-analysis`](../../skills/jpsp-data-analysis/SKILL.md) treat as load-bearing when the
    procedure *is* the contribution.
  - **Self-check:** if your contribution is a paradigm, do successive studies show it captures something
    self-report cannot, rather than re-demonstrating one effect?

### Multi-study causal experiment adjudicating a mechanism (social cognition, ASC)

- **Steele & Aronson, "Stereotype Threat and the Intellectual Test Performance of African Americans,"
  _Journal of Personality and Social Psychology_ 1995, 69(5):797–811.**
  Verified: APA `doi.org/10.1037/0022-3514.69.5.797`; NYU Scholars record; Stanford SPARQ archived PDF
  (journal name on page 1).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a package of experiments manipulates the diagnosticity framing of a test
    and rules out alternative accounts study by study, so a single theoretical construct ("stereotype
    threat") is *built* and defended rather than asserted — the
    [`jpsp-theory-and-hypotheses`](../../skills/jpsp-theory-and-hypotheses/SKILL.md) "each hypothesis is
    one a competing theory would not predict" logic across a cumulative study set.
  - **Self-check:** does each study in your package test a distinct, diagnostic derivation, pre-empting
    the salient alternative explanation rather than re-running the first demonstration?

### Experiments + diary/dyadic data on a relationship process (IRGP)

- **Gable, Reis, Impett & Asher, "What Do You Do When Things Go Right? The Intrapersonal and Interpersonal
  Benefits of Sharing Positive Events," _Journal of Personality and Social Psychology_ 2004,
  87(2):228–245.**
  Verified: APA `doi.org/10.1037/0022-3514.87.2.228`; multiple reference indexes carry "Journal of
  Personality and Social Psychology, 87(2), 228–245."
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** combines experiments with daily-diary and dyadic designs to establish that
    *capitalization* (sharing good news) carries intra- and interpersonal benefits — a model of the
    IRGP "prioritize integrative analyses across data sets" norm and the
    [`jpsp-study-design`](../../skills/jpsp-study-design/SKILL.md) multi-method package, where each method
    buys inferential value (causal control + ecological validity + partner effects).
  - **Self-check:** does each additional design (experiment, diary, dyadic) add an inference the others
    cannot, in line with this pack's multi-study logic — rather than repeat one demonstration?

### Longitudinal predictive validity of an early measure (PPID)

- **Mischel, Shoda & Peake, "The Nature of Adolescent Competencies Predicted by Preschool Delay of
  Gratification," _Journal of Personality and Social Psychology_ 1988, 54(4):687–696.**
  Verified: APA `doi.org/10.1037/0022-3514.54.4.687`; PubMed PMID 3367285.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** links a behavioral measure taken in preschool to ratings obtained a decade
    later — a **longitudinal predictive-validity** contribution that shows an individual difference *has
    lasting consequences*, the kind of cumulative, theory-relevant evidence
    [`jpsp-data-analysis`](../../skills/jpsp-data-analysis/SKILL.md) expects (report the prospective
    association and its uncertainty, not a one-shot correlation).
  - **Self-check:** does your longitudinal design connect an early construct to a consequential later
    outcome, with the temporal ordering doing real inferential work?

## Omitted for lack of full verification (do not attribute to JPSP without re-checking)

To keep the list zero-hallucination, these strong candidates were **excluded** after web-checking — each
is a real, important paper, but **not in JPSP** (the sibling-journal traps the brief warned of):

- **Tangney, Baumeister & Boone, "High Self-Control Predicts Good Adjustment…" (2004)** — verified in
  **_Journal of Personality_** 72(2):271–324, **not JPSP**. The widely used (Brief) Self-Control Scale.
- **Gosling, Rentfrow & Swann, "A Very Brief Measure of the Big-Five Personality Domains" (2003)** —
  verified in **_Journal of Research in Personality_** 37(6):504–528, **not JPSP**. The TIPI.
- **Diener, Emmons, Larsen & Griffin, "The Satisfaction With Life Scale" (1985)** — verified in
  **_Journal of Personality Assessment_** 49(1):71–75, **not JPSP**.
- **Cuddy, Fiske & Glick, "Warmth and Competence as Universal Dimensions of Social Perception: The
  Stereotype Content Model and the BIAS Map" (2008)** — verified in **_Advances in Experimental Social
  Psychology_** vol. 40:61–149 (a review serial), **not JPSP**. (The original SCM paper, Fiske, Cuddy,
  Glick & Xu 2002, *is* in JPSP — cite that one if you need the JPSP source.)
- **Williams & Bargh, "Experiencing Physical Warmth Promotes Interpersonal Warmth" (2008)** — verified in
  **_Science_** 322:606–607, **not JPSP**.
- **Bandura, Ross & Ross, "Transmission of Aggression Through Imitation of Aggressive Models" (1961)** —
  verified in the **_Journal of Abnormal and Social Psychology_** 63:575–582, the **pre-1965 precursor**
  that split into JPSP and JASP, **not JPSP itself**.

**omit**. If web verification is unavailable, mark new rows **待核实 / TO VERIFY** and attach **no**
journal attribution until checked.
