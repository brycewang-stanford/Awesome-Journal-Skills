# JPSP Official Source Map

Primary and authoritative sources behind the *Journal of Personality and Social Psychology* (JPSP)
facts injected into this skill pack. Access date for all entries: **2026-06-01**. Re-verify volatile
items (current section editors and term, exact word/abstract caps, study caps, fee/APC, TOP-level and
data-policy wording, accepted manuscript types) on the official page before relying on them — APA
journal pages and section editorial teams change. JPSP's three sections are **independently edited**
and each section periodically issues its **own** revised submission guidelines, so a rule verified for
one section may not hold for another.

## Official journal & per-section submission pages (APA)

- **https://www.apa.org/pubs/journals/psp** — JPSP journal home + Submission Guidelines (APA).
  Verifies: publisher = **American Psychological Association (APA)**; the journal is split into
  **three independently edited sections**, each with its own editor and **Editorial Manager**
  submission portal — **Attitudes and Social Cognition (ASC)**, **Interpersonal Relations and Group
  Processes (IRGP)**, **Personality Processes and Individual Differences (PPID)**; **masked review**
  ("policy of masked review for all submissions" — both the authors' and reviewers' identities are
  not known to the other); **abstract maximum of 250 words**; ASC limits the **introduction +
  discussion (introduction, any study introductions, Discussion, and General Discussion) to no more
  than 3,500 words**; IRGP limits the **introduction + discussion to no more than 5,000 words in
  total**; tables and figures are **embedded in the manuscript text** and information in tables does
  not count toward the word limit; authors must adhere to the **Journal Article Reporting Standards
  (JARS)**; APA Style follows the **Publication Manual (7th edition)**; **Transparency and Openness
  Promotion (TOP) Guidelines at Level 2 (Requirement)** effective **July 1, 2021**; articles must
  **state whether data, code, and study materials are posted to a trusted repository** and **whether
  the work was preregistered**; JPSP **publishes Registered Reports**; **open-science badges are not
  offered**; APA expects authors to keep data available for **at least 5 years after publication**.
- **https://www.editorialmanager.com/asc/default1.aspx** — ASC submission portal (Editorial Manager).
- **https://www.editorialmanager.com/irg/default1.aspx** — IRGP submission portal (Editorial Manager).
- **https://www.editorialmanager.com/pid/default1.aspx** — PPID submission portal (Editorial Manager).
- **https://www.apa.org/pubs/journals/psp/sample** — JPSP sample articles (per-section examples of the
  long-format, multi-study house style).

## Section editorial guidelines & vision

- **https://spsp.org/sites/default/files/2023-12/New_JPSP_IRGP_Submission_Guidelines.pdf** — "Announcing
  New Submission Guidelines for JPSP: Interpersonal Relations and Group Processes" (incoming editor
  Sandra L. Murray, effective **January 2024**). Verifies for **IRGP**: submissions can report a
  **maximum of 5 studies** in the manuscript text (studies not reported in the main text go in
  supplemental materials with results summarized briefly); **introductory and discussion sections are
  limited to no more than 5,000 words in total** (including general and study-specific introductions
  and discussions); results sections written for **readers with general statistical expertise**,
  relegating complex justification to tables/notes/supplements; authors are to **prioritize
  integrative analyses across data sets and/or meta-analyses whenever possible** (i.e., internal
  meta-analysis); authors must include a **focused discussion of salient alternative explanations**;
  tables/figures **embedded** and not counted toward the word limit; supplements must include a
  **table of contents**. Review model: papers limited in theoretical scope or methodologically
  deficient are **triaged**; reviewers first judge theoretical **innovation** (a "yes/probably yes"
  vs. "no" gate); limitations are categorized as **central** ("must" address for interpretability)
  vs. **peripheral** ("could" address), and central limitations as addressable with **existing**
  vs. **new** studies/data; the section will extend **"accept with revision"** invitations on the
  first round when a paper is sufficiently innovative with no central limitations, **revise and
  resubmit** when central limitations are addressable with existing studies/data, and **reject**
  papers that need new studies/data to fix central limitations (a resubmission with new data is
  treated as a **new** submission); revised manuscripts are often decided **without further reviews**.

## Owner, publisher, history, editors

- **https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Journal_of_Personality_and_Social_Psychology** — Verifies: JPSP was
  **established in 1965**, published **monthly** by the **American Psychological Association**; it is
  divided into **three independently edited sections** (ASC — domains of social behavior in which
  cognition plays a major role; IRGP — psychological and structural features of interaction in dyads
  and groups; PPID — all aspects of personality psychology); **2024 impact factor 6.7**; the
  house format is **a lengthy introduction and literature review followed by several related studies
  that explore different aspects of a theory or test multiple competing hypotheses** (the long-format,
  multi-study norm). The journal's most-cited paper is a methods piece on mediation/moderation cited
  90,000+ times.
- **https://www.annenbergpublicpolicycenter.org/dolores-albarracin-named-editor-of-journal-of-personality-and-social-psychology/**
  — Verifies: **Dolores Albarracín (University of Pennsylvania)** named editor of the **Attitudes and
  Social Cognition** section.

## Reporting standards & open science (APA-wide, applied by JPSP)

- **https://apastyle.apa.org/jars** — APA **Journal Article Reporting Standards (JARS)**: JARS-Quant
  (quantitative), JARS-Qual (qualitative), and JARS-Mixed; tables/checklists for experimental,
  non-experimental, replication, and meta-analytic designs. JPSP requires JARS compliance.
- **https://www.apa.org/pubs/journals/resources/publishing-tips/openness-promotion** — APA on the
  **TOP Guidelines** and how APA journals (including JPSP at **Level 2**) implement data, code,
  materials, design, analysis, preregistration, and replication standards.
- **https://www.apa.org/pubs/journals/resources/data-publishing-policy** — APA data-sharing /
  availability expectations (data available throughout review and for **at least 5 years** after
  publication).
