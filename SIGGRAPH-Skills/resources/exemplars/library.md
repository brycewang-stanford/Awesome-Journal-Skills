> **dblp** and the **ACM Digital Library** to confirm it appeared at **ACM SIGGRAPH** (published in
> the SIGGRAPH proceedings or, since the TOG integration, the SIGGRAPH issue of **ACM Transactions
> on Graphics**) — matching title, authors, year, and venue string. Papers that could not be
> cleanly confirmed as SIGGRAPH (as opposed to SIGGRAPH Asia, Eurographics/EGSR, SGP, SCA, UIST,

> techniques debuted at those venues; a famous author or a familiar method name does **not** prove
> a SIGGRAPH placement. Each row was checked to be a SIGGRAPH edition specifically (see omissions).

> **Use principle (zero hallucination):** this file gives **design positioning only**. It does not

## How to use this library

Pick the row whose **contribution shape × method** is closest to yours, then study how that paper
poses a **graphics problem an expert recognizes**, backs it with **results shown (figures + video)
and honest comparisons**, and moves a **capability axis** (quality, speed, generality, robustness)
— the SIGGRAPH bar (see [`../../skills/siggraph-writing-style/SKILL.md`](../../skills/siggraph-writing-style/SKILL.md),
[`../../skills/siggraph-experiments/SKILL.md`](../../skills/siggraph-experiments/SKILL.md), and
[`../../skills/siggraph-topic-selection/SKILL.md`](../../skills/siggraph-topic-selection/SKILL.md)).
For each, ask the self-check question before claiming your paper is "SIGGRAPH-shaped." Several rows
are decade-defining works that also model what "influence at this venue" looks like.

## By contribution shape

### Rendering technique — Monte Carlo light transport

- **Veach & Guibas, "Metropolis Light Transport," SIGGRAPH 97, pp. 65-76.** Verified: dblp / ACM DL.
  Introduced an unbiased Monte Carlo renderer that samples light paths by *mutating* a current path
  and accepting mutations in proportion to their image contribution.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a general, principled algorithm that is orders of magnitude more
    efficient on hard illumination than prior unbiased methods — a technique the whole rendering
    field adopts, evaluated on light-transport cases practitioners recognize as difficult.
  - **Self-check:** does your rendering method move a real axis (variance/speed at equal bias) on
    scenes reviewers agree are hard, and is it general rather than tuned to one scene?

### Imaging / gradient-domain — seamless editing

- **Pérez, Gangnet & Blake, "Poisson Image Editing," ACM SIGGRAPH 2003 (TOG 22(3), pp. 313-318),
  DOI 10.1145/1201775.882269.** Verified: ACM DL / dblp. Seamless region editing by solving a
  Poisson equation in the gradient domain.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a compact mathematical idea that yields a suite of visibly better
    editing tools; the results *are* the argument, shown on real photographs.
  - **Self-check:** can a reader see the improvement at a glance against the obvious alternative,
    and does one clean idea generate the whole result set?

### Physical simulation — real-time fluids

- **Macklin & Müller, "Position Based Fluids," ACM SIGGRAPH 2013 (TOG 32(4)).** Verified: ACM DL /
  dblp. A density-constraint solver inside the Position Based Dynamics framework giving
  SPH-like incompressibility with large, stable time steps.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** it moves a clear axis — stability and speed at real-time budgets —
    and the claim lives in the **motion**, exactly what a supplemental video must show.
  - **Self-check:** is your simulation's advantage (stability / time-step / scale) demonstrated over
    time against a named baseline, not in a single frame?

### Animation + learning — data-driven character control

- **Holden, Komura & Saito, "Phase-Functioned Neural Networks for Character Control," ACM SIGGRAPH
  2017 (TOG 36(4)), DOI 10.1145/3072959.3073663.** Verified: ACM DL / dblp. A network whose weights
  are generated by a cyclic phase function, producing real-time responsive locomotion over terrain.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** the contribution is a *graphics capability* (controllable, real-time,
    high-quality motion), with the network in service of it — the model-swap test passes.
  - **Self-check:** if the architecture changed, would the animation lesson survive — or is the
    model the whole paper (a `siggraph-topic-selection` re-route signal toward a learning venue)?

### Neural rendering + systems — fast trainable primitives

- **Müller, Evans, Schied & Keller, "Instant Neural Graphics Primitives with a Multiresolution Hash
  Encoding," ACM SIGGRAPH 2022 (TOG 41(4)), DOI 10.1145/3528223.3530127.** Verified: ACM DL / dblp;
  SIGGRAPH 2022 Best Paper. A multiresolution hash encoding and fully-fused CUDA kernels that train
  neural graphics primitives in seconds.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a dramatic speed axis (orders of magnitude) with released, runnable
    code — the modern SIGGRAPH ideal of a result you can *see fast* and *run yourself*.
  - **Self-check:** is your speed/quality gain demonstrated at matched quality on stated hardware,
    with code a replicability volunteer could actually build (see `siggraph-artifact-evaluation`)?

## Omitted for lack of clean SIGGRAPH verification (do not attribute to SIGGRAPH without re-checking)

To keep the list zero-hallucination, the following were **excluded** after checking — several are
exactly the sibling-venue confusions the header warns about:

- **Sorkine & Alexa, "As-Rigid-As-Possible Surface Modeling"** — verified to be **SGP 2007**
  (Eurographics Symposium on Geometry Processing), *not* SIGGRAPH. A classic geometry-processing
  trap; listed only as a guardrail.
- **Newcombe/Izadi et al., "KinectFusion: Real-Time Dense Surface Mapping and Tracking"** — the
  dense-reconstruction paper is **UIST 2011 / ISMAR 2011**, *not* SIGGRAPH. Omitted.
- **Kazhdan, Bolitho & Hoppe, "Poisson Surface Reconstruction"** — widely cited geometry work
  generally attributed to **SGP 2006**; **待核实** against dblp before any SIGGRAPH attribution —
  omitted here to avoid venue misattribution.

Before adding any paper, confirm it on **dblp** and **ACM DL** by matching the venue string to a
SIGGRAPH edition (not SIGGRAPH Asia, Eurographics/EGSR, SGP, SCA, UIST/ISMAR, or a vision venue),
then record authors, year, and DOI/TOG volume-issue, and update the access-date header. When in
doubt, omit. If web search is unavailable, add the row as **待核实 / TO VERIFY** with **no
attribution**.
