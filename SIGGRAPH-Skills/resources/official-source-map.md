# Official Source Map - ACM SIGGRAPH Technical Papers

Access date: 2026-07-09

This map records the official and cross-check sources for the SIGGRAPH-specific facts used in
this pack. SIGGRAPH is the ACM SIGGRAPH annual conference; its **Technical Papers** program is
**journal-integrated** — accepted research is published as a special issue of **ACM Transactions
on Graphics (TOG)** (the SIGGRAPH North America issue and, for the sister conference, the
SIGGRAPH Asia issue). Calendar, page budget, and review mechanics are re-decided per edition and
run on **two cycles a year** (SIGGRAPH North America, deadline ~January; SIGGRAPH Asia, deadline
~May). Reopen the current-cycle technical-papers call, the submissions FAQ, the TOG author
guidelines, and the reviewer-instructions page before giving submission-ready advice.

## Verified 2026 facts used in skills

- **SIGGRAPH 2026 is the 53rd ACM SIGGRAPH annual conference**, held at the **Los Angeles
  Convention Center, 19-23 July 2026** (source 1; ACM SIGGRAPH press release, 2026-01-29). As of
  the 2026-07-09 access date the conference is imminent and its Technical Papers are already
  published (official publication date 3 July 2026).
- **SIGGRAPH 2026 Technical Papers calendar:** submission-form deadline **15 January 2026**
  (22:00 UTC/GMT — title, abstract, topic areas, complete co-author list; authors locked after
  this), complete-submission deadline **23 January 2026** (22:00 UTC/GMT — paper PDF or MD5
  checksum plus supplemental materials) (source 2). Rebuttal window **5-12 March 2026**, decisions
  and camera-ready thereafter (source 3).
- **The rebuttal is ≤1000 words of text only** — no images, no video, no URLs to external pages —
  uploaded via the online submission system before 22:00 UTC 12 March 2026; it addresses factual
  errors and specific reviewer questions, not new results (source 3).
- **Two integrated tracks:** *Journal* (ACM Transactions on Graphics) and *Conference*.
  Submissions are either **dual-track** (eligible for Journal or Conference publication) or
  **Journal only**. All submissions are reviewed on **one single scale** — Strong Reject, Reject,
  Borderline Reject, Borderline Accept, Accept, Strong Accept. The Technical Papers Committee
  assigns the track for dual-track papers, being less demanding about formal completeness and
  validation when accepting to the **Conference** track (allowing riskier/earlier-stage work)
  (sources 1, 3).
- **Conditional acceptance + second stage:** the full Technical Papers Committee finalizes
  conditional acceptance or rejection; conditionally accepted papers undergo a **second reviewing
  process** in which a committee member verifies the final version is acceptable and required
  changes were made (source 3).
- **Publication venue is ACM Transactions on Graphics (TOG).** The SIGGRAPH 2026 proceedings are
  **TOG Volume 45, Issue 4**; SIGGRAPH Asia issues appear as separate TOG issues in the same
  volume year (sources 1, 5).
- **Format:** ACM **acmart** class, **version 2.16 or higher required** (earlier versions produce
  an invalid submission). Dual-track (Conference) papers are **≤7 pages excluding references and
  figures-only pages**, with **at most two figures-only pages** placed after the references; the
  reference list has no length limit. A **representative image** and supplemental materials
  (video, code, data, appendices) are uploaded with the paper (sources 2, 7).
- **Submission system is Linklings** (source 2).
- **SIGGRAPH Asia 2026** is held in **Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia, 1-4 December 2026**; its Technical
  Papers used a two-stage submission (abstract/registration then full paper) with the Stage-2 full
  paper deadline **12 May 2026** and upload **13 May 2026** (both now closed); at least one author
  must present in person (source 4).
- **Direct-to-TOG submissions are rolling** through ScholarOne, outside the conference cycle but
  less common; the SIGGRAPH (~January) and SIGGRAPH Asia (~May) conference deadlines are the de
  facto journal deadlines for most authors (source 6).
- **Reproducibility culture:** the **Graphics Replicability Stamp Initiative (GRSI,
  replicabilitystamp.org, since 2016)** awards a stamp when volunteers verify submitted code
  replicates a paper's results, archiving accepted code via **Software Heritage** (since 2023); the
  related **Code Replicability in Computer Graphics (CRCG, replicability.graphics)** has checked
  SIGGRAPH papers' replicability since July 2020 (sources 8, 9). These are **community-run stamps**,
  not the ACM Artifact Review and Badging scheme used at SIGSOFT/systems venues.
- **Awards:** Best Papers, Honorable Mentions, and a Test-of-Time award are given each year
  (source 10).

## The journal-integrated model (honest reading)

SIGGRAPH's defining trait among CS venues is that its Technical Papers **are** ACM TOG journal
articles: acceptance yields a TOG DOI and issue placement, not merely conference proceedings. The
single-round review nonetheless resembles a conference — one deadline, external referees, a short
**rebuttal**, then a **Technical Papers Committee meeting** — but folds in a journal-grade
**conditional-accept / second-stage revision** so the published article meets TOG's standard. The
**dual-track** choice (Journal vs Conference publication) is decided by the committee at accept
time, and a "Conference-track" accept is a genuine, lighter-validation path for earlier-stage
work, published in the SIGGRAPH Conference Proceedings rather than the TOG issue. Verify per cycle
whether both tracks are offered and how the track boundary is drawn.

## No editor-in-chief role in the conference; rotating committee

The SIGGRAPH Technical Papers process is run by a per-edition **Technical Papers Chair** and a
**Technical Papers Committee** (with associate/sort chairs and external tertiary reviewers), not a
standing journal editor-in-chief — even though the output is a journal. ACM TOG itself has an
Editor-in-Chief for the rolling direct-submission stream; do not conflate the two paths. Cost model
is ACM/conference publication; at least one author presents in person.
