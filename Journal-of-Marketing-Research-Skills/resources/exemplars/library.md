# JMR Exemplars Library (method × topic)

> **Verified via web search, access date 2026-06-07.** Every paper below was checked to confirm it
> actually appeared in the **Journal of Marketing Research** (AMA / SAGE), with year and volume/issue.
> Verification anchored on the SAGE article page DOI prefixes that are unique to JMR —
> `journals.sagepub.com/doi/.../10.1177/002224377...`–`002224379...` (print-era JMR) and
> `journals.sagepub.com/doi/.../10.1509/jmkr.*` or `10.1509/jmr.*` (modern JMR). Papers that resolved to
> a **sibling journal** were **omitted** (see the omissions list); it is deliberately a short, clean list
> rather than a long, uncertain one.
>
> **Sibling-confusion guard.** JMR is **not** *Journal of Marketing* (JM), *Marketing Science*,
> *Journal of Consumer Research* (JCR), *Journal of Consumer Psychology*, or *Quantitative Marketing and
> Economics* (QME). Several famous marketing landmarks live in those venues; they are listed under
> "Omitted" so they are not misattributed to JMR.
>
> **Use principle (zero hallucination):** this file gives **design positioning only**. It does not
> reproduce or invent coefficients, effect sizes, or specific findings — read the original on SAGE / the
> authors' pages before citing any number. For JMR-specific policy and limits, see
> [`../official-source-map.md`](../official-source-map.md).

## How to use this library

Pick the row whose **method × question** is closest to yours, then study how that paper clears JMR's
**dual bar** — a substantive marketing insight *and* the methodological credibility that makes it
believable (see [`../../skills/jmr-contribution-framing/SKILL.md`](../../skills/jmr-contribution-framing/SKILL.md)
and [`../../skills/jmr-topic-selection/SKILL.md`](../../skills/jmr-topic-selection/SKILL.md)). For each,
ask the self-check question before claiming your paper is "JMR-shaped." The list deliberately spans JMR's
genres — measurement, behavioral, econometric/modeling, and big-data/text methods — per the genre
taxonomy in [`../../skills/jmr-methods/SKILL.md`](../../skills/jmr-methods/SKILL.md).

## By method

### Measurement / scale-development procedure (methods contribution)

- **Churchill, "A Paradigm for Developing Better Measures of Marketing Constructs," JMR 1979, 16(1):64–73.**
  Verified: `journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/002224377901600110`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** the method *is* the contribution — a reusable procedure for constructing
    and validating multi-item measures that other marketing researchers adopt wholesale. The canonical
    JMR "we give the field a tool" paper (`jmr-contribution-framing`: methods genre).
  - **Self-check:** does your procedure solve a real marketing-research measurement problem better than
    existing practice, with an application that demonstrates the payoff?

- **Fornell & Larcker, "Evaluating Structural Equation Models with Unobservable Variables and Measurement Error," JMR 1981, 18(1):39–50.**
  Verified: `journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/002224378101800104`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** introduces criteria (composite reliability, average variance extracted /
    discriminant validity) that became standard tooling for evaluating latent-variable models in
    marketing — a methodological advance other researchers reuse, not an application.
  - **Self-check:** is your methodological contribution a criterion/estimator others can apply to *their*
    data, stated precisely enough to be adopted?

- **Voss, Spangenberg & Grohmann, "Measuring the Hedonic and Utilitarian Dimensions of Consumer Attitude," JMR 2003, 40(3):310–320.**
  Verified: `journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1509/jmkr.40.3.310.19238`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** develops and validates a parsimonious, generalizable two-dimensional scale
    (the HED/UT scale) — measurement done to the JMR rigor bar, with the construct itself the substantive
    payoff. Pairs with [`../../skills/jmr-theory-development/SKILL.md`](../../skills/jmr-theory-development/SKILL.md).
  - **Self-check:** is your new measure both psychometrically credible *and* substantively useful for a
    marketing question, clearing both bars?

### Behavioral / consumer process with field data (behavioral genre)

- **Berger & Milkman, "What Makes Online Content Viral?," JMR 2012, 49(2):192–205.**
  Verified: `journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1509/jmr.10.0353`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a psychological *mechanism* (emotional arousal as the driver of social
    transmission) tested against large-scale real behavioral data (article sharing), not a black-box
    effect — exactly the tested-process expectation in `jmr-theory-development`.
  - **Self-check:** have you named and tested the mechanism (mediator/moderator) rather than asserting a
    directional effect, and shown it in real consumer behavior?

### Dynamic choice model on panel/scanner data (modeling / econometric genre)

- **Mela, Gupta & Lehmann, "The Long-Term Impact of Promotion and Advertising on Consumer Brand Choice," JMR 1997, 34(2):248–261.**
  Verified: `journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/002224379703400205`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** uses long-horizon household panel data and a multi-stage choice model to
    deliver a substantive market insight (how promotion/advertising reshape long-run price sensitivity) —
    the modeling-genre pattern where the estimator serves a marketing question, not the reverse
    (`jmr-methods` / `jmr-topic-selection`).
  - **Self-check:** does your model's variation credibly identify the parameters, and does the result
    *teach something about the market* rather than just fit the data?

### Big-data text mining for substantive insight (methods × modeling)

- **Tirunillai & Tellis, "Mining Marketing Meaning from Online Chatter: Strategic Brand Analysis of Big Data Using Latent Dirichlet Allocation," JMR 2014, 51(4):463–479.**
  Verified: `journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1509/jmr.12.0106`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** brings an unsupervised method (LDA topic modeling) to user-generated
    content and converts it into brand/competitive insight — a methodological advance whose payoff is a
    substantive marketing question, clearing the dual bar from both sides.
  - **Self-check:** does the new method *unlock* a marketing insight that prior tools could not reach,
    rather than re-deriving a known result with fancier machinery?

## Omitted for sibling-journal placement or lack of full verification (do not attribute to JMR)

  ***Marketing Science*** 24(2):275–284 (INFORMS), **not JMR**.
- **Rossi, McCulloch & Allenby, "The Value of Purchase History Data in Target Marketing" (1996)** —
  verified to be in ***Marketing Science*** 15(4):321–340 (INFORMS), **not JMR**.
- **Anderson & Simester, "Effects of $9 Price Endings on Retail Sales" (2003)** — verified to be in
  ***Quantitative Marketing and Economics*** 1(1):93–110 (Springer), **not JMR**.
- **Netzer, Feldman, Goldenberg & Fresko, "Mine Your Own Business" (2012)** — verified to be in
  ***Marketing Science*** 31(3):521–543 (INFORMS), **not JMR**.
- **Rust, Lemon & Zeithaml, "Return on Marketing: Using Customer Equity…" (2004)** — verified to be in the
  ***Journal of Marketing*** (JM) 68(1):109–127, **not JMR**.

Before adding any new paper to this library, confirm it on `journals.sagepub.com` as a JMR article (a
`10.1177/00222437…` or `10.1509/jmkr|jmr.*` DOI with volume/issue) and update the access-date header.
When the venue is a sibling (JM, Marketing Science, JCR, JCP, QME), omit it.
