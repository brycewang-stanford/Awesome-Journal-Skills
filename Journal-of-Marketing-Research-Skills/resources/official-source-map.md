## VERIFIED facts injected into the skills (with source #)

- **Publisher / sponsor:** published by SAGE Publishing on behalf of the American Marketing Association (AMA). (#1, #7)
- **Scope:** the full spectrum of marketing topics with an emphasis on methodological rigor; welcomes a wide variety of data and methodological approaches — behavioral experiments, econometric/quantitative modeling, and methods contributions. (#1, #7)
- **Submission system:** ScholarOne / Manuscript Central at **mc.manuscriptcentral.com/ama_jmr**; questions to jmr@ama.org. (#2, #3)
- **Length:** **50-page maximum**, properly formatted and inclusive of title, abstract, keywords, text, footnotes, references, tables, figures, and print appendices; the **Web Appendix does NOT count** toward the 50 pages. JMR uses a **page limit, not a word limit**. (#2)
- **Formatting:** 12-point Times New Roman (or 12-point LaTeX font), double-spaced (tables/references may be single-spaced), 1-inch margins, no page/line numbers, no headers/footers. (#1, #2)
- **Abstract:** **200 words maximum, written in third person.** Title <= 25 words; up to 8 primary keywords; no limit on number of references. (#1, #2)
- **Review:** **double-anonymized (double-blind)**; **two independent reviews required** to reach a Revise or Accept decision; initial editorial evaluation with possible desk rejection. (#1, #4)
- **Exact-statistics mandate:** empirical papers must report **actual p-values to three digits** (not thresholds like p<.05, not asterisks), **standard errors** of parameter estimates, and **effect sizes**; no leading zero before the decimal (e.g., .97); no more than three decimal places. (#1, #4)
- **Replication / transparency:** governed by the AMA Research Transparency Policy; a **Data Availability Statement is required on the title page**; papers must contain enough information (in-text, Web Appendix, or online supplements) to let a reasonably trained researcher replicate; on editor request authors share computer code, instruments, and materials; data/materials provided before final acceptance. (#2, #4)
- **Web Appendix convention:** submitted as a **single separate PDF**, excluded from the 50-page limit, with tables/figures labeled using a **'W' prefix** (e.g., "Table W1", "Figure W1"). (#2)
- **Reference style:** **AMA author-year** style (e.g., "Thorelli (1960)" / "(Thorelli 1960)"; list all authors up to three, "et al." for four or more). (#1, #2)
- **AI disclosure:** any AI-generated content must be identified in the main document (e.g., Methods) and noted in Acknowledgments. (#2)
- **Integrity:** every submission screened with **iThenticate**. (#2)
- **Editorial structure / routing:** a single EIC plus a panel of Coeditors (currently 4; incoming team has 5) who handle manuscripts by methodological/substantive domain, alongside Associate Editors and an Editorial Review Board. (#6, #5)
- **Editor transition:** **Rebecca Hamilton (Georgetown)** is EIC of record through **30 June 2026**; **Raphael Thomadsen (Washington University in St. Louis)** is incoming EIC for **1 July 2026 - 30 June 2029**, and the incoming team began processing new manuscripts on **1 April 2026** (so authors submitting now are effectively handled by the incoming team). (#5, #6)
- **Indexing/reputation:** listed on the Financial Times (FT50) and UT Dallas research-rankings lists. (#7)
