# Official Source Map - SIGMOD

Access date: 2026-07-08

This map records the official sources behind every SIGMOD-specific fact in this
pack. SIGMOD research submissions run through PACMMOD (Proceedings of the ACM
on Management of Data) on a multi-round calendar that is republished for each
conference cycle; reopen the current cycle's Call for Papers, important-dates
page, PACMMOD author guidelines, and the ARI pages before giving
submission-ready advice.

**Access-method note (2026-07-08):** direct fetches of `sigmod.org`,
`2027.sigmod.org`, `dl.acm.org`, and `dblp.org` returned HTTP 403 through the
network environment used for verification. Official-page contents were
therefore read through search-engine renderings of those exact URLs and
cross-checked across the 2026/2027 conference sites, the sigmod.org awards
pages, `reproducibility.sigmod.org`, and `vldb.org`. Where renderings of two
pages disagreed (see the exemplars library's omissions section), the fact was
excluded or marked 待核实. Open the primary URLs directly before relying on
any deadline or rule.

## Verified facts used across the skills (as of 2026-07-08)

- SIGMOD research papers are journal publications: accepted papers appear in
  PACMMOD issues, one issue per submission round, and authors are invited to
  present at the conference (sources 1, 3, 4).
- SIGMOD 2027 runs four research rounds with paper deadlines on Jan 17,
  Apr 17, Jul 17, and Oct 17, 2026, abstract+COI a week earlier, feedback
  phases about two months in, first verdicts about three months in, a
  one-month revision window, and final decisions roughly five months after
  submission (sources 1, 2).
- Submissions: 12 pages + unlimited references, current ACM template,
  Microsoft CMT, strict double-anonymity including funding and colleague
  acknowledgements (source 1).
- Revision mechanics: +1 content page, ≤4-page letter, per-reviewer color
  highlighting with precise locators (source 1).
- Rejection triggers a 12-month research-track embargo; late withdrawal
  counts as rejection; each author is capped at 10 research submissions per
  cycle (source 1).
- ARI is optional, post-acceptance, HotCRP-registered in recent editions, and
  awards the Available / Evaluated / Results Reproduced badge ladder judged
  on coverage, ease, flexibility, and portability (sources 5, 6).
- SIGMOD 2026 took place in Bengaluru, May 31 - June 5, 2026; SIGMOD 2027 is
  scheduled for Huntington Beach, California, June 13-19, 2027 (sources 1, 7).

## 待核实 (to verify before relying)

- SIGMOD 2027 industrial-track calendar — the CFP was not yet posted as of
  2026-07-08; the 2026-cycle shape (source 8) is the best available anchor.
- The exact CMT site URL for the current round, and any per-cycle character
  or length limits in the feedback-phase reply box.
- ARI 2027-edition registration site, dates, and whether hardware support
  (cloud credits or author-provided access) is offered this edition.
- PACMMOD open-access / APC mechanics under ACM's publishing model — the DL
  pages could not be opened directly; do not state a fee policy without
  first-hand confirmation.
- Whether in-PDF appendices beyond the 12 pages are permitted in the current
  cycle's submission form.

## Cycle-volatile by construction

Round dates, page budgets, revision-letter mechanics, embargo wording, the
author cap, platform choice, ARI edition details, and track structure are all
re-declared each cycle. This pack teaches the durable machinery — quarterly
rounds into PACMMOD issues, revision-letter culture, ARI badging — and treats
every number above as a dated observation, not a promise.
