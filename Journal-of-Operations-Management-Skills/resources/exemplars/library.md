# Exemplar Library — *Journal of Operations Management* (JOM)

> **Verified via web search, access date 2026-06-08.** Each paper was confirmed on Wiley Online
> Library under *Journal of Operations Management* (DOI stem `10.1002/joom` for recent articles, or
> `10.1016/j.jom` for the Elsevier-era articles before the journal moved to Wiley). The DOI is the
> verification anchor. Positioning aid — read the originals; do not reproduce numbers.
>
> **Sibling-confusion guard.** JOM is NOT: *Journal of Supply Chain Management* (JSCM, DOI
> `1745-493X` / `jscm`), *Operations Research*, *Management Science*, *M&SOM*, *Production and
> Operations Management* (POM), *Decision Sciences*, *International Journal of Operations & Production
> Management* (IJOPM). Several famous Pagell sustainable-SCM **case-study** papers are in **JSCM**, not
> JOM — check the stem.

## How to use

- **Empirical OM/SCM is the core.** JOM rewards a real operations/supply-chain phenomenon, a clear
  theoretical contribution, and credible measurement/identification — lead with phenomenon and theory,
  not method (`jom-writing-style`, `jom-contribution-framing`).
- **Take measurement and endogeneity seriously.** Sourcing/integration/analytics choices are *chosen*;
  the vendored `code/` (panel FE, IV, first-stage diagnostics) supports defending the causal claim
  (`jom-methods`).
- **Position within the OM/SCM family.** Be explicit why the paper is JOM rather than JSCM / POM /
  M&SOM / Decision Sciences (`jom-literature-positioning`).
