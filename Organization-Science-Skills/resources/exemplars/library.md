# *Organization Science* Exemplars Library (method × topic)

> **Verified via web search, access date 2026-06-08.** Every paper below was checked to confirm it
> actually appeared in **_Organization Science_** — the interdisciplinary, theory-driven organizations
> journal published by **INFORMS** (Institute for Operations Research and the Management Sciences) — with
> year and volume/issue, against the journal's own article pages on INFORMS PubsOnLine
> (`pubsonline.informs.org/doi/...orsc...`), corroborated against RePEc (`inm/ororsc`), Google Scholar, and
> JSTOR. Papers without a clean **_Organization Science_** placement match were **omitted** — this is
> deliberately a short, clean list, not a long, uncertain one.
>
> **Sibling-journal guard.** *Organization Science* is **not** *Administrative Science Quarterly* (ASQ),
> *Academy of Management Journal* / *Academy of Management Review* (AMJ / AMR), *Management Science*, or
> *Strategic Management Journal* (SMJ). Many famous "organizations" papers live in those venues; the
> [Omitted](#omitted-for-lack-of-full-verification-do-not-attribute-to-organization-science) section
> records the traps checked and rejected. *Organization Science* is the INFORMS theory-driven venue that
> is methodologically eclectic and distinctively open to qualitative/inductive work; *Management Science*
> is its more identification-leaning INFORMS sibling with a code-and-data mandate; ASQ is the Cornell/SAGE
> contextual venue; AMJ/AMR are the AOM empirical/theory journals.
>
> **Use principle (zero hallucination):** this file gives **design positioning only**. It does not
> reproduce or invent coefficients, effect sizes, or specific findings — read the original on INFORMS
> PubsOnLine before citing any number. For *Organization Science*-specific scope, length norms, the
> mandatory contribution statement, and house style, see [`../official-source-map.md`](../official-source-map.md).

## How to use this library

Pick the row whose **method × organizational question** is closest to yours, then study how that paper
turns an organizational phenomenon into a **theory-driven contribution** — the *Organization Science* bar,
where "theoretical novelty is neither necessary nor sufficient" and overall contribution governs (see
[`../../skills/orgsci-topic-selection/SKILL.md`](../../skills/orgsci-topic-selection/SKILL.md),
[`../../skills/orgsci-theory-development/SKILL.md`](../../skills/orgsci-theory-development/SKILL.md), and
[`../../skills/orgsci-contribution-framing/SKILL.md`](../../skills/orgsci-contribution-framing/SKILL.md)).
For each, ask the self-check question before claiming your paper is "*Organization Science*-shaped."

## By method

### Formal / computational model of an organizational process (analytical simulation)

- **March, "Exploration and Exploitation in Organizational Learning," _Organization Science_ 1991, 2(1):71–87.**
  Verified: `pubsonline.informs.org/doi/10.1287/orsc.2.1.71` (RePEc `inm/ororsc`; Google Scholar carrying journal *Organization Science*, vol. 2).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a spare formal model of mutual learning between members and an organizational
    code makes a durable *theoretical* point — that adaptive systems refining exploitation faster than
    exploration are effective short-run but self-destructive long-run. The
    [`orgsci-theory-development`](../../skills/orgsci-theory-development/SKILL.md) move of naming the
    organizational mechanism (the learning engine) and its boundary conditions rather than a bare
    relationship; method (simulation) is demoted to a tool.
  - **Self-check:** does your model yield an *organizational* theoretical claim with scope conditions, not
    just a parameter sweep?

### Conceptual theory-building of the firm (a-priori / deductive)

- **Kogut & Zander, "Knowledge of the Firm, Combinative Capabilities, and the Replication of Technology," _Organization Science_ 1992, 3(3):383–397.**
  Verified: `pubsonline.informs.org/doi/abs/10.1287/orsc.3.3.383` (RePEc `inm/ororsc`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** builds a knowledge-based theory of why firms exist — what they do better than
    markets is the sharing and transfer of individual and group knowledge — articulating the
    organizational mechanism (combinative capabilities) at the meso level. The
    [`orgsci-contribution-framing`](../../skills/orgsci-contribution-framing/SKILL.md) "new theory"
    contribution type, stated as an extension/challenge to a recognizable conversation rather than a gap.
  - **Self-check:** is your conceptual claim a *mechanism* legible across levels, or a relabeling of an
    existing construct?

### Inductive / interpretive theory from rich data (qualitative)

- **Dougherty, "Interpretive Barriers to Successful Product Innovation in Large Firms," _Organization Science_ 1992, 3(2):179–202.**
  Verified: `pubsonline.informs.org/doi/10.1287/orsc.3.2.179` (RePEc `inm/ororsc`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** abstracts an inductive account — departmental "thought worlds" and product
    routines that inhibit linking technology to market — into a process model, not thick description. The
    journal's signature openness to inductive work done as
    [`orgsci-theory-development`](../../skills/orgsci-theory-development/SKILL.md) prescribes: build, not
    just describe, and name the organizational interpretive mechanism.
  - **Self-check:** does your qualitative study deliver an *abstracted* model or set of propositions, or
    stop at description?

- **Orlikowski, "Knowing in Practice: Enacting a Collective Capability in Distributed Organizing," _Organization Science_ 2002, 13(3):249–273.**
  Verified: `pubsonline.informs.org/doi/10.1287/orsc.13.3.249.2776` (RePEc `inm/ororsc`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** reframes "knowing" as an ongoing social accomplishment enacted in practice,
    grounded in a field study of dispersed product development — a cross-level bridge from micro practice
    to a collective, distributed capability. The
    [`orgsci-theory-development`](../../skills/orgsci-theory-development/SKILL.md) level-bridging logic done
    explicitly, avoiding level conflation.
  - **Self-check:** when you cross from individual action to organizational capability, do you state the
    bridging logic (enactment/emergence), or assert the macro claim from micro data?

### Network econometrics on large-scale archival data (quantitative)

- **Fleming, King & Juda, "Small Worlds and Regional Innovation," _Organization Science_ 2007, 18(6):938–954.**
  Verified: `pubsonline.informs.org/doi/10.1287/orsc.1070.0289` (RePEc `inm/ororsc/v18y2007i6p938-954`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** builds a patent-coauthorship database to test whether small-world structure
    raises regional innovative output, and reports a *disconfirmation* of the popular intuition — a
    contribution from new data and careful measurement rather than a confirmed hypothesis. The
    [`orgsci-data-analysis`](../../skills/orgsci-data-analysis/SKILL.md) discipline of letting the design,
    not the prior, decide.
  - **Self-check:** is your network claim identified from the data's structure, with a result you would
    report even if it contradicts the fashionable mechanism?

### Multilevel / cross-national quantitative test of an institutional argument (quantitative)

- **Zhang, "An Institutional Approach to Gender Diversity and Firm Performance," _Organization Science_ 2020, 31(2):439–457.**
  Verified: `pubsonline.informs.org/doi/10.1287/orsc.2019.1297` (RePEc `inm/ororsc`; Google Scholar carrying *Organization Science* 31(2)).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** resolves conflicting prior findings by theorizing that gender diversity's
    performance link depends on its normative and regulatory acceptance in the institutional environment,
    tested across 35 countries and 24 industries — a country-level moderator theorized as a *level*, not a
    control. The [`orgsci-theory-development`](../../skills/orgsci-theory-development/SKILL.md) boundary-
    condition contribution and [`orgsci-literature-positioning`](../../skills/orgsci-literature-positioning/SKILL.md)
    "reconcile a contradiction" move.
  - **Self-check:** does your moderator carry a theorized mechanism that explains *when* the main effect
    holds or reverses, rather than a black-box interaction term?
