# Organization Science Official Source Map

Canonical sources for verifying every *Organization Science*-specific claim made in this skill pack.
Access date for this refresh: **2026-06-20**. INFORMS pages are the live authority for submission
workflow, transparency policy, editorial roster, and publication choices; check the linked pages again
immediately before upload when a portal prompt or named editor matters.

## Official Journal, Editorial Scope, and Masthead

- **https://pubsonline.informs.org/journal/orsc** — journal landing page. Verifies publisher
  **INFORMS**, frequency **bimonthly**, Print ISSN **1047-7039**, Online ISSN **1526-5455**, current
  Editor-in-Chief **Lamar Pierce**, current metrics shown by PubsOnLine, and quicklinks to submission
  guidelines, data/methods transparency, ethical guidelines, and the author portal. Use metrics only as
  live context, not as manuscript-fit criteria.
- **https://pubsonline.informs.org/page/orsc/editorial-statement** — editorial statement. Verifies that
  the journal publishes fundamental research about organizations and their processes, structures,
  technologies, identities, forms, and people; welcomes work across organizational behavior and theory,
  psychology, sociology, economics, political science, strategic management, information systems,
  innovation, entrepreneurship, and cognitive science; covers levels from human behavior and teams to
  networks and organizations; emphasizes overall contribution over theoretical novelty; and welcomes
  contributions from new theory, data, methods, settings, mechanisms, or social-problem / grand-challenge
  relevance. It also verifies methodological breadth and the policy that causal inference is valued but
  not always necessary or possible.
- **https://pubsonline.informs.org/page/orsc/editorial-board** — editorial board page. Verifies current
  Editor-in-Chief **Lamar Pierce** of Olin Business School, Washington University in St. Louis (**re-verified current 2026-06-22** via the INFORMS landing/editorial-board pages, the WashU Olin appointment announcement, and a 2026 Organization Science editorial bylined by Pierce; editor terms rotate, so confirm the live masthead before naming him in a cover letter); deputy
  editors including Stefano Brusoni, Gokhan Ertug, Martin Ganco, Sharique Hasan, Lindy Greer, Cindy Muir
  (Zapata), Andrew Nelson, Michelle Rogan, and Lamar Pierce (ex officio); senior editors; promotion and
  outreach editors; regional outreach editors; and the Editorial Review Board. Use this page before
  naming a specific editor in a cover letter.

## Submission Route, Article Types, Length, and Review

- **https://pubsonline.informs.org/page/orsc/submission-guidelines** — submission guidelines. Verifies
  the submission route **ScholarOne Manuscripts** at **https://mc.manuscriptcentral.com/orsc**; article
  categories **Original Manuscript**, **Perspectives**, and **Crossroads**; submitting-author ORCID
  requirement; abstract maximum **250 words**; **3 to 10 keywords**; author-date reference style;
  optional nomination of up to **three** potential reviewers; simultaneous review at conferences when
  the author discloses that status to the editor; a **six-month** invited-revision window unless the
  editor grants an exception; appeals filed no earlier than **four weeks** and no later than **eight
  weeks** after final decision; final files requested within **14 days** after acceptance; and required
  final files including source manuscript, main-paper PDF, online-appendix PDF, figure source files,
  copyright form, editorial checklist, author biography, and optional media-promotion form.
- **https://pubsonline.informs.org/pb-assets/filesorsc/PageLengthPolicyFinal-1728561990830.pdf** —
  official manuscript length policy. Verifies initial-submission guidance of less than **50 pages** of
  double-spaced 12-point text with 1-inch margins, all-inclusive, or **40 pages** in 11-point font; fonts
  similar to Garamond or Times New Roman; no Helvetica Narrow; desk-reject option for excessive length;
  standalone anonymized appendices for review and eventual online supplement; accepted manuscripts
  typically conforming to a 50-page limit; and revision-response norms: efficient responses, no duplicate
  response material across reviewers, editor/reviewer comments in italics verbatim, and no combative
  tone.
- **Submission-guidelines review wording and ethical-guidelines wording** — the submission guidelines
  instruct authors to prepare a fully anonymized main manuscript and describe double-blind review by at
  least two reviewers, whereas the ethical-guidelines page currently says Organization Science uses a
  single-blind peer-review process in which author identities are known to editors and reviewers. This
  pack uses the submission workflow as the operational default: anonymize the manuscript and separate
  cover/title material exactly as the upload instructions require, while avoiding claims about what any
  individual reviewer sees unless the current portal instructions settle the wording at submission time.

## Transparency, Data, Code, and Ethics

- **https://pubsonline.informs.org/page/orsc/data-and-methods-transparency** — data and methods
  transparency policy. Verifies that submissions received on or after **2025-08-01** are governed by
  policies designed to improve research transparency; authors should provide enough detail for editors,
  reviewers, and later readers to understand setting, data, processes, distributions, results, and
  methods; sufficient detail and references should enable replication; all authors should disclose AI
  and AI-assisted technologies in the text or footnotes when used; authors should disclose research
  sequence, related overlapping papers, IRB/ethics concerns, and vulnerable-party issues.
- **Data and Methods Transparency, quantitative policy** — verifies code and data expectations. Authors
  are expected to share statistical code during review upon editor request except where confidentiality
  or legal requirements prevent it; accepted papers must publicly share data that allow code to produce
  all reported results unless there is a strong reason for withholding; shared data and code should be
  archived in a third-party repository such as **OSF** or **openICPSR**; exceptions require an
  alternative transparency plan such as synthetic data, detailed instructions for lawful access,
  distributional or summary statistics, or a replication plan with a data provider.
- **Data and Methods Transparency, qualitative policy** — verifies qualitative-methods transparency:
  authors should provide as much detail as confidentiality, anonymity, legal agreements, researcher
  safety, and participant safety allow; describe setting, data gathered, analytic approach, coding,
  interpretation, and the path from raw data to findings; and use tables/figures or confidential
  identifiers where they improve transparency without harming confidentiality.
- **https://pubsonline.informs.org/pb-assets/filesorsc/ORSC-transparency-checklist-06-17-2025-1750173412207.pdf**
  — Organization Science transparency submission checklist, version **05.12.2025**, effective
  **2025-03-12**. Verifies checklist items for legal data access, data origins and distributions,
  causal/correlational language, effect-size magnitude and precision, research-method descriptions,
  sample selection/exclusions/specification choices/model assumptions/sample sizes/measurement
  construction, experimental conditions, preregistration links/deviations, research sequence, AI use,
  IRB/ethical considerations, and attribution of related data or code.
- **https://pubsonline.informs.org/page/orsc/senior-editors** — ethical-guidelines route currently
  rendered at this URL. Verifies general duties for editors, authors, and reviewers, including
  objectivity, confidentiality, conflict-of-interest management, honest source/funder disclosure,
  nondiscrimination in publication decisions, and appeal routing through the Editor-in-Chief.

## Fees, Open Access, and Publication Choices

- **https://pubsonline.informs.org/authorportal/open-option** — INFORMS Open Option. Verifies Green
  Option immediate posting of the author accepted manuscript after acceptance, Gold Option open access
  for accepted manuscripts, one-time **USD 3,000** IOO fee after acceptance, no impact of IOO selection
  on peer review or publication decision, and invoicing only after acceptance.
- **Submission Guidelines, After Acceptance / Open Access** — cross-checks final-file requirements and
  the **USD 3,000** Open Option fee for accepted papers that authors choose to publish open access.

## Pack-Level Guardrails

- Do not treat theoretical novelty alone as the bar. The editorial statement prioritizes overall
  contribution and accepts multiple contribution routes.
- Do not tell authors that Organization Science has no data/code expectations. The 2025 transparency
  policy expects code sharing on request during review and public data/code sharing for accepted
  quantitative papers unless a documented exception and alternative transparency plan applies.
- Do not represent IOO as a submission fee. It is an optional post-acceptance open-access publication
  choice.
- Do not advise authors to skip anonymization because one ethics page contains single-blind wording.
  The submission workflow still requires an anonymized main manuscript and separate identifying
  materials.
- Do not use impact factor, review speed, or acceptance odds as a fit argument; use the editorial
  statement, contribution route, transparency policy, and length policy instead.
