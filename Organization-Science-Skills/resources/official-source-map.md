## VERIFIED facts injected into the skills (with source #)

- **Publisher:** INFORMS (Institute for Operations Research and the Management Sciences). (#1)
- **Field focus:** interdisciplinary, theory-driven research about organizations — their processes, structures, technologies, identities, capabilities, forms, and performance — spanning micro (individual/team) to macro (organizational/field/population) levels, drawing on organization theory, strategy, sociology, economics, and psychology. (#1, #4)
- **Editor-in-Chief:** Lamar Pierce (Washington University in St. Louis; oseic@wustl.edu). (#2, #5)
- **Submission system:** ScholarOne Manuscripts at mc.manuscriptcentral.com/orsc; ORCID required for the submitting author. (#3, #7)
- **Fees:** no mandatory submission or publication fee; open access is optional via the INFORMS Open Option (IOO), a post-acceptance APC of US$3,000 with no bearing on the peer-review decision. (#6)
- **Length norm:** initial submissions should strive to be under 50 pages of double-spaced 12-pt text (or 40 pages 11-pt) with 1-inch margins, all-inclusive; no rigid universal hard cap, but editors may desk-reject excessively long submissions; accepted manuscripts typically conform to a 50-page limit; appendices submitted as a SEPARATE anonymized standalone document for review and eventual online supplementary posting. (#2)
- **Abstract:** not to exceed 250 words. (#3)
- **Review model:** double-anonymous (double-blind) by at least two reviewers; main manuscript fully anonymized (no names, affiliations, acknowledgements; self-citations suppressed or in third person). (#3)
- **Dominant methodology:** methodologically eclectic / pluralistic — explicitly prizes theory building and refinement; distinctive strength in and openness to qualitative and inductive work relative to other elite management journals, while also publishing quantitative, experimental, computational, and formal-analytical research; causal inference valued but "not necessary and often impossible." (#4)
- **Formatting style:** INFORMS author-date house style (author last name + year, e.g., Norman 1977 / (Norman 1977); references alphabetical); footnotes minimized; tables/figures numbered consecutively with self-explanatory titles/notes; double-spaced, 1-inch margins, Garamond- or Times-New-Roman-like fonts (explicitly no Helvetica Narrow); PDF or MS Word. (#2, #3)
- **Replication/data policy:** manuscripts must contain sufficient detail and references to permit replication; authors may be asked to provide raw data for editorial review, should be prepared to provide access, and should retain data for a reasonable time after publication. (#3)
- **Contribution statement:** mandatory <500-word statement in the cover letter, required since June 1, 2023; read by the EIC and Senior Editor during desk review alongside the abstract; submissions without it are returned for revision before editorial review. (#4)
- **Editorial philosophy:** "theoretical novelty is neither necessary nor sufficient"; overall contribution outweighs novelty; contributions may come from new theory, new data, methodological insight, new settings/generalizability, mechanisms, or relevance to social problems and grand challenges. (#4)
- **Editorial structure:** decentralized senior-editor model with strong SE autonomy; conflict-of-interest cascade routing (EIC handles deputy-editor submissions; deputy editors handle senior-editor submissions) rather than fixed departmental area-editor routing. (#5)
- **Author-response norms (from the official length policy):** quote each editor/reviewer comment verbatim in italics; do not duplicate responses across reviewers; avoid combative tone; avoid effusive flattery ("we are neither oracles nor infallible"). (#2)
