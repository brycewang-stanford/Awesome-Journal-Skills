## 中文内容提要（约 300 字）

> 提升全要素生产率是中国经济转向高质量发展的核心，而企业所处的征管环境是一个长期被忽视的效率决定因素。本文利用"金税三期"工程在各省的交错上线构造准自然实验，基于 2010—2018 年中国 A 股上市公司数据，采用异质性稳健的双重差分方法，识别税收征管数字化对企业全要素生产率的因果效应。研究发现：金税三期上线使企业全要素生产率显著提高约 0.038（约相当于均值的 0.9%），且上线前不存在预趋势、效应逐年走强。机制检验表明，征管数字化通过提高税收遵从度与缓解融资约束两条渠道提升企业效率；异质性分析显示，效应在民营企业、市场化程度较低地区以及外部融资依赖度较高的行业中更为明显。本文揭示了税收征管能力作为制度性基础设施的微观效率含义，为以征管现代化服务高质量发展提供了经验证据。

**关键词**：税收征管；金税三期；全要素生产率；融资约束；高质量发展

**JEL Classification**：H26；D24；G38

## English Summary（五要素，浓缩全文的英文长摘要）

> *Summary 须覆盖五要素：①背景与意义 ②主题与思路 ③方法、理论依据、数据及来源 ④主要结论及政策含义 ⑤重要创新与贡献。下文按此结构组织（节选示意）。*

**[Background & significance]** Raising total factor productivity (TFP) is central to China's transition toward high-quality development. While a large literature studies how tax *rates* shape firm behavior, the efficiency consequences of the state's *enforcement* capacity—the ability to make firms actually comply—have received far less attention, even though weak enforcement lets firms divert resources from production toward tax avoidance and rent-seeking.

**[Topic & approach]** This paper asks whether, and through which channels, the digitalization of tax enforcement raises firm-level TFP, exploiting the staggered provincial rollout of China's "Golden Tax Phase III" (GTP3) system as a quasi-natural experiment.

**[Method, theory & data]** Using a panel of Chinese A-share listed firms over 2010–2018 merged with hand-collected provincial rollout dates, we estimate heterogeneity-robust difference-in-differences models (Callaway and Sant'Anna, 2021; Borusyak et al., 2024) and validate the parallel-trends assumption with an event-study design. Mechanism tests follow the design-based approach of 江艇 (2022), estimating only the causal D→M links and arguing the M→Y links from theory.

**[Main findings & policy implications]** GTP3 raises firm TFP by about 0.038 (≈0.9% of the mean), with no pre-trend and effects that strengthen over three years. The effect operates through higher tax compliance (a rise in effective tax rates) and relaxed financing constraints (a fall in the SA index), and is larger for private firms, firms in less marketized regions, and industries more dependent on external finance. The findings imply that enforcement capacity is an under-appreciated piece of institutional infrastructure with real efficiency consequences.

**[Innovation & contribution]** The paper (i) brings the "enforcement technology–tax compliance–allocative efficiency" chain into the determinants of firm TFP; (ii) uses staggered rollout with heterogeneity-robust estimators to credibly identify the effect; and (iii) opens the black box from enforcement to real efficiency by documenting two complementary channels.

**Keywords**: tax enforcement; Golden Tax Phase III; total factor productivity; financing constraints; high-quality development
