## How to use this library

Pick the row whose **method × question** is closest to yours, then study how that paper makes a
**substantial theoretical advance** carry a **long, integrative, model-driven** article — the _Cognitive
Psychology_ bar (see
[`../../skills/cogpsych-topic-selection/SKILL.md`](../../skills/cogpsych-topic-selection/SKILL.md) and
[`../../skills/cogpsych-theory-and-hypotheses/SKILL.md`](../../skills/cogpsych-theory-and-hypotheses/SKILL.md)).
For each, ask the self-check question before claiming your paper is "_Cognitive Psychology_-shaped." Note
that **multi-experiment programs that develop or test a formal theory** are the venue's center of
gravity; a single isolated effect is the wrong shape.

## By method

### Attention — a formal theory tested by a program of search experiments

- **Treisman & Gelade, "A Feature-Integration Theory of Attention," _Cognitive Psychology_ 1980,
  12(1):97–136.** Verified: `sciencedirect.com` article under DOI 10.1016/0010-0285(80)90005-5.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a sustained series of visual-search experiments builds and tests a
    general theory — features registered in parallel, conjunctions requiring focal attention — rather
    than reporting one search effect. The prototype of "experiments in service of a theory of
    cognition," exactly the arc this pack trains
    ([`cogpsych-theory-and-hypotheses`](../../skills/cogpsych-theory-and-hypotheses/SKILL.md),
    [`cogpsych-study-design`](../../skills/cogpsych-study-design/SKILL.md)).
  - **Self-check:** do your experiments jointly constrain a *theory*, with each search/manipulation
    adding inference, rather than demonstrating one effect?

### Problem solving — a multi-experiment program with an explicit theoretical account

- **Gick & Holyoak, "Analogical Problem Solving," _Cognitive Psychology_ 1980, 12(3):306–355.**
  Verified: `sciencedirect.com` article under DOI 10.1016/0010-0285(80)90013-4.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** five experiments map *when* an analogy from a distant domain transfers to
    a new problem, and tie the pattern to a process account of analogical mapping — an integrative,
    theory-developing program, not a single transfer demonstration.
  - **Self-check:** does your experimental series move from a phenomenon to a process account, with each
    experiment isolating a condition the account predicts?

### Judgment & decision making — a heuristic account formalized and tested

- **Kahneman & Tversky, "Subjective Probability: A Judgment of Representativeness," _Cognitive
  Psychology_ 1972, 3(3):430–454.** Verified: `sciencedirect.com` article under DOI
  10.1016/0010-0285(72)90016-3.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a small set of incisive judgment problems converges on one general
    mechanism (the representativeness heuristic), giving a broad theoretical claim about probability
    judgment — breadth and a falsifiable account in an integrative article.
  - **Self-check:** do your manipulations triangulate one general mechanism that a wide cognition
    audience inherits, rather than one effect in one paradigm?

## Omitted for lack of _Cognitive Psychology_ placement (do not attribute without re-checking)

To keep the list zero-hallucination, these frequently-cited candidates were **excluded** after web
checking — several are classic sibling-journal confusions:

- **Wixted, "Dual-Process Theory and Signal-Detection Theory of Recognition Memory" (2007)** —
  **_Psychological Review_** 114(1):152–176, **not** _Cognitive Psychology_. (A model-comparison review
  in the recognition-memory area, but the wrong venue.)
- **Medin & Schaffer, "Context Theory of Classification Learning" (1978)** — **_Psychological Review_**
  85(3):207–238, **not** _Cognitive Psychology_.
- **Posner & Keele, "On the Genesis of Abstract Ideas" (1968)** — **_Journal of Experimental
  Psychology_** 77:353–363, **not** _Cognitive Psychology_.
- **Nosofsky, the Generalized Context Model papers (1984, 1986, 1988)** — primarily in **_JEP: General_
  / _JEP: LMC_** (APA), **not** _Cognitive Psychology_; verify the exact venue before attributing any GCM
  paper here.
- **Rosch, "Cognitive Representations of Semantic Categories" (1975)** — **_JEP: General_** 104:192–233,
  **not** _Cognitive Psychology_.
- **Sternberg, "High-Speed Scanning in Human Memory" (1966)** — **_Science_** 153:652–654, **not**
  _Cognitive Psychology_.
- **Raaijmakers & Shiffrin, "Search of Associative Memory" (SAM, 1981)** — **_Psychological Review_**
  88(2):93–134, **not** _Cognitive Psychology_.
