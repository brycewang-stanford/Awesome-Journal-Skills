# AERJ Official Source Map

Primary and authoritative sources behind the AERJ-specific facts injected into this skill pack.
Access date for all entries: **2026-06-20**. Re-check volatile items (current editors, submission
portal details, fee/OA wording, and policy text) on the official pages before relying on them.

## Official journal, submission & author pages

- **https://journals.sagepub.com/author-instructions/aer**
  — SAGE Submission Guidelines: American Educational Research Journal. Verifies: AERJ publishes
  original empirical and theoretical studies and analyses in education that make significant
  contributions to the understanding or improvement of educational processes and outcomes; manuscripts
  follow **Publication Manual of the American Psychological Association (APA), 7th edition**; abstract
  **100–120 words**; manuscript **maximum 50 pages** including all tables, figures, notes, and
  references; 8.5" x 11" paper; **1" margins**; **double-spaced**; **12-point type**; **double-anonymize
  all manuscripts** with a separate title page file; submission via **ScholarOne Manuscripts** at
  **https://mc.manuscriptcentral.com/aerj**; ORCID required for corresponding author; no fee to publish
  or submit; SAGE Choice gold open access available for a fee; AERJ uses the SAGE Harvard reference
  style on the submission-guidelines page.
- **https://www.aera.net/Publications/Journals/American-Educational-Research-Journal**
  — AERA's AERJ home. Verifies: AERJ is the **flagship journal of the American Educational Research
  Association**; publishes original peer-reviewed analyses across all subfields and disciplines, all
  levels of analysis, all levels of education throughout the life span, and all forms of learning;
  AERA links to the SAGE journal page, manuscript guidelines, submission website, and editors page;
  2025–2027 team announcement and editorial are linked from the journal home.
- **https://www.aera.net/Publications/Journals/American-Educational-Research-Journal/AERJ-Editors**
  — AERJ Editors masthead. Verifies: **2025–2027 Editors**; Editor-in-Chief **Motoko Akiba** (Florida
  State University) — **re-confirmed current 2026-06-22** via the AERA newsroom announcement and the
  SAGE "Editors' Introduction" — with coeditors **Alison Dover**, **Mariela Rodríguez**, **Timothy
  Ford**, **Saran Stewart**, **Conra Gist (on leave)**, **Alex Wiseman**, and **Thomas Luschei**;
  associate-editor roster.
- **https://journals.sagepub.com/loi/aer**
  — SAGE all-issues page. Verifies archive coverage **January 1964 – June 2026**; current issue
  Volume 63, Issue 3, June 2026; ISSN **0002-8312** and Online ISSN **1935-1011**.
- **https://www.aera.net/Publications/Journals/AERA-Journal-Operations-2016**
  — AERA Journal Operations 2016. Verifies the historical correction: AERJ was formerly divided into
  Social and Institutional Analysis and Teaching, Learning, and Human Development sections, but the
  integrated AERJ began receiving new manuscripts on **July 1, 2015** and began publishing manuscripts
  on **January 1, 2016**. Current guidance should not tell authors to choose between those former
  sections.

## Reporting & transparency standards

- **https://www.aera.net/Publications/Standards-for-Research-Conduct**
  — AERA Standards for Research Conduct (landing). Points to the reporting standards below.
- **https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.3102/0013189X035006033**
  — **Standards for Reporting on Empirical Social Science Research in AERA Publications** (AERA, 2006;
  *Educational Researcher* 35(6):33–40). Verifies: applies to education research grounded in the
  **empirical traditions of the social sciences**, covering **both quantitative and qualitative**
  methods; two organizing principles — reports should be **warranted** (adequate evidence) and
  **transparent** (explicit logic of inquiry); humanities-oriented scholarship (history, philosophy,
  arts-based inquiry) is explicitly **outside** its scope.
- **https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.3102/0013189X09341833**
  — **Standards for Reporting on Humanities-Oriented Research in AERA Publications** (AERA, 2009).
  Verifies: complementary reporting standards for **humanities-oriented** education scholarship.
- **https://www.aera.net/About-AERA/AERA-Rules-Policies/Professional-Ethics**
  — **AERA Code of Ethics**. Verifies: expectations on **data sharing / making data available** for
  reanalysis after results are published (subject to participant-confidentiality and legal limits),
  and on research integrity.

## Verify before you rely

Current editor roster, SAGE Choice fee, submission portal behavior, and policy language can change.
Always confirm current requirements on the SAGE author-instructions page, AERA journal home, and AERJ
Editors masthead before submitting.
