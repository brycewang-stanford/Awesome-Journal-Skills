## The two sections

- AERJ is organized into **two separately edited sections**, each with its own editorial team and each
  publishing across a wide range of disciplines and methods:
  - **Social and Institutional Analysis (SIA)** — significant **political, cultural, social, economic,
    and organizational** issues in education.
  - **Teaching, Learning, and Human Development (TLHD)** — the **processes and outcomes of teaching,
    learning, and human development** at all educational levels and in both formal and informal
    settings.
  - Source: AERA AERJ page + SAGE catalog descriptions. Authors **choose the section** at submission.

## Reporting & transparency standards

- **https://www.aera.net/Publications/Standards-for-Research-Conduct**
  — AERA Standards for Research Conduct (landing). Points to the reporting standards below.
- **https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.3102/0013189X035006033**
  — **Standards for Reporting on Empirical Social Science Research in AERA Publications** (AERA, 2006;
  *Educational Researcher* 35(6):33–40). Verifies: applies to education research grounded in the
  **empirical traditions of the social sciences**, covering **both quantitative and qualitative**
  methods; two organizing principles — reports should be **warranted** (adequate evidence) and
  **transparent** (explicit logic of inquiry); humanities-oriented scholarship (history, philosophy,
  arts-based inquiry) is explicitly **outside** its scope.
- **https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.3102/0013189X09341833**
  — **Standards for Reporting on Humanities-Oriented Research in AERA Publications** (AERA, 2009).
  Verifies: complementary reporting standards for **humanities-oriented** education scholarship.
- **https://www.aera.net/About-AERA/AERA-Rules-Policies/Professional-Ethics**
  — **AERA Code of Ethics**. Verifies: expectations on **data sharing / making data available** for
  reanalysis after results are published (subject to participant-confidentiality and legal limits),
  and on research integrity.
