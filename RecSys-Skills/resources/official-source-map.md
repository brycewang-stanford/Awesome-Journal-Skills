## Verified 2026-cycle facts used in the skills

- RecSys 2026 is the **20th** ACM Conference on Recommender Systems, held in **Minneapolis,
  Minnesota, USA**, main program **September 28 - October 2, 2026** (Doctoral Symposium
  September 27, tentative).
- **Main Track deadlines (AoE):** abstracts **April 14, 2026**; full papers **April 21, 2026**;
  rebuttal window **June 4-9, 2026**; notifications **July 9, 2026**. As of the 2026-07-09
  access date the paper and rebuttal deadlines have **passed** and Main Track notifications are
  **being issued today** — the pack's live phase is decision/camera-ready, not submission.
- **Reproducibility Track deadlines (AoE):** abstracts **April 28, 2026**; papers **May 5,
  2026**; notifications **July 9, 2026**; camera-ready **July 27, 2026**.
- **Industry Track deadlines (AoE):** submission site open March 2, 2026; papers **May 21,
  2026**; notifications **July 9, 2026**; camera-ready **July 27, 2026**.
- **R&P Notes and Demos** deadlines are **July 15, 2026** (AoE) — still open on the access
  date; R&P Notes are presented as posters.
- **Page budget:** long papers **8 content pages**, short papers and "Past, Present and Future"
  papers **4 content pages**, using the **ACM two-column template**; **references are excluded**
  from the count and **appendices count inside** it. (2026 numbers; verify each cycle.)
- **Review model:** mutually anonymous (double-blind); each submission is read by **at least
  three PC members** and overseen by a **Senior PC member**; there is an author **rebuttal**
  phase.
- **Reproducibility posture:** RecSys runs a dedicated **Reproducibility Track**; authors are
  encouraged to include a link to an **anonymous repository** for code, data, and
  pre-registered studies rather than uploading separate supplementary files, and reviewers are
  not obliged to open auxiliary material.
- RecSys **removed the Late-Breaking Results (LBR)** poster track for 2026 and introduced
  **Research & Practice (R&P) Notes** in its place.
- RecSys proceedings are published in the **ACM Digital Library**.

## Organizers and fee model (verified 2026-07-09)

- RecSys is an ACM conference run by a per-edition organizing committee under the RecSys
  Steering Committee. Like every CS/AI conference in this collection, it has **NO standing
  editor-in-chief**; the rotating analogue is the per-edition **General Chairs** and **Program
  Chairs**. RecSys 2026 General Chairs were **Joseph A. Konstan, George Karypis, and Gediminas
  Adomavicius**; Program Chairs were **Minmin Chen, Bart Goethals, and Martijn Willemsen**.
  These appointments rotate every cycle — re-check the current committees page before relying
  on a name.
- There is **no article-processing charge (APC)**. RecSys funds the conference through
  registration fees; at least one author must register and present. Accepted papers appear in
  the **ACM Digital Library** under the ACM's publication and open-access terms (exact
  open-access mechanics are cycle- and agreement-dependent: 待核实 against the current
  camera-ready and ACM rights pages).
