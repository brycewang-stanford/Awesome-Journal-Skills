# Psychological Science Exemplars Library (method × topic)

> **Verified via web search, access date 2026-06-08.** Every paper below was checked to confirm it
> actually appeared in **_Psychological Science_** (Association for Psychological Science / SAGE; article
> pages on `journals.sagepub.com`, journal code `pss`/`pssa`), with year and volume(issue):pages. Papers
> that could not be cleanly verified as _Psychological Science_ — including ones often **confused** with
> it — were **omitted** and documented below. It is deliberately a short, clean list (6 verified) rather
> than a long, uncertain one.
>
> **Sibling-confusion guard.** _Psychological Science_ is **not** any of: _Journal of Personality and
> Social Psychology_ (JPSP), _Psychological Bulletin_, _Journal of Experimental Psychology_ (and its
> series, e.g. JEP:LMC), _Nature Human Behaviour_, or _Perspectives on Psychological Science_ (the APS
> review/replication sibling that carries Registered Replication Reports). Several famous "psychology"
> findings live in _Science_, _Nature_, or _Developmental Psychology_ — not here. Each row below was
> checked against this list.
>
> **Use principle (zero hallucination):** this file gives **design positioning only**. It does not
> reproduce or invent effect sizes, sample sizes, or specific findings — read the original on SAGE / the
> authors' pages before citing any number. For venue policy, scope, and format facts, see
> [`../official-source-map.md`](../official-source-map.md).

## How to use this library

Pick the row whose **method × question** is closest to yours, then study how that paper makes a finding
that is **important, robust, and broadly relevant** carry in a **short, broad-interest** format — the
_Psychological Science_ bar (see
[`../../skills/psci-topic-selection/SKILL.md`](../../skills/psci-topic-selection/SKILL.md) and
[`../../skills/psci-writing-style/SKILL.md`](../../skills/psci-writing-style/SKILL.md)). For each, ask the
self-check question before claiming your paper is "_Psychological Science_-shaped." Note that
**experiments and naturalistic/observational designs dominate** this venue; the vendored econometric
[`../code/`](../code/) kit is **supporting infrastructure** for the occasional quasi-experimental or
panel component, not the center of a typical submission.

## By method

### Metascience / methods that reframed how the field works (simulation + demonstration experiments)

- **Simmons, Nelson & Simonsohn, "False-Positive Psychology: Undisclosed Flexibility in Data Collection
  and Analysis Allows Presenting Anything as Significant," _Psychological Science_ 2011, 22(11):1359–1366.**
  Verified: `journals.sagepub.com/doi/abs/10.1177/0956797611417632` (DOI 10.1177/0956797611417632).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a short paper whose single, plainly stated point — undisclosed
    researcher degrees of freedom inflate false positives — reorganized methods norms across psychology;
    the prototype of "small format, field-wide lesson," and the intellectual root of this pack's
    preregistration/transparency emphasis ([`psci-study-design`](../../skills/psci-study-design/SKILL.md),
    [`psci-open-science-and-transparency`](../../skills/psci-open-science-and-transparency/SKILL.md)).
  - **Self-check:** does your contribution change how readers _do_ or _trust_ research, not just add one
    more effect?

### Naturalistic observation at scale (real-world behavior sampling)

- **Mehl, Vazire, Holleran & Clark, "Eavesdropping on Happiness: Well-Being Is Related to Having Less
  Small Talk and More Substantive Conversations," _Psychological Science_ 2010, 21(4):539–541.**
  Verified: `journals.sagepub.com/doi/full/10.1177/0956797610362675` (DOI 10.1177/0956797610362675).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** an unobtrusive real-world measurement method (the Electronically Activated
    Recorder) answers a broadly resonant question — what kind of talk tracks well-being — in a very short
    article; breadth and a memorable result carry a compact format.
  - **Self-check:** does a naturalistic method let you say something a wide audience cares about, without
    a sprawling narrative?

### Memory / cognition lab experiment with an evolutionary frame (within-subjects encoding paradigm)

- **Nairne, Pandeirada & Thompson, "Adaptive Memory: The Comparative Value of Survival Processing,"
  _Psychological Science_ 2008, 19(2):176–180.**
  Verified: `journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1111/j.1467-9280.2008.02064.x` (DOI 10.1111/j.1467-9280.2008.02064.x,
  journalCode `pssa`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a tightly controlled encoding manipulation delivers a counterintuitive,
    replicable advantage and ties it to a broad theoretical claim (memory "tuned" by fitness) — robustness
    plus breadth in a few pages.
  - **Note (sibling guard):** the closely related "survival processing _enhances retention_" paper is in
    _Journal of Experimental Psychology: LMC_ (2007), **not** _Psychological Science_ — cite the correct
    one.
  - **Self-check:** is your lab effect both well-controlled _and_ attached to a question outside your
    paradigm?

### Social-cognition / behavior experiment with a real-stakes outcome (manipulation → behavior)

- **Vohs & Schooler, "The Value of Believing in Free Will: Encouraging a Belief in Determinism Increases
  Cheating," _Psychological Science_ 2008, 19(1):49–54.**
  Verified: `journals.sagepub.com/doi/abs/10.1111/j.1467-9280.2008.02045.x` (DOI 10.1111/j.1467-9280.2008.02045.x).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a clean manipulation moves a consequential _behavior_ (cheating), with a
    stated mediator — the kind of vivid, broadly discussable causal claim the venue favors.
  - **Note (rigor caveat):** this is a much-debated, hard-to-replicate effect (see later registered
    replications in the APS sibling _Advances in Methods and Practices_). Study it for the design _arc_,
    not as settled evidence — exactly the robustness scrutiny
    [`psci-study-design`](../../skills/psci-study-design/SKILL.md) trains.
  - **Self-check:** does your manipulation move a behavior an outsider finds consequential, and is it
    powered/preregistered enough to survive replication scrutiny?

### Judgment / metacognition lab experiment (processing-fluency manipulation)

- **Alter & Oppenheimer, "Effects of Fluency on Psychological Distance and Mental Construal (or Why New
  York Is a Large City, but New York Is a Civilized Jungle)," _Psychological Science_ 2008, 19(2):161–167.**
  Verified: `journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1111/j.1467-9280.2008.02062.x` (DOI 10.1111/j.1467-9280.2008.02062.x).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** several small fluency manipulations converge on one abstract construct
    (psychological distance / construal level), showing how a short paper can build a general claim from
    a few crisp experiments — and how a memorable title carries breadth.
  - **Self-check:** do your manipulations triangulate one general mechanism, rather than one effect in one
    paradigm?

### Multimethod social experiment with physiological evidence (economic games + neurophysiology)

- **Hobson, Gino, Norton & Inzlicht, "When Novel Rituals Lead to Intergroup Bias: Evidence From Economic
  Games and Neurophysiology," _Psychological Science_ 2017, 28(6):733–750.**
  Verified: `journals.sagepub.com/doi/abs/10.1177/0956797617695099` (DOI 10.1177/0956797617695099).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** converging evidence (behavioral economic games + EEG/neurophysiology)
    raises confidence in a single causal story about how rituals create in-group bias — a model of using
    a second method to harden, not pad, the claim.
  - **Self-check:** does each additional method buy _inferential_ value (mechanism, convergence), in line
    with this pack's multi-study logic, rather than repeat the first?

## Omitted for lack of _Psychological Science_ placement (do not attribute without re-checking)

To keep the list zero-hallucination, these frequently-cited candidates were **excluded** after web
checking — several are classic sibling-journal confusions:

- **Strack, Martin & Stepper, facial-feedback "pen in mouth" (1988)** — original is in **_JPSP_**; the
  multilab Registered Replication Report is in **_Perspectives on Psychological Science_** (the APS
  sibling), **not** _Psychological Science_.
- **Klein et al., "Investigating Variation in Replicability: A 'Many Labs' Replication Project" (2014)**
  — published in **_Social Psychology_** (Hogrefe), **not** _Psychological Science_.
- **Killingsworth & Gilbert, "A Wandering Mind Is an Unhappy Mind" (2010)** — **_Science_**.
- **Dunn, Aknin & Norton, "Spending Money on Others Promotes Happiness" (2008)** — **_Science_**; their
  later "Prosocial Spending and Happiness" review is in **_Current Directions in Psychological Science_**,
  a sibling — neither is _Psychological Science_.
- **Hamlin, Wynn & Bloom, "Social Evaluation by Preverbal Infants" (2007)** — **_Nature_**.
- **Shoda, Mischel & Peake, preschool delay-of-gratification follow-up (1990)** — **_Developmental
  Psychology_**.
- **Simons & Chabris, "Gorillas in Our Midst" (1999)** — **_Perception_**; Shepard & Metzler mental
  rotation (1971) — **_Science_**.
- **Nairne, Thompson & Pandeirada, "Adaptive Memory: Survival Processing _Enhances Retention_" (2007)**
  — **_Journal of Experimental Psychology: LMC_**, distinct from the 2008 _Psychological Science_ paper
  listed above (same lab, different venue and title).

Before adding any new paper to this library, confirm it on `journals.sagepub.com` under journal code
`pss`/`pssa` (article page with volume/issue/pages) and update the access-date header. When in doubt,
omit. If web verification is unavailable, mark new rows **待核实 / TO VERIFY** and attach **no** journal
attribution until checked.
