# Psychological Science Official Source Map

Primary and authoritative sources behind the Psychological Science-specific facts injected into this
skill pack. Access date for all entries: **2026-06-01**. Re-verify volatile items (editor, word/format
rules, open-science requirements, accepted manuscript types) on the official page before relying on
them — journal policies change, and Psychological Science substantially revised its open-science
requirements effective 1 January 2024.

## Official submission & policy pages (APS)

- **https://www.psychologicalscience.org/publications/psychological_science/ps-submissions**
  — Psychological Science Submission Guidelines (APS). Verifies: **Research Articles** cap the
  **Introduction, Discussion, Footnotes, Acknowledgments, and Appendices at 2,000 words combined**;
  **Method and Results do not count** toward that limit (typically ≤ 2,500 words); a **150-word
  abstract** (excluded from the limit) that **identifies sample sizes, participant populations, and
  important limitations of the research design**; roughly **40 references** suffice for most articles;
  **APA 7th edition** style with **tables and figures embedded in the main text**; initial submissions
  are **anonymized**; manuscript types: **Research Articles, Registered Reports (Stage 1 / Stage 2),
  Registered Reports with Existing Data, Commentary (≤ 1,000 words), Reply to Commentary (≤ 1,000,
  by invitation)** — Short Reports and Preregistered Direct Replications are **no longer accepted**;
  submission via **Manuscript Central** at `mc.manuscriptcentral.com/psci`. Statistical/disclosure
  norms: **effect sizes** with **a measure of uncertainty (e.g., confidence intervals)** for major
  results; **sample-size justification** and a **formal power analysis if appropriate**; **full
  disclosure** of excluded observations and all method/analysis details needed to replicate; analysis
  scripts and data dictionaries.
- **https://www.psychologicalscience.org/publications/psychological_science/preregistration**
  — Preregistration of Research Plans (APS). Verifies: preregistration guidance; preregistration
  status is reported and its quality is **factored into editorial decisions**.
- **https://www.psychologicalscience.org/publications/badges** — Open Practice Badges (APS).
  Verifies: badges were introduced in **2014**; for submissions on/after **1 January 2024**, the badge
  program was replaced — **open data and materials are required** (with case-by-case exemptions) and a
  **Research Transparency Statement** is required.
- **https://www.psychologicalscience.org/publications/ms-structure-guidelines** — Manuscript
  Structure, Style, and Content Guidelines (APS). Verifies: structure/style detail, the **Research
  Transparency Statement** placed **between the Introduction and the Methods**, and persistent
  identifiers (DOIs) for shared data/materials.
- **https://journals.sagepub.com/author-instructions/pss** — Psychological Science author instructions
  on SAGE (publisher). (SAGE pages may return HTTP 403 to automated fetch; the APS pages above are the
  primary source for the facts in this pack.)

## Open-science requirements (effective 1 Jan 2024)

- **Open data and materials are required** at submission, with **case-by-case exemptions** that must
  be justified; **"limits on transparency will be a factor in editorial decisions."**
- A **Research Transparency Statement** is a **required separate section between the Introduction and
  Methods** for all empirical manuscripts; it reports availability of data, analysis scripts,
  materials, and preregistrations and is shared with editors and reviewers during peer review.
- **Persistent identifiers (DOIs)** to all shared materials and data.
- **Preregistration** quality is considered in editorial evaluation (the separate "preregistered"
  badge is discontinued).

## Owner, publisher, history

- **https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Psychological_Science** — Verifies: Psychological Science is the
  flagship journal of the **Association for Psychological Science (APS)**, published by **SAGE**;
  founded **1990**; among the highest-impact journals in psychology.
