## VERIFIED facts injected into the skills (with source #)

- **Identity & sponsor:** JIBS is the official (first) journal of the Academy of International Business (AIB), established 1970, published by Palgrave Macmillan (Springer Nature); ranked 1st in International Business. (#3, #7, #9)
- **Field focus:** the cross-border, multi-country, and multilevel study of MNEs, internationalization, cross-cultural management, international strategy/finance/economics, and global political economy; distinctively phenomenon-driven, with country and culture treated as levels of analysis. (#1, #3)
- **Length:** Articles ~10,000 words for strong/broad contributions, ~7,000 words for narrower contributions; word count is **inclusive of everything** (abstract, text, endnotes, references, tables, figures, appendices). Research Notes in principle under 4,000 words (inclusive). (#1, #2)
- **Abstract:** up to **200 words** (raised from the historical 100-word limit). (#1, #2)
- **Formatting:** follow the **JIBS Style Guide** — 11-point Times New Roman, double-spaced; author-date (name, year) in-text citation style. (#1, #2, #8)
- **Integrity screening:** iThenticate/CrossCheck plagiarism screening; format-compliance checks on the submission platform. (#1)
- **Review model:** double-blind peer review (authors and reviewers mutually anonymous), preceded by an editorial desk-screen for fit and contribution before papers go to reviewers. (#2, #4)
- **Turnaround (approximate):** ~5-day desk-reject and ~62-day first-decision targets reported in the EIC vision piece. (#4)
- **Methodological pluralism:** paradigmatically and methodologically pluralistic by policy (quantitative, qualitative, mixed, theory); rigor and a clear IB theoretical contribution are the gatekeepers. (#2, #4, #6)
- **CMV gatekeeping:** survey/same-respondent papers that appear to suffer from common method variance are routinely asked to run validity checks and resubmit, backed by a dedicated "From the Editors" CMV editorial. (#6)
- **Methods-editorial canon:** the AIB Research Methods SIG curates ~28 JIBS "From the Editors" methods editorials (2008–2022) covering endogeneity/dynamic endogeneity, multilevel models, interaction effects, QCA, replication/reproducibility, CMV, p-values, DART; these function as de facto methodological standards reviewers hold authors to. (#5)
- **Endogeneity norm:** "dynamic endogeneity" is a named, recurring rigor demand specific to internationalization-as-process designs, with its own editorial guidance. (#5, #6)
- **Data transparency:** the **JIBS Data Access and Research Transparency (DART)** policy applies; on acceptance, authors provide a **Data Accessibility Statement (DAS)** describing the extent to which data are available (and reasons for any limits); authors making data available are asked to deposit them in a reputable repository with a DOI. Authors must read the **JIBS Code of Ethics** before submitting. (#2)
- **Fees:** **no submission fee** (submission fees abolished); subscription-based publication with no mandatory APC. (#1, #9)
- **Editorial routing:** departmental / area-editor routing by IB subfield (e.g., economics-finance-accounting, strategy, organization & management, marketing & operations, entrepreneurship, political economy/public policy, culture-conflict-cognition, HR/IR) tied to AIB's scholarly structure. (#4, #9)
- **Society embedding:** as AIB's official journal, JIBS is tightly coupled to the AIB annual conference and paper-development workshops. (#3, #4)
