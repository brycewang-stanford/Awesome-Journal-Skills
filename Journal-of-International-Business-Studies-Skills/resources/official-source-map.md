## Submission guidelines, review & article types

- **https://link.springer.com/journal/41267/submission-guidelines**
  — JIBS submission guidelines. Verifies:
  - JIBS publishes insightful, innovative, and impactful international-business research.
  - Manuscripts are evaluated for quality, fit with the Statement of Editorial Policy, and
    contribution-to-length ratio.
  - Full articles making strong contributions should be about **10,000 words**; narrower contributions
    about **7,000 words**. Word count includes abstract, text, endnotes, and references; tables, figures,
    and online supplemental appendices can follow.
  - **Research Notes** should be **4,000-6,000 words including everything in the manuscript** and should
    have the same rigor, style, and tone as full-length articles.
  - Authors should not post submitted manuscripts, working papers, or prior drafts on easily discoverable
    websites where potential reviewers could find them.
  - Manuscripts involving human subjects should comply with the corresponding author's human-subjects
    protocol requirements.
  - Submit online through the JIBS submission tool at **mc.manuscriptcentral.com**; the Managing Editor
    contact listed is **Anne Hoekman**.
  - Title page uploaded separately; main manuscript starts with an anonymized abstract page.
  - Abstract is up to **200 words** and should not identify the authors.
  - Keywords: three to six keywords; first three required from the JIBS Keywords list.
  - Formatting: **one-inch margins**, **11-point Times Roman**, **double-spaced** body text including
    abstract, references, and appendices; pages numbered from the Abstract Page; one space between
    sentences; left-aligned text.
  - References follow JIBS author-date style, with alphabetical reference list and full page ranges.
  - Reporting: actual p-values and effect sizes are expected in hypothesis-testing work.
  - Review model: **double-blind review**, with reviewer evaluations shared with the JIBS Area Editor.
  - Statement of Editorial Policy: JIBS is multidisciplinary, interdisciplinary, and seeks cutting-edge
    work that makes more than an incremental contribution; suitable work has substantial international
    business content as a primary focus; JIBS is paradigmatically and methodologically pluralistic.
  - DART: authors must comply with the AIB Journals Code of Ethics, disclose prior use of data and how
    the manuscript adds to prior data-based work, provide supplementary information when requested, file a
    data-sharing "comply or explain" statement, write a **Data Accessibility Statement** on acceptance,
    and deposit fully available data in a reputable repository with a **DOI** where data are shared.
  - Subscription-route self-archiving has a **12-month embargo**; gold open access is optional on
    acceptance.
- **https://link.springer.com/journal/41267/how-to-publish-with-us**
  — "How to publish with us" page. Verifies: hybrid publishing model; submit link at
  **mc.manuscriptcentral.com**; open access is optional after acceptance; subscription route has **no
  APC charges**; current open-access APC is **GBP 3,090 / USD 4,990 / EUR 3,990**; CC BY is listed for
  open-access articles.

## Methods and editorial-standard sources

- **https://rmsig.aib.world/resources-2/jibs-methods-editorials/**
  — AIB Research Methods SIG JIBS methods-editorials page. Verifies the JIBS "From the Editors" methods
  canon curated by AIB RM SIG, including endogeneity/dynamic endogeneity, multilevel models, interaction
  effects, QCA, replication/reproducibility, common method variance, p-values, and DART.
- **https://link.springer.com/article/10.1057/s41267-020-00398-8**
  — JIBS methods piece on common method variance. Verifies JIBS-specific treatment of CMV and the norm
  that survey/same-respondent designs require validity checks and careful resubmission when CMV is a
  concern.

## Pack-level guardrails

- Use **mc.manuscriptcentral.com** as the live submission route unless the Springer/AIB page changes it.
- Treat **Rosalie L. Tung** as current Editor in Chief and **Anne Hoekman** as Managing Editor based on
  the live Springer journal home and editorial board (re-confirmed 2026-06-22; the Springer masthead
  still lists Tung). Note her originally announced term was 2023–2025, so re-verify the Editor in Chief
  on the Springer/AIB editorial-board page before submission in case of an editorial transition.
- Do not describe the full-article word count as including tables/figures/supplemental appendices; the
  current official wording includes abstract, text, endnotes, and references, while tables, figures, and
  online supplemental appendices can follow.
- Research Notes use the current official **4,000-6,000 words including everything** rule.
- Do not state a mandatory APC for subscription-route publication; the official OA APC applies only if
  authors choose open access after acceptance.
