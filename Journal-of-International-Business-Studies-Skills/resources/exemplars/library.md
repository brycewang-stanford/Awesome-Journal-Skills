## How to use this library

Pick the row whose **topic × method** is closest to yours, then study how that paper (a) names a
distinctively *international* phenomenon, (b) theorizes **country/culture as a level of analysis** rather
than a control, and (c) makes an explicit, non-incremental contribution to **IB theory** — the JIBS bar
(see [`../../skills/jibs-topic-selection/SKILL.md`](../../skills/jibs-topic-selection/SKILL.md),
[`../../skills/jibs-theory-development/SKILL.md`](../../skills/jibs-theory-development/SKILL.md), and
[`../../skills/jibs-contribution-framing/SKILL.md`](../../skills/jibs-contribution-framing/SKILL.md)). For
each, ask the self-check question before claiming your paper is "JIBS-shaped."

## By method

### Internationalization-as-process theory (conceptual / model-building)

- **Johanson & Vahlne, "The Uppsala internationalization process model revisited: From liability of
  foreignness to liability of outsidership," JIBS 2009, 40(9):1411–1431.**
  Verified: `link.springer.com/article/10.1057/jibs.2009.24` (2019 JIBS Decade Award).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** revisits a workhorse IB process theory and relocates the root of uncertainty
    from psychic distance to *outsidership* in business networks — a theory paper that reframes a
    conversation rather than gap-spots. The model is JIBS's signature dynamic, path-dependent mechanism.
  - **Self-check:** does your argument *qualify or extend a named IB theory*, or merely add the word
    "international" to a general-management mechanism (`jibs-theory-development`)?

### Emerging-market MNE theory (conceptual / perspective)

- **Luo & Tung, "International expansion of emerging market enterprises: A springboard perspective,"
  JIBS 2007, 38(4):481–498.**
  Verified: `link.springer.com/article/10.1057/palgrave.jibs.8400275`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** builds the "springboard" lens — EM MNEs internationalize to *acquire* the
    strategic assets and escape the home constraints their institutional context denies them — explicitly
    challenging classic OLI/process predictions for a new phenomenon.
  - **Self-check:** does your contribution *contrast with a prevailing IB expectation* (non-incrementality),
    not confirm a known relationship in a new country (`jibs-contribution-framing`)?

### National-culture → firm choice (archival, discrete-choice estimation)

- **Kogut & Singh, "The Effect of National Culture on the Choice of Entry Mode," JIBS 1988,
  19(3):411–432.**
  Verified: `link.springer.com/article/10.1057/palgrave.jibs.8490394`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** derives hypotheses a priori from cultural distance and uncertainty
    avoidance, then tests them with a **multinomial logit** on cross-border entries — the origin of the
    Kogut–Singh distance index and a template for theorizing culture as a level, then estimating a
    discrete cross-border choice. Pairs with the discrete-choice/archival branch of
    [`../code/`](../code/).
  - **Self-check:** is your distance construct *theorized and measured consistently*, with the country
    level doing real work — not a dummy control (`jibs-topic-selection`)?

### Cross-national distance measurement (construct-building, archival/multidimensional)

- **Berry, Guillén & Zhou, "An institutional approach to cross-national distance," JIBS 2010,
  41(9):1460–1480.**
  Verified: `link.springer.com/article/10.1057/jibs.2010.28`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** replaces a single culture-distance number with a **multidimensional**
    (economic, financial, political, administrative, cultural, demographic, knowledge, global-connectedness,
    geographic) measure of distance between countries — a methods-and-measurement contribution that
    directly answers the "distance as black-box covariate" anti-pattern.
  - **Self-check:** are you measuring *which* distance your mechanism actually invokes, and its
    **direction/asymmetry** (`jibs-theory-development`)?

### Cross-cultural values research (systematic review / measurement critique)

- **Kirkman, Lowe & Gibson, "A quarter century of Culture's Consequences: a review of empirical research
  incorporating Hofstede's cultural values framework," JIBS 2006, 37(3):285–320.**
  Verified: `link.springer.com/article/10.1057/palgrave.jibs.8400202` (2016 JIBS Decade Award).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a disciplined review that both organizes 25 years of Hofstede-based work and
    *critiques its measurement and level-of-analysis assumptions* — the model for engaging the cultural-
    values canon without essentializing nations.
  - **Self-check:** when you invoke cultural values, do you specify the **level and mechanism** rather than
    asserting a nation "is" a value (`jibs-writing-style`)?

### Methods editorial / identification standard (JIBS "From the Editors")

- **Reeb, Sakakibara & Mahmood, "From the Editors: Endogeneity in international business research,"
  JIBS 2012, 43(3):211–218.**
  Verified: `link.springer.com/article/10.1057/jibs.2011.60`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** the canonical JIBS FtE editorial reviewers treat as a de facto standard on
    endogeneity and approximating a randomized experiment — align your identification section with it and
    cite it, as [`../../skills/jibs-methods/SKILL.md`](../../skills/jibs-methods/SKILL.md) advises.
  - **Self-check:** does your design *explicitly* state how it approximates random assignment, rather than
    bolting on an endogeneity fix late?

## Omitted for lack of JIBS confirmation (do not attribute to JIBS without re-checking)

To keep the list zero-hallucination and to defuse sibling-confusion, the following landmark IB constructs
were **excluded** after checking — each is real and influential, but **not published in JIBS**:

- **Zaheer, "Overcoming the Liability of Foreignness" (1995)** — verified to be in the *Academy of
  Management Journal* 38(2):341–363, **not JIBS**. Listed here only as a guardrail (the construct is
  heavily *used* in JIBS, but the origin paper is AMJ).
- **Kostova & Zaheer, "Organizational Legitimacy Under Conditions of Complexity: The Case of the
  Multinational Enterprise" (1999)** — verified to be in the *Academy of Management Review* 24(1):64–81,
  **not JIBS**. Guardrail only.

Before adding any new paper to this library, confirm it on `link.springer.com` under a JIBS DOI
(`10.1057/...jibs...`) with volume/issue, and update the access-date header. When in doubt — especially
for papers that "feel" like IB but may sit in JWB, MIR, GSJ, SMJ, AMJ, AMR, or the *Journal of
International Economics* — **omit**.
