# Worked Example: A JIBS-Style Introduction (before → after)

This demonstrates the **JIBS introduction arc** distilled from this pack's own skills:
**phenomenon → cross-level IB puzzle → the live IB conversation it problematizes → design that supports a
cross-country inference → finding → explicit IB-theory contribution stated early → boundary conditions and
(theory / practice / societal) implications.** JIBS is **phenomenon-driven**: the first page must make the
reader feel a concrete cross-border problem and know, explicitly, what the field learns about *international*
business that no single-country study could show (`jibs-writing-style`, `jibs-topic-selection`).

**Illustrative paper (fictional):** *"Reaching Across the Void: How Home-Country Institutional Weakness
Shapes Where Emerging-Market Firms Place Their First Foreign R&D Unit."* Fictional setting: a panel of
manufacturing firms headquartered across a set of emerging economies, observed as they establish first
foreign R&D subsidiaries in advanced and other emerging host countries.

---

## Before (buries the phenomenon and the IB contribution — typical first-draft intro)

> The relationship between institutions and firm strategy has received considerable attention in the
> management literature. While many studies examine domestic firms, comparatively little research has
> examined R&D location in emerging markets, leaving a gap in our understanding. In this paper, we use a
> dynamic-panel GMM estimator on a multi-country firm dataset to study the determinants of foreign R&D
> placement. We include cultural distance and institutional quality as controls. Country X is a
> collectivist, high-uncertainty-avoidance context, which may matter for innovation. This is an important
> topic for managers and policymakers. Section 2 reviews the literature, Section 3 describes the data,
> Section 4 presents the method, Section 5 reports results, and Section 6 concludes.

**What's wrong (against this pack's skills):**

- **Gap-spotting, not a phenomenon.** "Little research has examined… leaving a gap" is the named
  anti-pattern in `jibs-topic-selection` and `jibs-literature-positioning`; there is no concrete
  cross-border puzzle and no IB conversation joined.
- **Leads with method** ("we use a dynamic-panel GMM estimator") instead of the phenomenon — the
  method-first open `jibs-writing-style` warns against.
- **Country/culture as a control, not a level.** Distance and institutional quality are dumped in as
  covariates, violating the `jibs-topic-selection` level-of-analysis gate and `jibs-theory-development`'s
  demand to theorize the cross-level mechanism.
- **Essentializing.** "Country X is a collectivist… context" anthropomorphizes a nation
  (`jibs-writing-style`, `jibs-theory-development` anti-pattern) instead of naming the level and the
  micro mechanism.
- **No explicit IB-theory contribution on page one**, and no statement of why a single-country study
  would not answer the question — the core `jibs-contribution-framing` bar.
- **Over-signposted roadmap** doing the work the argument should do.

---

## After (JIBS arc — phenomenon-forward, country/culture as a level, IB contribution early)

> **When a firm grows up amid weak home-country institutions, does that weakness push its first foreign
> R&D unit *toward* institutionally strong host countries — or hold it back?** Tracking manufacturing
> firms from several emerging economies as they establish their first foreign R&D subsidiary, we find that
> firms from **weaker home rule-of-law contexts are 18 percentage points more likely** to place that first
> unit in an institutionally strong host, and that the pull is **strongest when home–host institutional
> distance is large and asymmetric**. *(phenomenon + headline finding + the cross-level, cross-country
> hook, in the first breath — units included.)*
>
> This is a distinctively *international* puzzle: the decision exists only because home and host
> institutional environments differ, and it cannot be observed in any single-country sample.
> Internationalization-process accounts expect firms to move first into *near*, familiar environments, yet
> the springboard view of emerging-market multinationals expects exactly the opposite — that firms reach
> *toward* strong institutions to acquire what their home environment denies them. *(problematizes a live
> IB conversation with named, opposed camps — `jibs-literature-positioning`.)*
>
> We theorize the mechanism **across levels**: a country-level condition (home institutional weakness)
> operates through a firm-level process (the search for an external locus of credible property-rights and
> talent protection for knowledge work), shaping a subsidiary-level outcome (where the first R&D unit
> lands). Treating institutional **distance as directional and asymmetric** rather than as a black-box
> covariate, we derive a priori that the reaching-toward effect intensifies with the home–host gap and
> attenuates once a firm has prior advanced-economy operating experience. *(country/culture-as-level
> mechanism with a boundary condition — `jibs-theory-development`.)*
>
> Our design supports a genuinely cross-country inference: a longitudinal multi-country firm panel, with
> home institutional quality measured at the country level and identification that confronts dynamic
> endogeneity — past internationalization, performance, and unobserved firm heterogeneity co-evolve — via
> a dynamic-panel strategy and a home-institutional-reform shock, rather than reading causation off a
> single cross-section. *(design matched to the claim; the JIBS endogeneity demand met up front —
> `jibs-methods`.)*
>
> Our contribution is to **qualify the internationalization-process account and extend the springboard
> view**: the first foreign R&D step is driven less by minimizing psychic distance than by *reaching
> across an institutional void* to source what the home context cannot supply — and crucially, this
> reaching is conditional on the **direction and asymmetry** of institutional distance, not its mere
> magnitude. *(explicit IB-theory contribution, stated early and against a specific prior view —
> `jibs-contribution-framing`.)* No single-country study could establish this, because the effect is
> defined by home–host institutional difference. The finding travels to knowledge-intensive
> emerging-market firms but, we are careful to note, **not necessarily** to resource-seeking or
> market-seeking entry, nor beyond the sampled economies. *(boundary conditions and responsible scope —
> not over-generalized.)* Beyond theory, it reframes how emerging-market firms — and the home governments
> that worry about R&D "flight" — should read first foreign R&D placement: as institutional arbitrage that
> can build, rather than drain, home innovative capability. *(practice and societal-impact implication, in
> line with JIBS's mission.)*
>
> The paper proceeds as follows. Section 2 develops the cross-level theory and hypotheses; Section 3
> describes the multi-country panel and the identification strategy; Section 4 reports the results and
> boundary conditions. *(brief roadmap — no over-signposting.)*

## Why the "after" meets the JIBS bar

Mapped to this pack's skill checklists:

- **Phenomenon-forward, IB contribution early** — the first paragraph names a concrete cross-border
  decision and its headline finding (18 pp; conditional on distance asymmetry), and the contribution is
  stated explicitly before the data (`jibs-writing-style`, `jibs-contribution-framing`).
- **Country/culture as a level of analysis, not a control** — home institutional weakness is theorized as
  a country-level condition operating through a firm-level process onto a subsidiary-level outcome,
  clearing the `jibs-topic-selection` level-of-analysis gate.
- **Distance theorized as directional and asymmetric** — the mechanism turns on the *direction* of the
  home–host gap, not a black-box covariate, exactly as `jibs-theory-development` demands.
- **Problematizes a live IB conversation** — process/Uppsala-style "near first" vs. springboard "reach for
  strong institutions," with named camps and a stated intervention (`jibs-literature-positioning`), rather
  than gap-spotting an "understudied context."
- **Design supports the cross-country claim** — a longitudinal multi-country panel with an explicit
  dynamic-endogeneity strategy, the rigor demand `jibs-methods` flags reviewers apply most insistently.
- **No essentializing** — no nation "is collectivist"; the macro condition is named at the country level
  and translated into a micro mechanism (`jibs-writing-style` anti-pattern avoided).
- **Why-not-single-country and responsible scope** — the paper states why no single-country study could
  show this and bounds generalization to the sampled, knowledge-intensive context
  (`jibs-contribution-framing`).
- **Triple implications** — theory (qualify process / extend springboard), practice (how firms read R&D
  placement), and societal/policy (home-government R&D-flight concern), per JIBS's mission.
