# GCB Official Source Map

Primary and authoritative sources behind the *Global Change Biology* (GCB) facts injected into this
skill pack. Access date for this refresh: **2026-06-20**. Wiley pages may require browser
verification for some users, so volatile operational details should be checked on the live official
pages immediately before submission.

## Article Types, Length, and Required Elements

- The GCB Author Guidelines public official snippet lists **Research Articles, Technical Advances,
  Reviews, GCB Reviews, Mini Reviews, Opinions, and Perspectives**. The pack routes original
  empirical/model papers as Research Articles, method/tool papers as Technical Advances, syntheses as
  Reviews/GCB Reviews/Mini Reviews where appropriate, and argued forward-looking pieces as Opinions or
  Perspectives.
- Current official search snippets for the GCB Author Guidelines state that **Research Articles are up
  to 8000 words**. Treat older high-word-count third-party guide renderings as stale for this pack
  unless the live Wiley page says otherwise.
- The official 2024 initial submission checklist snippet states **abstract - 300 word limit** and
  **keywords - 6-10 words or phrases**. The pack uses these values while still prompting a live check
  before upload because Wiley author-guideline wording can change.
- The Author Guidelines snippets verify that a **graphical abstract is required**. The pack interprets
  this as a driver-to-biological-response mechanism exhibit, not a site map or phylogeny standing in
  for mechanism.

  editorial board. Editor-in-Chief re-verified **2026-06-22** as **Prof. Danielle Way** (appointed 2025,

- The pack should describe GCB review as editor-mediated expert peer review and a selective desk screen
  for scope, broad relevance, mechanism, uncertainty, and reproducibility. Live-check the current
  anonymity / transparent-review options on the official author-guidelines page rather than asserting a
  fixed review model.

## Research Data, Code, and Open-Data Policy

- The GCB Author Guidelines public official snippets verify that supporting code/software/data should
  be archived in an appropriate public repository such as **Zenodo** with a persistent identifier such
  as a **DOI**, and that this information must be included in the manuscript. This pack treats data
  availability statements and DOI-stable deposits as core submission work, with sensitive or licensed
  data handled through a stated restriction plus access route.
- **https://datadryad.org/**, **https://zenodo.org/**, and **https://www.pangaea.de/** — DOI-capable
  repositories commonly suitable for ecological, code, and Earth/environmental data deposits. Use the
  live Wiley page for the current accepted-repository wording.
