# Field Crops Research Official Source Map

Primary and authoritative sources behind the *Field Crops Research* (FCR)-specific facts injected into
this skill pack. Access date for this refresh: **2026-06-20**. ScienceDirect and Elsevier pages are the
live authority for upload rules, so check them immediately before submission for article-type
availability, file prompts, editor names, and price changes.

## Official Journal, Scope, and Fit Boundary

- **https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/field-crops-research** — ScienceDirect journal home. Verifies
  publisher **Elsevier**, Print ISSN **0378-4290**, Online ISSN **1872-6852**, open-access support,
  linked datasets route, current metrics shown by ScienceDirect, and the journal's aims-and-scope
  summary.
- **https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/field-crops-research/publish/guide-for-authors** — Guide for
  Authors. Verifies the detailed scope and presentation standard used by this pack: FCR publishes
  original experimental and modelling research plus meta-analysis of published data; articles must
  demonstrate new scientific insights, original technologies, or novel methods at crop, field, farm, or
  landscape levels; the focus is crop ecology, crop physiology, agronomy, and crop improvement of field
  crops for food, fibre, feed, and biofuel; yield data are encouraged to show how field experiments
  improve understanding of biophysical processes related to crop growth, development, and yield.
- **Guide for Authors, Aims and scope** — Verifies the out-of-scope boundaries central to this pack:
  corroborative, descriptive, or only locally significant work; studies exclusively under controlled
  environment conditions such as greenhouse, pot, or systems constricting root growth; natural
  grasslands; horticultural, woody perennial, medicinal, and non-cultivated species; one-year field
  studies in one location or environment; crop storage, transportation, usage, and social studies on
  crops and cropping systems.

## Study Design, Article Types, and Review Process

- **Guide for Authors, Scientific and presentation standard** — Verifies that objectives should flow
  from a complete, brief, unbiased, updated literature review; experimental design must match
  objectives; field experiments must be repeated in **at least two seasons or locations**; key agronomic
  practices and environmental conditions including soil and weather must be detailed; weather should be
  shown in relation to crop phenology; data must be analysed with statistics appropriate to the design;
  and the discussion should not repeat results but place findings in agronomic context with conclusions
  justified by data.
- **Guide for Authors, Article types** — Verifies current article types: **Original Research Papers**,
  **Review Articles**, **Short Communications**, **Opinion Papers**, and **Loomis Reviews**. Review
  Articles are normally by invitation, but prospective authors are encouraged to send a proposal and
  outline to the Editors-in-Chief. Short Communications should not exceed **6 printed pages** including
  tables, figures, and references; Opinion Papers typically should not exceed **4 printed pages**;
  Loomis Reviews are invitation-only.
- **Guide for Authors, Peer review** — Verifies **single anonymized** peer review: editors first assess
  suitability, suitable submissions typically go to a minimum of **two reviewers**, and editors make the
  accept/reject decision.
- **https://submit.elsevier.com/FIELD** — Elsevier Editorial Manager submission route linked from the
  ScienceDirect journal and guide pages.

## Formatting, Declarations, Data, and References

- **Guide for Authors, Declaration of generative AI use** — Verifies that authors must declare use of
  generative AI or AI-assisted tools in manuscript preparation at first submission; the declaration goes
  before references; basic grammar, spelling, and reference-checking tools are exempt when there is
  nothing to disclose; AI tools must not be listed or cited as authors.
- **Guide for Authors, Abstract / Highlights / Keywords / Writing and formatting** — Verifies
  operational writing checks used by this pack: **abstract up to 400 words**; **3 to 5 highlights**,
  each **no more than 85 characters**; highlights are mandatory for the journal; keywords and standard
  Elsevier article structure are required on submission.
- **Guide for Authors, Research data / Data statement / Data linking / Research Elements** — Verifies
  that FCR follows Elsevier's **Research Data Policy, Policy Type 3 (Option C)**; authors must state
  data availability at submission, even if data are unavailable or unsuitable to post; shared datasets
  can be linked to the article, and related data, methods, or protocol papers may be routed to
  **Data in Brief** or **MethodsX**.
- **Guide for Authors, Data references / Reference examples** — Verifies that data and software can be
  cited in the reference list with dataset/software labels, repository, version where available, year,
  and global persistent identifier.
- **https://www.elsevier.com/about/policies-and-standards/research-data** — Elsevier research-data
  policy. Verifies the publisher-level principles that data should be made available where possible,
  authors should retain control of access, data availability statements improve transparency, permanent
  identifiers support linking, and proper data citation should give credit.
- **https://data.mendeley.com/journal/03784290** — Mendeley Data collection for FCR. Verifies a linked
  dataset route for FCR articles and gives examples of open datasets associated with FCR publications.

## Pack-Level Guardrails

- Do not route greenhouse-, pot-, or root-constriction-only experiments to FCR unless the manuscript
  has a field/crop-level scaling argument that satisfies the Guide for Authors.
- Do not treat a one-year, one-location trial as submission-ready; FCR states that field experiments
  must be repeated in at least two seasons or locations.
- Do not over-route descriptive cultivar, input, or management comparisons. The contribution must be
  new scientific insight, original technology, or a novel method with general application to field crops.
- Do not label FCR as double-anonymous; the Guide for Authors states single-anonymized peer review.
- Do not describe APC as mandatory for all authors. APC applies when authors choose the open-access
  publication route; subscription publication has no open-access publication fee.
