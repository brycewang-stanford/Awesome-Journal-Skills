# Field Crops Research Official Source Map

Primary and authoritative sources behind the FCR-specific facts injected into this skill pack.
Access date for all entries: **2026-06-02**. Re-verify volatile items (editors-in-chief and term,
exact abstract/word limits, article-type list, fee/APC, data-policy wording, accepted-scope wording)
on the official page before relying on them — journal pages change. The official guide on
ScienceDirect (`sciencedirect.com`) returned **HTTP 403** to automated fetching at access time, so
several items below were verified from search renderings of the same Elsevier guide; those are
flagged as **待核实** in the section at the end.

## Official journal, scope & author pages

- **https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/field-crops-research** — Field Crops Research on
  ScienceDirect (publisher home, current issues, aims & scope). Verifies: publisher = **Elsevier**;
  **ISSN 0378-4290**; scope = experimental and modelling research on **crop ecology, crop physiology,
  agronomy, and crop improvement of field crops** grown for **food, fibre, feed, and biofuel**, at the
  **crop, field, farm, and landscape levels**; inclusion of **yield data is encouraged**.
- **https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/field-crops-research/publish/guide-for-authors** — FCR
  Guide for Authors (Elsevier). Verifies (via search renderings; live page 403 to automated fetch):
  submission via **Editorial Manager**; field experiments should, **unless exceptional circumstances
  apply, include at least two seasons and/or multiple locations/environments**; manuscripts must
  demonstrate **new scientific insight, original technologies, or novel methods of general application
  and relevance to field crops**; **abstract ≤ 400 words**; **highlights = 3–5 bullet points, each ≤ 85
  characters including spaces**; authors must **declare any use of generative AI** in manuscript
  preparation; **research-data availability must be stated at submission**.
- **https://www.journals.elsevier.com/field-crops-research** — Elsevier journal landing page
  (editorial board entry point).

## Scope boundary (what FCR does NOT consider)

- **https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/field-crops-research** (aims & scope) — Verifies the
  exclusions central to this pack: studies carried out **exclusively under controlled conditions**
  (greenhouse, pots, or **any system that constricts root growth**) are **out of scope**; so are
  **horticultural (vegetable and fruit), woody-perennial, medicinal, and non-cultivated species**, and
  **natural grasslands**; research that is **corroborative, descriptive, or of only local
  significance** is **not considered**; and **one-year studies in a single location/environment** do
  not meet the multi-season/-environment expectation.

## Article types & peer review

- **https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/field-crops-research/publish/guide-for-authors** — Verifies
  (via search renderings): article types include **Original Research Papers**, **Review Articles**
  (normally by invitation; proposals to the Editors-in-Chief encouraged), **Short Communications**
  (concise, complete, focused), **Opinion Papers**, and **Loomis Reviews** (by invitation); the
  journal follows a **single anonymized** review process; suitable submissions are typically sent to a
  **minimum of two reviewers**; related data/methods co-submissions can be forwarded to **Data in
  Brief** / **MethodsX**.

## Research data policy

- **https://www.elsevier.com/about/policies-and-standards/research-data** — Elsevier Research Data
  policy. Verifies the journal-level expectation that authors **state data availability at
  submission**; where data cannot be shared (sensitive/confidential), authors **state the reason**, and
  the statement appears with the published article on ScienceDirect.
- **https://data.mendeley.com/journal/03784290** — Field Crops Research collection on **Mendeley
  Data**. Verifies: datasets linked to FCR articles are hosted openly on Mendeley Data under Creative
  Commons terms (no separate policy text on the page itself).

## Indexing & metrics
