## How to use this library

Pick the row whose **topic × method** is closest to yours, then study how that paper makes a
**field-based, multi-environment** result carry a **general** (not local/descriptive) agronomic lesson —
the FCR bar (see [`../../skills/fcr-topic-selection/SKILL.md`](../../skills/fcr-topic-selection/SKILL.md)
and [`../../skills/fcr-literature-positioning/SKILL.md`](../../skills/fcr-literature-positioning/SKILL.md)).
For each, ask the self-check question before claiming your paper is "FCR-shaped."

## By method

### Critical review that defines a field-wide method (yield-gap analysis)

- **van Ittersum, Cassman, Grassini, Wolf, Tittonell & Hochman, "Yield gap analysis with local to global
  relevance — A review," *Field Crops Research* 2013, 143:4–17.**
  Verified: DOI **10.1016/j.fcr.2012.09.009** → resolves to ScienceDirect `pii/S037842901200295X`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** sets out a **consistent, transferable framework** (actual vs. potential vs.
    water-limited yield; how to estimate each) that travels from a single field to a global atlas — the
    paradigm of an FCR contribution of *general* relevance rather than a local result.
  - **Self-check:** does your work define or apply a method whose logic an agronomist in **another crop
    and country** could reuse, not a result confined to your sites?

### Crop-model upscaling from sites to regions (modelling + spatial generalisation)

- **van Bussel, Grassini, Van Wart, Wolf, Claessens, Yang, Boogaard, de Groot, Saito, Cassman & Van
  Ittersum, "From field to atlas: Upscaling of location-specific yield gap estimates," *Field Crops
  Research* 2015, 177:98–108.**
  Verified: DOI **10.1016/j.fcr.2015.03.005** → resolves to ScienceDirect `pii/S0378429015000878`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** an explicit **protocol for scaling** location-specific, model-based yield
    gaps to national maps under contrasting data availability — exactly the "what does the model add
    beyond the trial sites?" move that `fcr-experimental-design` asks of modelling work.
  - **Self-check:** does your modelling **generalise beyond the calibration sites** with a stated,
    defensible upscaling logic — not just fit at the points you measured?

### Crop-model yield-gap benchmarking against farm data (simulation + on-farm)

- **Hochman, Gobbett, Horan & Navarro Garcia, "Data rich yield gap analysis of wheat in Australia,"
  *Field Crops Research* 2016, 197:97–106.**
  Verified: DOI **10.1016/j.fcr.2016.08.017** → resolves to ScienceDirect `pii/S0378429016302775`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** confronts simulated **water-limited yield potential** with large-scale
    actual farm yields to quantify a continent-wide yield gap — model and field evidence integrated, with
    a conclusion that is agronomic and general, not a single-site report.
  - **Self-check:** is your simulated potential **validated against independent field/farm data** before
    you draw a yield-gap conclusion?

### Multi-environment agronomy / nutrient balance (on-farm METs, mixed-model inference)

- **Tenorio, Eagle, McLellan, Cassman, et al. (incl. Sadras & Grassini), "Assessing variation in maize
  grain nitrogen concentration and its implications for estimating nitrogen balance in the US North
  Central region," *Field Crops Research* 2019, 240:185–193.**
  Verified: DOI **10.1016/j.fcr.2018.10.017** → resolves to ScienceDirect `pii/S0378429018315247`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** turns variation observed **across many environments** into a predictive,
    region-scale tool (grain-N concentration → grain-N removal / N balance), with uncertainty propagated
    and reliability stated at the climate-soil-domain level — the FCR norm of effect + uncertainty +
    agronomic meaning over bare significance (`fcr-data-analysis`).
  - **Self-check:** does your analysis report **magnitude and uncertainty** and aggregate to the level at
    which the agronomic claim is actually reliable, rather than over-claiming at the plot?

### Crop physiology across many environments (G×E via phenotypic plasticity)

- **Sadras, Reynolds, de la Vega, Petrie & Robinson, "Phenotypic plasticity of yield and phenology in
  wheat, sunflower and grapevine," *Field Crops Research* 2009, 110(3):242–250.**
  Verified: DOI **10.1016/j.fcr.2008.09.004** → resolves to ScienceDirect `pii/S0378429008001883`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a **physiological** framework for **genotype × environment** interaction —
    quantifying plasticity of yield and phenology with linear models across many environments and crops —
    showing FCR rewards mechanism that links a process to **yield formation** and generalises across the
    environment range, not a single-site agronomy result.
  - **Self-check:** does your G×E story expose a **transferable mechanism** (here, plasticity of yield vs.
    phenology) rather than just reporting that ranks changed across your sites?
