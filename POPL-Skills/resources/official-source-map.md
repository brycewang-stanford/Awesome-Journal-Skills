## Verified facts used in the skills

- POPL 2027 is the 54th ACM SIGPLAN Symposium on Principles of Programming Languages,
  January 10-16, 2027, at the Hilton Mexico City Reforma, Mexico City — sponsored by
  SIGPLAN in cooperation with SIGACT and SIGLOG, with CPP among the co-hosted events.
- The POPL 2027 timetable: one submission deadline, July 9, 2026 at 11:59 PM AoE,
  with **no abstract deadline**; acceptance notification October 5, 2026; conference
  in January 2027. The deadline sits exactly one day after this pack's check date.
- Submission runs on HotCRP at `popl27.hotcrp.com`, not OpenReview.
- Format: no more than **25 pages of text excluding bibliography**, in the PACMPL
  layout — acmart with the acmsmall option, single column, 10 pt font, 12 pt line
  spacing, text block 5.478 in wide by 7.884 in tall. A different format (smaller
  fonts, larger text block) is grounds for **summary rejection**, and Word
  submissions are not supported.
- POPL 2027 uses **full double-blind** reviewing, continuing the POPL 2023-2026
  practice and explicitly departing from the *lightweight* double-blind process POPL
  used before 2023: reviewers never learn author identities until after
  conditional-acceptance decisions, and then only for the conditionally accepted
  papers.
- The review cycle includes an **optional, multi-day author-response period**;
  responses must be concise and address specific points raised in the reviews.
- Decisions are **conditional acceptances**: authors must deliver a satisfactory
  revision to the Review Committee by the requested deadline or risk rejection —
  POPL's version of a shepherded second phase.
- Since POPL 2018 era, accepted papers appear as **PACMPL, Issue POPL** — a Gold Open
  Access journal issue, not a conference proceedings volume. Volume anchors: Vol 2 =
  POPL 2018, Vol 7 = POPL 2023, Vol 10 = POPL 2026 (published January 8, 2026, 2675
  pages). PACMPL's APC was 400 USD, with SIGPLAN covering authors who cannot pay,
  ACM Open institutions exempt, and ACM fully open access since January 1, 2026.
- Artifact evaluation is by **invitation to authors of conditionally accepted
  papers**. Badges: Artifacts Evaluated - Functional, Artifacts Evaluated - Reusable,
  and Artifacts Available (archival repository such as Zenodo or the ACM DL).
  **Mechanized proofs are first-class artifacts**: Coq/Isabelle proof libraries are
  the track's own examples of reusable artifacts, and reusable proof claims require
  completeness — no `admit` (Coq/Rocq) and no `sorry` (Lean/Isabelle).
- At most 10% of accepted papers are designated **Distinguished Papers** (relevance,
  originality, significance, clarity — per the POPL 2026 call).
- POPL 2026, the most recent completed edition, ran January 11-17, 2026 in Rennes,
  France (Le Couvent des Jacobins), with CPP 2026 on January 12-13 and workshops
  including PriSC and RocqPL; at least one POPL 2026 Distinguished Paper award is
  publicly attested (Moine, Westrick & Tassarotti).
- POPL has no standing editor-in-chief; PACMPL has an editorial board, but Issue POPL
  acceptance decisions come from that edition's POPL Review Committee, whose chairs
  rotate yearly.
