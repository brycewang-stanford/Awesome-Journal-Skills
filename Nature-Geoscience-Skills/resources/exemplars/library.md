# Nature Geoscience Exemplars Library (subfield × evidence type)

> **Design-positioning library, not a citation database.** Each entry is a **well-known Earth-system
> advance** used to illustrate the *kind* of result that clears the Nature Geoscience bar: a broad,
> cross-disciplinary advance grounded in quantitative evidence with quantified uncertainty. Citations are
> deliberately **coarse** (topic + lead author/collaboration, approximate era) because exact
> author/year/venue must be checked at the source.
>
> **Venue guard (read before citing):** landmark Earth-science results are split across
> ***Nature Geoscience***, ***Nature***, ***Science***, ***PNAS***, ***Nature Climate Change***, and
> community journals (***JGR***, ***GRL***, ***Climate of the Past***). Several advances below were
> published in *Nature* or *Science*, **not** Nature Geoscience. Do **not** attribute any of them to
> Nature Geoscience without confirming the article page on `nature.com/ngeo`. This library teaches
> *shape*, not provenance.
>
> **Zero-fabrication rule:** no measured values, DOIs, or exact dates are asserted here. Read the original
> before citing any number or the venue.

## By subfield × evidence type

### Solid Earth / tectonics — geodetic + seismic monitoring reveals a new process

- **Episodic tremor and slow-slip events at subduction zones (Cascadia; Rogers, Dragert and others,
  ~2000s).** Continuous GPS and seismic arrays revealed slow slip that is neither an ordinary earthquake
  nor steady creep, reshaping how subduction-zone hazard and stress transfer are understood.
  - **Why it exemplifies the bar:** a *newly identified process* with immediate relevance beyond
    seismology (hazard, fault mechanics, geodesy).
  - **Self-check:** does your monitoring reveal a qualitatively new process, or refine a known rate?

### Deep interior / seismology — tomography settles a first-order structural debate

- **Broad mantle plumes imaged rising from the core–mantle boundary (French, Romanowicz, ~2010s).**
  Full-waveform tomography resolved whether hotspots connect to deep, wide plumes — a decades-old
  controversy in geodynamics.
  - **Why it exemplifies the bar:** a *decisive constraint* on a long-standing, field-defining question.
  - **Self-check:** does your image or inversion resolve a debate the whole community follows?

### Paleoclimate / ice cores — a proxy record extends and reframes the carbon–climate link

- **The 800,000-year atmospheric CO2 record from EPICA Dome C (Lüthi and colleagues, ~2008).** Antarctic
  ice-core CO2 across eight glacial cycles tightened the observed coupling of CO2 and temperature over
  deep time.
  - **Why it exemplifies the bar:** a *dataset revealing a hidden long-term signal* bearing on a
    first-order question (carbon-cycle sensitivity).
  - **Self-check:** does your record extend the reach of a first-order constraint, with a stated age model
    and uncertainty?

### Ocean circulation — observations + fingerprinting bear on a first-order climate question

- **Evidence for a weakening Atlantic overturning circulation (RAPID array; Caesar, Rahmstorf and others,
  ~2007–2018).** Sustained mooring arrays and proxy/model fingerprints quantified AMOC variability and a
  possible long-term decline.
  - **Why it exemplifies the bar:** *quantitative evidence* on a mechanism with broad climate reach.
  - **Self-check:** is your circulation inference grounded in observations with quantified uncertainty,
    not model-only?

### Cryosphere / geodesy — satellite gravimetry quantifies ice-sheet mass loss

- **Greenland and Antarctic mass balance from GRACE and altimetry (Velicogna, Shepherd, the IMBIE team,
  ~2010s).** Independent satellite methods converged on accelerating ice loss and its sea-level
  contribution.
  - **Why it exemplifies the bar:** a *decisive, cross-method constraint* on sea level — maximal breadth.
  - **Self-check:** do independent methods converge, and is the uncertainty budget explicit?

### Hydrology — satellite gravimetry reveals global groundwater depletion

- **Groundwater depletion detected from space (GRACE; Rodell, Famiglietti and others, ~2000s–2010s).**
  Basin-scale water-storage trends exposed unsustainable extraction across major aquifers.
  - **Why it exemplifies the bar:** a *new observational capability* with societal and cross-disciplinary
    reach (water security, geodesy, climate).
  - **Self-check:** does your result generalise beyond one basin to a first-order global pattern?

### Biogeochemistry — observations document ocean deoxygenation

- **Expansion of oxygen-minimum zones in the tropical oceans (Stramma and colleagues, ~2008).**
  Long hydrographic records showed declining dissolved oxygen with implications for ecosystems and the
  carbon cycle.
  - **Why it exemplifies the bar:** a *large-scale trend* connecting physics, chemistry, and biology.
  - **Self-check:** does your trend rest on a long, quantified record rather than a single transect?

### Deep-time geobiology — an integrative hypothesis reorganises Earth history

- **The Snowball Earth hypothesis for Neoproterozoic glaciations (Hoffman, Schrag and others, ~1998).**
  Sedimentary, geochemical, and paleomagnetic evidence assembled a global glaciation framework.
  - **Why it exemplifies the bar:** an *organising principle* that unified disparate observations.
  - **Self-check:** does your synthesis reframe many separate facts into one mechanism?

### Atmospheric science / carbon cycle — a constraint on the terrestrial sink

- **Magnitude and variability of the land carbon sink (tropical and boreal forests; multiple groups,
  ~2000s–2010s).** Inventory, flux, and atmospheric-inversion evidence constrained where and how much
  carbon the biosphere takes up.
  - **Why it exemplifies the bar:** a *first-order constraint* on the global carbon budget.
  - **Self-check:** is your sink estimate reconciled across independent lines of evidence with uncertainty?

### Planetary science — a rover finds a habitable ancient environment on Mars

- **Evidence for an ancient habitable lake environment at Gale crater (Curiosity; Grotzinger and the MSL
  team, ~2010s).** Sedimentology and geochemistry from the rover established a former lacustrine setting.
  - **Why it exemplifies the bar:** a *decisive finding* on a question of the broadest interest
    (planetary habitability).
  - **Self-check:** does your planetary result rest on direct measurement, not inference alone?

## Before adding an entry

Confirm the article page on `nature.com/ngeo` (or the journal of record) that the paper is where you
think it is — many of the above appeared in *Nature* or *Science*, not Nature Geoscience. Keep the entry
coarse unless you have read the original; never assert a value, DOI, or venue you have not verified. When
in doubt, position by *shape* (subfield × evidence type × breadth), not by provenance.
