# Nature Geoscience — Manuscript Skeleton

> A Nature Geoscience Article is SHORT (main text ~3,000 words) and makes ONE broad-interest
> Earth-system advance. The reproducible protocol goes to online Methods (excluded from the word
> count); extended data goes to Supplementary Information. Verify current limits, content types,
> and the Reporting Summary requirement on the official author pages (Checked: 2026-07-16).

## Title

> Encode the ADVANCE, not just the topic or region. ≤ ~90 characters including spaces.
> [Concise, advance-bearing title]

> 1 Institution, City, Country

> * Corresponding author: email; ORCID for all authors
> (For DOUBLE-BLIND opt-in: keep author/affiliation info OUT of the manuscript file and enter it

## Abstract

> ≤ ~200 words, UNREFERENCED, intelligible to a non-specialist Earth scientist.
> Pattern: background / why it matters (1–3 sentences) → the gap or controversy
>          → "Here we show ..." (the advance + key number + uncertainty)
>          → mechanism / interpretation → broad implication.

[Abstract text — lead into the advance with "Here we show ..."]

## Main text (~3,000 words, excluding abstract, Methods, references, legends)

### Opening (the gate paragraphs)

> Within the first paragraph or two:
> 1. Earth-system context (non-specialist accessible)
> 2. The open question / controversy (a scientific problem, not a regional description)
> 3. What this study shows — the headline advance, with the key number
> 4. Why it matters across the field

> Divide with 3–4 topical subheadings that are brief but informative.
> State each result with its uncertainty. Ground model results in observations.
> Interpret; do not just transcribe figures. Point extended analyses to Methods / SI.

> Tie the advance to the first-order question or prior belief it changes.
> State the cross-disciplinary consequence. Keep claims calibrated to evidence and uncertainty;

# Supplementary Information (separate file)

> The main text MUST stand alone without this. Every item is cited from the main text or Methods.
> This holds EXTENDED material, not the reproducible protocol (which is in online Methods).

## Supplementary Figures / Tables
> Fig. S1, Table S1, ... — extended maps/sections, full ensembles, sensitivity tests, secondary proxies.

## Supplementary Notes
> Supporting derivations / lengthy algebra; additional discussion. Notation must match the main text.

# Cover letter (separate; NOT seen by reviewers)

> See `ngeo-cover-letter`. Core job: convince the in-house editor this is a broad-interest Earth-system
> advance (there is NO presubmission enquiry route).
> 1. Submitted to Nature Geoscience as an Article/Brief Communication; topic area.
> 2. Headline advance (1–2 sentences + key number).
> 3. Why it is an Earth-system advance (belief changed / new process / decisive constraint).
> 4. Why broad interest (named out-of-subfield audiences + concrete reason).
> 5. Grounding + quantified uncertainty; originality; prior preprint; suggested/excluded referees;
>    double-blind opt-in if chosen.
