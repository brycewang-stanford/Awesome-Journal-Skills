# Nature Geoscience Scope Fit (ngeo-scope-fit)

## When to trigger

- You suspect the result is solid but may be "regional," "incremental," or "specialist"
- You cannot articulate why an oceanographer should read your volcanology paper (or vice versa)
- A coauthor asks "Nature Geoscience or JGR?"
- The study is model-only, or observation-only, and you are unsure it clears the evidence bar
- Before investing in figures and prose, you want a go / no-go on venue

## The two-part gate (both must pass)

Nature Geoscience publishes across **all** of the Earth sciences — solid Earth (tectonics, volcanology, geochemistry, deep interior), surface processes and geomorphology, hydrology and the cryosphere, oceanography, atmospheric science and climate (past, present, future), biogeochemistry, and planetary science. The gate ANDs two tests:

1. **A genuine Earth-system advance** — the result changes a quantitative understanding of how the Earth (or another planet) works: a newly identified process, a decisive observational constraint, a mechanism that resolves a standing controversy, a proxy or dataset that reveals a previously hidden signal, or a model result that overturns an expected behavior.
2. **Broad, cross-disciplinary interest** — Earth scientists *outside* your subfield would want to know this. A result is broad if it reframes a debate a neighboring community follows, transfers a method across domains, or bears on a first-order question (climate sensitivity, carbon cycle, sea level, deep-time evolution, habitability).

A rigorous, careful, but locally-scoped or "next-increment" result can **pass technical review and still fail the gate**. That is the most common Nature Geoscience rejection — and it happens at the editor's desk, before review, not as a quality judgment but a venue judgment.

## The evidence bar (a Nat. Geosci. distinctive)

Beyond breadth, Nature Geoscience expects **quantitative evidence with quantified uncertainty**:

- Observation-driven claims need field, remote-sensing, or proxy data with error bars.
- Model-driven claims need **observational grounding** — a model result unanchored to data reads as speculation and is a classic desk-reject.
- Any headline number needs a stated uncertainty; "missing uncertainty quantification" is a named rejection pattern.

## Decision table

| Situation                                                                    | Verdict                                   |
|------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------|
| New process / mechanism + clear cross-subfield relevance + quantified data   | Nature Geoscience — strong fit            |
| Decisive constraint settling a first-order, widely-debated question          | Nature Geoscience — strong fit            |
| Novel proxy/dataset revealing a hidden global signal                         | Nature Geoscience — fit (frame the reach) |
| Solid regional case study, "next step" in an established local program       | JGR / community journal (subfield match)  |
| Thorough model intercomparison, specialist parameter sweeps                  | Climate Dynamics / GMD / community        |
| Sound result, broad topic, but sub-flagship novelty                          | *Communications Earth & Environment* / *Nature Communications* |
| Model-only projection with no observational anchor                           | Retarget or add grounding (likely desk-reject) |
| Confirmation of an expected result with no new mechanism                      | Community journal                          |

## How to stress-test the gate

- **The neighbor test**: name a specific Earth scientist in a *different* subfield and state, in one sentence, why they would read this. If you cannot, breadth is weak.
- **The "Here we show" test**: draft the abstract's headline sentence. If it reads "we present the first regional record of X," importance is likely incremental; if it reads "we show that process Y controls Z," it is an advance.
- **The displacement test**: what prior belief does this overturn, sharpen, or unlock? "Nothing, it extends the record" → specialist.
- **The grounding test**: is every headline claim tied to data with uncertainty (or a model validated against data)? If not, expect a desk-reject.

## Checklist

- [ ] The single headline advance is stated in one sentence
- [ ] The advance is a process/mechanism/constraint, not just a new local dataset
- [ ] Broad interest is named for a concrete out-of-subfield Earth-science audience
- [ ] Every headline claim is grounded in quantitative data or a data-validated model
- [ ] Uncertainty is quantified for the headline number
- [ ] You have honestly considered a community journal or *Communications Earth & Environment*
- [ ] If borderline, you have a broad-interest argument ready for the cover letter

## Anti-patterns

- Equating a *new regional record* with a broad advance — Nature Geoscience rewards mechanism and reach, not first-ness
- Claiming global relevance a local dataset cannot support
- Submitting a "big-journal-ized" version of a specialist study to dodge the interest gate
- A model-only result with no observational anchor and no uncertainty budget
- Overreaching climate/impact claims the analysis does not license

## Output format

```
【Headline advance】one sentence (process / mechanism / constraint)
【Evidence】data type (field / remote-sensing / proxy / model) + uncertainty stated?
【Broad interest】named out-of-subfield Earth-science audience + why they care
【Prior belief changed】...
【Verdict】Nat. Geosci. strong / with breadth framing / retarget community journal / retarget CEE-NatComms
【Next】ngeo-results-framing (if Nat. Geosci.) or retarget venue
```
