# External Tools - VLDB / PVLDB

Access date: 2026-07-08

PVLDB republishes its rules with every volume. Before using anything below, work out
which volume is currently accepting papers (Volume 20 on the access date) and open that
volume's own pages.

## What to check on the author side

- Which monthly cycle you are entering: abstract due the 25th of the prior month, CMT
  open from the 20th, full paper due the 1st at 5:00 PM Pacific Time.
- Your live submission count against the per-month and per-year caps before promising a
  co-author another submission.
- The volume-specific template and category page budget for the paper type you chose,
  plus the PDF/A and font-embedding rules at camera-ready time.
- Cover-page completeness under single-blind review: author names and affiliations
  belong on the paper — confirm the current wording rather than assuming.
- The artifact URL and availability statement if you want the ACM badge, and the
  Reproducibility Evaluation requirements if the paper is EA&B.
- Revision logistics if a revise verdict is possible on your timeline: the three-month
  window, the one-shot rule, and what the reviewers' required-changes list demands.

## Do not infer

- Do not carry Volume 19 dates, caps, categories, or cutoffs into Volume 20 advice;
  each volume's guidelines govern.
- Do not treat the 5:00 PM Pacific deadline as AoE — PVLDB's clock is stricter than
  most CS venues' midnight convention.
- Do not assume the June 15 conference-cutoff date generalizes across volumes.
- If two official pages disagree, prefer the newer volume's guidelines, record the
  conflict, and treat direct word from the PVLDB Editors-in-Chief as controlling.
