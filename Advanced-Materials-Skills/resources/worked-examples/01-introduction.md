# Worked Example: An Adv. Mater. Communication Opening (before → after)

In an Advanced Materials **Communication there is no separate abstract — the opening paragraph must
serve as the abstract** (see [`advmat-results-framing`](../../skills/advmat-results-framing/SKILL.md)).
This demonstrates the **Adv. Mater. opening arc** — *materials context → open problem/trade-off → the
central advance (with the key benchmarked metric) → why it matters broadly* — together with the
`advmat-scope-fit` two-part gate (**genuine advance AND broad impact**), the `advmat-writing-style`
rule to **de-hype and readable out-of-subfield**, and the `advmat-methods` demand for **multi-technique
proof and fair benchmarking**.

**Illustrative Communication (fictional):** *"A Strain-Engineered Layered Oxide Cathode with
Zero-Strain Multivalent Intercalation."* Fictional advance: an interlayer-expanded oxide whose lattice
barely changes during cycling, breaking the capacity-vs-cycle-life trade-off.

---

## Before (buries the advance; hype-stacked; no benchmarking; fails the gate on the page)

> In recent years, rechargeable batteries have attracted tremendous attention due to the ever-growing
> demand for energy storage. Many materials have been investigated as cathodes [1–20]. In this work, a
> novel layered oxide was successfully synthesized via a facile and cost-effective method. The material
> was characterized by XRD, which confirmed its layered structure. Electrochemical testing showed that
> the material exhibits remarkably enhanced capacity and significantly improved cycling performance
> compared with previous works, indicating great potential for next-generation energy storage
> applications.

**What's wrong (against the Adv. Mater. skills):**

- **Leads with generic background**, not the advance — the `advmat-results-framing` anti-pattern
  (opening buried under context; the Communication's abstract-in-paragraph-one is missing).
- **Hype-stacked** ("tremendous," "novel," "successfully," "facile," "remarkably," "significantly") —
  the named `advmat-writing-style` failure; adjectives replace a benchmarked, mechanistic claim.
- **Single-technique proof** ("XRD confirmed the layered structure") — fails `advmat-methods`
  triangulation; a reviewer will ask how the phase and the intercalation are really proven.
- **No number, no conditions, no benchmark** — "enhanced capacity" against unnamed "previous works" is
  exactly the unfair/vague comparison `advmat-methods` warns about.
- **No mechanism** — the structure→property→function chain is absent; there is no reason *why* it works,
  so an editor cannot see the advance, only a claim.
- **No broad-impact leg** — "great potential for applications" names no out-of-subfield beneficiary, so
  the `advmat-scope-fit` gate is not visible on the page.

---

## After (Adv. Mater. arc — advance first, mechanism, benchmarked, broad impact named)

> Layered oxide cathodes store more charge when they host multiple electrons per transition metal, but
> multivalent redox usually drives large, anisotropic lattice changes that crack the particles and shorten
> cycle life — the capacity-versus-durability trade-off that has limited high-energy cathodes.
> *(materials context → the open trade-off, in words any materials scientist grasps.)* Here we report a
> strain-engineered layered oxide in which pillaring cations expand the interlayer spacing so that
> multivalent intercalation proceeds with near-zero lattice strain. *(the central advance — a new material
> with a new mechanism — stated plainly, up front.)* The cathode delivers **235 mAh g⁻¹ at 1C with 94%
> capacity retention over 800 cycles**, measured on 12 cells (mean ± s.d.), outperforming the best
> reported oxides of this family under matched conditions (Table 1); *operando* X-ray diffraction shows a
> lattice-parameter change below **0.3%** across the full state of charge, and electron diffraction plus
> X-ray photoelectron spectroscopy confirm the pillared structure and the multivalent redox.
> *(the key benchmarked metric with conditions and statistics, plus the multi-technique proof.)* Because
> the advance comes from a *strain-decoupling design rule* rather than a specific chemistry, it transfers
> to other layered hosts and gives battery, catalysis, and intercalation-electronics researchers a route
> to high-capacity materials that do not self-destruct while cycling. *(why it matters broadly — named
> out-of-subfield beneficiaries and the design rule they can reuse.)*

---

## Why the "after" meets the Adv. Mater. bar

Mapped to the skill checklists:

- **One central advance, stated first** — "near-zero-strain multivalent intercalation from interlayer
  pillaring"; the title encodes it and the opening leads with it, satisfying `advmat-results-framing`
  (one primary advance; Communication opening paragraph serves as the abstract).
- **Structure → property → function chain is explicit** — pillared structure (structure) → <0.3% strain
  during redox (mechanism/property) → 235 mAh g⁻¹, 94% over 800 cycles (function), the `advmat-methods`
  chain closed end to end.
- **Clears the two-part `advmat-scope-fit` gate on the page:** *advance* = a new material + new mechanism
  (a design rule), not an optimized sample; *broad impact* = named neighbors (catalysis, intercalation
  electronics) plus the transferable rule. The design-rule test and the trade-off test both pass.
- **Multi-technique, benchmarked, statistical** — *operando* XRD + electron diffraction + XPS triangulate
  the claim, the metric carries conditions and n, and the comparison is under matched conditions with a
  cited table, meeting `advmat-methods`.
- **De-hyped, calibrated prose** — every hype adjective from the "before" is gone; numbers and mechanism
  carry the claim, per `advmat-writing-style`.
- **Out-of-subfield readable** — the trade-off is explained in plain terms before any electrochemical
  notation, meeting the broad-readability requirement.
