## How to use this library

Pick the row whose **material class × advance type** is closest to yours, then study how that paper
converts one result into an advance the whole materials community cares about — a new material family,
a transferable design rule, or a mechanism that reorganizes a field — and how it carries the
structure→property→function chain demanded by
[`../../skills/advmat-methods/SKILL.md`](../../skills/advmat-methods/SKILL.md). Note also the split
between primary research papers and invited review-type formats (Reviews, Progress Reports, the
25th-anniversary series): the review entries below were field-defining precisely because *Adv. Mater.*
commissions them from groups that own the topic — you do not cold-submit one
(see [`../../skills/advmat-scope-fit/SKILL.md`](../../skills/advmat-scope-fit/SKILL.md)).

## Verified Adv. Mater. papers, by material class × advance type

### 2D materials — a new material family from a transferable synthesis rule

- **Naguib, Kurtoglu, Presser, Lu, Niu, Heon, Hultman, Gogotsi & Barsoum, "Two-Dimensional Nanocrystals
  Produced by Exfoliation of Ti₃AlC₂," *Adv. Mater.* 2011, 23, 4248–4253.**
  Verified: DOI `10.1002/adma.201102306`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** the first MXene paper. Selectively etching the A layer out of a MAX phase
    is a *rule*, not a sample — it implied an entire family of 2D carbides/nitrides, which is exactly the
    "new material class + reusable synthesis principle" shape the scope-fit gate rewards.
  - **Self-check:** does your preparation generalize to a family, or does it produce one compound?

- **Naguib, Mochalin, Barsoum & Gogotsi, "25th Anniversary Article: MXenes: A New Family of
  Two-Dimensional Materials," *Adv. Mater.* 2014, 26, 992–1005.**
  Verified: DOI `10.1002/adma.201304138`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** three years after the discovery paper, the same journal hosts the
    field-consolidating review — a model of how *Adv. Mater.* curates a field it launched, and of the
    invited review/Progress Report culture.
  - **Self-check:** if your topic deserves a review, has the editor invited one — or are you actually
    holding a primary advance that should be a research paper?

### 1D nanostructures — the reference map for a growing field (invited review culture)

- **Xia, Yang, Sun, Wu, Mayers, Gates, Yin, Kim & Yan, "One-Dimensional Nanostructures: Synthesis,
  Characterization, and Applications," *Adv. Mater.* 2003, 15, 353–389.**
  Verified: DOI `10.1002/adma.200390087`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** organized nanowires/rods/belts/tubes into growth strategies others could
    execute; for years the on-ramp citation for the whole 1D field. Reviews at this level *organize* a
    field's thinking rather than enumerate papers.
  - **Self-check:** does your framing give readers categories they will reuse, or a list?

### Bioinspired surfaces — a concept communication built on a mechanism

- **Feng, Li, Li, Li, Zhang, Zhai, Song, Liu, Jiang & Zhu, "Super-Hydrophobic Surfaces: From Natural to
  Artificial," *Adv. Mater.* 2002, 14, 1857–1860.**
  Verified: DOI `10.1002/adma.200290020`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** four pages that tie the lotus effect to cooperating micro/nanoscale
    structure and hand the reader a design rule for artificial superwetting surfaces — concise,
    mechanism-anchored, and broad: the classic *Adv. Mater.* communication shape.
  - **Self-check:** can your central claim be stated as "structure X produces property Y because Z"?

### Carbon / porous architectures — a record property with a stated structural reason

- **Sun, Xu & Gao, "Multifunctional, Ultra-Flyweight, Synergistically Assembled Carbon Aerogels,"
  *Adv. Mater.* 2013, 25, 2554–2560.**
  Verified: DOI `10.1002/adma.201204576`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** an extreme-property material (ultralow density, wide-temperature
    elasticity) where the paper's story is the *synergy* between CNT ribs and graphene cell walls — the
    record is explained by an architecture principle, not presented as a bare number.
  - **Self-check:** if your headline is a record, does the paper say *which structural feature buys it*?

- **Lou, Archer & Yang, "Hollow Micro-/Nanostructures: Synthesis and Applications," *Adv. Mater.* 2008,
  20, 3987–4019.**
  Verified: DOI `10.1002/adma.200800854`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** turned scattered template tricks into a taxonomy (hard/sacrificial/soft/
    template-free) connected to applications (batteries, catalysis, sensing) — a synthesis-principle
    review that neighboring communities could act on.
  - **Self-check:** do your categories map methods to *when to use them*, not just what exists?

### Photophysics / soft matter — a mechanism that reorganizes molecular design

- **Mei, Hong, Lam, Qin, Tang & Tang, "Aggregation-Induced Emission: The Whole Is More Brilliant than
  the Parts," *Adv. Mater.* 2014, 26, 5429–5479.**
  Verified: DOI `10.1002/adma.201401356`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** consolidates AIE around a mechanistic picture (restricted intramolecular
    motion) that inverts the usual quenching intuition — a mechanism-first framing that converted a
    curiosity into a design platform for emissive materials.
  - **Self-check:** does your mechanism *change what molecules/materials people will design next*?

### Flexible electronics / devices — a device field defined through materials requirements

- **Hammock, Chortos, Tee, Tok & Bao, "25th Anniversary Article: The Evolution of Electronic Skin
  (E-Skin): A Brief History, Design Considerations, and Recent Progress," *Adv. Mater.* 2013, 25,
  5997–6038.**
  Verified: DOI `10.1002/adma.201302240`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** frames e-skin as a set of materials problems (stretchability, sensing,
    healing, power) rather than a gadget catalog — the device-level breadth *Adv. Mater.* expects when a
    paper claims application relevance.
  - **Self-check:** are your "application" claims backed by device-level requirements you actually meet?

## Venue-trap list (landmark materials papers that are NOT in *Adv. Mater.*)

Flagship materials advances are scattered across venues, and mis-attributing them is a credibility leak
in an introduction. Do **not** cite these as *Adv. Mater.* papers:

- **Graphene** — Novoselov, Geim *et al.*, "Electric Field Effect in Atomically Thin Carbon Films" is in
  ***Science*** (2004), not *Adv. Mater.*
- **MOF-5** — Li, Eddaoudi, O'Keeffe & Yaghi's exceptionally stable porous MOF paper is in ***Nature***
  (1999); the isoreticular IRMOF series is in ***Science*** (2002).
- **Halide-perovskite photovoltaics** — Kojima & Miyasaka's first perovskite-sensitized cell is in
  ***J. Am. Chem. Soc.*** (2009); the solid-state cell of Lee & Snaith is in ***Science*** (2012).
- **LiCoO₂ cathode** — Mizushima & Goodenough's paper is in ***Materials Research Bulletin*** (1980).
- **The bilayer OLED** — Tang & VanSlyke is in ***Applied Physics Letters*** (1987).
- **Monodisperse CdSe quantum dots** — Murray, Norris & Bawendi is in ***J. Am. Chem. Soc.*** (1993).

Before adding any paper to the verified list above, confirm it on
`advanced.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1002/adma...` (the article page must show *Advanced Materials*
with the DOI and byline) and update the access-date header. When the venue is in doubt — especially
between *Adv. Mater.*, *Advanced Functional Materials*, *Advanced Science*, *Small*, *Nature Materials*,
and *ACS Nano* — **omit rather than guess**.
