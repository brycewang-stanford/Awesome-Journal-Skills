# Advanced Materials — Manuscript Skeleton

> An Adv. Mater. paper is built around ONE central advance (new material / mechanism /
> property) and is budgeted against a typeset page limit that counts figures, schemes,
> tables, and text together. Choose the article type first (Communication vs. Research
> Article) — it sets the whole structure. Verify current limits and specs on the official
> Wiley Advanced Materials author page.

## Article type

> [ ] Communication (~4 typeset pages; opening paragraph = abstract; ~4 display items)
> [ ] Research Article (~10 typeset pages; short abstract + 3–7 keywords; ~3–8 display items)

## Title

> Encode the ADVANCE (the new material / mechanism / property), not just the topic.
> [Concise, advance-bearing title]

## Abstract / opening

> Research Article — Abstract (~200 words), finding-first:
>   context (1 sentence) → what we made/did (1 clause) → the advance + key benchmarked metric
>   → why it matters broadly (1 sentence).
> Communication — NO separate abstract: the OPENING PARAGRAPH must serve as the abstract,
>   self-contained and intelligible to a materials scientist outside the subfield.

[Abstract / opening-paragraph text]

> Keywords (Research Article): 3–7 terms.

## Body

> Typeset budget set by article type. Argue the structure → property → function chain;
> do not narrate the experiment chronologically.

### Opening / introduction (the gate paragraph)

> 4 moves, tight:
> 1. Materials context (non-specialist accessible)
> 2. The open problem / trade-off / instability that blocked progress
> 3. What this work reports — the central advance, with the key metric
> 4. Why it matters broadly (design consequence for a wider community)
[...]

### The material — structure and composition (decisive characterization)

> Prove what the material IS with complementary techniques (e.g. XRD + electron diffraction
> + HRTEM; XPS + EDS/EELS + ICP). Show the decisive evidence here; extended data → SI.
[ Figure 1: concept schematic + decisive structural evidence ]
[...]

### The property and its mechanism

> The measured property the structure produces, and WHY (the mechanism). This link is what
> makes the paper an advance rather than a metric report.
[ Figure 2: property data + mechanism ]
[...]

### Function / device demonstration + benchmarking

> The property in a working device, with full measurement conditions and a fair comparison
> to the best reported systems (benchmark table or plot). Report n and spread.
[ Figure 3: device performance + benchmarking ]
[...]

### Conclusion / outlook

> Tie the advance to the design rule or trade-off it changes; state the consequence for the
> neighboring field. Do not re-summarize; do not overclaim applications.
[...]

# Cover letter (separate)

> See `advmat-cover-letter`. Core job: convince the editor this is a broadly important
> materials advance, not incremental optimization.
> 1. Article type + title; submitted to Advanced Materials.
> 2. The advance (2 sentences, finding-first).
> 3. Why it is an advance, not an increment (contrast with the state of the art).
> 4. Why broad impact (named out-of-subfield / device audience + concrete reason).
> 5. One benchmarked headline metric with conditions.
> 6. Originality; prior preprint; related manuscripts; suggested/opposed referees.
