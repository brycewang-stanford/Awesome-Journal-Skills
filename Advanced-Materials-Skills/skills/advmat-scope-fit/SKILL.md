# Advanced Materials Scope Fit (advmat-scope-fit)

## When to trigger

- You suspect the work is solid but may be "incremental optimization"
- You cannot articulate why a materials scientist outside your subfield should care
- A coauthor asks "Adv. Mater. or Adv. Funct. Mater.?"
- An editor has previously bounced similar work on novelty/impact grounds
- Before investing in cover-quality figures and full characterization, you want a go / no-go on venue

## The two-part gate (both must pass)

Advanced Materials publishes across all of materials science — materials chemistry, nanotechnology, energy materials (batteries, photovoltaics, catalysis), electronics and optoelectronics, biomaterials, soft matter, and structural materials. The gate is two tests, AND-ed:

1. **Genuine advance** — Is there a *new material*, a *new mechanism*, or a *new property/performance regime* — not an incremental gain on an existing system? A record efficiency alone is not an advance if it comes from routine tuning; a new design principle that unlocks it is.
2. **Broad impact** — Would researchers in a *neighboring* materials community (or a device/application field) find this consequential? An advance is broad if it transfers a design rule, opens a new material class, or resets a performance–property trade-off others were stuck on.

A careful, well-characterized, but incremental result can **pass rigor and still fail the gate**. That is the most common Adv. Mater. rejection mode, and it is a venue judgment, not a quality judgment.

## Decision table

| Situation                                                                    | Verdict                              |
|------------------------------------------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------|
| New material class or design principle + clear cross-field relevance         | Adv. Mater. — strong fit             |
| New mechanism explaining a property, with general design consequences        | Adv. Mater. — strong fit             |
| Record device metric driven by a genuinely new material/interface concept    | Adv. Mater. — fit (frame the advance)|
| Solid performance gain by established optimization on a known system         | Adv. Funct. Mater. (functional focus)|
| Rigorous nanoscale study, well-defined but specialist audience               | Small                                |
| Sound, broad, but best served by gold open access and rapid dissemination    | Advanced Science                     |
| Interface / thin-film / surface study, incremental or specialist             | Advanced Materials Interfaces        |
| Characterization-only paper with no property or device consequence           | Specialist / methods journal         |

## How to stress-test the gate

- **The neighbor test**: name a specific researcher in a *different* materials subfield and state, in one sentence, why they would read this. If you cannot, broad impact is weak.
- **The design-rule test**: does the paper hand the reader a transferable rule ("do X to the lattice/interface and Y follows"), or just one more optimized sample? A rule travels; a sample does not.
- **The trade-off test**: does the advance break a known property trade-off (strength vs. ductility, efficiency vs. stability, conductivity vs. transparency)? Breaking one is a classic Adv. Mater. story.
- **The "so a record?" test**: if the headline is "highest X to date," ask what *new material physics or chemistry* produced it. No new principle → likely a functional/specialist venue.

## Checklist

- [ ] The single central advance is stated in one sentence (new material / mechanism / property)
- [ ] The advance is distinguished from incremental optimization, explicitly
- [ ] Broad impact is named for a concrete out-of-subfield or device audience
- [ ] You can name the design rule or trade-off the result changes
- [ ] You have honestly considered AFM / Adv. Sci. / Small / AMI as the better venue
- [ ] If borderline, you have an impact argument ready for the cover letter

## Anti-patterns

- Equating a *record number* with an *advance* — Adv. Mater. rewards the new principle behind the number
- Claiming broad impact by listing applications the material has not been shown to enable
- "Salami-slicing" a broader study into a thin Communication to chase the venue
- Submitting an optimization paper reframed with impact language but no new material science
- Ignoring that editors desk-reject on novelty/impact before any characterization is read

## Output format

```
【Central advance】one sentence (new material / mechanism / property)
【Not incremental because】...
【Broad impact】named out-of-subfield or device audience + why they care
【Design rule / trade-off changed】...
【Verdict】Adv. Mater. strong / Adv. Mater. with impact framing / retarget to AFM / Adv. Sci. / Small / AMI
【Next】advmat-results-framing (if Adv. Mater.) or retarget venue
```
