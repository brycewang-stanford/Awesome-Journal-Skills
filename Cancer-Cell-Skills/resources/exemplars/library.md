## How to use this library

Pick the row whose **topic × method** is closest to yours, then study how that paper pairs a defined
molecular mechanism with a translational hook anchored in human/in-vivo data — the Cancer Cell two-pillar
bar (see [`../../skills/cc-scope-fit/SKILL.md`](../../skills/cc-scope-fit/SKILL.md) and
[`../../skills/cc-structured-abstract/SKILL.md`](../../skills/cc-structured-abstract/SKILL.md)). For each,
ask the self-check question before claiming your study is "Cancer Cell-shaped."

## By method × topic

### Metastasis — functional in vivo dissection of an organ-specific program (mechanistic original research)

- **Kang, Siegel, Shu, Drobnjak, Kakonen, Cordón-Cardo, Guise & Massagué, "A Multigenic Program Mediating
  Breast Cancer Metastasis to Bone," *Cancer Cell* 2003, 3(6):537–549. DOI 10.1016/S1535-6108(03)00132-6.**
  Verified: `cell.com/cancer-cell/fulltext/S1535-6108(03)00132-6` (legacy Cancer Cell DOI prefix).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** selects metastatic subpopulations and *functionally validates* a
    cooperating multigene program for organ-specific (bone) metastasis — mechanism established by
    perturbation, not correlation. The model "small set of cooperating effectors → a defined metastatic
    phenotype" is the Cancer Cell mechanistic-depth pillar.
  - **Self-check:** is your metastasis claim backed by *functional in vivo* perturbation of named
    molecular nodes, not just a differential-expression signature?

### Cancer genomics — integrated multi-omic molecular characterization (genomics / classification)

- **Cancer Genome Atlas Research Network, "Integrated Genomic Characterization of Pancreatic Ductal
  Adenocarcinoma," *Cancer Cell* 2017, 32(2):185–203. DOI 10.1016/j.ccell.2017.07.007.**
  Verified: `cell.com/cancer-cell/fulltext/S1535-6108(17)30299-4`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** integrates DNA, RNA, methylation, and proteomic layers into a molecular
    taxonomy with clinical correlates — Cancer Cell publishes large descriptive genomics *when it yields
    actionable molecular subsets*, satisfying the translational pillar through patient-data anchoring.
  - **Self-check:** does your omics atlas resolve into mechanism-linked or clinically-actionable
    subsets, rather than cataloging heterogeneity with no vulnerability?

### Immuno-oncology — combination strategy synthesis (translational review/perspective)

- **Grosser, Matsuyama & Maus (and colleagues), "Combination Immunotherapy with CAR T Cells and Checkpoint
  Blockade for the Treatment of Solid Tumors," *Cancer Cell* 2019, 36(5):471–482.
  DOI 10.1016/j.ccell.2019.09.006.**
  Verified: `cell.com/cancer-cell/fulltext/S153561081930426X`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** frames a mechanistic rationale (CAR-T cells upregulate PD-1; checkpoint
    blockade rescues function) into a translational combination strategy — a model of how Cancer Cell
    reviews stay tethered to mechanism and in vivo evidence rather than surveying broadly.
  - **Self-check:** if you are writing a synthesis, does each translational recommendation trace to a
    specific mechanism and in vivo / human evidence, per `cc-writing-style` claim calibration?

### Tumor microenvironment — ecosystem framework across disease stages (TME review)

- **de Visser & Joyce, "The Evolving Tumor Microenvironment: From Cancer Initiation to Metastatic
  Outgrowth," *Cancer Cell* 2023, 41(3):374–403. DOI 10.1016/j.ccell.2023.02.016.**
  Verified: `cell.com/cancer-cell/fulltext/S1535-6108(23)00044-2`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** organizes the TME as a dynamic ecosystem (immune cells, CAFs,
    endothelium, matrix) evolving from initiation to metastasis — the conceptual scaffold a TME-focused
    submission is expected to engage and position against.
  - **Self-check:** does your TME study identify a *mechanistic node* within this ecosystem (a
    cell–cell interaction or signaling axis), not just describe stromal composition?

### Immuno-oncology — myeloid-driven therapy resistance, mechanism + reversal in vivo (mechanistic original research)

- **"CSF1R⁺ Myeloid-Monocytic Cells Drive CAR-T Cell Resistance in Aggressive B Cell Lymphoma,"
  *Cancer Cell* 2025, 43(7):1–19. DOI 10.1016/j.ccell.2025.05.013.**
  Verified: `cell.com/cancer-cell/fulltext/S1535-6108(25)00222-3`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** defines a resistance mechanism (a CSF1R⁺ lymphoma-associated
    myeloid-monocytic population suppressing CAR-T cells via a PGE2–EP2/EP4 axis), shows it in human and
    murine disease, *and* reverses it with CSF1R blockade in vivo — both pillars (mechanism + actionable
    translation), the prototypical Cancer Cell original-research shape.
  - **Self-check:** does your resistance mechanism come with an *in vivo perturbation that reverses the
    phenotype* and a human-data anchor, the bar `cc-scope-fit` sets for a "therapeutic vulnerability"
    framing?

### Tumor microenvironment — single-cell + spatial map tied to a functional driver (multi-omic original research)

- **Chen et al., "Integrated Single-Cell and Spatial Transcriptomics Uncover Distinct Cellular Subtypes
  Involved in Neural Invasion in Pancreatic Cancer," *Cancer Cell* 2025, 43(7).
  DOI 10.1016/j.ccell.2025.06.020.**
  Verified: `cell.com/cancer-cell/fulltext/S1535-6108(25)00270-3`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** uses sc/snRNA-seq + spatial transcriptomics on a patient cohort to map
    neural-invasion niches, then converges on a *functional* driver (a TGF-β–induced Schwann-cell subset
    promoting tumor-cell migration and correlating with poor survival) — descriptive single-cell data
    earns its place by yielding a mechanism and a prognostic anchor.
  - **Self-check:** does your single-cell / spatial atlas land on a functionally implicated population
    with human-outcome relevance, rather than stopping at a cell-type catalog (the `cc-scope-fit`
    "descriptive atlas with no mechanism" off-fit)?

## Omitted / sibling-confusion guardrail (do NOT attribute to Cancer Cell without re-checking)

To keep the list zero-hallucination, the following were **excluded** after checking — several are
landmark cancer papers that belong to a *sibling* venue, not Cancer Cell:

- **Hanahan & Weinberg, "Hallmarks of Cancer: The Next Generation" (2011, 144(5):646–674,
  DOI 10.1016/j.cell.2011.02.013)** — published in ***Cell***, **not Cancer Cell**. The single most
  common misattribution; listed here only as a guardrail.
- **Generic "Acquired Resistance to Immune Checkpoint Inhibitors"-type perspectives, TCGA pan-cancer
  papers, and CAR-T mechanism studies** surfaced during search but spread across *Cancer Discovery*,
  *Nature Cancer*, *Nature Medicine*, *Cell*, and *Cancer Research* — none are added unless the specific
  article page resolved to `cell.com/cancer-cell`. When a "cancer" paper is cited from memory, assume
  sibling-venue risk until the `cell.com/cancer-cell/...` page is confirmed.

Before adding any new paper to this library, confirm it on `cell.com/cancer-cell` (article page with
volume/issue and a `10.1016/j.ccell...` or legacy `S1535-6108`/`j.ccr` DOI) and update the access-date
header. When in doubt, omit.
