# Worked Example: Reframing an Introduction for Journal of Environmental Economics and Management

This is a fictional example. It does not describe a real paper.

## Before

> This paper studies an important topic using a large dataset and several robustness checks. We contribute to the literature by adding new evidence and showing heterogeneous effects.

## Diagnosis

- The first sentence does not name the JEEM audience or the journal's environmental economics, resource economics, climate policy, regulation, valuation, and natural-resource management scope.
- The contribution could fit several siblings: Review of Environmental Economics and Policy, AEJ Economic Policy, Journal of Public Economics, and Nature Climate Change.
- The mechanism words that matter here are absent: environmental externality, regulatory design, valuation evidence, climate adaptation.

## After

> This paper asks how **environmental externality** changes the decision margin that JEEM readers care about. The design ties the estimate to **regulatory design**, separates the main mechanism from **valuation evidence**, and reports uncertainty in a way that makes the policy or theory implication portable beyond the study setting.
