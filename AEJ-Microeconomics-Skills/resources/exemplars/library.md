interesting theory result** answer a substantive microeconomic question — the AEJ: Micro bar (see
[`../../skills/aejmic-topic-selection/SKILL.md`](../../skills/aejmic-topic-selection/SKILL.md) and

## Mechanism design & market design

- **Dworczak, "How to Allocate Money?," AEJ: Micro 2026, 18(1):312–339.**
  Verified: `aeaweb.org/articles?id=10.1257/mic.20230263` (DOI `10.1257/mic.20230263`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** an equity–efficiency allocation problem with no transfers screened by a
    wasteful "ordeal" — a clean mechanism-design result with a one-sentence message (when an ordeal beats a
    lump-sum transfer), exactly the AEJ: Micro sweet spot. Routes to
    [`../../skills/aejmic-theory-model/SKILL.md`](../../skills/aejmic-theory-model/SKILL.md).
  - **Self-check:** does your mechanism deliver a memorable, broadly interesting comparative statement, not
    just an optimal-mechanism formula?

- **Echenique & Núñez, "Price and Choose," AEJ: Micro 2025, 17(2):1–27.**
  Verified via the AEA `aea/aejmic` feed (DOI stem `10.1257/mic`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a constructive mechanism (price-then-choose) with a transparent strategic
    logic — generality where it is cheap, an example where it illuminates.
  - **Self-check:** is your solution concept matched to the question, with existence handled cleanly?

## Industrial-organization theory

- **Bergemann, Brooks & Morris, "On the Alignment of Consumer Surplus and Total Surplus under Competitive Price Discrimination," AEJ: Micro 2025, 17(4):234–259.**
  Verified: `aeaweb.org/articles?id=10.1257/mic.20240197` (DOI `10.1257/mic.20240197`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a sharp information-and-competition result (when consumer and total surplus
    are aligned, and when partial information breaks the alignment), illustrated in a Hotelling duopoly — a
    broadly interesting IO-theory message, not a one-off model. Connects to
    [`../../skills/aejmic-identification/SKILL.md`](../../skills/aejmic-identification/SKILL.md) (which
    assumption — consumers knowing their values — is load-bearing).
  - **Self-check:** can you name the single assumption whose failure flips your result?

- **Myatt & Ronayne, "Asymmetric Models of Sales," AEJ: Micro 2026, 18(1):281–311.**
  Verified via the AEA `aea/aejmic` feed (DOI stem `10.1257/mic`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a tractable model of mixed-strategy pricing ("sales") with asymmetric firms
    — the minimal environment that exposes the mechanism.
  - **Self-check:** is your model the simplest one in which the pricing pattern is visible?

## Game theory

- **Catonini & Penta, "Backward Induction Reasoning beyond Backward Induction," AEJ: Micro 2026, 18(1):30–58.**
  Verified via the AEA `aea/aejmic` feed (DOI stem `10.1257/mic`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a foundational reasoning result framed for a broad audience rather than as a
    purely technical refinement — AEJ: Micro rather than a specialist-only venue.
  - **Self-check:** is your equilibrium/reasoning notion justified, not invoked to rescue a result?

- **Jackson & Storms, "Behavioral Communities and the Atomic Structure of Networks," AEJ: Micro 2026, 18(1):146–173.**
  Verified via the AEA `aea/aejmic` feed (DOI stem `10.1257/mic`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a network-theory result with a clean structural object and broad appeal.
  - **Self-check:** does the result travel beyond the specific network model?

## Decision theory & behavioral theory

- **Apesteguia & Ballester, "A Measure of Behavioral Heterogeneity," AEJ: Micro 2026, 18(2):1–21.**
  Verified via the AEA `aea/aejmic` feed (DOI stem `10.1257/mic`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a decision-theoretic construct (a heterogeneity measure) built to be applied,

  - **Self-check:** if your contribution is a concept, is it characterized and shown to be useful?

- **Chambers, Echenique & Miller, "Decreasing Impatience," AEJ: Micro 2023, 15(3):527–551.**
  Verified via the AEA `aea/aejmic` feed (DOI stem `10.1257/mic`).

  - **Self-check:** do you prove both directions of your characterization (necessity, not just sufficiency)?

## Sibling-confusion guard & omissions (do not attribute to AEJ: Micro without re-checking)

To keep the list zero-hallucination:

- Entries whose **full volume/issue/pages** were confirmed on the AEA `aea/aejmic` feed are listed with the
  `10.1257/mic` stem; the two marquee entries were additionally confirmed against AEA article pages with the
  full DOI suffix (`…/mic.20230263`, `…/mic.20240197`). For the remaining entries the **DOI suffix is
  marked as the stem only** (`10.1257/mic`) — confirm the exact suffix on the AEA article page before citing
  the DOI verbatim. 待核实 on the exact suffix where not shown.
- A persuasion/IO paper that turns out to carry a **`10.3982/TE…`** (Theoretical Economics) or a
  **`10.1257/app…`** (AEJ: Applied) DOI is **not** AEJ: Micro — re-route it. JET and GEB papers carry
  Elsevier DOIs (`10.1016/…`), not `10.1257/mic`.

Before adding any new paper to this library, confirm it on `aeaweb.org` (article page with volume/issue/
pages) **and** that the DOI begins **`10.1257/mic`**, then update the access-date header. When in doubt, omit.
