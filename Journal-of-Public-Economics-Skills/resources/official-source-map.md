## Official journal pages (Elsevier / ScienceDirect)

- **https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/journal-of-public-economics** — JPubE homepage on ScienceDirect. Verifies: publisher = Elsevier; scope is public economics / public finance.
- **https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/journal-of-public-economics/publish/guide-for-authors** — Guide for Authors. Verifies: US$165 submission fee for unsolicited manuscripts, waived for article-transfer submissions; abstract should not exceed **250 words**; **single anonymized** review with a minimum of two reviewers; **author-date (name-and-year)** in-text citation; generative-AI use must be declared at submission; **one appeal per submission** under Elsevier's Appeal Policy; optional free **SSRN preprint** posting at submission; submission via **Editorial Manager**; Word (single-column) and LaTeX (.tex, double-column allowed) source files accepted. (Page hosted on ScienceDirect returned HTTP 403 to direct automated fetch; facts taken from search-result snippets of this page — re-read verbatim on the live page.)
- **https://www.elsevier.com/researcher/author/policies-and-guidelines/submission-fees** — Elsevier submission-fees policy. Verifies: reduced submission fee of **US$82.50 for full-time students**, applied via a student discount code obtained by contacting the journal and entered during submission in Editorial Manager.

## Editors, history, scope

- **https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Journal_of_Public_Economics** — Verifies: established **1972** by **Anthony (Tony) Atkinson**; publisher **Elsevier**; current Co-Editors-in-Chief **John N. Friedman (Brown University)** and **Wojciech Kopczuk (Columbia University)**; scope is public economics with emphasis on modern economic theory and quantitative methods applied to public-policy questions of interest to an international readership.
- **https://home.watson.brown.edu/news/2019-04-08/john-friedman-named-editor-chief-journal-public-economics** — Brown University announcement (2019). Verifies: John N. Friedman named Editor-in-Chief, joining Wojciech Kopczuk as co-Editor-in-Chief.

## Process facts (fee, review model, AI, appeals, preprint)

- Submission fee **US$165** (unsolicited), **US$82.50** for full-time students, **waived** for manuscripts transferred via Elsevier's article transfer service — source: Guide for Authors + Elsevier submission-fees policy (above).
- **Single anonymized** peer review (reviewers' identities hidden from authors; authors' identities known to reviewers/editors); suitable submissions sent to a **minimum of two reviewers** — source: Guide for Authors (above).
- **Generative-AI declaration** required at submission; AI may support but not substitute for human critical thinking — source: Guide for Authors (above), consistent with Elsevier's publishing-ethics policy.
- **One appeal per submission** considered under Elsevier's Appeal Policy — source: Guide for Authors (above).
- Optional free **SSRN preprint** posting at submission after initial quality checks, with no effect on the editorial process or outcome — source: Guide for Authors (above).
