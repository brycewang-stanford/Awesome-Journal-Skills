# JPubE Official Source Map

Primary sources behind the *Journal of Public Economics* facts used in this skill pack.
Access date for this refresh: **2026-06-20**. Before an actual upload, live-check
ScienceDirect and Editorial Manager for fee amounts, upload fields, and any policy wording
that may have changed after this refresh.

## Official Journal and Scope Pages

  — ScienceDirect Guide for Authors. Verifies the JPubE aim: original scientific
  contributions on public-economics problems, with particular emphasis on modern
  economic theory and quantitative analysis, and a forum for public-policy discussion
  relevant to an international readership. Also verifies submission fees, short-paper
  track, peer-review model, ethics policies, AI declaration, SSRN preprint option,
  file formats, abstract limit, highlights, research-data policy, references, checklist,
  transfer fee waiver, and proof timing.

  Technology) and **Wojciech Kopczuk, PhD** (Columbia University Department of
  Economics and School of International and Public Affairs), the Co-Editors list,

  editorial assistant, support for open access, open archive status, and journal metrics

## Submission, Fees, and Article Types

- **Guide for Authors, "Aims and scope" / "SubmissionStart link"** — Verifies the
  official submission-start route at `submissionstart.elsevier.com` and the journal
  contact email.
- **Guide for Authors, "Submission fees"** — Verifies **US$165** full submission fee
  for unsolicited manuscripts (re-verified 2026-06-22), **US$82.50** reduced fee for
  full-time students via a discount code used in Editorial Manager, no page charges,
  payment before consideration,
  non-refundable fee, desk rejection when aims/scope or style requirements are not met,
  and no additional fee when a manuscript is transferred via Elsevier's Article Transfer
  Service.
- **https://www.elsevier.com/researcher/author/policies-and-guidelines/submission-fees**
  — Elsevier submission-fees policy. Verifies that about 50 economics, finance, and
  accounting journals published by Elsevier levy submission fees; journals differ in
  waiver/discount policies; JPubE is one journal offering student discounts; and authors
  should consult the relevant Guide for Authors for details.
- **Guide for Authors, "Article types"** — Verifies the **Short Paper** article type:
  no more than **6,000 words**, about **15 pages** of double-spaced 11-point text with
  one-inch margins, up to **five exhibits**; main text and exhibits must stand alone;
  the journal aims for decisions on refereed short papers within **4-6 weeks** of initial
  submission; possible short-paper outcomes include desk rejection, rejection after
  review, conditional acceptance, and rare revise-and-resubmit; and authors normally
  return conditionally accepted short papers within **4 weeks**.

## Peer Review and Editorial Process

- **Guide for Authors, "Peer review"** — Verifies **single anonymized** review; initial
  editorial suitability assessment; suitable manuscripts typically sent to a minimum of
  **two reviewers** for independent expert assessment; editors make the final accept/
  reject decision; editor conflict rules; special issues and article collections follow
  the same process, with the journal editor retaining final responsibility; and only
  **one appeal per submission** is considered under Elsevier's Appeal Policy.
- **ScienceDirect editorial-board page** — Verifies current editors and board structure.
  Use this page instead of third-party pages for current names and affiliations.
- **https://www.editorialmanager.com/jpube/default.aspx** — Editorial Manager route for
  JPubE submission and correspondence.

## Ethics, AI, Preprints, and Files

- **Guide for Authors, "Declaration of generative AI use"** — Verifies that manuscript
  preparation with generative AI or AI-assisted tools must be declared upon submission,
  authors remain responsible for accuracy and originality, AI tools must not be listed
  as authors or co-authors, and the journal does not currently allow reviewers or editors
  to use generative AI tools in peer review or manuscript evaluation.
- **Guide for Authors, "Preprints"** — Verifies that sharing preprints is allowed under
  Elsevier policy and that JPubE offers free **SSRN** posting during submission after the
  manuscript passes initial desk review; this choice has no effect on editorial process
  or publication outcome.
- **Guide for Authors, "File format" / "LaTeX"** — Verifies that editable source files
  are required; Word files use `.doc/.docx` and single-column layout; LaTeX submissions
  use `.tex`; PDF is not an acceptable source file; and double-column formatting is
  permitted only for LaTeX submissions.
- **Guide for Authors, "Abstract" / "Keywords" / "Highlights"** — Verifies an abstract
  maximum of **250 words**, 1-7 English keywords, and required highlights of 3-5 bullet
  points with each bullet at most 85 characters including spaces.

## Research Data, References, and Final Files

- **Guide for Authors, "Research data" / "Data statement" / "Data linking"** — Verifies
  that JPubE follows Elsevier **Option C** research-data instructions: authors are
  required to deposit research data in a relevant repository, cite and link to the
  dataset in the article, or provide a statement explaining why research data cannot be
  shared. The guide also encourages availability statements at submission, explains that
  statements may appear with the published article, and supports repository linking.
- **Guide for Authors, "Data references"** — Verifies dataset-reference elements
  (authors, dataset title, repository, version where available, year, persistent
  identifier) and the `[dataset]` marker in references.
- **Guide for Authors, "References" / "Reference style"** — Verifies that submission
  references may use any consistent style, but the journal reference style is author-date
  in text; the reference list is alphabetical and chronological where needed; DOI use is
  recommended; and preprints should be clearly marked.
- **Guide for Authors, "Submission checklist" / "Proof correction"** — Verifies the
  corresponding-author/contact, files, references, permissions, and APC checklist, plus
  a two-day proof-correction expectation after acceptance.
