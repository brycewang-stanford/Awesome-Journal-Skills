> the official Annals of Mathematics site (`annals.math.princeton.edu`) to confirm it actually
> appeared in *Annals of Mathematics*, with year and volume/issue. Papers that could not be

## How to use this library

Pick the row whose **area × kind-of-advance** is closest to yours, then study how that paper
clears the Annals bar described in [`anmath-scope-fit`](../../skills/anmath-scope-fit/SKILL.md):
a **major, deep result of lasting importance** — it settles a recognized conjecture, removes a
long-standing hypothesis, or introduces a method with reach. For each, ask the self-check
question before claiming your paper is "Annals-shaped."

## By area

### Number theory — settling a centuries-old problem via modularity

- **Wiles, "Modular elliptic curves and Fermat's Last Theorem," Annals of Mathematics 1995, 141(3): 443–551.**
  Verified: `annals.math.princeton.edu/1995/141-3/p01`. *(Companion: Taylor & Wiles,
  "Ring-theoretic properties of certain Hecke algebras," same issue, 553–572.)*
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** the canonical "settles a famous open problem" Annals paper —
    establishes modularity for semistable elliptic curves, completing the proof of Fermat's
    Last Theorem. Significance is unmistakable on the first page.
  - **Self-check:** does your result *close* a recognized problem, with the headline theorem
    stated precisely and early (`anmath-results-framing`), not buried after preliminaries?

### Number theory — additive combinatorics meets the primes

- **Green & Tao, "The primes contain arbitrarily long arithmetic progressions," Annals of Mathematics 2008, 167(2): 481–547.**
  Verified: `annals.math.princeton.edu/wp-content/uploads/annals-v167-n2-p03.pdf`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a deep new method (a transference principle bringing
    Szemerédi-type machinery to a sparse set) with reach far beyond the headline theorem — the
    "new technique with cross-area consequences" profile from `anmath-scope-fit`.
  - **Self-check:** is the genuinely new ingredient isolated and named as the crux
    (`anmath-methods`), so others can reuse it — not buried inside a long computation?

### Number theory — a barrier broken in the distribution of primes

- **Zhang, "Bounded gaps between primes," Annals of Mathematics 2014, 179(3): 1121–1174.**
  Verified: `annals.math.princeton.edu/2014/179-3/p07`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** breaks a barrier experts had pushed against for years —
    the first proof that infinitely many prime pairs differ by a bounded amount — via a
    strengthened equidistribution input. A modest-sounding statement of first-order importance.
  - **Self-check:** can you state, in one sentence, exactly which barrier your result crosses
    and why it had resisted prior approaches (`anmath-results-framing` "quantify the advance")?

### Arithmetic geometry — statistics of elliptic curves

- **Bhargava & Shankar, "Binary quartic forms having bounded invariants, and the boundedness of the average rank of elliptic curves," Annals of Mathematics 2015, 181(1): 191–242.**
  Verified: `annals.math.princeton.edu/wp-content/uploads/annals-v181-n1-p03-p.pdf`
  (DOI `10.4007/annals.2015.181.1.3`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** geometry-of-numbers counting yields an arithmetic-statistics
    theorem (bounded average rank; mean size of the 2-Selmer group) long out of reach — a new
    method opening a whole program.
  - **Self-check:** does your technique generalize into a *program* others can extend, or is it
    a one-off that settles a single case only?

### Geometry / minimal surfaces — a conjecture resolved by min-max methods

- **Marques & Neves, "Min-max theory and the Willmore conjecture," Annals of Mathematics 2014, 179(2): 683–782.**
  Verified: `annals.math.princeton.edu/2014/179-2/p06`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** resolves the Willmore conjecture (the sharp lower bound for the
    Willmore energy of tori) by developing min-max theory of minimal surfaces into a working
    tool — deep result *and* method with reach.
  - **Self-check:** does your proof develop machinery the field will adopt, beyond the single
    conjecture it settles (`anmath-scope-fit` "new method with reach")?

### Geometric analysis — regularity of Einstein manifolds

- **Cheeger & Naber, "Regularity of Einstein manifolds and the codimension 4 conjecture," Annals of Mathematics 2015, 182(3): 1093–1165.**
  Verified: `annals.math.princeton.edu/wp-content/uploads/annals-v182-n3-p05-p.pdf`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** settles the codimension-4 conjecture (the singular set of a
    Gromov–Hausdorff limit of Einstein manifolds has codimension at least 4) — a structural
    theorem of lasting interest across geometric analysis.
  - **Self-check:** is the main theorem a *structural* statement of broad interest, with
    hypotheses stated cleanly and the crux of the regularity argument exposed?

### Dynamics / Teichmüller theory — orbit closures on moduli space

- **Eskin, Mirzakhani & Mohammadi, "Isolation, equidistribution, and orbit closures for the SL(2,ℝ) action on Moduli space," Annals of Mathematics 2015, 182(2): 673–721.**
  Verified: `annals.math.princeton.edu/2015/182-2/p07`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a Ratner-type rigidity theorem in a non-homogeneous setting —
    equidistribution and orbit-closure results for the SL(2,ℝ) action, with consequences across
    dynamics, geometry, and billiards.
  - **Self-check:** does your result connect several communities (here: dynamics, geometry,
    number theory) so that adjacent-area experts would want to know it exists?

because they appeared in a *different* journal. Listed here only as guardrails:

  **Publications mathématiques de l'IHÉS** (PMIHES 113), *not* Annals.
- **Hairer, "A theory of regularity structures" (2014)** — verified to **Inventiones
  Mathematicae** (vol. 198), *not* Annals.
- **Agol, "The virtual Haken conjecture" (2013)** — verified to **Documenta Mathematica**
  (vol. 18), *not* Annals.

Before adding any new paper to this library, confirm it on `annals.math.princeton.edu` (the
article page or the `wp-content/uploads/annals-vNNN-...pdf` file with volume/issue) and update
the access-date header. When in doubt, omit — and never attribute an Inventiones / Acta / JAMS
/ IHÉS / Duke paper to Annals.
