# *Journal of Communication* Exemplars Library (method × topic)

> **Verified via web search, access date 2026-06-08.** Every paper below was checked to confirm it
> actually appeared in **_Journal of Communication_ (JoC)** — the flagship journal of the **International
> Communication Association (ICA)**, published by **Oxford University Press** (ISSN 0021-9916 print /
> 1460-2466 online; pre-2017 issues via Wiley) — with year and volume/issue, against the journal's own
> article pages (`academic.oup.com/joc/...`), the pre-2017 Wiley record
> (`onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/.../jcom...` or `.../j.1460-2466...`), and corroborating indexes
> (Semantic Scholar, Google Scholar lookups carrying the journal name). Papers that could not be fully
> verified **as _Journal of Communication_** were **omitted** — this is deliberately a short, clean list,
> not a long, uncertain one.
>
> **Sibling-journal guard.** *Journal of Communication* is **not** *Communication Research*, *Human
> Communication Research*, *Communication Theory*, *Communication Methods and Measures*, or *Journal of
> Computer-Mediated Communication* — several of which are also ICA journals. Many famous "communication"
> papers live in those venues; the [Omitted](#omitted-for-lack-of-full-verification-do-not-attribute-to-journal-of-communication)
> section records the traps checked and rejected.

> **Use principle (zero hallucination):** this file gives **design positioning only**. It does not

## By method

### Content analysis with explicit codebook and reliability (content analysis)

- **Semetko & Valkenburg, "Framing European Politics: A Content Analysis of Press and Television News," _Journal of Communication_ 2000, 50(2):93–109.**
  Verified: `academic.oup.com/joc/article-abstract/50/2/93/4110003` (Wiley `10.1111/j.1460-2466.2000.tb02843.x`; ERIC EJ607863).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** operationalizes five *a-priori* news frames (responsibility, conflict,
    human interest, economic consequences, morality) and applies them to a large sampled corpus of press
    and television stories — a measurement-forward design where frames are defined before coding, not
    discovered after. The model [`joc-research-design`](../../skills/joc-research-design/SKILL.md)
    content-analysis move: documented codebook, justified text sampling, and a stated unit of analysis.
  - **Self-check:** are your frame categories defined and reliability-tested *before* you read the
    findings into them, with the text-sampling frame justified?

### Between-subjects experiment with delayed measurement (experiment)

- **Lecheler & de Vreese, "Getting Real: The Duration of Framing Effects," _Journal of Communication_ 2011, 61(5):959–983.**
  Verified: `academic.oup.com/joc/article-abstract/61/5/959/4098493` (Wiley `10.1111/j.1460-2466.2011.01580.x`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a framing experiment that measures the outcome not once but after 1 day,
    1 week, and 2 weeks, turning "does framing work?" into "how long does it last, and for whom?" — and
    conditioning the effect on political knowledge. Treats the manipulation as a designed cause and the
    decay as the estimand, the [`joc-research-design`](../../skills/joc-research-design/SKILL.md)
    experiment standard (manipulation realism, pre-specified moderation).
  - **Self-check:** does your experiment identify *when and for whom* the effect holds, rather than
    reporting a single post-test mean as if effects were timeless?

### Multi-wave panel survey testing a reciprocal process (survey / panel)

- **Feldman, Myers, Hmielowski & Leiserowitz, "The Mutual Reinforcement of Media Selectivity and Effects: Testing the Reinforcing Spirals Framework in the Context of Global Warming," _Journal of Communication_ 2014, 64(4):590–611.**
  Verified: `academic.oup.com/joc/article-abstract/64/4/590/4086023` (Wiley `10.1111/jcom.12108`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** uses a two-wave within-subjects panel of a representative sample to test a
    *reciprocal* model — media use shapes belief certainty, which in turn shapes later media selection —
    rather than reading a one-way effect from a cross-section. Exactly the
    [`joc-research-design`](../../skills/joc-research-design/SKILL.md) caution that process claims need
    panel/experimental leverage, not cross-sectional mediation.
  - **Self-check:** if your story is a feedback loop, does your design (panel waves, lagged structure)
    let selection and effects be separated, or are you inferring process from a single snapshot?

### Computational / big-data analysis of attention dynamics (computational)

- **Neuman, Guggenheim, Mo Jang & Bae, "The Dynamics of Public Attention: Agenda-Setting Theory Meets Big Data," _Journal of Communication_ 2014, 64(2):193–214.**
  Verified: `academic.oup.com/joc/article-abstract/64/2/193/4086099` (Wiley `10.1111/jcom.12088`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** brings large-scale traditional- and social-media data to a classic
    communication theory (agenda setting), reframing it as a dynamic, two-way interaction across 29
    issues rather than a one-directional flow from press to public. The
    [`joc-theory-building`](../../skills/joc-theory-building/SKILL.md) computational move: state the
    substantive communication question first; let the pipeline serve a recognizable theoretical claim.
  - **Self-check:** does your computational measure answer a *communication-theory* question (here, where
    attention originates and how it propagates), not just demonstrate that big data can be processed?

### Theory-integrating / conceptual contribution (theory building)

- **Valkenburg & Peter, "The Differential Susceptibility to Media Effects Model," _Journal of Communication_ 2013, 63(2):221–243.**
  Verified: `academic.oup.com/joc/article-abstract/63/2/221/4085964` (Wiley `10.1111/jcom.12024`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** integrates fragmented micro-level media-effects findings into one model
    with named susceptibility types (dispositional, developmental, social) and mediating response states
    — a portable framework other subfields can import. The
    [`joc-theory-building`](../../skills/joc-theory-building/SKILL.md) "portability" test made concrete:
    a mechanism with scope conditions, not a one-platform effect.
  - **Self-check:** could a researcher in another communication subfield import your mechanism and scope
    conditions to their own problem, or does the argument only work for your exact message and audience?

### Field-defining synthesis / agenda-setting essay (theory & positioning)

- **Bennett & Iyengar, "A New Era of Minimal Effects? The Changing Foundations of Political Communication," _Journal of Communication_ 2008, 58(4):707–731.**
  Verified: `academic.oup.com/joc/article-abstract/58/4/707/4098406` (Wiley `10.1111/j.1460-2466.2008.00410.x`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** argues that a changed media environment requires rethinking the field's
    foundational "minimal effects" premise — a positioning piece that names the rival paradigm and stakes
    a claim the field had to answer (it drew a published response). A model of the
    [`joc-literature-positioning`](../../skills/joc-literature-positioning/SKILL.md) move: locate the
    contribution against the strongest prevailing account, not a strawman.
  - **Self-check:** does your framing engage the leading account on its own terms and say precisely what
    has changed, rather than asserting novelty against a weak foil?

## Omitted for lack of full verification (do not attribute to _Journal of Communication_ without re-checking)

To keep the list zero-hallucination, these strong candidates were **excluded** after web-checking — each
is a real, important paper, but **not in _Journal of Communication_** (the sibling-venue traps the brief
warned of):

- **Walther, "Interpersonal Effects in Computer-Mediated Interaction: A Relational Perspective" (1992)** —
  verified in **_Communication Research_** 19(1):52–90, **not _JoC_**. The foundational
  hyperpersonal/SIP statement.
- **Boyd & Ellison, "Social Network Sites: Definition, History, and Scholarship" (2007)** — verified in
  the **_Journal of Computer-Mediated Communication_** 13(1):210–230, **not _JoC_**. (Both are ICA
  journals on Oxford Academic — easy to misattribute.)
- **Lombard & Ditton, "At the Heart of It All: The Concept of Presence" (1997)** — verified in the
  **_Journal of Computer-Mediated Communication_** 3(2), **not _JoC_**.
- **Hayes & Krippendorff, "Answering the Call for a Standard Reliability Measure for Coding Data" (2007)**
  — verified in **_Communication Methods and Measures_** 1(1):77–89, **not _JoC_**. The Krippendorff's-
  alpha reference everyone cites for intercoder reliability.

Before adding any new paper to this library, confirm it on `academic.oup.com/joc` (or the pre-2017 Wiley
`onlinelibrary.wiley.com` record) with volume/issue, and update the access-date header. When in doubt,
**omit**.
