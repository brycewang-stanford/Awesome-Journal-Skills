## VERIFIED facts injected into the skills (with source #)

- **Identity & sponsor:** JAR is sponsored by the Chookaszian Accounting Research Center at the University of Chicago Booth School of Business and published in partnership with Wiley-Blackwell since 2001; the journal was founded in 1963 and appears five times a year; one of the FT-50 journals; described as the oldest private research journal in the field. (#1, #8)
- **Field focus:** original research in all areas of accounting and accounting-related topics drawing on finance, economics, statistics, psychology, and sociology, using analytical, empirical-archival, experimental, and field-study methods (or combinations). The defining tradition is empirical-archival capital-markets work (the Ball-Brown lineage). (#1, #7)
- **Governance / editors:** no single editor-in-chief; a panel of Senior Editors — Philip G. Berger, Anna Costello, Luzi Hail, Valeri Nikolaev, Haresh Sapra, Laurence van Lent, and Regina Wittenberg Moerman — supported by ~18 Associate Editors and Editorial Manager Lisa M. Heiberger. (#4)
- **Submission system:** Wiley Research Exchange / Atypon Rex at wiley.atyponrex.com/journal/JOAR (as of Oct 2, 2023). (#3, #7)
- **Submission fee (tiered, country-of-residence based, effective Jan 1, 2023):** Tier One $750; Tier Two $500; Tier Three $50 — all authors must reside in Tier Two/Three countries to qualify for the lower rate; previously a flat $500. Fee must be received within one week of submission or the paper is withdrawn; generally non-refundable, but a desk-rejected non-conforming submission may receive a half refund. Separate $250 conference-registration fee and a $250 survey-proposal reviewer fee exist. (#2, #3)
- **Article types:** (i) Regular Manuscripts, (ii) Surveys/Methodological Contributions (standing call, separate proposal-review fee), and (iii) Registered Reports — each with its own review track. (#7)
- **Submission cap:** an author may submit at most four NEW papers over a rolling two-year period (R&R resubmissions excluded). (#7)
- **Review model:** double-anonymized (double-blind) peer review for regular manuscripts (authors anonymize the manuscript); Registered Reports follow a two-stage process (Stage 1 protocol → in-principle acceptance; Stage 2 results). (#7, #5, #9)
- **Data-and-code sharing policy:** JAR requires and hosts replication materials; accepted papers' datasheets and code are posted as online supplements; downloaders certify academic-research-only use, may not use materials commercially without code-author permission, must acknowledge the JAR publication and code authors, and authors retain copyright. JAR is distinctive among the top-three accounting journals in actually requiring/hosting (not merely encouraging) these materials. (#1, #6)
- **Ray Ball JAR Annual Conference:** flagship annual conference at Chicago Booth funded by the Chookaszian Center; selected papers are considered simultaneously for the conference and for publication and appear in the dedicated conference issue (historically June) subject to passing normal editorial review. The 62nd (2027) conference is themed "Current Topics in Accounting Research by Emerging Scholars," giving preference to untenured authors who received a PhD no earlier than 2017; submission deadline Dec 1, 2026; conference dates Apr 30-May 1, 2027. (#1, #3)
- **Registered Reports program:** pioneered at JAR in 2017 with Robert Bloomfield; authors pre-register hypotheses and an analysis plan; after Stage 1 protocol review the journal grants in-principle acceptance guaranteeing publication regardless of whether results support predictions, provided the approved protocol is followed; aimed at higher-outcome-risk research involving new data collection. (#5, #9)
- **Citation style:** a custom JAR house author-date style (consistent with Chicago author-date conventions used across Chicago Booth journals); not APA/numeric; authors are directed to match recent JAR articles. (#10, #1)
- **Dominant methodology:** empirical-archival capital-markets research (large-sample financial-statement/market-data econometrics) is the historically defining methodology, alongside strong experimental and analytical/theory streams and a growing field-study/registered-report presence. (#1, #7, #8)
