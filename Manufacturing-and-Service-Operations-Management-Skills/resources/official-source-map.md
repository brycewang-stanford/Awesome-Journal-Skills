## Facts injected into the skills

- **Journal & publisher:** Manufacturing & Service Operations Management (M&SOM), published by INFORMS and sponsored by the MSOM Society; bimonthly; ISSN 1523-4614 (print) / 1526-5498 (online). (#5, #12)
- **Mission/scope:** the premier journal for the operations-management research community; production and operations management of goods and services including technology management, productivity and quality management, product development, cross-functional coordination, and practice-based research. (#1, #2)
- **Core gate:** an operations decision/problem must be central to the contribution; method novelty alone is not enough for M&SOM fit. (#1, #2)
- **Review model for authors:** author-facing submission guidance requires double-anonymous preparation; remove names, institutions, acknowledgments, and self-identifying language from the manuscript. (#1)
- **Submission system:** ScholarOne Manuscripts at `mc.manuscriptcentral.com/msom`; upload an anonymized manuscript PDF and anonymized supplement; select manuscript type, primary department, two preferred Department Editors, six preferred reviewers, and three preferred Associate Editors. (#1)
- **Length and format:** new submissions may not exceed 32 pages, including references, tables, graphs/figures, and appendices, when typeset with the official M&SOM LaTeX or Word style files; one column, 11-point font, 1-inch margins, double-spaced at no more than 33 lines per page; no footnotes. (#1)
- **Supplement:** online supplement or e-companion is a separate anonymized PDF and is limited to 16 manuscript pages for new submissions after 1 January 2021. (#1)
- **Abstract:** structured, no more than 300 words, no technical jargon, with three required subsections: Problem definition; Methodology/results; Managerial implications. (#1)
- **Routing:** authors choose a primary department and two preferred Department Editors; if Manufacturing and Supply Chain Management is chosen, the first preferred DE must come from that department and the second from another department. (#1, #3)
- **Departments:** the current board page organizes Department Editors under AI in Operations, Analytics in Operations, Environment Health and Society, Manufacturing and Supply Chain Management, Operational Innovation, Services Platforms and Revenue Management, Practice Platform, and Outreach. The older editorial-statement page records the six-department structure that the pack uses for stable routing language. (#2, #3)
- **Decision process:** initial Department Editor review may take up to one month; if advanced, an Associate Editor may reject for insufficient contribution or recruit two to three reviewers; decision categories include accept, conditionally accept, minor revision, major revision, reject and resubmit, and reject. (#1)
- **Citation style:** INFORMS author-year / author-date style, with bibliography sorted by first author, number of authors, then year. (#11)
- **Data & code / replication:** manuscripts must contain enough detail and references to permit replication; authors should be prepared to share raw data and should provide code plus access/linking instructions for licensed data. (#4)
- **Generative AI:** authors remain responsible for accuracy of AI-assisted manuscript content; review-team members must not upload manuscript material into AI tools in ways that compromise confidentiality or copyright. (#6)
- **Fees:** the M&SOM submission guidelines list an optional INFORMS Open Option fee of USD 3,000 after acceptance. They do not list a regular submission fee for M&SOM on the checked page; the pack treats regular-submission payment as a portal-screen check rather than a manuscript-design assumption. (#1)
- **Editor:** Georgia Perakis is listed as Editor-in-Chief on the M&SOM home and editorial-board pages. INFORMS announced that her final term expires on 31 December 2026 and that the search committee aims to propose a successor by 1 July 2026. **Re-verified 2026-06-23:** Perakis remains the sitting EiC; the successor has **not** been publicly named in authoritative sources as of this check — the EiC search is still open. (Caution: a UCLA newsroom page headlines "Christopher Tang new editor-in-chief, M&SOM" but refers to Tang's **2015–2020** EiC term, announced August 2014 — it is **not** the 2026 succession; do not treat it as the current successor.) Confirm the named EiC on the live INFORMS M&SOM masthead before relying on this. (#3, #5, #10)
- **Metrics:** the M&SOM home page lists 2024 Impact Factor 4.2 and five-year Impact Factor 6.6. (#5)
- **Practice tracks:** Practice Platform, Practice-Based Research Competition, and OM Grand Challenges give practice-facing pathways; OM Grand Challenges proposals accepted for the 2026 MSOM Conference proceed toward a second review cycle for a dedicated special issue. (#7, #8, #9)
