## VERIFIED facts injected into the skills (with source #)

- **Journal & publisher:** Manufacturing & Service Operations Management (M&SOM); published by INFORMS (Institute for Operations Research and the Management Sciences); flagship journal sponsored by the MSOM Society. Founded 1999; quarterly; ISSN 1523-4614 (print) / 1526-5498 (online). (#9, #10, #12)
- **Mission/scope:** premier journal for the operations management research community; research on the design, procurement, production, delivery, and recovery of goods and services; an **operations decision/problem must be central** to the contribution. (#2, #3)
- **Dominant methodology:** historically analytical/stochastic OM modeling (optimization, queueing, stochastic models, game theory, revenue management), increasingly paired with rigorous empirical OM and data-driven analytics; both welcomed when routed to the right department. (#2, #4, #10)
- **Review model:** double-anonymous (double-blind); author identities known only to the EIC, Department Editor, Associate Editor, and editorial staff; authors must remove names, institutions, and acknowledgments. (#1)
- **Submission system:** ScholarOne Manuscripts at https://mc.manuscriptcentral.com/msom; single PDF; authors choose a primary Department and name **2 preferred Department Editors** with the Manufacturing-and-Supply-Chain first/second rule. (#1)
- **Length:** 32-page maximum for a new submission, **including** all references, tables, graphs/figures, and appendices, typeset with the official M&SOM LaTeX/Word style files (one column, 11-pt font, 1-inch margins). Online supplement limited to 16 pages for new submissions. (#1)
- **Abstract:** structured, no more than 300 words, with required subsections (no technical jargon) — Problem definition; Methodology; Results; Managerial implications. (#1)
- **Citation style:** INFORMS author-year (author-date) with cited pages; reference list ordered by first author + number of authors + year (INFORMS style file v1.6). (#8)
- **Six editorial departments:** Manufacturing & Supply Chain Operations; Services, Platforms & Revenue Management; Environment, Health & Society; Operational Innovation; Analytics in OM; Practice Platform. (#4)
- **Special categories:** OM Forum (thought-leadership/perspective) published under an identifying banner; a dedicated Practice Platform department. (#2, #3)
- **Data & code policy:** manuscripts must permit replication; authors may be asked for raw data and must be prepared to share and retain it; licensed-data use requires authors' own code plus access/linking instructions; reuse beyond replicability verification must cite and acknowledge the source. (#7)
- **Editor:** Georgia Perakis (MIT Sloan), Editor-in-Chief since January 2021; final term expires 31 December 2026; INFORMS search committee to propose a successor no later than 1 July 2026. (#4, #5, #6)
- **OM Grand Challenges (2026):** SDG-aligned academic-practitioner proposals; accepted proposals presented at the 2026 MSOM Conference (12–14 July 2026) automatically advance into a second review cycle for a dedicated M&SOM special issue. (#11)
