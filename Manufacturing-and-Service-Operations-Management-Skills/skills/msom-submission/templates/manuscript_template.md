# M&SOM Manuscript Template

> Draft inside the **official M&SOM LaTeX/Word style files** from the start. The
> **32-page cap includes references, tables, figures, and appendices**, typeset at
> one column, 11-pt, 1-inch margins. Push proofs and extra studies to the **online
> supplement (≤ 16 pages)**. Submit a single anonymized PDF via
> mc.manuscriptcentral.com/msom. Check the official submission-guidelines page
> and ScholarOne screens before final upload.

---

## Title page (separate / portal metadata — NOT in the anonymized PDF)

- Title
- Author(s), affiliation(s), contact, ORCID
- Acknowledgments and funding
- Chosen **Department** + two preferred **Department Editors** (respect the Manufacturing-and-Supply-Chain first/second rule)
- Article type: standard research / OM Forum / Practice Platform

## Structured abstract (≤ 300 words, no technical jargon)

- **Problem definition:** the operational decision/problem studied.
- **Methodology-results:** analytical model class or empirical identification strategy, followed by the structural result or identified effect with magnitude.
- **Managerial implications:** what an operations manager should do differently.

Keywords / history: prior conference or working-paper versions disclosed in the cover letter.

## 1. Introduction

- State the **operational problem, decision, and insight in words** before any notation.
- Why it matters operationally and managerially; the gap in the OM conversation you join.
- Preview the contribution and the structure of the optimal policy / identified effect.

## 2. Literature / positioning

- Engage the canonical OM streams; make the target Department evident.
- Differentiate on the **operational lever/mechanism**, not just the setting.

## 3. Model (analytical) or Research design (empirical)

- **Analytical:** decision variables, objective, dynamics, stochastic primitives; model class (newsvendor / MDP / queueing / stochastic-program / robust / game-theoretic); assumptions, with binding ones flagged.
- **Empirical:** data and operational unit; identification strategy; treatment of endogenous operational decisions (capacity, inventory, staffing).

## 4. Analysis / Results

- **Analytical:** propositions/theorems (structure: monotonicity, threshold, base-stock, comparative statics, equilibrium). Keep intuition in the body; full proofs in the supplement.
- **Empirical:** main estimates with clustered SEs; identification diagnostics (trends/first-stage/balance).

## 5. Numerical study / Robustness

- **Analytical:** realistic parameter ranges; magnitude vs. benchmarks/heuristics; sensitivity to assumptions; instance generation and seeds.
- **Empirical:** alternative specifications, samples, and operational measures; effect sizes in operational units.

## 6. Managerial insights

- Translate the result into operational guidance; this echoes the abstract's Managerial-implications part.

## 7. Conclusion

- Contribution, limitations, and operations-research directions.

## References

- INFORMS **author-year**; list ordered by first author, number of authors, then year (style file v1.6).

## Online supplement (≤ 16 pages, separate)

- Full proofs; additional numerical experiments; notation table; data/code and licensed-data access instructions.
