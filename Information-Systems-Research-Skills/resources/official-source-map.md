# ISR Official Source Map

Canonical sources for verifying every Information Systems Research (ISR)-specific claim made in this
skill pack. All source entries were refreshed on **2026-06-20** from official INFORMS PubsOnline pages
and related INFORMS author resources. Re-check volatile items on the live pages before submission.

## Official journal, scope & masthead

- **https://pubsonline.informs.org/journal/isre**
  — ISR journal home. Verifies current Editor-in-Chief **Suprateek Sarker**, **quarterly** frequency,
  Print ISSN **1047-7047**, Online ISSN **1526-5536**, and the 2026 current issue context (Volume 37,
  Issue 1, March 2026).
- **https://pubsonline.informs.org/page/isre/editorial-statement**
  — Editorial statement. Verifies ISR's mission to advance knowledge about effective and efficient use
  of information technology by individuals, groups, organizations, society, and nations; scope over the
  **design, management, use, valuation, and impacts of information technologies** at individual, group,
  firm, network, society, and nation levels; welcome for high-quality theoretical, empirical, design, and
  analytical work; and research traditions including cognitive psychology, economics, computer science,
  operations research, design science, organization theory/behavior, sociology, and strategic management.
  Verifies three submission genres: **Research Article**, **Research Commentary**, and **Research Note**.
- **https://pubsonline.informs.org/page/isre/editorial-board**
  — Editorial board. Verifies current **Editor-in-Chief Suprateek Sarker** (McIntire School of Commerce,
  University of Virginia), Advisory Editors, Senior Editors, Associate Editors/ERB structure, Managing
  Editor **Chris Asher**, and former Editors-in-Chief including Alok Gupta (2017-2022), Ritu Agarwal
  (2011-2016), and Vallabh Sambamurthy (2005-2010).
- **https://pubsonline.informs.org/doi/10.1287/isre.2023.1207**
  — Sarker inaugural EIC editorial, "Continuing on an Inclusive Path to Scholarly Excellence with Renewed
  Vigor," ISR 34(1):1-4, published February 27, 2023. Verifies the EIC transition context and the
  inclusive-excellence direction: broader research traditions, expanded editorial structures, and ERB
  members as reviewers/associate-editor pipeline.
- **https://pubsonline.informs.org/doi/10.1287/isre.2025.editorial.v36.n2**
  — 2025 ISR editorial "Advancing Next-Generation Multimethod Research in Information Systems." Verifies
  the multimethod framework (methodological distance and nature of integration; assembly, blend, bridge,
  fusion) and recommendations for authors/evaluators of multimethod IS work.

## Submission guidelines & review process

- **https://pubsonline.informs.org/page/isre/submission-guidelines**
  — ISR submission guidelines. Verifies:
  - **Article types:** Research Articles; Research Commentaries; Research Notes.
  - **Research Article fit:** salient IS phenomenon, robust connections to literature/theory, appropriate
    theory/model, valid empirical methods, competent analyses, interesting results, and compelling
    conclusions.
  - **Research Commentaries:** critical evaluation and roadmap for future research on an important IS
    phenomenon; **no more than 20 pages** plus up to **8 pages of figures and tables**.
  - **Research Notes:** incremental extension of well-researched phenomena or methodological commentary;
    **no more than 20 pages** plus up to **8 pages of figures and tables**.
  - **Full manuscript length:** no more than **32 pages of text** and no more than **38 pages including all
    material**; online appendix/electronic companion for proofs, psychometric details, measurement items,
    and other support material.
  - **Format:** manuscript files, including abstract, references, and footnotes, must be **double-spaced**,
    at least **11-point** standard font, and **one-inch margins** on all sides.
  - **Double-anonymous peer review:** remove author names, institutions, acknowledgments, and identifying
    self-references while keeping proper attribution.
  - **Contribution statement:** starting **June 1, 2023**, every cover letter requires a contribution
    statement of **fewer than 500 words**; papers without it do not move forward.
  - **Provenance:** prior/related work and previous submissions must be disclosed; prior versions rejected
    by ISR cannot simply be resubmitted; the manuscript must not be published or under review elsewhere.
  - **Data provenance:** authors must certify rights to use data and publish results; required legal or
    corporate permissions must be obtained before submission; violations can withdraw the manuscript even
    after acceptance.
  - **Abstract:** no more than **300 words**.
  - **References:** alphabetical by author; in-text citations use author name and year.
  - **Submission portal:** ScholarOne Manuscripts at **http://mc.manuscriptcentral.com/isr**.
  - **ScholarOne steps:** Step 1 includes manuscript type, title, abstract, and two preferred Senior
    Editors; Step 4 requires **5 preferred reviewers** including **at least 2 ERB members**, **2 preferred
    Associate Editors**, and **2 preferred Senior Editors**, with conflict avoidance rules.
  - **Submitting author ORCID:** the submitting author is required to provide an ORCID ID.
  - **Review process:** Senior Editor manages evaluation; Associate Editor solicits **2-3 reviewers** and
    integrates recommendations; Senior Editor makes the final decision. ISR seeks decisions within
    **120 days** from original submission, and invited revisions should be resubmitted within **six
    months**.
  - **Appeals:** limited to demonstrable factual/technical errors or significant procedural errors; appeal
    window is at least one week after, and no more than eight weeks after, the final decision.
  - **After acceptance:** final files due within **14 days**; final package includes manuscript, PDF,
    supplementary material/e-companion, **75-word author bios**, **200-word practice/policy-oriented
    abstract**, copyright transfer, editorial checklist, and optional media form.
  - **Open Access:** INFORMS Open Option fee is **US$3,000**, invoiced after acceptance only.
- **https://pubsonline.informs.org/authorportal/open-option**
  — INFORMS Open Option author portal. Verifies the Green Option for author accepted manuscripts and the
  Gold/INFORMS Open Option: accepted manuscripts can be made open access for a one-time **US$3,000** fee;
  selecting IOO does not affect peer review or editorial decisions and invoicing occurs after acceptance.

## Pack-level guardrails

- Treat ISR's page limits and nomination counts as active current rules because they are available on
  the official submission-guidelines page.
- Do not import Management Science's $79 original-submission fee into ISR; this pack records only the
  ISR/INFORMS Open Option post-acceptance open-access fee.
- Do not describe ISR data policy as a replication deposit mandate. Active source-backed guidance is data
  provenance certification plus the practical reproducibility package expected for credible empirical,
  analytical, and design-science work.
