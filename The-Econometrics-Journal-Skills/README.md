# The Econometrics Journal Skills

Twelve agent skills for manuscripts targeted at **The Econometrics Journal (EctJ)**. The pack
focuses on leading-case econometric contributions, applied value, compact RES/OUP formatting,
asymptotic identification, Monte Carlo evidence, the required empirical application, Editorial
Express submission, rapid editorial screening, and acceptance-stage replication files.

Official basis checked on 2026-06-01: OUP general instructions, RES journal page, RES
submissions page, Editorial Express portal, RES review-process page, RES submission-fee policy,
and RES replication policy. See `resources/official-source-map.md` for exact source mapping and
unresolved `待核实` items.

## Skills

- `ectj-workflow` - route the project through fit, theory, empirics, compression, submission,
  review, replication, and rebuttal.
- `ectj-topic-selection` - decide whether the project clears EctJ's leading-case and applied-
  value bar.
- `ectj-literature-positioning` - position against econometrics, statistics, ML, and applied
  literatures.
- `ectj-identification-strategy` - audit identification, assumptions, asymptotics, proofs, and
  leading-case theory.
- `ectj-data-analysis` - design Monte Carlo and empirical-application evidence.
- `ectj-tables-figures` - compress exhibits under the printed-paper constraint.
- `ectj-contribution-framing` - make the methodological and applied-value contribution explicit.
- `ectj-writing-style` - revise for 150-word summary, 20-page discipline, template compliance,
  and theorem readability.
- `ectj-replication-and-data-policy` - prepare README, software versions, data documentation,
  seeds, restricted-data explanations, and OUP Supporting Information.
- `ectj-review-process` - plan around one-week screening, three-month target decisions, and
  resubmission timing.
- `ectj-submission` - preflight Editorial Express submission, fee, cover letter, empirical
  application, proof placement, and replication disclosure.
- `ectj-rebuttal` - draft response letters and revision plans for assumptions, proofs,
  simulations, application, compression, and replication.
