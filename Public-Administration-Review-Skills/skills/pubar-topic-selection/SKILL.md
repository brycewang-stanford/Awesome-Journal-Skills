# Topic Selection & Fit (pubar-topic-selection)

PAR is the **flagship of the American Society for Public Administration** and is explicitly designed to
**bridge research and practice**. The bar is not "new to my subfield" alone — it is **"advances PA
scholarship *and* speaks to practitioners."** Use this skill to pressure-test fit before you invest.

## When to trigger

- Choosing among possible projects or framings for a PAR submission
- A reviewer/colleague said the paper is "too narrow," "purely academic," or "just a case write-up"
- Deciding which **article type** to target
- Worried the paper belongs at a sibling venue (JPART, Governance, JPAM) instead

## The PAR fit test

A strong PAR paper usually clears all four:

1. **PA significance across subfields.** A scholar in another PA area — public HR/personnel, public
   budgeting & finance, performance management, collaborative governance, public service motivation,
   policy implementation — should see why it matters to the field, not just to one literature.
2. **A real contribution, not just a finding.** It changes how the field theorizes, measures, or
   manages a public-sector problem — a new mechanism, a decisive test, a reconceptualization.
3. **Credible practitioner relevance (the PAR signature).** You can write 3–5 honest **Evidence for
   Practice** points: what a public manager, agency, or policymaker should do differently.
4. **A clean, answerable scope** that fits **≤ 8,000 words** (incl. abstract, endnotes, references)
   (检索于 2026-06；以官网为准).

## Sibling-venue fit (decide before you write)

| If the paper is… | Better venue than PAR | PAR fit only if… |
|------------------|------------------------|------------------|
| A formal/theory-driven test with no practitioner stake | **JPART** | you add a credible managerial "so-what" |
| A cost-benefit / program-evaluation policy analysis | **JPAM** | the contribution is to *administration*, not policy choice |
| A comparative-institutions governance piece | **Governance** | the lens is public-management practice, not regime design |
| A subfield-only HR or budgeting note | a field journal | it generalizes to public management broadly |

## Article-type choice

- **Scholarly Take** — a full empirical research manuscript; the main research format.
- **Conceptualizing Public Administration** — a thought/theory piece or "state of PA" essay (route
  early to `pubar-theory-building`).
- **Early Career Intel** — research manuscripts by junior scholars; same rigor, tighter scope.
- **Practically Speaking** — practitioner–scholar co-authored, often growing from PAR Talk webinars or
  the On PAR podcast; lead with the practice problem.

## Anti-patterns

- "It's never been studied in agency/jurisdiction X" as the whole contribution (descriptive, narrow)
- A rigorous study with no honest practitioner takeaway (PAR will press the Evidence for Practice)
- Sending a pure-theory paper here because PAR is high-impact — it reads as off-fit vs. JPART
- A sprawling question that cannot be answered within ~8,000 words

## Output format

```
【Question】one sentence
【PA significance】who across PA subfields cares, and why
【Practitioner so-what】the Evidence-for-Practice seed (1-2 lines)
【Contribution type】theory / test / measurement / reconceptualization
【Article type】Scholarly Take / Conceptualizing PA / Early Career Intel / Practically Speaking
【Fit verdict】strong / needs reframing / off-fit (which sibling venue?)
【Next】pubar-literature-positioning
```
