## Official journal, submission & author pages

- **https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/page/journal/15406210/homepage/forauthors.html**
  — Wiley Online Library, *Public Administration Review* — For Authors. Verifies: published by
  **Wiley** for the **American Society for Public Administration (ASPA)**; manuscripts should generally
  contain **no more than 8,000 words, including abstract, endnotes, and references** (tables, figures,
  charts, and appendices excluded from the count); all submissions should include **3–5 sentence-length
  takeaway points** providing **Evidence for Practice**; article types include **Conceptualizing Public
  Administration**, **Scholarly Takes**, **Early Career Intel**, **Practically Speaking**, and **Public
  Administration in Print** (book reviews). Wiley's page should be re-opened for exact live wording
  during submission.
- **https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/puar.13767** and **/10.1111/puar.13872**
  — "Information for Contributors," *Public Administration Review* (2023 / 2024 versions). Verifies:
  word and abstract limits, double-blind preparation, Evidence for Practice, TOP transparency standards.
- **https://publicadministrationreview.wordpress.com/submission-process/**
  — PAR "Submission Process" guidance. Verifies: submission portal listed as **Editorial Manager**
  (`http://www.editorialmanager.com/par/`); **≤ 8,000 words** including abstract/endnotes/references;
  **abstract maximum 150 words** on a separate page; **3–5 Evidence for Practice** points (encouraged
  at submission, required in revised manuscripts); **double-blind** anonymization (no names on title
  page; refer to own prior work in the third person; remove acknowledgments, institution names,
  identifying URLs); **TOP guidelines** with four transparency principles (cite data/materials; share
  data via **Dataverse**/quantitative and **Qualitative Data Repository**/qualitative; document
  reporting following CONSORT/STROBE/PRISMA/COREQ; pre-registration **badges** available).

## Research transparency & data policy

- PAR is a **signatory of the Center for Open Science Transparency and Openness Promotion (TOP)
  Guidelines** and has adopted **four transparency standards**: (1) cite research materials and data
  sources; (2) encourage data sharing via **Dataverse** (quantitative) or the **Qualitative Data
  Repository** (qualitative); (3) recommend documenting research design/data prep/analysis in a
  supplementary document following relevant reporting standards (CONSORT/STROBE/PRISMA/COREQ); (4) make
  pre-registration **badges** available. Source: PAR submission-process guidance + Wiley For Authors.
  Re-open the live policy/FAQ page before copying exact current wording.

## Sibling-journal guard (do not conflate)

- **JPART** (*Journal of Public Administration Research and Theory*, Oxford) — theory-driven PA;
  publisher and scope differ from PAR.
- **Governance** (Wiley) — comparative institutions / governance, not practice-bridging PA management.
- **JPAM** (*Journal of Policy Analysis and Management*, Wiley/APPAM) — policy analysis & program
  evaluation, not public administration as such.
- **The American Review of Public Administration (ARPA)** and **Public Administration** (Wiley) — both
  PA journals but distinct from PAR; do not attribute PAR policies to them.
