# External Tools - ICASSP

Access date: 2026-07-09

Use these surfaces only after reopening the current ICASSP cycle. Because a new local
committee rebuilds the site each year and the IEEE Signal Processing Society periodically
revises its editorial and conference policies, treat every rule below as provisional.

## Official workflow surfaces

- Current-year conference site: `https://<year>.ieeeicassp.org/` (ICASSP 2026 Barcelona:
  https://2026.ieeeicassp.org/ ; ICASSP 2027 Toronto: https://2027.ieeeicassp.org/).
- Submission portal (Conference Management Services / CMS):
  https://cmsworkshops.com/ICASSP2026/papers.php — the paper kit, templates, and upload live here.
- IEEE Signal Processing Society (governing body, policies, awards):
  https://signalprocessingsociety.org/
- ICASSP event hub on the SPS site: https://signalprocessingsociety.org/event-names/icassp
- Proceedings after acceptance: IEEE Xplore (https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/).
- Proceedings index for verification and related work: https://dblp.org/db/conf/icassp/

## Author-side verification passes

Run these against the live pages before trusting any advice in this pack:

1. **Format check** — confirm the current page count and the "4+1" split, the two-column IEEE
   template version, figure/image resolution rules, and that the PDF passes IEEE PDF eXpress.
2. **Identity check (inverted from anonymous venues)** — ICASSP is single-blind, so the author
   block *must* be present and correct. The failure mode here is a *missing* or wrong author
   list, not an identity leak.
3. **EDICS and track choice** — pick the EDICS subject category that routes the paper to the
   right technical committee, and decide between the standard track, OJSP-ICASSP, a Grand
   Challenge, and the SPS-journal presentation option.
4. **Policy checkboxes** — dual-submission attestation, the Compliance-with-Ethical-Standards
   statement, any AI-use disclosure, and the 9-papers-per-author cap.
5. **Rebuttal readiness** — confirm whether the current cycle runs an author-response window,
   its dates, and its length/format limits before promising reviewers anything.

## Do not infer

- Do not carry ICASSP 2026 dates, deadlines, or the Barcelona committee's choices forward to
  the Toronto 2027 cycle; each edition republishes its own kit.
- Do not assume anonymization — verify the single-blind policy every cycle rather than
  importing double-blind habits from ISCA/OpenReview venues.
- Do not assume a rebuttal exists in a given year, or that the CMS portal (not CMT) is used,
  without checking the current instructions.
- If official pages disagree, the newest paper kit and SPS editorial policy control; record the
  conflict rather than guessing.
