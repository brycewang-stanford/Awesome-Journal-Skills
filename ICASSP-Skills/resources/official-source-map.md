# Official Source Map - ICASSP

Access date: 2026-07-09

This map records the sources behind every ICASSP-specific fact in this pack. ICASSP is the
flagship conference of the **IEEE Signal Processing Society (SPS)** and is re-hosted by a new
local organizing committee each year, so the paper kit, dates, portal, and even the review
mechanics are cycle-specific. Reopen the current-year call for papers, paper kit, and SPS
policy pages before giving submission-ready advice.

## Access-method note (read first)

Several primary ICASSP surfaces are served behind an edge gateway that returned **HTTP 403**
to direct automated fetches on 2026-07-09 (`2027.ieeeicassp.org`, the `cmsworkshops.com`
paper-kit pages). Those pages were therefore read through **search-engine renderings of the
exact URLs** and **cross-checked** against the IEEE SPS society site, dblp, and IEEE Xplore
DOIs. Where a value could be confirmed from at least two independent renderings it is listed
as verified; where it rested on a single secondary rendering it is marked **待核实**. The
first maintenance task each cycle is to re-open the primary URLs directly and refresh this

## Verified 2026-cycle facts used in skills

- ICASSP 2026 is the **51st** IEEE International Conference on Acoustics, Speech, and Signal
  Processing, held in **Barcelona, Spain, 4-8 May 2026** (CCIB), theme
  **"Where Signals Meet Intelligence."** (Some listings show tutorials beginning 3 May; 待核实.)
- **Format:** a regular paper is **4 pages** of technical content including all text, figures,
  and references, with an **optional 5th page** that may contain **only** references, funding
  acknowledgements, and a Compliance-with-Ethical-Standards statement — the IEEE "4+1" rule.
- **Review is single-blind.** ICASSP does **not** anonymize submissions; the author list must
  appear in the submitted PDF. Reviewers see author identities; authors do not see reviewers.
- **A rebuttal/author-response stage exists.** In the 2026 cycle authors could reply to
  reviewer comments during a roughly week-long window (reported open until **22 Dec 2025**).
  This is a comparatively recent addition — older ICASSP cycles had **no** rebuttal.
- **Submission portal is CMS** (`cmsworkshops.com`, Conference Management Services), not
  Microsoft CMT. Selection is by **EDICS** subject categories.
- **Paper deadline for ICASSP 2026 was 17 September 2025**; the accepted-paper revision window
  ran to **28 January 2026** (待核实 exact time zone/date).
- **No single author may appear on more than 9 papers.**
- **Alternative tracks:** the **OJSP-ICASSP** track (8+1 pages, open-access publication in the
  IEEE Open Journal of Signal Processing, presented at ICASSP but not in the proceedings) and
  the **SPS-journal presentation** option (present a recently accepted SPS-journal paper, not
  re-reviewed, not in proceedings). Signal Processing **Grand Challenges** invite top-ranked
  teams to submit a short paper and present.
- Proceedings are published in **IEEE Xplore**; presentation is in person by a registered author.
