# Official Source Map - INTERSPEECH

Access date: 2026-07-08

This map records the sources behind every Interspeech-specific fact in this pack.
Interspeech is organized by ISCA — the **International Speech Communication
Association** (not the computer-architecture symposium with the same acronym) —
together with a local host each year, and virtually every mechanic (format, tracks,
portal, rebuttal, attachment rules) is re-decided per edition. Reopen the current
edition's site before giving deadline-sensitive advice.

**Access-method note (2026-07-08):** direct fetches of `interspeech2026.org`,
`isca-speech.org`, `isca-archive.org`, `assta.org`, and `dblp.org` were blocked by
the network gateway used for verification (HTTP 403 at the proxy). All facts below
were therefore established through search-engine renderings of the exact official
URLs, cross-checked against one another and against secondary trackers where noted.
Before relying on any date, cap, or policy, open the primary URLs below directly.

## Verified 2026-cycle facts used in the skills

- Interspeech 2026 is the ISCA flagship conference, held in Sydney, Australia,
  27 September – 1 October 2026, hosted with ASSTA; theme "Speaking Together".
- Timeline: submission portal opened 17 January 2026; paper deadline 25 February
  2026; post-deadline **paper-update window** until 4 March 2026 (PDF revisions;
  metadata frozen); acceptance notification 5 June 2026; camera-ready 19 June 2026.
- Registration: early-bird closed 15 July 2026; standard closes 16 August 2026; at
  least one author must complete author registration for an accepted paper to
  appear in the proceedings and program.
- Format: regular papers are 4 pages of content in two columns plus one page for
  references and acknowledgments only (2025 kit wording, carried as the 2026
  baseline); submissions are validated mechanically at upload.
- Review is double-anonymous, with an anonymity period (per the 2025 policy
  precedent: from one month before the deadline until decisions) forbidding
  non-anonymized online postings of the work.
- Proceedings are published open access in the ISCA Archive
  (www.isca-archive.org) with DOIs under prefix 10.21437; there is no
  article-processing charge — the cost model is registration.
- ISCA confers three Best Student Paper awards per Interspeech and the annual ISCA
  Medal for Scientific Achievement; like all conferences in this collection,
  Interspeech has no standing editor-in-chief — Technical Program Chairs and
  organizing committees rotate per edition.
- Interspeech 2027: São Paulo, Brazil, 29 August – 2 September 2027.
