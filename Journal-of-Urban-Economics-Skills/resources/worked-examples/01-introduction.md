# Worked Example: Reframing an Introduction for Journal of Urban Economics

This is a fictional example. It does not describe a real paper.

## Before

> This paper studies an important topic using a large dataset and several robustness checks. We contribute to the literature by adding new evidence and showing heterogeneous effects.

## Diagnosis

- The first sentence does not name the JUE audience or the journal's urban economics, spatial equilibrium, housing, transport, local public finance, and neighborhood sorting scope.
- The contribution could fit several siblings: Journal of Public Economics, Journal of Economic Geography, Regional Science and Urban Economics, and AEJ Applied.
- The mechanism words that matter here are absent: spatial equilibrium, housing supply, commuting margin, local public goods.

## After

> This paper asks how **spatial equilibrium** changes the decision margin that JUE readers care about. The design ties the estimate to **housing supply**, separates the main mechanism from **commuting margin**, and reports uncertainty in a way that makes the policy or theory implication portable beyond the study setting.
