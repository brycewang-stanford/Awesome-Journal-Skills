## How to use this library

Pick the row whose **topic × design** is closest to your study, then study how that paper meets the NEJM bar:
a pre-specified primary outcome reported as an effect size with a confidence interval, terse IMRAD, sober
clinical implications, and full CONSORT/ICMJE compliance (see
[`../../skills/nejm-writing/SKILL.md`](../../skills/nejm-writing/SKILL.md),

question before claiming your write-up is "NEJM-shaped."

## By design

### Randomized controlled trial — superiority (cardiology / prevention)

- **The SPRINT Research Group, "A Randomized Trial of Intensive versus Standard Blood-Pressure Control,"
  N Engl J Med 2015;373:2103–2116. DOI 10.1056/NEJMoa1511939.**
  Verified: `nejm.org/doi/full/10.1056/NEJMoa1511939` (also `/doi/abs/10.1056/NEJMoa1511939`).
  - **Design positioning:** large multicenter superiority RCT randomizing 9,361 high-cardiovascular-risk
    adults without diabetes to a systolic target <120 vs <140 mm Hg; a single pre-specified composite
    primary outcome; stopped early on benefit. A model for reporting a composite cardiovascular endpoint with
    a hazard ratio and CI, plus the higher adverse-event rates in the intensive arm.
  - **Self-check:** is your primary outcome single, pre-specified, and reported as an effect size with a 95%
    CI — and do you report the harms alongside the benefit?

### Randomized controlled trial — non-inferiority (anticoagulation / atrial fibrillation)

- **Connolly SJ et al. (RE-LY Steering Committee and Investigators), "Dabigatran versus Warfarin in Patients
  with Atrial Fibrillation," N Engl J Med 2009;361:1139–1151. DOI 10.1056/NEJMoa0905561.**
  Verified: `nejm.org/doi/full/10.1056/NEJMoa0905561`.
  - **Design positioning:** 18,113-patient non-inferiority trial of two blinded dabigatran doses vs open-label
    warfarin for stroke/systemic-embolism prevention. The exemplar for a **non-inferiority** design — where
    [`../../skills/nejm-statistics/SKILL.md`](../../skills/nejm-statistics/SKILL.md) requires stating the
    pre-specified margin and reporting both ITT and per-protocol analyses.
  - **Self-check:** for a non-inferiority claim, did you pre-specify and justify the margin, and report both
    analysis populations rather than ITT alone?

### Randomized controlled trial — vaccine efficacy (infectious disease)

- **Polack FP et al. (C4591001 Trial Group), "Safety and Efficacy of the BNT162b2 mRNA Covid-19 Vaccine,"
  N Engl J Med 2020;383:2603–2615. DOI 10.1056/NEJMoa2034577.**
  Verified: `nejm.org/doi/full/10.1056/NEJMoa2034577`.
  - **Design positioning:** large, placebo-controlled, observer-blinded efficacy RCT (two 30-µg doses 21 days
    apart) reporting vaccine efficacy with a credible interval and a paired safety profile. A reference point
    for reporting efficacy as an effect estimate with interval and tying enrollment to the analysis cohort.
  - **Self-check:** is efficacy reported as an effect estimate with an interval (not a bare percentage), with
    the safety profile given equal prominence?

### Randomized controlled trial — pragmatic platform trial (critical care / Covid-19)

- **The RECOVERY Collaborative Group, "Dexamethasone in Hospitalized Patients with Covid-19,"
  N Engl J Med 2021;384:693–704. DOI 10.1056/NEJMoa2021436.**
  Verified: `nejm.org/doi/full/10.1056/NEJMoa2021436`.
  - **Design positioning:** controlled, open-label, randomized **platform** trial showing a mortality benefit
    concentrated in patients receiving respiratory support, with a signal toward no benefit (and possible
    harm) in those on no oxygen. A model for honest **subgroup-by-respiratory-support** reporting with rate
    ratios and CIs — exactly the pre-specified-subgroup discipline in
    [`../../skills/nejm-statistics/SKILL.md`](../../skills/nejm-statistics/SKILL.md).
  - **Self-check:** are your subgroups pre-specified and read through an interaction test, rather than a
    headline-grabbing within-subgroup result?

### Randomized controlled trial — chronic disease, drug class effect (heart failure)

- **Packer M et al. (EMPEROR-Reduced Trial Investigators), "Cardiovascular and Renal Outcomes with
  Empagliflozin in Heart Failure," N Engl J Med 2020;383:1413–1424. DOI 10.1056/NEJMoa2022190.**
  Verified: `nejm.org/doi/full/10.1056/NEJMoa2022190`.
  - **Design positioning:** double-blind, placebo-controlled RCT in 3,730 patients with heart failure and
    reduced ejection fraction, reporting a composite of cardiovascular death or heart-failure hospitalization
    with a hazard ratio and CI, with effects examined regardless of diabetes status. A model for a clean
    composite primary endpoint plus pre-specified key secondary (renal) outcomes.
  - **Self-check:** is your composite primary outcome pre-specified and its components reported, with key
    secondary outcomes inside a controlled testing hierarchy?

### Clinical review / synthesis article (nutrition–cardiovascular)

- **Mozaffarian D, Katan MB, Ascherio A, Stampfer MJ, Willett WC, "Trans Fatty Acids and Cardiovascular
  Disease," N Engl J Med 2006;354:1601–1613. DOI 10.1056/NEJMra054035.**
  Verified: `nejm.org/doi/full/10.1056/NEJMra054035` (the `NEJMra` prefix marks an NEJM **review** article).
  - **Design positioning:** a commissioned **review** synthesizing physiologic and epidemiologic evidence
    linking trans-fat intake to coronary heart disease. The exemplar for the **Review** article type in
    [`../../skills/nejm-writing/SKILL.md`](../../skills/nejm-writing/SKILL.md) — a vetted clinical synthesis
    with a different structure from an Original Article, drawing a calibrated practice-relevant conclusion
    from observational evidence with appropriately cautious, non-causal framing.
  - **Self-check:** if you are writing synthesis rather than a single trial, is the evidence weighed
    transparently and the language kept non-causal where the underlying data are observational?
