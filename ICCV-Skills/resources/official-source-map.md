# Official Source Map - ICCV

Access date: 2026-07-08

This map records the official sources behind every ICCV-specific fact in this pack.
ICCV is biennial (odd years) and each edition's chairs rewrite process details; before
giving submission-ready advice, reopen the current-cycle Call for Papers, Dates,
Author Guidelines, Reviewer Guidelines, Submission Checklist, and the OpenReview
group for the edition you are targeting.

**Access-method note (2026-07-08):** direct fetches of `iccv.thecvf.com` and
`tc.computer.org` returned HTTP 403 from the verification environment's gateway.
Official-page contents were therefore read through search-engine renderings of the
exact URLs listed below, and cross-checked against the ICCV OpenReview groups, the
CVF open-access repository, dblp records, the Computer Vision Foundation site, and
the official @ICCVConference announcements. Open the primary URLs directly before
relying on any date, cap, or policy sentence in this pack.

**Cycle status at access time:** ICCV 2025 (Honolulu, October 19–23, 2025) is the
most recent completed edition and supplies every verified anchor here. ICCV 2027 was
announced for Hong Kong, October 2–8, 2027, with chairs listed on the CVF upcoming-
conferences page, but **no CFP, deadlines, or policy pages existed at check time** —
every 2027 process statement is 待核实.

## Verified 2025-cycle facts used in the skills

- ICCV 2025 ran October 19–23, 2025 in Honolulu, Hawai'i (workshops October 19–20,
  main conference October 21–23), under IEEE/CVF sponsorship.
- Submission ran on OpenReview: registration (title, abstract, authors, complete
  profiles) due March 3, 2025; the paper **and** supplementary material due together
  March 7, 2025, 11:59pm HST — the unified deadline was an announced 2025 change.
- Scale: 11,239 valid submissions, 2,699 accepted, a 24% acceptance rate (program-
  chair statistics as relayed by the official conference account and press).
- Format: 8 pages including figures and tables; extra pages for cited references
  only; official author kit with a one-page rebuttal template.
- Review timeline: reviews to authors May 9, 2025; one-page rebuttal May 10–16,
  2025; decisions late June 2025 (the exact decision date was not re-fetchable —
  treat "June 25/26" datings as reported).
- Reviewer duty: every author served as a reviewer unless exempt (new to or outside
  the vision community) or holding another role; authors were capped at 25
  registered submissions; late or highly irresponsible reviewing was sanctioned by
  desk rejection of the reviewer's authored papers.
- 2025 enforcement outcome: 25 reviewers were found highly irresponsible and 29 of
  their authored papers were desk-rejected, including 12 that would otherwise have
  been accepted (program-chair announcement, widely mirrored).
- Reviewer LLM rule: reviews and meta-reviews may not be written with LLMs; LLM-
  generated reviews were listed among highly irresponsible behaviors.
- Anonymity: double-blind; arXiv posting is not an author-side anonymity violation;
  authors must not cite their public codebase (write "code will be made publicly
  available"); papers under review are media-embargoed until acceptance, with
  removal from conference and proceedings as the sanction.
- Dual submission: GitHub documents are not publications for the policy's purposes.
- Publication: CVF open access (free) plus IEEE Xplore (version of record); no
  article-processing charge — and like every CS conference in this collection, ICCV
  has **no standing editor-in-chief**: General and Program Chairs rotate per
  edition.
- The Marr Prize (ICCV best paper) 2025 went to the BrickGPT paper; earlier Marr
  Prizes anchor the exemplars library (Mask R-CNN 2017, Swin Transformer 2021,
  ControlNet 2023).
- The Findings workshop (new in 2025) offered a peer-reviewed outlet for technically
  sound papers rejected from or withdrawn from the main conference.
