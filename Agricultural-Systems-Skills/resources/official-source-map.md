## Aims & scope

- Agricultural Systems deals with interactions among components and hierarchical levels of agricultural
  systems, between agricultural and other land-use systems, and between agricultural systems and their
  natural, social, and economic environments.
- Submissions generally need both **substantive natural-science content** and substantive analysis or
  discussion of interactions within or among agricultural-system components and other systems.
- The journal gives preference to **whole-farm and landscape-level issues** via integration of
  conceptual, empirical, and dynamic-modelling approaches.
- Discouraged submissions include systems-free econometric or descriptive studies, typologies without
  downstream systems analysis, crop/livestock trials that do not address systems issues, social or
  political studies without a clear agricultural-systems framework, conceptual frameworks without
  empirical implementation unless submitted as a short communication, chemical-constituent studies,
  machinery/food-processing/supply-chain studies without substantive biology, and descriptive LCA
  without interaction-focused systems analysis.

## Article types, length, and front matter

- Article types: **original scientific papers, short communications, reviews, perspectives, and
  comments**.
- Review, perspective, and book-review submissions should be made only after consultation with the
  editors. Reviews generally focus on application of specific methods rather than descriptive analyses
  of agricultural production systems or supply chains.
- No absolute word or page limit is stated. Recommended maximums: **8,000 words** for research papers,
  **4,000** for short communications, **2,000** for perspectives, and **1,000** for comments. Longer
  submissions may be returned for modification.
- Perspective articles are future-focused, usually invited, considered if volunteered, and should seek
  editor advice before submission; they use a rapid review process.
- Abstract: **250 words or fewer**, stating purpose, principal results, and major conclusions.
- Keywords: **1 to 7**.
- Highlights: **required at submission** as a separate editable file; **3 to 5** bullet points, each
  **85 characters or fewer including spaces**.
- Graphical abstract: **required** as a separate file; minimum **531 x 1328 pixels** height by width or
  proportionally larger, readable at **5 x 13 cm** with preferred formats TIFF, EPS, PDF, or MS Office.

## Peer review & editorial policy

- Peer review is **single anonymized**: reviewers know author identities, authors do not know reviewer
  identities.
- The editor first checks suitability for the journal. Suitable submissions are typically sent to a
  **minimum of two independent expert reviewers**.
- The journal editor makes the accept-or-reject decision. For special issues, the guest editor may
  recommend a decision, but a journal editor makes the final decision.
- Perspective articles use a **rapid review** process because they are expected to be topical and
  time-sensitive.

## Research data, code & model availability

- The Guide for Authors states that research data can include software, code, models, algorithms,
  protocols, methods, and related project materials.
- For Agricultural Systems, **Option C** research-data instructions apply: authors are required to
  deposit research data in a relevant repository, cite and link to the dataset in the article, or make
  a statement explaining why sharing is not possible.
- A data statement is required at submission and will appear with the published article on
  ScienceDirect.
- The guide encourages dataset citation in the article and reference list, including author names,
  dataset title, repository, version where available, year, persistent identifier, and the `[dataset]`
  marker before the reference.

## Declarations and submission checklist

- Submission declaration: the work is not under consideration elsewhere, has not been previously
  published except for allowed forms such as preprints or theses, and may be checked by Elsevier
  screening tools.
- Competing interests: all authors must disclose relevant relationships; the Elsevier declarations tool
  should be completed and uploaded as a Word document when required.
- Funding: authors must disclose financial support and sponsor roles; if no funding supported the work,
  the guide recommends a no-funding statement.
- Generative AI: use of AI tools in manuscript preparation must be declared at submission; authors
  remain accountable for accuracy, originality, source checking, and privacy/IP safeguards.
- CRediT: corresponding authors are required to acknowledge co-author contributions using CRediT roles.
- Submission checklist covers corresponding-author contact details, all files and supplementary
  materials, spelling/grammar checks, reference cross-checking, permissions, and APC responsibility for
  open-access articles if accepted.
