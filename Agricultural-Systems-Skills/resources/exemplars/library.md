## How to use this library

Pick the row whose **topic × method** is closest to yours, then study how that paper makes the *system*
(interactions, hierarchical levels, trade-offs) — not a single factor — carry the contribution. That is
the AgSy bar (see [`../../skills/agsy-topic-selection/SKILL.md`](../../skills/agsy-topic-selection/SKILL.md)
and [`../../skills/agsy-systems-framing-and-modeling/SKILL.md`](../../skills/agsy-systems-framing-and-modeling/SKILL.md)).
For each, ask the self-check before claiming your paper is "AgSy-shaped."

## By method

### Field-to-global synthesis / state-of-science review (systems-modelling agenda)

- **Jones, Antle, Basso, Boote, Conant, Foster, Godfray, Herrero, Howitt, Janssen, Keating,
  Munoz-Carpena, Porter, Rosenzweig & Wheeler, "Toward a new generation of agricultural system data,
  models, and knowledge products: State of agricultural systems science," _Agricultural Systems_ 2017,
  155:269–288.** Verified: DOI `10.1016/j.agsy.2016.09.021` (ScienceDirect pii `S0308521X1630590X`;
  open access on PMC `PMC5485672`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** the touchstone statement of what "agricultural systems science" *is* —
    integrated **data + models + knowledge products** evaluated across field → farm → landscape →
    regional → global use cases. The reference frame for the whole pack.
  - **Self-check:** does your work locate itself on this field-to-global ladder and say which level(s) it
    models and links?

### Process / cropping-systems simulation framework (model development)

- **McCown, Hammer, Hargreaves, Holzworth & Freebairn, "APSIM: a novel software system for model
  development, model testing and simulation in agricultural systems research," _Agricultural Systems_
  1996, 50(3):255–271.** Verified: DOI `10.1016/0308-521X(94)00055-V` (ScienceDirect pii
  `0308521X9400055V`; the `0308-521X` ISSN confirms _Agricultural Systems_, not a sibling).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** establishes a **re-configurable** crop–soil–water–nutrient simulator whose
    point is to study *production systems*, not one crop — the model-as-systems-tool stance behind
    `agsy-systems-framing-and-modeling`.
  - **Self-check:** is your model described well enough — components, structure, version — that another
    group could re-configure and re-run it?
  - **Sibling caution:** the widely cited Keating et al. 2003 "An overview of APSIM" is in the **European
    Journal of Agronomy**, not _Agricultural Systems_ — see omissions.

### Whole-farm bio-economic model + multi-objective optimisation (Pareto trade-offs)

- **Groot, Oomen & Rossing, "Multi-objective optimization and design of farming systems using a
  whole-farm model coupled to an evolutionary algorithm," _Agricultural Systems_ 2012, 110:63–77.**
  Verified: DOI `10.1016/j.agsy.2012.03.012` (ScienceDirect pii `S0308521X12000558`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** the **FarmDESIGN** + evolutionary-algorithm approach that generates a set
    of **Pareto-optimal** farm configurations — the canonical "trade-offs and synergies, not one optimal
    number" design that `agsy-data-and-model-evaluation` rewards.
  - **Self-check:** do you compare configurations on **multiple objectives** and show a frontier, rather
    than declaring one scenario "best"?

### Whole-farm bio-economic model extended to the household (social–economic–environmental trade-offs)

- **Ditzler, Komarek, Chiang, Alvarez, Chatterjee, Timler, Raneri, Estrada-Carmona, Kennedy & Groot, "A
  model to examine farm household trade-offs and synergies with an application to smallholders in
  Vietnam," _Agricultural Systems_ 2019, 173:49–63.** Verified: ScienceDirect pii `S0308521X18307340`
  (RePEc `agisys v173 2019 p49-63`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** extends a whole-farm model with **budget, labour and human-nutrition**
    modules and positions the enterprise inside the **household**, surfacing trade-offs among
    environmental, economic and **social** objectives — the integration AgSy's scope asks for.
  - **Self-check:** does your system include the **social/economic** side (labour, nutrition, livelihood),
    not just the biophysical, when those interactions drive the result?

### Whole-farm economics on a process-model base, under risk (decision-relevant trade-off)

- **Cann, Hunt & Malcolm, "Long fallows can maintain whole-farm profit and reduce risk in semi-arid
  south-eastern Australia," _Agricultural Systems_ 2020, 178:102721.** Verified: DOI
  `10.1016/j.agsy.2019.102721` (ScienceDirect pii `S0308521X18307406`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** drives **whole-farm** economic analysis with crop-model output to evaluate
    a rotation choice on **both profit and inter-annual risk** under rainfall variability — a clean
    profit-vs-risk trade-off scaled from field to farm with a clear management decision.
  - **Self-check:** is your result a **trade-off scaled to the whole farm** (e.g., mean vs. variability),
    tied to a concrete management choice — not a plot-level mean?

### Regional / multi-scale integrated assessment framework (policy-to-farm linkage)

- **van Ittersum, Ewert, Heckelei, Wery, Alkan Olsson, Andersen, Bezlepkina, Brouwer, Donatelli,
  Flichman, Olsson, Rizzoli, van der Wal, Wien & Wolf, "Integrated assessment of agricultural systems –
  A component-based framework for the European Union (SEAMLESS)," _Agricultural Systems_ 2008,
  96(1–3):150–165.** Verified: DOI `10.1016/j.agsy.2007.07.009` (ScienceDirect pii `S0308521X07000893`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a modular framework linking **micro (farm) to macro (regional/EU)** models
    to assess policy impacts on the **economic, environmental and social** sustainability of agricultural
    systems across scales — the integrated-assessment end of `agsy-systems-framing-and-modeling`.
  - **Self-check:** when you cross scales (farm → region → policy), do you state **how** levels link and
    aggregate, and check that aggregation does not hide compensating errors?

  the article's ScienceDirect/DOI page before adding any of them here.

in doubt, omit.
