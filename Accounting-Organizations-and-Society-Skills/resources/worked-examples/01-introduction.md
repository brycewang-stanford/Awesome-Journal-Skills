# Worked Example: An AOS-Style Introduction (before → after)

The genre demonstrated is the **qualitative field study** — AOS's modal form — where the introduction
must do four things the sibling journals' intros do not: enter one of the journal's own long-running
conversations, put a named theoretical lens visibly to work, preview a *conceptual* (not correlational)
answer, and keep the accounting practice analytically central throughout.

**Illustrative paper (fictional):** *"Making Green Calculable: Carbon Accounting and the Remaking of
Operational Authority at an Industrial Firm."* Fictional setting: "NordSteel," a pseudonymous European
manufacturer that introduced an internal carbon-accounting system, studied through 26 months of
interviews and observation.

---

## Before (competent but wrong for AOS — a management-journal intro)

> Sustainability is increasingly important for firms, and carbon accounting is a growing practice.
> However, little is known about how carbon accounting is implemented inside organizations. We address
> this gap through a qualitative case study of a European manufacturer. Drawing on 74 interviews, we
> identify three implementation challenges and develop a framework with practical implications for
> managers seeking to implement carbon accounting successfully. Our study contributes to the literatures
> on sustainability accounting, management control, and organizational change.

**What fails (against the aos-* skills):**

- **Gap-spotting** ("little is known…") — no live disagreement, no AOS conversation entered
  (`aos-literature-positioning` anti-pattern).
- **No theoretical lens** — the study promises a "framework" and "challenges," i.e., description with
  headings (`aos-theory-development`: theory as wallpaper, or here, absent).
- **Managerialist payoff** ("practical implications for managers") — AOS's currency is conceptual
  insight into accounting's social operation, not implementation advice
  (`aos-contribution-framing` anti-pattern).
- **Inventory contribution** ("contributes to three literatures") — lists, not a claim.
- The accounting practice is scenery; nothing here requires *accounting* rather than any new IT system.

---

## After (the AOS arc — conversation, lens, conceptual answer, accounting central)

> When NordSteel's engineers first saw their furnaces described in tonnes of CO₂ per production run,
> the plant's oldest authority relation began to shift: decisions that had belonged to metallurgical
> judgment became answerable to a spreadsheet maintained two floors up. This paper asks how a new
> calculative practice — internal carbon accounting — redistributes the authority to say what counts as
> good operational work. *(concrete scene; the accounting practice and the social stake in the first
> breath.)*
>
> Since Burchell et al. (1980), AOS scholarship has held that accounting acquires organizational roles
> far beyond its official ones, and the governmentality tradition has specified one such role with
> precision: calculation renders persons and domains governable (Miller & O'Leary 1987). That
> conversation, however, developed around practices — standard costing, budgeting — whose object was
> already economic. Carbon accounting is different: it must first make an *engineering* world calculable
> in a currency nobody previously managed by. Whether the governable-person account survives this
> translation, and what happens to existing expertise when it occurs, is unresolved. *(enters a named
> AOS conversation; states the settled view and the crack in it — not a "gap" but a live theoretical
> question.)*
>
> We study this question through a 26-month field study at NordSteel, combining 74 interviews across
> four occupational groups with observation of the monthly "carbon review" meetings and the system's
> design documents. *(design in one sentence-pair, as a tool; the data inventory is previewed, not
> paraded.)*
>
> Our answer is that carbon accounting did not simply extend calculative government to a new domain — it
> produced what we call **calculative brokerage**: a new position, occupied by the site's carbon
> controllers, whose authority rested on translating between metallurgical judgment and the metric, and
> who could therefore discipline *both* sides of that boundary. Existing expertise was neither displaced
> (the deskilling reading) nor preserved (the decoupling reading); it was re-anchored in a relation of
> translation. *(the conceptual answer previewed — a named concept, positioned against two rival
> readings; the mystery-novel structure refused.)*
>
> The contribution is threefold in substance but single in claim: the governable-person account requires
> a broker wherever calculation must annex a domain with its own strong epistemic culture. This
> qualifies the governmentality conversation's assumption that programmes and technologies meet the
> governed directly; it returns something to the imported theory — a scope condition — rather than
> merely applying it; and it explains why carbon numbers, so often dismissed as decoupled greenwash,
> can nonetheless reorganize authority on the shop floor. *(one falsifiable-in-spirit conceptual claim;
> the give-back to the theory named; the accounting practice still central.)*
>
> The paper proceeds by developing the theoretical frame (Section 2), describing the site and method
> (Sections 3–4), presenting the findings as three movements in the making of calculative brokerage
> (Section 5), and drawing out the implications for the governmentality conversation and for research on
> environmental calculation (Section 6). *(brief functional roadmap.)*

---

## Why the "after" clears the AOS bar

- **Conversation, not gap** — the intro argues with Burchell et al. (1980) and Miller & O'Leary (1987)
  as a live position, and names the unresolved question the case can decide
  (`aos-literature-positioning`).
- **The lens works from sentence one** — governmentality vocabulary (calculability, government,
  programmes/technologies) organizes the question rather than decorating it; two rival readings
  (deskilling vs decoupling) give the finding something to beat (`aos-theory-development`).
- **Conceptual answer previewed** — the named concept ("calculative brokerage") appears in the
  introduction, with its scope condition; AOS readers learn the claim before the methods
  (`aos-writing-style`: no suspense).
- **Give-back stated** — the imported theory receives a scope condition, satisfying the AOS expectation
  that cases teach theory, not just borrow it (`aos-contribution-framing`).
- **Accounting stays central** — the object throughout is the calculative practice and its authority
  effects; remove the accounting and the paper collapses, which is exactly the topic-selection test.
- **Evidence discipline previewed** — data inventory (74 interviews, four groups, 26 months,
  observation, documents) foreshadows the Table 1 that `aos-tables-figures` requires.
