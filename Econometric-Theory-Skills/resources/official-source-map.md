## Verified facts from official pages

- [官] ET publishes original, rigorous theoretical econometrics across major areas of the field, including probabilistic and statistical foundations for modeling, estimation, prediction, and inference.
- [官] The journal welcomes theory connected to large-scale data, high-performance computing, artificial intelligence, probability, stochastic processes, statistics, mathematical finance, and computational economics.
- [官] Accepted article types listed by Cambridge include Articles, Miscellanea, and ET Interviews.
- [官] Miscellanea is for short papers with one or two key innovations, stated and proved concisely; Cambridge describes typical length as fewer than 20 pages and often around 15 pages.
- [官] Peer review is single-anonymous: reviewers know author identity, authors do not know reviewer identity.
- [官] Supplementary material can hold long derivations, supporting lemmas, tables, figures, and similar material when directed by the editor/coeditors; it should be clearly labeled and only include material that has been reviewed before acceptance.

## Sibling-journal guard

- *Econometric Theory* (Cambridge University Press) is not *The Econometrics Journal* (RES/OUP).
- *Econometric Theory* is not *Journal of Econometrics* (Elsevier).
- *Econometric Theory* is not *Econometrica* (Econometric Society).
