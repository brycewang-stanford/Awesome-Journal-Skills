# Econometric Theory Official Source Map

Primary and authoritative sources behind the **Econometric Theory (ET)**-specific facts injected
into this skill pack. ET is a Cambridge University Press journal founded by Peter C. B. Phillips.

**Access date for all entries: 2026-06-01.** Re-verify volatile items (editors, fees, portal URL,
exact word/length caps, policy wording) before relying on them — journal pages change, and ET went
through a major transition in January 2026 (new editors, new submission system).

## Official journal pages (Cambridge University Press)

- **https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/econometric-theory** — ET home page. Verifies:
  publisher = Cambridge University Press; aims/scope = mathematical and statistical foundations of
  econometrics; editorial leadership.
- **https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/econometric-theory/information/author-instructions/submitting-your-materials**
  — Submitting Your Materials. Verifies: submission via **ScholarOne / Manuscript Central** at
  `https://mc.manuscriptcentral.com/econ-theory`, effective **28 January 2026**, replacing the
  previous Editorial Express system; manuscripts submitted as a **single PDF** with all fonts,
  tables and graphics embedded; **cover letter required only** when authors must convey
  review-relevant information (e.g., to share prior reviews from another journal), otherwise
  optional; work must not be published or under review elsewhere.
- **https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/econometric-theory/information/instructions-contributors**
  — Instructions for Contributors. Verifies: **single-anonymous** peer review ("the author does
  not know the identity of the reviewers, but the reviewers know the identity of the author");
  article types = **Articles** (Regular, Invited, Themed, ET-lecture), **Miscellanea** (short
  papers, typically <20 pages, often ~15), and **ET Interviews**.
- **https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/econometric-theory/information/author-instructions/preparing-your-materials**
  — Preparing Your Materials. Verifies: **minimum 11-point font, at least 1.5 line spacing,
  margins no less than 1.25 inches** on all sides, consecutively numbered pages; **Regular Articles
  must not exceed 50 pages** including title page, tables, figures, references and appendices;
  **running head no more than 40 characters**; **abstract no more than 200 words**; references and
  in-text citation follow the **APA Style Guide** (author-date); figures as **TIFF** (line art
  >=600 dpi, greyscale >=300 dpi), **EPS** (fonts embedded) or **PDF**, legible at 50% reduction.
- **https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/econometric-theory/information/instructions-contributors/author-instructions-for-online-publication-of-supplementary-material**
  — Supplementary-material policy. Verifies: only critical, **already-reviewed** material (long
  mathematical derivations, supporting lemmas, extra tables/figures) may be posted online with the
  article; submitted in a **separate clearly labeled file** with the final manuscript; **not
  copyedited or typeset**; **may not introduce new unseen material**.
- **https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/econometric-theory/information/about-this-journal/editorial-board**
  — Editorial board. Verifies: **three joint Editors-in-Chief effective 1 January 2026** —
  **Patrik Guggenberger** (Penn State), **Liangjun Su** (Tsinghua University), **Yixiao Sun**
  (UC San Diego) — succeeding **founding editor Peter C. B. Phillips** (ET Editor 1985–2025); new
  programs include invited papers with discussions, themed special issues, and the **biennial
  Peter C. B. Phillips Award** for the best paper by a scholar within 6 years of their first PhD.
- **https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/econometric-theory/et-interviews** — The **ET
  Interviews**. Verifies: a signature journal-unique genre running since the **1985** first issue
  (which opened with Phillips interviewing Denis Sargan); in-depth, invited interviews with leading
  econometricians; an invited feature, not author-submitted.

## Distinctive-norms cross-references

- **Theorem-proof house style; overflow to online Supplementary Material** — preparing-your-materials
  (50-page ceiling) + supplementary-material policy pages above.
- **Two-track Articles vs Miscellanea structure** — instructions-contributors page.
- **APA author-date references** — preparing-your-materials page.
- **Single-anonymous review** — instructions-contributors page.
- **Jan 2026 editorial program** (invited papers with discussions; themed special issues on
  Econometric Theory for Machine Learning/AI, Causal Inference under Complex Interference, Robust
  Inference and Partial Identification, High-Dimensional and Non-Standard Environments,
  Network/Spatial Econometrics; Phillips Award) — editorial-board page + search-result excerpts.
