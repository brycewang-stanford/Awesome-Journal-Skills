# External Tools & Resources for AHR-Style Historical Scholarship

Archives, databases, reference tools, and software useful when preparing a submission to *The American
Historical Review* (AHR). The AHR is a **humanities** flagship spanning **all periods and all places**
of the human past — ancient to contemporary; Africa, Asia, Oceania, Latin America, the Middle East,
Europe, and the Americas. The craft is **archival and primary-source research, source criticism,
historiographical argument, and interpretation** — not statistical modeling. Match tools to your
field, period, and languages. Check access terms, rights, and reproduction permissions before use, and
verify any AHR-specific policy in [`official-source-map.md`](official-source-map.md).

## 1. Finding primary sources (archives & catalogs)

| Resource | Provider | Typical use |
|----------|----------|-------------|
| ArchiveGrid | OCLC | Locating archival collections and finding aids worldwide |
| WorldCat | OCLC | Books, manuscripts, and holdings across libraries |
| National Archives (NARA, TNA, etc.) | National governments | Government records, correspondence, official series |
| Library of Congress / national libraries | LoC and equivalents | Manuscripts, maps, photographs, newspapers |
| Europeana / DPLA | EU / US aggregators | Aggregated digitized cultural-heritage objects |
| Internet Archive / HathiTrust | Nonprofit / consortium | Digitized books, periodicals, out-of-copyright texts |

## 2. Digitized primary-source databases (subject to subscription/licensing)

| Resource | Strength |
|----------|----------|
| JSTOR / Project MUSE | Scholarly journals and primary-source collections |
| Gale Primary Sources (e.g., State Papers Online, ECCO) | Government papers, eighteenth-century print |
| ProQuest Historical Newspapers / Newspapers.com | Long-run newspaper runs |
| EEBO / ECCO | Early English Books Online; Eighteenth Century Collections Online |
| Adam Matthew, Readex, Empire Online | Themed archival microform-to-digital collections |
| Regional/national portals (Gallica, Trove, etc.) | French BnF, Australian, and other national digitizations |

Cite the **archive, collection, box/folder, and document** for unpublished materials; cite the
**database and stable URL/DOI** where a source was consulted digitally (note the version/date).

## 3. Reference management & citation (Chicago notes)

- **Reference managers:** Zotero (with the *Chicago Manual of Style* note styles), EndNote, or
  BibLaTeX with a Chicago/`biblatex-chicago` style. The AHR uses **Chicago notes** — keep one
  consistent note style and **do not** generate a works-cited list / bibliography or in-text
  parenthetical citations.
- **The Chicago Manual of Style** (online or print) — the authority for note form, shortened
  citations, and ibid. usage; confirm the current edition and any AHR house departures.
- **Turabian** (*A Manual for Writers*) — a student-facing companion to CMOS notes.

## 4. Reading, transcription & language tools

- **Paleography / handwriting:** Transkribus (HTR for historical manuscripts), DigiPal-style tools.
- **OCR:** ABBYY FineReader, Tesseract for printed sources; always proof against the original.
- **Translation aids:** dictionaries and corpora for the source language; quote in the original where
  the wording is load-bearing and supply your own translation (note the translator).
- **Maps & spatial:** QGIS, ArcGIS StoryMaps, Palladio for exploratory historical geography.

## 5. Notes, drafting & manuscript prep

- **Note-taking:** Obsidian, DEVONthink, Tropy (photographs of archival documents organized by source).
- **Writing:** Microsoft Word (AHR requires a **Word** document) or LaTeX exported to Word; prepare a
  **masked** manuscript — **8½ × 11, Times New Roman 12 pt, double-spaced, numbered pages** — with no
  identifying metadata.
- **Images:** prepare figures at print resolution; write **alt-text** for every image (the AHR
  requires alt-text directly under each figure legend) and obtain **reproduction permissions** and
  rights clearance yourself before publication.

## 6. Process & portal

| Item | Note |
|------|------|
| Submission portal | **ScholarOne / Manuscript Central** (`mc.manuscriptcentral.com/ahrev`) — Word doc; no email |
| Owner / publisher | **American Historical Association (AHA)** / **Oxford University Press** |
| Review model | **Double-blind / anonymous** — mask the author's identity in the manuscript |
| Article length | Ideally **≤ 8,000 words**, not counting notes, tables, and charts |
| Notes | Chicago **footnotes/endnotes**; ~**2:1** text-to-notes guideline at initial submission |
| Reviewers | **At least six scholars**, sometimes more; decision in **6–8 months** |
| Acceptance | Roughly **8–10%** of ~360 submissions per year |
| Fees | **No author processing fees**; 50% discount on up to 10 print copies |
| Reviews section | Large **commissioned** book/media review section (~650/year) — not author-solicited |

## 7. Cautions

1. **Verify volatile specifics** (current editor, exact word/notes figures, whether an abstract is
   required, OA/APC options, review counts and timelines) on the official OUP/AHA pages — they change;
   unverified items are marked 待核实.
2. **Mask the manuscript properly** — the AHR is anonymous; strip identifying metadata and avoid
   self-revealing phrasing ("in my earlier book…").
3. **Criticize your sources** — provenance, audience, genre, and silences matter; do not treat a
   document as a transparent window onto the past (see `ahr-sources-and-archives`).
4. **Clear image rights yourself** — reproduction permissions and copyright clearance are the author's
   responsibility; budget time for them.
5. **Write for the whole discipline** — an AHR article must matter to historians of other places and
   periods, not only to specialists in your subfield (see `ahr-topic-selection`).
