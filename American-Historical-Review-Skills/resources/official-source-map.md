# AHR Official Source Map

Primary and authoritative sources behind the AHR-specific facts injected into this skill pack.
Access date for all entries: **2026-06-02**. Re-verify volatile items (current editor and term, exact
word/notes figures, whether an abstract is required, OA/APC options, review counts and timelines,
formatting rules) on the official page before relying on them — journal pages change.

## Official journal, submission & author pages

- **https://academic.oup.com/ahr/pages/Author_Guidelines** — *The American Historical Review*
  Author Guidelines (Oxford Academic). Verifies: publisher = **Oxford University Press**; owner =
  **American Historical Association (AHA)**; submission via **ScholarOne / Manuscript Central**
  (`mc.manuscriptcentral.com/ahrev`); manuscripts submitted as a **Microsoft Word** document
  (**email submissions not accepted**); article should **"ideally not exceed 8,000 words in length,
  not counting notes, tables, and charts"**; a **2:1 text-to-notes ratio** is "a good guideline for
  an initial submission" and a typical 8,000-word article carries **4,500–5,500 words of notes**;
  manuscript prepared **8½ × 11**, **Times New Roman 12 pt**, **double-spaced**, **numbered pages**,
  with the **author's identity masked**; **alt-text descriptions required for all images, figures,
  illustrations, and photographs**, placed directly under the figure legend; **"The *AHR* does not
  accept author processing fees"**; authors receive a **50% discount on up to 10 print copies**;
  decision targeted within **six to eight months**; the journal publishes roughly **8–10 percent** of
  the **~360 manuscripts** received per year; English-language requirement; explicit encouragement of
  histories of **Africa, Asia, Oceania, Latin America, and the Middle East**.
- **https://academic.oup.com/ahr/pages/General_Instructions** — General Instructions (Oxford
  Academic). Verifies: distinctive AHR content forms beyond research articles (e.g., **History
  Unclassified, History Lab, AHR Syllabus, Art as Historical Method, History in Focus, Engaged
  History**) and links to the manuscript and reviews guides.
- **https://academic.oup.com/ahr** — AHR on Oxford Academic (publisher home, current issues, advance
  articles).

## Peer review & exclusivity

- **https://academic.oup.com/ahr/pages/Author_Guidelines** — Verifies: review is **double-blind /
  anonymous**; **"Every article published in the *AHR* has been reviewed by at least six scholars,
  and sometimes more"**; roughly a quarter of submissions are sent out for full review; **exclusivity**
  — "No manuscript will be considered for publication if it is concurrently under consideration by
  another journal or press or if it has been published or is in submission, under contract, or in
  press elsewhere."

## Reviews (books and other media)

- **https://academic.oup.com/ahr/pages/reviews_guide** — Reviews Guide (Oxford Academic). Verifies:
  the AHR runs a large **commissioned** review section — **"We do not assign book reviews to scholars
  at their own suggestion; invitations to review are based on independent staff judgments"**; reviews
  span **books, exhibits, films, documentaries, podcasts, video games, graphic histories, and digital
  history**; **AHA membership is neither required for nor a guarantee of** selection as a reviewer.
- **https://www.historians.org/news-publications/american-historical-review/reviews/** — AHA Reviews
  landing page (owner side). Context on the review section and its scope.

## Citation style

- **https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_American_Historical_Review** and discipline style guides
  (e.g., university library guides citing AHR style) — Verifies: AHR uses **Chicago Manual of Style**
  notes (footnotes/endnotes); **all citation information lives in the notes**, with **no separate
  works-cited list or bibliography** and **no in-text parenthetical citations**. Confirm the current
  CMOS edition and any AHR house departures on the live submission/style guides.
- **https://www.historians.org/wp-content/uploads/2024/05/AHR-Style-Guide-for-Reviewers.pdf** —
  AHR Style Guide for Reviewers (AHA, PDF). Verifies AHR house style for reviews follows Chicago notes.

## Owner, publisher, editor, history

- **https://www.historians.org/news-publications/american-historical-review/** — AHA's AHR home
  (owner side). Verifies: the AHR is the **official publication / flagship journal of the AHA**.
- **https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_American_Historical_Review** — Verifies: founded **1895**;
  published by **Oxford University Press since 2023** (previously by the AHA, with University of
  Chicago Press handling production 2007–2011); quarterly (**March, June, September, December**);
  ISSN **0002-8762** (print) / **1937-5239** (online).
