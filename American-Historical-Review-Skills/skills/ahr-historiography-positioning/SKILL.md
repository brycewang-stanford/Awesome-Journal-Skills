# Historiography Positioning (ahr-historiography-positioning)

For the AHR, the **historiographical intervention is the contribution**. An article is not "here is
what I found in the archive" — it is "historians have understood X this way; the evidence shows it is
better understood this other way." Positioning is therefore not throat-clearing; it is the spine of the
article.

## When to trigger

- Drafting or revising the introduction and the "what this changes" passage
- A reader said you "don't engage the literature" or "missed the obvious work"
- Your archival findings are strong but you cannot say what they revise
- Distinguishing your argument from the closest prior interpretations

## How the AHR wants historiography engaged

1. **Name the conversation, not a citation pile.** Identify the live interpretive debate or the
   reigning account your article speaks to. Cite the works that *define* it.
2. **State the intervention precisely.** Not "little has been written" — say what the prevailing
   reading gets wrong, flattens, omits, or misperiodizes, and how your evidence revises it.
3. **Two audiences at once.** Satisfy specialists (you command the field) *and* generalists (why it
   matters to historians elsewhere). Avoid insider shorthand that locks others out.
4. **Be fair to your predecessors.** Represent prior interpretations accurately; the strongest
   interventions revise serious work, not strawmen.
5. **Locate yourself in historiographical turns where relevant** — social, cultural, transnational,
   global, environmental, the history of knowledge — without genuflecting to fashion.

## Intervention types (pick the honest one)

| Type | The move |
|------|----------|
| Revision | the standard interpretation is wrong or incomplete; here is the better reading |
| Reframing | the same events look different under a new category or scale |
| Recovery | a subject, archive, or actor the literature ignored, with stakes for the general picture |
| Synthesis | reconciling or reorganizing a fragmented historiography into a new account |
| Reperiodization | the conventional turning points mislead; the break lies elsewhere |

## Anti-patterns

- A "literature review" that lists works without an organizing debate
- Engaging only your own subfield when the claim implicates broader scholarship
- "First to study" framing when the real contribution is a reinterpretation
- Strawmanning or hiding the closest competing account
- Treating a recovered topic as self-justifying without a historiographical stake

## Output format

```
【Conversation】the live interpretive debate / reigning account
【Key works】the 3-6 that define it
【Intervention type】revision / reframing / recovery / synthesis / reperiodization
【What it changes】one sentence a non-specialist historian would grasp
【Closest rival reading】and how your evidence answers it
【Next】ahr-argument-development
```
