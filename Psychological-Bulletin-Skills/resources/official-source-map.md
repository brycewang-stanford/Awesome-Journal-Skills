# Psychological Bulletin Official Source Map

Primary and authoritative sources behind the Psychological Bulletin-specific facts injected into this

## Official journal & submission pages (APA)

- **https://www.apa.org/pubs/journals/bul** — Psychological Bulletin journal home (APA). Verifies:
  publisher = **American Psychological Association (APA)**; the journal **"publishes syntheses of
  research in scientific psychology"** — **evaluative and integrative research reviews and
  interpretations**, with **qualitative (narrative) and/or quantitative (meta-analytic)** content
  depending on the database under review; it is among psychology's **highest-impact** journals.
- **https://www.apa.org/pubs/journals/bul/index?tab=4** — Psychological Bulletin Submission
  Guidelines (APA, "Instructions to Authors" tab). Verifies:
  - **Scope / what it does NOT publish:** "Research syntheses should be submitted to *Psychological
    Bulletin* even when they develop integrated theoretical statements." **Original theoretical
    articles** go to *Psychological Review*; **original primary empirical studies** are not the
    journal's remit. Acceptable forms include **systematic reviews, meta-analyses, meta-reviews,
    meta-synthesis**, and **rigorous and thorough qualitative reviews**.
  - **Masked review:** "The identities of authors will be withheld from reviewers and will be
    revealed after determining the final disposition of the manuscript only upon request and with the
    permission of the authors." The manuscript itself must contain **no clues to author identity**
    (including deleting easily identified self-references from the reference list).
  - **Abstract:** "All manuscripts must include an abstract containing a **maximum of 250 words**
    typed on a separate page."
  - **Style:** the **Publication Manual of the American Psychological Association, 7th edition**.
  - **MARS:** authors using **meta-analytic methods must consult the APA Style Meta-Analysis
    Reporting Standards (MARS)**.
  - **PRISMA:** **all systematic reviews must conform to PRISMA** (Preferred Reporting Items for
    Systematic Reviews and Meta-Analyses).
  - **JARS:** quantitative and mixed-methods submissions must adhere to **APA Style Journal Article
    Reporting Standards (JARS)**.
  - **Preregistration:** the article must **state whether the design and (if applicable) hypotheses
    were preregistered and, if so, where to access it**.
  - **PROSPERO note:** *Bulletin* submissions registered on **PROSPERO** can attach the registration
    details as an appendix, **omitting author names and the registration number** (for masking).
  - **Transparency / data:** reviews with quantitative analyses (e.g., meta-analyses) must **state
    whether data and study materials are available and where**; authors who submit quantitative
    analyses must **supply their databases, codebook, and relevant scripts**.
  - No submission fee is stated in the guidelines.

## Reporting standards & transparency frameworks

- **https://apastyle.apa.org/jars/quant-table-9.pdf** — **MARS** (Meta-Analysis Reporting Standards)
  table within APA Style JARS. Verifies the standardized items meta-analyses must report (search,
  eligibility, coding, effect-size metric, model, heterogeneity, moderators, bias assessment).
- **https://apastyle.apa.org/jars** — APA Style **JARS** hub (quantitative, qualitative, mixed,
  and the meta-analysis standards / MARS). Verifies JARS-Quant / JARS-Qual / MARS structure.
- **http://www.prisma-statement.org/** — **PRISMA 2020** statement, checklist, and **flow diagram**.
  Verifies systematic-search reporting expectations and the PRISMA flow diagram (records identified
  → screened → eligible → included, with exclusion reasons).
- **https://www.crd.york.ac.uk/prospero/** — **PROSPERO**, the international prospective register of
  systematic reviews (Centre for Reviews and Dissemination, University of York). Verifies the
  preregistration venue referenced in the guidelines.
- **https://www.cos.io/initiatives/top-guidelines** — **TOP Guidelines** (Transparency and Openness
  Promotion), Center for Open Science. APA journals including *Psychological Bulletin* adopt TOP;
  *Bulletin* requires transparent reporting for empirical work including meta-analyses **effective
  February 1, 2022** (see 需复核 on the exact level wording on the live page).

## Owner, publisher, history

- **https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Psychological_Bulletin** — Verifies: Psychological Bulletin is
  published by the **American Psychological Association**; founded **1904**; publishes evaluative and
  integrative research reviews (qualitative/narrative and quantitative/meta-analytic); among the
  highest-impact journals in psychology.
