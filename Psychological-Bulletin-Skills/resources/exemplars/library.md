# *Psychological Bulletin* Exemplars Library (method × topic)

> **Verified via web search, access date 2026-06-08.** Every paper below was checked to confirm it
> actually appeared in **_Psychological Bulletin_** — published by the **American Psychological
> Association (APA)**, founded 1904, the APA flagship for **syntheses of research in scientific
> psychology** (evaluative and integrative reviews; quantitative meta-analyses and/or qualitative
> narrative reviews — **not** primary empirical studies and **not** original theory) — with year and
> volume(issue):pages, against the APA record (`psycnet.apa.org` / `doi.org/10.1037/...`), PubMed, and
> Google Scholar lookups carrying the journal name. Papers that could not be fully verified **as
> _Psychological Bulletin_** were **omitted** — this is deliberately a short, clean list (5 verified),
> not a long, uncertain one.
>
> **Sibling-journal guard.** _Psychological Bulletin_ is **not** *Psychological Review* (original
> **theory**), *Annual Review of Psychology* or *Perspectives on Psychological Science* (invited /
> agenda reviews), *American Psychologist*, *Psychological Science*, *Review of General Psychology*, or
> medical-synthesis venues like *PLoS Medicine*. Many famous "review" and "meta-analysis" papers live in
> those venues — and the most common trap is attributing a **theory** paper (which belongs in
> *Psychological Review*) to *Bulletin*. The
> [Omitted](#omitted-for-lack-of-full-verification-do-not-attribute-to-psychological-bulletin) section
> records the traps checked and rejected.
>
> **Use principle (zero hallucination):** this file gives **design positioning only**. It does not
> reproduce or invent effect sizes, k (study counts), heterogeneity statistics, or moderator results —
> read the original on APA PsycNet before citing any number. For _Psychological Bulletin_ scope,
> synthesis types, masking, MARS/PRISMA/PROSPERO, and TOP policy, see
> [`../official-source-map.md`](../official-source-map.md).

## How to use this library

Pick the row whose **synthesis type × question** is closest to yours, then study how that review turns a
scattered literature into a **single, general, defensible conclusion about an effect's size, its
moderators, or the state of a debate** — the _Psychological Bulletin_ bar (see
[`../../skills/psychbull-topic-selection/SKILL.md`](../../skills/psychbull-topic-selection/SKILL.md),
[`../../skills/psychbull-meta-analysis-methods/SKILL.md`](../../skills/psychbull-meta-analysis-methods/SKILL.md),
and [`../../skills/psychbull-theory-integration/SKILL.md`](../../skills/psychbull-theory-integration/SKILL.md)).
For each, ask the self-check question before claiming your review is "_Bulletin_-shaped." Remember the
scope gate: a primary study belongs elsewhere, and **pure theory belongs in _Psychological Review_** —
*Bulletin* publishes the **synthesis**.

## By method

### Random-effects meta-analysis with a substantive cross-cultural moderator

- **Bond & Smith, "Culture and Conformity: A Meta-Analysis of Studies Using Asch's (1952b, 1956) Line
  Judgment Task," _Psychological Bulletin_ 1996, 119(1):111–137.**
  Verified: APA `doi.org/10.1037/0033-2909.119.1.111`; ProQuest record; multiple indexes carry
  "Psychological Bulletin, 119, 111–137."
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** pools 100+ conformity studies across 17 countries and tests
    **individualism–collectivism** as a moderator — a model of using a **moderator** to convert a pile of
    replications into a general claim about *when and where* an effect is larger, the central move in
    [`psychbull-moderators-and-bias`](../../skills/psychbull-moderators-and-bias/SKILL.md) and the
    inclusion/coding discipline in
    [`psychbull-inclusion-and-coding`](../../skills/psychbull-inclusion-and-coding/SKILL.md).
  - **Self-check:** does your moderator answer a *theoretical* question (why the effect varies), not just
    catalog subgroups?

### Meta-analysis adjudicating a stereotype against the data

- **Eagly & Johnson, "Gender and Leadership Style: A Meta-Analysis," _Psychological Bulletin_ 1990,
  108(2):233–256.**
  Verified: APA `doi.org/10.1037/0033-2909.108.2.233`; Northwestern Scholars record; multiple indexes
  carry "Psychological Bulletin, 108, 233–256."
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** quantifies whether male and female leaders *actually* differ in style and
    separates organizational from lab settings, so a **stereotype is tested as an empirical question** and
    the answer depends on context — the
    [`psychbull-topic-selection`](../../skills/psychbull-topic-selection/SKILL.md) "resolve a debate or
    reconcile conflicting findings" fit, with setting as a coded moderator
    ([`psychbull-inclusion-and-coding`](../../skills/psychbull-inclusion-and-coding/SKILL.md)).
  - **Self-check:** does your synthesis put a contested belief to a quantitative test and report where it
    holds vs. fails, rather than declare a winner?

### Large cross-cultural meta-analysis with explicit bias handling (contested literature)

- **Anderson, Shibuya, Ihori, Swing, Bushman, Sakamoto, Rothstein & Saleem, "Violent Video Game Effects
  on Aggression, Empathy, and Prosocial Behavior in Eastern and Western Countries: A Meta-Analytic
  Review," _Psychological Bulletin_ 2010, 136(2):151–173.**
  Verified: APA `doi.org/10.1037/a0018251` (`apa.org/pubs/journals/releases/bul-136-2-151.pdf`); PubMed
  PMID 20192553.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** synthesizes a **contested** literature across cultures and outcomes while
    addressing study quality and publication bias head-on — the
    [`psychbull-moderators-and-bias`](../../skills/psychbull-moderators-and-bias/SKILL.md) discipline of
    confronting small-study/bias threats so the pooled estimate survives scrutiny, and the
    [`psychbull-open-science-and-transparency`](../../skills/psychbull-open-science-and-transparency/SKILL.md)
    expectation of a reproducible search and database.
  - **Note (sibling guard):** the **2001** Anderson & Bushman violent-video-game meta-analysis is in
    **_Psychological Science_**, **not** _Bulletin_ — cite the correct year/venue for the claim you make.
  - **Self-check:** in a debated area, do you pre-empt the "it's just publication bias / weak studies"
    rebuttal with a bias-aware analysis, not a single funnel plot?

### Integrative narrative review building a "fundamental motivation" case (qualitative synthesis)

- **Baumeister & Leary, "The Need to Belong: Desire for Interpersonal Attachments as a Fundamental Human
  Motivation," _Psychological Bulletin_ 1995, 117(3):497–529.**
  Verified: APA `doi.org/10.1037/0033-2909.117.3.497`; PubMed PMID 7777651.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** an **integrative (narrative) review** marshals evidence across many
    literatures against a set of explicit criteria for a "fundamental need," showing that *Bulletin*
    rewards qualitative syntheses that **build a general theoretical claim from existing evidence** (not
    new data, and not freestanding theory) — the
    [`psychbull-theory-integration`](../../skills/psychbull-theory-integration/SKILL.md) move, with the
    scope discipline that original theory would go to *Psychological Review*.
  - **Self-check:** does your narrative review state in advance the criteria a claim must meet, then judge
    the literature against them — rather than assemble supportive citations?

### Quantitative-methods synthesis that standardized practice (methods primer)

- **Cohen, "A Power Primer," _Psychological Bulletin_ 1992, 112(1):155–159.**
  Verified: APA `doi.org/10.1037/0033-2909.112.1.155`; ProQuest record; archived PDFs (MIT, UBC) header
  "Psychological Bulletin, 112(1), 155–159."
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a compact **Quantitative Methods in Psychology** contribution that fixed
    field-wide conventions (small/medium/large effect sizes; required sample sizes) — a reminder that
    *Bulletin* also carries **methodological syntheses** that change how reviewers compute and report,
    the backbone of [`psychbull-meta-analysis-methods`](../../skills/psychbull-meta-analysis-methods/SKILL.md)
    (effect-size metrics) and [`psychbull-tables-figures`](../../skills/psychbull-tables-figures/SKILL.md)
    (reporting conventions).
  - **Self-check:** if your contribution is methodological, does it give readers a *reusable standard*
    (metric, table, decision rule), not just commentary?

## Omitted for lack of full verification (do not attribute to _Psychological Bulletin_ without re-checking)

To keep the list zero-hallucination, these strong candidates were **excluded** after web-checking — each
is a real, important paper, but **not in _Psychological Bulletin_** (the sibling-venue traps the brief
warned of — note how many are *theory* in _Psychological Review_ or reviews in invited-review venues):

- **Hyde, "The Gender Similarities Hypothesis" (2005)** — verified in **_American Psychologist_**
  60(6):581–592, **not _Psychological Bulletin_**. A meta-analysis *of* meta-analyses, but the wrong
  venue.
- **Holt-Lunstad, Smith & Layton, "Social Relationships and Mortality Risk: A Meta-Analytic Review"
  (2010)** — verified in **_PLoS Medicine_** 7(7):e1000316, **not _Psychological Bulletin_**.
- **Richard, Bond & Stokes-Zoota, "One Hundred Years of Social Psychology Quantitatively Described"
  (2003)** — verified in **_Review of General Psychology_** 7(4):331–363, **not _Psychological
  Bulletin_**.
- **Rosenthal & DiMatteo, "Meta-Analysis: Recent Developments in Quantitative Methods for Literature
  Reviews" (2001)** — verified in **_Annual Review of Psychology_** 52:59–82, **not _Psychological
  Bulletin_**. (A methods review, but the invited-review sibling.)
- **Sin & Lyubomirsky, "Enhancing Well-Being and Alleviating Depressive Symptoms With Positive
  Psychology Interventions: A Practice-Friendly Meta-Analysis" (2009)** — verified in **_Journal of
  Clinical Psychology_** 65(5):467–487, **not _Psychological Bulletin_**.
- **Anderson & Bushman, violent-video-game meta-analysis (2001)** — verified in **_Psychological
  Science_** 12(5):353–359, **not _Bulletin_** (distinct from the 2010 _Bulletin_ paper listed above).

Before adding any new paper to this library, confirm it on APA PsycNet (`psycnet.apa.org`) or via its
`10.1037/0033-2909...` (or current) DOI with volume/issue/pages, and check it is a **synthesis** (not a
primary study and not original theory). Update the access-date header. When in doubt, **omit**. If web
verification is unavailable, mark new rows **待核实 / TO VERIFY** and attach **no** journal attribution
until checked.
