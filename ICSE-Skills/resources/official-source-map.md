## Verified facts used in the skills

- ICSE 2027 (49th edition) runs April 25 – May 1, 2027 in Dublin, Ireland;
  core days April 28–30.
- The 2027 research track runs a **single submission cycle**: mandatory
  abstract June 23, 2026; paper June 30, 2026; both 23:59:59 AoE. ICSE 2025
  and 2026 ran two cycles — the cycle count is itself cycle-volatile.
- 2027 pipeline: author response September 2026 → notification October 20,
  2026 → Major Revision due November 17, 2026 → final MR decision
  December 18, 2026. The Accept / Major Revision / Reject decision model is
  retained within the single cycle.
- Four posted review criteria with definitions: Novelty; Rigor (soundness,
  clarity, depth of contribution and thoroughness of evaluation); Relevance
  (significance/impact on software engineering); Verifiability and
  Transparency (sufficient information to understand the innovation and the
  data handling, and support for independent verification or replication).
- Format: 10 pages main text inclusive of all figures/tables/appendices + 2
  pages references only; camera-ready receives one extra main-text page; IEEE
  conference proceedings template, with desk rejection for format deviations.
- Review is double-anonymous; own prior work cited in the third person.
- Open science: results should be public and studies reproducible where
  possible; sharing is the default and non-sharing needs justification;
  anonymized links or HotCRP supplementary upload at review time; a Data
  Availability section after the Conclusion when data cannot be shared;
  sharing is not formally mandatory for acceptance.
- Artifact evaluation is post-acceptance and opt-in, follows ACM badging
  (Available, Reusable for new papers; Results Reproduced/Replicated for
  prior work), and in the 2026 cycle ran its deadlines in early January.
- Journal-first partners are IEEE TSE, ACM TOSEM, and EMSE, with the 2027
  acceptance window October 11, 2025 – October 10, 2026.
- ICSE 2026 (48th) was held April 12–18, 2026 in Rio de Janeiro; its research
  track accepted 321 of 1,469 submissions across two cycles, with most
  acceptances arriving via Major Revision (189 of 321).
- Most Influential Paper lineage facts used in `exemplars/library.md`:
  ICSE 2019 MIP to "Automatically Finding Patches Using Genetic Programming"
  (ICSE 2009); ICSE 2022 MIP to "On the Naturalness of Software" (ICSE 2012);
  ICSE 2024 MIP to "Coverage Is Not Strongly Correlated with Test Suite
  Effectiveness" (ICSE 2014) — each cross-checked against university or
  SIGSOFT announcements.
