# Worked Example: Reframing an Introduction for Journal of Management Information Systems

This is a fictional example. It does not describe a real paper.

## Before

> This paper studies an important topic using a large dataset and several robustness checks. We contribute to the literature by adding new evidence and showing heterogeneous effects.

## Diagnosis

- The first sentence does not name the JMIS audience or the journal's information systems, digital transformation, platforms, analytics, IT governance, and organizational impacts of technology scope.
- The contribution could fit several siblings: MIS Quarterly, Information Systems Research, Journal of the AIS, and Management Science.
- The mechanism words that matter here are absent: digital platform, IT governance, analytics adoption, information systems theory.

## After

> This paper asks how **digital platform** changes the decision margin that JMIS readers care about. The design ties the estimate to **IT governance**, separates the main mechanism from **analytics adoption**, and reports uncertainty in a way that makes the policy or theory implication portable beyond the study setting.
