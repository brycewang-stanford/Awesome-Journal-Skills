# External Tools - ACM/IEEE HRI

Access date: 2026-07-09

Use these surfaces after reopening the current HRI cycle pages. HRI mechanics — dates, the
abstract/paper two-step, track names, page budget, rebuttal format, whether an artifact-badge
track runs — are decided per edition, and the `humanrobotinteraction.org` / `dl.acm.org` /
`ieeexplore.ieee.org` gateway may block direct fetches (read via search renderings and cross-check
ACM DL, IEEE Xplore, and dblp). Do not confuse this **conference** with the **THRI** or **JHRI**
**journals**, or with the **HAI** / **RO-MAN** conferences.

## Cross-check surfaces (when the gateway blocks the primary page)

- ACM DL HRI proceedings series (HRI '26 = 21st edition): https://dl.acm.org/conference/hri
- IEEE Xplore HRI proceedings (dual publication mirror of the ACM DL record).
- dblp HRI proceedings stream: https://dblp.org/db/conf/hri/index.html — use to verify a paper is
  an HRI *conference* placement (not THRI/JHRI/IJSR/CHI) before citing it as an exemplar.
- The PCS submission site's per-track deadline fields — the machine-readable cutoff the CFP
  paraphrases.
- Conference social announcements (X/Mastodon @hri) for the short-track deadlines the main pages
  sometimes surface late.

## Author-side checks

- PCS profile, coauthors, conflicts, and the **abstract registration** step that precedes the full
  paper by about a week — miss it and no PDF slot exists.
- Correct **track choice** among User Studies / Technical / Design / Theory and Methods / Systems;
  the paper is judged by that subcommittee's contribution-type bar.
- ACM `acmart` template compliance in **anonymous review** mode, the **8-page** budget (incl.
  figures, excl. references), and a clean PDF export.
- Double-blind sweep: anonymize the PDF metadata, author-revealing citations, the robot platform's
  provenance if it identifies a lab, the video (faces/voices/lab logos), and any repository links.
- A **video figure** that shows the interaction, plus supplementary study instruments (consent
  form summary, questionnaires, coding schemes) referenced from the paper.
- Human-subjects posture: IRB/ethics approval statement, informed consent, and honest reporting
  consistent with ACM's human-participants policy.

## Do not infer

- Do not infer the exact `acmart` class options or template revision for the current cycle — one
  secondary rendering referenced "IEEE Proceedings specifications" (consistent with dual
  sponsorship); confirm on the current author kit (**待核实**).
- Do not assume the abstract/paper two-step, the track list and names, the rebuttal length/format,
  or a formal artifact-badge track carry over between editions.
- Do not treat reported acceptance counts or per-track best-paper winners as primary — verify
  against the official program.
- If pages disagree, treat the newest full-papers call, the PCS deadline fields, or a direct chair
  announcement as controlling, and record the conflict.
