# Official Source Map - ACM/IEEE HRI

Access date: 2026-07-09

This map records the official and cross-check sources used for HRI-specific facts in this
pack. HRI is the **ACM/IEEE International Conference on Human-Robot Interaction**, the flagship
*interdisciplinary* venue for human-robot interaction, dual-sponsored by ACM (SIGCHI and the AI
SIG) and IEEE (Robotics and Automation Society). Its five-track full-paper model, two-phase
double-blind review with rebuttal, page budget, and human-subjects expectations are decided per
edition. Reopen the current-cycle full-papers call, the Overview of Review Process page, the
Reviewer Guidelines, and the ACM/IEEE author kits before giving submission-ready advice.

> Name-collision warnings. **THRI** (ACM *Transactions on Human-Robot Interaction*) and the
> older **JHRI** (*Journal of Human-Robot Interaction*) are journals, **not** this conference —
> several methodological classics (e.g., the Wizard-of-Oz reporting-guidelines review) live there,
> not in the HRI proceedings. **HAI** (Human-Agent Interaction) and **RO-MAN** (IEEE International
> Conference on Robot & Human Interactive Communication) are separate conferences. And

> from those surfaces except where explicitly flagged as a journal/venue guard.

## Verified 2026 facts used in skills

- **HRI 2026 is the 21st ACM/IEEE International Conference on Human-Robot Interaction**, theme
  **"HRI Empowering Society,"** held in **Edinburgh, Scotland, UK, 16-19 March 2026**. As of the
  2026-07-09 access date this edition has **already concluded**, so the live *next* full-paper
  target is **HRI 2027** (source 4, 10).
- **HRI 2026 full-paper calendar:** mandatory **abstract deadline 22 September 2025** (required
  for every full paper), **paper submission 30 September 2025**, both 23:59 AoE; **rebuttal due
  ~12 November 2025 AoE**; decisions in **December 2025**; **camera-ready ~9-12 January 2026**
  (待核实 exact camera-ready date). Submitted via **Precision Conference Solutions (PCS)** at
  `new.precisionconference.com` (sources 1, 2).
- **Five full-paper tracks**, each mapped to one review subcommittee by contribution type:
  **User Studies, Technical, Design, Theory and Methods, Systems.** Each paper is assigned to
  exactly one track/subcommittee; **User Studies** typically receives the most submissions and may
  be split. Reproducibility studies are directed to the **User Studies** track (sources 1, 3).
- **Page budget: up to 8 pages**, including figures, **excluding references**; over-length is a
  desk-reject ground. **Template: the ACM Primary Article Template (`acmart`)** in anonymous review
  mode; the review-copy class options (e.g. `manuscript,review,anonymous`) and exact
  `acmart` revision are **待核实 per cycle** (a secondary rendering also referenced "IEEE
  Proceedings specifications," consistent with dual sponsorship — verify the current author kit)
  (source 1).
- **Review is double-blind and two-phase:** an initial PC-chair/track-chair/AC desk check
  (desk rejections + track reassignment), then each paper goes to **one 1AC and one 2AC** with
  **three external reviewers**; authors get a **rebuttal**; then online discussion among reviewers
  and a **program-committee meeting** where final decisions are made. Every aspect of a submission
  must be anonymized; violations lead to desk rejection (sources 2, 3).
- **Human-subjects governance:** by submitting, authors accept ACM Publications Policies,
  including ACM's **Policy on Research Involving Human Participants and Subjects**; alleged
  violations can lead to full retraction and other penalties (source 3).
- **Dual publication:** accepted papers appear in **both the ACM Digital Library and IEEE
  Xplore**, reflecting the ACM/IEEE co-sponsorship (source 8).
- **Selectivity:** HRI is highly selective. HRI 2025 accepted **100 of 400** full-paper
  submissions (**25%**); HRI 2024 accepted **87 (~24.7%)** (reported via HRI community/press
  renderings; treat exact counts as reported, not primary — 待核实 to the official program).
- **Best-paper awards are given per track** (e.g., HRI 2018 awarded best full paper in HRI User
  Studies, Technical Advances, Design, and Theory and Methods; HRI 2026 announced per-track winners
  including User Studies and Theory) (sources 3, 4).

## Companion / short tracks (verify each cycle)

- **alt.HRI** — bold, creative, unconventional or subversive work; up to 8 pages excl. refs;
  papers, videos, and pictorials; double-blind; **deadline 17 Oct 2025 AoE**; published in the
  **companion proceedings** and presented orally (source 6).
- **Late-Breaking Reports (LBR)** — early-stage results/new ideas, **2-4 pages excl. refs**;
  **deadline 8 Dec 2025**; entry point for newcomers (source 5).
- **Interactivity / Videos & Demos** and the **Student Design Competition** — **deadline
  8 Dec 2025** (announced together with LBR). At least one author must register for the papers to
  appear in the proceedings/program (source 5 and conference announcements).
- **Workshops + Tutorials** and the **HRI Pioneers Workshop** (21st edition, held 16 Mar 2026)
  run alongside the main program; **Industry Day / Industry White Papers** run on the workshop day.

## No editor-in-chief; no journal continuity

Like every conference in this collection, HRI has **no standing editor-in-chief**; the rotating
analogue is the per-edition **General Chairs, Program Chairs, Track Chairs, and Area Chairs**,
appointed with ACM SIGCHI / the AI SIG and IEEE-RAS. Proceedings are conference proceedings (not a
standing journal); do **not** confuse the conference with **THRI** (ACM Transactions on Human-Robot
Interaction) or the legacy **JHRI**, which are journals.
