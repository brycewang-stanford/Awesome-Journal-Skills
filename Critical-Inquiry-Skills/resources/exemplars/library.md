# Critical Inquiry Exemplars Library (field × method)

> **Verified via web search, access date 2026-06-08.** Every essay below was checked to confirm it
> actually appeared in *Critical Inquiry* (University of Chicago Press; DOIs in the **`10.1086/…`**
> range), with author, title, year, and volume/issue. Placement was confirmed through publisher DOI
> pages, the journal's own listings, JSTOR volume pages, and indexing services that reproduce the CI
> citation; the verifying DOI/URL is given per entry. Essays lacking enough CI placement evidence were
> **omitted** — this is deliberately a short, clean list rather than a long, uncertain one.
>
> **Sibling-confusion guard.** *Critical Inquiry* is **not** PMLA, *New Literary History*,
> *Representations*, *October*, *Diacritics*, or *boundary 2*. Many landmark theory essays live in
> those siblings, not in CI; several are flagged in the **Omitted** section below as guardrails.
>
> **Use principle (zero hallucination):** this file gives **positioning only** — what makes each essay
> a CI-shaped *intervention*. It does **not** quote the essays or paraphrase their arguments as fact
> beyond their titles; read the original before citing anything. For CI scope, word caps, and format
> policy see [`../official-source-map.md`](../official-source-map.md).

## How to use this library

Pick the row whose **field × kind of move** is closest to yours, then study how that essay turns a
close reading of objects (texts, images, cases) into a claim that **crosses disciplines** — the CI bar
(see [`../../skills/ci-argument-and-intervention/SKILL.md`](../../skills/ci-argument-and-intervention/SKILL.md)
and [`../../skills/ci-scholarly-positioning/SKILL.md`](../../skills/ci-scholarly-positioning/SKILL.md)).
For each, ask the self-check question before claiming your essay is "CI-shaped." Model your own opening
on [`../worked-examples/01-introduction.md`](../worked-examples/01-introduction.md).

## By field × method

### Critique of critique itself (theory / philosophy of method)

- **Bruno Latour, "Why Has Critique Run Out of Steam? From Matters of Fact to Matters of Concern,"
  *Critical Inquiry* 2004, 30(2):225–248.** DOI **10.1086/421123**.
  Verified: `journals.uchicago.edu/doi/10.1086/421123` (citation confirmed via the publisher DOI page
  listing and corroborating indexes).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** turns the critic's own method into the object of inquiry and **reconceives**
    what critique is for — the kind of high-stakes, field-reorienting intervention CI prizes
    (`ci-argument-and-intervention`, type "reconceive / historicize a category we took as natural").
  - **Self-check:** does your essay press on an assumption your *own* field shares, not just a claim
    inside it?

### Reader, text, and the authority of interpretation (literary theory)

- **Stanley Fish, "Interpreting the *Variorum*," *Critical Inquiry* 1976, 2(3):465–485.**
  Verified: confirmed as CI 2(3) via `journals.uchicago.edu` indexing and the reply it provoked —
  Steven Mailloux, "Stanley Fish's 'Interpreting the *Variorum*': Advance or Retreat?," *Critical
  Inquiry* 3(1), DOI **10.1086/447880** — which fixes the original in the surrounding CI volumes.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a single, sharply stated **reconception** (meaning is made by
    interpretive communities, not lodged in the text) that reorganized a whole debate — and CI then
    hosted the **Critical Response** that contested it, the journal's signature argumentative form
    (`ci-scholarly-positioning`: "CI's own thread").
  - **Self-check:** can your central move be stated in one sentence a non-specialist grasps, and is it
    contestable enough that a reply would be worth writing?

### Narrative, history, and form (theory / historiography)

- **Hayden White, "The Value of Narrativity in the Representation of Reality," *Critical Inquiry* 1980,
  7(1):5–27.** DOI **10.1086/448086**.
  Verified: `journals.uchicago.edu/doi/abs/10.1086/448086` (CI Vol. 7, No. 1 article page).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** **historicizes** a category taken to be natural (narrative itself),
    crossing from literary theory into the philosophy of history — close reading of forms of
    historical writing doing conceptual work (`ci-evidence-and-objects`, `ci-argument-and-intervention`
    type "historicize").
  - **Self-check:** are you showing how something we treat as given (a form, a category) came to be,
    rather than assuming it?

### Race and the category of "race" in literary study (cultural criticism / special issue)

- **Henry Louis Gates Jr., "Editor's Introduction: Writing 'Race' and the Difference It Makes,"
  *Critical Inquiry* 1985, 12(1):1–20.** DOI **10.1086/448318**.
  Verified: `journals.uchicago.edu/doi/abs/10.1086/448318` (CI Vol. 12, No. 1); the special issue
  *"Race," Writing, and Difference* (CI 12.1, Autumn 1985) is confirmed on
  `criticalinquiry.uchicago.edu/past_issues/issue/autumn_1985_v12_n1/`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** frames a field-defining special issue and **names** a problem — the
    work the category "race" does in criticism — that reorganized literary study; the model of CI as a
    convened conversation, not a set of standalone papers (`ci-scholarly-positioning`).
  - **Self-check:** does your piece convene a debate the field needs now, with a clearly named
    pressure point — or only add one more reading?

### Postcolonial reading of the canon (literary criticism)

- **Gayatri Chakravorty Spivak, "Three Women's Texts and a Critique of Imperialism," *Critical
  Inquiry* 1985, 12(1):243–261.** DOI **10.1086/448328**.
  Verified: `journals.uchicago.edu/doi/abs/10.1086/448328` (CI Vol. 12, No. 1, same special issue as
  Gates above).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a **close reading that overturns a commonplace** — that
    nineteenth-century British literature can be read apart from imperialism — letting a few canonical
    texts, read deep, carry a claim that crosses literary studies and political theory
    (`ci-evidence-and-objects`: "few and deep," objects that *test* the claim).
  - **Self-check:** does your reading of a small number of objects unsettle something the field had
    stopped noticing, rather than confirm it?

### Image, archive, and the politics of the document (visual culture / political theory)

- **Ariella Aïsha Azoulay, "Potential History: Thinking through Violence," *Critical Inquiry* 2013,
  39(3):548–574.** DOI **10.1086/670045**.
  Verified: `journals.uchicago.edu/doi/abs/10.1086/670045` (CI Vol. 39, No. 3, Spring 2013);
  cross-checked on the JSTOR volume listing for CI 39(3).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** reads photographs and archival documents as objects (not data) to
    **reconceive** how violence is recorded and rendered legible — visual culture pressing on history
    and political theory at once, the cross-field reach CI rewards (`ci-scholarly-positioning`,
    `ci-evidence-and-objects`: images as objects of analysis).
  - **Self-check:** do your objects (images, documents, cases) bear a conceptual claim that matters
    beyond their own domain?

## Omitted for lack of CI placement (do not attribute to *Critical Inquiry* without re-checking)

To keep the list zero-hallucination, these well-known essays were **checked and excluded** because they
appeared in a **sibling** venue, not CI — useful guardrails against the sibling-confusion trap:

- **W. J. T. Mitchell, "There Are No Visual Media"** — verified to be in the *Journal of Visual
  Culture* (2005), **not CI**, despite Mitchell being CI's long-time editor (1978–2020). His CI essays
  exist, but this specific title is not one of them.
- **W. J. T. Mitchell, "The Commitment to Form; or, Still Crazy after All These Years"** — *PMLA*
  118(2) (2003), **not CI**.
- **Barbara Johnson, "Apostrophe, Animation, and Abortion"** — *Diacritics* 16(1) (1986), **not CI**.
- **Franco Moretti, "Conjectures on World Literature"** — *New Left Review* 1 (2000), **not CI**.
- **Fredric Jameson, "Postmodernism, or, the Cultural Logic of Late Capitalism"** — *New Left Review*
  I/146 (1984), **not CI**.
- **Eve Kosofsky Sedgwick, "Paranoid Reading and Reparative Reading"** — a chapter in *Touching
  Feeling* (Duke University Press, 2003) / *Novel Gazing* (Duke, 1997), **not a CI article**.
- **Stephen Greenblatt, "Towards a Poetics of Culture"** — lacked CI placement evidence in this pass;
  omitted rather than attributed.

Before adding any new essay to this library, confirm it on `journals.uchicago.edu` (DOI in the
`10.1086/…` range with CI volume/issue) and update the access-date header. **When in doubt, omit.**
