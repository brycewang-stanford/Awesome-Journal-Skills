# AEJ: Macroeconomics Exemplars Library (method × topic)

> **Verified via web search, access date 2026-06.** Every paper below was checked to confirm it actually
> appeared in the ***American Economic Journal: Macroeconomics*** and carries a **`10.1257/mac…` DOI stem**
> — the marker that confirms AEJ: Macro specifically. Year, volume(issue), and pages were read off the AEA
> article pages (`aeaweb.org/articles?id=…`). Papers that could not be fully verified as AEJ: Macro were
> **omitted** — this is deliberately a short, clean list rather than a long, uncertain one.
>
> **Sibling-confusion guard (do not misattribute).** AEJ: Macro is **not** any of these, which share the
> subject area or part of the AEA DOI prefix:
> *American Economic Review* (`10.1257/aer…`), *AEJ: Economic Policy* (`10.1257/pol…`),
> *AEJ: Applied Economics* (`10.1257/app…`), *AEJ: Microeconomics* (`10.1257/mic…`),
> *Econometrica* (`10.3982/ECTA…`), *J. Monetary Economics*, and *Review of Economic Dynamics (RED)*.
> Only the **`10.1257/mac…`** stem confirms AEJ: Macro. The omissions section records two papers excluded
> on exactly this ground.
>
> **Use principle (zero hallucination):** this file gives **design positioning only**. It does not
> reproduce or invent estimates, multipliers, or specific findings — read the original before citing any
> number. For AEJ: Macro-specific policy, scope, and house rules, see
> [`../official-source-map.md`](../official-source-map.md).

## How to use this library

Pick the row whose **method × question** is closest to yours, then study how that paper makes a
*quantitatively serious* result answer a *broad-interest* macro question — the AEJ: Macro bar
(see [`../../skills/aejmac-topic-selection/SKILL.md`](../../skills/aejmac-topic-selection/SKILL.md) and
[`../../skills/aejmac-writing-style/SKILL.md`](../../skills/aejmac-writing-style/SKILL.md)). For each, ask
the self-check question before claiming your paper is "AEJ: Macro-shaped." Remember the AEJ: Macro
breadth bar: the answer must matter to a *general* macroeconomist, not only your subfield.

## By method

### Time-series macro-econometrics: reconciling shock-identification approaches

- **Coibion, "Are the Effects of Monetary Policy Shocks Big or Small?," AEJ: Macroeconomics 2012, 4(2):1–32.**
  Verified: `aeaweb.org/articles?id=10.1257/mac.4.2.1` (DOI `10.1257/mac.4.2.1`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** takes a substantive macro question (how large are the real effects of
    monetary shocks?) and shows that the *answer depends on the identification* — reconciling small-VAR and
    narrative (Romer–Romer) estimates by accounting for the contractionary impetus, the reserves-targeting
    period, and lag choice. A model of how to make method choices *visible* in the economic conclusion.
    Routes to [`../../skills/aejmac-identification/SKILL.md`](../../skills/aejmac-identification/SKILL.md)
    (SVAR + narrative) and [`../../skills/aejmac-robustness/SKILL.md`](../../skills/aejmac-robustness/SKILL.md).
  - **Self-check:** does your identification choice change the substantive answer, and have you shown that
    explicitly rather than reporting one specification?

### High-frequency / SVAR identification with the central-bank information effect

- **Jarociński & Karadi, "Deconstructing Monetary Policy Surprises—The Role of Information Shocks," AEJ: Macroeconomics 2020, 12(2):1–43.**
  Verified: `aeaweb.org/articles?id=10.1257/mac.20180090` (DOI `10.1257/mac.20180090`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** separates a pure monetary surprise from a central-bank *information* signal
    using high-frequency co-movement of interest rates and stock prices in a structural VAR — exactly the
    [`aejmac-identification`](../../skills/aejmac-identification/SKILL.md) Branch C concern (high-frequency
    surprises confounded by the Fed information effect), with the two components shown to have very
    different macro effects.
  - **Self-check:** if you use high-frequency surprises, have you confronted the information effect rather
    than assuming the surprise is a clean monetary shock?

### Labor-macro / business-cycle measurement (new measurement → cyclical mechanism)

- **Mukoyama, Patterson & Şahin, "Job Search Behavior over the Business Cycle," AEJ: Macroeconomics 2018, 10(1):190–215.**
  Verified: `aeaweb.org/articles?id=10.1257/mac.20160202` (DOI `10.1257/mac.20160202`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** constructs a novel measure of job-search effort and uses it to answer a
    macro question (is search effort pro- or counter-cyclical, and does it amplify or dampen labor-market
    fluctuations?) — measurement disciplined into a broad-interest cyclical mechanism, not measurement for
    its own sake.
  - **Self-check:** does your new measure deliver a macro mechanism a general reader cares about, with the
    cyclicality and its aggregate consequence quantified?

### Quantitative growth/development: misallocation accounting

- **Bento & Restuccia, "Misallocation, Establishment Size, and Productivity," AEJ: Macroeconomics 2017, 9(3):267–303.**
  Verified: `aeaweb.org/articles?id=10.1257/mac.20150281` (DOI `10.1257/mac.20150281`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a quantitative misallocation framework linking establishment size to
    aggregate productivity across countries — a calibrated, broad-interest growth/development result that
    delivers a headline aggregate-productivity number. Routes to
    [`../../skills/aejmac-theory-model/SKILL.md`](../../skills/aejmac-theory-model/SKILL.md)
    (calibration discipline).
  - **Self-check:** does your quantitative framework deliver one memorable aggregate quantity (a TFP gap, a
    growth share), disciplined by data, rather than an undisciplined calibration?

### Quantitative spatial / growth accounting (cross-city misallocation → aggregate growth)

- **Hsieh & Moretti, "Housing Constraints and Spatial Misallocation," AEJ: Macroeconomics 2019, 11(2):1–39.**
  Verified: `aeaweb.org/articles?id=10.1257/mac.20170388` (DOI `10.1257/mac.20170388`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** maps a micro distortion (housing-supply constraints in productive cities) to
    a first-order *aggregate* quantity (the growth cost of spatial misallocation) — the partial-to-general
    mapping that [`aejmac-identification`](../../skills/aejmac-identification/SKILL.md) Branch D and
    [`aejmac-theory-model`](../../skills/aejmac-theory-model/SKILL.md) both insist be made explicit.
  - **Self-check:** when you scale a local elasticity to the aggregate, is the spatial/GE mapping stated
    and defended, not assumed?

### International / open-economy macro (model-based policy design)

- **Coulibaly, "Monetary Policy in Sudden Stop-Prone Economies," AEJ: Macroeconomics 2023, 15(4):141–176.**
  Verified: `aeaweb.org/articles?id=10.1257/mac.20200201` (DOI `10.1257/mac.20200201`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a quantitative open-economy model used to ask a policy-relevant question
    (how should emerging economies set monetary policy when exposed to sudden stops?) and to compare
    welfare across regimes — broad-interest international macro with a disciplined model and an explicit
    welfare comparison. Routes to
    [`../../skills/aejmac-theory-model/SKILL.md`](../../skills/aejmac-theory-model/SKILL.md)
    (counterfactual/welfare validity).
  - **Self-check:** does your model deliver a welfare-ranked policy comparison with the metric stated, not
    just a qualitative "this regime is better"?

## Omitted to avoid sibling-confusion (do not attribute to AEJ: Macro without re-checking)

To keep the list zero-hallucination, the following were **excluded** after checking — they are natural
AEJ: Macro-area candidates but belong to *sibling* journals, and serve here as guardrails:

- **Auerbach & Gorodnichenko, "Measuring the Output Responses to Fiscal Policy" (2012)** — a canonical
  state-dependent fiscal-multiplier paper, but web search confirms it is in ***AEJ: Economic Policy***
  (4(2):1–27, DOI `10.1257/pol.4.2.1`), **not AEJ: Macro**. The `pol` stem, not `mac`, is the tell.
- **Schmitt-Grohé & Uribe, "What's News in Business Cycles" (2012)** — a leading anticipated-shock /
  business-cycle estimation paper, but it is in ***Econometrica*** (80(6):2733–2764, DOI
  `10.3982/ECTA8050`), **not AEJ: Macro**. The `10.3982/ECTA` stem is the tell.

Several frequently-cited AEJ: Macro-area papers were also left off because their **exact AEJ: Macro
volume/issue/pages** could not be confirmed to the same standard in one pass; add them only after reading
the AEA article page and confirming the `10.1257/mac…` DOI.

Before adding any new paper to this library, confirm it on `aeaweb.org/articles?id=…` (article page with
volume/issue/pages) **and** that the DOI begins `10.1257/mac`, then update the access-date header. When in
doubt, omit.
