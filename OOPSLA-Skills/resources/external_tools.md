# External Tools - OOPSLA

Access date: 2026-07-08

Links an agent should open (or ask the author to open) when working on an OOPSLA paper.
OOPSLA's machinery is reset per yearly volume — a new HotCRP instance, a new SPLASH site,
and a new pair of Review Committee chairs — so replace the year in each URL with the volume
actually being targeted before trusting anything on the page.

## What to check in each, author-side

- **Track page**: which round is currently open; the outcome definitions for this volume
  (they moved between 2025 and 2026); the page caps for initial submissions vs revisions;
  the Data-Availability Statement requirement.
- **HotCRP**: account, conflicts, topics, anonymous author list, abstract/PDF consistency,
  and — for Major-Revision resubmissions — that the paper is filed as a revision in the
  round the invitation names, not as a fresh submission.
- **SPLASH site**: registration and travel constraints for the in-person talk; whether the
  poster option for accepted OOPSLA papers is worth taking.
- **Artifact track**: kick-the-tires dates relative to the acceptance round, packaging
  expectations, and the Zenodo deposit step for the Available badge.
- **ACM DL**: the open-access/APC terms in force for the target volume, and the published
  form of any paper you cite from a PACMPL OOPSLA issue.

## Rules of engagement for stale data

- A deadline in this pack proves only what *one* volume did. Two rounds per year is the
  standing model since 2024, but each volume's actual dates exist only on that volume's
  pages.
- If the SPLASH track page and the HotCRP instance disagree, treat HotCRP as controlling
  for submission mechanics and the track page as controlling for policy, and say so to the
  author; email the Review Committee chairs when the conflict is material.
- Never extrapolate outcome categories across volumes: the Conditional-Accept/Minor-Revision
  merge between 2025 and 2026 is exactly the kind of silent change that invalidates cached
  advice.
