# External Tools - ACM PODS

Access date: 2026-07-09

Use these surfaces after reopening the current PODS cycle pages. PODS mechanics — the number of
submission cycles, every date, the page budget, the `acmart` variant, and the revision/shepherd
process — are decided per edition, and the `sigmod.org`/`dl.acm.org` gateway may block direct
fetches (read via search renderings and cross-check ACM DL / PACMMOD and dblp). Do not confuse this
venue with **PODC** (Principles of *Distributed* Computing), a different ACM symposium.

## Cross-check surfaces (when the gateway blocks the primary page)

- ACM DL / PACMMOD PODS tables of contents (PODS 2024 = PACMMOD Vol 2 N2 and N5; PODS 2025 = Vol 3 N2).
- dblp PACMMOD stream and the pre-PACMMOD PODS proceedings series: https://dblp.org/db/conf/pods/
- The `databasetheory.org` community CFP mirror: https://databasetheory.org/taxonomy/term/17
- The EasyChair CFP page (`easychair.org/cfp/PODS2026`) for the machine-readable deadline list.

## Author-side checks

- EasyChair profile, coauthor emails, declared conflicts of interest, and the abstract that precedes
  the full-paper deadline in each cycle.
- `acmart` compliance in the exact variant the call names (`\documentclass[acmsmall,review,anonymous]{acmart}`
  for PODS 2026), the current page budget (15 pages excluding references + unlimited references +
  an at-submission appendix), and PDF export in the single-column review format.
- Lightweight double-anonymous sweep: strip author names/affiliations/acknowledgements, phrase
  self-citations in the third person, and remove identity-revealing links (a personal homepage, a
  named system, a funding line) from the paper and the appendix.
- A complete **proof appendix** incorporated at submission (no external/online appendix), and — by
  community norm — a **full version on arXiv** to be linked at camera-ready.

## Do not infer

- Do not infer the number of cycles or any date: PODS runs multiple cycles per year and each cutoff
  is AoE and edition-specific. Confirm on the current Important Dates page.
- Do not assume the page budget, `acmart` variant, revision/shepherd mechanics, cross-cycle
  resubmission rule, double-anonymity scope, or any AI-disclosure wording carry between editions.
- Do not import an artifact-evaluation or reproducibility-badge process: PODS is a theory symposium
  with no systems artifact track. The analogue is the proof appendix and the arXiv full version.
- If pages disagree, treat the newest PODS research call, the EasyChair deadline list, or a direct
  PC-chair announcement as controlling, and record the conflict.
