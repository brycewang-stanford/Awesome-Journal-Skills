# APSR Official Source Map

Primary and authoritative sources behind the APSR-specific facts injected into this skill pack.
Access date for all entries: **2026-06-01**. Re-verify volatile items (editors and editorial term,
exact word/abstract caps, fee/APC, data-policy wording, accepted tracks) on the official page before
relying on them — journal pages change.

## Official journal, submission & author pages

- **https://apsanet.org/publications/journals/american-political-science-review/apsr-submission-guidelines/**
  — APSA APSR Submission Guidelines. Verifies: most **Articles < 11,000 words**; **Research Notes
  < 7,000 words** (word counts exclude reference lists and appendices but include tables, figures,
  and footnotes printed in the text); **abstract ≤ 150 words**; a **word count on the front page**;
  manuscripts must **preserve author anonymity** (avoid unnecessary self-citation, remove obvious
  self-references); **ORCID iD required for corresponding authors** submitting after 1 Jan 2019;
  formatting follows the **APSA Style Manual for Political Science**; submission via **Editorial
  Manager** (`editorialmanager.com/apsr`); five tracks — **Regular Articles, Research Notes,
  Replications and Reappraisals, Syntheses, Registered Reports**; preregistration / pre-analysis
  plans are encouraged and must be shared anonymized (appendix or OSF) with registered vs.
  unregistered analyses clearly marked. No submission fee or APSA-membership requirement is stated.
- **https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/american-political-science-review/information/author-instructions/preparing-your-materials**
  — Cambridge Core author instructions (manuscript preparation). Verifies: publisher = **Cambridge
  University Press**; preparation/format requirements; double-anonymous preparation.
- **https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/american-political-science-review**
  — APSR on Cambridge Core (publisher home, current issues).

## Peer-review & editorial policy

- **https://apsanet.org/publications/journals/american-political-science-review/apsr-submission-guidelines/**
  — APSR Submission Guidelines (the standalone `peer-review-policy/` page was folded into this page in
  the 2026 APSA site update; the desk-reject/peer-review policy below now lives here). Verifies: papers
  are first screened for possible **desk rejection** on
  grounds of **remit** (not research / not political science / out of scope / not in adequate
  English), **ethics** (plagiarized or unattributed text; unethical procedures), and **substance**
  (missing a key element / insufficiently significant findings / failure to engage relevant
  literatures); **substantive desk rejections require at least two editors**; decision categories
  are **reject / accept / revise-and-resubmit**, with R&R **reserved for papers very close to
  publishable quality**; **strong support from all or nearly all reviewers** is generally necessary,
  but publication decisions are **at the editor's discretion**.
- **https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/american-political-science-review/information/peer-review-information/instructions-for-peer-reviewers**
  — Instructions for peer reviewers. Verifies: **double-anonymous** review; a **review-transfer
  initiative** under which anonymized manuscripts/reviews may be forwarded only to journals that
  also use double-anonymous protocols.

## Research transparency & data policy

- **https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/american-political-science-review/information/journal-policies/research-transparency**
  — APSR Research Transparency policy. Verifies: authors of **conditionally accepted** manuscripts
  must deposit a **reproducibility package in the APSR Dataverse** (a dedicated repository with
  permanent identifiers); the editorial office **reviews the package to verify that the manuscript's
  tables and figures can be reproduced** and that the research process is documented; when data
  cannot be shared (ethical/privacy/legal reasons) authors must **explain why**, give **README
  instructions on how to obtain the data**, and where possible **provide synthetic data**.
- **https://dataverse.harvard.edu/dataverse/the_review** — **American Political Science Review**
  collection on **Harvard Dataverse** (the APSR Dataverse). Verifies: replication materials are
  hosted on Harvard Dataverse.

## Owner, publisher, editors, history

- **https://apsanet.org/PUBLICATIONS/Journals/American-Political-Science-Review/About-the-Editors/**
  — About the Editors. Verifies (as of access date): Lead Editors **John Gerring (UT Austin)** and
  **Monika Nalepa (University of Chicago)**; a Managing Editor and ~14 Associate Editors across
  multiple institutions; owner = **American Political Science Association**; publisher = **Cambridge
  University Press**.
- **https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_Political_Science_Review** — Verifies: APSR founded
  **1906**; flagship general-interest journal of the discipline; published by Cambridge University
  Press for APSA.
