# Official Source Map - SIGIR

Access date: 2026-07-08

This map records the official sources behind every SIGIR-specific fact in this pack.
SIGIR reorganizes its track lineup, page budgets, deadlines, and review mechanics per
edition (each year's site is run by that year's organizing committee), so reopen the
current edition's track pages before giving any deadline- or policy-sensitive advice.

## Verified 2026-cycle facts used in the skills

- SIGIR 2026 is the **49th** International ACM SIGIR Conference on Research and
  Development in Information Retrieval, held **in person, July 20-24, 2026, in
  Melbourne | Naarm, Australia** (source 1). SIGIR '98 — the edition that introduced
  Ponte & Croft's language-modeling approach — was also held in Melbourne.
- The 2026 track lineup includes at least: **Full Papers, Short Papers, Perspectives,
  Resources, Reproducibility, Low Resource Environments, and Industry** tracks, plus
  workshops (sources 2-6, 8, 9, 11). Resources and Reproducibility, a combined track
  in recent editions, were **split into two distinct tracks for 2026** (sources 4, 5).
- **Full papers: up to 9 pages excluding references. Short papers: up to 4 pages
  excluding references.** Both use the ACM Primary Article Template (LaTeX `sigconf`,
  Word Interim) and are reviewed double-blind, fully anonymized (sources 2, 3). The
  SIGIR 2025 wording of the same rule makes the sharp edge explicit: figures, tables,
  proofs, **appendixes**, and acknowledgments all count **inside** the page budget —
  only references are free (source 13).
- **Resource papers: maximum 6 pages plus unlimited references, reviewed
  single-anonymous** so reviewers may inspect shared resources that cannot be
  anonymized (source 4).
- Full-paper submitters are expected to **nominate one author per submission as a PC
  member** (source 2).
- Review and submission run on **OpenReview**, with separate venue groups per track
  (source 11).
- Policy layer (source 7): ACM authorship policy; generative-AI tools cannot be
  authors and AI-generated content must be disclosed; automated compliance checks
  reserved; **parallel dual submission is desk-rejected**; **cross-track double
  submission inside SIGIR 2026 is prohibited**; the ACM Peer Review Policy governs.
- Partial 2026 timeline verified: Resources/LRE abstract deadline February 5, 2026;
  LRE presentation proposals February 12, 2026; Resources/LRE notification April 2,
  2026; camera-ready and author registration April 29, 2026 (sources 4, 8).
- SIGIR proceedings are published by **ACM in the Digital Library** (source 12), so
  accepted papers go through ACM's e-rights and TAPS production pipeline; SIGIR 2025's
  record (DOI 10.1145/3726302) is the most recent published volume.
- SIGIR is run by **ACM SIGIR**, the special interest group. Like every conference in
  this collection it has **no standing editor-in-chief**; program leadership rotates
  per edition (the SIGIR 2025 proceedings name six volume editors, source 12), and
  publication is funded through conference registration under ACM's publication
  model, not through a SIGIR-specific author fee (ACM Open status per institution —
  see 待核实).

## Reported but not officially confirmed (treat as 待核实)

- **SIGIR 2026 full/short-paper deadlines.** Deadline trackers (getpaperpilot,
  the Hugging Face ai-deadlines dataset) reported **abstract January 15, 2026 and
  paper January 22, 2026, 23:59:59 AoE**, which matches the SIGIR 2025 pattern
  (abstract Jan 16 / paper Jan 23, 2025 AoE); one third-party aggregator instead
  claimed "mid-December 2025." The official Key Dates page (source 10) could not be
  rendered numerically. Do not state 2026 submission dates as fact without opening
  source 10. (Both dates have passed as of 2026-07-08; the 2026 cycle is now in its
  post-camera-ready, pre-conference phase.)
- A SIGIR-family **revise-and-resubmit** route (response letter of 1-3 pages
  accompanying a revised previously-rejected paper) surfaced in SIGIR-AP CFP
  renderings, not on the SIGIR 2026 pages; treat R&R as a SIGIR-AP mechanism unless
  the main-conference CFP says otherwise.
