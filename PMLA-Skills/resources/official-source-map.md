# PMLA Official Source Map

Primary and authoritative sources behind the PMLA-specific facts injected into this skill pack.
Access date for all entries: **2026-06-01**. Re-verify volatile items (editor and editorial term,
exact word caps, special-feature deadlines, membership requirement, AI-disclosure wording, accepted
features) on the official page before relying on them — journal pages change.

## Official journal, submission & author pages

- **https://www.mla.org/Publications/Journals/PMLA/Submitting-Manuscripts-to-PMLA**
  — MLA "Submitting Manuscripts to PMLA." Verifies: **articles 6,000–9,000 words**, the count
  **including discursive notes** but **excluding the list of works cited and translations**;
  **authors must be members of the MLA** (all coauthors of a collaborative essay must be members);
  articles **cannot have been previously published in any language** and **cannot be under
  consideration by other journals or publishers**; **revised versions of manuscripts previously
  submitted to PMLA will not be considered**; authors **must fully cite, at submission, their use of
  all content created by an AI tool**; manuscripts follow **MLA style as set out in the most recent
  edition of the MLA Handbook**; anonymity — authors should **not refer to themselves in the first
  person** where it would identify them and any reference to their prior work should be in the
  **third person**.
- **https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/pmla/information/author-instructions**
  — Cambridge Core author instructions for PMLA. Verifies: submission as **Word files through PMLA's
  ScholarOne site** (`mc.manuscriptcentral.com/pmla`); the 6,000–9,000-word range; the AI-content
  citation requirement; **Special Features** (Theories and Methodologies; The Changing Profession)
  with a proposal deadline of **15 October** and proposal **abstracts not exceeding 100 words**;
  special-feature contributions limited to about **3,500 words** (including discursive endnotes, not
  translations or the works-cited list); **Criticism in Translation** and **Little-known Documents**
  as ongoing categories.
- **https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/pmla/information/instructions-contributors**
  — Cambridge Core "Instructions for contributors." Verifies: **each eligible article is sent to at
  least two reviewers**; recommended articles go to the **Editorial Board**, which meets periodically
  with the editor to **make final decisions**; until a final decision is reached the **author's name
  is not made known to readers, board members, or the editor**, and reviewers/board/editor **recuse
  themselves** on possible conflicts.
- **https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/pmla** — PMLA on Cambridge Core (publisher home, current
  issues).

## Scope, values & review process

- **https://www.mla.org/Publications/Journals/PMLA/What-PMLA-Values** — "What PMLA Values." Verifies:
  PMLA is a **generalist journal** open to **a variety of topics, whether general or specific, and to
  all scholarly methods and theoretical perspectives**; it publishes essays judged to be of **interest
  to scholars and teachers of language and literature**; an ideal essay should **address a significant
  problem, draw out the implications of its findings, and engage its audience through a concise,
  readable presentation**; the **blind review process** fosters inclusion of newer scholars alongside
  established members; the journal seeks to **represent the diversity of the MLA membership** in
  reviewers, Editorial Board, and Advisory Committee; **Criticism in Translation** and **Little-known
  Documents** bring newly translated works and archival findings to a wide audience.
- **https://www.mla.org/About-Us/Governance/Committees/Committee-Listings/Publications/PMLA-Editorial-Board**
  — PMLA Editorial Board. Verifies: the **Editorial Board makes final decisions** on acceptance.
- **https://www.mla.org/About-Us/Governance/Committees/Committee-Listings/Publications/PMLA-Advisory-Committee**
  — PMLA Advisory Committee. Verifies: the **Advisory Committee reports on articles** submitted for
  publication in PMLA.

## Owner, publisher, history

- **https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/pmla** — Verifies: published by **Cambridge University
  Press on behalf of the Modern Language Association**; appears in **January, March, May, and
  October**, with a **September** issue carrying the association's annual convention program; serves
  the **large and heterogeneous** MLA membership. (Frequency/issue months are volatile — 待核实.)
- **https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PMLA** — Verifies: PMLA is the journal of the **Modern Language
  Association of America**; one of the oldest scholarly journals in the United States; "PMLA" stands
  for **Publications of the Modern Language Association of America**.
