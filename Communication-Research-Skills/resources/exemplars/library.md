# *Communication Research* Exemplars Library (method × topic)

> **Verified via web search, access date 2026-06.** Every paper below was checked to confirm it actually
> appeared in **_Communication Research_ (CR)** — SAGE's quantitative, social-scientific communication
> journal (ISSN 0093-6502 print / 1552-3810 online; SAGE DOI prefix **10.1177/009365…** / **0093650…**;
> portal `crx.sagepub.com`) — with year and volume/issue, against the journal's own article pages
> (`journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/…`) and corroborating indexes (ERIC, Semantic Scholar, OA.mg).
> Papers that could not be fully verified **as _Communication Research_** were **omitted** — this is
> deliberately a short, clean list, not a long, uncertain one.
>
> **Sibling-journal guard.** *Communication Research* is **not** *Journal of Communication* (ICA
> all-paradigm flagship, Oxford UP), *Human Communication Research* (ICA interpersonal), *Communication
> Theory* (ICA theory), *Journal of Computer-Mediated Communication* (ICA, digital), *Science
> Communication* (SAGE), or *New Media & Society* (SAGE). Many famous "communication" papers live in
> those venues; the [Omitted](#omitted-for-lack-of-full-verification-do-not-attribute-to-communication-research)
> section records the traps checked and rejected.
>
> **Use principle (zero hallucination):** this file gives **design positioning only**. It does not
> reproduce or invent effect sizes, coefficients, or specific findings — read the original on SAGE
> Journals before citing any number. For *CR*-specific scope, caps, blinding, and house style, see
> [`../official-source-map.md`](../official-source-map.md).

## How to use this library

Pick the row whose **method × communication question** is closest to yours, then study how that paper
turns an observation about messages, audiences, or media use into a **tested communication mechanism** —
the *CR* bar (see [`../../skills/commres-topic-selection/SKILL.md`](../../skills/commres-topic-selection/SKILL.md),
[`../../skills/commres-theory-building/SKILL.md`](../../skills/commres-theory-building/SKILL.md), and
[`../../skills/commres-research-design/SKILL.md`](../../skills/commres-research-design/SKILL.md)). For each, ask the
self-check question before claiming your paper is "*CR*-shaped."

## By method

### Theory integration / model-building (CMC theory)

- **Walther, "Computer-Mediated Communication: Impersonal, Interpersonal, and Hyperpersonal Interaction," _Communication Research_ 1996, 23(1):3–43.**
  Verified: `journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/009365096023001001` (ERIC EJ521347).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** integrates fragmented CMC findings into the **hyperpersonal model** — a
    named mechanism (selective self-presentation, idealized perception, feedback loops) with scope
    conditions, not a one-platform effect. The model
    [`commres-theory-building`](../../skills/commres-theory-building/SKILL.md) move: a portable mechanism
    other areas can import.
  - **Self-check:** does your argument name a mechanism with boundary conditions that travels beyond your
    exact medium and message?

### Foundational relational experiment / quasi-experiment (interpersonal CMC)

- **Walther, "Interpersonal Effects in Computer-Mediated Interaction: A Relational Perspective," _Communication Research_ 1992, 19(1):52–90.**
  Verified: `journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/009365092019001003` (ERIC EJ439006; `crx.sagepub.com`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** reframes "CMC is impersonal" as a claim bounded by **time and message
    accumulation**, testing relational development rather than asserting a fixed media effect — the
    [`commres-research-design`](../../skills/commres-research-design/SKILL.md) discipline of stating the
    boundary condition the design probes.
  - **Self-check:** does your design separate a genuine process from a situational boundary condition,
    rather than reading one snapshot as a timeless effect?

### Survey testing a cognitive-process model (survey / mediation)

- **Eveland, "The Cognitive Mediation Model of Learning From the News," _Communication Research_ 2001, 28(5):571–601.**
  Verified: `journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/009365001028005001`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** models news learning as **gratifications → information processing
    (attention, elaboration) → knowledge** — an explicit mediation chain, not a bare exposure→knowledge
    correlation. The [`commres-theory-building`](../../skills/commres-theory-building/SKILL.md)
    mediation core: name the process variables and test the path.
  - **Self-check:** is your "effect" specified as a measured mediating process, with the path tested,
    rather than inferred from a direct exposure–outcome association?

### Between-subjects experiment with a moderated effect (experiment / political comm)

- **Hart & Nisbet, "Boomerang Effects in Science Communication: How Motivated Reasoning and Identity Cues Amplify Opinion Polarization About Climate Mitigation Policies," _Communication Research_ 2012, 39(6):701–723.**
  Verified: `journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/0093650211416646`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a message experiment where the effect of victim-identification cues is
    **contingent on partisanship**, producing a boomerang among one group — a "for whom" moderation
    result, the [`commres-research-design`](../../skills/commres-research-design/SKILL.md) standard of
    pre-specified moderation, not a single average effect.
  - **Self-check:** does your experiment test *for whom and when* the message works, including the
    possibility that it backfires for some audiences?

### Behavioral selective-exposure experiment (experiment / media processing)

- **Knobloch-Westerwick & Meng, "Looking the Other Way: Selective Exposure to Attitude-Consistent and Counterattitudinal Political Information," _Communication Research_ 2009, 36(3):426–448.**
  Verified: `journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/0093650209333030`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** uses **unobtrusively logged reading time** as a behavioral exposure
    measure rather than self-report — answering the
    [`commres-research-design`](../../skills/commres-research-design/SKILL.md) objection that "exposure
    is assumed, not measured" with an actual behavioral trace.
  - **Self-check:** is your exposure or use construct measured behaviorally, or are you leaning on a
    self-report proxy a reviewer will discount?

### Replication-and-extension experiment adjudicating rival biases (experiment / political comm)

- **Knobloch-Westerwick, Mothes & Polavin, "Confirmation Bias, Ingroup Bias, and Negativity Bias in Selective Exposure to Political Information," _Communication Research_ 2020, 47(1):104–124.**
  Verified: `journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/0093650217719596`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** pits **three competing selective-exposure mechanisms** against one another
    in one design — the high-end [`commres-theory-building`](../../skills/commres-theory-building/SKILL.md)
    move of adjudicating rivals rather than documenting a single bias.
  - **Self-check:** does your study let competing accounts be compared on the same data, or does it test
    only the one mechanism you favor?
