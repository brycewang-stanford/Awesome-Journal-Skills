# JME Official Source Map

Primary and authoritative sources behind the *Journal of Monetary Economics* (JME)-specific
facts injected into this skill pack. **Access date for all entries: 2026-06-01.**
Re-verify volatile items (editors, fee amounts, page caps, policy wording) before relying on
them — journal pages change. Items that could not be confirmed from an authoritative live
page are marked **待核实** (to be verified).

## Official journal / author pages

- **https://sites.google.com/view/journal-of-monetary-economics/for-authors** — JME "For Authors" page. Verifies: submission via Elsevier **Editorial Manager** at `editorialmanager.com/monec/`; **submission fee US$350** (reduced to **US$200 for full-time PhD students**); **no fee** for revised/resubmitted manuscripts; **one half of the fee refunded** if a paper is directly returned without full review (≈ within 3 months); **accepted papers must not exceed 40 pages of text, references, and footnotes and no more than 10 tables and figures combined**; manuscripts **double-spaced, 12-point type, 1-inch margins**; **"up or out"** on resubmission after a first-round reject/revise decision; resubmission invited only with **~50% chance of ultimate publication**.
- **https://sites.google.com/view/journal-of-monetary-economics/** — JME official site (home). Verifies: editors are **S. Borağan Aruoba (University of Maryland)** and **Eric Swanson (University of California, Irvine)**; JME uses Editorial Manager / Elsevier / ScienceDirect; publishes **eight volumes a year plus one Carnegie-Rochester-NYU Conference Series on Public Policy issue annually** and a **periodic Swiss National Bank Study Center Gerzensee** macro-policy issue.
- **https://sites.google.com/view/journal-of-monetary-economics/faq** — JME FAQ. Verifies: authors may **suggest up to five referees** and request that some not be used, in the cover letter; **formal reconsideration of a decision is costly, rare, and unlikely to reverse** an outcome.
- **https://jmeonline.org/5-2/authors/implementing-jme-style/** — "Implementing JME Style." Verifies: **abstract of not more than 100 words that does not start with "This paper" or "We"**; **author-date reference style** ("Smith (1992) reported that..."; "(e.g., Smith et al., 1969)") with **journal titles not abbreviated** and the **reference list placed after appendices but before tables/figures**; **double-spaced 12-point with 1-inch margins and required line numbers**; **footnotes (superscript Arabic numerals), not endnotes**; **up to five keywords** and **at least one JEL classification code**; tables and figures numbered consecutively with Arabic numerals.
- **https://jmeonline.org/5-2/authors/revise-and-resubmit/** — JME revise-and-resubmit policy. Verifies: editors invite a resubmission **only when there is at least a 50% likelihood of ultimate publication** (not guaranteed), and it is **"up or out"** for the resubmission after a first-round reject/revise decision.

## Elsevier / ScienceDirect pages

- **https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/journal-of-monetary-economics/publish/guide-for-authors** — Elsevier Guide for Authors. Verifies: publisher **Elsevier**; **submission fee US$350 (US$200 for full-time students)**, payable at submission and **required before consideration**; **single anonymized review** with a **minimum of two reviewers**; **40-page / 10 tables-and-figures** cap on accepted papers; authors must **declare any use of generative AI**; the critical **first revision must be rejected or accepted subject to a specified plan of further revisions**. **待核实 caveat:** this page returned **HTTP 403** on direct fetch; its facts were confirmed via Elsevier search-result snippets rather than a successful page load — re-confirm on the live page.
- **https://www.sciencedirect.com/journal/journal-of-monetary-economics/about/editors-interview/interview-with-editor-in-chief-dr-eric-swanson** — Elsevier editor interview. Verifies: **Eric Swanson** is identified as **editor-in-chief** of JME. **待核实 caveat:** the official JME site lists **two editors** (Aruoba and Swanson) without distinguishing a single chief; the exact title hierarchy is not fully confirmed from one authoritative page.
- **https://data.mendeley.com/journal/03043932** — JME on Mendeley Data. Verifies: **supplementary-materials / replication policy** — an editor **may require supplemental materials (proofs, data sets, computational programs) as a condition of publication**; authors are **strongly advised to deposit appendices, computer programs, and data files on ScienceDirect** (and **Mendeley Data** is supported) to enhance replication of and citation to the research.

## Distinctive norms (cross-referenced above)

- **"Up or out" first-revision rule** — for-authors page + revise-and-resubmit page.
- **~50% publication-likelihood threshold for an R&R** — revise-and-resubmit page.
- **Abstract ≤ 100 words, may not begin with "This paper" or "We"** — Implementing JME Style.
- **Submission fee charged up front (US$350 / US$200 PhD), half refunded on direct return** — for-authors page + Guide for Authors.
- **Carnegie-Rochester-NYU Conference Series on Public Policy** annual issue, plus a periodic **Swiss National Bank / Gerzensee** macro-policy issue — JME home page.
- **40 pages of text/references/footnotes + ≤ 10 tables and figures combined** for accepted papers — for-authors page + Guide for Authors.
- **Author-date references, journal titles spelled out in full; line numbers; footnotes not endnotes** — Implementing JME Style.

- **Word-count cap:** JME specifies a **page limit** for accepted papers (40 pages), **not** an explicit word count; no official word-count cap was found. An abstract word cap (100 words) *is* official. A body word-count limit is **待核实** / not applicable.
