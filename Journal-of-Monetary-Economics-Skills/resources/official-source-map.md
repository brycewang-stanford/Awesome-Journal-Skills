# JME Official Source Map

Primary and authoritative sources behind the *Journal of Monetary Economics* (JME)-specific
facts injected into this skill pack. **Access date for all entries: 2026-06-20.**
Re-verify volatile items (editors, fee amounts, page caps, policy wording) before relying on

## Official journal / author pages

- **https://sites.google.com/view/journal-of-monetary-economics/for-authors** — JME "For Authors" page. Verified 2026-06-20: submission via Elsevier **Editorial Manager** at `editorialmanager.com/monec/`; **submission fee US$350** for all manuscripts (reduced to **US$200 for full-time PhD students**); fee **not required for revised/resubmitted manuscripts**; **one half of the submission fee refunded** if a paper is directly returned without full review; **"accepted papers must not exceed 40 pages of text, references, and footnotes"** and **"should not include more than 10 tables and figures (F+T<=10)"**; manuscripts **double-spaced, 12-point type, 1-inch margins**; the decision after the first revision is **"up or out"**; editors ask for a resubmission **only when there is at least a 50% chance the manuscript is ultimately published**.
- **https://sites.google.com/view/journal-of-monetary-economics/** — JME official site (home). Verified 2026-06-20: editors are **S. Borağan Aruoba (University of Maryland)** and **Eric Swanson (University of California, Irvine)**, both as **co-Editors-in-Chief**; JME uses **Editorial Manager** (`editorialmanager.com/monec/default.aspx`) / Elsevier / ScienceDirect; **"The JME publishes eight volumes each year"**, with **one issue each year dedicated to the Carnegie-Rochester-NYU Conference on Public Policy**, and **"Every two years, the JME publishes a ninth volume with the proceedings from a conference on macroeconomic policy sponsored by the Study Center Gerzensee of the Swiss National Bank."** Note: the alternate official domain **https://jme.rochester.edu/** issues a **301 redirect** to this Google Sites home.
- **https://sites.google.com/view/journal-of-monetary-economics/faq** — JME FAQ. Verifies: authors may **suggest up to five referees** and request that some not be used, in the cover letter; **formal reconsideration of a decision is costly, rare, and unlikely to reverse** an outcome.
- **https://jmeonline.org/5-2/authors/implementing-jme-style/** — "Implementing JME Style." Verifies: **abstract of not more than 100 words that does not start with "This paper" or "We"**; **author-date reference style** ("Smith (1992) reported that..."; "(e.g., Smith et al., 1969)") with **journal titles not abbreviated** and the **reference list placed after appendices but before tables/figures**; **double-spaced 12-point with 1-inch margins and required line numbers**; **footnotes (superscript Arabic numerals), not endnotes**; **up to five keywords** and **at least one JEL classification code**; tables and figures numbered consecutively with Arabic numerals.
- **https://jmeonline.org/5-2/authors/revise-and-resubmit/** — JME revise-and-resubmit policy. Verifies: editors invite a resubmission **only when there is at least a 50% likelihood of ultimate publication** (not guaranteed), and it is **"up or out"** for the resubmission after a first-round reject/revise decision.

Verifies: publisher **Elsevier**; **submission fee US$350 (US$200 for full-time students)**, payable at submission and **required before consideration**; **single anonymized review** ("Your submission will initially be assessed by editors... typically be sent to a minimum of two reviewers") with a **minimum of two reviewers**; **40-page / 10 tables-and-figures** cap on accepted papers; **generative-AI / AI-authorship policy** — authors must **declare use of generative AI** in manuscript preparation at submission (does **not** apply to basic grammar/spelling/reference tools; no statement needed if nothing to disclose), and the journal **does not allow reviewers or editors to use generative AI** in peer review / manuscript evaluation; **research-data / data-availability policy** — Elsevier's **"encourage to state data availability"** option: authors are **encouraged to state the availability of any data at submission**, and **if data cannot be shared they should provide a statement explaining why** (deposit/citation of data is supported but a verified-reproducibility mandate is not stated here); the critical **first revision must be rejected or accepted subject to a specified plan of further revisions**. **待核实 caveat:** this page returned **HTTP 403** on direct fetch again on **2026-06-20**; its facts were confirmed via Elsevier/ScienceDirect search-result snippets (official domain) rather than a successful page load — re-confirm on the live page.

## Distinctive norms (cross-referenced above)

- **"Up or out" first-revision rule** — for-authors page + revise-and-resubmit page.
- **~50% publication-likelihood threshold for an R&R** — revise-and-resubmit page.
- **Abstract ≤ 100 words, may not begin with "This paper" or "We"** — Implementing JME Style.
- **Submission fee charged up front (US$350 / US$200 PhD), half refunded on direct return** — for-authors page + Guide for Authors.
- **Carnegie-Rochester-NYU Conference on Public Policy** annual issue, plus a **Swiss National Bank / Study Center Gerzensee** macro-policy ninth volume **every two years** — JME home page.
- **40 pages of text/references/footnotes + ≤ 10 tables and figures combined** for accepted papers — for-authors page + Guide for Authors.
- **Author-date references, journal titles spelled out in full; line numbers; footnotes not endnotes** — Implementing JME Style.
