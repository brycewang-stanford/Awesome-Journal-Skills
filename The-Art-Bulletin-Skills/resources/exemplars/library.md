> **Verified via web search, access date 2026-06-08.** Every article below was checked to confirm it
> actually appeared in **_The Art Bulletin_** (College Art Association, published by Taylor & Francis /
> Routledge; DOI prefix **10.1080/00043079…**, with older volumes also on JSTOR), with author, year, and
> volume/issue. Pieces that could not be fully verified *as Art Bulletin* are **omitted** — this is
> deliberately a short, clean list rather than a long, uncertain one.
>
> **Sibling-confusion guardrail.** *The Art Bulletin* is the College Art Association's flagship and is
> **not** any of these adjacent journals: *Art History* (AAH/Wiley), *October* (MIT Press),
> *Journal of the Society of Architectural Historians* (JSAH), *The Burlington Magazine*, or *Artforum*.

> original on Taylor & Francis / JSTOR before citing anything. For Art Bulletin scope, length, and

## By method

### Iconographic / single-painting close reading (early modern, attribution & meaning)

- **Madlyn Millner Kahr, "Velázquez and *Las Meninas*," *The Art Bulletin* 1975, 57(2):225–246.**
  Verified: DOI `10.1080/00043079.1975.10787153` (tandfonline.com/doi/abs/10.1080/00043079.1975.10787153).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a sustained argument about one canonical picture, built from close
    looking at the painting's internal logic (mirror, sightlines, the act of depiction) rather than from
    external theory — the model of letting the object carry the claim
    ([`artbull-visual-analysis`](../../skills/artbull-visual-analysis/SKILL.md)).
  - **Self-check:** does your reading of a single work turn on what the picture *itself* shows, tied to
    a figure a reader can hold in view?

### Technical / geometric analysis of a work's construction (material & perspectival method)

- **David L. Carleton, "A Mathematical Analysis of the Perspective of the Arnolfini Portrait and Other
  Similar Interior Scenes by Jan van Eyck," *The Art Bulletin* 1982, 64(1):118–124.**
  Verified: DOI `10.1080/00043079.1982.10787953` (tandfonline.com/doi/abs/10.1080/00043079.1982.10787953;
  JSTOR issue `i354305`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** reconstructs *how the picture was built* — its perspectival construction —
    and lets that technical reading bear on interpretation, the kind of material/technical close looking
    [`artbull-visual-analysis`](../../skills/artbull-visual-analysis/SKILL.md) calls for ("attend to the
    material object").
  - **Self-check:** does your technical evidence (construction, facture, condition) do *argumentative*
    work, not just document the object?

### Reflexive re-reading of a much-debated picture (object-grounded historiographic intervention)

- **Byron Ellsworth Hamann, "The Mirrors of *Las Meninas*: Cochineal, Silver, and Clay," *The Art
  Bulletin* 2010, 92(1–2):6–35.**
  Verified: DOI `10.1080/00043079.2010.10786133` (tandfonline.com/doi/abs/10.1080/00043079.2010.10786133).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** re-opens a saturated debate by grounding it in the *materials* of the
    objects depicted (the literal mirrors, pigments, and global trade behind them) — fresh stakes for a
    picture everyone thought was settled, exactly the Art Bulletin move of carrying a big claim on close
    material looking.
  - **Self-check:** can you make a heavily-studied object yield a genuinely new argument by looking
    harder at its material specifics, not by adding theory?

### Field-defining methodological critique (historiography of a sub-discipline)

- **Sheila S. Blair and Jonathan M. Bloom, "The Mirage of Islamic Art: Reflections on the Study of an
  Unwieldy Field," *The Art Bulletin* 2003, 85(1):152–184.**
  Verified: DOI `10.1080/00043079.2003.10787065` (tandfonline.com/doi/abs/10.1080/00043079.2003.10787065).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a historiographic intervention that interrogates how an entire field has
    been constructed — the kind of self-aware methodological argument
    [`artbull-scholarly-positioning`](../../skills/artbull-scholarly-positioning/SKILL.md) describes
    ("method with self-awareness"; engage the field, not just the file).
  - **Self-check:** if your contribution is methodological, do you name precisely what prior framings
    miss, rather than survey them?

### Feminist historiography revisited through the archive (gender & method)

- **Hannah Wirta Kinney / et al. — "Why Do We Think There Have Been No Great Women Artists? Revisiting
  Linda Nochlin and the Archive," *The Art Bulletin* 2022, 104(4).**
  Verified as Art Bulletin: DOI `10.1080/00043079.2022.2070397`
  (tandfonline.com/doi/abs/10.1080/00043079.2022.2070397). *(Authorship line shown partially in search
  results; confirm the full byline on the article page before citing — the venue, year, and volume are
  verified.)*
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** returns to a foundational feminist question and re-grounds it in archival
    evidence — historiography plus sources, the interlock
    [`artbull-argument-development`](../../skills/artbull-argument-development/SKILL.md) and
    [`artbull-evidence-and-sources`](../../skills/artbull-evidence-and-sources/SKILL.md) reward.
  - **Self-check:** does revisiting a canonical claim let new archival material change the conclusion,
    not just restate the debate?

### Global / cross-regional reframing of the discipline (premodern global art history)

- **"Premodern Globalism in Art History: A Conversation," *The Art Bulletin* 2022, 104(4).**
  Verified as Art Bulletin: DOI `10.1080/00043079.2022.2070394`
  (tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/00043079.2022.2070394). *(A multi-author conversation feature;
  confirm the participant list on the article page — venue, year, and volume are verified.)*
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** stages the field's reckoning with non-Western and cross-regional framing
    at the discipline's flagship — the
    [`artbull-topic-selection`](../../skills/artbull-topic-selection/SKILL.md) imperative to "make the
    methodological and conceptual stakes legible beyond area specialists."
  - **Self-check:** does your global or non-Western case carry a conceptual stake a non-specialist
    reader can grasp, not only area expertise?

## Omitted for lack of full *Art Bulletin* verification (do not attribute without re-checking)

To keep the list zero-hallucination, the following were **excluded** after checking:

- **Erwin Panofsky, "Jan van Eyck's 'Arnolfini' Portrait" (1934)** — verified to be in **_The Burlington
  Magazine_** (64, no. 372), **not _The Art Bulletin_**. Listed here only as a sibling-confusion
  guardrail; do not place it under this journal.
- **Leo Steinberg / Joel Snyder, *Las Meninas* essays** — frequently cited in Art Bulletin debates, but
  the most-cited versions appeared elsewhere (e.g., *Critical Inquiry*); a clean *Art Bulletin*
  placement of the specific essays could not be confirmed by web search, so they are omitted. Verify
  before attributing.
- **Linda Nochlin, "Why Have There Been No Great Women Artists?" (1971)** — appeared in **_ARTnews_**,
  not *The Art Bulletin*. Only the **2022 Art Bulletin** article *revisiting* Nochlin (above) is listed
  here.

Before adding any new article to this library, confirm it on **tandfonline.com** under DOI prefix
`10.1080/00043079…` (or on JSTOR's `artbulletin` archive) with volume/issue, and update the access-date
header. When in doubt, omit.
