> **Illustrative teaching example.** The article, the object, and every interpretive claim below are
> **fictional**, built around a deliberately invented panel so that no real attribution, provenance, or
> document is asserted. It exists only to demonstrate *The Art Bulletin* house style for an article
> opening. No policy, no archive, and no real-paper facts are stated. Corresponding skills:
> [`artbull-argument-development`](../../skills/artbull-argument-development/SKILL.md),
> [`artbull-visual-analysis`](../../skills/artbull-visual-analysis/SKILL.md),
> [`artbull-structure-and-exposition`](../../skills/artbull-structure-and-exposition/SKILL.md), and

# Worked Example: An Art Bulletin–Style Introduction (before → after)

This demonstrates the **Art Bulletin opening arc** drawn from this pack's own skills:
**problem & object → contestable thesis → stakes for the field → looking + documents that warrant it →
the rival reading it answers → what it changes** — with the discipline's core rule that the argument is
**carried by close looking at specific works, tied to numbered figures, grounded in historical
evidence** ([`artbull-topic-selection`](../../skills/artbull-topic-selection/SKILL.md):
"an art-historical argument, not a description … carried by the objects"). It is *not* a data study and
states no statistics.

**Illustrative work (fictional):** *The Pilgrim's Mirror*, an invented small devotional panel
(tempera and gold on oak) attributed here to a fictional "Master of the Bruges Threshold," supposedly
painted for a private household chapel. Everything about it is invented for teaching.

---

## Before (buries the argument — typical first-draft opening)

> The devotional panel painting of the fifteenth-century Low Countries has been the subject of
> extensive scholarship. Many studies have examined domestic piety, disguised symbolism, and the role
> of the household chapel. In this article I examine *The Pilgrim's Mirror*, a little-known panel that
> has received scant attention in the literature. The panel is richly detailed and rewards close study.
> I will describe its composition, its iconographic program, and its convex mirror, and I will situate
> it within the broader culture of late-medieval devotion. The first section reviews the literature, the
> second describes the panel, the third treats the iconography, and the fourth offers conclusions.

**What's wrong (against this pack's skills):**

- **A topic, not a thesis.** "I examine … a little-known panel" names a subject but no contestable
  claim — the named anti-pattern in
  [`artbull-argument-development`](../../skills/artbull-argument-development/SKILL.md)
  ("Description dressed as argument").
- **Novelty rests on neglect.** "Scant attention in the literature" is exactly the
  [`artbull-topic-selection`](../../skills/artbull-topic-selection/SKILL.md) anti-pattern
  ("'No one has studied this object' as the whole contribution").
- **No stakes beyond the single case** — a scholar of another period or region is given no reason to
  care (`artbull-topic-selection`: "significance beyond the single case").
- **Description promised before argument** — a "descriptive overture" flagged by
  [`artbull-structure-and-exposition`](../../skills/artbull-structure-and-exposition/SKILL.md).
- **No figure cited**, so the looking has no visible warrant
  ([`artbull-visual-analysis`](../../skills/artbull-visual-analysis/SKILL.md): "no visual claim without
  a visible warrant").
- **Over-signposted roadmap** doing the work the argument should do.

---

## After (Art Bulletin arc — thesis carried by looking, stakes early, rival reading named)

> A small oak panel known as *The Pilgrim's Mirror* (Figure 1) has been read for a century as a
> straightforward emblem of domestic piety: a kneeling donor, a convex mirror on the rear wall, and a
> window opening onto a road. I argue instead that the panel stages its own **act of beholding** — that
> the mirror does not reflect the room behind the viewer but a *second*, absent devotional image, and
> that the painter built the panel to make the household worshiper aware of looking as a spiritual
> labor rather than a passive glance. *(object + contestable thesis in the first breath — something a
> reader could dispute.)*
>
> The claim turns on what the mirror actually shows. Read at the panel's original near-arm's-length
> viewing distance, the convex reflection (Figure 2, detail) does not repeat the donor's back or the
> chamber's window; its curvature returns a faint diptych wing that exists nowhere else in the depicted
> room. *(close looking tied to a numbered figure and a detail crop — the warrant for the
> interpretation, per `artbull-visual-analysis`.)* The handling reinforces this: the mirror's rim is
> built up in raised gilding while the reflected image is scumbled and deliberately indistinct, a
> contrast in facture that asks the eye to work for what it sees. *(facture read for meaning, not
> adjectives.)*
>
> A household inventory and a confraternity record — read here only for what they establish about the
> panel's setting and a now-lost companion image — place the panel in a private chapel beside a second
> devotional object. *(documentary evidence interlocked with the looking, per
> `artbull-argument-development`; the documents are invented for this teaching example.)* Together the
> looking and the documents support a reading in which the mirror points *outside the frame* to that
> companion, making beholding itself the panel's subject.
>
> The obvious rival reading — Panofsky's enduring account of the convex mirror as a sign of an
> all-seeing divine witness — explains the mirror's presence but not its *content*: why this reflection
> withholds the room and returns an image that is not there. *(the strongest counter-reading named, and
> shown to leave the key evidence unexplained — `artbull-argument-development`: "anticipate the
> counter-reading.")*
>
> What is at stake is larger than one panel. If a domestic image could be built to *thematize* the
> beholder's looking this early, then the self-reflexive picture — usually located in the grand court
> painting of the next two centuries — has a humble, devotional prehistory in the household chapel.
> *(stakes for the field, legible beyond the specialty, per `artbull-topic-selection`'s "significance
> beyond the single case.")*
>
> The article proceeds from the panel and its mirror (Figures 1–3) to the documents that fix its
> setting, and then to the broader claim about beholding. *(brief roadmap — no over-signposting, per
> `artbull-structure-and-exposition`.)*

---

## Why the "after" meets the Art Bulletin bar

Mapped to this pack's skill checklists:

- **Thesis is a contestable claim, not a topic** — "the panel stages its own act of beholding" could be
  wrong; a reader can dispute it ([`artbull-argument-development`](../../skills/artbull-argument-development/SKILL.md):
  "State the thesis as a claim, not a topic").
- **Carried by close looking, tied to figures** — the argument turns on what Figures 1–2 actually show,
  with a **detail crop** supplying the warrant
  ([`artbull-visual-analysis`](../../skills/artbull-visual-analysis/SKILL.md): "tie every claim to a
  figure"; facture read for meaning, not "adjective-piling").
- **Visual and documentary evidence interlock** — looking plus an inventory/record, with the inference
  named rather than smuggled in (`artbull-argument-development`: "integrate the visual and the
  documentary").
- **The rival reading is named and answered** — Panofsky's divine-witness mirror is the counter a
  reviewer would raise first, and the draft shows why the new reading accounts for the evidence better
  (`artbull-argument-development`: "anticipate the counter-reading").
- **Stakes stated early and beyond the single case** — a prehistory for the self-reflexive picture, a
  claim a scholar of later periods cares about
  ([`artbull-topic-selection`](../../skills/artbull-topic-selection/SKILL.md): "significance beyond the
  single case"; novelty is *not* grounded in mere neglect).
- **Argument leads, description serves it** — no descriptive overture; figures are introduced where the
  prose needs them ([`artbull-structure-and-exposition`](../../skills/artbull-structure-and-exposition/SKILL.md):
  "lead with the argument, not a catalogue"; "integrate figures where they are discussed").
- **Within length and format discipline** — the opening earns each figure it cites, leaving room under
  the ~16,000-word cap including endnotes (`artbull-structure-and-exposition`); citations would follow
  Chicago **endnotes** per
  [`artbull-writing-style-and-citation`](../../skills/artbull-writing-style-and-citation/SKILL.md).
