# External Tools - ACM FAccT

Access date: 2026-07-09

Use these surfaces after reopening the current FAccT cycle pages. FAccT mechanics — deadlines,
page budget, endmatter statements, the Revise round, and the submission platform — are decided per
edition, and several were **newly introduced in 2026** (OpenReview, the Revise-and-resubmit
decision, the required Generative AI Usage Statement). The `facctconference.org`/`dl.acm.org`
gateway may block direct fetches (read via search renderings and cross-check OpenReview, ACM DL, and
dblp). Note the naming history: the venue was **FAT\*** in 2018-2019, renamed **FAccT** after 2020;
the dblp key is `db/conf/fat`.

## Cross-check surfaces (when the gateway blocks the primary page)

- OpenReview FAccT 2026 group for machine-readable dates, fields, and the submission form.
- ACM DL FAccT proceedings (2019 onward) for accepted-paper verification.
- PMLR vol 81 for the FAT* 2018 proceedings (the inaugural edition was published in PMLR, not ACM DL).
- dblp FAccT/FAT* stream: https://dblp.org/db/conf/fat/index.html
- The FAccT Blog posts announcing the CFP and reflecting on each year's review process.

## Author-side checks

- OpenReview profile, conflicts, coauthor emails, and the mandatory **abstract-registration** step
  (with **focus-area** selection) that precedes the paper deadline.
- `acmart` compliance built anonymized for review (e.g. `manuscript,screen,review,anonymous`), the
  current page budget (14 content pages + 1 endmatter page; references unlimited), and PDF export.
- Mutual-anonymity sweep: anonymize the PDF, released links, system and field-site names, and — 
  critically — **withhold the Positionality / Acknowledgements / Author-Contributions / Competing-
  Interests statements** from the anonymous submission.
- The **required Generative AI Usage Statement**, plus optional **Ethical Considerations** and
  **Adverse Impacts** statements.
- Accountability documentation where relevant: datasheet, model card, data statement, impact
  assessment (see [`../skills/facct-artifact-evaluation`](../skills/facct-artifact-evaluation/SKILL.md)).
- The **archival vs non-archival** choice, and the in-person presentation requirement.

## Do not infer

- Do not assume the **Revise** decision, the **OpenReview** platform, or the **Generative AI Usage
  Statement** carry over — all three are 2026 firsts. Confirm on the current CFP/Author Guide.
- Do not import a SIGSOFT-style ACM artifact **badging** track; FAccT centers documentation and
  release, not Available/Functional/Reusable badges (**待核实** whether any optional scheme exists).
- Do not assume FAccT covers APCs. ACM went 100% Open Access on 1 Jan 2026; whether FAccT authors
  pay an APC or are covered by ACM Open, and any fee-waiver, is **待核实**.
- If pages disagree, treat the newest CFP, Author Guide, or OpenReview deadline as controlling, and
  record the conflict.
