# Source Basis and Official-Check Notes

This pack encodes journal-fit and house-style heuristics for 40 flagship
English-language **engineering and technology** journals — the venues the
sibling `English-NaturalScience-Journal-Skills` bundle deliberately leaves thin.
It is a positioning and re-framing aid, **not** a static substitute for any
journal's live author instructions.

For per-journal starting queries, use `official-source-map.md` in this directory.

## What this bundle covers (and why these venues)

- **IEEE Transactions / Proceedings family.** Stable, public prestige conventions
  in electrical, control, communications, and computer engineering: Proceedings of
  the IEEE (authoritative tutorials/reviews), and the field-defining Transactions
  on Automatic Control, Information Theory, Signal Processing, Communications,
  Wireless Communications, Power Electronics, Power Systems, Industrial
  Electronics, Antennas and Propagation, Robotics, Medical Imaging, and Biomedical
  Engineering, plus the IEEE Journal on Selected Areas in Communications.
- **Control & robotics.** Automatica (IFAC) and The International Journal of
  Robotics Research (IJRR).
- **Optics & photonics.** Optica (Optica Publishing Group), Light: Science &
  Applications (Nature Portfolio / CIOMP), and Laser & Photonics Reviews (Wiley).
- **Nature Portfolio engineering.** Nature Electronics and Nature Biomedical
  Engineering.
- **Mechanics, solids, fluids, manufacturing, aerospace.** Journal of the
  Mechanics and Physics of Solids (JMPS), International Journal of Plasticity,
  Journal of Fluid Mechanics (JFM), International Journal of Machine Tools and
  Manufacture, Additive Manufacturing, Composites Part B: Engineering, and
  Progress in Aerospace Sciences.
- **Chemical, process & energy engineering.** AIChE Journal, Chemical Engineering
  Journal, Progress in Energy and Combustion Science, Journal of Membrane Science,
  Applied Energy, Journal of Power Sources, and Energy Storage Materials.
- **Materials engineering, civil & transportation.** Acta Materialia, Progress in
  Materials Science, Transportation Research Part B: Methodological, and Cement and
  Concrete Research.

## Stable, public facts used for fit (no volatile numbers)

Only durable structural conventions are used to inform fit — e.g. the IEEE
double-column Transactions format and the regular-paper / letter / brief / survey
article-type structure; the IFAC affiliation of Automatica; the Optica Publishing
Group, Nature Portfolio, Wiley, Elsevier, and Cambridge imprints; the
review/tutorial character of Proceedings of the IEEE, Progress in Materials
Science, Progress in Aerospace Sciences, and Progress in Energy and Combustion
Science; the methodological (vs. applied) focus of Transportation Research Part B;
and the data/code-availability and reproducibility expectations now standard in
engineering venues.

## What each profile deliberately does NOT assert

To stay correct over time, every profile **omits volatile facts** and defers them
to the official-submission checklist instead of stating a number:

- No impact factors, CiteScores, acceptance rates, or "ranked #N" claims.
- No ISSNs, exact word/page/figure limits, APC amounts, or editor names.
- No fabricated citations (literatures are referred to generically).

## Operational rule for every journal skill

1. Before submitting, search the **live official site or submission system** for
   "<journal name> author instructions / information for authors" and follow the
   current version.
2. Trust the publisher's own domain or submission system (IEEE Author Center /
   ScholarOne, Elsevier Editorial Manager, Optica Prism, the Nature/Wiley/Cambridge
   systems) over any third-party or aggregator copy.
3. Re-check article-type and length limits, abstract format, figure/display limits,
   the applicable reporting/reproducibility expectations, data / code / materials
   availability and deposition, ethics/consent (for biomedical or human-subjects
   work), competing-interests and funding disclosure, AI-use disclosure, preprint
   policy, and open-access / licensing terms.
4. For umbrella families (IEEE, Nature Portfolio, Elsevier, Wiley), check both the
   journal-specific page and the family/author-center policy page.
5. If the live official instructions conflict with a skill, the official
   instructions win.
