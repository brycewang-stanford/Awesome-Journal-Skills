# Official Source Map — Academy of Management Review (AMR)

Citable sources for the AMR-specific facts used across these skills. AMR is the Academy of
Management's (AOM) **theory-development journal** — it publishes purely conceptual/theoretical
articles that develop **testable knowledge-based claims** and does **not** publish empirical
hypothesis-testing studies (those belong at its empirical sibling, the *Academy of Management
Journal*, AMJ). Sources were refreshed on **2026-06-21** against the current AOM journal page,
AOM author resources, AMR editorial-team page, AOM style guide PDF, and AOM AI policy.

## Verified facts (with source #)

- AMR is a **theory-development journal**; it publishes conceptual articles, NOT empirical
  hypothesis tests. (Sources 1, 2)
- AMR scope (Source 2): submissions "must extend theory in ways that develop **testable
  knowledge-based claims**," via one of four paths (new theory; challenge/clarify existing
  theory; synthesize advances into fresh theory; identify a novel theoretical problem).
- AMR mission (Source 1): publish "theoretical insights that advance our understanding of
  management and organizations"; conceptual articles "that challenge conventional wisdom."
- AMR metrics displayed by AOM on 2026-06-21: **13.9 Impact Factor**, **19.3 Five-Year
  Impact Factor**, and **10.19 Article Influence Score** (Clarivate 2025). (Source 1)
- Published by the **Academy of Management** (on the SAGE platform); appears quarterly.
  (Sources 1, 2)
- AMR is the **theory counterpart to AMJ** (empirical); distinct from ASQ (theory +
  empirical) and SMJ (strategy, largely empirical). (Sources 1, 2)
- AOM journals (incl. AMR) use the **AOM Style Guide** author–date citation/reference style
  and submit via **ScholarOne Manuscripts**. The AMR portal target is
  `https://mc.manuscriptcentral.com/amr`. (Sources 4, 5)
- Current AMR editor: **Kris Byron**, Georgia State University. AOM lists her as Editor, and
  the Georgia State announcement states her three-year editor-in-chief term began July 1,
  2023. Current Associate Editors are listed on the AOM editorial-team page. (Sources 3, 6)
  - **Live-check note (2026-06-23):** re-verified against the AOM AMR editorial-team page and
    Wikipedia — Byron is still listed as the current AMR Editor and is sitting now. The GSU
    announcement (#6) dates the editor-in-chief term to "began July 1, 2023"; AOM's incoming-editor
    framing puts the three-year term at **1 Jan 2024 – 31 Dec 2026** (with a 1 July 2023 transition/
    shadow start), so Byron remains the editor through the end of 2026, after which a transition is
    expected. **No named successor / incoming AMR editor was found in a 2026-06-23 web search** (AOM
    has not yet announced one publicly), so do not assert a successor; confirm the named editor on the
    live AOM masthead before relying on it in a cover letter.
- **Submission fee**: AOM states that the Academy of Management does not charge submission
  fees. New users establish a ScholarOne account with name, affiliation, address details, and
  keywords before using the Author Center. (Source 4)
- **Formatting / abstract**: Word file; Times New Roman 12-point; Letter page; double-spaced
  body; 1.5-spaced headings; 1-inch margins; no page numbers or running heads; title page
  excluded from the version under review; abstract is no more than **200 words** on page 2.
  (Source 5)
- **AI policy**: AI use must be disclosed by research stage with author accountability; language
  model use for journal submissions belongs in the cover letter and acknowledgements with model,
  purpose, source, verification, and limitation details. (Source 7)
- **Review process**: AOM says designated reviewers advise the editor and revisions can proceed
  through multiple rounds; editors retain broad discretion and content-based appeals are not
  eligible. (Source 4)
- The contribution bar and construct-clarity criteria are defined in AMR-published editorials
  (Whetten 1989; Suddaby 2010). (Landmark table)

## Bounded live-check notes

- **Content length beyond the abstract.** The AOM Style Guide points authors to the journal
  submission pages for desired content length; this source map verifies the 200-word abstract and
  formatting rules but does not assert a current AMR word/page cap.
- **APA edition language.** AOM publishes its own Style Guide. Treat "APA-style" in pack prose as
  shorthand for AOM's author–date/reference mechanics rather than a claim that AOM follows a
  named APA edition.
- **Portal-stage account details.** AOM's public author page requires a ScholarOne account for
  new users. Any membership/subscription prompts inside ScholarOne should be checked at submission
  time.
- **Reviewer count and acceptance rate.** The public author page says designated reviewers advise
  the editor and multiple revision rounds may occur, but it does not publish an exact AMR reviewer
  count or acceptance rate.
- **Year AMR was established: 1976** (Vol. 1, No. 1, January 1976) — verified 2026-06 via the
  AOM journal archive (`journals.aom.org/loi/amr`) and corroborated by JSTOR's AMR run.
