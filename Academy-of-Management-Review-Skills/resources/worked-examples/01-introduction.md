# Worked Example: An AMR-Style Theory Introduction (before → after)

This demonstrates the **AMR introduction arc** from `amr-writing-style`:
**theoretical puzzle (the tension) → the conversation it lives in → what theory cannot currently do →
the move this paper makes → the contribution, stated early** — for a purely *conceptual* contribution.
The deliverable is **constructs + a "why/how" mechanism + numbered propositions**, never
hypotheses-with-data. The vocabulary rule (`amr-writing-style`, `amr-theory-development`) is absolute:
**propositions, not hypotheses; mechanisms, not findings; no "H1," no "supported," no data, ever.**

**Illustrative paper (fictional):** *"Borrowed Legitimacy: How Organizations Convert Audience Doubt
into a Renewable Resource."* Fictional conceptual setting: organizations that operate under chronic
audience skepticism (the example is invented to carry the argument; it asserts nothing about any real
organization, industry, or policy).

---

## Before (reads as a literature review / essay — typical first-draft intro)

> The concept of legitimacy has been studied extensively in institutional theory. Many scholars have
> examined how organizations gain, maintain, and repair legitimacy. Suchman (1995) distinguished
> pragmatic, moral, and cognitive legitimacy, and subsequent work has added many refinements. In this
> paper, we hypothesize that organizations facing audience doubt will engage in legitimacy-repair
> behaviors, and we propose a model in which doubt is a moderator of the legitimacy–performance
> relationship. Legitimacy is an important topic for organizations. This paper reviews the literature
> and integrates several streams. Section 2 reviews institutional theory, Section 3 develops our model,
> Section 4 discusses implications, and Section 5 concludes.

**What's wrong (against `amr-writing-style` / `amr-theory-development` / `amr-contribution-framing`):**

- **Opens with a definition and a literature survey** ("has been studied extensively"), not the
  theoretical *tension* — a named anti-pattern in `amr-writing-style` (dictionary/survey opening).
- **Uses hypothesis-and-data vocabulary** ("we hypothesize," "moderator of the … relationship"). That
  is AMJ's register; it reads as **wrong-journal** to AMR editors (`amr-theory-development`: never "H1,"
  never moderation-to-be-tested).
- **No theoretical puzzle on page one.** A reader cannot tell what existing theory *cannot* explain.
- **Contribution is "we integrate streams"** — reviewing/organizing a literature is *not* a theoretical
  contribution at AMR (`amr-contribution-framing` sizing table).
- **No mechanism (the Why)** — the engine that would make any relationship hold is absent
  (`amr-theory-development` build order, step 4; Whetten's Why).
- **Over-signposted roadmap** doing the work the argument should do.

---

## After (AMR arc — puzzle → conversation → gap → move → contribution early)

> Audience doubt is treated everywhere in institutional theory as a **threat** to be repaired: a deficit
> that drains legitimacy until the organization restores conformity. Yet some organizations seem to
> *run on* the very doubt that should deplete them — their standing does not recover in spite of
> skepticism but **through** it. Existing theory has no vocabulary for this: it can model how doubt is
> reduced, but not how doubt is **converted into a resource that regenerates**. *(the theoretical
> puzzle — a tension current theory cannot resolve — in the first breath.)*
>
> This puzzle lives in the conversation on organizational legitimacy, where legitimacy is theorized as a
> stock that audiences confer and withdraw. That framing makes doubt purely subtractive, so the field
> reaches for repair, signaling, and conformity as the only moves. *(the conversation, and the specific
> commitment that creates the blind spot.)*
>
> We argue that audience doubt can function as a **renewable legitimacy input** rather than a drain, and
> we build the theory that specifies when and why. We define two constructs — **borrowed legitimacy**
> (provisional standing an organization draws against an audience's *unresolved* judgment) and
> **doubt-conversion capacity** (an organization's capability to route skepticism into renewed
> evaluative engagement rather than withdrawal) — and we specify the mechanism that links them: each
> episode of survived scrutiny **re-enlists** the doubting audience as an active evaluator, which
> replenishes the borrowed stock so long as the organization keeps the judgment open rather than closing
> it. *(the move: new constructs + the Why-mechanism, not a data plan.)*
>
> From this we derive propositions. **P1:** the greater an organization's doubt-conversion capacity, the
> more its borrowed legitimacy is replenished by episodes of audience scrutiny, because each survived
> episode re-enlists the audience as an active evaluator. **P2:** borrowed legitimacy is renewable only
> while the audience's judgment remains unresolved; once the audience reaches a settled verdict —
> acceptance or rejection — conversion capacity ceases to replenish it. *(propositions stated as
> relationships between constructs, each with a one-line mechanism — never an empirical magnitude.)*
>
> Our contribution is to **respecify doubt as a renewable theoretical input** rather than a deficit:
> before this paper, legitimacy theory could explain how doubt is reduced; after it, theory can explain
> how doubt is **metabolized** into standing, and can state the boundary at which that metabolism stops.
> This differs from the nearest prior view — legitimacy-as-stock and its repair logic — not by adding a
> contingency but by changing what doubt *is* in the theory. *(contribution framed before → after,
> against a named prior view, per `amr-contribution-framing`.)* The reframing opens implications for any
> conversation in which an evaluative audience's *unsettled* judgment, not its approval, is the operative
> resource — trust, reputation, and status among them. *(implication for theory — reasoned, not
> empirical advice.)*
>
> The paper proceeds as follows. We first define the two constructs and their assumptions, then specify
> the conversion mechanism and derive the propositions, and finally state the boundary conditions and
> implications for theory. *(brief roadmap — no over-signposting.)*

---

## Why the "after" meets the AMR bar

Mapped to the skill checklists:

- **Opens with the theoretical puzzle, not a definition or survey** — the tension ("organizations that
  run on doubt") is on page one (`amr-writing-style`: puzzle within ~2 pages; no dictionary opening).
- **Constructs are defined on first use and labeled consistently** — *borrowed legitimacy* and
  *doubt-conversion capacity* each get a one-sentence conceptual definition and are reused verbatim
  (`amr-theory-development` construct template; Suddaby construct clarity).
- **The mechanism (the Why) is explicit** — "each survived episode re-enlists the audience" is the
  engine, stated before the propositions (`amr-theory-development` build order, step 4; Whetten's Why).
- **Propositions, not hypotheses** — P1–P2 are relationships between constructs, each earned by a
  preceding argument and carrying a mechanism; no "H1," no magnitudes, no "supported"
  (`amr-theory-development` proposition discipline; `amr-writing-style` no-hypothesis-vocabulary rule).
- **Contribution stated early and as before → after, against a named prior view** ("legitimacy-as-stock
  and its repair logic"), and explicitly **not** a relabel or an added moderator
  (`amr-contribution-framing` two questions + differentiation).
- **No methods/results/data** anywhere — the reasoning *is* the contribution; the implication is a
  reasoned theoretical consequence, not proven advice (`amr-writing-style`: theory-only structure).
- **Boundary condition is theorized, not apologized for** — P2 bounds the theory (renewable only while
  judgment is unsettled), which is itself part of the contribution (`amr-theory-development`).
