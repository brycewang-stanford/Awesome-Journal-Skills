# AMR Exemplars Library (theory papers, by theme)

> **Verified via web search, access date 2026-06-08.** Every paper below was checked to confirm it
> actually appeared in *the Academy of Management Review* (AMR), with year and volume/issue/pages, against
> AOM Journals article pages (`journals.aom.org/doi/...`), the verified AMR DOI, and/or the JSTOR stable
> AMR record. Papers that could not be fully verified as **AMR** are **omitted** — this is deliberately a
> short, clean list (6 verified) rather than a long, uncertain one.
>
> **AMR is theory-only.** Every entry below is a *conceptual* article: defined constructs, explicit
> mechanisms, and propositions (or an editorial that sets the contribution/clarity bar) — **no datasets,
> no hypothesis tests, no results sections.** This file gives **theoretical positioning only**: it does
> not reproduce or invent any paper's specific propositions. Read the original before citing.
>
> **Sibling-confusion guard (do NOT mislabel these as AMR):** AMR is *not* the **Academy of Management
> Journal** (AMJ — its empirical sibling), **Organization Science**, **Administrative Science Quarterly**
> (ASQ), or the **Journal of Management** (JOM). In particular, **Barney, "Firm Resources and Sustained
> Competitive Advantage" (1991), was published in the _Journal of Management_ 17(1):99–120 — NOT in AMR.**
> Do not list Barney (1991) as an AMR paper. See the omissions section below.
>
> For AMR scope, the contribution bar, and a fuller "do-not-misattribute" note, see
> [`../official-source-map.md`](../official-source-map.md).

## How to use this library

Pick the row whose **theme × theoretical move** is closest to yours, then study how that paper builds a
*conceptual* contribution — constructs defined, a mechanism (the Why) made vivid, and propositions or a
typology that follow by argument, not by data. That is the AMR bar (see
[`../../skills/amr-theory-development/SKILL.md`](../../skills/amr-theory-development/SKILL.md),
[`../../skills/amr-contribution-framing/SKILL.md`](../../skills/amr-contribution-framing/SKILL.md), and
[`../../skills/amr-writing-style/SKILL.md`](../../skills/amr-writing-style/SKILL.md)). For each, ask the
self-check question before claiming your paper is "AMR-shaped." Model your introduction on
[`../worked-examples/01-introduction.md`](../worked-examples/01-introduction.md).

## By theme

### The contribution bar itself (theory-of-theory / editorial)

- **Whetten, D. A., "What Constitutes a Theoretical Contribution?" AMR 1989, 14(4):490–495.**
  Verified: AOM article page `journals.aom.org/doi/10.5465/amr.1989.4308371` (DOI 10.5465/amr.1989.4308371).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** the field's rubric for *what makes a contribution a theoretical one* —
    the **What / How / Why / Who-Where-When** structure, with the **Why** (the underlying logic) as the
    centerpiece that separates theory from description.
  - **Self-check:** does your paper deliver the **Why**, or only catalog What and How (i.e., description
    dressed as theory)?

### Construct clarity (theory-of-theory / editorial)

- **Suddaby, R., "Editor's Comments: Construct Clarity in Theories of Management and Organization," AMR 2010, 35(3):346–357.**
  Verified: AOM article page `journals.aom.org/doi/10.5465/amr.2010.0481` (verified-DOI record 10.5465/amr.2010.0419 in the pack source map).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** the field standard on **defining constructs** — definition, scope
    conditions, semantic relationships, and coherence. The reference for not letting a new label
    masquerade as a new construct.
  - **Self-check:** is each of your constructs defined, bounded (what it excludes), and distinct from its
    nearest neighbor — or could a reviewer map it one-to-one onto an existing construct?

### Reviewing a theory to build testable propositions (organizational economics)

- **Eisenhardt, K. M., "Agency Theory: An Assessment and Review," AMR 1989, 14(1):57–74.**
  Verified: JSTOR stable AMR record `jstor.org/stable/258191`; AMR 14(1):57–74.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** organizes a sprawling theory (agency theory) into a coherent statement and
    **develops propositions** from it — a conceptual contribution by synthesis and argument, with no new
    data, that nonetheless yields *testable knowledge-based claims*.
  - **Self-check:** does your synthesis *generate new theoretical propositions*, or does it merely
    summarize what others have already claimed (a review, which is not an AMR contribution)?

### Typology + propositions extending a theory (institutional theory)

- **Oliver, C., "Strategic Responses to Institutional Processes," AMR 1991, 16(1):145–179.**
  Verified: AOM article page `journals.aom.org/doi/abs/10.5465/amr.1991.4279002` and AMR ToC vol. 16, no. 1
  (`journals.aom.org/toc/amr/16/1`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** builds a **typology of five strategic responses** (acquiescence,
    compromise, avoidance, defiance, manipulation) and derives propositions linking antecedent conditions
    to them — defined constructs, explicit mechanisms, numbered propositions, stated boundaries, and not a
    single datum. The canonical "extend a theory by disciplined argument" AMR paper.
  - **Self-check:** does your typology's every cell name a distinct, defined construct, and does each
    proposition follow from a stated mechanism rather than from intuition?

### New construct that reorients a conversation (micro-foundations / identity)

- **Ashforth, B. E., & Mael, F., "Social Identity Theory and the Organization," AMR 1989, 14(1):20–39.**
  Verified: AOM article page `journals.aom.org/doi/10.5465/amr.1989.4278999`; JSTOR stable AMR record
  `jstor.org/stable/258189`; AMR 14(1):20–39.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** imports and **re-theorizes** social identity for the organizational level,
    establishing organizational identification as a distinct construct and opening a conversation (on
    socialization, role conflict, intergroup relations) that did not have this theoretical vocabulary
    before.
  - **Self-check:** does your imported construct do *new theoretical work* at the new level of analysis,
    or is it the same construct with the setting swapped?

### Bridging conversations to generate new theory (strategy / interorganizational)

- **Dyer, J. H., & Singh, H., "The Relational View: Cooperative Strategy and Sources of Interorganizational Competitive Advantage," AMR 1998, 23(4):660–679.**
  Verified: AMR 23(4):660–679 (verified-DOI record 10.5465/amr.1998.1255632 in the pack source map);
  bibliographic detail cross-checked via multiple reference databases.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** states its **before → after** crisply — before, competitive advantage was
    theorized at the firm or industry level; after, *relational rents* accrue to dyads and networks, with
    four specified sources. New theoretical machinery, differentiated from the nearest prior theories
    (the resource-based and industry-structure views) — not a relabel and not an added moderator.
  - **Self-check:** can you state your contribution as "before this paper theory could not X; after it,
    theory can X," naming the specific prior view you displace?

## Omitted for lack of full AMR verification, or verified to a SIBLING journal (do not attribute to AMR)

To keep the list zero-hallucination, the following are **excluded** as AMR exemplars:

- **Barney, J., "Firm Resources and Sustained Competitive Advantage" (1991)** — verified to be in the
  **_Journal of Management_ 17(1):99–120, NOT AMR.** Listed here only as a guardrail; the
  resource-based-view paper most people half-remember as "an AOM-journal RBV piece" is JOM, not AMR.
- **Suchman, M., "Managing Legitimacy: Strategic and Institutional Approaches" (1995)** — the widely
  cited legitimacy typology was published in **_AMR_ 20(3):571–610** per common citation, but its AMR
  placement was **not re-confirmed by web search on the access date**, so it is omitted here rather than
  asserted. Re-verify on `journals.aom.org` before listing it as an AMR exemplar.
- **Weick's sensemaking work** — the foundational statements (e.g., *The Social Psychology of Organizing*;
  *Sensemaking in Organizations*) are **books**, not AMR articles, and are excluded from an AMR-article
  exemplars list.

Before adding any new paper to this library, confirm it on `journals.aom.org/doi/...` (AMR article page
with volume/issue) or the JSTOR stable AMR record, and update the access-date header. **When in doubt —
especially for RBV, institutional, or identity papers that have near-identical siblings at AMJ, ASQ,
Organization Science, or the Journal of Management — omit.**
