# External Tools - WACV

Access date: 2026-07-09

Official surfaces for WACV work, plus the author-side verification passes to run against
them. WACV policy is re-set by each edition's chairs, and — unlike its CVF siblings — the
calendar has **two submission rounds** with a Revise-and-Resubmit path between them, so
"the deadline" is really two deadlines and two OpenReview groups. The 2027 links below are
the live cycle; swap the year in each URL once a later cycle opens.

## Author-side verification passes

1. **Round pass** — decide Round 1 or Round 2 first, because they are different
   OpenReview groups with different consequences: only Round 1 offers the rebuttal and
   the Revise-and-Resubmit safety net; Round 2 is Accept/Reject. Confirm which round the
   target deadline belongs to before anything else.
2. **Track pass** — choose Applications vs Algorithms deliberately and match the paper's
   framing to that track's review criteria; the primary-track field is not a formality.
3. **Identity pass** — every coauthor's OpenReview profile complete and valid before the
   round deadline; missing/invalid profiles are desk-reject grounds.
4. **Format pass** — build from the current WACV author kit, count pages including
   figures and tables against the 8-page cap, confirm overflow pages hold references only.
5. **Anonymity pass** — PDF metadata, acknowledgements, self-citations in third person,
   and an anonymized supplement with no author-identifying links, filenames, or movie
   titles.

## Do not infer

- Do not treat WACV like a single-deadline conference: the Revise-and-Resubmit round and
  the no-rebuttal Round 2 change the whole strategy — reopen the Dates page each cycle.
- Do not project the reported WACV 2027 dates or the Round 2 deadline onto later cycles,
  and reconfirm even the 2027 dates on the live page (they were read via search rendering).
- Do not assume supplementary size caps, camera-ready page allowance, or presentation
  obligations from sibling venues (CVPR/ICCV/ECCV publish similar-looking but distinct
  rules, and none of them use WACV's two-round model).
- Where official pages and OpenReview instructions disagree, treat the newest official
  posting as controlling and record the discrepancy alongside the decision it forced.
