# Official Source Map - WACV

Access date: 2026-07-09

This map records the official sources behind every WACV-specific fact in this pack. WACV
policy is re-set by each edition's organizing and program chairs, and the venue runs a
**two-round** review calendar that is unusual among the CVF conferences; before giving
submission-ready advice, reopen the current-cycle Call for Papers, Author Guidelines,
Dates page, Author/Reviewer Guides, and the two OpenReview groups (Round 1 and Round 2)
for the year you are targeting.

## Verified facts used in the skills

- WACV is the **IEEE/CVF Winter Conference on Applications of Computer Vision**,
  co-sponsored by the IEEE Computer Society and the Computer Vision Foundation, and is
  the applications-first winter member of the CVF's CVPR / ICCV / ECCV / WACV calendar.
- WACV runs a **two-round submission model**. In Round 1, each paper gets at least three
  reviews and the area chair issues one of three recommendations: **Accept**, **Revise
  and Resubmit** (not accepted, but potentially acceptable after revision, invited into
  Round 2), or **Reject**. Round 1 authors may file an **optional one-page rebuttal**.
  Round 2 evaluates the revised R1 papers plus brand-new Round 2 submissions and issues
  **Accept** or **Reject**; **Round 2 has no rebuttal**. (Sources 1, 3.)
- WACV has **two tracks** with distinct review criteria: an **Applications track**
  (evaluated on systems-level innovation, novelty of the application domain, and
  comparative assessment against real-world constraints) and an **Algorithms track**
  (evaluated on algorithmic novelty and quantified evaluation against current
  alternatives). Authors pick one primary track. (Source 1.)
- Format: **8 pages including figures and tables**, in the WACV style, with additional
  pages for cited references only. Submission is **double-blind** on **OpenReview**; every
  author needs a valid OpenReview profile by the deadline or the paper is desk-rejected;
  supplementary material and acknowledgements must be anonymized. (Sources 3, 6, 7.)
- Publication is dual: accepted WACV papers appear in **CVF open access**
  (`openaccess.thecvf.com`, accepted version plus watermark, no fee) and as the **version
  of record in IEEE Xplore**. Like every conference in this collection, WACV has **no
  standing editor-in-chief**; organizing and program chairs rotate per edition. (Source 9.)
- WACV 2026 was held **March 6-10, 2026 at the JW Marriott Starr Pass, Tucson, Arizona**.
  (Source 7.)
- WACV 2027 reported two-round timetable (via search rendering of source 2): **Round 1**
  paper deadline June 26, 2026 → reviews due July 24 → decisions August 7, 2026;
  **Round 2** paper + supplementary deadline August 28, 2026 → reviews due September 25 →
  decisions October 9, 2026; registration deadline November 2, 2026 (AoE). Reconfirm on
  the live page before quoting.
- Two-round scale, verified from the WACV 2025 conference account (source 8): of **1381
  Round 1 submissions**, ~36.5% were rejected, ~51.4% were invited to revise, and ~12.1%
  were accepted outright in Round 1 — the majority landed in the Revise-and-Resubmit
  middle, which is the mechanic these skills are built around.
