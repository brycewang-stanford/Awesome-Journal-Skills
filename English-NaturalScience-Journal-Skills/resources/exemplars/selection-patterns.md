# Venue-Selection Patterns

These patterns guide routing across natural-science venue families. They are not
paper exemplars.

## General Versus Specialist

- General-science journals need broad conceptual reach; more experiments alone
  do not create broad reach.
- Specialist journals can be stronger fits when the result changes a specific
  community's practice, mechanism, or method.

## Clinical Versus Mechanistic

- Clinical journals ask whether the result changes patient care, guidelines, or
  risk-benefit understanding.
- Translational and basic-science journals ask whether the mechanism, model
  system, or technology changes scientific understanding.
- Trial design, registration, endpoint hierarchy, safety, CONSORT-style
  reporting, and ethics approvals must be re-checked on live official sources.

## Chemistry, Materials, And Energy

- Chemistry flagships prioritize molecular mechanism, synthesis, reactivity, or
  conceptual chemical advance.
- Materials journals prioritize structure-property relationships, device
  performance, durability, and scalable characterization.
- Energy journals require application-relevant benchmarks, controls, and
  stability claims.

## Formal Sciences

- Mathematics venues need theorem-level novelty and proof completeness.
- Physics venues need physical insight, not only computation.
- CS/AI venues need algorithmic contribution, benchmarks, artifacts, and
  reviewer-facing evaluation.
