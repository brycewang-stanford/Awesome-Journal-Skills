# Source Basis and Official-Check Notes

This pack encodes journal-fit and house-style heuristics for 36 flagship
English-language **humanities** journals — the category the existing breadth
bundles leave thin. It is a positioning and re-framing aid, **not** a static
substitute for any journal's live submission guidelines or style guide.

For per-journal starting queries, use `official-source-map.md` in this directory.

## What this bundle covers (and why these venues)

- **History.** The American Historical Review, Past & Present, The Journal of Modern
  History, The English Historical Review, The William and Mary Quarterly, History and
  Theory, and the Journal of Social History.
- **Philosophy.** The Philosophical Review, Noûs, The Journal of Philosophy, Ethics,
  Philosophy & Public Affairs, Mind, and Analysis.
- **Literary studies.** PMLA, Critical Inquiry, New Literary History, Representations,
  Modern Language Quarterly, and Comparative Literature.
- **Classics.** The Classical Quarterly and The Journal of Roman Studies.
- **Art & architectural history.** The Art Bulletin, Art History, October, and The
  Burlington Magazine.
- **Religious studies.** The Journal of Religion, History of Religions, and the
  Harvard Theological Review.
- **Linguistics.** Language, Linguistic Inquiry, the Journal of Linguistics, and
  Natural Language & Linguistic Theory.
- **Musicology & aesthetics.** The Journal of the American Musicological Society,
  Music Theory Spectrum, and The Journal of Aesthetics and Art Criticism.

## Stable, public facts used for fit (no volatile numbers)

Only durable structural conventions inform fit — e.g. the field-defining role of these
journals; their learned-society or university-press affiliations (the American
Historical Association, the Modern Language Association, the Linguistic Society of
America, the American Musicological Society, the College Art Association, and the
Oxford / Cambridge / Chicago / Duke / Johns Hopkins university presses, among others);
the prevailing citation and style conventions of each field (Chicago notes-and-
bibliography in history and art history, MLA in literary studies, author-date or
discipline-specific styles in philosophy and linguistics); double-blind peer review as
the norm; and the image-rights / permissions burden in art-historical and
musicological work.

## What each profile deliberately does NOT assert

To stay correct over time, every profile **omits volatile facts** and defers them to
the official-submission checklist instead of stating a number:

- No impact factors, acceptance rates, ISSNs, exact word/figure limits, fees, or
  editor names.
- No fabricated citations, quotations, or claims about specific articles.
- No claims about a living scholar's positions or a specific work's reception.

## Operational rule for every journal skill

1. Before submitting, find the journal's **live official submission guidelines and
   style guide** ("<journal name> submission guidelines / instructions for authors /
   style sheet") and follow the current version.
2. Trust the learned society's or university press's own domain or submission system
   (Editorial Manager / ScholarOne / Manuscript Central / the press's portal) over any
   third-party or aggregator copy.
3. Re-check article types and word limits, the required citation/style guide (Chicago,
   MLA, or a discipline style), abstract and keyword requirements, anonymization for
   double-blind review, **image rights / reproduction permissions and figure
   specifications**, language and translation policy for non-English sources, archival
   and primary-source citation form, and any open-access or licensing terms.
4. For society or press families (AHA, MLA, LSA, AMS, CAA, OUP, CUP, Chicago, Duke,
   JHU), check both the journal-specific page and the family/style-guide page.
5. If the live official instructions conflict with a skill, the official instructions
   win.
