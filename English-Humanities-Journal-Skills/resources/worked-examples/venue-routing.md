# Worked Routing Examples

These examples are fictional diagnostic scenarios for venue routing. They do not
describe real submissions or publication facts.

## Case 1: History Generalist Or Period/Area Specialist?

Manuscript: an archive-based study of a single early-modern community with a
broader argument about social change.

Route:
- Test `the-american-historical-review` only if the argument speaks across
  fields and periods, not because the archive is rich.
- Route to `past-and-present` when the contribution is social/cultural and
  reframes how a problem is understood.
- Route to `the-journal-of-modern-history` or `the-english-historical-review`
  when period and national field define the real reviewer community, and to
  `the-william-and-mary-quarterly` for early-America scope.
- Route to `history-and-theory` only when the claim is meta-level about method,
  historiography, or what counts as explanation.

## Case 2: Philosophy Flagship, Specialist, Or Short Note?

Manuscript: an analytic argument with a normative payoff and a compact result.

Route:
- Test `the-philosophical-review`, `nous`, or `the-journal-of-philosophy` when
  the argument advances core analytic debate broadly.
- Route to `ethics` or `philosophy-and-public-affairs` when the contribution is
  moral, political, or public-affairs theory; route to `mind` for language,
  mind, or logic emphasis.
- Route a short, self-contained point to `analysis` rather than padding it into
  a flagship-length article.

## Case 3: Literary Studies Idiom And Field

Manuscript: a reading of texts that mounts a theoretical intervention.

Route:
- Route to `pmla` for a broad MLA audience; to `critical-inquiry` for high
  theory; to `new-literary-history` for theory-of-literature stakes.
- Route to `representations` for new-historicist framing, to
  `modern-language-quarterly` for literary history, and to
  `comparative-literature` for world/comparative scope.
- Match the theoretical idiom and period to the journal's conversation; a strong
  reading aimed at the wrong idiom reads as off-key.

## Case 4: Art History Generalist Or Mode-Specialist?

Manuscript: an attribution and interpretation of a single object.

Route:
- Route to `the-art-bulletin` for a generalist intervention; to `art-history`
  when theory drives the claim; to `october` for contemporary/avant-garde
  stakes; to `the-burlington-magazine` for connoisseurship and attribution.
- Confirm image and example permissions early, since reproduction and figure
  clearances are substantive to the submission, not an afterthought.
