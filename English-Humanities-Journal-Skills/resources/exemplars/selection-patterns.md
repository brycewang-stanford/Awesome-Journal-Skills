# Venue-Selection Patterns

These patterns guide routing across humanities venue families. They are not paper
exemplars.

## Generalist Flagship Versus Period/Area Specialist

- Generalist flagships need an argument that travels across periods and fields;
  command of the archive alone does not create that reach.
- Period and area specialists are stronger fits when the real reviewer community
  is defined by a national field, era, or sub-discipline.
- In history, weigh `the-american-historical-review` against `past-and-present`,
  `the-journal-of-modern-history`, `the-english-historical-review`,
  `the-william-and-mary-quarterly`, and the meta-level `history-and-theory`.

## Argument And Primary-Source Command Over Data Volume

- Humanities venues reward the strength of the argument and command of primary
  sources, not the quantity of material assembled.
- Match the theoretical idiom and period/field to the journal's conversation:
  `critical-inquiry` and `new-literary-history` expect theory stakes, while
  `modern-language-quarterly` expects literary-history rigor.
- In philosophy, fit the contribution's scope to the venue: broad analytic
  argument to `the-philosophical-review`, `nous`, or `the-journal-of-philosophy`;
  domain argument to `ethics`, `philosophy-and-public-affairs`, or `mind`; a
  short note to `analysis`.

## Classics, Religion, And Linguistics Sub-Fields

- Classics splits philology (`the-classical-quarterly`) from history and
  archaeology (`the-journal-of-roman-studies`); route by what carries the claim.
- Religion routes by approach across `the-journal-of-religion`,
  `history-of-religions`, and `harvard-theological-review`.
- Linguistics matches framework: disciplinary flagship `language`, generative
  `linguistic-inquiry`, framework-neutral `journal-of-linguistics`, and
  `natural-language-and-linguistic-theory` for formal syntactic theory.

## House Style And Field Practice As Substantive

- House style is substantive: Chicago versus MLA versus discipline style,
  double-blind anonymization, and complete referencing are conditions of fit.
- Art history and musicology require image and example permissions; route
  attribution work to venues like `the-burlington-magazine` and analysis to
  `music-theory-spectrum` versus historical work in
  `journal-of-the-american-musicological-society`.
- Aesthetics as philosophy of art routes to
  `the-journal-of-aesthetics-and-art-criticism`; clear the theoretical idiom
  before choosing the venue.
