# Official Source Map — Humanities journals

Use these as **first-stop** source families, then drill down to the specific
journal's current submission guidelines and style guide. Always prefer the learned
society's or university press's own domain or submission system over any aggregator
copy. Access notes reflect checks made while building this bundle (2026-06);
re-verify the live page before any submission-ready advice.

## Cross-cutting humanities anchors

- **Citation/style**: Chicago notes-and-bibliography (history, art history, religion),
  MLA (literary studies), or a discipline-specific style (philosophy, linguistics);
  use the journal's current style sheet.
- **Double-blind review**: anonymize the manuscript (remove self-identifying citations
  and acknowledgements) where the journal requires it.
- **Image rights / permissions**: art-historical, musicological, and material-culture
  submissions must secure reproduction permissions and meet figure specifications;
  this is often the rate-limiting step.
- **Primary sources / languages**: archival citation form, original-language quotation
  and translation policy.

## Operational rule

1. Open the journal-specific official page above; do not infer rules from a sibling
   title or assume the press is unchanged.
2. Re-check article type and word limit, style guide, anonymization, image permissions,
   and any open-access terms.
3. If the live official instructions conflict with a skill, the official instructions
   win.
