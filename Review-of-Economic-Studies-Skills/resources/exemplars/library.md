## How to use this library

Pick the row whose **method × question** is closest to yours, then study how that paper makes **one
clean original contribution** read well to **both a theorist and an applied economist** — the REStud bar
(see [`../../skills/restud-topic-selection/SKILL.md`](../../skills/restud-topic-selection/SKILL.md) and
[`../../skills/restud-writing-style/SKILL.md`](../../skills/restud-writing-style/SKILL.md)). REStud
weights theory and applied work equally, so the list spans pure theory, econometric method, and
empirical/field work. For each, ask the self-check before claiming your paper is "REStud-shaped."

## By method

### Mechanism-design theory that reframes a policy problem (microeconomic theory)

- **Mirrlees, "An Exploration in the Theory of Optimum Income Taxation," REStud 1971, 38(2):175–208.**
  Verified: `academic.oup.com/restud/article-abstract/38/2/175/1527903`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a single tractable framework turns "how much should the top rate be?" into
    a solvable trade-off between equality and efficiency under private information — the prototypical
    REStud "new model that reorganizes a whole literature." Optimal-taxation theory was founded here.
  - **Self-check:** does your model deliver an economic insight others did not have, statable cleanly,
    rather than add machinery to a known result?

### Contract / incentive theory under asymmetric information (microeconomic theory)

- **Stiglitz, "Incentives and Risk Sharing in Sharecropping," REStud 1974, 41(2):219–255.**
  Verified: `academic.oup.com/restud/article-abstract/41/2/219/1582329`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** explains a centuries-old institution (sharecropping) as the equilibrium
    compromise between risk-sharing and incentives — a small model whose lesson travels to labor, credit,
    and the theory of the firm. See [`../../skills/restud-theory-model/SKILL.md`](../../skills/restud-theory-model/SKILL.md).
  - **Self-check:** can your mechanism explain a real institution a non-specialist already recognizes,
    in one paragraph?

### Dynamic-panel econometric method (methodology)

- **Arellano & Bond, "Some Tests of Specification for Panel Data: Monte Carlo Evidence and an Application
  to Employment Equations," REStud 1991, 58(2):277–297.**
  Verified: `academic.oup.com/restud/article-abstract/58/2/277/1563354`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a GMM estimator + specification tests for dynamic panels, demonstrated on
    Monte Carlo *and* real employment data — REStud publishes method when it becomes a field-wide tool
    (this is the "Arellano–Bond estimator"). Pairs with the panel/IV machinery in [`../code/`](../code/).
  - **Self-check:** is your method a general tool others will reuse, validated by simulation and a real
    application, not a one-off trick for one dataset?

### Growth/inequality theory with a sharp macro implication (macroeconomic theory)

- **Galor & Zeira, "Income Distribution and Macroeconomics," REStud 1993, 60(1):35–52.**
  Verified: `academic.oup.com/restud/article-abstract/60/1/35/1576085`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** with credit-market imperfections and indivisible human-capital investment,
    the *initial wealth distribution* moves long-run output — a clean model that made distribution a
    first-order macro variable, not a second-order concern.
  - **Self-check:** does your model turn a variable the field treated as a footnote into the driver of an
    aggregate the whole field cares about?

### Unified theory + empirics on long-run development (growth / economic history)

- **Galor, Moav & Vollrath, "Inequality in Landownership, the Emergence of Human-Capital Promoting
  Institutions, and the Great Divergence," REStud 2009, 76(1):143–179.**
  Verified: `academic.oup.com/restud/article-abstract/76/1/143/1574777`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a micro-founded model predicts that land-inequality delayed public
    schooling and thus industrialization, and the prediction is taken to early-20th-century U.S. data —
    theory and applied evidence carried in one paper, the REStud balance made literal.
  - **Self-check:** does your theory make a falsifiable prediction you then confront with data, rather
    than calibrate to fit?

### Randomized field experiment with a mechanism (development / applied micro)

- **Feigenberg, Field & Pande, "The Economic Returns to Social Interaction: Experimental Evidence from
  Microfinance," REStud 2013, 80(4):1459–1483.**
  Verified: `academic.oup.com/restud/article/80/4/1459/1582821`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** randomizing microfinance group meeting *frequency* isolates social
    interaction as a causal channel for lower default — a design where the randomization cleanly
    identifies a mechanism, not just an average effect. See
    [`../../skills/restud-identification/SKILL.md`](../../skills/restud-identification/SKILL.md).
  - **Self-check:** does your experiment manipulate the *mechanism* itself, so the result identifies
    *why*, not only *whether*?
