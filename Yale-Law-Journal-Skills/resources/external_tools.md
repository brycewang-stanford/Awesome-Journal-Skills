# External Tools — Legal Research & Citation (YLJ)

Tools an agent should reach for when preparing a Yale Law Journal piece. Legal scholarship's "data" is
**authority**: cases, statutes, regulations, legislative history, and the secondary literature. The
core jobs are (1) the **preemption sweep**, (2) **building Bluebook cites**, and (3) the **cite-check /
source-pull**. Access to paywalled databases varies by institution; nothing here is YLJ-endorsed.

## Preemption & secondary-literature search

| Tool | Use for | Notes |
|------|---------|-------|
| **SSRN — Legal Scholarship Network** | Unpublished/forthcoming law-review pieces — the **highest** preemption risk | Free; follow authors in your subfield; search the *claim*, not just the topic |
| **Westlaw — Secondary Sources / Law Reviews & Journals** | Published articles; KeyCite to trace who cites a near-miss | Terms-and-connectors; institutional login |
| **HeinOnline — Law Journal Library** | Full-text historical coverage older databases miss | Phrase search across the full back-run |
| **Lexis+ — Law Reviews & Journals** | Cross-check Westlaw coverage; Shepard's for citing references | Institutional login |
| **Google Scholar** | Cross-disciplinary work, books, recent preprints; "cited by" on the nearest neighbor | Free; good for catching non-law sources |

See [`../skills/ylj-preemption-check/SKILL.md`](../skills/ylj-preemption-check/SKILL.md) for the four-source sweep protocol.

## Primary authority (build and verify cites)

| Source | Use for | Notes |
|--------|---------|-------|
| **Westlaw / Lexis+** | Cases, statutes, regulations with pinpoint pagination; KeyCite/Shepard's for validity | Confirm a case is still good law before relying on it |
| **CourtListener / RECAP (Free Law Project)** | Free case access, dockets, oral arguments | Good for primary verification when paywalls block |
| **Congress.gov** | Federal statutes, bills, legislative history | Authoritative for U.S. Code and legislative materials |
| **govinfo.gov (GPO)** | U.S.C., C.F.R., Federal Register, official PDFs | Authoritative for regulations and codified text |
| **Supreme Court / U.S. Courts sites** | Slip opinions, official reporters | Cite the official reporter where available |

## Citation form & cite-checking (Bluebook)

| Tool | Use for | Notes |
|------|---------|-------|
| **The Bluebook: A Uniform System of Citation** | The controlling citation manual for YLJ | YLJ supplements it with its own citation requirements |
| **KeyCite (Westlaw) / Shepard's (Lexis)** | Confirm a case/statute is still good law; find subsequent history | Run before relying on any authority |
| **Reference managers (Zotero, with a legal/Bluebook style)** | Organize sources; draft cites for adaptation | Always hand-conform to Bluebook — auto-styles drift |
| **Word/LaTeX footnote tooling** | Manage the heavy footnote apparatus, short forms, cross-refs | Re-audit `id.`/`supra` chains after every reorganization |

See [`../skills/ylj-sources-and-bluebook/SKILL.md`](../skills/ylj-sources-and-bluebook/SKILL.md) and [`../skills/ylj-footnotes-and-cite-check/SKILL.md`](../skills/ylj-footnotes-and-cite-check/SKILL.md).

## Submission & placement

| Tool | Use for | Notes |
|------|---------|-------|
| **YLJ author submission portal** | Submitting and expediting **to YLJ** | YLJ does **NOT** use Scholastica/ExpressO — use its own system (检索于 2026-06；以官网为准) |
| **Scholastica** | Multi-submitting to the rest of the law-review market | The market standard for *other* reviews; not for YLJ |
| **ExpressO** | Alternative multi-submission service for other reviews | Same caveat — not the YLJ channel |
