# Official Source Map - ECAI

Access date: 2026-07-09

This map records the official and cross-check sources used for ECAI-specific facts in this
pack. ECAI is the **European Conference on Artificial Intelligence**, the flagship general-AI
conference of **EurAI (the European Association for Artificial Intelligence)** and its member
national societies. Its calendar, page budget, review mechanics, and *even its publisher* are
decided per edition — and the current edition is unusual: **ECAI 2026 is being run as the joint
IJCAI-ECAI 2026 in Bremen**, so this cycle's proceedings, format, and process follow IJCAI, not
ECAI's usual IOS Press book series. Reopen the current-cycle call, the Important Dates page, and
the publisher page before giving submission-ready advice.

## Access method note

Direct fetches of the conference and publisher domains (`2026.ijcai.org`, `ecai2024.eu`,
`ebooks.iospress.nl`, `eurai.org`) were treated as **egress-blocked in the build sandbox**
(CONNECT returns 403, the same block seen when verifying other conference/publisher venues on
2026-07-09). Official pages were therefore read through **search-engine renderings of the exact
URLs** and cross-checked across ≥2 independent surfaces: the IJCAI-ECAI 2026 call pages, the
`ecai2024.eu`/`ecai2023.eu` calls, the IOS Press FAIA volume pages, `researchr.org`, `eurai.org`,
AIhub award posts, and dblp. Facts that could not be pinned to ≥2 renderings are labeled **待核实**.
Beware two name traps: **FAIA** here is IOS Press's *Frontiers in Artificial Intelligence and
Applications* book series (ECAI's usual proceedings) — not the *Federated AI Meeting (FAIM)*; and
the old **ECCAI** acronym is the former name of what is now **EurAI** (renamed 2015).

## Verified facts used in skills

### The live cycle: ECAI 2026 is the joint IJCAI-ECAI 2026

- **ECAI 2026 is not a standalone ECAI.** It is being held as **IJCAI-ECAI 2026**, the 35th
  International Joint Conference on Artificial Intelligence held jointly with ECAI under EurAI, in
  **Bremen, Germany, 15-21 August 2026** (sources 1-3, 12). This repeats the IJCAI-ECAI-18
  (Stockholm) precedent of merging when IJCAI is hosted in Europe.
- **Two-deadline flow (2026):** abstract **12 January 2026**, full paper **19 January 2026**
  (23:59 AoE); **summary-reject** notification **4 March 2026**; **author response** window
  **7-10 April 2026**; final notification **29 April 2026** (source 2).
- **Page budget (2026):** **7 pages** for the body plus **2 pages** for references only, with an
  optional **ethics statement** that may sit in the body or the reference pages (source 1). This
  is the IJCAI budget, not ECAI's usual 7+1.
- **Review is double-blind**, with a **summary-reject** first phase and a **single author-response
  (rebuttal)** window before the final decision (sources 1-2).
- **Author submission limit:** no more than **8 submissions** per author to the main track
  (source 1).
- **Proceedings (2026):** published by **IJCAI** as its own open-access, online-only proceedings
  (IJCAI has self-published since 2017), **not** the IOS Press FAIA series this cycle. The FAIA
  book-series identity returns for the next standalone ECAI (**待核实** for exactly which future
  edition and publisher arrangement).
- **Generative-AI policy (2026):** LLMs do not qualify for authorship; authors are fully
  responsible for all content; LLM-generated text used as part of methodology/analysis must be
  documented (source 1).

### The standing ECAI identity (standalone editions, e.g. 2023/2024)

- **Publisher = IOS Press FAIA.** ECAI's proceedings are volumes of **Frontiers in Artificial
  Intelligence and Applications (FAIA)**, open access: **ECAI 2024 = FAIA Vol. 392**, ECAI 2023 =
  FAIA Vol. 372 (sources 6-7). Prepared with the **ECAI LaTeX template (`ecai.cls`)** — not
  `acmart`, not `IEEEtran`, not the AAAI/NeurIPS styles.
- **Two-deadline abstract-then-paper flow.** ECAI 2024 required **abstract/paper registration one
  week before** the PDF: registration **19 Apr 2024**, paper **25 Apr 2024** (source 4). The
  registered abstract (~100-300 words, plain text) drives reviewer bidding.
- **Page budget (standalone):** **7 pages** of body plus **up to 1 page** of references
  (source 4). PAIS uses the same 7+1 (source 5).
- **PAIS co-location.** The **Conference on Prestigious Applications of Intelligent Systems**
  (PAIS 2024 = 13th; PAIS 2023 = 12th) runs alongside ECAI as its deployed-application track,
  its papers appearing in the same FAIA volume (sources 5-6).
- **EurAI governance.** ECAI is owned by **EurAI** (the European Association for Artificial
  Intelligence, formerly ECCAI), organised with a national member society each edition; the
  **EurAI Dissertation Award** talk is a fixture (sources 8-9).

### Scale (context, not permanent)

- ECAI 2023 (Kraków, 26th): a record **1896 submissions**, **~23%** acceptance (source 11 and
  ECAI 2023 pages). ECAI 2024 (Santiago de Compostela, 27th): **544/2344 main-track** accepted
  (**~23%**); PAIS 2024 **35/71** (**~49%**). Treat as edition-specific, not a target.

## No editor-in-chief; open access, no APC

Like every conference in this collection, ECAI has **no standing editor-in-chief**; the rotating
analogue is the per-edition Conference/Program Chairs appointed by EurAI and the host society (for
2026, the joint IJCAI-ECAI organisation with EurAI, DFKI, U Bremen Research Alliance, and the
German Informatics Society **GI**). FAIA proceedings are **open access with no article-processing
charge**; the cost model is conference registration, and at least one author presents.
