# External Tools - ECAI

Access date: 2026-07-09

Use these surfaces after reopening the current ECAI cycle pages. ECAI mechanics — publisher
(IOS Press FAIA vs IJCAI proceedings), template (`ecai.cls` vs `ijcai.sty`), page budget, and the
two-deadline calendar — are decided per edition, and **the current edition is the joint IJCAI-ECAI
2026**, which follows IJCAI rather than the standing ECAI/FAIA conventions. The conference and
publisher domains may block direct fetches (read via search renderings and cross-check dblp and
the FAIA volume pages). Do not confuse the FAIA book series with the Federated AI Meeting (FAIM),
and note that **ECCAI** is the old name of **EurAI**.

## Author-side checks

- The **abstract/registration** step that precedes the paper PDF (IJCAI-ECAI 2026: abstract
  12 Jan, paper 19 Jan 2026; standalone ECAI: registration one week before the PDF).
- **Template compliance:** `ijcai.sty` for the 2026 joint edition (7 pages body + 2 reference
  pages, optional ethics statement); `ecai.cls` for a standalone ECAI (7 pages + 1 reference page).
- **Double-blind sweep:** no author names/affiliations, anonymized self-citations, no
  identity-revealing repository or acknowledgement text.
- **Per-author submission cap** (IJCAI-ECAI 2026: 8) and topic/keyword selection that routes your
  paper to the right area chair and reviewer pool.
- **Ethics statement** decision (recommended for sensitive data/tasks) and generative-AI
  disclosure per the current policy.
- **PAIS routing** for a deployed-application paper (standalone editions), which has its own
  call and emphasis on real-world impact.

## Do not infer

- Do not assume the publisher: a **joint IJCAI-ECAI** year publishes with **IJCAI** (open-access,
  self-published, `ijcai.sty`); a **standalone ECAI** publishes in **IOS Press FAIA** (`ecai.cls`).
  Confirm which regime the target edition is in before touching a template.
- Do not assume the page budget (7+1 standalone vs 7+2 in 2026) or that PAIS runs this cycle.
- Do not carry a prior edition's dates, summary-reject/rebuttal timing, or supplementary policy
  forward.
- If pages disagree, treat the newest official call and Important Dates page (or a direct chair
  announcement) as controlling, and record the conflict.
