# Exemplar Library — *Criminology*

> **Verified via web search, access date 2026-06-08.** Every paper below was confirmed on Wiley
> Online Library under *Criminology* (the flagship journal of the American Society of Criminology;
> ISSN/DOI stem `1745-9125`). Papers outside *Criminology* were omitted and are listed at the bottom
> as sibling-journal guardrails. This is a positioning aid —
> read the originals; do not reproduce their numbers.
>
> **Sibling-confusion guard.** *Criminology* is NOT: *Journal of Quantitative Criminology* (JQC),
> *Justice Quarterly*, *Criminology & Public Policy* (CPP, the ASC's policy sister journal),
> *Journal of Research in Crime and Delinquency* (JRCD). Verify the Wiley `1745-9125` DOI before citing.

## How to use

- **Locating your contribution.** Place your study against the live debate the closest of these joins
  (strain vs. control vs. life-course; structure vs. process in neighborhoods), per
  `cr-literature-positioning`.
- **Matching method to claim.** Criminology rewards designs that take *selection* and *neighborhood
  endogeneity* seriously — the vendored `code/` (multilevel, panel FE, IV/RDD) supports exactly the
  identification moves these exemplars model.
- **Address-the-self-control/strain debates** explicitly; a Criminology referee will name the theory
  your result confirms or overturns.
