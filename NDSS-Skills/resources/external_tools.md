## Author-side passes to run against the live pages

1. **Cycle pass** — which cycle are you actually in? Confirm the paper deadline, early-reject
   date, rebuttal window, and final notification for *your* cycle, all in AoE (UTC-12).
2. **Format pass** — current NDSS template version; 13-page budget excluding the Ethics
   Considerations section, references, and appendices; no template edits.
3. **Anonymity pass** — no names/affiliations, third-person self-citation, scrubbed PDF
   metadata, anonymized artifact links, no acknowledgements that identify a group.
4. **Ethics pass** — does the work touch live systems, users, or unpatched vulnerabilities?
   If yes: disclosure plan, Menlo-style harm analysis, and an Ethics Considerations section;
   remember IRB paperwork alone does not satisfy the ERB.
5. **Cap pass** — count every submission each coauthor is on this cycle against the
   six-per-author limit, and check nothing overlaps a paper rejected in the earlier cycle.
6. **Post-acceptance pass** — camera-ready date (January 6, 2027 for the 2027 edition),
   artifact-evaluation opt-in timing, badge requirements, Seoul registration and travel.

## Do not infer

- Do not project 2027 dates onto 2028, and do not assume the host city: NDSS sat in San
  Diego every February through 2026 and then moved to Seoul in March for 2027.
- Do not assume the fall cycle mirrors the summer cycle in anything but shape; verify its
  own dates and its own HotCRP site.
- Do not assume Major Revision terms, the per-author cap, the ethics-section rules, or the
  badge set carry over between editions.
- When two official pages disagree, the newer CFP or a direct chairs' announcement controls;
  write the discrepancy down next to the affected advice.
