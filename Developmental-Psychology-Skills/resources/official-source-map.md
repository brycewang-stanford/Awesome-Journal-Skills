## Official journal & submission pages (APA)

- **https://www.apa.org/pubs/journals/dev/**
  — Developmental Psychology journal home (APA). Verifies: **publisher** American Psychological
  Association; **scope** — "articles that significantly advance knowledge and theory about development
  across the life span," with seminal empirical contributions, welcoming laboratory experiments and other
  rigorous methods (ethnographies, field research, secondary analyses of large datasets); **ISSN**
  0012-1649 (print), **eISSN** 1939-0599; **submission portal** `editorialmanager.com/dvl`; links to APA
  JARS and Transparency and Openness Promotion (TOP) resources.
- **https://www.apa.org/pubs/journals/dev/?tab=4** (Submission Guidelines tab)
  — Developmental Psychology Instructions to Authors (APA). Verifies (检索于 2026-06；以官网为准):
  **manuscript length tiers** — roughly a maximum of **~4,500 words for a brief report**, **~10,500 words
  for a larger research report**, and **~15,000 words for a report containing multiple studies or
  comprehensive longitudinal studies**; total length **includes the cover page, abstract, main text,
  references, tables, and figures**; a **Public Significance Statement** of **2–3 brief sentences
  (~30–70 words)** on the abstract/keywords page, written for a lay audience; **masked review** available
  (authors submit a masked copy via stable link or supplemental material); **APA 7th-edition** style;
  **APA Style Journal Article Reporting Standards (JARS-Quant, JARS-Qual, and/or MARS)** required;
  empirical work expected to meet at least the "requirement" level of research planning and reporting;
  **Transparency and Openness Promotion (TOP)** guidelines (data/materials transparency, preregistration,
  sample-size justification).
- **https://apastyle.apa.org/jars** — APA Journal Article Reporting Standards (JARS). Verifies: the
  JARS-Quant / JARS-Qual / MARS reporting standards APA journals apply.
- **https://www.apa.org/pubs/journals/resources/open-science** — APA open-science / TOP resources.
  Verifies: APA's adoption of the Transparency and Openness Promotion guidelines and data-sharing
  expectations referenced by the journal.
- **https://www.editorialmanager.com/dvl/default.aspx** — Editorial Manager submission portal for
  Developmental Psychology (login/landing page).

## Sibling-journal boundary (for fit/positioning)

- **Child Development** (Wiley, for the **Society for Research in Child Development, SRCD**) — society
  flagship centered on childhood; not the broad APA lifespan venue.
- **Developmental Science** (Wiley) — faster, often briefer developmental venue, strong in infant/cognitive
  mechanism work.
- **Journal of Personality and Social Psychology (JPSP)** (APA) — personality/social processes, not a
  developmental-change journal.
  Developmental Psychology is the **broad APA lifespan-development** journal; positioning and exemplar
  attribution must respect these boundaries.
