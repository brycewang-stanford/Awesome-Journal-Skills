# Developmental Psychology Exemplars Library (method × developmental domain)

> **Verified via web search, access date 2026-06.** Every paper below was checked to confirm it actually
> appeared in **_Developmental Psychology_** (American Psychological Association; journal abbreviation
> *Dev Psychol*; DOI prefix `10.1037/dev...` for recent articles, `10.1037/0012-1649...` for older ones),
> with year, volume(issue):pages. Papers that could not be cleanly verified as _Developmental Psychology_ —
> including ones often **confused** with it — were **omitted** and documented below. It is deliberately a
> short, clean list (5 verified) rather than a long, uncertain one.
>
> **Sibling-confusion guard.** _Developmental Psychology_ (APA) is **not** any of: _Child Development_
> (Wiley, for the **Society for Research in Child Development**), _Developmental Science_ (Wiley),
> _Developmental Review_, _Journal of Personality and Social Psychology_ (JPSP), or _Psychological Science_.
> Several famous "development" findings live in _Science_, _Nature_, or _Child Development_ — not here.
> Each row below was checked against this list.
>
> **Use principle (zero hallucination):** this file gives **design positioning only**. It does not
> reproduce or invent effect sizes, sample sizes, or specific findings — read the original on APA PsycNET /
> the authors' pages before citing any number. For venue policy, scope, and format facts, see
> [`../official-source-map.md`](../official-source-map.md).

## How to use this library

Pick the row whose **method × developmental domain** is closest to yours, then study how that paper makes a
claim about **development across the life span** — a trajectory, an age contrast, or a mechanism of change —
in a way that **advances theory**, the _Developmental Psychology_ bar (see
[`../../skills/devpsych-topic-selection/SKILL.md`](../../skills/devpsych-topic-selection/SKILL.md) and
[`../../skills/devpsych-study-design/SKILL.md`](../../skills/devpsych-study-design/SKILL.md)). For each,
ask the self-check before claiming your paper is "_Developmental Psychology_-shaped." Note that
**experiments, longitudinal/cohort designs, and large-panel secondary analyses** dominate this venue.

## By method × developmental domain

### Foundational typology / observational study (parenting × early childhood)

- **Baumrind, "Current Patterns of Parental Authority," _Developmental Psychology_ 1971, 4(1, Pt.2):1–103
  (Monograph).** Verified: APA record, DOI 10.1037/h0030372.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** an observational study that built the enduring authoritative/authoritarian/
    permissive **typology** of parenting and tied it to preschoolers' competence — a foundational
    theory-advancing contribution about how a developmental context shapes the child.
  - **Self-check:** does your contribution build or sharpen a developmental construct, not just report a
    correlation in one age group?

### Longitudinal prediction / delay-of-gratification (preschool → adolescence trajectory)

- **Shoda, Mischel & Peake, "Predicting Adolescent Cognitive and Self-Regulatory Competencies From
  Preschool Delay of Gratification: Identifying Diagnostic Conditions," _Developmental Psychology_ 1990,
  26(6):978–986.** Verified: DOI 10.1037/0012-1649.26.6.978.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a longitudinal design links a preschool self-regulation measure to
    adolescent outcomes *and* identifies the **conditions** under which the prediction holds — a model of
    a developmental claim that specifies *when*, not just *that*, an early measure forecasts later change.
  - **Self-check:** does your longitudinal link specify the conditions/mechanism, or only an unconditional
    correlation across time?

### Large-panel secondary analysis / mediation (attention as a developmental mechanism)

- **NICHD Early Child Care Research Network, "Do Children's Attention Processes Mediate the Link Between
  Family Predictors and School Readiness?" _Developmental Psychology_ 2003, 39(3):581–593.** Verified:
  DOI 10.1037/0012-1649.39.3.581.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a large longitudinal panel is used to test a **mediation** pathway —
    family predictors → attention → school readiness — exactly the change-mechanism logic
    [`devpsych-data-analysis`](../../skills/devpsych-data-analysis/SKILL.md) trains, with the rigor of a
    documented multi-site cohort.
  - **Self-check:** does your secondary analysis test a developmental *mechanism*, with provenance and a
    principled missing-data plan, rather than mining a panel for associations?

### Longitudinal parenting × self-regulation (effortful-control development)

- **Eisenberg, Vidmar, Spinrad, Eggum, Edwards, Gaertner & Kupfer, "Mothers' Teaching Strategies and
  Children's Effortful Control: A Longitudinal Study," _Developmental Psychology_ 2010, 46(5):1294–1308.**
  Verified: DOI 10.1037/a0020376.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a multi-wave study connects parenting to the **development of effortful
    control**, modeling within-person change and the role of a parenting input — the prototypical
    trajectory-plus-mechanism contribution this venue rewards.
  - **Self-check:** does your study model *change* in the construct over time and test a developmental
    driver of that change, not just a single-wave parenting correlate?

### Multi-generation / life-course longitudinal (cumulative advantage across generations)

- **Burger & Mortimer, "The Long Arm of Adolescent Educational Expectations: A Three-Generation
  Longitudinal Study of the Persistence of Family Advantage," _Developmental Psychology_ 2021** (verified
  via DOI 10.1037/dev0001238; confirm exact volume(issue):pages on APA PsycNET).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a life-course, multi-generation longitudinal design tracks how family
    advantage persists across generations — a lifespan/intergenerational developmental claim that uses
    long-horizon data to test transmission, the broad end of this journal's remit.
  - **Self-check:** does your design exploit a long time horizon (or generations) to make a developmental
    transmission/persistence claim, with cohort and attrition addressed?

## Omitted for lack of _Developmental Psychology_ placement (do not attribute without re-checking)

To keep the list zero-hallucination, these frequently-cited candidates were **excluded** after web
checking — several are classic sibling-journal confusions:

- **Ainsworth Strange Situation work** — the canonical reports are in **_Child Development_** and
  Ainsworth's books, **not** _Developmental Psychology_.
- **Hamlin, Wynn & Bloom, "Social Evaluation by Preverbal Infants" (2007)** — **_Nature_**.
- **Mischel & Ebbesen original marshmallow studies (1970)** — **_JPSP_** (the 1990 follow-up listed above
  is the _Developmental Psychology_ paper; do not conflate them).
- **Carlson & Meltzoff executive-function / bilingualism studies** — several are in **_Developmental
  Science_**, not _Developmental Psychology_; check each.
- **Diamond & colleagues' executive-function training reports** — several appear in **_Science_** or other
  venues; verify before attributing.
- **Dweck implicit-theories/mindset experiments** — many are in **_Child Development_**, **_JPSP_**, or
  **_Psychological Science_**; confirm the venue per paper.

Before adding any new paper to this library, confirm it on APA PsycNET (journal *Developmental Psychology*,
DOI prefix `10.1037/dev...` or `10.1037/0012-1649...`, with volume/issue/pages) and update the access-date
header. When in doubt, omit. If web verification is unavailable, mark new rows **待核实 / TO VERIFY** and
attach **no** journal attribution until checked.
