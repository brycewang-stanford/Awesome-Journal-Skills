# Molecular Cell Exemplars Library (method × topic)

> **How to read this file (zero-hallucination policy).** This library gives **design positioning only** —
> what a *Molecular Cell*-shaped mechanistic story looks like by method and topic. It **does not** reproduce
> or invent experimental numbers, effect sizes, volumes, pages, or DOIs. The named works are landmark
> **programs/findings** that are widely known to exist; several classic mechanism papers people associate
> with "Molecular Cell" actually ran in a **sibling venue**. **Before citing any specific paper, confirm on
> PubMed and the publisher record that the journal field literally reads *Molecular Cell*** — capture the
> year, volume(issue):pages, and DOI yourself.
>
> **Sibling-confusion guard (read this).** *Molecular Cell* is **not** *Cell*, **not** *Nature*/*Science*,
> and **not** *Nature Structural & Molecular Biology (NSMB)*, *EMBO J*, *Genes & Development*, *Nucleic
> Acids Research*, or *Cell Reports*. Many landmark structural and mechanistic studies (e.g., several RNA
> Pol II and spliceosome cryo-EM structures, and the original CRISPR/Cas9 biochemistry) appeared in
> *Science*/*Nature*/*NSMB*, **not** *Molecular Cell*. Use those only after verifying the venue.

---

## How to use this library

Pick the row whose **method × question** is closest to yours, then study how that *kind* of paper makes a
*deep, orthogonally validated* mechanism carry a *physiological* lesson — the Molecular Cell bar (see
[`../../skills/molcell-fit/SKILL.md`](../../skills/molcell-fit/SKILL.md) and
[`../../skills/molcell-framing/SKILL.md`](../../skills/molcell-framing/SKILL.md)). For each, ask the
self-check question before claiming your paper is "Molecular Cell-shaped."

## By method × topic (Molecular Cell home territory)

### 1. Transcription mechanism — RNA polymerase and general factors
- **Exemplar type:** structure + biochemistry that defines a **step** in initiation, elongation, or
  termination (e.g., how a general factor loads Pol II, how the CTD is read). Verify venue: many Pol II
  structures ran in *Science*/*Nature*; Molecular Cell publishes the mechanistic follow-through.
- **Self-check:** does your work show a molecular *step* (a contact, a conformational change), backed by
  reconstitution or mutants — not just an occupancy correlation?

### 2. Chromatin & epigenetics — remodelers, modifiers, nucleosome dynamics
- **Exemplar type:** a chromatin remodeler or histone-modifying enzyme whose **catalytic mechanism** or
  substrate selectivity is worked out with structure + biochemistry + a separation-of-function allele in
  cells.
- **Self-check:** do you tie the enzymatic mechanism to a chromatin/transcription outcome with a mutant
  that breaks only the catalytic step?

### 3. RNA biology — splicing, RNA modification, decay, RNP assembly
- **Exemplar type:** the mechanism by which an RNP recognizes, modifies, or degrades a substrate, shown by
  reconstitution and orthogonal in-cell readouts (e.g., a decay factor's substrate hand-off).
- **Self-check:** is the RNA–protein recognition demonstrated biochemically and validated in cells, not
  inferred from a CLIP correlation alone?

### 4. DNA replication — replisome, helicase, clamp loading
- **Exemplar type:** how a replisome component couples ATP hydrolysis to a mechanical step (unwinding,
  loading, proofreading), shown with single-molecule + structure + mutants.
- **Self-check:** do your kinetics and structure agree on the same molecular step, and does a point mutant
  uncouple activity from the phenotype?

### 5. DNA repair, damage response & checkpoint
- **Exemplar type:** the molecular logic of lesion recognition or checkpoint activation (e.g., how a sensor
  discriminates a break), with reconstitution and a physiological genome-stability readout.
- **Self-check:** is the "sensing" step defined molecularly and shown to matter for genome integrity in
  cells?

### 6. Structural mechanism by cryo-EM of a machine/complex
- **Exemplar type:** a near-atomic structure of a molecular machine used to **decide** a mechanism (not to
  describe a fold), paired with function (interface mutants tested biochemically and in cells). Verify
  venue — the largest first-in-class machines often go to *Science*/*Nature*.
- **Self-check:** does the structure *decide* a mechanistic question, with every asserted contact supported
  by the density and tested functionally?

### 7. Signaling — the molecular logic of a pathway at residue resolution
- **Exemplar type:** how a kinase/phosphatase/second-messenger enzyme achieves specificity, shown by the
  modified residue, structure of the enzyme–substrate pair, and a separation-of-function readout.
- **Self-check:** is specificity explained at the level of the modified residue/interface, not just
  pathway epistasis?

### 8. Proteostasis — folding, ubiquitin–proteasome, autophagy, stress responses
- **Exemplar type:** how a degradation or folding machine selects and processes a substrate (e.g., an E3
  ligase's substrate hand-off, a chaperone's cycle), with reconstitution + structure + cellular validation.
- **Self-check:** do you reconstitute the selection/processing step and show the same logic operates in
  cells?

### 9. Biomolecular condensates / phase separation with a mechanism
- **Exemplar type:** condensate work that goes **beyond "it phase-separates"** to a molecular mechanism and
  a separation-of-function mutant that changes a biochemical output in cells. This is a crowded field —
  Molecular Cell rewards the mechanistic, functionally validated version.
- **Self-check:** do you have a mutant that alters condensation *and* the downstream biochemistry, with the
  causal link tested — not just droplets in vitro?

### 10. Functional genomics that lands on a molecular mechanism
- **Exemplar type:** a CRISPR or genomics screen whose hit is followed to a **defined molecular mechanism**
  (structure/biochemistry/mutant), not left at the level of "gene X is required."
- **Self-check:** does the paper convert a screen hit into a molecular mechanism with orthogonal
  validation, or stop at the phenotype?

### 11. Single-molecule / reconstitution biochemistry
- **Exemplar type:** rebuilding an activity from defined components to isolate a step invisible in cells,
  with kinetics that constrain the mechanism and an in-cell test of the same variable.
- **Self-check:** does reconstitution isolate the step you claim, and do cellular data confirm it is used in
  vivo?

### 12. Method/Resource that enables a mechanism (Resource / Technology)
- **Exemplar type:** a new molecular method paired, in the same paper, with a **mechanistic biological
  payoff** — Molecular Cell rewards a method only when it delivers a mechanism, not a tool demo.
- **Self-check:** does your method answer a molecular question in the same paper?

## Sibling-confusion guardrails (verify venue before attributing to *Molecular Cell*)

These high-profile works are commonly misremembered — confirm the journal field before citing:

- **Original CRISPR/Cas9 programmable-endonuclease biochemistry (Jinek et al., 2012)** — ran in ***Science***, not Molecular Cell.
- **Many RNA Pol II and spliceosome cryo-EM structures (Cramer, Shi, Nogales groups)** — largely ***Science***/***Nature***/***NSMB***.
- **cGAS as a cytosolic DNA sensor (Sun et al., 2013)** — ***Science***.
- Landmark condensate papers by the Brangwynne/Rosen/Hyman groups are spread across ***Cell***, ***Nature***, ***Science***, and ***eLife*** — check each.

Before adding any paper to this library, confirm on PubMed and the publisher record that the **journal
field literally reads *Molecular Cell*** (not *Cell* / *Cell Reports* / *NSMB* / *EMBO J* / *Genes & Dev* /
*Nucleic Acids Research*, and not *Nature* / *Science*), capture the volume(issue):pages and DOI, and note
the access date. When in doubt, omit.
