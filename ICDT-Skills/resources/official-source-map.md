# Official Source Map - ICDT

Access date: 2026-07-09

This map records the official and cross-check sources used for ICDT-specific facts in this
pack. ICDT is the **International Conference on Database Theory**, the European foundations-of-
data-management venue held jointly with **EDBT** (the shared EDBT/ICDT event) and published
**open access in LIPIcs** (Leibniz International Proceedings in Informatics, Schloss Dagstuhl).
Its calendar runs **two submission cycles per year**, the first offering a revision option; its
review has been **anonymous since 2024**. Reopen the current-cycle databasetheory.org call, the
EDBT/ICDT event site, and the LIPIcs author instructions before giving submission-ready advice.

## Access method note

Direct fetches of `databasetheory.org`, `drops.dagstuhl.de`, and the `edbticdt20xx.github.io`
event sites returned connection failures / HTTP 403 from the verification gateway on 2026-07-09
(the same egress block seen for other publisher/venue domains). Official pages were therefore
read through **WebSearch renderings of the exact URLs** and cross-checked across independent
surfaces: the databasetheory.org calls, the EDBT/ICDT event GitHub-Pages sites, the DROPS/LIPIcs
volume pages, the Microsoft CMT CfP listing, the EasyChair CfP mirror, dblp, and mailing-list
archives (TYPES/announce, DMANET, LogicList). Facts that could not be pinned to two independent
renderings are labeled **待核实**.

> Name-collision warning: several unrelated events also abbreviate to "ICDT" — the SID
> *International Conference on Display Technology* (`sidicdt.org`) and IARIA's *International
> Conference on Digital Telecommunications*. **Neither is this ICDT.** No fact in this pack
> derives from them. This ICDT is the database-theory conference at `databasetheory.org`.

## Verified 2026/2027 facts used in skills

- **ICDT 2026 is the 29th edition**, held within the **EDBT/ICDT 2026** joint event in
  **Tampere, Finland**; the ICDT 2026 proceedings are **LIPIcs Volume 365** on DROPS. The
  **EDBT/ICDT 2026 event site advertises 24-27 March 2026**; a separate CfP announcement listed
  **9-14 March 2026** — the two renderings disagree, so exact ICDT session dates are **待核实**.
  As of the 2026-07-09 access date this edition is past; the live *next* target is ICDT 2027.
- **ICDT 2026 review chair: Balder ten Cate** (ILLC, University of Amsterdam) (source 2).
- **ICDT 2027 is the 30th edition**, within **EDBT/ICDT 2027** in **Lille, France, 6-9 April
  2027** (ICDT technical sessions listed as 6-8 April in the CfP mirror). **PC chair: Stijn
  Vansummeren** (Data Science Institute, Hasselt University) (sources 1, 4).
- **ICDT 2027 two-cycle calendar (AoE):** Cycle 1 — abstracts **3 March 2026**, papers
  **10 March 2026**, notification **1 June 2026**; Cycle 2 — abstracts **3 September 2026**,
  papers **10 September 2026**, notification **1 December 2026** (source 1). As of 2026-07-09
  **Cycle 1 is decided and Cycle 2 is the immediate live deadline.**
- **Two submission cycles per year**, and the **first cycle carries a revision option**; a paper
  **rejected** in the first cycle **cannot be resubmitted to the second cycle unless the
  reviewers explicitly invite it** (sources 1, 2).
- **Anonymous submission since 2024** for the regular research track: no author names or
  affiliations, no acknowledgments to funding sources or collaborators. The **Database Theory in
  Action** track is **not** anonymous (sources 1, 2).
- **Format: the `lipics-v2021` LIPIcs style, at most 15 pages excluding references.** Additional
  material may go in a **clearly marked appendix read at the discretion of the PC**; **online
  appendices are not allowed** (sources 1, 7).
- **Publication: LIPIcs** (Leibniz International Proceedings in Informatics), Schloss Dagstuhl,
  **open access, CC-BY 4.0**, archived on **DROPS**, with DOIs; ICDT 2026 = Vol 365 (sources
  5-7).
- **Submission portal: Microsoft CMT** (`cmt3.research.microsoft.com/ICDT2027`) (source 10).
- **Awards:** the **ICDT Test-of-Time Award** (started 2013; since 2020 each year honors the
  best paper from the ICDT held ten years prior), plus a **Best Paper** and a **Best Newcomer
  Paper** award (newcomer to database theory; preferentially given to papers authored solely by
  students) (sources 8, 9).

## The LIPIcs open-access model (what differs from an ACM/PACMMOD venue)

ICDT does not publish through the ACM Digital Library. Its papers appear in **LIPIcs**, an
**open-access, no-embargo, author-retains-copyright-under-CC-BY** proceedings series run by
Schloss Dagstuhl. There is **no article-processing charge to authors** (LIPIcs runs a low-cost
model funded through the series, not per-article fees; confirm current terms each year). The
camera-ready is a **LaTeX-only** `lipics-v2021` document with mandatory ACM CCS concepts,
keywords, and (where available) author ORCIDs; the final PDF is minted with a DOI on DROPS. This
is the concrete publishing difference from the co-located systems track (EDBT) and from the
North-American database-theory venue PODS, whose research papers now appear in ACM **PACMMOD**.

## No editor-in-chief; governance

ICDT has **no standing editor-in-chief**. Each edition has a **Program Committee (review) chair**
appointed under the ICDT community / EDBT Association governance reflected at databasetheory.org;
the venue is steered by the ICDT council and organized jointly with EDBT. Treat the per-edition
PC chair as the rotating analogue of an editor, and never assume continuity of people across
editions.
