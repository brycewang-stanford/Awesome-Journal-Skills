# External Tools - ICDT

Access date: 2026-07-09

Use these surfaces after reopening the current ICDT cycle pages. ICDT mechanics — which cycles run,
the exact deadlines, the revision option, the page limit, and the anonymity rule — are decided per
edition, and the `databasetheory.org` / `drops.dagstuhl.de` / `edbticdt20xx.github.io` gateway may
block direct fetches (read via search renderings and cross-check dblp and the DROPS volume pages). Do
not confuse this venue with the SID *International Conference on Display Technology* (`sidicdt.org`)
or IARIA's *International Conference on Digital Telecommunications* — neither is this ICDT.

## Cross-check surfaces (when the gateway blocks the primary page)

- dblp ICDT proceedings stream: https://dblp.org/db/conf/icdt/ (per-edition tables of contents).
- dblp LIPIcs stream for the published volumes and DOIs.
- The DROPS volume page for the exact page counts, DOIs, and open-access PDFs.
- The EasyChair CfP mirror (`easychair.org/cfp/ICDT20NN`) and mailing-list archives (TYPES/announce,
  DMANET, LogicList) for the machine-readable deadlines the CfP paraphrases.
- arXiv for the **full versions** with complete proofs that ICDT papers routinely maintain.

## Author-side checks

- CMT profile, conflicts, coauthor emails, subject areas, and the **abstract registration** that
  precedes the paper deadline in the chosen cycle.
- `lipics-v2021` compliance, the 15-page limit excluding references, and a **clearly marked appendix**
  (no online appendices) with the complete proofs.
- Anonymity sweep (since 2024): remove author names, affiliations, acknowledgments to funding and
  collaborators; neutralize first-person self-citations; scrub PDF metadata.
- Camera-ready metadata: ACM CCS concepts, keywords, ORCIDs, CC-BY licensing, and the
  `\relatedversion` link to the arXiv full version.

## Do not infer

- Do not infer which cycle to target or that both cycles run: confirm on the current call, and
  remember a **Cycle-1 reject cannot be resubmitted to Cycle 2 uninvited**.
- Do not assume the page limit, the `lipics-v2021` revision, the anonymity wording, or any AI-usage
  disclosure rule carry over between editions.
- Do not assume ICDT shares PODS's publisher or calendar: ICDT is **LIPIcs open access** and
  **two-cycle**, PODS is ACM **PACMMOD**. Record the difference rather than guessing parity.
- If pages disagree, treat the newest databasetheory.org call, the CMT deadline, or a direct
  PC-chair announcement as controlling, and record the conflict (e.g., the ICDT 2026 24-27 March vs
  9-14 March date discrepancy).
