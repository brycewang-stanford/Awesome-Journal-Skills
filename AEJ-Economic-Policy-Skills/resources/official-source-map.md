# AEJ: Economic Policy — Official Source Map

Primary and authoritative sources behind every AEJ: Economic Policy–specific fact injected into this
skill pack. **Access date for this refresh: 2026-06-20.** AEJ: Economic Policy is published by the
**American Economic Association (AEA)**, which owns and operates the editorial and publication process.
The AEA pages below are the live authority; re-confirm volatile items (editor roster, the exact fee
schedule, policy effective dates) on the official page immediately before relying on them, since AEA
pages change. Items that could not be confirmed from a primary fetch are marked **待核实 (to be verified)**.

## Official AEA / AEJ: Economic Policy pages

- **https://www.aeaweb.org/journals/pol** — AEJ: Economic Policy journal home (AEA). Verifies that the
  journal "focuses on the role of economic policy in economic outcomes," covering public economics; urban
  and regional economics; the public-policy aspects of health, education, welfare, and political
  institutions; law and economics; economic regulation; and environmental economics; that it publishes
  **four issues per year (quarterly)**; and the metadata **ISSN 1945-7731 (print) / 1945-774X (online)**.
  It links to the editors, editorial policy, submission guidelines, and style guide.
- **https://www.aeaweb.org/journals/pol/editors** — Editors of AEJ: Economic Policy. Verifies the masthead
  used in this pack (see Editor facts below).
- **https://www.aeaweb.org/journals/pol/editorial-policy** — AEJ: Economic Policy Editorial Policy. Verifies
  the **single-blind** review model: "All manuscripts submitted to the *AEJ: Economic Policy* undergo
  single-blind peer review—the name of the author is revealed to the referee(s), while the referee(s)
  remain anonymous to authors."
- **https://www.aeaweb.org/journals/pol/submissions** — AEJ: Economic Policy Submission Guidelines. Verifies
  the fee schedule, publication fee, abstract limit, and length recommendation injected below.
- **https://www.aeaweb.org/journals/policies/data-code** — AEA Data and Code Availability Policy (applies to
  all AEA journals incl. AEJ: Economic Policy). Verifies that the **AEA Data Editor** assesses compliance,
  including by conducting **reproducibility checks** and verifying the accuracy of the information provided,
  **before final acceptance**; that the **AEA Data and Code Repository** (hosted at **openICPSR**) is the
  deposit venue and the Data Editor has automatic access to draft deposits; and that for data that cannot be
  made public, authors must preserve materials, make code public, and document sources, with the policy
  stating it is "generally not acceptable that data be provided 'upon request' if the request must be
  approved by the authors themselves."
- **https://www.aeaweb.org/journals/policies/disclosure-policy** — AEA Disclosure Policy (applies to all AEA
  journals incl. AEJ: Economic Policy). Verifies that each author must disclose interested-party financial
  relationships and funding sources relevant to the research.
- **https://www.aeaweb.org/journals/policies/low-income-country-fees** — AEA Country Designation for
  Submission Fees. Verifies that the submission-fee tier depends on the income classification of the
  country in which the authors work.

## VERIFIED facts injected into the skills

- **Publisher / cadence / scope.** American Economic Association; quarterly (four issues per year); scope is
  the economic analysis of policy across public economics, urban/regional economics, the public-policy
  aspects of health/education/welfare/political institutions, law and economics, economic regulation, and
  environmental economics. ISSN 1945-7731 (print) / 1945-774X (online). (journals/pol)
- **Review model — single-blind.** The name of the author is revealed to the referee(s); the referee(s)
  remain anonymous to authors. The submitted manuscript therefore carries the title, byline, and author
  affiliations rather than being anonymized. (journals/pol/editorial-policy)
- **Editor:** C. Kirabo Jackson (Northwestern University). **Co-editors:** Nathaniel Baum-Snow (University of
  Toronto), Lucas Davis (University of California, Berkeley), Naomi Feldman (The Hebrew University of
  Jerusalem), Brian Knight (Brown University), David Seim (Stockholm University), Amanda Starc (Northwestern
  University). (journals/pol/editors)
- **Submission fee:** AEA members — USD 200 (high-income), USD 100 (middle-income), USD 0 (low-income).
  Nonmembers — USD 300 (high-income), USD 200 (middle-income), USD 0 (low-income). Papers rejected without
  review are refunded 50 percent of the submission fee. (journals/pol/submissions)
- **Publication fee:** accepted papers first submitted on or after **1 February 2024** are assessed **USD 15
  per typeset page**. (journals/pol/submissions)
- **Front matter / length:** abstract of **100 or fewer words**; JEL codes and keywords; recommended maximum
  equivalent to **40 pages at 11-point / 1.5 spacing** or **45 pages at 12-point / 1.5 spacing**.
  (journals/pol/submissions)
- **Data & code:** the AEA Data and Code Availability Policy applies; the AEA Data Editor (Lars Vilhuber)
  runs a reproducibility check on an openICPSR deposit **before** final acceptance. (journals/policies/data-code)
- **Disclosure:** AEA Disclosure Policy — each author discloses interested-party funding and relevant
  financial relationships. (journals/policies/disclosure-policy)
- **House style:** AEA house style — report standard errors (no significance asterisks), online appendix for
  long material, author–year references. (journals/pol style guide)
