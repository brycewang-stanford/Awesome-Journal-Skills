## Verified 2026-cycle facts used in the skills

- SOSP 2026 is the 32nd ACM Symposium on Operating Systems Principles, Sep 29 -
  Oct 2, 2026, Prague, Czech Republic (workshops open Sep 29).
- Submissions ran on HotCRP (`sosp26.hotcrp.com`); abstract registration March 27,
  2026 and full papers April 2, 2026, 7:59:59 AM EDT (equivalently March 26 /
  April 1 AoE); notification July 3, 2026; camera-ready August 28, 2026.
- Submissions: at most 12 pages of technical content (all text, figures, tables);
  bibliographic references uncounted. Camera-ready: 13 pages, plus one
  shepherd-approved page to a 14-page maximum.
- Reviewing is double-blind with a good-faith anonymization standard; declared
  conflicts are audited by the PC chairs, who may add or remove entries.
- The author response exists to correct factual errors and answer questions posed
  by reviewers; no hard length limit, but under 500 words is strongly encouraged;
  no new experiments, data, or promised work.
- Accepted papers are shepherded; proceedings publish in the ACM Digital Library.
- Artifact evaluation is optional, post-acceptance, run through
  `sysartifacts.github.io` with a dedicated HotCRP instance, single-blind and
  cooperative, awarding ACM badges (Available; Functional; Reusable) with
  Distinguished Artifact recognition on top.
- SOSP has been annual since 2024; before that it alternated years with OSDI.

## Organizers and fee model

- SOSP is an ACM SIGOPS conference. Like every conference in this collection it has
  NO standing editor-in-chief; leadership is a per-edition General Chair /
  Program-Chair team appointed through SIGOPS and rotating every cycle. The 2026
  chair roster was not re-verified for this pack (待核实 at
  `sigops.org/s/conferences/sosp/2026/` organization pages before citing names).
- There is NO article-processing charge. SOSP is funded through conference
  registration; accepted papers appear in the ACM Digital Library under ACM's
  rights-management and open-access options, whose per-paper terms arrive with the
  ACM rights form after acceptance (待核实 per paper — terms and TAPS workflow
  details come from the acceptance email, not from this pack).
