Primary and authoritative sources behind the JPART-specific facts injected into this skill pack.

## Official journal, submission & author pages

- **https://academic.oup.com/jpart/pages/Instructions_To_Authors**
  — JPART Instructions to Authors. Verifies: manuscripts **no longer than ~12,000 words, including
  abstract, tables, and references**; abstract must state the **theoretical approach, method and data,
  results, and implications for theory**; **3–5 keywords**, with the first three indicating **theory,
  research theme, and method**; **author-date** citations with an alphabetical reference list **with
  DOIs**; submission as a **single PDF** (title, abstract, text, references, tables/figures, appendices)
  via **Editorial Express** (`editorialexpress.com/jpart`); the manuscript must be **anonymous** (omit
  name and funding, cite own work in the third person; acknowledgments/funding on the cover sheet only);
  **double-blind** review; blinded pre-registration reports (with hyperlinks) go in the data and methods
  section or, if not anonymizable, on the cover sheet; **JPART requires all authors, where ethically
  possible, to publicly release all data and software code underlying any published paper as a condition
  of publication**, with a mandatory **Data Availability Statement** (exemptions require explicit editor
  approval).
- **https://academic.oup.com/jpart/pages/General_Instructions**
  — JPART General Instructions (submission system, file requirements).
- **https://academic.oup.com/jpart** — JPART on Oxford Academic (publisher home, current issues).

## Scope, society & "why submit"

- **https://academic.oup.com/jpart/pages/About**
  — About JPART. Verifies: published by **Oxford University Press** on behalf of the **Public Management
  Research Association (PMRA)**; the journal "seeks to advance public administration scholarship by
  publishing the highest quality **theoretical and empirical** work in the field," covering
  "organizational, administrative, managerial, and policy-based research that improves our understanding
  of the public sector," and is "committed to developing diverse and rigorous research that **extends and
  builds public administration theory**."
- **https://academic.oup.com/jpart/pages/why-submit**
  — Why Submit to JPART. Verifies: a **detailed, double-blind review process** that gives authors the
  perceived strengths/weaknesses, the basis for the decision, and advice on how to proceed.
- **https://academic.oup.com/jpart/pages/about_the_public_management_research_association**
  — About the PMRA (owning society).

## Editors, history & standing

- **https://academic.oup.com/jpart/pages/Editorial_Board**
  — Editorial Board. Verifies (as of access date): Editors **Ole Helby Petersen (Roskilde University)**
  and **Kim Sass Mikkelsen (Roskilde University)**, plus Associate Editors and a Social Media Editor.
- **https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Journal_of_Public_Administration_Research_and_Theory**
  — Verifies: JPART **established 1991**; quarterly, peer-reviewed; published by OUP for the PMRA; a
  leading journal in public administration (high ranking in Public Administration and Political Science).

## Data, transparency & replication context

- **https://academic.oup.com/jpart/pages/Instructions_To_Authors** (data section, above) — mandatory
  public release of data and code as a condition of publication + Data Availability Statement.
- **https://academic.oup.com/jpart/article/29/4/609/5074357**
  — "Best Practice Recommendations for Replicating Experiments in Public Administration," JPART 29(4):609
  — evidence of JPART's active experimental-replication and transparency norm (context, not policy text).
