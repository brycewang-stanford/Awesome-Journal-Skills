# Worked Example: Reframing an Introduction for Journal of Health Economics

This is a fictional example. It does not describe a real paper.

## Before

> This paper studies an important topic using a large dataset and several robustness checks. We contribute to the literature by adding new evidence and showing heterogeneous effects.

## Diagnosis

- The first sentence does not name the JHE audience or the journal's health economics, insurance, provider incentives, medical technology, health policy, and health behavior scope.
- The contribution could fit several siblings: American Journal of Health Economics, Journal of Public Economics, Health Economics, and AEJ Economic Policy.
- The mechanism words that matter here are absent: provider incentives, insurance design, patient selection, clinical-policy margin.

## After

> This paper asks how **provider incentives** changes the decision margin that JHE readers care about. The design ties the estimate to **insurance design**, separates the main mechanism from **patient selection**, and reports uncertainty in a way that makes the policy or theory implication portable beyond the study setting.
