# Worked Example: An RER-Style Review Introduction (before → after)

This demonstrates the **RER review-opening arc** from
[`revedres-writing-style`](../../skills/revedres-writing-style/SKILL.md):
**frame the field and the problem → why a synthesis is needed now → the organizing framework (the
contribution) → the bottom line and open agenda, stated early → brief roadmap.**

Two RER facts drive the rewrite and distinguish it from a primary-research journal:

- RER is **AERA's review journal of record** (founded 1931), read **across education and adjacent
  disciplines**. An RER article **synthesizes** a literature; it reports **no original empirical research of
  its own** ([`revedres-topic-selection`](../../skills/revedres-topic-selection/SKILL.md)). It is not AERJ /
  *AERA Open* (primary research), not *Educational Researcher* (short commentary).
- An RER review's contribution is an **organizing framework that advances theory or policy**, built on a
  **documented, reproducible search** — not an annotated bibliography and not a vote-count
  ([`revedres-organizing-framework`](../../skills/revedres-organizing-framework/SKILL.md)). The bottom line is
  stated **early**, and rival camps are **steelmanned**
  ([`revedres-comprehensiveness-and-balance`](../../skills/revedres-comprehensiveness-and-balance/SKILL.md)).

**Illustrative review (fictional):** *"Worked Examples and Problem-Solving in K–12 Mathematics: A Systematic
Review and Meta-Analysis."* Fictional field: a (made-up) literature on whether studying worked examples beats
unguided problem-solving, with invented schools and debates. Every characterization of "the literature" is
invented for teaching.

---

## Before (an annotated bibliography — typical first-draft review intro)

> The literature on worked examples is large. Many studies have compared worked examples to problem-solving.
> Some studies find worked examples help, others find no difference, and a few find problem-solving is better.
> Researcher A and colleagues argue worked examples are superior, while Researcher B's group disagrees. In this
> review we summarize the main studies. Section 2 covers the early lab studies, Section 3 covers the classroom
> studies, Section 4 covers recent studies, and Section 5 concludes. We discuss each study in turn and report
> what each one found.

**What is wrong (against `revedres-writing-style` / `revedres-organizing-framework` / `revedres-comprehensiveness-and-balance`):**

- **No organizing framework.** Sections are "early / classroom / recent" — a chronological list, not an
  analytic spine. This is the cardinal RER sin: an **annotated bibliography**.
- **Vote-counting.** "Some find it helps, others find no difference" tallies signs instead of *reconciling*
  studies by what they measure and how credibly.
- **No bottom line.** A broad reader finishes the paragraph not knowing what the field has *settled* or why a
  synthesis is needed *now*.
- **No systematic process.** Nothing about a search, inclusion rules, or a PRISMA flow — fatal for a
  "systematic review and meta-analysis" ([`revedres-literature-synthesis`](../../skills/revedres-literature-synthesis/SKILL.md)).
- **Camps named, not steelmanned**, and **"we discuss each study in turn"** announces the summary-not-synthesis
  failure outright.

---

## After (RER arc — frame the field, document the search, impose a spine, state the bottom line)

> **Should students who are learning a new mathematical procedure study fully worked examples, or learn by
> solving problems themselves — and why do rigorous studies of the same question keep disagreeing?** The
> worked-example effect is among the most-tested ideas in instructional research, yet headline estimates of its
> benefit range from large and positive to indistinguishable from zero. *(frame the field + the animating
> question in the first breath — for a broad education reader.)*
>
> A synthesis is needed now because the disagreement is **no longer mainly about whether the effect exists** —
> it is about **for whom and under what conditions**: a wave of classroom studies has made clear that the
> benefit appears to depend on learners' prior knowledge, the complexity of the material, and how "problem
> solving" is implemented. Reading the studies as rival verdicts on one universal effect is the source of the
> apparent contradiction. *(why a synthesis is needed now — a shift from "whether" to "when";
> [`revedres-topic-selection`](../../skills/revedres-topic-selection/SKILL.md).)*
>
> We searched ERIC, PsycINFO, Education Source, Scopus, and ProQuest Dissertations to a preregistered protocol
> (OSF), screened 1,—— records in duplicate (κ = .——), and included —— studies (PRISMA flow, Figure 1). We then
> organize the evidence not by chronology but by a **moderator framework**: we classify each effect by (i)
> learner prior knowledge, (ii) element-interactivity (material complexity), and (iii) the fidelity of the
> problem-solving comparison. This spine turns scattered estimates into a coherent map — studies that looked
> contradictory occupy **different cells** and are, on inspection, **largely consistent**. *(documented search +
> the organizing framework — the review's contribution;
> [`revedres-literature-synthesis`](../../skills/revedres-literature-synthesis/SKILL.md),
> [`revedres-organizing-framework`](../../skills/revedres-organizing-framework/SKILL.md).)*
>
> Read through this framework, the literature is more settled than the headline range suggests: for novices on
> complex material, well-controlled studies cluster on a **positive** benefit of worked examples, while the wide
> dispersion comes almost entirely from studies that differ in learner expertise, complexity, or comparison
> fidelity — not from genuine disagreement about the same parameter. The genuinely **open questions** concern
> the **expertise-reversal boundary**, **long-run retention and transfer**, and **implementation at classroom
> scale**, where credible evidence remains thin. *(bottom line + open agenda, stated up front;
> [`revedres-comprehensiveness-and-balance`](../../skills/revedres-comprehensiveness-and-balance/SKILL.md).)*
>
> Throughout, we state each research program's **strongest case before its limits** — the cognitive-load view
> that worked examples reduce extraneous load, and the constructivist view that productive struggle aids
> learning, are each given the evidence that most favors them — and we are explicit about where our own reading
> sits and what evidence would change it. *(steelman + labelled author's read, not consensus-by-assertion.)*
>
> The review proceeds as follows. Section 2 reports the search and coding; Section 3 sets out the moderator
> framework; Section 4 maps the well-controlled estimates into it; Section 5 turns to retention, transfer, and
> scale; Section 6 collects the open agenda. *(brief roadmap — mirrors the framework, no over-signposting.)*

## Why the "after" meets the RER bar

Mapped to this pack's skill checklists:

- **The opening follows the review arc** — frame → why-now → documented search + framework → bottom-line-early →
  roadmap — and states the **bottom line up front**
  ([`revedres-writing-style`](../../skills/revedres-writing-style/SKILL.md): "state the bottom line early, not
  withheld to the conclusion").
- **It is systematic, not narrative-by-vibe** — databases, a preregistered protocol, a reliability statistic,
  and a PRISMA flow are named in the intro
  ([`revedres-literature-synthesis`](../../skills/revedres-literature-synthesis/SKILL.md)). (Counts shown as
  `——` because this is fictional — a real review fills them in and they must reconcile.)
- **There is a real spine, not a list** — a three-axis moderator framework (prior knowledge × complexity ×
  comparison fidelity) that **reconciles an apparent contradiction** (studies sit in different cells), exactly
  the [`revedres-organizing-framework`](../../skills/revedres-organizing-framework/SKILL.md) test (MECE,
  reconciling, generative — thin cells become the open agenda).
- **Conflicting findings are weighed, not tallied** — dispersion is explained by *moderators and credibility*,
  the [`revedres-comprehensiveness-and-balance`](../../skills/revedres-comprehensiveness-and-balance/SKILL.md)
  standard, and each camp is **steelmanned**.
- **It is written for a broad audience** — no notation in the intro; the debate is explained by the *question it
  answers*, the RER voice ([`revedres-writing-style`](../../skills/revedres-writing-style/SKILL.md)).
- **Sibling check passes:** this is a comprehensive, systematic synthesis for the review-of-record — not an
  original-data AERJ study and not a short *Educational Researcher* commentary. It reports **no new empirical
  data of its own**; it appraises others'.
