# RER Exemplars Library (review type × topic)

> **Verified via Crossref, access date 2026-06.** Every article below was checked to confirm it actually
> appeared in the ***Review of Educational Research*** (AERA's review journal of record, published by SAGE)
> — journal name, year, volume(issue), and pages were read off the Crossref record for each DOI. Articles
> that could not be confirmed as RER were **omitted** — this is deliberately a short, clean list rather than
> a long, uncertain one.
>
> **Sibling-confusion guard (do not misattribute).** AERA journals **share the `10.3102/` DOI prefix**, so
> the stem alone does **not** confirm RER. *Educational Researcher* (`10.3102/0013189X…`), the *American
> Educational Research Journal* (AERJ, original research), and *AERA Open* also use `10.3102/`; and several
> education meta-analyses that *sound* like RER pieces actually ran in *Educational Measurement: Issues and
> Practice*, *Psychological Bulletin*, or the *Journal of Economic Perspectives*. Only the **journal name on
> the Crossref/SAGE record** confirms RER. Two omissions on exactly these grounds are recorded below.
>
> **Use principle (zero hallucination):** this file gives **design positioning only** — it shows how a great
> RER review is *built* (its scope, its framework, its synthesis). It does **not** reproduce or invent the
> articles' specific findings — read the original before citing any claim. For RER-specific policy, scope, and
> house rules, see [`../official-source-map.md`](../official-source-map.md).

## How to use this library

Pick the row whose **review type × question** is closest to yours, then study how that review turns a
*comprehensive* reading of a literature into an *argument about the field* that **advances theory or policy**
— the RER bar (see [`../../skills/revedres-topic-selection/SKILL.md`](../../skills/revedres-topic-selection/SKILL.md)
and [`../../skills/revedres-organizing-framework/SKILL.md`](../../skills/revedres-organizing-framework/SKILL.md)).
For each, ask the self-check before claiming your draft is "RER-shaped." Remember RER's identity: a review
**appraises** the literature even-handedly and reports **no original empirical research of its own**
([`revedres-comprehensiveness-and-balance`](../../skills/revedres-comprehensiveness-and-balance/SKILL.md)).

## By review type

### Meta-analysis (pooled effect sizes + moderators)

- **Sirin, "Socioeconomic Status and Academic Achievement: A Meta-Analytic Review of Research," RER 2005, 75(3):417–453.**
  Verified (Crossref): DOI `10.3102/00346543075003417`, *Review of Educational Research*.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a disciplined meta-analysis that pools a large, heterogeneous correlational
    literature and explains variation through **moderators** (how SES and achievement are measured), rather
    than reporting one number — the [`revedres-comprehensiveness-and-balance`](../../skills/revedres-comprehensiveness-and-balance/SKILL.md) standard.
  - **Self-check:** does your meta-analysis explain *why* effects vary (moderators), not just report a pooled mean?

- **Cooper, Robinson & Patall, "Does Homework Improve Academic Achievement? A Synthesis of Research, 1987–2003," RER 2006, 76(1):1–62.**
  Verified (Crossref): DOI `10.3102/00346543076001001`, *Review of Educational Research*.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** an exhaustive, policy-salient synthesis that weighs evidence by design and
    grade level and is explicit about what the evidence does and does not support — comprehensiveness with a
    bottom line for a broad audience ([`revedres-writing-style`](../../skills/revedres-writing-style/SKILL.md)).
  - **Self-check:** can a non-specialist finish your review knowing what is settled and where evidence is thin?

### Quantitative synthesis of a construct's intervention base

- **Hattie & Timperley, "The Power of Feedback," RER 2007, 77(1):81–112.**
  Verified (Crossref): DOI `10.3102/003465430298487`, *Review of Educational Research*.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** synthesizes a sprawling feedback literature under a **conceptual model** (the
    three feedback questions × four levels) so the evidence is organized by a frame that advances theory and
    practice — the [`revedres-organizing-framework`](../../skills/revedres-organizing-framework/SKILL.md) ideal.
  - **Self-check:** does your framework reorganize the field into something a reader could not build from the abstracts?

### Critical / integrative synthesis (reframe a field)

- **Yeager & Walton, "Social-Psychological Interventions in Education: They're Not Magic," RER 2011, 81(2):267–301.**
  Verified (Crossref): DOI `10.3102/0034654311405999`, *Review of Educational Research*.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a *critical* synthesis that explains **why** brief interventions work, when they
    do, and what the field misreads — judging the literature's assumptions, not just cataloguing it
    ([`revedres-comprehensiveness-and-balance`](../../skills/revedres-comprehensiveness-and-balance/SKILL.md)).
  - **Self-check:** does your review critically judge the field's mechanisms and assumptions, not merely summarize results?

### Construct clarification / conceptual synthesis

- **Fredricks, Blumenfeld & Paris, "School Engagement: Potential of the Concept, State of the Evidence," RER 2004, 74(1):59–109.**
  Verified (Crossref): DOI `10.3102/00346543074001059`, *Review of Educational Research*.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** disentangles a **conflated construct** (behavioral / emotional / cognitive
    engagement) and re-reads the evidence through the clarified scheme — the construct-clarification spine in
    [`revedres-organizing-framework`](../../skills/revedres-organizing-framework/SKILL.md).
  - **Self-check:** if your field conflates distinct ideas, does your review separate them before weighing evidence?

- **Martin & Dowson, "Interpersonal Relationships, Motivation, Engagement, and Achievement," RER 2009, 79(1):327–365.**
  Verified (Crossref): DOI `10.3102/0034654308325583`, *Review of Educational Research*.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** integrates several literatures around a relational frame, yielding implications
    for theory and practice — an integrative synthesis with a generative agenda.
  - **Self-check:** do your thin/empty framework cells become a concrete theory/research/practice agenda?

### Historical / systematic field review

- **Brookhart, Guskey, Bowers, McMillan et al., "A Century of Grading Research: Meaning and Value in the Most Common Educational Measure," RER 2016, 86(4):803–848.**
  Verified (Crossref): DOI `10.3102/0034654316672069`, *Review of Educational Research*.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a systematic, century-spanning review that maps a large literature and renders
    both what is established and what remains contested — comprehensiveness with an analytic structure
    ([`revedres-literature-synthesis`](../../skills/revedres-literature-synthesis/SKILL.md)).
  - **Self-check:** is your coverage provably exhaustive *and* organized by an argument, not by chronology alone?

## Omitted for lack of full verification, or to avoid sibling-confusion (do not attribute to RER without re-checking)

To keep the list zero-hallucination, the following were **excluded** after checking:

- **Kraft, "Interpreting Effect Sizes of Education Interventions" (`10.3102/0013189X20912798`)** — frequently
  cited in education-synthesis contexts, but Crossref records it in ***Educational Researcher***, not RER.
  The shared `10.3102/` prefix makes this an easy misattribution; only the **journal name** confirms RER.
- **Kingston & Nash, "Formative Assessment: A Meta-Analysis and a Call for Research"** — published in
  ***Educational Measurement: Issues and Practice***, not RER. Listed here only as a guardrail: an
  education meta-analysis is not automatically an RER article.
- Several canonical education meta-analyses that *sound* like RER pieces were left off because their **exact
  RER volume/issue/pages and Crossref journal record** could not be confirmed to the same standard in one
  pass; add them only after confirming the journal name on Crossref/SAGE.

Before adding any new article to this library, confirm on **Crossref/SAGE** that the **journal name is
*Review of Educational Research*** (not *Educational Researcher*, AERJ, *AERA Open*, *Educational Measurement*,
or *Psychological Bulletin*), then update the access-date header. When in doubt, omit.
