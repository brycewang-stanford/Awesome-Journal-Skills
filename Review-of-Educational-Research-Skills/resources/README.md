# Review of Educational Research — Resources

Capability layer for the **Review of Educational Research (RER)** skill pack. RER is a
**review journal**: an RER article synthesizes a body of research bearing on education
(a systematic review, a meta-analysis, or a critical/conceptual synthesis) rather than
reporting original empirical results. There is therefore **no code kit** in this pack —
a review runs no original primary data collection of its own. Instead, the cross-cutting
empirical-methods guidance is linked below as **background for appraising the primary
studies a review covers** (you are the field's reviewer-of-record), and the review-craft
skills live in `../skills/`.

## How to use

1. **Before synthesizing** — for each included primary study you will weigh, run it
   against the reviewer-objection checklist and the reporting standards to set its
   **risk-of-bias / credibility tier** (this feeds the study-characteristics table and
   the even-handed treatment of conflicting evidence in
   `../skills/revedres-comprehensiveness-and-balance/SKILL.md`).
2. **When building exhibits** — only pool *commensurable* effect sizes; the reporting
   standards tell you when an apparent consensus is real
   (`../skills/revedres-tables-figures/SKILL.md`).
3. **Before submission** — walk `official-source-map.md` for APA 7, the 150-word
   abstract, masking, the Word file set, and PRISMA/MARS attachments.

> The empirical-methods hub is venue-neutral and is used here only to **appraise** the
> studies a review covers — this pack ships **no runnable code**, because an RER review
> reports no original estimates of its own (its reproducible contribution is its search,
> coding, and synthesis; see
> `../skills/revedres-transparency-and-reproducibility/SKILL.md`). Always defer to this
> pack's skills and `official-source-map.md` for what RER specifically requires.
