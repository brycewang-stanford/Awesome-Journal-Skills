# Official Source Map - IEEE S&P

Access date: 2026-07-08

This map records the official sources behind every IEEE Symposium on Security and
Privacy (IEEE S&P, historically "Oakland") fact in this pack. S&P re-issues its Call
for Papers every year and runs two submission cycles per symposium, each with its own
HotCRP site and deadline page; reopen the current `sp<year>.ieee-security.org` CFP and
the live cycle site before giving deadline-ready advice.

**Access-method note (2026-07-08):** direct fetches of `sp2026.ieee-security.org`,
`sp2027.ieee-security.org`, `cycle1.sp2027.ieee-security.org`, and CFP mirrors returned
HTTP 403 in the verification environment. Official-page contents were therefore read
through search-engine renderings of those exact URLs and cross-checked against the IEEE
TC on Security and Privacy index, dblp's `conf/sp` stream, IEEE Xplore's proceedings
listing, and institutional award announcements. Every dated claim below carries that
caveat: open the primary URL yourself before acting on a deadline, cap, or policy.

## Verified live-cycle facts used in the skills

- The live target on 2026-07-08 is **S&P 2027, the 48th symposium, Montreal, Canada,
  May 2027**. Its **Cycle 1 submission deadline (June 12, 2026) passed about four weeks
  before this pack's check date**; the open path is Cycle 2, with abstract registration
  November 10, 2026 and full papers November 17, 2026.
- S&P 2027 deadlines on the cycle sites are stated in a fixed clock time — 7:59:59 AM
  EDT for Cycle 1 — not the Anywhere-on-Earth convention many CS venues use. Convert to
  your own timezone rather than assuming AoE.
- Registration is substantive at S&P: the deadline requires the final title, the full
  abstract, the complete author list with ORCIDs, and declared conflicts on HotCRP, and
  none of those may change afterwards.
- Format: up to 13 pages of body text plus up to 5 pages of references and appendices,
  never more than 18 pages total, in the IEEE conference-proceedings ("compsoc"
  IEEEtran) template per the 2026 wording.
- Review runs in rounds within each cycle: papers cut in the first round receive an
  early-reject notice (January 18, 2027 for Cycle 2); survivors see their reviews
  (February 11, 2027) and file a rebuttal (due February 16, 2027) before the March 5,
  2027 decision.
- The decision set includes a **Revise** outcome — a structured major revision with an
  expectations summary, resubmitted for re-review — in addition to Accept and Reject;
  accepted papers get a draft meta-review whose remaining concerns a **shepherd**
  checks off during camera-ready.
- Rejected papers may not return for one year measured from the original submission
  date, and a new paper is treated as a resubmission when roughly 40% or more overlaps
  the rejected one.
- Ethics is structural, not decorative: an "Ethics Considerations" disclosure is
  entered on HotCRP at registration, a dedicated ethics section is added to accepted
  papers without counting against page limits, a Research Ethics Committee reviews
  flagged papers, and the Menlo Report is the named reference framework. IRB
  approval/waiver status must be disclosed where applicable, and PII-touching work must
  document harm mitigation in the reviewed version.
- Artifact evaluation is a post-acceptance opt-in with three badges: Available
  (DOI-backed permanent deposit), Functional, and Results Reproduced.
- SoK papers are a first-class submission type in both the 2026 and 2027 CFPs, marked
  by the "SoK:" title prefix and a checkbox, and judged on systematization value.
- The 2027 CFP invokes the IEEE generative-AI policy (no AI authors; model-generated
  ideas must be independently developed and validated; AI-introduced limitations must
  be discussed) and desk-rejects papers whose authors lack matching ORCID records.
- The series has run annually since 1980 (Claremont Resort, hence "Oakland"), under
  the IEEE Computer Society TC on Security and Privacy with IACR cooperation, and
  publishes through IEEE (proceedings on IEEE Xplore); dblp's `conf/sp` stream confirms
  edition numbering.
