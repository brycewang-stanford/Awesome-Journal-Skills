## By method

### Venture-capital cognition / policy-capturing experiment (decision research)

- **Zacharakis & Meyer, "A lack of insight: Do venture capitalists really understand their own decision process?," JBV 1998, 13(1):57–76.**
  Verified: ScienceDirect article page (pii `S0883902696000638`) and RePEc `eee/jbvent` `v13y1998i1p57-76`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** uses a policy-capturing design to show investors' *introspected* decision
    models diverge from their *actual* judgment policies — the phenomenon (entrepreneurial-finance
    judgment) is the contribution, not a generic decision-science demo. Pairs with the experiment /
    judgment row of [`../../skills/jbv-methods/SKILL.md`](../../skills/jbv-methods/SKILL.md).
  - **Self-check:** does your design measure the entrepreneurial *judgment process* itself, not just a
    self-report of it?

### Verbal-protocol / lab experiment on entrepreneurial reasoning (effectuation)

- **Dew, Read, Sarasvathy & Wiltbank, "Effectual versus predictive logics in entrepreneurial decision-making: Differences between experts and novices," JBV 2009, 24(4):287–309.**
  Verified: ScienceDirect article page and RePEc `eee/jbvent` `v24y2009i4p287-309`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** an expert–novice think-aloud contrast turns an abstract construct
    (effectuation) into an observable reasoning difference — a model of building entrepreneurship theory
    from process data rather than importing a generic cognition framework.
  - **Self-check:** does your manipulation/contrast isolate something distinctively *entrepreneurial*
    about how the decision is made, with a boundary (here: expertise)?

### Large survey + emotion-regulation model (psychology of the entrepreneurial career)

- **Patzelt & Shepherd, "Negative emotions of an entrepreneurial career: Self-employment and regulatory coping behaviors," JBV 2011, 26(2):226–238.**
  Verified: ScienceDirect article page (pii `S0883902609000834`) and RePEc `eee/jbvent` `v26y2011i2p226-238`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** builds a psychology-engine model (regulatory coping) of the *lived
    experience of self-employment* and tests it on a large sample — the contribution is to entrepreneurial
    well-being, not generic job stress. Maps to the experiment/survey rows of `jbv-data-analysis`.
  - **Self-check:** is the psychological mechanism scoped to the entrepreneurial context (self-employment
    risk, autonomy), not a construct that would read identically for any employee?

### Meta-analysis with contextual / methodological moderators (cumulative evidence)

- **Stam, Arzlanian & Elfring, "Social capital of entrepreneurs and small firm performance: A meta-analysis of contextual and methodological moderators," JBV 2014, 29(1):152–173.**
  Verified: ScienceDirect article page (pii `S088390261300013X`) and RePEc `eee/jbvent` `v29y2014i1p152-173`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** aggregates a fragmented social-capital literature *and* theorizes *when*
    the effect is strong via entrepreneurial moderators (firm age, industry, network measure) — meta-
    analysis as theory contribution, not just an effect-size average.
  - **Self-check:** do your moderators advance *entrepreneurship* theory (stage, novelty, ecosystem),
    rather than only correcting for measurement artifacts?

### Archival platform data, descriptive + predictive (new-venture financing)

- **Mollick, "The dynamics of crowdfunding: An exploratory study," JBV 2014, 29(1):1–16.**
  Verified: ScienceDirect article page and the RePEc/JBV record; widely indexed as JBV 29(1):1–16.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** uses novel platform (Kickstarter) data to establish the basic empirical
    regularities of a then-new entrepreneurial-finance mechanism — a model of how JBV rewards
    well-documented *novel-dataset* work when the phenomenon is genuinely new (`jbv-methods`: "novel
    datasets … document construction, coverage, and the sampling frame").
  - **Self-check:** is your dataset's novelty an asset because the *sampling frame is defensible*, and does
    it illuminate an entrepreneurial mechanism rather than just showcase the data?

### Venture panel, moderated/configurational regression (entrepreneurial orientation)

- **Wiklund & Shepherd, "Entrepreneurial orientation and small business performance: A configurational approach," JBV 2005, 20(1):71–91.**
  Verified: ScienceDirect article page (pii `S0883902604000047`) and RePEc `eee/jbvent` `v20y2005i1p71-91`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** shows a main-effects-only story is incomplete and that EO's performance
    payoff is *configurational* (interacting with capital access and environmental dynamism) — entrepre-
    neurial moderators carrying the theory, the pattern `jbv-theory-development` calls for.
  - **Self-check:** are your interactions theorized *entrepreneurial* contingencies, not a fishing
    expedition over generic firm covariates?
