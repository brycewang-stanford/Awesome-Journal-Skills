## Official journal, submission & author pages

- **https://www.journals.uchicago.edu/journals/jop/instruct** — The Journal of Politics, Instructions
  to Authors (University of Chicago Press). Verifies: **Research Articles no longer than 35 pages**,
  **including all text, footnotes, references, and tables/figures**; **Short Articles no longer than
  10 pages** on the same inclusive basis; manuscripts **double-spaced in 12-point type with one-inch
  margins**; supplementary material goes in a separate **"Online Appendix"** uploaded in Editorial
  Manager, **no more than 25 pages**, which **does not count against the 35-page limit**; **double-blind
  review** with an anonymous version of the manuscript for review; submission via **Editorial Manager**

### Abstract & keywords

- The abstract is a **single paragraph of 150 words or less** describing the research question, the
  analytical approach, and the major findings; **references/citations are not included** in the
  abstract; **4–5 keywords** are listed below the abstract on the title/abstract page. Source: JOP
  Instructions to Authors (via UChicago Press search rendering; 待核实 verbatim on the live page).

## Data-access & replication policy (JOP's distinctive in-house verification)

- **https://www.journals.uchicago.edu/journals/jop/data-replication** — The Journal of Politics,
  Guidelines for Data Replication. Verifies: it is JOP policy to **publish papers only if the data
  used are accessible, the analyses fully documented, and the empirical findings reproducible**;
  authors of accepted papers with **empirical, simulation-based, or formal-theoretical** analyses must
  provide datasets, code, and other information necessary for replication; **"All manuscripts are
  accepted contingent on their replicability, which will initially be assessed by the JOP replication
  analyst(s) who will be assigned to your manuscript at the conditional acceptance stage"**;
  **manuscripts that are not replicable will be rejected**; replication materials comprise **four
  items — a readme file, dataset(s), a codebook, and code** — uploaded to the **JOP Dataverse in a
  single .zip file**; the readme describes every file plus the operating system and software version;
  the codebook names and defines every variable; materials are made **publicly available at the time
  of publication**. (Page returned HTTP 403 to direct fetch; verified via UChicago Press search
  renderings — re-confirm verbatim on the live page. 待核实.)
- **https://www.journals.uchicago.edu/journals/jop/conditional-accepts** — The Journal of Politics,
  information for authors of conditional accepts (the replication-upload workflow). 待核实 verbatim
  (403 to direct fetch).
- **https://dataverse.harvard.edu/dataverse/jop** — **The Journal of Politics Dataverse** on
  **Harvard Dataverse**. Verifies: JOP replication materials are hosted on Harvard Dataverse under a
  dedicated JOP collection with permanent DOIs (e.g., datasets under `doi:10.7910/DVN/...`).

## Owner, publisher, editors, history

- **https://spsa.net/about-spsa/journal-of-politics/** — Journal of Politics page on the **Southern
  Political Science Association** site. Verifies: JOP is described as **"a leading general interest
  journal in political science"** that **"publishes theoretically innovative and methodologically
  diverse research in all subfields of the discipline including, but not limited to, American politics,
  comparative politics, formal theory, international relations, methodology, political theory, public
  administration and public policy"**; conceptions of theory and method are **"both broad and
  encompassing"**; submission via Editorial Manager; **University of Chicago Press has been the
  publisher since January 2015**; owner = **SPSA**.
- **https://spsa.net/the-spsa-and-the-journal-of-politics-welcome-new-editors/** — SPSA & JOP welcome
  new editors. Verifies (as of access date): **Co-Editors-in-Chief Timothy Hellwig (University at
  Buffalo, SUNY) and Timothy P. Nokken (Texas Tech University)**, **starting January 1, 2025**, with
  Associate Editors Amy Catalinac (NYU), Katja B. Kleinberg (Binghamton), Olga Shvetsova (Binghamton),
  Nicholas Tampio (Fordham), and Julie Wronski (Mississippi).
- **https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Journal_of_Politics** — Verifies: JOP **founded 1939**;
  **published quarterly** (February, May, August, November) by the University of Chicago Press for the
  SPSA; the **oldest regional political-science journal in the United States** and widely regarded as a
  top general-interest journal of the discipline.
