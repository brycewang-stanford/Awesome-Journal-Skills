# JOP Official Source Map

Primary sources behind the JOP-specific facts injected into this skill pack.
Access date for all entries: **2026-06-20**.

Use this map as a provenance ledger, not as a substitute for the final live upload screen.
JOP's publisher pages are controlled by the University of Chicago Press and can change without
repo notice, so the last pre-submission pass should re-open the current Instructions to Authors,
Style Guide, Data Replication page, and Editorial Manager form.

## Publisher author rules and portal

- **https://www.journals.uchicago.edu/journals/jop/instruct** — The Journal of Politics,
  Instructions to Authors (University of Chicago Press). Official search-index snippets on
  2026-06-20 verify the abstract rule (**single paragraph, 150 words or less, 4-5 keywords**),
  the Editorial Manager workflow, and the replication-contingent acceptance language repeated
  on the data-policy page. The same author-page snippets show an optional **USD 2,500**
  open-access request fee; treat it as an OA/publication-budget item rather than an upload-stage
  charge unless the live submission screen says otherwise.
- **https://www.journals.uchicago.edu/journals/jop/style** — The JOP Style Guide. Official
  search-index snippets on 2026-06-20 verify that initial submissions under review as
  **Research Articles may not exceed 35 pages** and **Short Articles may not exceed 10 pages**;
  the same snippet family verifies **Online Appendices may be no longer than 25 pages**.
- **https://www.journals.uchicago.edu/journals/jop/reviewer-info** — The JOP reviewer page.
  Official search-index snippets on 2026-06-20 verify the reviewer-facing page budget:
  Research Articles are capped at **35 pages inclusive of notes, references, tables, and figures**,
  while Short Articles are capped at **10 pages**.
- **https://www.editorialmanager.com/jop** — JOP submission portal on **Editorial Manager**.
  The SPSA journal page links to this portal as the manuscript submission entry point.

## Data-access and replication policy

- **https://www.journals.uchicago.edu/journals/jop/data-replication** — The Journal of Politics,
  Guidelines for Data Replication. Official search-index snippets on 2026-06-20 verify that
  **all manuscripts are accepted contingent on replicability**, that the check is initially
  assessed by **JOP replication analyst staff** assigned at conditional acceptance, and that
  manuscripts failing the replication gate are rejected for publication.
- The same official data-replication snippets verify that, as a general rule, replication materials
  include **four items: a readme file, dataset(s), codebook, and codes**, and that these materials
  should be uploaded to **JOP's Dataverse in a single zip file**.
- **https://www.journals.uchicago.edu/journals/jop/conditional-accepts** — JOP page for authors
  of conditional accepts. Use it with the data-replication page when preparing the post-decision
  upload workflow.
- **https://dataverse.harvard.edu/dataverse/jop** and
  **https://dataverse.harvard.edu/api/dataverses/jop** — Harvard Dataverse collection and API
  record for **The Journal of Politics Dataverse**. The API record on 2026-06-20 returns alias
  `jop`, name **The Journal of Politics Dataverse**, type `JOURNALS`, parent **Harvard Dataverse**,
  and description: "The Journal of Politics supports the growing effort in political science to
  establish best practices for the dissemination of replication files."

## Owner, publisher, editors, and scope

- **https://spsa.net/about-spsa/journal-of-politics/** — Journal of Politics page on the
  **Southern Political Science Association** site. Direct rendering on 2026-06-20 verifies that
  JOP is a leading **general interest** political-science journal; publishes theoretically
  innovative and methodologically diverse work across subfields including American politics,
  comparative politics, formal theory, international relations, methodology, political theory,
  public administration, and public policy; has broad and encompassing conceptions of theory
  and method; and has been published by **University of Chicago Press** since January 2015.
- The same SPSA page directly verifies the current editorial team whose term began
  **January 1, 2025**: **Timothy Hellwig** and **Timothy P. Nokken** as Co-Editors in Chief,
  with **Amy Catalinac**, **Katja B. Kleinberg**, **Olga Shvetsova**, **Nicholas Tampio**, and
  **Julie Wronski** listed as the rest of the editorial team. Re-confirmed current 2026-06-22
  (SPSA Journal of Politics page; UChicago Press editorial board / new-editor announcement).
- **https://www.journals.uchicago.edu/journals/jop/new-editor** — UChicago Press announcement of
  the new editorial team. Official snippets on 2026-06-20 confirm the January 1, 2025 transition
  and the Hellwig/Nokken leadership.

## Operational notes for this pack

- The pack should state JOP's binding length constraint as **pages, not words**:
  Research Article **<= 35 pages**, Short Article **<= 10 pages**, and Online Appendix
  **<= 25 pages**.
- The pack should state the review model as **double-blind** and require an anonymous manuscript
  file plus separate identifying materials.
- The pack should state that JOP has a **replicability-contingent acceptance** gate, with a
  **JOP replication analyst** assigned at conditional acceptance.
- The pack should not promise a fixed review clock, desk-rejection rate, reviewer count, or
  guaranteed short-track speed. It may describe JOP as efficiency-minded, but only as a qualitative
  expectation.
- The pack should not tell authors to budget for an upload-stage submission charge. It should
  mention the **USD 2,500 optional open-access request fee** only as an OA/publication-budget item
  to confirm on the live UChicago page or Editorial Manager screen.
- The pack should avoid describing the **Short Article** format as a separate "Letters" track unless
  a current official JOP page uses that label.
