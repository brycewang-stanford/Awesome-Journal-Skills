# Official Source Map - CVPR

Access date: 2026-07-08

This map records the official sources behind every CVPR-specific fact in this pack.
CVPR policy is rewritten by each edition's Program Chairs; before giving submission-ready
advice, reopen the current-cycle Author Guidelines, Dates page, Call for Papers, Reviewer
Guidelines, and the OpenReview group for the year you are targeting.

**Access-method note (2026-07-08):** direct fetches of `cvpr.thecvf.com` and
`openaccess.thecvf.com` returned HTTP 403 from the verification environment's gateway,
so the contents of the official pages were read through search-engine renderings of the
exact URLs below, and cross-checked against the CVPR OpenReview group, the CVF
open-access repository index, the `cvpr-org/author-kit` GitHub repository, dblp records,
and official CVPR press announcements mirrored on PR Newswire. Open the primary URLs
directly before relying on any date, cap, or policy sentence.

**Cycle status at access time:** CVPR 2026 (Denver) concluded in June 2026 and is the
most recent completed cycle; every verified fact below is a CVPR 2026-cycle snapshot.
The CVPR 2027 cycle (Seattle) had an announced venue/date window but **no published CFP,
deadlines, or policies** as of 2026-07-08 — everything about 2027 is 待核实.

## Verified 2026-cycle facts used in the skills

- CVPR 2026 was held June 3–7, 2026 in Denver, Colorado (workshops/tutorials June 3–4,
  main program June 5–7), organized under IEEE Computer Society and CVF co-sponsorship.
- Submission ran on OpenReview (`thecvf.com/CVPR/2026/Conference`). Every author needed a
  complete, valid OpenReview profile by the abstract-registration deadline (November 6,
  2025); the full-paper deadline was November 13, 2025, 11:59pm AoE and was announced as
  fixed; supplementary material was due November 20, 2025.
- Papers were capped at 8 pages **including figures and tables**, with additional pages
  allowed only for cited references. Formatting violations and over-length papers are
  rejected without review.
- Reviews were due January 12 and released to authors on January 22, 2026; authors could
  file an optional **one-page** rebuttal PDF on the official template (longer or
  reformatted rebuttals are simply not read); reviewer–AC discussion ran January 30 –
  February 5; final decisions were released February 20, 2026.
- Authors are expected to review: qualifying authors were enrolled into the reviewer pool,
  and papers authored by reviewers who missed review deadlines or wrote highly
  irresponsible reviews could be desk-rejected in their entirety at PC discretion.
- The Compute Reporting Form was required with every 2026 submission (two mandatory
  sections, the rest optional, with a documented opt-out route).
- Dual submission: no substantially similar paper may be under review at another
  conference or workshop during the defined review period (Nov 13, 2025 – Feb 20, 2026);
  prior publications of four pages or fewer do not trigger the rule; GitHub documents are
  not counted as publications; posting to arXiv is not treated as an anonymity violation.
- External links in the paper or supplement that expand content or subvert review
  (anonymity, the media embargo, length or deadline limits) are banned.
- Papers under review are embargoed from proactive media discussion until acceptance;
  violations can remove a paper from the conference and proceedings. Third-party posts
  made without author involvement are not violations.
- Reviewer-side LLM rules: reviews and meta-reviews may not be LLM-written, and
  substantial submission content may not be shared with an LLM; author-side wording makes
  authors fully responsible for tool-assisted text ("an LLM did it" is no defense), with
  fabricated citations or factual inaccuracies grounds for rejection without review.
- Scale: 16,092 reviewed submissions, 4,090 acceptances (25.42%), ~25,149 reviewers from
  97 countries, 909 area chairs, 44,011 authors; about 12,200 registrants attended.
- A dataset claimed as a scientific contribution must be publicly available no later than
  the camera-ready deadline.
- Publication: all accepted papers appear open-access on `openaccess.thecvf.com`
  (accepted version plus watermark) and as the version of record in IEEE Xplore. There is
  no article-processing charge; the conference is funded via registration, and like every
  CS conference in this collection CVPR has **no standing editor-in-chief** — General and
  Program Chairs rotate per edition.
