# Review Process (revacc-review-process)

## When to trigger

- You have submitted to RAST and want to understand what the timeline and decision will look like
- You received a terse first-round decision and need to interpret it
- You are deciding whether RAST's fast, decisive process fits your paper's stage of polish
- You are weighing the **RAST Conference** path against the regular track
- You are choosing between RAST and a sibling (TAR/JAR/JAE/CAR) on process grounds

## RAST's distinguishing process culture

RAST advertises **prompt turnaround** and — importantly — an explicit tradition of making an **accept/reject decision on the first round for the majority of manuscripts**, rather than running paper after paper through multiple R&R cycles (待核实; 检索于 2026-06；以官网为准). This shapes strategy more than any other RAST fact:

- **The first submission must be near-final.** Because many papers get a first-round decision, you do not get the long iterative runway some journals offer. Identification, the model, exhibits, and the contribution should be settled *before* you submit — there may be no second chance to fix them.
- **Decisions can be terse.** A first-round reject may come with limited feedback; do not read brevity as carelessness — read it as the process working as designed.
- **An R&R is a strong signal.** When RAST does issue an R&R rather than a first-round decision, it is a meaningful vote of confidence; treat the editor's letter as the binding map (see `revacc-rebuttal`).

Process facts to keep straight: **double-blind** review, **Editorial Manager** portal (both 待核实; 检索于 2026-06), and the **USD $500 fee within 7 days** (verified on the Springer submission guidelines, 核查于 2026-06-22; desk rejection refunds the fee minus a US$75 admin charge — re-verify before submitting).

## The RAST Conference path

RAST runs a recurring **conference** whose accepted papers are considered for a **conference issue**, while *all* conference submissions are also reviewed for the regular journal (verified via the Wharton accounting page, 检索于 2026-06). Strategic reads:

- The conference offers presentation plus a fast read; a non-accepted conference paper still enters regular consideration, so it is a low-downside route for a paper that is ready.
- Confirm the current year's deadlines, host, and scope on the official call before committing.

## When a sibling fits the process better

| If you need… | Consider |
|--------------|----------|
| A long, multi-round runway to develop a rough-but-promising paper | TAR or CAR may offer more iterative review than RAST's first-round culture |
| A posted data-and-code package and Registered Reports as the headline | JAR |
| The economics-of-accounting house style and the JAE data archive | JAE |
| Equal standing for an analytical/disclosure model with fast turnaround | **RAST** is the natural home |

## Calibrate and plan

1. Confirm the paper is near-final before submitting — the first-round-decision norm is unforgiving of half-built work.
2. Set internal expectations: a fast decision, possibly terse; an R&R is good news.
3. Decide the conference-vs-regular path deliberately.
4. Pre-stage the response-letter workflow (`revacc-rebuttal`) only if an R&R arrives — do not pre-write it.

## Checklist

- [ ] The paper was near-final before submission (no reliance on multiple R&R rounds)
- [ ] Expectations set for a fast, possibly first-round, decision
- [ ] Double-blind / portal / fee facts confirmed for the current cycle (待核实)
- [ ] Conference vs. regular path chosen on purpose
- [ ] Sibling-venue fit considered if RAST's process is a poor match
- [ ] An R&R, if received, is treated as a strong positive signal

## Anti-patterns

- **Submitting half-built work** expecting RAST to develop it across rounds — its first-round norm punishes this.
- **Misreading a terse reject** as a process failure rather than the design working.
- **Ignoring the conference path** for a ready paper that would benefit from the fast read.
- **Trusting cached timeline/fee facts** instead of re-verifying for the current cycle.
- **Pre-writing a rebuttal** before any R&R exists.
