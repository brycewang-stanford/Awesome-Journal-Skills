# Venue-Selection Patterns

These patterns guide routing across agriculture and environment venue families.
They are not paper exemplars.

## Significance Versus Field Result

- Food-systems and broad-significance journals (`nature-food`) need wide
  conceptual reach; a larger trial alone does not create reach.
- Field and agronomy journals (`field-crops-research`) ask whether the result
  holds under realistic management, across sites and seasons, with replication.

## Ecology Versus Molecular Mechanism

- Plant ecology and physiology venues (`new-phytologist`) ask whether the
  ecological or whole-plant insight changes understanding.
- Molecular plant venues (`the-plant-journal`) ask whether the pathway, gene, or
  mechanism is established.
- Field-evidence replication is a gate: untested mechanism or single-site results
  weaken either route.

## Water And Earth Sciences

- Fundamental water-science venues (`water-resources-research`) prioritize
  process, theory, and quantitative mechanism.
- Broad hydrology venues (`journal-of-hydrology`) prioritize application breadth
  across methods and basins.
- `earth-and-planetary-science-letters` suits long quantitative letters; `geology`
  suits short letters with immediate broad significance.

## Data Journals And Reviews

- `earth-system-science-data` is a data journal: the dataset itself is the
  contribution and must be FAIR-deposited in a persistent, citable repository.
- FAIR data-deposition is a gate for any reuse claim; verify the deposit on the
  official repository before routing.
- Annual Review and Trends titles (`annual-review-of-plant-biology`,
  `trends-in-food-science-and-technology`) are invited reviews; confirm invitation.
