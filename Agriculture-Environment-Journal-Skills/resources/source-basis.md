# Source Basis and Official-Check Notes

This pack encodes journal-fit and house-style heuristics for 30 flagship
English-language **agriculture, environment, and earth-science** journals — the
category the sibling `English-NaturalScience-Journal-Skills` bundle leaves almost
empty. It is a positioning and re-framing aid, **not** a static substitute for any
journal's live author instructions.

For per-journal starting queries, use `official-source-map.md` in this directory.

## What this bundle covers (and why these venues)

- **Agriculture, food, plant, soil & crop science.** Nature Food and Nature Plants
  (Nature Portfolio), New Phytologist and The Plant Journal, the Annual Review of
  Plant Biology, Field Crops Research, Agriculture, Ecosystems & Environment, Soil
  Biology and Biochemistry, Agronomy for Sustainable Development, Food Chemistry,
  and Trends in Food Science & Technology.
- **Environment, sustainability & pollution.** Global Change Biology, Environment
  International, Environmental Health Perspectives, Journal of Cleaner Production,
  Resources, Conservation & Recycling, the Annual Review of Environment and
  Resources, and Environmental Pollution.
- **Earth, atmosphere, hydrology, ocean & climate.** Communications Earth &
  Environment (Nature Portfolio), Journal of Climate (AMS), Earth System Science
  Data, Journal of Hydrology, Water Resources Research (AGU), Earth and Planetary
  Science Letters, Geology (GSA), Global Biogeochemical Cycles (AGU), Agricultural
  and Forest Meteorology, Forest Ecology and Management, the Bulletin of the
  American Meteorological Society, and Limnology and Oceanography (ASLO).

## Stable, public facts used for fit (no volatile numbers)

Only durable structural conventions inform fit — e.g. the Nature Portfolio
significance-statement and reporting-summary system; the society affiliations of the
AGU (Water Resources Research, Global Biogeochemical Cycles), AMS (Journal of
Climate, BAMS), GSA (Geology), and ASLO (Limnology and Oceanography) titles; the
review character of the Annual Review and Trends titles and BAMS's mix of reviews
and society content; the data-journal nature of Earth System Science Data (data
papers with mandatory public datasets); and the field-data / reproducibility and
data-availability expectations now standard in environmental and earth science.

## What each profile deliberately does NOT assert

To stay correct over time, every profile **omits volatile facts** and defers them
to the official-submission checklist instead of stating a number:

- No impact factors, CiteScores, acceptance rates, or "ranked #N" claims.
- No ISSNs, exact word/figure limits, APC amounts, or editor names.
- No fabricated citations (literatures are referred to generically).

## Operational rule for every journal skill

1. Before submitting, search the **live official site or submission system** for
   "<journal name> author guidelines / instructions for authors" and follow the
   current version.
2. Trust the publisher's own domain or submission system (Nature, Elsevier Editorial
   Manager, Wiley, the AGU/AMS/GSA/ASLO systems, Copernicus for ESSD) over any
   third-party or aggregator copy.
3. Re-check article-type and length limits, abstract format, figure/display limits,
   the applicable reporting expectations, **data and code availability and mandatory
   deposition** (especially for data journals and earth-science datasets),
   field/site and sampling reporting, ethics/permits where relevant, competing-interests
   and funding disclosure, AI-use disclosure, preprint policy, and open-access terms.
4. For umbrella families (Nature Portfolio, Elsevier, Wiley, AGU, AMS), check both the
   journal-specific page and the family/society policy page.
5. If the live official instructions conflict with a skill, the official
   instructions win.
