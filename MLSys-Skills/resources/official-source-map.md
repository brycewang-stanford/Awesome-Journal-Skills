## Verified 2026-cycle facts used in skills

- MLSys 2026 was the ninth edition, held May 18-22, 2026 at the Hyatt Regency Bellevue;
  the conference began as SysML in 2018 at Stanford and adopted the MLSys name with the
  2020 Austin edition (history cross-checked against mlsys.org/Conferences/2018/ and the
  proceedings archive).
- Research-track submissions: two-column, up to 10 pages excluding references; every
  reference must list all authors; appendices unlimited but uploaded separately and
  reviewers are not obliged to read them; appendix due at the same time as the paper.
- Review was double-blind, but posting the paper on arXiv or other public forums was
  explicitly allowed; authors had to anonymize affiliations, self-references, and
  acknowledgements in the submission itself.
- Dual submission: work under review elsewhere or already published was ineligible;
  arXiv technical reports were fine; expanded versions of workshop papers with
  proceedings could be submitted subject to program-chair approval and were judged on
  the delta over the workshop version.
- 2026 introduced an Industrial Track with relaxed novelty requirements, mandatory
  benchmark depth, and partial anonymization (author names hidden, company identity
  allowed).
- The 2026 author-response window was extremely short: reviews out January 12,
  responses due January 16 — four days.
- Artifact evaluation happens after acceptance, is optional, and awards Availability,
  Functional, and Reproducible badges plus Distinguished Artifact Awards; the 2026 AE
  ran March 8 - April 8.
- Proceedings are published on proceedings.mlsys.org, the venue's own open archive.
- The MLPerf Training Benchmark paper (Mattson et al.) appeared at MLSys 2020,
  anchoring the venue's benchmark-rigor culture; MLPerf later grew into MLCommons.

## Organizers and fee model (checked 2026-07-08)

- MLSys is a conference run by a rotating per-edition committee; there is no standing
  editor-in-chief. The 2026 General Chair was Luis Ceze; Program Chairs were Zhihao Jia
  and Aakanksha Chowdhery. Affiliations listed on the committee page change as people
  move — re-check the live organizing-committee page before citing one. 待核实:
  individual affiliations beyond the names themselves.
- There is no article-processing charge. Publication on proceedings.mlsys.org is free
  and open; the venue is funded by registration and sponsorship. Accepted-paper authors
  receive a reserved-ticket registration window (source 9).
