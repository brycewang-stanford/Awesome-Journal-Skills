# External Tools - MLSys

Access date: 2026-07-08

Open these official systems before acting on any cycle-specific advice in this pack.
MLSys rewrites its CFP, track list, and artifact-evaluation call every year, and the
2026 cycle added a whole new track — assume the next cycle changes something again.

## Official workflow

- MLSys home and current-cycle entry point: https://mlsys.org/
- MLSys 2026 Call for Research Papers: https://mlsys.org/Conferences/2026/CallForResearchPapers
- MLSys 2026 Call for Industrial Track Papers: https://mlsys.org/Conferences/2026/CallForIndustrialTrackPapers
- MLSys 2026 Call for Artifact Evaluations: https://mlsys.org/Conferences/2026/CallForAEs
- MLSys 2026 dates and deadlines: https://mlsys.org/Conferences/2026/Dates
- MLSys 2026 OpenReview group: https://openreview.net/group?id=MLSys.org/2026/Conference
- Proceedings archive (2019 onward): https://proceedings.mlsys.org/
- Registration FAQ for authors: https://mlsys.org/FAQ/RegistrationForAuthors

## Author-side checks

- Which track: research (novelty required) or industrial (production-scale system,
  design methodology, and benchmarks required; company identity may remain visible).
- Current LaTeX style kit (mlsys2026style.zip for the 2026 cycle) applied unmodified,
  two-column, 10-page body excluding references, all authors listed in every reference.
- Appendix prepared as a separate upload, due with the main paper, and written knowing
  reviewers are not obliged to open it.
- Anonymization sweep of the PDF: affiliations, self-references, acknowledgements,
  repository URLs in the research track. arXiv posting is allowed, but the submission
  itself must still be blinded.
- Dual-submission declaration: nothing under review elsewhere; expanded workshop papers
  need program-chair approval and an explanation of the delta.
- Author-response calendar: in 2026 the window was four days (January 12-16). Block it
  on every co-author's calendar in advance.
- Post-acceptance: artifact abstract + Artifact Appendix to the AE site by its own
  deadline (March 8 in 2026) if you want Availability / Functional / Reproducible
  badges; camera-ready and registration through the reserved-ticket author window.

## Measurement and benchmarking references

- MLPerf / MLCommons benchmark suites: https://mlcommons.org/ — the community's shared
  vocabulary for training and inference performance claims. Not a submission
  requirement, but reviewers know these workloads and their reporting rules.

## Do not infer

- Do not project the 2026 dates, the four-day response window, the 10-page limit, or
  the Industrial Track's existence onto a later cycle without reopening mlsys.org.
- Do not assume camera-ready limits, presentation obligations, or AI-use policy — none
  of these could be verified for 2026 (see the 待核实 list in
  `official-source-map.md`).
- If the CFP, OpenReview instructions, and an organizer email disagree, the newest
  organizer communication controls; record the conflict and the date you resolved it.
