# Official Source Map - AAMAS

Access date: 2026-07-09

This map records the official sources used for AAMAS-specific facts in this pack. AAMAS is
organized by the International Foundation for Autonomous Agents and Multiagent Systems
(IFAAMAS) and hosted by a different local team each edition, so deadlines, tracks, page
limits, review workflow, and portal all move every cycle. Reopen the current-edition Call for
Papers, submission instructions, important-dates page, and OpenReview group before giving
submission-ready advice.

## Verified 2026 facts used in skills

- AAMAS 2026 was the 25th International Conference on Autonomous Agents and Multiagent
  Systems, held May 25-29, 2026 at the Coral Beach Hotel & Resort, Paphos, Cyprus, organized
  under IFAAMAS.
- AAMAS 2026 used OpenReview (`ifaamas.org/AAMAS/2026/Conference`) for submission and review.
- Main-track timetable: abstract deadline October 1, 2025; full-paper deadline October 8,
  2025; rebuttal period November 21-25, 2025; camera-ready February 11, 2026. All deadlines
  at end of day Anywhere on Earth (AoE).
- Main-track papers were at most 8 pages, with **any number of additional pages for
  references**; typesetting tricks to force material into 8 pages were disallowed.
- Main-track submissions could be **full papers (8 pages + references)** or **extended
  abstracts (2 pages + references)**.
- Review was **double-blind**: authors replaced name and affiliation on page one with the
  paper tracking number and removed acknowledgements.
- A **rebuttal** let authors respond to preliminary reviews before the final decision.
- Accepted papers are published by **IFAAMAS**, open-access on the IFAAMAS website; all
  accepted papers **and their reviews** are public on OpenReview.
- Supplementary material was a single zip file, not exceeding 25 MB.
- Special tracks alongside the main track: **AAAI Track** (deadline November 17, 2025; same
  format as the main track, no extended-abstract option, plus a document of up to 2 pages
  explaining how the AAMAS submission addresses the AAAI reviewers' comments, with changes
  highlighted), **JAAMAS Track** (deadline January 6, 2026; 2-page extended abstracts of
  articles published or accepted in JAAMAS within the 12 months before the AAMAS notification
  date), **Blue Sky Ideas Track** (papers at most 4 pages including references), **Demo
  Track**, and **Competitions Track**.

## Organization and fee model

- AAMAS is owned by **IFAAMAS**, a standing foundation, but each edition is run by rotating
  **General Chairs** and **Program Chairs** appointed for that year; like every conference in
  this collection it has **no editor-in-chief**. Re-check the current organization page before
  naming any chair.
- There is **no article-processing charge**. Accepted papers are published open-access by
  IFAAMAS; the cost model is **registration**, and at least one author is expected to attend
  and present. Confirm the current registration and presentation policy each cycle.
