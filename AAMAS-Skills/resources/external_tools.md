# External Tools - AAMAS

Access date: 2026-07-09

Use these tools after reopening the current AAMAS cycle pages. AAMAS is a rotating-host,
IFAAMAS-owned conference, so tracks, deadlines, and even the submission template can change
between editions. Treat every 2026 value as an anchor, not a rule.

## Author-side checks

- OpenReview account, profile, conflicts, coauthors, subject-area keywords, title, and the
  short plain-text abstract due before the paper deadline.
- Correct track: main track (full paper vs 2-page extended abstract), AAAI Track, JAAMAS
  Track, Blue Sky Ideas, Demo, or Competitions - each with its own deadline and format.
- Current IFAAMAS/AAMAS style file, the 8-page main-track body limit, and the unlimited
  reference pages that follow it.
- Double-blind compliance: tracking number instead of names on page one, no acknowledgements,
  no identifying metadata, anonymized supplement, code, data, and repository links.
- Supplementary material packaged as a single zip within the current size cap (25 MB in 2026).
- Rebuttal-window logistics: dates, per-reviewer response mechanics, and the no-new-paper rule.
- Camera-ready package, IFAAMAS proceedings metadata, registration, and presentation plan.

## Do not infer

- Do not infer AAMAS 2027 (or later) dates, host city, page limits, or track deadlines from
  the 2026 cycle.
- Do not assume the rebuttal format, extended-abstract option, supplement size cap, template,
  or presentation obligation carried forward without reopening the current CFP.
- If official pages disagree, treat the newest CFP, OpenReview announcement, or a direct chair
  communication as controlling, and record the conflict with its access date.
