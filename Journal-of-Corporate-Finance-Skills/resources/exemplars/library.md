# *Journal of Corporate Finance* Exemplars Library (method × topic)

> **Verified via web search, access date 2026-06-08.** Every paper below was checked to confirm it
> actually appeared in the **_Journal of Corporate Finance_** (JCF) — the Elsevier corporate-finance field
> journal — with year and volume/issue, against the publisher record on ScienceDirect
> (`sciencedirect.com/.../journal-of-corporate-finance`), the IDEAS/RePEc series record
> (`ideas.repec.org/a/eee/corfin/...`), EconPapers, and corroborating Google Scholar lookups carrying the
> journal name. Papers that could not be fully verified **as JCF** were **omitted** — this is deliberately
> a short, clean list, not a long, uncertain one.
>
> **Publisher / identity.** JCF is published by **Elsevier** and handled through Editorial Manager; it uses
> **author-date (Harvard)** referencing and a **≤250-word abstract**. For JCF-specific scope, fees,
> blinding, and house style, see [`../official-source-map.md`](../official-source-map.md).
>
> **Sibling-journal guard (read this before citing anything).** JCF is a strong field journal, but it sits
> **below the top three** finance outlets — the **_Journal of Finance_ (JF)**, **_Review of Financial
> Studies_ (RFS)**, and **_Journal of Financial Economics_ (JFE)**. The most famous corporate-finance papers
> — family-firm value, diversification discount, governance-and-acquirer-returns, dividends-and-
> expropriation — are almost all in those three (or in **_JFQA_** / the **_AER_**), **not in JCF**. The
> RePEc code for JCF is `eee/corfin`; a corporate-finance paper indexed under `eee/jfinec` (JFE),
> `bla/jfinan` (JF), `oup/rfinst` (RFS), or `cup/jfinqa` (JFQA) is **not** a JCF paper. The
> [Omitted](#omitted-for-lack-of-full-verification-do-not-attribute-to-jcf) section records the specific
> traps checked and rejected. When a corporate-finance paper "feels canonical," that is a signal to
> **suspect JF/RFS/JFE**, not JCF.
>
> **Use principle (zero hallucination):** this file gives **design positioning only**. It does not
> reproduce or invent coefficients, valuation discounts, or specific findings — read the original on
> ScienceDirect before citing any number.

## How to use this library

Pick the row whose **method × corporate-finance question** is closest to yours, then study how that paper
turns a firm-level quantity into a contribution that survives the JCF desk screen — *a crisp "what is new
and why it matters to corporate finance" claim with a credible design* (see
[`../../skills/jcf-topic-selection/SKILL.md`](../../skills/jcf-topic-selection/SKILL.md),
[`../../skills/jcf-contribution-framing/SKILL.md`](../../skills/jcf-contribution-framing/SKILL.md), and
[`../../skills/jcf-identification-strategy/SKILL.md`](../../skills/jcf-identification-strategy/SKILL.md)).
For each, ask the self-check question before claiming your paper is "JCF-shaped."

## By method

### Cross-country panel with ownership-type splits (family ownership / firm value)

- **Maury, "Family ownership and firm performance: Empirical evidence from Western European corporations," _Journal of Corporate Finance_ 2006, 12(2):321–341.**
  Verified: RePEc `ideas.repec.org/a/eee/corfin/v12y2006i2p321-341.html`; ScienceDirect `pii/S0929119905000404`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a Western-European **panel** that does not stop at "family firms differ" but
    splits family control into **active vs. passive** ownership, showing the value effect lives only in one
    type — the [`jcf-contribution-framing`](../../skills/jcf-contribution-framing/SKILL.md) move of refining
    a coarse category until the finding becomes a mechanism, not a label. The cross-country design also
    confronts institutional confounds head-on, per
    [`jcf-data-analysis`](../../skills/jcf-data-analysis/SKILL.md).
  - **Self-check:** when you disaggregate your headline category into the subtypes theory predicts, does the
    effect concentrate where the mechanism says it should?

### Cross-sectional governance test of a specific agency story (CEO/director pay / cronyism)

- **Brick, Palmon & Wald, "CEO compensation, director compensation, and firm performance: Evidence of cronyism?," _Journal of Corporate Finance_ 2006, 12(3):403–423.**
  Verified: RePEc `ideas.repec.org/a/eee/corfin/v12y2006i3p403-423.html`; SSRN `abstract_id=303574`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** tests a sharply stated hypothesis — that **excess** CEO and director pay
    co-move and are associated with worse performance ("cronyism") — rather than a vague pay-performance
    correlation. The contribution is a named agency mechanism with an observable signature, the
    [`jcf-contribution-framing`](../../skills/jcf-contribution-framing/SKILL.md) "turn the result into a
    falsifiable corporate-governance claim" pattern.
  - **Self-check:** is your governance finding a *specific* agency story with a predicted comovement, or
    just a sign on a controls-laden regression?

### Conditional event-study / value-of-acquisitions test (M&A / governance)

- **Bauguess & Stegemoller, "Protective governance choices and the value of acquisition activity," _Journal of Corporate Finance_ 2008, 14(5):550–566.**
  Verified: RePEc `ideas.repec.org/a/eee/corfin`; ScienceDirect `pii/S0929119908000916`; SSRN `abstract=1028590`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** the contribution is not whether acquisitions create value on average but
    **how governance conditions it** — value destruction for one entrenchment dimension (founding-family
    presence) and value creation for another (large boards with more insiders) in the *same* sample. That
    is the [`jcf-identification-strategy`](../../skills/jcf-identification-strategy/SKILL.md) discipline of
    making the cross-section of returns interact with a governance moderator rather than reporting a pooled
    announcement effect.
  - **Self-check:** does your M&A result identify *which* firms create or destroy value and why, instead of
    an average CAR everyone already expects?

### Real-options structural model (capital structure / investment timing)

- **Lyandres & Zhdanov, "Convertible debt and investment timing," _Journal of Corporate Finance_ 2014, 24:21–37.**
  Verified: RePEc `ideas.repec.org/a/eee/corfin`; ScienceDirect (JCF vol. 24, pp. 21–37); SSRN `abstract_id=884305`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a **real-options / structural** model showing how convertible debt mitigates
    the debt-overhang distortion in investment *timing* — a theoretical contribution with sharp comparative
    statics, demonstrating that JCF publishes well-posed theory, not only empirics
    ([`jcf-topic-selection`](../../skills/jcf-topic-selection/SKILL.md): JCF's remit is empirical **and**
    theoretical corporate finance).
  - **Self-check:** if your contribution is theoretical, does the model deliver testable comparative statics
    on a recognizably corporate-finance friction (here, debt overhang under option value)?

## Omitted for lack of full verification (do not attribute to _JCF_ without re-checking)

To keep the list zero-hallucination, these strong candidates were **excluded** after web-checking — each
is a real, important paper, but **not in JCF** (the sibling-journal traps the brief warned of). Most famous
corporate-finance papers live in the top three (JF / RFS / JFE):

- **Villalonga & Amit, "How do family ownership, control and management affect firm value?" (2006)** —
  verified in the **_Journal of Financial Economics_** 80(2):385–417, **not JCF**. The canonical
  family-firm-value paper (easy to confuse with the genuinely-JCF Maury 2006 above — different paper,
  different venue).
- **Faccio, Masulis & McConnell, "Political Connections and Corporate Bailouts" (2006)** — verified in
  **_The Journal of Finance_** 61(6):2597–2635, **not JCF**.
- **Adams & Ferreira, "Women in the boardroom and their impact on governance and performance" (2009)** —
  verified in the **_Journal of Financial Economics_** 94(2):291–309, **not JCF**.
- **Berger & Ofek, "Diversification's effect on firm value" (1995)** — verified in the **_Journal of
  Financial Economics_** 37(1):39–65, **not JCF**. The canonical diversification-discount paper.
- **Faccio, Lang & Young, "Dividends and Expropriation" (2001)** — verified in the **_American Economic
  Review_** 91(1):54–78, **not JCF**.
- **Cronqvist & Nilsson, "Agency Costs of Controlling Minority Shareholders" (2003)** — verified in the
  **_Journal of Financial and Quantitative Analysis_** 38(4):695–719, **not JCF**.

Before adding any new paper to this library, confirm it on ScienceDirect (or the
`ideas.repec.org/a/eee/corfin/...` RePEc record) with volume/issue, and update the access-date header.
When in doubt — especially when a corporate-finance paper "feels canonical" — **suspect JF/RFS/JFE and
omit**.
