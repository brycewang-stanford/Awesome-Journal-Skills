# PNAS Nexus Official Source Map

Primary source map for facts used in the PNAS Nexus skill pack. **Access date: 2026-06-23.**
Re-check editor names, the APC figure, license options, exact length and word limits, portal forms,
data policies, and style details before relying on them. Items not confirmed from an official page are
marked **待核实 (to be verified)** in the skills.

> **PNAS Nexus is not flagship PNAS.** PNAS Nexus is the **fully open-access sibling journal**, published
> by **Oxford University Press (OUP)** in partnership with the **National Academy of Sciences (NAS)**.
> It has a **different editor-in-chief, a different (gold-OA + APC) business model, no Direct/Contributed
> submission track, and different length/style rules** from the flagship *PNAS* (Proceedings of the
> National Academy of Sciences). Do not transcribe facts from the `PNAS-Skills` pack into this one.

## Verified on official / corroborated sources (2026-06-23)

### Identity & governance
- **Publisher:** Oxford University Press, **in partnership with the National Academy of Sciences (NAS)**. PNAS Nexus is the **gold open-access sibling journal to PNAS** (the flagship NAS journal). **CONFIRMED.** Verbatim (OUP About): "Published by the NAS in partnership with Oxford University Press"; "A sibling journal to the Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences (PNAS)..."
- **Launched 2022** (inaugural issue opened 2 March 2022). **CONFIRMED.**
- **e-ISSN: 2752-6542** (online-only; no print ISSN). **CONFIRMED** (OUP; DOAJ TOC keyed to 2752-6542).
- **Editor-in-Chief: Yannis C. Yortsos** (Dean, USC Viterbi School of Engineering; National Academy of Engineering). Appointed late 2022; succeeded the **inaugural EIC Karen E. Nelson** (J. Craig Venter Institute). **CONFIRMED** across OUP editorial-board page + NAS + PNAS announcements (≥3 sources). *Editors rotate — re-verify the sitting EIC at submission time.*
- **Editorial structure:** EIC + **Deputy Editors** + **Associate Editors** + a **Board of Reviewing Editors**, organized into **three subject divisions**: (1) **Biological, Health, and Medical Sciences**; (2) **Physical Sciences and Engineering**; (3) **Social and Political Sciences**. The Editorial Board spans members of the **NAS, the National Academy of Engineering (NAE), and the National Academy of Medicine (NAM)**, plus select nonmembers in emerging fields. **CONFIRMED** (structure). Individual deputy/associate names rotate — verify live before naming anyone.
- **Indexing:** DOAJ, Scopus, **Web of Science Emerging Sources Citation Index (ESCI)**, and **PubMed Central (PMC)**. **CONFIRMED** (OUP About). MEDLINE inclusion is **待核实** — only PMC is confirmed; do not claim MEDLINE.
- **Submission portal:** **https://pnasnexus.msubmit.net/** (eJournalPress-family portal; **not** ScholarOne). **CONFIRMED** (portal URL).

### Open access, APC & licensing
- **Fully gold open access.** Every article is OA on publication; there is **no subscription/hybrid option**. **CONFIRMED.** Verbatim: "As a gold open access journal, PNAS Nexus complies with all major funder mandates, and all articles are freely and publicly available to read."
- **APC (article processing charge): indicative only ≈ US$2,200–4,200 — confirm live.** OUP's author page publishes **no fixed standard research-article APC**; it prices charges through a **region/category-personalized calculator**, so the amount billed depends on **article category, the corresponding author's region/country, and any membership/institutional discount** — and **LMIC waivers or Read & Publish agreements can reduce or eliminate it.** Public third-party trackers **disagree**: the **DOAJ** record reports ≈**US$4,202** while another tracker reports ≈**US$2,200**; neither is confirmable on the live OUP calculator, and **neither is clearly more authoritative**. So treat the APC as **indicative only and confirm the exact charge live in the OUP calculator at submission.** No separate submission/editorial fee is documented (the APC is the only charge — LIKELY). *(DOAJ and the OUP calculator both returned HTTP 403 to automated fetch on 2026-06-23; the figures here are third-party-tracker values surfaced via web search, not eyeballed on the live pages — re-confirm before relying on them.)*
- **Licenses:** **CC BY 4.0** and **CC BY-NC 4.0** offered. (As of February 2024, PNAS Nexus changed from CC BY + CC BY-NC-ND to **CC BY + CC BY-NC**.) **CONFIRMED.** The named technical default is **待核实**; CC BY is the funder-compliant choice. CC BY-NC-ND is **no longer offered**.
- **Waivers/discounts:** **Full waiver** for corresponding authors in **low-income** countries and a **discount** for **middle-income** countries under OUP's low- and middle-income-countries initiative; discretionary waivers on request (openaccess@oup.com) at submission; **Read & Publish / transformative agreements** at participating institutions; a **membership-discount** option exists in the calculator (amount/eligibility 待核实). Changing the corresponding author to access Read & Publish funding is not permitted. **CONFIRMED** (mechanisms); specific amounts 待核实.
- **Funder compliance & copyright:** the journal states it "complies with all major funder mandates"; a CC BY route in a fully-OA journal satisfies Plan S/cOAlition S and CC-BY funder requirements (per-funder endorsements inferred, not individually quoted → 待核实). **Authors retain copyright.** **CONFIRMED** (general).

### Scope, positioning & process
- **Scope (verbatim):** "PNAS Nexus is a gold open access journal dedicated to publishing high-quality original research from across the biological, medical, physical, social, and political sciences, and engineering and mathematics, promoting collaboration across diverse fields." **CONFIRMED.**
- **Positioning:** **sibling / complementary** to PNAS, differentiated by **broader, cross-disciplinary scope and a broader editorial base** (three academies) — **not** by an explicitly stated "lower-tier" or "sound-science-only" quality bar. Any "sound-science only / not-top-1%" framing is **待核实** — the official pages do **not** use that language; do not assert it.
- **PNAS → PNAS Nexus transfer/cascade:** **CONFIRMED.** Manuscripts declined by PNAS may be offered transfer into the PNAS portfolio (incl. PNAS Nexus), and **prior PNAS reviews transfer with the manuscript**. Verbatim: "Any previous reviews of your PNAS submission will be transferred to PNAS Nexus alongside your manuscript, potentially reducing the number of reviews required." Note the qualifier: "Research Reports declined by PNAS for reasons other than soundness or technical merit may be recommended for transfer to PNAS Nexus." Transfer is not a guarantee of acceptance; PNAS Nexus assesses independently; author consent required.
- **Submission tracks:** **NO Contributed (NAS-member-communicated) track and NO Direct/prearranged-editor track.** **CONFIRMED.** All submissions undergo the same editor-assigned peer review: assigned first to an Editorial Board member, then to a handling editor from the Board of Reviewing Editors (or a guest editor), with **at least two independent expert reviews**.

### Article types & length (CONFIRMED, verbatim where quoted)
- **Research Report:** preferred **6 pages**, **maximum 12 pages**. "A standard 6-page article is approximately **4,000 words, 50 references, and 4 medium-size graphical elements** (i.e., figures and tables)."
- **Brief Report:** "limited to **3 pages**" (≈**1,600 words, 15 references**). (Official label is **"Brief Report,"** not "Brief Communication.")
- **Registered Report — Stage 1:** "should not exceed **3 pages**" (≈**1,600 words**). **Stage 2:** preferred 6 pages / 4,000 words, max 12 pages.
- **Perspective:** "up to a maximum of **10 pages**." **Review:** "up to a maximum of **10 pages**." Perspectives and Reviews may be submitted unsolicited.
- The page limit counts the **whole package** — abstract, significance statement, main text, figure captions, and table legends all count. A separate hard word cap for the 12-page maximum is not stated; a "6,000-word cap" seen in third-party guides is **待核实** (official limit is page-based).

### Front matter, structure & policy (CONFIRMED, verbatim where quoted)
- **Significance Statement:** required for **all Research Reports and Stage 2 Registered Reports**. Verbatim: "The maximum length is **120 words**, and minimum length is **50 words**." (Note the **50-word minimum** — distinct from flagship PNAS, which emphasizes only the ≤120 ceiling.) The flagship-PNAS audience phrasing ("undergraduate-educated scientist outside their field") is **待核实** for Nexus — write for a broad scientific audience.
- **Abstract:** verbatim "Abstracts have a maximum length of **250 words**. They should appear as a **single paragraph with no headings**."
- **Classification:** authors select from the **three major research-area categories** above, each with subcategories. Exact subcategory counts are **待核实** (do not state precise numbers).
- **Materials and Methods in the main text:** verbatim "The main text must contain a materials and methods section ... This information cannot be provided solely as supporting information." (Shared with flagship PNAS.)
- **Data & code availability (strict):** verbatim "Authors must make all materials, data, and associated protocols, including code and scripts, used in the analysis of the study available to readers in a **public repository upon publication**." "All data and any direct outputs from imaging systems must be retained in their **raw, unprocessed versions**." "Failure or refusal to provide data upon request may be grounds for **rejection of the manuscript or retraction of the article**." Data citations use the **`[dataset]`** tag (FORCE11 Data Citation Principles).
- **References:** verbatim "You may format references in any **readable style at submission**." (A key contrast with flagship PNAS, which enforces numbered-by-appearance up front.) The final/published numbered style is **待核实** — prepare a single consistent numbered style and confirm production requirements on acceptance.
- **Templates:** Word template and LaTeX (Overleaf) offered. **CONFIRMED.**
