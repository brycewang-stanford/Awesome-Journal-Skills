## Verified 2026-cycle facts used in the skills

- UIST 2026 is the 39th Annual ACM Symposium on User Interface Software and
  Technology, held November 2-5, 2026 at the GM Renaissance Center in Detroit,
  Michigan, USA.
- The Papers track ran on PCS with abstracts due March 24 and full papers due
  March 31, 2026 (11:59 pm AoE). Both deadlines have passed as of the 2026-07-08
  check; the live phases are notification aftermath and camera-ready.
- The 2026 review pipeline: rebuttal window May 28 - June 5 (5,000 characters,
  self-contained), decisions June 27, camera-ready July 24. Acceptances are
  conditional: final acceptance depends on delivering the changes promised in the
  rebuttal.
- Format: two-column `acmart` with `[sigconf,review,anonymous]` for review; 10-page
  standard and 5-page short limits excluding references and appendices; exceeding the
  limit is a desk reject; the camera-ready may grow by 10%.
- Video figures are optional but highly encouraged: at most 3 minutes, 1080p or 4K,
  MP4 encoded with H.264, and fully anonymized for review.
- Anonymization is "reasonable effort": strip author and institutional identity from
  the paper, the video, and all supplementary files; cite your own prior work in the
  third person with full references; attach an anonymized copy of any heavily
  overlapping prior submission as supplementary material.
- Camera-ready flows through ACM TAPS with accessible tagging (APTARA); alt text is
  required for every figure, subfigure, and table.
- Demos and Posters are separate adjunct tracks with their own chairs; the same work
  may not be submitted to both (a double submission is treated as a demo and dropped
  from Posters). Poster extended abstracts (2 pages) are archived in the ACM DL
  adjunct proceedings.
- UIST 2025 (38th edition) took place in Busan, Republic of Korea, September 28 -
  October 1, 2025; its main and adjunct proceedings are DOI 10.1145/3746059 and
  10.1145/3746058.
- UIST has no standing editor-in-chief; general, program, and track chairs are
  appointed per edition and rotate (source 4 lists the 2026 committee).
