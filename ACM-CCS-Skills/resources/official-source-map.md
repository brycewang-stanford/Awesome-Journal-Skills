# Official Source Map - ACM CCS

Access date: 2026-07-08

This map records the official sources used for CCS-specific facts in this pack. CCS policies
are cycle-specific and set separately for each of the two annual review cycles; reopen the
current Call for Papers, the cycle's HotCRP site, the call for artifacts, the ACM `sigconf`
template, and the SIGSAC pages before giving submission-ready advice.

## Access method note

Direct fetches of `www.sigsac.org` were blocked (HTTP 403) in the verification environment on
2026-07-08. Official pages were therefore read via search-engine renderings of the exact
official URLs and cross-checked against the CCS HotCRP cycle sites, the ACM Digital Library
CCS proceedings, dblp, and organizer announcements. Any fact that could not be confirmed this
way is marked 待核实 below and phrased as a question, never as an established fact.

## Verified 2026 facts used in skills

- CCS 2026 is held in The Hague, Netherlands, November 15-19, 2026.
- CCS 2026 used HotCRP with two review cycles. Cycle A: abstract registration Jan 7, full
  paper Jan 14, rebuttal Mar 17-20, notification Apr 9, 2026. Cycle B: abstract registration
  Apr 22, full paper Apr 29, rebuttal Jun 29-Jul 1, notification Jul 17, 2026. All AoE.
- Abstract registration is mandatory and precedes the full-paper deadline by one week each
  cycle; no abstract, no paper.
- Submissions are anonymized: self-cite in the third person, blind references only where
  third-person phrasing is infeasible; improper anonymization risks rejection without review.
- Main text is limited to 12 pages in the unaltered ACM `sigconf` two-column template,
  excluding bibliography, well-marked appendices, and supplementary material. Template
  tampering (whitespace/margin edits) is forbidden.
- Work touching human subjects, user data, or real-world vulnerabilities must include an
  ethics considerations section, placed (in 2026) as an appendix after the body and
  bibliography; IRB/ERB approval is neither necessary nor sufficient on its own.
- Decisions include accept, minor revision (revise within the same cycle), and reject; a
  first-cycle reject may not be resubmitted the same year.
- Authors may submit at most 7 papers per cycle (2026).
- Artifact evaluation is optional and post-acceptance; ACM badges (Artifacts Available,
  Artifacts Evaluated - Functional, Artifacts Evaluated - Reusable, Results Reproduced)
  appear on the paper's first page.
- Camera-ready reported due Aug 21, 2026; accepted papers publish in the ACM Digital Library.
