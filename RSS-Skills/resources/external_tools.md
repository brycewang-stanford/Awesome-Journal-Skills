# External Tools - RSS

Access date: 2026-07-08

Open these surfaces after confirming which RSS edition is live. `roboticsconference.org`
is rebound to the newest edition each cycle, so a URL that showed 2026 content in July
2026 will show 2027 content later — check the edition banner before trusting any date.

## Official workflow surfaces

- Current-edition site (rebinds each cycle): https://roboticsconference.org/
- Call for Papers: https://roboticsconference.org/information/cfp/
- Review-process description: https://roboticsconference.org/reviewps/
- OpenReview venue group (2026): https://openreview.net/group?id=roboticsfoundation.org/RSS/2026/Conference
- Free open-access proceedings archive: https://www.roboticsproceedings.org/
- RSS Foundation (awards, policies, calendar): https://roboticsfoundation.org/
- dblp cross-check index: https://dblp.org/db/conf/rss/index.html

## Five author-side verification passes

1. **Edition pass** — confirm which year the site is currently serving, the host city,
   the conference dates, and whether the deadlines shown belong to the cycle you are
   planning for.
2. **Format pass** — reconfirm the page ceiling (8 pages excluding references in 2026),
   the template, PDF-only main-file rule, and the self-containment requirement before
   deciding what moves to the supplement.
3. **Anonymity pass** — double-blind held in 2026; sweep text, footage, supplementary
   archives, and OpenReview metadata for identity leaks, and remember external links
   are welcome only in the camera-ready.
4. **Review-mechanics pass** — verify the two-stage design is still in force: whether a
   rebuttal exists, who is invited to write one, its length cap, and its deadline.
5. **Post-decision pass** — camera-ready deadline (never publicly surfaced for 2026;
   take it from the acceptance email), registration and presentation obligations, and
   the proceedings-publication timeline on roboticsproceedings.org.

## Do not infer

- Do not project RSS 2026 dates onto RSS 2027; nothing for 2027 was posted at check
  time.
- Do not treat the 8-page ceiling as an RSS invariant — the 2021 and 2022 editions ran
  with no page limit at all, so this policy demonstrably moves.
- Do not assume every submission gets a rebuttal; in 2026 only papers above a
  first-round threshold were invited to write one.
- Do not assume a demo-paper track exists; the 2026 CFP said there is no separate demo
  category even though notifications mention demos.
- If sources disagree, rank them: current CFP and review-process page, then OpenReview
  venue instructions, then chair email, then everything else — and record the conflict
  in your notes.
