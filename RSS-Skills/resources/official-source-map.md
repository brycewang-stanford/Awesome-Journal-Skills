Access date: 2026-07-08

## Verified 2026-cycle facts used in the skills

- RSS 2026 is the 22nd edition, held July 13-17, 2026 in Sydney, Australia (UTS + ICC
  Sydney) — i.e., **conference week begins five days after this pack's check date**.
- The 2026 submission cycle is closed: abstracts January 23, papers January 30,
  supplementary February 6, invited rebuttals April 3, notifications April 27 — all
  2026, all AoE.
- Submissions ran on OpenReview and were double-blind.
- Page policy: at most 8 pages excluding references, explicitly framed as a ceiling
  rather than a floor; the main PDF must be self-contained, with appendices demoted to
  supplementary material.
- Review is two-stage: only papers above a first-round threshold are invited to submit
  a one-page rebuttal; the Area Chair reads it and final decisions come out of an AC
  meeting.
- RSS 2026 has no separate demo-paper category; external links are welcome only in the
  camera-ready version.
- Proceedings appear free and open-access at roboticsproceedings.org with
  `10.15607/RSS.*` DOIs; there is no APC anywhere in the pipeline. The cost model is
  conference registration.
- RSS is single-track: every accepted paper is presented to the whole audience, which
  is the structural reason for its selectivity and its science-first evaluation bar.
- RSS has no standing editor-in-chief; the rotating analogue is the per-edition
  Program Chair (2026: Matei Ciocarlie) and Area Chairs appointed under the RSS
  Foundation.
