# Annual Review of Psychology Delivery Checklist

Use this before delivering a **commissioned** ARPsych review to Annual Reviews. Re-check
volatile items against the official author pages (检索于 2026-06；以官网为准).

1. **Invited, not cold.** This is an Editorial-Committee-commissioned article delivered in the invited workflow — not an unsolicited submission.
2. **Organizing question.** The review answers one organizing question, not "research on X" (see `arpsych-topic-selection`).
3. **Framework.** A single analytical spine organizes the literature; no annotated bibliography (see `arpsych-organizing-framework`).
4. **Coverage.** Comprehensive within the frame and current to the frontier; search protocol documented (see `arpsych-literature-synthesis`).
5. **Balance.** Even-handed across labs, paradigms, and rival theories; evidence weighed by credibility, not vote-counted; self-citation proportional (see `arpsych-comprehensiveness-and-balance`).
6. **Replication hygiene.** Non-replicating effects flagged, not reviewed as settled.
7. **Exhibits.** One conceptual framework figure makes the spine visible; synthesis table compresses the evidence; permissions secured for reproduced exhibits (see `arpsych-tables-figures`).
8. **Voice.** Authoritative yet accessible to an adjacent psychologist; abstract and intro written last; substantive future-directions close (see `arpsych-writing-style`).
9. **Transparency.** Search documented; any meta-analysis reproducible with deposited data/code (OSF DOI) (see `arpsych-transparency-and-reproducibility`).
10. **Length.** Within the length assigned in the invitation letter, figures and tables included.
11. **Prep & references.** Double-spaced, 12-point; references complete and accurate (house style applied at production).
12. **Declarations.** Conflict-of-interest / potential-bias disclosure and funding prepared; AI not listed as an author.
13. **Final pass.** The next action is delivery, not another uncontrolled rewrite.
