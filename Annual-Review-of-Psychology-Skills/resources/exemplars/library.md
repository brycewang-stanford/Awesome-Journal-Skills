record (title, author, volume, year). Re-confirm on the official DOI page before citing, and
never import a sibling-journal paper as an ARPsych exemplar (see the guardrail rows).

> Use these to study *form*: each one imposes an organizing idea, covers rival accounts, and
> closes forward — they are not annotated bibliographies. When citing as examples, re-verify
> the volume/page/DOI on the live Annual Reviews record.

## Sibling-journal guardrails (do NOT cite these as ARPsych exemplars)

- Baumeister et al., "Ego Depletion" (1998) — *J. Personality and Social Psychology*, a **primary** journal, not ARPsych.
- Diener, "Subjective Well-Being" (1984) — *Psychological Bulletin*, a **submitted-review** venue, not ARPsych.
- Any *Annual Review of Clinical Psychology* article — the **clinical sister** series, distinct from ARPsych.
