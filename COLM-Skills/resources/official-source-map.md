Access date: 2026-07-08

This map records the official sources behind every COLM-specific fact in this pack.

being written edition by edition. Reopen the current `colmweb.org` Call for Papers,
key-dates page, submission instructions, author guide, and FAQ before giving any

## Verified 2026-cycle facts used in the skills

- COLM 2026 is the third edition of the Conference on Language Modeling, held at the
  Hilton Union Square, San Francisco, USA, October 6-9, 2026 (main conference
  October 6-8, workshops October 9).
- The 2026 main-track timeline: abstract deadline March 26; full paper March 31;
  reviews released May 22; author rebuttal May 22 - June 8; decisions July 8;
  camera-ready August 7. **On the pack's access date (2026-07-08), the cycle sits at
  decision day**: the rebuttal window has closed and camera-ready work begins now.
- Submissions run entirely on OpenReview
  (`colmweb.org/COLM/2026/Conference`); the venue opened for submissions in
  February 2026. COLM papers are published openly on OpenReview.
- Format: strict 9-page limit on main text; unlimited additional pages for
  citations; an optional acknowledgments section (at most 1 page) sits outside the
  limit; review is double-blind, so submissions carry no acknowledgments and no
  identity-revealing links.
- Every author needs an up-to-date OpenReview profile, and all authors must
  explicitly acknowledge the COLM Code of Ethics at submission.
- Reciprocal reviewing has per-submission and per-reviewer components; authors with
  4+ submissions join the reviewer pool automatically; distinct submissions may not
  reuse the same reciprocal reviewer except as a last resort.
- The 2026 LLM-usage policy (adopted from ICLR 2026, relaxed for minor assistance)
  requires disclosure of LLM-originated ideas, LLM-written content, LLM-generated
  data/plots, and LLM-based evaluation — and discloses all LLM use in reviewing.
- Editions to date: 2024 Philadelphia (University of Pennsylvania), 2025 Montreal,
  2026 San Francisco. Each of the first two editions gave four Outstanding Paper
  Awards.
- COLM was announced publicly in October 2023 as a venue dedicated to the theory,
  practice, and applications of language models; the founding motivation, per the
  official site, is that LM technology raises research questions distinct from the
  typical focus of adjacent venues.
