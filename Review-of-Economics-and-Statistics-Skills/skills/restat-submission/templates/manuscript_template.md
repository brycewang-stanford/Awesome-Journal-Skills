# REStat Manuscript Skeleton (applied economics / applied econometrics)

A neutral starting skeleton for a *The Review of Economics and Statistics* (REStat)
manuscript. REStat is empirical-first: lead with the question and the estimate,
right-size theory, and make measurement and identification legible. Format rules
(12pt, double-spaced throughout, abstract ≤100 words, online appendix ≤20pp) are
volatile — verify on the official MIT Press REStat submission guidelines
before upload.

---

**Title**

**Authors & affiliations**  ·  **JEL codes**  ·  **Keywords**

**Abstract** (≤100 words — state the question, the design, and the finding with a number)

## 1. Introduction
- Question + headline estimate (units + standard error) in the first paragraph
- Why it is hard: the identification or measurement obstacle
- Approach: data + design (DID / RD / IV / shift-share) + measurement credibility
- Result restated with interpretation and uncertainty
- Contribution as a delta against named prior work, scope bounded
- Brief roadmap

## 2. Setting / Data / Measurement
- Institutional setting; data sources; construction and validity of key measures
- Measurement-error treatment where relevant

## 3. Empirical Strategy (Identification)
- The estimand and the design that identifies it; assumptions stated and probed
- Diagnostics (pre-trends / density / first-stage / share-or-shock exogeneity)

## 4. (Optional) Model
- Minimal theory to define the estimand or interpret the coefficient (right-sized)

## 5. Results
- One legible main exhibit; SEs in parentheses; clustering stated

## 6. Robustness
- Specification / sample / measurement / alternative-estimator / inference checks

## 7. Mechanisms / Interpretation / Counterfactual (as applicable)

## 8. Conclusion
- What was learned; scope and limits; what it implies for the applied literature
