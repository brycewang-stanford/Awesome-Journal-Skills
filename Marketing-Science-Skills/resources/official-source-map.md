## VERIFIED facts injected into the skills (with source #)

- **Publisher / society:** published by INFORMS (Institute for Operations Research and the Management Sciences); flagship journal of the INFORMS Society for Marketing Science (ISMS). (#1)
- **Scope:** empirical and theoretical quantitative research in marketing — pricing, advertising/digital, branding, channels/retail, model-tied consumer behavior, marketing strategy, analytics/ML on marketing problems. (#1, #3)
- **Dominant methodology:** mathematical/quantitative modeling — the journal "focuses primarily on articles that answer important research questions in marketing using mathematical modeling," with structural econometric and analytical (game-theoretic) modeling as the genres most associated with it; econometric/statistical estimation, ML tools, surveys, and experiments are welcome when rigorously tied to formal models. (#3)
- **Editor-in-Chief:** Puneet Manchanda (University of Michigan, Ross School of Business); term Jan 1, 2025 – Dec 31, 2027, renewable through Dec 31, 2030; succeeded Olivier Toubia. Re-verified current 2026-06-22 (INFORMS ORMS-Today appointment notice + Manchanda CV/Michigan Ross page). (#2)
- **Editorial structure:** Editor-in-Chief oversees Senior Editors who handle assigned papers with Associate Editors — a Senior-Editor/Associate-Editor routing model rather than Management Science's standing departmental Area-Editor structure. (#12)
- **Review model:** double-anonymous (double-blind) for regular submissions, per INFORMS' organization-wide double-anonymous mandate; authors must blind the manuscript (remove names, institutions, acknowledgments). A single-blind process applies to Practice Paper / Science-to-Practice tracks. (#4, #13)
- **Submission system:** ScholarOne Manuscripts at mc.manuscriptcentral.com/mksc. (#5)
- **Abstract & keywords:** abstract not more than 200 words (first page of the manuscript); 3–6 keywords describing theoretical and methodological orientation. (#4)
- **Formatting:** INFORMS author style; author-year in-text citations, e.g., (Norman 1977) or Norman (1977); alphabetical reference list in INFORMS style; double-spaced, single column, 11- or 12-pt font, one-inch margins; prepared in MS Word or LaTeX (INFORMS LaTeX style files + Style-Instructions.pdf on the Author Portal); uploaded as Word or PDF. (#4, #11)
- **Length:** regular (Original Article) submissions have no fixed page/word limit at initial submission but are expected to be written succinctly; the Frontiers in Marketing Science section is strictly capped at 6,000 words for the entire paper (including references, tables, graphs, appendices). (#4, #8)
- **Frontiers section:** Science-like, shorter, faster-review track requiring a major contribution on ONE primary dimension (methodological, modeling, or substantive) with relaxed thresholds on the others — "different, but equal" to regular articles. (#8, #9)
- **Replication policy:** Marketing Science Replication and Disclosure Policy — on acceptance, authors submit data and estimation code sufficient for a peer to reproduce the essential content; for licensed data (Census, Compustat, CRSP) provide access instructions and linking code. Field-leading policy that seeded similar policies across INFORMS journals. (#6, #7)
- **Fees:** no mandatory submission or publication fee for standard subscription publication; optional INFORMS Open Option (gold OA) is a one-time US$3,000 fee charged only after acceptance and before publication; choosing it does not affect peer review. (#10)
- **Cover letter & self-overlap:** cover letter is visible to the Editor/AE but not reviewers; authors may suggest 2 preferred Associate Editors and 3 preferred reviewers; if the submission builds on the authors' own published or under-review work, they must cite it and state how the contribution goes beyond it. (#4)
- **Specialized tracks:** Database papers (built around a novel dataset); Practice Papers / Practice Prize (500–1,000-word Impact Statement, single-blind); Science-to-Practice submissions — reflecting INFORMS' analytics/practice orientation. (#13, #14)
