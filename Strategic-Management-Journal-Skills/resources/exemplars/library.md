# SMJ Exemplars Library (method × theme)

> **Verified via web search, access date 2026-06-07.** Every paper below was checked to confirm it
> actually appeared in the **Strategic Management Journal** specifically (publisher: Wiley for the
> Strategic Management Society; article pages on `sms.onlinelibrary.wiley.com` / `onlinelibrary.wiley.com`
> with `smj` DOI, cross-checked against RePEc `bla:stratm`). Papers that could not be fully verified as
> SMJ — or that turned out to be in a **sibling venue** — were **omitted** or listed only as guardrails.
> It is deliberately a short, clean list rather than a long, uncertain one.
>
> **Sibling-confusion guard.** SMJ is *not* AMJ/AMR, Administrative Science Quarterly, Organization
> Science, Journal of Management, the Strategic Entrepreneurship Journal (SEJ), or the Global Strategy
> Journal (GSJ). Two famous strategy papers that scholars routinely *misattribute* to SMJ are documented
> at the bottom as a do-not-misattribute list.
>
> **Use principle (zero hallucination):** this file gives **design positioning only**. It does not
> reproduce or invent coefficients or specific findings — read the original on Wiley before citing any
> number. For SMJ-specific scope, dual-abstract policy, and the verified-landmark list, see
> [`../official-source-map.md`](../official-source-map.md).

## How to use this library

Pick the row whose **method × question** is closest to yours, then study how that paper makes a finding
land as a contribution to **strategy theory** — a change in how scholars understand firm performance or
competitive advantage — which is the SMJ bar (see
[`../../skills/smj-contribution-framing/SKILL.md`](../../skills/smj-contribution-framing/SKILL.md) and
[`../../skills/smj-literature-positioning/SKILL.md`](../../skills/smj-literature-positioning/SKILL.md)).
For each, ask the self-check question before claiming your paper is "SMJ-shaped."

## By method

### Foundational theory that reframes the firm (conceptual)

- **Wernerfelt, B., "A Resource-Based View of the Firm," SMJ 1984, 5(2):171–180.**
  Verified: `sms.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1002/smj.4250050207` (DOI 10.1002/smj.4250050207).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** names and launches the resource-based view by reframing the firm from the
    *resource* side rather than the product side — a short conceptual piece whose single reframing
    reorganized the field. The canonical "small idea, enormous lesson" SMJ paper.
  - **Self-check:** can your core construct be re-specified so the theory now predicts something the prior
    framing did not?

### Conceptual framework that opens a new construct (theory development)

- **Teece, D. J., Pisano, G., & Shuen, A., "Dynamic Capabilities and Strategic Management," SMJ 1997, 18(7):509–533.**
  Verified: `sms.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1002/(SICI)1097-0266(199708)18:7<509::AID-SMJ882>3.0.CO;2-Z`.
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** introduces *dynamic capabilities* to explain advantage under rapid
    technological change, giving the field a new construct rather than a new estimate — the template for a
    theory-development contribution. Anchor for
    [`../../skills/smj-theory-development/SKILL.md`](../../skills/smj-theory-development/SKILL.md).
  - **Self-check:** does your construct do explanatory work the existing vocabulary cannot, or is it a
    relabel?

### Variance decomposition — industry vs. firm effects (econometric)

- **Rumelt, R. P., "How Much Does Industry Matter?," SMJ 1991, 12(3):167–185.**
  Verified: `sms.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1002/smj.4250120302` (RePEc `bla:stratm:v:12:y:1991:i:3`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** partitions the variance in business-unit returns into industry, corporate,
    and business-specific effects and shows business-specific effects dominate — a *methodological move
    that settles a substantive question* the whole field argued about (do firms or industries drive
    profitability?).
  - **Self-check:** does your decomposition adjudicate a debate the field already has, rather than report a
    number no one disputes?

- **McGahan, A. M., & Porter, M. E., "How Much Does Industry Matter, Really?," SMJ 1997, Summer Special Issue 18(S1):15–30.**
  Verified: `sms.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/pdf/10.1002/(SICI)1097-0266(199707)18:1+<15::AID-SMJ916>3.0.CO;2-1` (RePEc `bla:stratm:v:18:y:1997:i:s1:p:15-30`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** revisits Rumelt's decomposition on broader data and across sectors, showing
    the firm-vs-industry balance is contingent on the sample — a model of how a *replication-plus* paper
    earns SMJ space by changing what the field believes, not just re-running a regression.
  - **Self-check:** if your paper extends a prior result, does the extension change a belief (a boundary, a
    contingency), or merely confirm it?

### Firm-effects panel grounded in RBV (empirical, fixed effects)

- **Henderson, R., & Cockburn, I., "Measuring Competence? Exploring Firm Effects in Pharmaceutical Research," SMJ 1994, 15(S1):63–84.**
  Verified: `sms.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1002/smj.4250150906` (RePEc `bla:stratm:v:15:y:1994:i:s1:p:63-84`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** operationalizes the slippery RBV notion of "competence" as estimable firm
    effects in pharmaceutical research productivity, separating *component* from *architectural*
    competence — turning a theoretical construct into a measured, performance-relevant object.
  - **Self-check:** can you measure your resource/capability cleanly enough that its performance effect is
    credible, not assumed?

### Learning-curve / event-study on alliances (empirical, abnormal returns)

- **Anand, B. N., & Khanna, T., "Do Firms Learn to Create Value? The Case of Alliances," SMJ 2000, 21(3):295–315.**
  Verified: `onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1002/(SICI)1097-0266(200003)21:3<295::AID-SMJ91>3.0.CO;2-O` (RePEc `bla:stratm:v:21:y:2000:i:3`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** measures value creation via abnormal stock returns around alliance
    announcements and shows learning effects appear for joint ventures but not licensing — a finding that
    *bounds* the "firms learn to manage alliances" claim by contract type. Pairs with the event-study
    template in [`../code/`](../code/).
  - **Self-check:** does your design locate *where* an effect holds and where it does not, rather than
    assert a blanket effect?

### Formal / simulation model of performance heterogeneity (theory + computation)

- **Zott, C., "Dynamic Capabilities and the Emergence of Intraindustry Differential Firm Performance: Insights from a Simulation Study," SMJ 2003, 24(2):97–125.**
  Verified: `sms.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1002/smj.288` (RePEc `bla:stratm:v:24:y:2003:i:2:p:97-125`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** uses a formal simulation to show that small differences in the timing, cost,
    and learning of resource deployment generate robust performance gaps among otherwise-similar firms —
    SMJ does publish formal/computational work when the lesson about advantage is first-order.
  - **Self-check:** does your model deliver one memorable, mechanism-level result about performance
    heterogeneity, not just a calibration?

### Quasi-experimental design from an exogenous demand shock (causal identification)

- **Lim, N., Kim, S., & Agarwal, R., "Weathering a Demand Shock: The Impact of Prior Vertical Scope on Post-Shock Firm Response," SMJ 2023, 44(8):1965–2004.**
  Verified: `onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1002/smj.3478` (DOI 10.1002/smj.3478; RePEc `bla:stratm:v:44:y:2023:i:8:p:1965-2004`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** uses a Medicare-reform demand shock in U.S. medical diagnostic imaging and a
    difference-in-difference-in-difference design to show integrated firms exit more than non-integrated
    firms — a contemporary template for credible causal identification of how *prior strategic choices*
    (vertical scope) shape shock response. Pairs with [`../code/stata/03_did_modern.do`](../code/stata/03_did_modern.do).
  - **Self-check:** does your shock provide exogenous variation that rules out the selection story a referee
    raises first, and does the design connect to a *strategy* construct (scope, capability), not a generic
    treatment?

## Omitted for sibling-venue or lack of full verification (do NOT attribute to SMJ)

To keep the list zero-hallucination, the following frequently-cited strategy papers were **excluded**
after checking — they belong to *sibling* venues, exactly the misattribution this pack warns about:

- **Cohen, W. M., & Levinthal, D. A., "Absorptive Capacity: A New Perspective on Learning and
  Innovation" (1990)** — verified to be in **Administrative Science Quarterly** 35(1):128–152, **not
  SMJ**. Listed here only as a guardrail.
- **Rothaermel, F. T., & Hess, A. M., "Building Dynamic Capabilities: Innovation Driven by Individual-,
  Firm-, and Network-Level Effects" (2007)** — verified to be in **Organization Science** 18(6):898–921,
  **not SMJ**. Listed here only as a guardrail.
- **Barney, J. B., "Firm Resources and Sustained Competitive Advantage" (1991)** — the canonical RBV
  statement, but published in the **Journal of Management**, **not SMJ** (see
  [`../official-source-map.md`](../official-source-map.md)). Use Wernerfelt (1984) as the verified SMJ RBV
  anchor.

Before adding any new paper to this library, confirm it on a Wiley SMJ article page (`smj` DOI,
`sms.onlinelibrary.wiley.com`) cross-checked with RePEc `bla:stratm`, and update the access-date header.
When in doubt, omit.
