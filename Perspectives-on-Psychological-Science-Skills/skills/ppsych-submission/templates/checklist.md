# Perspectives on Psychological Science — Submission Checklist

Re-confirm every volatile item on the SAGE/APS author pages before relying on it (检索于 2026-06；以官网为准).

1. **Venue fit.** The piece is a broad integrative review, theoretical/conceptual statement, or meta-science/methodology commentary — not a single empirical study (→ *Psychological Science*) or a routine meta-analysis (→ *Psychological Bulletin*), unless it carries a distinct superordinate theoretical message.
2. **Contribution type & proposal.** Category identified (stand-alone / cooperative / miscellaneous); a proposal was sent where required (cooperative + miscellaneous). A positive proposal reply is not an acceptance.
3. **Organizing argument.** The piece has a real spine (taxonomy / unifying theory / reframe), not an annotated bibliography; the one-sentence claim about the field is stated.
4. **Comprehensiveness & balance.** Coverage spans the relevant sub-areas (saturation log); rival camps are steelmanned; self-citation and any reform claims are calibrated to evidence.
5. **Abstract.** ≤ 200 words, perspective-style (the contribution to the reader), not a single finding.
6. **Style & length.** APA references/statistics/exhibits; concise (no strict page limit for stand-alone; brief reports 1,000–1,500 words).
7. **Open science.** Any original data deposited in a repository and cited; meta-science protocol/coding deposited and ideally preregistered, with reproducible figures; deposits mapped to TOP / Open-Practice expectations.
8. **Declarations.** Cover letter confirms ethical data collection; conflict-of-interest and funding disclosed; AI not an author and AI use disclosed.
9. **Suggested reviewers.** ≥5 objective reviewers across the integrated areas and rival camps, excluding mentors, colleagues, and collaborators.
10. **Sources.** Current process facts are backed by `resources/official-source-map.md` or marked 待核实.
11. **Final pass.** The next action is submission at http://mc.manuscriptcentral.com/pps, not another uncontrolled rewrite.
