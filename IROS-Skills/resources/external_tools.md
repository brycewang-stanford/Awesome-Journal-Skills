# External Tools - IROS

Access date: 2026-07-09

Open these surfaces after reopening the current IROS cycle pages. IROS rules are reset by a
new co-sponsoring committee every year, and 2026 shows why: the review model presents as
double-anonymous and the RA-L combined-submission option is gone, both departures from
older IROS habits. Verify before trusting any number below.

## Five author-side verification passes

Run these in order; each is a distinct failure surface, not a restatement of the last.

1. **Geometry pass.** Confirm the current content-page count and whether references and
   appendices sit inside the limit (IROS counts them in; do not import an ML-venue "8 pages
   excluding references" assumption). Check the extra-page charge, if any.
2. **Anonymity pass.** For the 2026 double-anonymous cycle: no author block, no
   affiliations, no grant numbers, no "our prior platform [x]" tells in text, figures, or
   the video. Keep the full author list only in PaperPlaza metadata.
3. **Video pass.** Verify the length cap (60 s in 2026), the size cap (10 MB), the accepted
   containers (MPG/MPEG/MP4), and the separate video deadline — it does not ride on the
   paper deadline.
4. **Evidence pass.** Confirm hardware, task distribution, trial counts, reset procedure,
   success criteria, failure cases, and any sim-to-real gap are inspectable in the body,
   since IROS has no supplementary PDF to hide them in.
5. **Timing pass.** Convert every deadline to your local zone from the CFP's stated zone
   (do not assume AoE), and note that the video and camera-ready deadlines are offset from
   the paper deadline.

## Do not infer

- Do not carry an IROS date, page rule, video spec, or review model forward from a previous
  cycle; the 2025-to-2026 changes prove they move.
- Do not assume a rebuttal phase exists — IROS has historically issued reviews with the
  decision and no author response window.
- Do not treat the RA-L presentation pathway as a submission channel into IROS; in the 2026
  cycle it is a transfer of an already-published journal paper, not a joint submission.
- If official pages disagree (as the March-2 vs April-30 deadline mention shows), use the
  current year-site CFP and important-dates page as controlling, and record the conflict.
