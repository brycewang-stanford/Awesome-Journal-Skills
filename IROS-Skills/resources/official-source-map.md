# Official Source Map - IROS

Access date: 2026-07-09

This map records the official sources behind every IROS-specific fact in this pack.
IROS is re-organized each year by a fresh committee under two co-sponsors — the IEEE
Robotics and Automation Society (RAS) and the Robotics Society of Japan (RSJ) — so its
deadlines, page rules, review model, and video specs are reset per edition. Reopen the
current year-site (`<year>.ieee-iros.org`), its call-for-papers and important-dates
pages, and the PaperPlaza portal before giving submission-ready advice.

**Access-method note (2026-07-09):** direct fetches of `2026.ieee-iros.org` returned
HTTP 403 from the verification environment's network gateway. Official-page contents were
therefore read through search-engine renderings of the exact official URLs below, and
cross-checked against IEEE RAS pages (`ieee-ras.org`), the RSJ co-sponsorship listing,
the shared PaperPlaza portal, and independent conference-tracking mirrors. Before relying
on any deadline, cap, fee, or policy, open the primary URLs below directly.

## Verified 2026-cycle facts used in the skills

- IROS 2026 is the 2026 IEEE/RSJ International Conference on Intelligent Robots and
  Systems, in Pittsburgh, PA at the David L. Lawrence Convention Center, in the fall slot
  (September 27 - October 1, 2026). IROS is the fall IEEE-RAS mega-venue, paired with ICRA
  in the spring.
- Submission runs on PaperPlaza (`ras.papercept.net`), the shared RAS portal; the initial
  PDF is capped at 6 MB.
- The 2026 cycle review model is **double-anonymous** — author names and affiliations are
  removed from the manuscript, with the author list held in PaperPlaza metadata.
- The optional video attachment is **1 minute, up to 10 MB, MPG/MPEG/MP4**, with its own
  deadline (March 5, 2026) a few days after the paper deadline.
- Timetable: paper March 2, 2026; video March 5, 2026; notification June 16, 2026;
  camera-ready July 10, 2026 — meaning that as of the 2026-07-09 access date, the live
  obligation for accepted authors is the camera-ready and registration, not submission.
- IROS proceedings are published in IEEE Xplore; publication requires the IEEE electronic
  copyright form.
- IROS 2026 does not offer a combined "RA-L + IROS" submission option; instead, already
  published RA-L (and T-RO, T-ASE, RAM) papers are eligible for presentation at IROS via a
  transfer/eligibility window. This is the current presentation pathway.
- Like every conference in this collection, IROS has no standing editor-in-chief; review is
  run per-edition through the RAS Associate-Editor / Editor structure inside PaperPlaza,
  and the co-sponsoring committees rotate annually between regions.
