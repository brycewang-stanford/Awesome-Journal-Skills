# Source basis - CS/AI conference breadth bundle

The pack uses stable official source anchors plus a conservative re-check rule. For high-priority AI venues, official current-cycle pages were consulted or selected as first-pass anchors on 2026-06-01; for the full 155-conference roster, each skill points to the conference or society home page so future agents can verify the current cycle.

Do not turn this file into a list of hard-coded annual facts. Conference policy changes are frequent: OpenReview/ARR/CMT/PCS workflows, page limits, AI-use rules, artifact badges, ethics forms, anonymity exceptions, registration obligations, and camera-ready release forms may all change by year.

## Conference governance framing (verified 2026-06-22)

Every venue in this bundle is a CONFERENCE, not a journal. Two cross-cutting facts hold across the whole roster and should never be replaced with journal assumptions:

- **No standing editor-in-chief.** A conference's rotating leadership is its per-edition Program Committee Chairs / Program Chairs (and, at larger venues, a General Chair). These appointments change every year, so this bundle deliberately names no single organizer — for any real submission, open the current-year CFP / committees page to find who is chairing this cycle.
- **No article-processing charge (APC).** Conferences charge registration fees, not per-article publication fees; at least one author registers and presents. Proceedings are published open-access by a society or publisher (e.g., PMLR for ICML/AISTATS, OpenReview for ICLR/NeurIPS, ACM/IEEE Digital Libraries for many others). A handful of venues add narrow extra-page or submission-volume fees (e.g., IJCAI's Primary Paper Initiative), but those are not journal APCs — verify the specific venue's cost model on its current registration page.
