# External Tools & Resources for Mind-Style Analytic Philosophy

Reference works, bibliographic sources, and software useful when preparing a *Mind* submission. Mind
is an **analytic-philosophy** journal: a submission is a **work of argument**, not of data. There are
no datasets, no statistics, and no archives in the empirical sense — the "instruments" here are
careful reading, precise concepts, and valid arguments. Match the tools below to that task, and verify
any Mind-specific policy in [`official-source-map.md`](official-source-map.md).

## 1. Reference works & encyclopedias (for definitions, debates, state of the art)

| Source | Provider | Typical use |
|--------|----------|-------------|
| Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy (SEP) | Stanford / Metaphysics Research Lab | Authoritative survey of a debate, canonical formulations, bibliographies |
| Internet Encyclopedia of Philosophy (IEP) | peer-reviewed, open | Second opinion on a topic's framing and history |
| Routledge Encyclopedia of Philosophy (REP) | Routledge | Entries on figures, schools, and technical terms |
| Oxford Bibliographies in Philosophy | Oxford University Press | Curated, annotated reading lists by topic |

## 2. Bibliographic search & literature discovery

| Source | Provider | Typical use |
|--------|----------|-------------|
| PhilPapers | PhilPapers Foundation | The discipline's index; find the closest prior papers, set up alerts |
| PhilArchive | PhilPapers Foundation | Open-access preprints and self-archived philosophy papers |
| JSTOR | ITHAKA | Back-run of journal articles, incl. Mind's archive |
| Google Scholar | Google | Citation chasing (who cites / is cited by a key paper) |
| Mind on Oxford Academic | OUP / Mind Association | What Mind has recently published in your area; house expectations |

## 3. Subfields Mind covers (orient your reading)

- **Epistemology** — knowledge, justification, evidence, disagreement, formal epistemology.
- **Metaphysics** — modality, time, causation, properties, grounding, personal identity.
- **Philosophy of language** — meaning, reference, context-sensitivity, speech acts, vagueness.
- **Philosophy of logic** — consequence, paradox, truth, non-classical logics, the philosophy of math.
- **Philosophy of mind** — consciousness, intentionality, mental content, perception, action.
- **Ethics & metaethics, history of philosophy, and interdisciplinary work** are also welcome —
  Mind excludes **no area, no style, and no school**; quality is the sole criterion.

## 4. Logic, formal methods & argument checking

- Proof assistants / checkers for formal results: Lean, Coq, Isabelle (when a result must be airtight)
- Logic teaching/working tools: Carnap, ProofTools, truth-table and model-builder utilities
- Modal/relevance/non-classical logic references when the argument turns on a logic other than classical
- Argument mapping: structure premises and inferences explicitly (e.g., outline tools, Argdown-style maps)

## 5. Writing, references & the MIND house style

- Reference managers: Zotero, BibTeX/BibLaTeX, EndNote — but **do not rely on a generic style**: Mind
  applies its own **MIND house style** (download the **MIND Stylesheet**), required for the final
  accepted version. Keep a single, consistent citation style from the start to ease conversion.
- Typesetting: LaTeX (TeX Live / MacTeX, Overleaf) or Word. For LaTeX, use the `lineno` package to add
  the **line numbers Mind requires for review**; in Word, enable line numbering in page setup.
- Prepare the manuscript **for anonymous review**: remove revealing acknowledgements and neutralize
  self-identifying references; strip identifying file metadata (document properties).
- Abstract: the published abstract is **50–200 words**; a longer submission abstract (up to ~250 words,
  待核实) may accompany the manuscript to aid referees.

## 6. Process & portal

| Item | Note |
|------|------|
| Submission portal | **ScholarOne** (`mc.manuscriptcentral.com/MIND`) — Word or PDF + high-res images |
| Owner / publisher | **Mind Association** / **Oxford University Press** |
| Review model | **Triple-anonymous** — author identity hidden from editors and referees until acceptance |
| Article length | **~8,000 words total**; longer only in exceptional circumstances (e.g., extensive quotations) |
| Abstract | Published **50–200 words**; submission abstract may be longer (~250, 待核实) |
| Content types | Articles · discussions · book reviews · critical notices |
| Submission limit | **One article per corresponding author per 12-month period** |
| Submission fee | None stated (verify); optional open-access charge handled by OUP after acceptance |
| References / style | **MIND house style** (MIND Stylesheet), applied for the final accepted version |

## 7. Cautions

1. **There is no data here.** Do not import a social-science template (datasets, robustness checks,
   preregistration). A Mind paper is judged on the **soundness and significance of its argument**.
2. **Verify volatile specifics** (editors, exact caps, open-access charges, house-style details,
   accepted content types) on the official OUP/Mind pages — they change; unverified items are 待核实.
3. **Prepare for anonymous review properly** — Mind is triple-anonymous; a manuscript not prepared for
   anonymous refereeing **will not be read**.
4. **Use the MIND Stylesheet for the final version** — a generic Chicago/APA export is not the house
   style; convert references to MIND style for the accepted copy.
5. **Write for a broad philosophical readership** — keep technical material accompanied by informal
   exposition so a non-specialist philosopher can follow the argument.
