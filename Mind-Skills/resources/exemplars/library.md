# Mind Exemplars Library (by area)

> **Verified via web search, access date 2026-06-08.** Every paper below was checked against
> Oxford Academic's Mind article pages (`academic.oup.com/mind/...`) and the DOI prefix
> **`10.1093/mind/...`** to confirm it actually appeared in *Mind* (Oxford University Press, on behalf of
> the **Mind Association**), with year and volume/issue. Candidates that could not be fully verified *as
> Mind* were **omitted** and recorded below — this is deliberately a short, clean list rather than a
> long, uncertain one (6 verified beats 15 guessed).
>
> **Use principle (zero hallucination):** this file gives **dialectical positioning only** — what the
> thesis is and why the paper is exemplary as a *move in a debate*. It does not paraphrase the full
> argument; read the original on Oxford Academic before attributing a view to any author. For
> Mind-specific scope, history, and house-style facts, see
> [`../official-source-map.md`](../official-source-map.md).
>
> **Sibling-confusion guard.** Mind is **not** *Noûs*, *The Journal of Philosophy*, *The Philosophical
> Review*, *Analysis*, *Philosophy and Phenomenological Research*, or the *Australasian Journal of
> Philosophy*. Several canonical papers people *assume* are "in Mind" are in fact in those siblings; the
> omissions list at the bottom records the ones checked here.

## How to use this library

Pick the row whose **area × kind of move** is closest to yours, then study how that paper states a
**sharp, deniable thesis** and makes it a genuine move in a live debate — the Mind bar (see
[`../../skills/mind-thesis-and-argument/SKILL.md`](../../skills/mind-thesis-and-argument/SKILL.md) and
[`../../skills/mind-literature-positioning/SKILL.md`](../../skills/mind-literature-positioning/SKILL.md)).
For each, ask the self-check question before claiming your paper is "Mind-shaped."

## By area

### Metaphysics (time, change, the structure of reality)

- **McTaggart, "The Unreality of Time," Mind 1908, XVII(4):457–474.**
  Verified: `academic.oup.com/mind/article-abstract/XVII/4/457/985062` (DOI `10.1093/mind/XVII.4.457`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a single sharp distinction (the A-series vs. the B-series) is pressed into
    a paradox that still organizes the metaphysics of time a century later — the model of a *deniable
    thesis* whose argument the reader can check step by step.
  - **Self-check:** does your thesis turn on one clean distinction a reader can grasp and then contest,
    rather than a vague "rethinking" of a topic?

### Philosophy of language & logic (reference, denoting)

- **Russell, "On Denoting," Mind 1905, XIV(4):479–493.**
  Verified: `academic.oup.com/mind/article/XIV/4/479/964157` (DOI `10.1093/mind/XIV.4.479`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** the theory of descriptions dissolves a cluster of puzzles about
    non-referring terms with one technical apparatus, then *glosses it informally* — exactly the
    "accompany formalism with informal exposition" expectation in
    [`../../skills/mind-writing-style/SKILL.md`](../../skills/mind-writing-style/SKILL.md).
  - **Self-check:** does your formal move come with a plain-language gloss a non-specialist philosopher
    can follow?

### Philosophy of language (reference, presupposition)

- **Strawson, "On Referring," Mind 1950, LIX(235):320–344.**
  Verified: `academic.oup.com/mind/article-abstract/LIX/235/320/1045845` (DOI `10.1093/mind/LIX.235.320`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** a direct, named challenge to a standing view (Russell's, above) — it
    enters a *live debate* and targets the strongest version of what it opposes, the discipline
    [`../../skills/mind-literature-positioning/SKILL.md`](../../skills/mind-literature-positioning/SKILL.md)
    demands. Read alongside "On Denoting" as a paired thesis/antithesis.
  - **Self-check:** can you name the exact prior claim you reject, in its proponent's own terms?

### Philosophy of mind & logic (machine intelligence)

- **Turing, "Computing Machinery and Intelligence," Mind 1950, LIX(236):433–460.**
  Verified: `academic.oup.com/mind/article/LIX/236/433/986238` (DOI `10.1093/mind/LIX.236.433`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** replaces an unanswerable question ("Can machines think?") with a sharper,
    operational one (the imitation game), then **anticipates and answers the strongest objections in
    turn** — a worked model of the dialectic in
    [`../../skills/mind-objections-and-replies/SKILL.md`](../../skills/mind-objections-and-replies/SKILL.md).
  - **Self-check:** have you reframed a stuck question into a tractable one, and met the best objections
    in the opponent's voice rather than a strawman?

### Ethics & philosophy of religion (the problem of evil)

- **Mackie, "Evil and Omnipotence," Mind 1955, LXIV(254):200–212.**
  Verified: `academic.oup.com/mind/article-abstract/LXIV/254/200/943307` (DOI `10.1093/mind/LXIV.254.200`).
  - **Why it is an exemplar:** states the logical problem of evil as an explicit **inconsistent set** of
    propositions and shows each escape route carries a cost — the "make the argument explicit, then
    defend the load-bearing premise" pattern of
    [`../../skills/mind-thesis-and-argument/SKILL.md`](../../skills/mind-thesis-and-argument/SKILL.md).
  - **Self-check:** can your central problem be set out as a small set of jointly inescapable claims, so
    the opponent must reject one and pay for it?

## Omitted for lack of full verification, or because they are NOT in Mind (do not attribute to Mind)

To keep the list zero-hallucination, the following frequently-confused candidates were **checked and
excluded** — each is a *sibling-journal* paper, not a Mind paper:

- **Gettier, "Is Justified True Belief Knowledge?" (1963)** — verified to be in ***Analysis*** 23(6):121–123,
  **not Mind**. (`academic.oup.com/analysis/article-abstract/23/6/121/109949`.) Listed only as a guardrail.
- **Quine, "On What There Is" (1948)** — verified to be in the ***Review of Metaphysics*** 2(5):21–38,
  **not Mind**.
- **Anscombe, "Modern Moral Philosophy" (1958)** — verified to be in ***Philosophy*** (Journal of the
  Royal Institute of Philosophy) 33(124):1–19, **not Mind**.
- **Geach, "Good and Evil" (1956)** and **Prior, "The Runabout Inference-Ticket" (1960)** — both verified
  to be in ***Analysis***, **not Mind**.
- **Strawson, "Freedom and Resentment" (1962)** — verified to be in the ***Proceedings of the British
  Academy*** 48:1–25, **not Mind** (despite Strawson's "On Referring" *being* a Mind paper, above).
- **Grice, "Meaning" (1957)** — verified to be in ***The Philosophical Review*** 66:377–388, **not Mind**.

Before adding any new paper to this library, confirm it on `academic.oup.com/mind` (article page with
volume/issue and a `10.1093/mind/...` DOI) and update the access-date header. When in doubt, omit.
