# Mind Official Source Map

Primary and authoritative sources behind the Mind-specific facts injected into this skill pack.
Access date for all entries: **2026-06-02**. Re-verify volatile items (editors and editorial term,
exact word/abstract caps, fee/open-access charges, house-style details, accepted content types) on
the official page before relying on them — journal pages change.

## Official journal, submission & author pages

- **https://academic.oup.com/mind/pages/General_Instructions**
  — Mind General Instructions (author guidance). Verifies: submission via the **ScholarOne** site
  (`mc.manuscriptcentral.com/MIND`) in **Word or PDF**, with high-resolution image files sent at the
  same time; **line numbering required** (LaTeX users are pointed to the `lineno` package); articles
  **no longer than around 8,000 words in total**, with submissions exceeding this considered only in
  **exceptional circumstances** (e.g., very extensive quotations from original texts); papers heavy in
  symbolism should paginate equivalently to an 8,000-word article; review is **triple-anonymous** —
  author identity is not revealed to editors or referees unless and until a paper is accepted; papers
  **must be prepared for anonymous review** (revealing acknowledgements and self-identifying references
  removed) and **papers not prepared for anonymous refereeing will not be read**; **no more than one
  article may be submitted by any corresponding author during any twelve-month period**; the published
  **abstract is 50–200 words** (a submission abstract to aid review may be longer, up to ~250 words);
  a **MIND Stylesheet** is provided and accepted papers must be prepared in the **MIND house style**;
  authors sign the **standard OUP copyright licence agreement**; an **open-access** option is available
  for a charge; one set of **proofs** is sent and only corrections (not material changes) are accepted
  at proof stage; alt-text for figures and funding declaration (CrossRef Funder Registry) are required.
- **https://academic.oup.com/mind/pages/why-submit**
  — Why submit to MIND. Verifies: a **triple-anonymised** peer-review process across all papers;
  **quality is the sole criterion of publication, with no area, no style, and no school of philosophy
  excluded**; the journal **receives over 800 submissions each year** and seeks advice from a large
  number of expert referees, associate editors, and the Editorial Board; MIND publishes **articles,
  discussions, book reviews, and critical notices**.
- **https://academic.oup.com/mind** — Mind on Oxford Academic (publisher home, current issues, advance
  articles).

## Owner, publisher, scope, history

- **https://academic.oup.com/mind/pages/About**
  — About Mind. Verifies: published by **Oxford University Press on behalf of the Mind Association**;
  known for cutting-edge work in **epistemology, metaphysics, philosophy of language, philosophy of
  logic, and philosophy of mind**, while welcoming **continental philosophy, history of philosophy, and
  interdisciplinary work**; **founded in 1876**; content includes **articles, discussion notes, book
  reviews, and critical notices**; **ISSN 0026-4423 (print), 1460-2113 (online)**.
- **https://academic.oup.com/mind/pages/Editorial_Board_and_Other_Officers**
  — Editorial Board and Other Officers (current editor, associate editors, board). Use this page to
  confirm the **current editorial team** rather than relying on any name cached here.
- **https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mind_(journal)** — Verifies: Mind is a **quarterly** peer-reviewed
  philosophy journal, founded **1876**, published by Oxford University Press for the Mind Association;
  long regarded as a leading journal in analytic philosophy.

## Book reviews & critical notices

- **https://academic.oup.com/mind** (Book Reviews section) — Verifies: each issue carries **book
  reviews** that summarise and evaluate recent publications, and **critical notices**, which are
  **longer than book reviews** and let the author develop their own ideas in response to the book under
  discussion.
