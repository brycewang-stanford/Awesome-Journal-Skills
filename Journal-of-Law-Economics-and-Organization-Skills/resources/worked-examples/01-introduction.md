# Worked Example: Reframing an Introduction for Journal of Law, Economics, and Organization

This is a fictional example. It does not describe a real paper.

## Before

> This paper studies an important topic using a large dataset and several robustness checks. We contribute to the literature by adding new evidence and showing heterogeneous effects.

## Diagnosis

- The first sentence does not name the JLEO audience or the journal's law, economics, and organization with emphasis on contracts, institutions, governance, and organizational design scope.
- The contribution could fit several siblings: Journal of Law and Economics, Journal of Legal Studies, Organization Science, and American Law and Economics Review.
- The mechanism words that matter here are absent: organizational governance, contracting institution, legal-economic mechanism, agency problem.

## After

> This paper asks how **organizational governance** changes the decision margin that JLEO readers care about. The design ties the estimate to **contracting institution**, separates the main mechanism from **legal-economic mechanism**, and reports uncertainty in a way that makes the policy or theory implication portable beyond the study setting.
